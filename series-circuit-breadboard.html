2. how to utilize the breadboard in an electronic circuit?​


Answer:

The purpose of the breadboard is to make quick electrical connections between components- like resistors, LEDs, capacitors, etc- so that you can test your circuit before permanently soldering it together. Breadboards have many small sockets on them, and some groups of sockets are electrically connected to each other.


3. why is there a need to utilize breadboard in an electronic circuit?​


Answer:

Why Use Breadboards? An electronics breadboard (as opposed to the type on which sandwiches are made) is actually referring to a solderless breadboard. These are great units for making temporary circuits and prototyping, and they require absolutely no soldering.

4. what is breadboards​


Explanation:

A breadboard is a construction base for prototyping of electronics. Originally the word referred to a literal bread board, a polished piece of wood used for slicing bread. In the 1970s the solderless breadboard became available and nowadays the term "breadboard" is commonly used to refer to these.

Answer:

The breadboard (seen at the left) is made up from metal strips. ... In the middle of the breadboard there is a small gap (a notch running in parallel to the long side) which separates the board into two halves. The gap is useful for placing typical DIP (Dual in-line Package) integrated circuits on it.
