INVERSE VARIATION

Problem:

» The amount of time t, needed to dig ditches varies inversely with the of people p, who are digging the ditches. If it takes 3 people 40 hours to dig a ditch, how many hours will it take for 5 people to dig the same size ditch?

a. 24 hrs.b. 42 hrs. c. 50 hrs. d. 52 hrs.

Final Answer:24 hours

Given: [tex] \: \: \: \: \: \sf{ t_{1}} = 40 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: p_{1} = 3 \\\: \: \: \: \: \sf{ t_{2}} = \: ? \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: p_{2} = 5[/tex]Solution:

First, write the equation of variation and find the constant (k) by substituting the values of [tex] \sf{ t_{1}}[/tex] and [tex] \sf{ p_{1}}.[/tex]

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf{ t_{1} = \frac{k}{ p_{1} } } \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf{ 40 = \frac{k}{ 3 } } \\ \sf{40 \: (3) = k} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \sf{ \purple{120 } = k}[/tex]

Next, rewrite the equation of variation then substitute the given value of [tex]\sf{ p_{2}}[/tex] to solve for the value of [tex] \sf{ t_{2}}.[/tex]

[tex] \: \: \: \sf{ t_{2} = \frac{120}{ p_{2}}} \\ \: \: \: \sf{ t_{2} = \frac{120}{ 5}} \\ \sf{ t_{2} = \purple{24}}[/tex]

Thus, the value of t (time) is 24.

14. Alone, brenda can dig a ditch in 5 hours. if jack helps her, the two of them can dig the ditch in 3 hours. how long would it take jack to dig the ditch himself?


let x= time taken by Jack

1/5 + 1/x = 1/3

LCM of 5, x, and 3 = 15x

Multiply both sides by 15x:

15x/5 + 15x/x = 15x/3

3x+15 = 5x

15 = 5x-3x

15 = 2x

Divide both sides by 2:

7½ = x or x = 7½

Answer = It would take Jack 7½ hours to dig the ditch by himself.
