3. 3. A line from the top of the building to the ground passes over 9-m pole and reaches the ground at a point 15 m from the base of the pole. if the pole is 12 m from the base of the building, how high is the building?​


Answer:

           


In the diagram, A represents the point where the line from the top of the building intersects the ground, and B represents the base of the pole. We are given that AB = 15 m and BC = 9 m, and we want to find the height of the building, which is represented by h.

Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can write:

AB^2 = AC^2 + BC^2

Substituting the given values, we get:

(15)^2 = AC^2 + (9)^2

Simplifying:

225 = AC^2 + 81

Subtracting 81 from both sides:

144 = AC^2

Taking the square root of both sides:

AC = 12

Therefore, the length of the line from the top of the building to the ground is 12 m.

Now, we can use similar triangles to find the height of the building. The triangles ABC and ADE are similar, where D represents the top of the building. This means that the corresponding sides are proportional:

AB/BC = AD/DE

Substituting the known values:

15/9 = (AD)/(AD + h)

Simplifying:

5/3 = AD/(AD + h)

Cross-multiplying:

5(AD + h) = 3AD

Expanding:

5AD + 5h = 3AD

Subtracting 3AD from both sides:

2AD + 5h = 0

Dividing by 2:

AD + (5/2)h = 0

Substituting AD = 12:

12 + (5/2)h = 0

Solving for h:

(5/2)h = -12

h = -24/5

Since height cannot be negative, we can conclude that there is an error in the given information or in our calculations.

The Angle of Elevation is the angle between a horizontal line and the line joining the observer's eye to some object above the horizontal line.

29. the supporting wire to the top of a vertical pole is 13 m long it is fastened to the ground at a point 5 m away from the foot of the pole how high is the pole


Answer:

The height of the vertical pole is 12 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Use Pythagorean Theorem to solve for the height of the pole.

c² = a² + b²

Where:

c = supporting wire, 13 m.

a = distance of the supporting wire from the foot of the pole along the ground, 5 m.

b = height of the pole

Equation:

(13 m)² = (5 m)² + b

Find the height of the pole, b:

b² = 169 m² - 25 m²

b² = 144 m²

√b² = √(144 m²)

b = 12 m

The height of the pole is 12 meters.
