[tex]s = \frac{total \: distance \: (d)}{total \: elapsed \: time \: (t)} [/tex]

[tex]s = \frac{200m + 100m}{60s \: + 20s} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{300m}{80s} [/tex]

16. The distance between two towns is 500 km. At the same moment, a passenger car and van start moving towards each other from different towns. They meet 8 hours later. If the car drives 5 km/hr faster than the van, what are their speeds?​


The van's speed is 57.5 km/h. The car's speed is the van's speed plus 5 km/h, so the car's speed is 57.5 km/h + 5 km/h = 62.5 km/h.

To solve this problem, we need to use the formula for the relative speed of two objects moving towards each other.

- This formula is:

Speed1 - Speed2 = Distance ÷ Time

- In this case, we can say that:

Car speed - Van speed = 500 km ÷ 8 hours

- We can then rearrange this equation to solve for the car's speed:

Car speed = (500 km ÷ 8 hours) + Van speed

- We also know that the car's speed is 5 km/h faster than the van's speed, so we can substitute 5 km/h for the difference between the two speeds:

Car speed = (500 km ÷ 8 hours) + (Van speed + 5 km/h)

- Since we know that the distance between the two towns is 500 km and the time it takes for the car and van to meet is 8 hours, we can plug in those values to solve for the van's speed:

Car speed = (500 km ÷ 8 hours) + (Van speed + 5 km/h)

     500 km ÷ 8 hours = (Van speed + 5 km/h)

     62.5 km/h = Van speed + 5 km/h

     57.5 km/h = Van speed

Therefore, the van's speed is 57.5 km/h. The car's speed is the van's speed plus 5 km/h, so the car's speed is 57.5 km/h + 5 km/h = 62.5 km/h.

25. Two cars start at the same time from two towns 792 kilometers apart and meet in 9 hours. One car can travel 8 km faster than the other car. How far did each car travel before they meet?


Answer:

Car 1 travel 40 km per hour and its total travel for 9 hours is 360km

Car 2 travel 48 km per hour and its total travel for 9 hours is 432km

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

2 town with the distance of 792 km.

2 cars meet in 9 hours.

1 car travel 8 hours faster than the other car.

Problem:

How far did each car travel before they meet.

Solution: please see for more info https://brainly.ph/question/2683550

To get the total travel of each car lets divide the distance of the two town assuming that the 2 cars meet at the center.

792 ÷ 2 = 396 km For each car to travel to reach the assumed center of the two town.

396 km per car

Let's divide the 396km to 9 hours (since each car travel for 9 hours before they meet) to get the km of each car travel per hour.

396 ÷ 9 = 44 km per hour    The travel rate of each car per hour.

Note:

One car travel 8 km faster than the other car, so lets divide 8 to 2 (since we have 2 cars) and add the result to the other car and subtract the result to the other.

8 ÷ 2 = 4km

Car 1: travel 44 km                              Car 2: travel 44 km

44 km - 4 km = 40km per hour          44 km + 4 km = 48km per hour

Now we can see that Car 2 travel 8km faster than Car 1.

To get the total km of each car travel for 9 hours before they meet. Multiply the the final rate of each car travel per hour.

Car 1: 40 x 9 = 360 km

Car 2: 44 x 9 = 432 km

The total travel of each car for 9 hours before they meet each other.

30. what is time taken of a car that travels 200m in 50m/s?​


Answer:

4 seconds

Explanation:

To determine the time taken by the car that travels 200m at a speed of 50m/s, we can use the formula:

time = distance / speed

where distance is the distance traveled by the car (in meters) and speed is the speed of the car (in meters per second).

Plugging in the values given in the problem, we get:

time = 200m / 50m/s

time = 4 seconds

Therefore, the car takes 4 seconds to travel a distance of 200 meters at a speed of 50 meters per second.
