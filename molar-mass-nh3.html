23. Commercial concentrated aqueous ammonia is 28% NH3 by mass and has a density of 0.90 g/mL. What is the molarity of this solution?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \%(m/m) = 28\% \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{17.031 g/mol} \\ & d_{\text{solution}} = \text{0.90 g/mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solute (NH₃).

Assume that the mass of solution is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{\%(m/m)}{100\%} \times mass_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \frac{28\%}{100\%} \times \text{100 g} \\ & = \text{28 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{28 g}}{\text{17.031 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{1.64 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the volume of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{solution}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solution}}}{d_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{100 g}}{\text{0.90 g/mL}} \\ & = \text{111 mL} \\ & = \text{0.111 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} M & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{1.64 mol}}{\text{0.111 L}} \\ & = \text{14.775 mol/L} \\ & \approx \boxed{15 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of the solution is 15 M.

24. calculate the molarity, molality and mass percent of an NH3 solution made up of 30.0g of NH3 in 70.0g of water. the density of the solution is 0.982g/mol


Solution: (Molarity)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (NH₃).

molar mass = (14.01 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 17.034 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 30.0 g NH₃} × \frac{\text{1 mol NH₃}}{\text{17.034 g NH₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 1.76118 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of solution.

mass of solution = mass of solute + mass of solvent

mass of solution = 30.0 g + 70.0 g

mass of solution = 100.0 g

Step 4: Calculate the volume of solution.

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{mass of solution}}{\text{density of solution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{100.0 g}}{\text{0.982 g/mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution = 101.833 mL}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{1.76118 mol}}{\text{0.101833 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 17.3 M}}[/tex]

Solution: (Molality)

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]
