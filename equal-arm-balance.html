2. a piece of aluminum is weighed with an equal arm laboratory balance, when the aluminum is in air, the balancing mass is 108 g. when it is immersed in water and in kerosene, the measured balancing masses are 68 and 78 g. respectively , find the densities of aluminum and kerosene.​


The densities of aluminum and kerosene : 2.7 g/cm³ and 0.751 g/cm³

Further explanation

Archimedes states that the buoyant force acting on an object which is partially or completely immersed into a fluid is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object

Buoyancy can be formulated

Fb = ρf. Vf. g

Fb = floating force

ρf = fluid density, kg / m3

Vf = volume of objects immersed in a fluid

Because the rock is totally immersed, so :

Vf = Vo(Vo= volume of the object), then

Fb = ρf. Vo. g

[tex]\rm V_o=\dfrac{Fb}{\rho_f.g}[/tex]

and

weight(w) = m.g = (ρo.Vo) g, so

[tex]\rm w=\rho\dfrac{F_b}{\rho_f.g}.g\Rightarrow \dfrac{\rho_o}{\rho_f}=\dfrac{w}{F_b}[/tex]

the aluminium is in air w air (wa) :

wa = 0.108 kg x 9.81 m/s² = 1.06 N

the aluminium is in water (ww) :

ww = 0.068 kg x 9.81 m/s² = 0.667 N

the aluminium is in kerosene (wk) :

wk = 0.078 x 9.81 m/s² = 0.765 N

immersed in water(ρ water = 1000 kg/m³)

Fb = wa - ww

Fb = 1.06 - 0.667

Fb = 0.393 N

[tex]\rm \dfrac{\rho_{al}}{\rho_w}=\dfrac{wa}{Fb}\\\\\dfrac{\rho_{al}}{1000~kg/m^3}=\dfrac{1.06}{0.393}\\\\\rho_{al}=2697.2~kg/m^3=2.7~g/cm^3[/tex]

immersed in kerosene

Fb = wa - wk

Fb = 1.06 - 0.765

Fb = 0.295 N

[tex]\rm \dfrac{\rho_{al}}{\rho_k}=\dfrac{wa}{Fb}\\\\\dfrac{2697.2}{\rho_k}=\dfrac{1.06}{0.295}\\\\\rho_{k}=750.6~kg/m^3=0.751~g/cm^3[/tex]
