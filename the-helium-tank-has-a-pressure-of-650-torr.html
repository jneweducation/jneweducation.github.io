7. a 1.) tanks of oxygen gas has a pressure of 621 torr at a 324 L container. What will be its pressure if it was tranferred another tank with a volume of 2 L?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the combined gas law:

P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

where P1, V1, and T1 are the initial pressure, volume, and temperature, respectively, and P2 and V2 are the final pressure and volume, respectively, that we are trying to find.

We are given that:

P1 = 621 torr

V1 = 324 L

V2 = 2 L

T1 = T2 (since the temperature is assumed to remain constant)

Substituting these values into the combined gas law, we get:

621 torr * 324 L / T = P2 * 2 L / T

Simplifying, we get:

P2 = 621 torr * 324 L / 2 L = 100692 torr

Therefore, the pressure of the oxygen gas transferred to the 2 L container would be 100692 torr.

9. a steel cylinder contains a mixture of nitrogen gas at 410 torr oxygen gas at 110 torr and helium gas at 232 torr what is the total pressure of the gas mixture?​

12. The pressure of a balloon filled woth helium gasis 650 torr at 25°C. If the balloon is cooled to 0°, what will the new pressure be?


ANSWER:

We can use the ideal gas law to solve this problem, which relates the pressure, volume, temperature, and number of moles of a gas:

PV = nRT

where P is the pressure, V is the volume, n is the number of moles, R is the ideal gas constant, and T is the temperature in Kelvin.

Since the number of moles, volume, and gas constant are constant for the balloon, we can use the equation to relate the initial and final pressures:

P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂

where P₁ is the initial pressure, T₁ is the initial temperature, P₂ is the final pressure, and T₂ is the final temperature.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

650 torr/298 K = P₂/273 K

Simplifying and solving for P₂, we get:

P₂ = (650 torr x 273 K)/298 K

P₂ = 594 torr (rounded to three significant figures)

Therefore, the new pressure of the balloon, cooled to 0°C, will be approximately 594 torr.

16. A 10g Helium gas inside the tank has a volume of 12.0 L under 70°C, what is the volume of gas if the pressure is 245 kpa?


Given:

[tex]mass_{\text{He}} = \text{10 g}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{12.0 L}[/tex]

[tex]T = \text{70°C + 273.15 = 343.15 K}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{245 kPa}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of He.

[tex]n = \frac{mass_{\text{He}}}{MW_{\text{He}}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{10 g}}{\text{4.003 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \text{2.49813 mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the initial pressure using ideal gas equation.

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = nRT[/tex]

[tex]P_{1} = \frac{nRT}{V_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{1} = \frac{(\text{2.49813 mol})(0.0820574 \: \frac{\text{L • atm}}{\text{K • mol}})(\text{343.15 K})}{\text{12.0 L}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{1} = \text{5.86186 atm}[/tex]

Step 3: Convert the initial pressure in atm to kPa.

[tex]P_{1} = \text{5.86186 atm} × \frac{\text{101.325 kPa}}{\text{1 atm}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{1} = \text{593.953 kPa}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the final volume using Boyle's law.

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{12.0 L} × \frac{\text{593.953 kPa}}{\text{245 kPa}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{29.1 L}}[/tex]

18. Calculate the final temperature in Rankine for a sample of helium gas with a pressure of 250 torr at 0 degrees Celsius that’s heated to give a pressure of 1500 torr


The final temperature for a sample of helium gas with a pressure of 250 torrs at 0 degrees celsius that is heated to give a pressure of 1500 torr is 2950.02 degrees Rankine

For this problem, we are going to use one of the formulas in the gas law: Gay-Lussac's Law. This law states that the temperature is directly proportional to the pressure. The formula is [tex]\frac{P_{1} }{P_{2} } = \frac{T_{1} }{T_{2} }[/tex].

Where:

[tex]P_{1}[/tex] is the initial pressure

[tex]P_{2}[/tex] is the final pressure

[tex]T_{1}[/tex] is the initial temperature

[tex]T_{2}[/tex] is the final temperature

Solving:

Note: Convert Celsius to Rankine.

[tex]T_{2} = \frac{T_{1}P_{2}}{P_{1} }[/tex] =[tex]\frac{491.67 x 1500}{250}[/tex]= 2950.02 degrees Rankine
