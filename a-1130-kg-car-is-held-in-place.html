4. A large wrecking ball is held in place by two light cables. If the mass m of the wrecking ball is 4090 kg. What is a) the tension tb in the cable that makes an angle of 40 degree with the vertical? b) the tension ta in the horizontal cable?


Answer:

The tension force Tb is 62356.52 N and the tension force Ta is 4767.87 N.

Explanation:

First is to provide a FBD or Free Body Diagram for the problem, shown below  this page.

From the given body diagram, let's collect all the vertical forces acting on the wrecking ball.

Since Tb creates an angle of 40°, it has two forces acting on it, vertical and horizontal forces

∑Fy = 0:      this represents all forces along y-axis or vertical force.

Tb Sin40° + Ta Sin0° - Fw (wrecking ball) = 0

Fw represents the weight force of the wrecking ball with a formula of Fw=mg and since Sin0 = 0, we get:

Tb Sin40° + 0 - mg = 0      

Tb Sin40° + 0 - 4090(9.8) = 0

Tb = [tex]\frac{4090(9.8)}{Sin 40}[/tex]

Tb = 62356.52 N

∑Fx = 0:     this represent all forces acting along the horizontal or x-axis

- Tb Cos40 + Ta Cos0 = 0     the negative sign(-) represent the horizontal        

                                                force in angle 40° is pulling to the left.

- (62356.52)(Cos40°) + Ta = 0     since Cos0 = 1

After simplifying and using transposition, we get:

Ta = 4767.87 N

Therefore, the tension Ta is 4767.87 N and Tb is 62356.52 N.

28. A 2 kg book is held 1 m above the floor for 50 s. the work done is


Answer:

The work done is 19.6 J.

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve for the work done on the book held a certain distance above the floor. Here, we are going to relate the potential energy with work. It is said that work is equivalent to the change of potential energy.

Therefore, for the formula we will use:

W = ΔPE

where

PE = mgh

PE     is the potential energy, unit is in Joules (J)

m      is the mass, unit is in kg

g       is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

h        is the height of the object, unit is in meter

For the given information

m = 2 kg

h = 1 m

PE = ?

Solving the problem

1. First. let us solve for the potential energy. Let us use the formula for PE and substitute the given information, we have:

PE = mgh

PE = (2 kg)(9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])(1 m)

PE = 19.6 J

2. Now, we are ready to solve for the work. Let us use the first formula above, we have:

W = ΔPE

Let us have some derivations and explanations:

W = mg(Δh)

F·s cos θ = mg(Δh)

(mg)(Δh) cos 0° = mg (Δh)

(mg)(Δh) ·1 = mg (Δh)

(mg)(Δh) = mg (Δh)

Here, the force is the weight force of the book therefore, F = mg. For s, this is the distance or the change in vertical distance of the book which is Δh = 1m. The direction of the forces on the book are parallel making an angle θ = 0° which is equivalent to one (1). Therefore,

W = PE

W = 19.6 J

Therefore, the work done on the book is 19.6 J.
