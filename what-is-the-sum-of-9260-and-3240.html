[tex]\boxed{C=klw}[/tex]

where:

[tex]C[/tex] is the labor cost;[tex]l[/tex] and [tex]w[/tex] are the length and width;[tex]k[/tex] is the constant of the variatiom

Solve for [tex]k[/tex] using the given data that [tex]C=3240[/tex] when [tex]l=9[/tex] and [tex]w=12[/tex]

[tex] C=klw [/tex]

[tex]3240= k \times 9 \times 12[/tex]

[tex]3240=108k[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle \therefore k=\frac{3240}{108}=30[/tex]

Now, solve for [tex]C[/tex] when [tex]l=6[/tex] and [tex]w=8[/tex]

[tex]C =klw[/tex]

[tex]C= 30 \times 6 \times 8[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{C=1440}[/tex]

Therefore, the labor cost would be 1440 pesos.

The measure of each exterior angle of a regular polygon is always 360/n, where n is the number of sides.

Therefore, we have:

360/n = 180 - 160 (since the exterior angle and interior angle form a linear pair)

Simplifying the equation, we get:

360/n = 20

Multiplying both sides by n, we get:

360 = 20n

Dividing both sides by 20, we get:

n = 18

Therefore, the polygon has 18 sides.
