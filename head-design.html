It is a part of the authority of the school head to designate special assignments to teachers in the school. If you were teacher A, what would you do to not violate ethical/moral standards?


Answer:

As a teacher, it is important to prioritize the education and well-being of your students. If you have been assigned to be the student publication adviser and you have no experience in campus journalism, it would be ethical and responsible for you to inform the school head of this fact and request additional support or resources to help you fulfill this role to the best of your ability.

It is also important to be open and honest with your students about your lack of experience in this area and to make it clear that you are willing to learn and work with them to develop their skills and knowledge in campus journalism. You may also want to consider seeking out professional development opportunities or seeking guidance from colleagues who have experience in this field.

Ultimately, it is important to approach this role with humility and a commitment to doing your best to support the students and to upholding the ethical standards of your profession.

Answer:

To the Hanunuo, clothing (rutay) is one of the most important criteria in distinguishing the Mangyan from the non-Manyan (damuong). A Hanunuo-Mangyan male wears a loin cloth (ba-ag) and a shirt (balukas). A female wears an indigo-dyed short skirt (ramit) and a blouse (lambung). Many of the traditional style shirts and blouses are embroidered on the back with a design called pakudos, based on the cross shape.
