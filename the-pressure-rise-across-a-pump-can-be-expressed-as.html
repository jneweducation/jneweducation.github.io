16. 3. Air fills a room with a volume a room with a volume of 5600L atmospheric pressure is 740 tort. What will be the pressure if all of the gas is pumped into an 80L tank? Convert pressure to kPa​


We can solve this problem using Boyle's Law, which states that the pressure of a gas is inversely proportional to its volume when temperature is constant.

First, we need to find the initial pressure of the air in the room:

P1 = 740 torr

Next, we can use the following formula to solve for the final pressure in the tank:

P2 = (P1 x V1) / V2

where P2 is the final pressure in the tank, V1 is the initial volume of the room (5600 L), and V2 is the volume of the tank (80 L).

P2 = (740 torr x 5600 L) / 80 L

P2 = 51,800 torr

To convert torr to kPa, we can use the conversion factor 1 torr = 0.133 kPa:

P2 = 51,800 torr x 0.133 kPa/torr

P2 = 6,890 kPa

Therefore, the pressure in the tank is 6,890 kPa.

30. A vacuum pump is used to drain a flooded mine shaft at 20 degrees celsius water. the pump pressure of water at this temperature is 2.34 kpa. the pump is incapable of lifting the water higher than 10.16 m. what is the atmospheric pressure


The atmospheric pressure is 101.9 kPa

DEFINITION OF ATMOSPHERIC PRESSURE:

Atmospheric pressure is the pressure at any point in the Earth's atmosphere. Atmospheric pressure is almost equal to hydrostatic pressure due to the weight of the air above the measuring point.

DATA FROM THE QUESTION:

Temperature (T) = [tex]20^oC[/tex]

The water pump pressure ([tex]P_2[/tex]) = 2.34 kPa

The pump is incapable of lifting the water higher than 10.16 m ([tex]z_2-z_1[/tex])

THE QUESTION:

What is the atmospheric pressure ([tex]P_1[/tex])?

FORMULA:

We can use Bernoulli's Theorem to find atmospheric pressure ([tex]P_1[/tex]). Here is the formula:

[tex]\frac{P_1}{w}+\frac{V_1^2}{2g}+z_1=\frac{P_2}{w}+\frac{V_2^2}{2g}+z_2[/tex]

Description of the formula above:

[tex]P_1 =[/tex] Pressure at elevation 1 (kPa)

[tex]w =[/tex] Total effort by force ([tex]m/s^2[/tex])

[tex]V_1 =[/tex] Velocity at elevation 1 ([tex]m/s[/tex])

[tex]g =[/tex] Gravity ([tex]9.8 m/s^2[/tex])

[tex]z_1 =[/tex] Potential head 1 ([tex]m[/tex])

[tex]P_2 =[/tex] Pressure at elevation 2 (kPa)

[tex]V_2 =[/tex] Velocity at elevation 2 ([tex]m/s[/tex])

[tex]z_2 =[/tex] Potential head 2 ([tex]m[/tex])

HOW TO SOLVE:

[tex]\frac{P_1}{w}+\frac{V_1^2}{2g}+z_1=\frac{P_2}{w}+\frac{V_2^2}{2g}+z_2\\\\\frac{P_1}{w}=\frac{P_2}{w}+\frac{V_2^2-V_1^2}{2g}+(z_2-z_1)\\\\\frac{P_1}{9.81}=\frac{2.34}{9.81}+0+(10.16)\\\\P_1=101.9kPa[/tex]


ANSWER:

So the atmospheric pressure is 101.9 kPa.
