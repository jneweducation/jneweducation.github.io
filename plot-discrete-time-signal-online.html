17. 14. What type of data is the time duration of a film? A. Categorical data Continuous data B. C. Discrete data D. All of the above‚Äã


Answer:

A.

Step-by-step explanation:

The time duration of a film is an example of continuous data. Continuous data is numerical data that can take any value within a range, and can be measured with a precision or accuracy that is limited only by the measuring instrument. The time duration of a film can be measured in seconds, minutes, or hours, and can take on any value within a continuous range. Categorical data, on the other hand, consists of values that represent categories or groups, such as the genre of a film (e.g. drama, comedy, action), and discrete data consists of numerical values that are countable and finite, such as the number of actors in a film.
