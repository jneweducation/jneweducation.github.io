14. before applying rescue breathing ventilation to the victim it is of vital important to open and check the of the victim ​


Answer:

Place your ear above the victim's mouth and nose. Watch for signs of breathing. Gasping is not breathing. If the victim isn't breathing, start Rescue Breathing. (Use a cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) barrier mask if one is available.) If you have any reservations about giving rescue breathing or consider the victim at high risk for disease transmission, perform Chest Compression-Only CPR. To perform rescue breathing:

Pinch the victim's nose to close nostrils, keeping the airway open by the head-tilt, chin-lift.

Take a deep breath and seal your lips around the outside of the victim's mouth, preferably with a CPR barrier mask, creating an airtight seal.

Give the victim 2 full breaths (1 second each), taking your lips off the victim's mouth to inhale between each breath.

Check for chest rising and falling with each breath you give. The rising of the chest during your exhalations indicates the effectiveness of your breaths.

Perform 5 sets of the 30-to-2 cycle within 2 minutes.

Continue cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) until an automated external defibrillator (AED) is applied, the person starts to move, help arrives, or until you can no longer continue.

24. how are you going to check the breathing of the victim​


Checking a victim's breath is usually done after a heart attack or drowning. This effort is done with the aim of confirming the presence of a heartbeat or breathing in the victim.

There are several things that can be done to check the victim's breathing, namely:

1. Look at his chest.

The first way that can be done is to pay attention to the victim's stomach whether it goes up and down as usually happens when we are breathing.

2. listen over their mouth and nose

The second way is to put your ear to the victim's mouth and nose. When we breathe we must make a smooth sound in our mouth and nose. This is what we can listen to when we check for a victim's breathing.

3. The last thing that can be done is to feel their breath against your cheek. Feel if there is breathing in the form of breath coming out of the victim's nose. Do this for ten to fifteen seconds.

After the above methods are carried out, as an effort to save victims, there are several steps that can be taken, such as:

1. Check pulse

Keep the victim's heart beating by checking the pulse on his wrist or checking the pulse on the side of his neck.

2. Call for medical help

If the person you want to help does not show a response or is unconscious, immediately contact the nearest medical personnel or hospital and perform CPR until help arrives.

3. Open the airways.

You can try to lift his head, then place your hand on his forehead. Next, gently lift the patient's chin to open the airway.

4. Give artificial respiration

Giving artificial respiration can be done by mouth to mouth or mouth to nose, especially if the mouth is badly injured or cannot be opened. How to give artificial respiration is as follows:

Pinch the victim's nose, then place your mouth to his.Give a breath or air from your mouth 2 times while seeing if the chest is raised like a person breathing or not. If not, try to correct the position of the neck or check again for obstruction in the airway.Repeat the chest compressions 30 times followed by 2 rescue breaths.
