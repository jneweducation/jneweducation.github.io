2. The premium on an insurance policy is 20% for the first year, 15% on the second year, and 10% for the third year. Find the total premium paid for a policy of $40,000.


Answer:

The first year premium would be 20% of $40,000, or $8,000.

The second year premium would be 15% of $40,000, or $6,000.

The third year premium would be 10% of $40,000, or $4,000.

The total premium paid over the three years would be $8,000 + $6,000 + $4,000 = $18,000.

Step-by-step explanation:

1. First, calculate the premium for the first year by multiplying the policy amount by the percentage: $40,000 x 20% = $8,000

2. Next, calculate the premium for the second year by multiplying the policy amount by the percentage: $40,000 x 15% = $6,000

3. Then, calculate the premium for the third year by multiplying the policy amount by the percentage: $40,000 x 10% = $4,000

4. Finally, add up the premiums from each year to find the total premium paid: $8,000 + $6,000 + $4,000 = $18,000


3. If availed for y years, a premium for insurance costs Php 12,000 for the first year, and Php 15,000 for every succeeding year. Write a function which gives the total amount of premiums paid by the client.


Answer:

15000y - 3000

Step-by-step explanation:

In this question, we are given an unknown variable y. This is unknown because the client is free to choose the number of years.

Let's first focus on the statement "... Php 15000 for every succeeding years." This means that we have to multiply 15000 into y to find the total cost for an unknown number of years.

Thus, we have the expression 15000y.

However, our expression isn't complete. The given states that the 15000 doesn't apply for the first year, just 12000. This means that we need to subtract 1 from our variable.

Now, we have the expression 15000(y-1) or 15000y - 15000.

Finally, note that the first year of premium isn't free; it costs 12000. Because of this, we need to add 12000 to our expression.

We now have 15000y - 15000 + 12000. We just need to simplify this and we finally have the final answer.

Therefore, the answer to cost for an insurance premium is 15000y - 3000, where y is the number of years.

20. 6. An insurance company is deciding whether to insure the lives of those leading a quest to Malaria. Based on past experience, the probability of surviving such a quest is 91.3%. If the insurance company charges a premium of Php 5,200.00 and would pay a death benefit of Php 93,000.00 if the insured were to die, what is the expected value of this insurance policy?​


The expected value of an insurance policy is the sum of the probability of each outcome multiplied by its corresponding payout. In this case, the probability of surviving the quest is 91.3% and the probability of dying is 8.7% (100% - 91.3%). The expected value of the policy is calculated as follows:

Expected value = (probability of surviving * premium) + (probability of dying * death benefit)

= (0.913 * 5200) + (0.087 * 93000)

= 4744.6 + 7901

= PHP 12645.6

Therefore, the expected value of the insurance policy is PHP 12645.6.

25. 7.) You take out a fire insurance policy on your home. The annual premium is Php 15 000.00. In case of fire, the insurance company will pay you Php 10 000 000.00. The probability of a house fire in your area is 0.0002. a. What is the expected value? b. What is the insurance company’s expected value? c. Suppose the insurance company sells 1,000 of these policies. What can the company expect to earn?


To solve this problem, you will need to calculate the expected value for both you and the insurance company.

a. The expected value for you is the amount of money you expect to receive in case of a house fire, multiplied by the probability of a house fire occurring. In this case, the expected value is 10,000,000 * 0.0002 = Php 2,000.

b. The expected value for the insurance company is the amount of money they will pay out in case of a house fire, multiplied by the probability of a house fire occurring. In this case, the expected value is 10,000,000 * 0.0002 = Php 2,000.

c. If the insurance company sells 1,000 of these policies, they can expect to earn a total of 1,000 * 15,000 = Php 15,000,000 in premiums. The company's expected value is their total expected earnings minus the expected payouts in case of a house fire. In this case, the company's expected value is 15,000,000 - (1,000 * 2,000) = Php 13,000,000.
