Answer:

The sum of the interior angles of a triangle is always 180 degrees. Therefore, we can use the information given to write an equation:

x + (x-11) + 73 = 180

Simplifying the left side of the equation, we have:

2x - 11 + 73 = 180

Combining like terms, we get:

2x + 62 = 180

Subtracting 62 from both sides, we obtain:

2x = 118

Dividing by 2, we get:

x = 59

Therefore, angle A measures 59 degrees, angle B measures x-11 = 48 degrees, and angle C measures 73 degrees.

To check our work, we can verify that the sum of the interior angles is indeed 180 degrees:

59 + 48 + 73 = 180
