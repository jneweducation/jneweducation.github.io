c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab*cos(C)

Answer:

The height of the bamboo pole is 32 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

8:6 = N:24

multiply the extremes

[tex]8 \times 24 = 192[/tex]

multiply the means

[tex]6 \times n = 6n[/tex]

then, equate

[tex] \frac{6n}{6} = \frac{192}{6} [/tex]

cancel 6 and divide 192 by 6

[tex]192 \div 6 = 32 \\ n = 32[/tex]

to check,

[tex] \frac{8}{6} = \frac{32}{24} \\ 8 \times 24 = 192 \\ 32 \times 6 = 192[/tex]

However, 32 meters is the height of the bamboo pole.#Carryonlearning

[tex]30:18=n:15[/tex]

Multiply the numbers inside and the numbers outside:

18 × [tex]n[/tex] = 18[tex]n[/tex]

30 × 15 = 450

Now, we can make a new equation:

18[tex]n[/tex] = 450

[tex]18n=450\\\frac{18n}{18} =\frac{450}{18} \\n=25[/tex]
