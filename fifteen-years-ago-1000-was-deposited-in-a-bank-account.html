6. A deposit of Rs. 6000 in a Bank account earns interest 5% compounded monthly for four years. How much amount is accumulated in the account after 4 years?


COMPOUND INTEREST

Problem:

A deposit of Rs. 6,000 in a bank account earns interest 5% compounded monthly for four years. How much amount is accumulated in the account after 4 years?

Solution:

This word problem involves compounded interest rate. In simple words, compound interest happens when your interest from your principal amount will also gain interest over a period of time. In order to solve for the future worth of money in a compound interest system, the formula to be used is:

[tex]F = P(1+\frac{NR}{n})^{nt}[/tex]

where F = the future worth of money after gaining interest

           P = the principal amount  of money or the initial amount deposited or borrowed

          NR = the given nominal rate

          n = the number of times to be compounded in a year

           t = the number of years

From our problem:

          P = Rs. 6,000

          NR = 5 percent

           n = 12 (Since it is compounded monthly and there are 12 months in a year)

          t = 4 years

If compounded quarterly, n should be equal to 4 since there are 4 quarters in a year. If compounded semi-annually, n should be equal to 2. If compounded bi-monthly,  should be equal to 6 since bi-monthly means every 2 months. If compounded semi-monthly, n should be 24 since the amount will be compounded twice in a month and we have 12 months in a year so it should be 24.

By direct substitution:

[tex]F = 6,000(1+\frac{0.05}{12})^{(12)(4)}\\F = 6,000(1+\frac{0.05}{12})^{48}\\F = Rs. 7,325.37[/tex]

Therefore, the total amount of money accumulated after 4 years in the bank account is Rs. 7,325.37.

16. A bank saving account offers 4% compounded on a quarterly basis. A customer deposits $200, in this type of account, at the start of each quarter starting with the first deposit on the first of January and the fourth deposit on the first of October. What is the total amount in his account at the end of the year?


[tex] \large \mathcal{ANSWER:} [/tex] 

[tex] \quad \large \boxed{\$ 820.20} [/tex]

[tex] \large \mathcal{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \large \underline{\textsf{Compound Interest Formula}} \\ \\ \large \quad \quad A = P\left(1 + \dfrac{r}{n}\right)^{nt} \\ \textsf{where:} \\ \begin{aligned} \quad A&=\textsf{Future Value} \\ P&=\textsf{Principal(Initial Value)} \\ r&=\textsf{Interest Rate} \\ n&=\textsf{number of times compounded in one}\:“t” \\ t&=\textsf{time} \end{aligned} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \large \underline{\textsf{Given:}} \\ \\ \begin{aligned} \quad P &=\$ 200 \:\textsf{(deposit at the start of each quarter)}\\ r&=4 \% = 0.04 \\ n&=4 \\ t_1&= 1\:\textsf{yr (First quarter deposit)} \\ t_2&= \dfrac{3}{4}\:\textsf{yr (Second quarter deposit)} \\ t_3&= \dfrac{1}{2}\:\textsf{yr (Third quarter deposit)} \\ t_4&= \dfrac{1}{4}\:\textsf{yr (Fourth quarter deposit)} \end{aligned} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \large \underline{\textsf{Required:}}\\ \\ \quad \textsf{Total Amount} = A_1 + A_2 + A_3 + A_4 \\ \\ \large \underline{\textsf{Solution:}} \\ \\ \begin{aligned} A_1 &= 200\left(1 + \dfrac{0.04}{4}\right)^{4(1)} = \$ 208.12 \\ A_2 &= 200\left(1 + \dfrac{0.04}{4}\right)^{4(\frac{3}{4})} = \$ 206.06 \\ A_3 &= 200\left(1 + \dfrac{0.04}{4}\right)^{4(\frac{1}{2})} = \$ 204.02 \\ A_4 &= 200\left(1 + \dfrac{0.04}{4}\right)^{4(\frac{1}{4})} = \$ 202 \end{aligned} \\ \begin{aligned} \footnotesize \textsf{Total Amount} &= \footnotesize \$ 208.12 + \$ 206.06 + \$ 204.02 + \$ 202 \\ &=\boxed{\$ 820.20} \end{aligned} \end{array} [/tex]

20. Kedron deposits $1000 into an account that earns 4% simple interest each year. Calculate the interest he earns after 3 years.


Answer:

To calculate the interest earned after 3 years with simple interest, you can use the formula:

Interest = Principal * Rate * Time

Where:

- Principal is the initial amount deposited, which is $1000 in this case.

- Rate is the interest rate per year, which is 4% or 0.04 as a decimal.

- Time is the number of years, which is 3 years.

Plugging in the values, we get:

Interest = $1000 * 0.04 * 3 = $120

Therefore, Kedron earns $120 in interest after 3 years.
