8. a spherical conductor with radius 70cm has a surface charged density of 60 uC/m², what is the total charge on this sphere?​


Answer:

The surface area of a sphere with radius r is given by:

A = 4πr²

The total charge on the sphere can be found by multiplying the surface area by the surface charge density:

Q = σA

where Q is the total charge, σ is the surface charge density, and A is the surface area.

Substituting the given values, we get:

A = 4π(0.7m)² = 1.54 m²

Q = (60 × 10^-6 C/m²) × 1.54 m² = 92.4 μC

Therefore, the total charge on the spherical conductor is 92.4 microcoulombs.

10. Find (a) magnitude of the electric field 0.5 m from a 2.5 -UC point charge and (b) the magnitude and direction of the electrostatic force acting on an electron placed at that point.​


Answer:

(a) The magnitude of the electric field due to a point charge q at a distance r away from it is given by Coulomb's law as:

E = k*q/r^2, where k is the Coulomb's constant.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

E = (9 x 10^9 N*m^2/C^2) * (2.5 x 10^-6 C) / (0.5m)^2

= 9 x 10^4 N/C

Therefore, the magnitude of the electric field at a distance of 0.5 m from a 2.5-μC point charge is 9 x 10^4 N/C.

(b) The electrostatic force acting on an electron placed at that point is given by:

F = q * E, where q is the charge of the electron, and E is the electric field at that point.

The charge of an electron is -1.6 x 10^-19 C. Substituting the given values, we get:

F = (-1.6 x 10^-19 C) * (9 x 10^4 N/C)

= -1.44 x 10^-14 N

The magnitude of the electrostatic force acting on the electron is 1.44 x 10^-14 N. Since the charge of the electron is negative, the force acts in the direction opposite to the electric field.
