14. A helicopter is flying at a height of 1.5km. If it has 50 x 10 6 Joules of potential energy, what is the mass of the helicopter?


The mass of the helicopter : 3401.36 kg

Further explanation

Potential energy (PE) is the energy that an object has because of the location of that object. Usually, it is related to the force of gravity

 [tex]\rm PE=m.g.h[/tex]

g = acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)

h = height (m)

m = mass (kg)

The higher the object from the surface of the earth and the greater the mass, the greater the potential energy of the object. Earth's gravity also affects the potential energy of objects

Height of helicopter : h = 1.5 km = 1500 m

Potential energy :PE = 50.10⁶ J

The mass of helicopter :

[tex]\rm mass=\dfrac{PE}{g.h}\\\\mass=\dfrac{50.10^6}{9.8\times 1500}\\\\mass=3401.36~kg[/tex]
