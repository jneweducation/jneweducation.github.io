2. The golf scores for a school team were normally distributed with a mean of 68 and a standard deviation of three. Find the probability that a randomly selected golfer scored less than 65


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

To find the propability of a randomly selected golfer that scored, we need to convert the normal distribution to standard normal distribution or in short is called Property of Normal Distribution.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{aligned} & \bold{Where,} \\ & \quad \boxed{\begin{array}{l} \rm{x = 65} \\ \rm{\mu = 68} \\ \rm{\sigma = 3} \end{array}}\end{aligned}\end{gathered}[/tex]

Then, The Probability is :

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{gathered}\begin{array}{cc}\rm p(x < 65) = \Phi \bigg\{\frac{x \: - \: \mu}{\sigma}\bigg\} \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad \: = \Phi \bigg(\frac{65 \: - \: 68}{3}\bigg) \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \: \: \: = \Phi (-1) = 1 - \Phi (1) \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad = 1 - 0.8413 \\ \quad \quad \: \: = 0.1587 \end{array}\end{gathered}\end{gathered}[/tex]

Hence, The final answer is 0.1587

To determine each player's final score, we need to add their scores from both rounds together.

For Nieves:

Round 1 score = 3 over par

Round 2 score = 4 under par

Final score = (3 + (-4)) = -1 under par

For Josue:

Round 1 score = 5 over par

Round 2 score = 7 under par

Final score = (5 + (-7)) = -2 over par

For Maria:

Round 1 score = 10 under par

Round 2 score = 7 over par

Final score = (10 + (-7)) = 3 under par

For Sam:

Round 1 score = 6 under par

Round 2 score = 4 over par

Final score = (6 + (-4)) = 2 under par

Now, to rank the players, we need to compare their final scores. The player with the lowest score (furthest under par) will be ranked first, and so on.

1st Place: Maria (3 under par)

2nd Place: Sam (2 under par)

3rd Place: Nieves (-1 under par)

4th Place: Josue (-2 over par)
