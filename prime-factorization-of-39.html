10. 39. Express 1584 in prime factors using exponential notation​


Answer:

2^4 × 3^2 × 11

Step-by-step explanation:

We start by the smallest prime number, which is 2.

1584 ÷ 2 = 792

You keep dividing the smallest prime number until it's unfactorable. Then, move to the next prime number which is 3.

792 ÷ 2 = 396

396 ÷ 2 = 198

198 ÷ 2 = 99

99 ÷ 3 = 33

33 ÷ 3 = 11

Now, we have to count how many times we divide by a certain number.

We divide 2 by 4 times so 2^4 is a factor of 1584

We divide 3 by 2 times so 3^2 is a factor of 1584.

Now, we have to include the last prime number left which is 11.

The factors are 2^4 × 3^2 × 11.
