29. How do you locate places on earth using the coordinate system?


Explanation:

To locate places on Earth using the coordinate system, we primarily use latitude and longitude coordinates. Here's how it works:

Latitude:

Latitude lines, also called parallels, are imaginary horizontal lines that circle the Earth from east to west. The equator is the starting point and is assigned a latitude of 0 degrees. The latitude lines are measured in degrees north or south of the equator, ranging from 0 degrees at the equator to 90 degrees at the North and South poles. The latitude coordinates specify the north-south position of a point on Earth.

Longitude:

Longitude lines, also known as meridians, are imaginary vertical lines that run from the North Pole to the South Pole. The prime meridian, located in Greenwich, London, is assigned a longitude of 0 degrees. The longitude lines are measured in degrees east or west of the prime meridian, ranging from 0 degrees at the prime meridian to 180 degrees east and 180 degrees west. The longitude coordinates specify the east-west position of a point on Earth.

Combining Latitude and Longitude:

To locate a specific point on Earth, you need to know both the latitude and longitude coordinates. The combination of latitude and longitude gives a unique position to every point on Earth's surface. The coordinates are typically written in the format of degrees (°), minutes ('), and seconds (") or in decimal degrees.

For example, the coordinates of the Statue of Liberty in New York City are approximately 40.6892° N latitude and 74.0445° W longitude. These coordinates specify that the Statue of Liberty is located at 40.6892 degrees north of the equator and 74.0445 degrees west of the prime meridian.

By using latitude and longitude coordinates, you can pinpoint any location on Earth, which is useful for navigation, mapping, and determining the exact position of places.

Answer:

To locate places on Earth using the coordinate system, you need to understand latitude and longitude, which are the two coordinates used to specify any location on the Earth's surface.

Latitude refers to the distance north or south of the equator, which is an imaginary line that circles the Earth at 0° latitude. The equator divides the Earth into the Northern Hemisphere and Southern Hemisphere, with latitude values ranging from 0° at the equator to 90° at the North and South poles.

Longitude refers to the distance east or west of the Prime Meridian, which is an imaginary line that runs from the North Pole to the South Pole and passes through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, England at 0° longitude. Longitude values range from 0° at the Prime Meridian to 180° east or west, depending on whether the location is east or west of the Prime Meridian.

To locate a place on Earth using the coordinate system, you need to know the latitude and longitude coordinates of that location. These coordinates are usually given in degrees, minutes, and seconds, or in decimal degrees. For example, the coordinates of the Statue of Liberty in New York City are approximately 40.6892° N, 74.0445° W, which means that the statue is located 40.6892 degrees north of the equator and 74.0445 degrees west of the Prime Meridian.

You can use maps, globes, or digital tools like Google Maps or GPS devices to find the latitude and longitude of a particular location. Once you have the coordinates, you can plot them on a map or use them to navigate to that location using a GPS device or other navigational tools.
