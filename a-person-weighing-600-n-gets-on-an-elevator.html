13. A person weighs 105 N on Earth, calculate the mass of the person. ​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The weight of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = mg}[/tex]

where:

W = weight

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for m

W = mg

[tex]\dfrac{W}{g} = \dfrac{mg}{g}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{W}{g}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{105 N}}{\text{9.8 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{10.71 kg}}[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of the person is 10.71 kg.

27. A Person Weighing 600N gets on elevator. The elevator lifts the person 6m in 10 seconds. How much power was used


Answer:

The power used is 360 Watts.

Explanation:

Power is the work done per unit time or it is the transfer of energy.

For the formula, we use:

a.  [tex]P=\frac{W}{t}[/tex]

b.  [tex]PE=mgh[/tex]

where

[tex]P[/tex]     is the power, unit is in Watts (W) or Joules/sec (J/s) or N.m/s

[tex]W[/tex]    is the work, unit is in Joules (J) or Newton-meter (N.m)

[tex]t[/tex]       is the time, unit is in seconds (s)

[tex]PE[/tex]   is the gravitational potential energy, unit is in Joules (J) or Newton-  

       meter (N.m)

[tex]m[/tex]     is the mass, unit is in kg

[tex]h[/tex]      is the height or vertical displacement of a body, unit is in meters (m)

For the given information

w = 600 N

h  = 6 m

t   = 10 seconds

Solving the problem

1. Solve the gravitational potential energy

 [tex]PE=mgh[/tex]

 [tex]PE=(600N)(6m)[/tex]

We use 600N for [tex]mg[/tex], since weight force of a body is, [tex]Fw=mg[/tex]

[tex]PE=3600N.m=3600J[/tex]

2. Applying the value of [tex]PE[/tex] to the formula of power, we get:

  [tex]P=\frac{W}{t}[/tex]

  [tex]P=\frac{PE}{t}[/tex]

  [tex]P=\frac{3600J}{10s}[/tex]

  [tex]P=360Watts[/tex]
