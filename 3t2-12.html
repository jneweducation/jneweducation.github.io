21. Which of the following mathematical statement is a quadratic inequality?a, 2r2 - 3r – 15 = 0c. 3t2 + 75 -2 > 0b. 7h + 12 < 0d. s2 + 8 + 15 = 0​


Answer:

3t² + 75 - 2 > 0

Step-by-step explanation:

because this sign → > ← is less than sign and it's called an inequality sign. It is said to be an quadratic inequality because you can change the inequality sign which is ">" to equal sign "=".

→ 3t² + 75 - 2 > 0→ 3t² + 75 - 2 = 0

as you can see the less than sign has changed into an equal sign. And it follows the quadratic formula which is "ax² + bx + c = 0".

Always remember that an quadratic equation that has an inequality signs like "≥ , > , ≤ , <," that can change into an equal sign and follows the quadratic formula is considered to be an QUADRATIC INEQUALITY.
