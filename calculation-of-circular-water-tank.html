8. the water tank of the school is a right circular cylinder with a diameter of 36 inches and a height 6 ft. what is the volume of the water in (a) in cubic feet (b) cubic meter (c) liters


Given:
dm = 36 in.
h = 6 ft.

Required:
V @

a.) cu. ft
b.) cu. m
c.) L

Solution:
The volume of a right circular-base cylinder is:

V = π•r^2•h
(In terms of radius)

V = π (d / 2)^2 (h)
(in terms of diameter)

Convert the dm first:
dm = 36 in (1 ft / 12 in) = 3 ft

Proceed on solving the V:
V = π (3 ft / 2)^2 (6 ft)
V = 42.41 cu. ft (final answer)

In solving for b and c; you can either convert the dm into their respective units to find the required. Or just convert the answer to cu. m and liters directly.

V = 42.41 cu. ft (0.305 m / 1 ft)^3
V = 1.20 cu. m (final answer)

V = 42.41 cu. ft (30.48 cm / 1 ft)^3 (1 L / 1000 cc)
V = 1,200.92 L (final answer)

22. A dairy farmer notices that a circular water tank near the barn has become rusty and now has a hole near the base. The hole is 0.30 m below the level of the water that is in the tank. If the top of the tank is open to the atmosphere, what is the speed of the water as it leaves the hole?


The speed of the water as it leaves the hole : 2.425 m/s

Further explanation

Torricelli's theorem states that the velocity of water spraying through a hole from the surface of the water is equal to the free-fall speed of the water.

This theorem is a simplified form of the Bernoulli equation

[tex]\tt P+\dfrac{1}{2}\rho v^2+\rho gh=constant[/tex]

Can be formulated :

[tex]\boxed{\bold{v=\sqrt{2gh}}}[/tex]

v  = fluid velocity (m/s)

g  = acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)

h  = height (m)⇒below the level of the water

Given

h = 0.3 m

Required

the speed of the water

Solution

Input the value :

[tex]\tt v=\sqrt{2\times 9.8\times 0.3}\\\\v=\boxed{\bold{2.425~m/s}}[/tex]
