4. Find the area of Kite No X-I3 cr=x 3x-5​


To find the area of a kite, we need to know the lengths of both the diagonals. Let's assume that cr is the length of one diagonal, and x and 3x-5 are the lengths of the two sides adjacent to that diagonal.

Using the properties of a kite, we know that the other diagonal must be perpendicular to the diagonal cr and have a length of:

d = √(4x^2 - cr^2)

To find the area of the kite, we can then use the formula:

Area = 0.5 × cr × d

Substituting the expression for d, we get:

Area = 0.5 × cr × √(4x^2 - cr^2)

Substituting cr = x + 3x-5 = 4x-5, we get:

Area = 0.5 × (4x-5) × √(4x^2 - (4x-5)^2)

Simplifying the expression inside the square root:

Area = 0.5 × (4x-5) × √(16x^2 - 16x^2 + 40x - 25)

Area = 0.5 × (4x-5) × √(40x - 25)

Therefore, the area of the kite with diagonals of lengths cr=x and 3x-5 is given by the expression:

Area = 0.5 × (4x-5) × √(40x - 25)

Kirchhoff's Circuit Laws

Attached below are the Solution and Diagram

By defining a collection of fundamental network principles and theorems for the voltages and currents around a circuit.

Kirchhoff's Circuit Laws enable us to tackle complex circuit problems.

If we know the values of the electrical components inside a closed circuit, we can calculate and find the currents and voltages around any such circuit using Kirchhoff's circuit law in relation to the junction rule and his closed loop rule.According to Kirchhoff's current law, "the algebraic sum of current flow in a network of wires meeting at a point is zero" and "At any node (junction) in an electrical system, the sum of currents coming into that junction is equal to the sum of current passing out of that node."

Thus, the sum of the currents entering and leaving the junction is equal. It follows from this that the current is maintained (no loss of current). According to the laws of energy conservation, any closed circuit's directed sum of the applied electric differences (voltage) is zero.
