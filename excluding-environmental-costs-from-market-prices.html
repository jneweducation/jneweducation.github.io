1. A service garage uses 120 boxes of cleaning cloths a year. the boxes cost $6 each. ordering cost is $3 and holding cost is 10 percent of purchase cost per unit on an annual basis. determine the total cost of carrying the cloths (excluding purchase price)


Answer:

A service garage uses 120 boxes of cleaning cloths a year. The boxes cost $6 each. Ordering cost is $3 and holding cost is 10 percent of purchase cost per unit on an annual basis.

Determine:

(A) the economic order quantity

(B) the total cost of ordering and carrying the cloths (excluding purchase price)

(C) the average inventory

A) 35 units

(B) $20.78

(C) 18 units

Explanation:

A) The economic order quantity is given by Q = Sqrt [(2SD)/(i*C)]

where

D = Demand rate, units per year = 120

S = Cost per order placed, or setup cost = $3

C = Unit cost, dollars per unit = $6

i = Carrying rate, percent of value per year = 10%

So Q = Sqrt {(23120)/(10%*6)} = 34.64 or 35 units

B) The total cost of carrying the cloths (excluding purchase price)

TC= total of ordering cost plus carrying cost

Ordering cost = (cost per order) x orders per year) = SD/Q = 3*120/35 = $10.28

Carrying cost per year = (annual carrying rate) x (unit cost) x average inventory = iCQ/2 = 10%635/2 = $10.5

So TC = 10.28+10.50 = $20.78

C) The average inventory is equal to EOQ/2 = 35/2 = 17.5 ie approx 18 units

23. provides blood to and from the tissues of the body,excluding the lungs?​


Answer:

Systematic

Explanation:

In the systemic loop, oxygenated blood is pumped from the left ventricle of the heart through the aorta, the largest artery in the body. The blood moves from the aorta through the systemic arteries, then to arterioles and capillary beds that supply body tissues. Here, oxygen and nutrients are released and carbon dioxide and other waste substances are absorbed. Deoxygenated blood then moves from the capillary beds through venules into the systemic veins. The systemic veins feed into the inferior and superior venae cavae, the largest veins in the body. The venae cavae flow deoxygenated blood to the right atrium of the heart.
