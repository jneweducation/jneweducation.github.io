To solve fractions involving improper and mixed fractions, convert all mixed numbers to improper fractions. Doing these we also get their equivalent fractions.

Let's convert the following mixed fractions.

2 ½, 3½, 2⅓

[tex]2 \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{2} \\ 3 \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{7}{2} \\ 2 \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{3} [/tex]

Here, the equivalent fractions of the given

3½ is 7/2 and the given 2⅓ is 7/3

Now, let's solve the numerical expression above while using the converted improper fractions.

[tex] = \frac{ \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{7}{18} \times 2 \times \frac{1}{2} }{ \frac{3 \times \frac{1}{2} }{2 \times \frac{1}{3} } } \\ = \frac{ \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{7}{18} \times \frac{5}{2} }{ \frac{\frac{7}{2} }{\frac{7}{3} } }[/tex]

Here, notice that we have to operations to solve, multiplication and division.

where L= lateral area

            B= base area

L= Perimeter x height

 = (10+13+13) x 22

 = 36 x 22

 = 792 cm^2
