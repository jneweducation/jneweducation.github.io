3. How would you describe the motion of the vehicle?


Rectilinear Motion

The car is traveling in a straight path while it is on a straight road. As a result, the motion is rectilinear. Each of the body's contained particles will follow a similar route in this rectilinear motion. As long as the automobile is going in a straight path, the motion will also be rectilinear.

Further explanation

Rectilinear (or linear) motion is the term used to describe a particle's movement when it is limited to traveling in a straight line. One coordinate alone can be used to describe such a motion. The particle's displacement and its derivatives, including its velocity and acceleration, are all one-dimensional vectors. A automobile travelling straight ahead in free fall under the influence of Earth's gravitational field can be compared to rectilinear movements.

The most fundamental type of motion is linear motion. Newton's first law of motion states that unless a net force is applied, an object moving without any net force will continue to travel in a straight path with a constant speed. An object's motion cannot be defined as linearly under normal conditions since external factors like gravity and friction can cause an item to shift its direction of travel.

General motion and linear motion can be compared. Vectors, which have a magnitude and direction, are used to characterize a particle's location and velocity in general motion. The items travel along the same axis and do not change direction while they are in linear motion since all the vectors characterizing the system have equal and constant directions. Therefore, the analysis of such systems may be made simpler by ignoring the vectors' direction components and focusing just on their magnitude.

5. how can you be safe in riding a vehicle ? apply the first law of motion​


Answer:

Newton's Law of Inertia:

Newton's first law tells us that an object in motion stays in motion, and an object at rest stays at rest unless acted upon a net force

The first law of motion affects us when we are in a moving vehicle because, no matter how fast the car is moving, we will be moving that fast also. Things have the tendency to keep doing what they are currently doing.

Inertia is the tendency to resist the change in motion. So, if you crash your car into something, your body will resist the change in motion and, unless something else stops it first, your body will crash into the object at the same speed that the car crashed into the object. This part is where the seatbelt and airbags comes into play.

Seatbelts and airbags can help protect people from this danger. Seatbelts safely provide a net force that can stop or slow down your body and airbags works by increasing the time of impact and decreasing the force of impact that will stop you from getting hurt or being killed.

3.) No, the speed of the vehicle did not remain the same.

4.) The vehicle moved fastest at the point when it was traveling at 45km/hr before it stopped at the red light. It moved slowest when it was initially moving at 15km/hr, and it traveled at a uniform or constant rate when it was moving at 35km/hr.

Answer:

1. The motion of the jeepney can be described as a combination of constant speed and acceleration/deceleration. It initially moves at a constant speed of 45 km/hr for 0.5 hrs, then slows down to a stop at a red light. After waiting for 5 minutes, it accelerates from rest to a speed of 15 km/hr over a period of 15 minutes, and then accelerates again to a speed of 35 km/hr over another 15 minutes. It then continues to move at a constant speed of 35 km/hr until it reaches another stop.

2. Yes, the speed of the vehicle changed multiple times throughout the ride. The initial speed of the jeepney was 45 km/hr, which then decreased to 0 km/hr when it stopped at the red light. The speed then increased to 15 km/hr, and then to 35 km/hr.
