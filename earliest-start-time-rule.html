14. what was the earliest baked goods made during ancient Greek times, Describe it?​


Answer:
One of the earliest baked goods made during ancient Greek times was the flatbread known as "plakous," which was made with barley or wheat flour, water, and sometimes olive oil. It was often eaten as a snack, and it could also be served with honey or cheese. Plakous was typically cooked on a hot griddle or hearthstone, and it was often sprinkled with sesame seeds or other toppings for added flavor and texture. Another popular baked good in ancient Greece was "pasteli," a sweet treat made with sesame seeds and honey that was similar to modern-day nougat or brittle.
