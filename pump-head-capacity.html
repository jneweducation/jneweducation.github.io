7. A pump must move a discharge of 1.1 cfs against a head of 30 ft. If the pump is driven by a 5 kW motor with an efficiency of 75%, what is the brake horsepower


Answer:

Brake horsepower = 5 hp

Explanation:

Given:

Volumetric flow rate of water, [tex]Q_{w} =1.1cfs[/tex]

Head, [tex]h=30ft[/tex]

Motor power, [tex]P_{m} =5 kW[/tex]

Pump efficiency, [tex]e_{m}=75%[/tex]

Required: Brake horse power [tex]P_{b}[/tex]

Solution:

The pump efficiency is taken as the ratio of water horsepower (output) to the brake horsepower (input).

[tex]e_{p}=\frac{P_{w}}{P_{b}}[/tex]

Solving for water horsepower first:

[tex]P_{w}=\gamma_{w}Q_{w}h[/tex]

Note: The specific weight of water at 4°C is [tex]9.81 \frac{kN}{m^{3}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{w}=9.81\frac{kN}{m^{3}}(1.1\frac{ft^{3}}{s})(\frac{1m}{3.28ft})^{3}(30ft)(\frac{1m}{3.28ft})[/tex]

[tex]P_{w}=2.7970 kW[/tex]

Converting to horsepower:

[tex]P_{w}=2.7970kW(\frac{1hp}{0.746kW})=3.7493hp[/tex]

Inserting this back to the efficiency formula, we get

[tex]e_{p}=\frac{P_{w}}{P_{b}}[/tex]

[tex]0.75=\frac{3.7493hp}{P_{b}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{b}=5hp[/tex]

Explanation:

To solve this problem, we are going to use the formula of theoretical pump head below;

[tex]\boxed{\bold{h = \frac{\omega^2 d^2}{8g}}}[/tex]

Wherein,

[tex]h[/tex] - represents the theoretical pump head[tex]g[/tex] - represents the gravitational acceleration[tex]d[/tex] - represents the diameter of shaft[tex]\omega[/tex] - represents the constant rate of the shaft notation

Substitute the given values.

[tex]\rm{h = \frac{(1800rpm \frac{0.14^{rad/s}}{1 rpm})^2 \cdot (35cm \cdot \frac{0.01cm}{1cm})^2}{8 \cdot 9.8m/s^2} \approx 55.5m}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The theoretical pump head is approximately measures 55.5m
