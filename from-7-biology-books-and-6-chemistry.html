11. From 7 Biology and 6 chemistry books,in how ways many can one select 2 Biology and 2 chemistry books to buy if all the said books are equally necessary ?


This is a question that can be answered using the concept of Combination because if I get 2 Biology books from the 7 available books, for example, the order of the two books I'm getting doesn't matter. As opposed to Permutation where the order or arrangement of things DOES matter.

So, taking 2 out of the 7 Biology books means 7C2 (read "7 choose 2") which is equal to 21. (Input this in your scientific calculator using the nCr button). Taking 2 out of the 6 chemistry books means 6C2 which is equal to 15.

Using the Fundamental Principle of Counting we multiply the (21)(15)= 315.

There are 315 ways.

Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

To select 2 Biology books from 7, there are 7C2 ways, which is equal to 21. Similarly, to select 2 Chemistry books from 6, there are 6C2 ways, which is equal to 15.

To select 2 Biology books and 2 Chemistry books from the given books, we can use the multiplication principle, which states that the total number of ways to perform two independent events is the product of the number of ways to perform each event.

Therefore, the total number of ways to select 2 Biology and 2 Chemistry books is:

21 x 15 = 315

We use multiplication instead of addition because the events of selecting 2 Biology books and 2 Chemistry books are independent, meaning that the choice of Biology books does not affect the choice of Chemistry books and vice versa. So, we can multiply the number of ways of selecting each set of books to get the total number of ways of selecting both sets together.

25. From 7 biology books and 6 chemistry books in how many ways can one select two biology and 2 chemistry books to buy if all the said books are equally necessary


This is a combination problem, where we need to choose 2 biology books out of 7, and 2 chemistry books out of 6.

We can use the combination formula to solve this problem:

nCr = n! / r!(n-r)!

where n is the total number of items, and r is the number of items being selected.

For selecting 2 biology books out of 7, we have:

7C2 = 7! / 2!(7-2)! = 21

For selecting 2 chemistry books out of 6, we have:

6C2 = 6! / 2!(6-2)! = 15

We must multiply the two combinations to determine the total number of possible selections for the two biology and two chemistry books:

21 x 15 = 315

Therefore, there are 315 ways to select two biology and two chemistry books out of 7 biology books and 6 chemistry books.
