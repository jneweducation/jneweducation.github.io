Constructing a triangle using SSS Postulate:

SSS postulate states that if three sides of one triangle are congruent to three sides of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

To construct a triangle using SSS postulate, we need to draw three sides of the triangle, each with the same length as the corresponding sides of another triangle. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Draw a line segment AB, which will be one side of the triangle.

Step 2: Using a compass, draw a circle with center A and radius equal to the length of the second side of the triangle.

Step 3: Draw another circle with center B and radius equal to the length of the third side of the triangle.

Step 4: The two circles will intersect at two points. Choose one of these points, and draw a line segment from A to this point, and another line segment from B to this point.

Step 5: The triangle with sides AB, AC, and BC is the triangle we have constructed using SSS postulate.

Constructing a triangle using SAS Postulate:

SAS postulate states that if two sides and the included angle of one triangle are congruent to two sides and the included angle of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

To construct a triangle using SAS postulate, we need to draw two sides of the triangle and the included angle, each with the same length as the corresponding sides and angle of another triangle. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Draw a line segment AB, which will be one side of the triangle.

Step 2: Using a protractor, draw an angle with vertex at A and the desired angle measure. This will be the included angle for the triangle.

Step 3: From point B, draw a line segment that makes the same angle with AB as the included angle drawn in Step 2. This line segment will be one of the sides of the triangle.

Step 4: Using a compass, measure the length of the second side of the triangle from the other triangle.

Step 5: From point B, draw a line segment of the same length as the second side of the other triangle.

Step 6: The triangle with sides AB, BC, and the included angle is the triangle we have constructed using SAS postulate.


30. the artwork A Burial at ornans is an example of this artstyle​


Answer:

In France, the year 1848 witnessed the overthrow of King Louis Philippe and the June Days Uprising. Little had changed since the French Revolution of 1789, and workers took to the streets in protest. Against this backdrop, Gustave Courbet was developing a radical new style of modern art that venerated the working man and his environment.

Known as Realism, this new style of French painting challenged the conventions of academic art - the traditional style of painting taught by the Ecole des Beaux Arts and promoted by the French Academy - by placing the lives of ordinary working men and women on a par with highbrow subjects such as classical mythology, heroic historical events, formal portraiture and picturesque or dramatic scenery.

NOTE: The rural counterpart of Courbet was the Barbizon realist painter Millet, whose most celebrated paintings of peasant life included The Gleaners (1857), The Angelus (1859) and the brutish Man with a Hoe (1862).
