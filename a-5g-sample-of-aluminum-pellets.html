1. A 100.0-g sample of hot water and a 10.0-g sample of aluminum pellets at room temperature are mixed in an insulated thermos bottle and allowed to equilibrate. If the initial temperature of the pellets is 25.0°C, and the initial temperature of the hot water is 90.0°C, what was the final temperature in °C? Specific Heat Capacity of Liquid Water = 4.184 J/g-°C Specific Heat Capacity of Aluminum = 0.903 J/g-°C Report your answer in correct number of significant figures.​


Answer:

To find the final temperature, we need to use the equation of heat transfer:

Q = mcΔT

where Q is the heat transferred, m is the mass, c is the specific heat capacity, and ΔT is the change in temperature.

Explanation:

We have two heat transfers happening here:

1. Heat lost by the hot water = m1c1ΔT1

2. Heat gained by the aluminum pellets = m2c2ΔT2

The heat lost by the hot water is equal to the heat gained by the aluminum pellets. So,

m1c1ΔT1 = -m2c2ΔT2

We know that the mass of hot water is 100.0 g and the specific heat capacity of liquid water is 4.184 J/g-°C.

The mass of aluminum pellets is 10.0 g and the specific heat capacity of aluminum is 0.903 J/g-°C.

Initial temperature of the pellets is 25.0°C and the initial temperature of the hot water is 90.0°C.

So,

(100.0g)(4.184 J/g-°C)(Tf - 90.0°C) = -(10.0g)(0.903 J/g-°C)(Tf - 25.0°C)

Solving for Tf, we get

Tf = (100.0g)(4.184 J/g-°C)(90.0°C) + (10.0g)(0.903 J/g-°C)(25.0°C) / (100.0g)(4.184 J/g-°C) + (10.0g)(0.903 J/g-°C)

Tf = (41840 + 225.75) / (418.4 + 0.903)

Tf = 42065.75 / 419.303

Tf = 99.9°C

Therefore, the final temperature of the mixture is 99.9°C.

10. What is the percent mass of aluminum in a sample of ore that contains 3.85 g of aluminum and 3.50g of oxygen​


To find the percent mass of aluminum in the sample of ore, we need to determine the total mass of the sample first.

Total mass of the sample = mass of aluminum + mass of oxygen

Total mass of the sample = 3.85 g + 3.50 g

Total mass of the sample = 7.35 g

Now, we can calculate the percent mass of aluminum in the sample by dividing the mass of aluminum by the total mass of the sample and then multiplying by 100 to get a percentage:

Percent mass of aluminum = (mass of aluminum / total mass of the sample) x 100

Percent mass of aluminum = (3.85 g / 7.35 g) x 100

Percent mass of aluminum = 52.38%

Therefore, the percent mass of aluminum in the sample of ore is 52.38%.

18. compute both the number of moles of atoms and the number of atoms in a 10.0 g sample aluminum


The number of moles : 0.37

The number of atoms : 2.23 x 10²³

Further explanation  

A mole is a unit of many particles (atoms, molecules, ions) where 1 mole is the number of particles contained in a substance that is the same amount as many atoms in 12 gr C-12  

1 mole = 6.02.10²³ particles  

While the number of moles can also be obtained by dividing the mass (in grams) with the molar mass of element or molecule  

So looking for moles we can search in 2 ways  

1 . Avogadro's number

N = number of gas particles  

No = Avogadro number (6.02.10²³)  

n = number of moles  

[tex]\tt n=\dfrac{N}{No}[/tex]  

2. Molar mass

m = mass  

M = relative molecular mass  

[tex]\tt n=\dfrac{m}{M}[/tex]

Given

10 g Aluminum

Required

The number of moles and   atoms

Solution

moles Aluminum :

[tex]\tt n=\dfrac{mass}{Ar~Al}\\\\n=\dfrac{10~g}{27~g/mol}\\\\n=\boxed{\bold{0.37}}[/tex]

The number of atoms :

[tex]\tt N=0.37\times 6.02\times 10^{23}\\\\N=\boxed{\bold{2.23\times 10^{23}}}[/tex]
