The main development tasks that adolescents must complete in order to establish identity are:

1. Achieving mature relations with both sexes.

2. Achieving a masculine or feminine social role.

3. Accepting one's physique.

4. Achieving emotional independence from parents and other adults.

5. Preparing for marriage and family life.

6. Preparing for an economic career.

7. Acquiring a set of values and an ethical system as a guide to behavior.

8. Desiring and achieving socially responsible behavior.

Further explanation:Developmental Task Model by Havighurst

According to Havighurst's developmental task model, there are six stages of life: infancy and early childhood, between the ages of birth and 5, middle childhood, between the ages of 6 and 12, adolescence, between the ages of 13 and 18, early adulthood, between the ages of 19 and 30, and later maturity, which occurs after the age of 60.

Life's Stages

1.) Early childhood, which spans the years from birth until age 5. These are infants just learning to walk, communicate, and comprehend their surroundings.

2.) Middle childhood spans the ages of six and twelve. Children grow increasingly independent during this period as they attend school and establish friends.

3.) Adolescence, which lasts from the ages of 13 to 18, is characterized by hormonal changes and the discovery of one's own identity.

4.) Early adulthood, which encompasses the years between the ages of 19 and 30, entails finding a job and, frequently, a life partner.

5.) The majority of people begin families and settle into their adult life throughout middle age, which is defined as the period between the ages of 30 and 60.

6.) Later Maturity refers to the period of time after the age of 60. People start to become used to life after employment and start getting ready to die at this time.

As you can see, each stage of life has particular developmental responsibilities that often take place during that time.
