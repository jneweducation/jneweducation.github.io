8. Enumerate the different types of accommodations. Explain each


Various types of accommodation

1. Hotels

2. Villas

3. Guest houses

4. Bungalows

5. Cottages

6. Service apartments

7. Hostels

8. Lodge

Explanation:

1. Hotels

Hotel is a type of accommodation where guests usually only use part of the facilities of the building. The facilities in the room, such as toiletries and toiletries, TV, air conditioning, wardrobe, slippers, tea/coffee maker, etc. are used free of charge. Meanwhile, for supporting facilities, some can be used free of charge, such as swimming pools and gyms. There are also those that are managed commercially. for example, ballrooms, conference rooms, restaurants, bars, spas, coffee shops, shuttle services, laundry services, and others.

2. Villas

Villas are accommodation in the form of houses and are located away from the crowds. Suitable for those of you who want to escape for a while from the fatigue of the capital. The villa building is built in an unspoiled or green place, so you can enjoy the beautiful scenery around it, as well as breathe fresh air. When compared to the budget to stay at the hotel, the price of renting a villa is relatively more expensive. However, you can stay overnight with friends if you choose a villa as accommodation. The villa also provides various room and support facilities that you can enjoy during your vacation. There are supporting facilities that are free or at an additional cost.

3. Guest Houses

Guest houses are generally managed directly by the owner and it is possible that the owner also lives in the house. So, you seem like a tourist who is riding in someone else's house. The facilities in the guest house are shared property, so you can use them while staying! Sometimes, there are also guest houses that provide conference rooms so that guests can hold meetings or business meetings.

4. Bungalows

The location is also far from the crowds and is in the mountains or the beach. Uniquely, the building is made in the traditional barn style which is also dominated by wood.

5. Cottages

Similar to villas, but the cottages are not too spacious because the buildings are more like separate small cottage houses. The location is also in a beautiful natural environment, such as a beach, lake, or green park. Usually, the price is relatively cheaper than a villa or bungalow.

6. Service apartments

Serviced apartments when on vacation that can be rented for long term or short term. The advantage of staying in a serviced apartment is that the atmosphere is more private, like your own apartment.

7. Hostels

Hostels are accommodations that are similar to dormitories. Because, guests who stay at the hostel have to share with other guests who are not known. Usually, those who stay at hostels are backpackers who vacation on a low budget. They only make accommodation as a place to sleep.

8. Lodge

The lodge stands in the middle of nature, where you can interact with nature and rare animals. Perfect for those of you who really like animals.
