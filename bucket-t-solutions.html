13. There are two buckets: A and B. There are 11 kg of pure alcohol in bucket A and 15 kg of water in bucket B. Pour a part of the pure alcohol in bucket A into the bucket B and mixed it with water. Pour part of the mixture in bucket B into bucket A, then the pure alcohol content in bucket A become 62.5% while the pure alcohol content in bucket B will be 25%. In the second mixing, how many kg is the mixture poured from bucket B into bucket A?


Answer:

See below.

Explanation:

When pouring pure alcohol from bucket A into bucket B, the content of bucket B becomes an alcohol solution. After pouring alcohol solution from bucket B to bucket A, the concentration of bucket B has not changed.

However, when pouring pure alcohol from bucket A into bucket B, the 15 kg of water in bucket B turns into a 25% concentration of alcohol solution, and subsequently, the solution in bucket B will be 15 ÷ (1 - 25%) = 20 kg.

So bucket B contains 20 - 15 = 5 kg of pure alcohol. The remaining pure alcohol in bucket A is 11 - 5 = 6 kg.

Let the alcohol solution poured from bucket B to bucket A be x kg, then it contains pure alcohol of 25% of x = x/4. Because 6 kg of pure alcohol in bucket A has an alcohol solution with a concentration of 62.5%,

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{6+\frac{x}{4}}{6+x}=\frac{62.5}{100}\implies x=6\end{aligned}[/tex]

Thus, the mixture poured from bucket B to bucket A is 6 kg on the second time.

17. Which graph shows the equation V = 4 + 2t, where V is the total volume of water in a bucket and t is the elapsed time in minutes?


Hi Peaceyow1535,

This equation that you have given is a linear equation in its slope intercept form. Typically, this type of function always form a straight line that passes to what it is called the y-intercept. Let us understand first the nature of this equation shall we?

The slope-intercept form

This form of linear equation reveals itself in this format.

[tex]y=mx+b[/tex]

such that

y = dependent variable

x = independent variable

m = slope

b = y-intercept

The y-intercept

In essence the y-intercept is the resulting value of y if the value of x is zero (0). Evidently, as the graph of the line equation approaches the y-axis it will cross to the said y-intercept.

The slope

Slope is the steepness of a line. It is defined mathematically as rise over run. This can be simplified through this equation.

[tex]slope=\frac{rise}{run}[/tex]

It needs a fraction to easily define a slope. For example if the slope is [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] it means that for every 2 units along the x-axis, there is a resulting 1 unit to be plotted along the y-axis.

However, if the slope is a whole number, we can rephrase the whole number form to fraction form by placing 1 as the denominator. This holds true because any number divided by 1 is the same number. For example, if the slope is 3 we can rephrase it by [tex]\frac{3}{1}[/tex]. This means that for every 1 unit that run along x-axis there is a resulting rise of 3 units along the y-axis.

How do these work?

Now that you have understood the two essential factors to describe the graph of the line, you can plot it without manually computing the values of y for every values of x. Such that for your problem

[tex]V=4+2t[/tex]

can be rephrased to follow the slope-intercept form

[tex]V=2t+4[/tex]

From that point you may now know that

the slope of the line is [tex]\frac{2}{1}[/tex] ;andthe y-intercept is 4

You can refer to the graph that I have made for you as your reference. I have used a rectangular coordinate system also known as the Cartesian plane.  You can test it and see for yourself.
