20. 1.Two objects A with a mass of 4.7kg and B with a mass of 3.8g are approaching each other with velocities 5.5m/s and 9.8m/s respectively. Find the A.) velocity after the impact assuming that this is a perfectly inelastic collision and B.) The KE lost during the collision2.Problem above is changed into an elastic collision. A.)How much kinetic energy lost? B.) How fast each mass is moving after colliding? ​


Answer:

1. A) For a perfectly inelastic collision, we can use the conservation of momentum equation:

m1v1 + m2v2 = (m1 + m2)v

where m1 and v1 are the mass and velocity of object A before the collision, m2 and v2 are the mass and velocity of object B before the collision, and v is the velocity of both objects after the collision.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

(4.7 kg)(5.5 m/s) + (0.0038 kg)(9.8 m/s) = (4.7 kg + 0.0038 kg)v

Solving for v, we get:

v = 5.47 m/s

Therefore, the velocity after the impact is 5.47 m/s.

B) To find the KE lost during the collision, we can use the conservation of energy equation:

KE1 + KE2 = KE1' + KE2' + heat

where KE1 and KE2 are the kinetic energies of objects A and B before the collision, KE1' and KE2' are their kinetic energies after the collision, and heat represents any energy lost to non-conservative forces such as friction.

Since this is a perfectly inelastic collision, the objects stick together after the collision and move with the same velocity. Therefore:

KE1' + KE2' = (m1 + m2)(v^2)/2

Substituting the given values, we get:

KE1 + KE2 = (4.7 kg)(5.5 m/s)^2/2 + (0.0038 kg)(9.8 m/s)^2/2

KE1' + KE2' = (4.7 kg + 0.0038 kg)(5.47 m/s)^2/2

Simplifying and subtracting the second equation from the first, we get:

KE lost = KE1 + KE2 - KE1' - KE2'

KE lost = 12.37 J

Therefore, the KE lost during the collision is 12.37 J.

2. A) For an elastic collision, we can use the conservation of momentum and conservation of energy equations:

m1v1 + m2v2 = m1v1' + m2v2'

(1/2)m1v1^2 + (1/2)m2v2^2 = (1/2)m1v1'^2 + (1/2)m2v2'^2

where v1, v2, v1', and v2' represent the velocities of objects A and B before and after the collision.

Substituting the given values, we get:

4.7 kg * 5.5 m/s + 0.0038 kg * 9.8 m/s = 4.7 kg * v1' + 0.0038 kg * v2'

Simplifying and solving for v1' and v2', we get:

v1' = (4.7 kg * 5.5 m/s - 0.0038 kg * 9.8 m/s + 0.0038 kg * 2 * 9.8 m/s) / 4.7 kg

v1' = 4.41 m/s

v2' = (4.7 kg * 5.5 m/s + 0.0038 kg * 9.8 m/s - 4.7 kg * v1') / 0.0038 kg

v2' = -84.21 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of object A after the collision is 4.41 m/s, and the velocity of object B after the collision is -84.21 m/s (indicating that it is moving in the opposite direction).

To find the KE lost during the collision, we can use the same equation as before:

KE lost = KE1 + KE2 - KE1' - KE2'

Substituting the given values and the velocities found above, we get:

KE lost = (1/2)(4.7 kg)(5.5 m/s)^2 + (1/2)(0.0038 kg)(9.8 m/s)^2 - (1/2)(4.7 kg)(4.41 m/s)^2 -(1/2)(0.0038 kg)(-84.21 m/s)^2

Simplifying, we get:

KE lost = 12.37 J

Therefore, the KE lost during the elastic collision is also 12.37 J.

1. For the perfectly inelastic collision:

- Velocity after impact: 5.47 m/s

- KE lost during collision: 12.37 J

2. For the elastic collision:

- KE lost during collision: 12.37 J

- Velocity of object A after collision: 4.41 m/s

- Velocity of object B after collision: -84.21 m/s (moving in opposite direction)
