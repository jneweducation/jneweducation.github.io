5. Tin(Sn) exists in earths crust as SNO2 Calculate the percentage composition by mass of Sn and O in SnO2​


Answer:

Sn = 78.77 %

Sn = 78.77 %O = 21.23 %

Explanation:

Find firat the atomic masses of the elements in the periodic table

Sn = 118.71

O = 16

Then determine how many grams of each element are present in one mole of SnO2

118.71 g (1 mole) of Sn

32.00 g (2 mole x 16.00 gram per mole) of O

Mass of one mole of SnO2 is:

118.71 g + 32.00 g = 150.71 g

Mass percentages of the elements

mass % Sn = 118.71 g / 150.71 g x 100 = 78.77 %

mass % O = 32.00 g / 150.71 g x 100 = 21.23 %

18. In an experiment, 0.500 g of tin reacted with nitric acid to give tin oxide. If the oxide had a mass of 0.635 g, what is the emperical formula of the tin oxide?please show your complete solution. ​


Answer:

empirical formula = SnO2

Explanation:

Calculating the mass of O

mass O = mass oxide - mass Sn

mass O = 0.635 g - 0.500 g

mass O = 0.135 g

Calculating the number of moles of each element

n Sn = 0.500 g × (1 mol / 118.7 g) = 0.00421 mol

n O = 0.135 g × (1 mol / 16.00 g) = 0.00844 mol

Determining the empirical formula

empirical formula = SnxOy

Dividing the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles

x = 0.00421 mol / 0.00421 mol = 1

y = 0.00844 mol / 0.00421 mol = 2

empirical formula = SnO2
