19. If theta = 60 degree and F = 450 N, determine the magnitude of the resultant force and its direction, measured counterclockwise from the positive x axis.


Vector

The answer counterclockwise from the positive x axis is 204.8°.

Vector Resolution:

Any vector that forms an angle θ with an axis in a given x-y coordinate system can be broken down into its component forms as follows:

[tex]A_x=A cos\theta \\and\\A_y=A sin \theta[/tex]

Where,

The vector A's x and y components are designated as Ax and Ay, respectively, while the angle formed with the x axis is designated as.

Solution:

Given:

Angle[tex]\theta=60^o[/tex]Magnitude of the force F =450 N

The force F's x component is represented by:

[tex]F_x=F\cos60^o\\=450\cos60^o\\=225 Nx[/tex]

Moreover, its y component is determined by:

[tex]F_y=F \sin60^o\\=450\sin 60^o\\=389.71 Ny[/tex]

The following equation gives the x component of the 700 N force:

[tex]F700_x=-700\cos15^o\\=-676.14 Nx[/tex]

Moreover, its y component is determined by:

[tex]F700_y=-700\sin15^o\\=181.17 Ny[/tex]

As a result, the force that results is provided by:

[tex]F=F_x+F_y+F700_x+F700_y\\= (225-676.14)x+(389.71-181.17)y\\=451.14x+208.54y[/tex]

Its magnitude is indicated by:

[tex]|F|=\sqrt{(-451.14)^2+(208.54)^2} \\=497 N[/tex]

In a clockwise orientation from the negative x axis, the direction of the resulting force is given by:

[tex]\beta =\tanx^{-1} (\frac{208.54}{451.14})\\=24.80^o[/tex]

And so, starting from the positive x axis, its direction in a clockwise sense is given by:

[tex]\alpha =\beta +180^o\\=24.80^o+180^o\\=204.8^o[/tex]

26. What is the coefficient of kinetic friction of a car with mass 60 kg moving on the road if the frictional force acting on it is 450 N, east?


Answer:

To determine the coefficient of kinetic friction, we first need to know the normal force acting on the car, which is equal in magnitude to the force with which the car is pressing down on the road. Assuming that the car is on a level road surface, the normal force is equal in magnitude to the weight of the car, which is given by:

weight = mass x acceleration due to gravity

= 60 kg x 9.81 m/s^2

= 588.6 N

Since the car is moving on the road, we know that the frictional force acting on it is kinetic friction, which is given by:

frictional force = coefficient of kinetic friction x normal force

We are given that the frictional force is 450 N, so we can solve for the coefficient of kinetic friction as follows:

coefficient of kinetic friction = frictional force / normal force

= 450 N / 588.6 N

= 0.764

Therefore, the coefficient of kinetic friction of the car with mass 60 kg moving on the road is approximately 0.764.

29. A gas at temperature of 95°C occupies a volume of 450 mL. What will be the new volume if it has a temperature of 60°C?


Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{450 mL}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{95°C + 273 = 368 K}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{60°C + 273 = 333 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{450 mL} × \frac{\text{333 K}}{\text{368 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{407 mL}}[/tex]

30. The volume of air in the marshmallow's blaster is 450 mL. The pressure is 1.00 atm. If you decrease the volume to 60 mL, what will the new pressure be?​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{1.00 atm}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{450 mL}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{60 mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = P_{1} × \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{1.00 atm} × \frac{\text{450 mL}}{\text{60 mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{7.50 atm}}[/tex]
