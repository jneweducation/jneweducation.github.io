1. A forklift in a warehouse lifts 1 000n of boxes, 34m from the floor.compute the work done by the forklift


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the work

W = Fd

W = (1,000 N)(34 m)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{34,000 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done by the forklift is 34,000 J.

14. what are the ppe of electrician


Answer:

Electrician's PPE

PPEs are clothing and equipment that are designed to protect a person or minimize the exposure to hazards or harm that may be present in the person's workplace environment.

There are different kinds of PPEs for different kinds of work/profession and electricians have their own type of PPE made for their work. Here are the parts:

Safety glasses - protects eyes from sparksHard hats - protects head from damageSafety shoes - prevents slipping and damage to feetFace shields - protects from sparksInsulating (rubber) gloves with leather protectors - prevents electrocution and gives protection from sharp objectsInsulating sleeves - prevents electrocutionFlame-resistant clothing - prevents cloth and body from being burnt in case of fire

However, their protective equipment are not always the same. They add and change some of them depending on the hazards assessed.

Fall protection equipment like harness maybe used if they need to work in high places.Respirators if there's harmful substance that can be inhaled while working.Chemical resistant gloves and clothing if working with harmful chemicals.

A forklift in a warehouse lifts 1000 N of boxes, 34 M from the floor. Compute the work done by the forklift.

Answer:

Letter A. 34000 J

the work done by the forklift is equal to 34,000 J or 34,000 kg·m²/s²

Note:

the formula of work is ( W = F × d ).

Given Data:

F ( Force ) = 1000 N or 1000 kg·m/s²

d ( distance ) = 34 m

W ( Work ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We cam use the formula of work which is ( W = F × d ).

W = F × d

    = 1000 kg·m/s² × 34 m

    = 34,000 kg·m²/s²

    = 34,000  J
