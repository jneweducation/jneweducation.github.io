16. give two reasons for:the existence of carbon compounds​


How does the existence of carbon compounds​

Because each carbon atom can establish four chemical bonds with other atoms and because carbon atoms are about the perfect size to fit comfortably as members of very massive molecules, carbon is the only element that can generate so many distinct compounds.

1st reason

Every carbon atom possesses a total of six electrons due to its atomic number of 6. The other four are valence electrons or outer electrons that can form bonds with other atoms. Two are a completed inner orbit, while the other two are valence electrons.

2nd reason

The second reason for carbon's remarkable compound-forming abilities is that carbon atoms may connect to one another not just in straight chains, but also in intricate branching's, similar to tree branches. They can even connect "head-to-tail" to form carbon atom rings. There is actually virtually no limit to the number of branches or rings that can be joined to them, and hence no limit to the number of distinct molecules that can be produced.
