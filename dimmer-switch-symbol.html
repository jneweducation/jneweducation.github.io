Part A. Color

1. Plug the incandescent lamp to a light dimmer switch.

2. Darken the room and turn on the lamp.

3. Adjust the dimmer switch slowly until the bulb becomes dim. Observe and note the color of the glowing filament.

4.

5. Adjust the dimmer switch slowly until the bulb becomes brighter and brighter.

6. Observe and note the color of the glowing filament.

Observations:

Dimming the incandescent bulb causes the color of the glowing filament to change. As the bulb becomes dimmer, the color of the filament shifts from a bright white to a warm, orange glow. The warmer color is due to the decreased temperature of the filament. As the bulb becomes brighter, the filament begins to glow white again.

Explanation:

The color of the glowing filament is determined by its temperature. As the bulb is dimmed, less current flows through the filament, causing it to cool down. This decreased temperature causes the filament to emit less energy in the blue end of the spectrum and more in the red end, resulting in the warm, orange glow. When the bulb is brightened again, the filament heats up, and the color of the glowing filament shifts back to white.

Significance:

Understanding how the color of an incandescent bulb changes with dimming can be useful for creating lighting schemes in different environments. For example, a warm, orange glow can create a cozy atmosphere in a living room or bedroom, while a bright white light can be useful for task lighting in a workspace. By adjusting the dimmer switch, the color of the light can be tailored to suit different moods and activities.
