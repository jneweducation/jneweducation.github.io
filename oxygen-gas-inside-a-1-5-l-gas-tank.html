5. A scuba diver’s tank contains 2.50 kg of oxygen gas compressed into a volume of 11.0L. Calculate the gas pressure inside the tank at 10℃


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V = \text{11.0 L} \\ & mass = \text{2.50 kg = 2500 g} \\ & MM = \text{31.998 g/mol} \\ & T = 10^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{283.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of the gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{mass}{MM} \\ & = \frac{\text{2500 g}}{\text{31.998 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{78.13 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Solve for the pressure of the gas by using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P & = \frac{nRT}{V} \\ & = \frac{(\text{78.13 mol})\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{283.15 K})}{\text{11.0 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{165 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the gas pressure inside the tank is 165 atm.

✏️IDEAL GAS EQUATION

[tex]\tt{\huge{\green{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to solve for the pressure of the gas inside the tank. First, we need to identify the quantities to be used. These are

P = pressure

V = volume

n = number of moles

R = universal gas constant = 0.082057 L • atm/K • mol

T = absolute temperature

m = mass

MW = molecular weight

Second, we need to identify the given values. These are

V = 25.0 L

T = 25°C + 273.15 = 298.15 K

m = 11.0 kg = 1.10 × 10⁴ g

Third, we need to find the molecular weight of gas. Since the given gas is butane, the molecular weight is

MW = (12.01 g/mol × 4) + (1.008 g/mol × 10)

MW = 58.12 g/mol

Fourth, we need to find the number of moles of gas. The number of moles of butane gas is

[tex]n = \dfrac{m}{\text{MW}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \dfrac{1.10 \times 10^{4} \: \text{g}}{\text{58.12 g/mol}}[/tex]

n = 189.26 mol

Finally, we can now solve for the pressure of the gas. Therefore, the pressure of the gas is

[tex]P = \dfrac{nRT}{V}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{(\text{189.26 mol})(0.082057 \: \text{L} \: \cdot \: \text{atm/}\text{K} \: \cdot \: \text{mol})(\text{298.15 K})}{\text{25.0 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{185 atm}}[/tex]

10. a 1.) tanks of oxygen gas has a pressure of 621 torr at a 324 L container. What will be its pressure if it was tranferred another tank with a volume of 2 L?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the combined gas law:

P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

where P1, V1, and T1 are the initial pressure, volume, and temperature, respectively, and P2 and V2 are the final pressure and volume, respectively, that we are trying to find.

We are given that:

P1 = 621 torr

V1 = 324 L

V2 = 2 L

T1 = T2 (since the temperature is assumed to remain constant)

Substituting these values into the combined gas law, we get:

621 torr * 324 L / T = P2 * 2 L / T

Simplifying, we get:

P2 = 621 torr * 324 L / 2 L = 100692 torr

Therefore, the pressure of the oxygen gas transferred to the 2 L container would be 100692 torr.

13. Oxygen gas inside a 1.5 L gas tank has a pressure of 0.95 atm. Provided that the temperature remains constant, how much pressure is needed to reduce its volume by 1/2?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use Boyle's Law, which states that the pressure and volume of a gas are inversely proportional when temperature is heldconstant. This means that pressure x volume = constant.

Let's call the initial pressure P1 and the final pressure P2. We also know that the initial volume is 1.5 L and the final volume is 0.75 L (constant. This means that if we decrease the volume of the gas by a factor of 2, we need to increase the pressure by a factor of 2 to keep the product of pressure and volume constant.

Mathematically, we can write this as:

P1V1 = P2V2

where P1 is the initial pressure, V1 is the initial volume, P2 is the final pressure, and V2 is the final volume. We are given P1 = 0.95 atm and V1 = 1.5 L. We want to find P2 when V2 = 0.5 V1 = 0.75 L.

Substituting the values into the equation, we get:

0.95 atm x 1.5 L = P2 x 0.75 L

Simplifying and solving for P2, we get:

P2 = (0.95 atm x 1.5 L) / 0.75 L

P2 = 1.9 atm

Therefore, the pressure needed to reduce the volume of the oxygen gas inside the 1.5 L gas tank by half is 1.9 atm.

26. A gas tank contains 34 L of carbon dioxide gas with a pressure of 4.2 atm at 15°C. If this tank is heated to 41°C, what will be the resulting pressure inside the tank?


Answer:

5.33 atm

Explanation:

To solve this problem, we can use the ideal gas law, which relates the pressure (P), volume (V), number of moles (n), and temperature (T) of a gas:

PV = nRT

where R is the gas constant.

Since we know the initial pressure, volume, and temperature of the gas, we can use the ideal gas law to calculate the number of moles of CO2 in the tank:

n = PV/RT

where P = 4.2 atm, V = 34 L, T = 15°C + 273.15 = 288.15 K, and R = 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K).

n = (4.2 atm)(34 L)/(0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K))(288.15 K) = 4.59 mol

Now we can use the ideal gas law again to calculate the pressure of the gas when the temperature is increased to 41°C:

P2 = nRT2/V

where T2 = 41°C + 273.15 = 314.15 K.

P2 = (4.59 mol)(0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K))(314.15 K)/(34 L) = 5.33 atm

Therefore, the resulting pressure inside the tank when it is heated to 41°C is 5.33 atm

30. a scuba diver needs a diving tank in order to provide breathing gas while he is underwater. how much pressure is needed for 5 L of oxygen gas at 1 atm pressure to be compressed to 2 L aT constant temperature ?


Boyle's LawP1V1 = P2V2

where in, P1 = first pressure

V1 = first volume

P2 = second pressure

V2 = second volume

Given: P1 = 1 atm

V1 = 5 L

V2 = 2 L

Unknown: P2 = ?

Formula: P2 = P1V1 / V2

Note: To get the formula, apply division and cancellation of like terms using the original formula of Boyle's Law.

P1V1 = P2V2 <--- divide both sides by V2

P1V1 / V2 = P2V2 / V2 <--- cancel like terms

P1V1 / V2 = P2 <--- final formula

Note: Easier method to get the formula is by Transposition.

P1V1 = P2V2 <--- transpose V2 to the other side

P1V1 / V2 = P2 <--- final formula

Solution: P2 = P1V1 / V2

= (1atm)(5L) / 2L

= 5atm•L / 2L

= 2.5atm

Note: "L" is cancelled out, so the unit for pressure will remain which is "atm".

Reminder: Cancellation of units is applicable when the other same unit is on the denominator.

Final Answer: P2 = 2.5 atm
