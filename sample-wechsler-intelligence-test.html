26. The first intelligence tests were developed for the purpose of


The English aristocrat Francis Galton made the first attempt to develop an Intelligence Test to reflect the person's intellectual ability. He believed that intelligence is a matter of possessing the right genes. Furthermore, to support his belief, he assumed that people with the superior intelligence would have the superior physical development of brain and body. However, in the course of his study, no evidence was found, thus, leading his intelligence test to fail.

The Binet-Simon Intelligence test was the first successful intelligence test. It was developed by French psychologist Alfred Binet to identify school children who would likely to fall behind their peers in terms of academic achievement courtesy of the request of a French public school.
