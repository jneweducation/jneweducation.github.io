The modern man in the poem "The Man with a Hoe" is the exploited worker who toils endlessly and becomes degraded so that a few people at the top of the social order can become very wealthy.

Exploitation of workers is a phenomenon that is still rife both at the industrial level and in large and small scale companies—which creates not only problems in terms of justice but also humanity.

Exploitation of workers is still a serious problem in the capitalist economic system. The term worker exploitation refers to a form of forcing workers to do work in unfit conditions and giving too little or no pay by their employers.

The purpose of worker exploitation is to gain the maximum profit at the lowest possible cost, which results in the neglect of the rights that workers should receive.

Unpaid or too little wages, excessive working hours and disregard for the health and safety of workers are some examples of worker exploitation.

Unlike the principles of a capitalist economy which pursues low production costs, the principles of industrial relations in Islam actually prioritize two things, namely equality and justice. Equality places employers and workers in an equal position.

The Man with the Hoe Poem

What is the image of the man with the hoe?

A worn-out peasant man toiling in a field. It was very divisive at the time and was meant to draw attention to the working class's situation. The poem expresses an ardent protest and lament on behalf of those who are in servitude, giving voice to the description of what workers become when they are yoked to the wheel of labor and the description of the intellectual and spiritual endowments workers are robbed of through the use of one symbolic laborer.

How does the society treat them?

They are prevented from attaining their aspirations by society's treatment of them as slaves, useless parts of machines, and other insignificant objects. The man with the hoe's situation or state is due to the strong and healthy individuals. He uses terms like taken their souls, snatched the joy out of their life, and rendered them worse than beasts to describe how their lives have been affected.

What are the different social issues which arise in the poem?

The poet brings up the concerns of injustice, scarcity, poverty, and hopelessness that are prevalent among slum kids. He begins by describing the situation of youngsters living in slums. In order for a select group of people at the top of the social hierarchy to amass enormous wealth, exploited workers toil ceaselessly and become degraded.
