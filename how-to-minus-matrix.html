11. How to minus this 009000 345178​


Answer:

To subtract two numbers, you need to align the digits in each place value and then subtract the smaller number from the larger one. If the result of a subtraction is less than zero, you will need to borrow from the next place value to complete the subtraction.

For example, to subtract 345178 from 009000, you can align the digits as follows:

009000

009000-345178

Starting with the units place, you can subtract 8 from 0, which is not possible. To complete the subtraction, you will need to borrow 1 from the tens place. This means that the units place will now be 10 - 8, or 2. The tens place will now be 9 - 1 - 3, or 5. You can then continue the subtraction as follows:

009000

009000-345178

009000-345178-----

009000-345178----- 642822

Therefore, 009000 - 345178 = 642822.

20. how to answer the synthesis matrix?​


Answer:

How to do synthesis matrix?

Synthesis matrix

Each column in the table lists one source. Each row is labelled with a specific concept, topic or theme that recurs across all or most of the sources. Then, for each source, you summarize the main points or arguments related to the theme.

Answer:

As you can see, you can list trends (ideas that are repeated or shared) and gaps (ideas about which sources seem to differ or conflict, or which are simply not addressed) in the left hand column. Then, you can list paraphrases or direct quotations from the sources in the following columns; this will enable you to “see” where these sources overlap or differ. (Be careful to use quotation marks correctly in your matrix; if you are directly quoting from the source, leave yourself those clues that the material should be paraphrased later when you incorporate the ideas into your Literature Review.)

It is important to remember that a trend does not exist unless two or more sources support it. Also, a gap cannot exist unless two or more sources disagree, report conflicting data, or fail to address a particular idea. For this reason, each body paragraph needs to represent one trend or gap in your research, and contain evidence from two or more sources to demonstrate that this trend or gap exists.

25. How to make a matrix puzzle with negative in math


Here's an example of a matrix puzzle that involves negative numbers:

1. Create a 3x3 matrix with random numbers, including negative numbers. For example:

[-2, 5, 3]

[1, -4, 7]

[-6, 2, -1]

2. Write a target sum, which can be any number you choose. For this example, let's use a target sum of -9.

3. The goal is to add or subtract the numbers in the matrix to reach the target sum. The rules are as follows:

- You must use every number in the matrix exactly once.

- You can only add or subtract the numbers (multiplication and division are not allowed).

- You can group numbers together with parentheses to change the order of operations.

4. Solve the puzzle by rearranging the numbers and grouping them differently until you reach the target sum of -9. Here's one possible solution:

(-2 + 5 - 3) + (1 - 4 - 7) + (-6 + 2 - 1) = -9

Note that there may be multiple solutions to the puzzle, depending on the matrix and target sum you choose.
