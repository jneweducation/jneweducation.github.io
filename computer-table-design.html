19. A W18 × 119 is used as a compression member with one end fixed and the other end fixed against rotation but free to translate. The length is 12 feet. If A992 steel is used, what is the available compressive strength?a. Use AISC Equation E3-2 or E3-3. Compute both the design strength for LRFD andthe allowable strength for ASD.b. Use Table 4-22 from Part 4 of the Manual. Compute both the design strength forLRFD and the allowable strength for ASD


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

FOMULAS:

Nominal compressive strength

[tex]P_n=F_{cr}A_g[/tex]

Euler's bucking stress

[tex]F_e=\dfrac{\pi^2E}{\left(\dfrac{KL}{r}\right)^2}[/tex]

AISC E3-2

[tex]\dfrac{KL}{r}\leq4.71\sqrt{\dfrac{E}{F_y}[/tex]

[tex]F_{cr}=\left(0.658^{F_y/F_e\right)F_y}[/tex]

AISC E3-3

[tex]\dfrac{KL}{r} > 4.71\sqrt{\dfrac{E}{F_y}[/tex]

[tex]F_{cr}=0.877F_e[/tex]

Relevant properties for W18 x 119

[tex]A=35.1[/tex] in²

[tex]r_y=2.69[/tex] in

SOLUTION:

K = 1.2 (recommended value of K from Table C-A-7.1)

Fy = 50 ksi (A992 steel)

L = 12 ft = 144 in

[tex]\dfrac{KL}{r}=\dfrac{1.2(144)}{2.69}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{KL}{r}=64.24[/tex]

[tex]4.71\sqrt{\dfrac{E}{F_y}}=4.71\sqrt{\dfrac{29,000}{50}}[/tex]

[tex]4.71\sqrt{\dfrac{E}{F_y}}=113.43[/tex]

[tex]64.29 < 113.43[/tex]

[tex]\therefore F_{cr}=\left(0.658^{F_y/F_e}\right)F_y}[/tex]

[tex]F_e=\dfrac{\pi^2(29,000)}{(64.24)^2}[/tex]

[tex]F_e=69.36[/tex] ksi

[tex]F_{cr}=\left(0.658^{69.36/50})(50)[/tex]

[tex]F_{cr}=28[/tex] ksi

[tex]P_n=28(35.1)[/tex]

[tex]P_n=982.8[/tex] kips

LRFD design strength

[tex]\phi P_n=0.9(982.8)[/tex]

[tex]\phi P_n=884.52[/tex] kips
