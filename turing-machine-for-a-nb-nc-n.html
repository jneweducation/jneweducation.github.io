19. 3. Which triangles are congruent if AN = LY, NC = YF, CA = FL, ∠A ≅ ∠L, ∠C ≅ ∠F, and ∠N ≅ ∠Y?​


Answer:

All 6 corresponding parts are congruent; but we need 3 congruent correpsonding parts which satisfy postulates or a theorem to prove triangle congruence.

Step-by-step explanation:

The definition of congruence for two triangle can be defined as follows:

Two triangles are congruent if their corresponding sides are congruent, and their corresponding angles are also congruent.

If ABC is congruent to triangle XYZ, the congruence can be stated as:

ΔABC ≅ ΔXYZ

By definition, if ΔABC≅ ΔXYZ, then the corresponding sides are congruent, and also the corresponding angles, this means

AB ≅ XY

BC ≅ YZ

AC ≅ XZ

∠A ≅ ∠X

∠B ≅ ∠Y

∠C ≅ ∠Z

You need 6 congruence statements to have 2 congruent triangles.

However, in most cases, it is enough to establish congruence of three of the corresponding parts. Once these are met, then we can say that the two triangles are congruent.

SAS (Side-Angle-Side): If two pairs of sides of two triangles are congruent, and their included angle is also congruent, then the triangles are congruent.

The included angles here mean that the angle inside those two sides.

In our example, we only need AB ≅ XY, BC ≅ YZ, and ∠B≅∠Y to prove ΔABC≅ΔXYZ.

SSS (Side-Side-Side): If three pairs of sides of two triangles are congruent, then the triangles are congruent.

In our example, we need all sides of the triangle to be congruent. AB ≅ XY, BC ≅ YZ, and AC ≅ XZ to prove ΔABC≅ΔXYZ.

ASA (Angle-Side-Angle): If two pairs of angles of two triangles are congruent, and the included sides are also congruent, then the triangles are congruent.

In our example, we need ∠A ≅∠X, ∠B ≅ ∠Y, and the included side, AB ≅ XY to prove ΔABC≅ΔXYZ.

SAA (Side-Angle-Angle): If two pairs of angles of two triangles are congruent, and the non-included sides are also congruent, then the triangles are congruent. This is similar to ASA and using the fact that the other angle should be equal to each other since the sum of the angles of any triangle is 180.

In our example, we need ∠A ≅ ∠X, ∠B≅∠Y, and any non-included side, say, AC≅XZ to prove ΔABC≅ΔXYZ.
