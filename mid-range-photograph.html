6. Why are the highest mountain ranges located in the mid-continental belt?


#BrainlyFast

The highest mountain ranges are found in the mid-continental belt like the Himalayas mountain, because they are produce between two continental plates colliding with each other making them in between two land masses or in the middle of the continental belt. They are formed between continental to continental plate convergence.

Types of convergent boundaries:

Oceanic to oceanic plate convergence Continental to continental plate convergence Oceanic continental convergence

Convergent boundary produces different types of geological features. The most common geological feature in convergent boundary are volcanoes or volcanic island, mountain ranges, Earthquake, and trenches.

Himalayas mountain ranges are formed between the two continental plates known as the Indian plate and the Eurasian plate.
