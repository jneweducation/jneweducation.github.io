24. the electrical resistance R of a wire varies directly as tge distance d it travels and inversely as the square of its dinameter​


Answer:

This statement can be expressed mathematically as:

R ∝ d

R ∝ 1/d^2

where R is the electrical resistance of the wire, d is the distance the wire travels, and ∝ represents "varies directly as" or "is proportional to."

This means that if the distance the wire travels (d) increases, the electrical resistance (R) of the wire will also increase. Similarly, if the diameter (d) of the wire decreases, the electrical resistance (R) will also increase.

The relationship between the electrical resistance of a wire and its distance and diameter can be expressed in a proportionality constant:

R = kd * 1/d^2

where k is the proportionality constant.

This equation can be used to predict the electrical resistance of a wire given its distance and diameter, or to determine the distance or diameter of a wire given its electrical resistance.

26. the electrical resistance of a copper wire varies directly on its length and inversely on the square of its radius. if the copper wire 500m long and radius 0.2cmhas a resistance of 30ohms, calculate the resistance of the same type of copper wire 750m long and of radius 0.25cm


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the equation R = kL/[tex]r^{2}[/tex], where R is the resistance, L is the length of the wire, r is the radius of the wire, and k is a constant that depends on the material of the wire and the temperature. In this case, we are given that the resistance of the 500m long, 0.2cm radius wire is 30 ohms, so we can set up the equation as follows:

30 = k(500)/(0.2)²

Solving for k, we find that k = 0.32.

Now we can use this value of k to calculate the resistance of the 750m long, 0.25cm radius wire using the equation R = kL/[tex]r^{2}[/tex]:

R = 0.32(750)/(0.25)²

R = 32 ohms

Therefore, the resistance of the 750m long, 0.25cm radius wire is 32 ohms.
