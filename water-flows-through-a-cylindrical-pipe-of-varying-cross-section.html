1. Water flows through a pipe with a cross-sectional area of 10cmsquared at 3m/s what is the flow speed in the pipe if the cross-sectional area is reduced to 5cmsquarrd


Answer: 6 m/s

Explanation:

     Let Q₁ = volumetric flow of the larger pipe

           Q₂ = volumetric flow of the smaller pipe

Using the Continuity Equation:

                   Q = AV, in which A = cross-sectional area

                                                V = flow speed

Then,

         Q₁ = Q₂

      A₁V₁ = A₂V₂ ; where A₁ = 10 cm² =  0.001 m²

                                        V₁ = 3 m/s

                                        A₂ = (10 cm² - 5 cm²) = 5 cm₂ = 0.0005 m²

                                        V₂ = ?

Finding V₂ through substitution:

                         A₁V₁ = A₂V₂

    (0.001 m²)(3 m/s) = (0.0005 m²) V₂

             0.003 m³/s = (0.0005 m²) V₂

             0.0005 m²      0.0005 m²

                            V₂ = 6 m/s

Correct option is
C
v/2
Answer is C.

An increase or decrease in flow rate will result in a corresponding increase or decrease in velocity. Smaller pipe causes a greater proportion of the liquid to be in contact with the pipe, which creates friction. Pipe size also affects velocity. Given a constant flow rate, decreasing pipe size increases the velocity, which increases friction. The friction losses are cumulative as the fluid travels through the length of pipe. The greater the distance, the greater the friction losses will be.
As the average velocity increases, pressure losses increase. Velocity is directly related to flow rate.
From the equation of continuity, av=constant.
In this case, the second pipe is of double the area of cross section.
Hence, if the area of cross section is doubled, the speed of the liquid becomes half. That is, v/2.

Given that the rate of flow is 1/3 when the radius is 30, we can write:

1/3 = k * (30^2)

Solving for k, we get:

k = 1/3 / (30^2)

That is, if you made a 10% change in each of the quantities below, which would cause the greatest change in the flow rate? A- the fluid viscosity B- the pressure difference C- the length of the pipe D- the radius of the pipe Q2- Water flows through a pipe. The diameter of the pipe at point B is larger than at point A. Where is the water pressure greatest? A- point A B- point B C- same at both A and B D- cannot be determined from the information given


Answer:

A1- The radius of the pipe has the greatest effect on the flow of fluid through a narrow pipe. The flow rate is directly proportional to the fourth power of the radius of the pipe, which means that a small change in the radius of the pipe can result in a large change in the flow rate. The effect of viscosity, pressure difference, and length of the pipe are relatively smaller compared to the radius of the pipe.

A2- The water pressure is greatest at point A, where the diameter of the pipe is smaller. According to Bernoulli's principle, the pressure in a fluid decreases as the fluid velocity increases. Since the diameter of the pipe is larger at point B, the water velocity at point B is higher than at point A, resulting in a lower pressure at point B. Therefore, the water pressure is greater at point A.

21. A certain pipe lay horizontally on the ground with water flowing at it. The cross sectional area at points 1 and 2 are 0.0008 m2 and 0.0002 m2 respectively. The speed of the water at the wider portion is 4 m/s. a. What is the speed at the narrow portion? b. What is the volume flow rate?


Answer:

1. 16 m/s

2. 0.0032 m³/s

Explanation:

FORMULAS:

Continuity Equation:

A₁v₁ = A₂v₂

Volume Flow rate:

Q = Av

where:

A = cross sectional area

v = velocity/speed

Q = Volume flow rate

SOLUTION:

1.

Let:

A₁ = cross sectional area of the wider portion

v₁ = velocity of the wider portion

A₂ = cross sectional area of the narrow portion

v₂ = velocity of the narrow portion

Given:

A₁ = 0.0008 m²

A₂ = 0.0002 m²

v₁ = 4 m/s

Unknown:

v₂ = ?

Using the continuity equation

A₁v₁ = A₂v₂

(0.0008)(4) = (0.0002)v₂

v₂ = (0.0008)(4)/(0.0002)

v₂ = 16 m/s (ANSWER)

2.

Consider the wider portion of the pipe (The answer will be the same if you used the narrow portion)

Given:

A = 0.0008 m²

v = 4 m/s

Unknown:

Q = ?

Using the formula for the volume flow rate

Q = Av

Q = (0.0008)(4)

Q = 0.0032 m³/s (ANSWER)
