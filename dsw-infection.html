  • 15. Editorial: Waiting for SAP (Excerpt) Who is not in need now? The Social Amelioration Program, much anticipated by citizens struggling under enhanced community quarantine (ECO), faces a challenging implementation. As reported in SunStar Cebu on Apr. 11, Cebu City barangays will start this week the profiling of households to determine the SAP recipients. The Bayanihan to Heal as One Act or Republic Act 11469 targets 18 million vulnerable households, which include “4Ps beneficiaries, informal economy workers (directly hired or occasional workers), subcontracted workers, home workers, house helpers, public transportation drivers (pedicab, tricycle, PUVs, TNVS), micro-entrepreneurs and producers, farnily enterprise owners, sub-minimum wage owners, owners, farmers, employees affected by the no-work, no-pay policy, stranded workers, other household members belonging to the vulnerable sectors (senior citizens, PWDs, pregnant and lactating women, solo parents, distressed OFWs, indigent IPs and homeless individuals)". However, based on the guidelines of the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), if there is one member of the household whose work was not disrupted, any vulnerable member, such as an elderly person, will be disqualified from availing of the SAP benefit. According to the same SunStar Cebu report, the household profiling begins when the barangay chiefs receive the Social Amelioration Card (SAC) forms from DSWD. The household profiles will be submitted to the Department of Social Welfare Services (DSWS) of the LGU for verification and then to the DSWD for finalization. Can this process be expedited? Will the SAP benefit be received during the ECO when it is most needed? It is clear that, for now, Filipinos will have to rely on fellow Filipinos to meet urgent needs of the present.
