11. The+density+of+a+solution+that+is+20.0%+hclo4+is+1.138+g/ml.+calculate+the+molarity+of+the+solution.


Answer:

To calculate the molarity of a solution, you need to know the number of moles of solute and the volume of the solvent.

In this case, the solute is HClO4 and the solvent is the solution, which has a density of 1.138 g/mL.

To find the number of moles of HClO4, you can use the mass percent formula:

Mass of solute (HClO4) = mass percent of solute x mass of solution / 100%

Plugging in the values given in the question, we get:

Mass of HClO4 = 20.0% x 1.138 g / 100% = 0.2276 g

The atomic weight of HClO4 is 97.45 g/mol, so the number of moles of HClO4 is:

Number of moles of HClO4 = 0.2276 g / 97.45 g/mol = 0.002330 moles

To find the volume of the solvent (the solution), you can use the density formula:

Volume = mass / density

Plugging in the values given in the question, we get:

Volume of solution = 1.138 g / 1.138 g/mL = 1 mL

Since the volume of the solvent is in milliliters (mL), we need to convert it to liters (L) by dividing it by 1000:

Volume of solution in liters = 1 mL / 1000 = 0.001 L

Now that we have the number of moles of solute and the volume of solvent, we can use the molarity formula to calculate the molarity of the solution:

Molarity = number of moles of solute / volume of solvent (in liters)

Plugging in the values we calculated above, we get:

Molarity = 0.002330 moles / 0.001 L = 2.33 M

So, the molarity of the solution is 2.33 M.

Chemical Reactions

Involves rearrangement of the molecular or ionic structure of a substance. Process in which one or more reactants are converted to one or more different products. Can be classified into five basic types. Analyzing reactants and products will allow placing it into one of these categories. Some reactions will fit into more than one category.

Types of Chemical Reactions:combination/synthesisdecompositionsingle replacement/single displacementdouble replacement/double displacementcombustion

A combination reaction, also known as a synthesis reaction, is a reaction in which two or more substances combine to form a single new substance. Combination reactions can also be called synthesis reactions. One combination reaction is two elements combining to form a compound.

A decomposition reaction is a reaction in which a compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances. Most decomposition reactions require an input of energy in the form of heat, light, or electricity.

A single replacement reaction is a reaction in which one element replaces a similar element in a compound. A metal must be replaced by another metal while a non - metal must be replaced also by a non - metal.

A double replacement reaction is a reaction in which the positive and negative ions of two ionic compounds exchange places to form two new compounds. Double-replacement reactions generally occur between substances in aqueous solution. In order for a reaction to occur, one of the products is usually a solid precipitate, a gas, or a molecular compound such as water.

A combustion reaction is a reaction in which a substance reacts with oxygen gas, releasing energy in the form of light and heat. Combustion reactions must involve  O₂  as one reactant.

✏️HYDROXIDE ION CONCENTRATION

Note that the hydogen ion concentration ([H]⁺)of a strong monoprotic acid is equal to the initial concentration of the acid.

Keep in mind that the value of the ionization constant of water (Kw) is 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{a. \: SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{OH}^-] & = \frac{K_{\text{w}}}{[\text{H}^+]} \\ & = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-14}}{7.6 \times 10^{-5}} \\ & = \boxed{1.32 \times 10^{-10} \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{b. \: SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{OH}^-] & = \frac{K_{\text{w}}}{[\text{H}^+]} \\ & = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-14}}{0.80} \\ & = \boxed{1.25 \times 10^{-14} \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{c. \: SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{OH}^-] & = \frac{K_{\text{w}}}{[\text{H}^+]} \\ & = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-14}}{4.7 \times 10^{-9}} \\ & = \boxed{2.13 \times 10^{-6} \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{d. \: SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{OH}^-] & = \frac{K_{\text{w}}}{[\text{H}^+]} \\ & = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-14}}{1.1} \\ & = \boxed{9.1 \times 10^{-15} \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

BALANCED CHEMICAL EQUATIONS

- In Chemistry, a balanced equation for a chemical reaction is an equation in which the number of atoms for each element or compound in the chemical reaction including the total charge is the same for both the reactants and the products. In other words, the mass and the charge are balanced on both sides of the reaction. The law of conservation of mass in balancing a chemical equation states that no atoms are lost or generated in any chemical reactions. Instead, the atoms join together in different reactions to produce products . This is why, in a balanced symbol equation, the total number of atoms of each element is the same or equal on both sides of the equation.

30. What volume of 0.250 M KOH solution contains 6.31 grams of KOH


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute (KOH)

The molar mass of KOH is 56.11 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{6.31 g}}{\text{56.11 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.1125 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solution.

1 molar (M) = 1 mole per liter (mol/L)

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of solution} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{molarity}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.1125 mol}}{0.250 \: M} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.1125 mol}}{\text{0.250 mol/L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.450 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of 0.250 M KOH solution is 0.450 L.
