Chemical Reactions

Involves rearrangement of the molecular or ionic structure of a substance. Process in which one or more reactants are converted to one or more different products. Can be classified into five basic types. Analyzing reactants and products will allow placing it into one of these categories. Some reactions will fit into more than one category.

Types of Chemical Reactions:combination/synthesisdecompositionsingle replacement/single displacementdouble replacement/double displacementcombustion

A combination reaction, also known as a synthesis reaction, is a reaction in which two or more substances combine to form a single new substance. Combination reactions can also be called synthesis reactions. One combination reaction is two elements combining to form a compound.

A decomposition reaction is a reaction in which a compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances. Most decomposition reactions require an input of energy in the form of heat, light, or electricity.

A single replacement reaction is a reaction in which one element replaces a similar element in a compound. A metal must be replaced by another metal while a non - metal must be replaced also by a non - metal.

A double replacement reaction is a reaction in which the positive and negative ions of two ionic compounds exchange places to form two new compounds. Double-replacement reactions generally occur between substances in aqueous solution. In order for a reaction to occur, one of the products is usually a solid precipitate, a gas, or a molecular compound such as water.

A combustion reaction is a reaction in which a substance reacts with oxygen gas, releasing energy in the form of light and heat. Combustion reactions must involve  O₂  as one reactant.

Further explanation

There are chemical reactions that may occur.

1. A single replacement reaction is a chemical reaction in which one element replaces the other elements of a compound to produce new elements and compounds

Not all of these reactions can occur. We can use the activity series, which is a list of elements that can replace other elements below / to the right of them in a single replacement reaction.

This series is better known as the Volta series, where the metal element with a more negative electrode potential is on the left, while the element with a more positive electrode potential on the right.

The more left the position of a metal in the series, the more reactive metal (easy to release electrons, the stronger the reductor)

Generally, the Volta series used is

Li K Ba Sr Ca Na Mg Al Mn Zn Cr Fe Cd Co Ni Sn Pb H Sb Bi Cu Hg Ag Pt Au

Example:

2Al (s) + 3Zn (NO₃) ₂ (aq) → 2Al (NO₃) ₃ (aq) + 3Zn (s)

The existing aluminum element can replace the zinc element, which is on the right side so the reaction will occur.

2. Double-Replacement reactions. Happens if there is an ion exchange between two ion compounds in the reactant to form two new ion compounds in the product

To predict whether this reaction can occur or not is one of them, the precipitation reaction. A precipitation reaction occurs if two ionic compounds which are dissolved reacted to produce one of the products of the ion compound does not dissolve. Formation of these precipitating compounds that cause reactions can occur

A combination of two or more elements or compounds to form a single compound

4. Combustion reaction: The reaction of a substance with oxygen

Perfect combustion of carbon compounds will get CO2 gas, whereas if it is not perfect it will produce CO gas

5. Acid-base reaction: the reaction between acid and base to produce salt

Explanation:

to balance an equation you need to know the following terms:

Coefficient which is a number that is written before the formula or the symbol of an element in an equation.

Subscript which is a number that is written slightly below a symbol of element.

in balancing equations you need to follow the following steps

1. write the unbalanced equation

2. list all elements present in the equation

3. identify and list the number of atoms of each element in both reactant side and the products side

4. write an appropriate coefficient that will balance the number of atoms of each element in the equation, this can be done looking at the subscript and giving a correct number that when multiplied by the subscript will balance the number of atoms of the element in both reactants and product.

Note: you cannot change the subscript because it will change the chemical compound in the equation.
