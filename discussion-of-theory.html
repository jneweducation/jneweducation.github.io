Answer:

Anomie theories (sometimes also called strain theories) deal with the question of why norm breaks occur more clearly in certain societies or historical epochs than in others. The focus is on the link between crime and the social structure of society.

In criminology, the idea of anomie is that the person chooses criminal activity because the individual believes that there is no reason not to. In other words, the person is alienated, feels worthless and that their efforts to try and achieve anything else are fruitless.

25. Are there any alternative theory aside from the bigbang theory that states the theory itself or discuss the reason of the theory?


Answer:                                                                                                                                              Yes, there are several alternative theories to the Big Bang which have been proposed over the years. The Steady State Theory suggests that the universe was in a constant state of expansion (with no beginning or end), while the Plasma Cosmology Theory suggests that the universe was created and is still expanding due to the electric and magnetic forces of the plasma that fills the universe. The Inflationary Universe Theory suggests that the universe underwent a period of rapid expansion shortly after it was created, while the Cyclic Model Theory suggests that the universe goes through a cycle of expansion, contraction, and re-expansion over time. Each of these theories has its own merits and drawbacks, and none of them have been conclusively proven. However, all of them offer interesting perspectives on the origin and evolution of the universe.
