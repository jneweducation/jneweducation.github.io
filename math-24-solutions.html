The formula for the surface area of a cylinder is:

SA = 2πr^2 + 2πrh

where r is the radius of the circular base, h is the height of the cylinder, and π is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 3.14.

To use this formula, we need to first find the radius of the cylinder. We know that the diameter is 40 centimeters, so the radius is half of that, or 20 centimeters.

Now we can plug in our values and solve for the surface area:

SA = 2π(20)^2 + 2π(20)(10)

SA = 2π(400) + 2π(200)

SA = 800π + 400π

SA = 1200π

Using a calculator, we can approximate the value of π as 3.14 and simplify the answer:

SA ≈ 1200(3.14)

SA ≈ 3768 square centimeters

Therefore, the surface area of the cylinder is approximately 3768 square centimeters.
