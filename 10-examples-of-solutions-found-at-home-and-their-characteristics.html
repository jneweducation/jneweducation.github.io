Detergent

It is a mixture of surfactants with cleaning properties in dilute solutions. Alkylbenzenesulfonates are commonly found in detergents. These compounds are similar to soap but are more soluble in hard water. That is because the polar sulfonate content of detergents is less likely than the polar carboxylate of soap to bind to calcium and other ions found in hard water.

Dish Washing Soap

These are manufactured variously as cartridges, gel, liquids, packs, powder, and tablets. Dish washing soaps usually contains bleach, enzymes, or rinsing aids. However, they are may be homemade using ingredients such as borax, essential oil, eucalyptus oil and grated bar soap, etc.

Shampoo

Shampoo is generally made up by combining surfactant, sodium lauryl sulfate and a cocamidopropyl betaine in water.

Chlorine-based Bleach

Chlorine is the basic solution for the most common bleaches: the combination of sodium hypochlorite and calcium hypochlorite. Oxidizing bleaching agents that do not contain chlorine are usually based on peroxides (hydrogen peroxide, sodium percarbonate and sodium perborate). Most bleaches are oxidizing agents, some are reducing agents such as sodium dithionite and sodium borohydride.

Baking Soda

Baking soda or sodium bicarbonate usually used in cooking, for cleaning, or in toothpaste. It is a combination of salt properties of sodium ions and bicarbonate ions. This white solid is crystalline but it appears as a fine powder. It is something salty, alkaline taste resembling that of washing soda (sodium carbonate).

Coffee

Coffee is a brewed drink prepared from roasted and ground coffee beans. More than 1,000 chemical compounds are found in coffee: Hdrocarbons, Alkaloids, Alcohols, acids and anhydrides, Nitrogenous compounds and amines, Sulfurs, Carbohydrates, Proteins, Lipids, Phenolic compounds, Oils, etc.

Milk

Milk is a pale liquid produced by the mammals' mammary gland. It is the primary source for infants as nutrition, including humans. A single glass of milk can contain painkillers, antibiotics and growth hormones.

Honey

It's natural sweetness from the monosaccharides fructose and glucose has about the same relative sweetness as granulated sugar. It is a sugary food substance produced and stored by certain bees called social hymenopteran insects. It is from nectar of floral or aphid honeydew, through regurgitation, enzymatic activity, and water evaporation.The variety of honey produced by honey bees (the genus Apis) is the most well-known.

Cooking Oil

This oil is a Saturated fat, Monounsaturated fat Polyunsaturated fat, or Trans fat. It comes from plant, animal, or synthetic fat used in frying, baking, and other types of cooking. It is usually used in food preparation and flavouring. Some oils contain saturated fat, such as coconut oil, palm oil and palm kernel oil are solid. The sources of ccoking oil are olive oil, palm oil, soybean oil, canola oil (rapeseed oil), corn oil, peanut oil and other vegetable oils, as well as animal-based oils like butter and lard.

Toothpaste

Toothpaste has three main ones being abrasives, fluoride, and detergents.
