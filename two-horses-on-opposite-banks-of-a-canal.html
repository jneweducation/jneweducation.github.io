2. Two persons on opposite side banks of a canal pull a vessel moving parallel to the banks by means of two horizontal ropes. The tensions in the ropes are 2000N and 2400 N while the angle between them is 70 deg. Determine the resultant pull on the vessel and the angle between each of the ropes and the sides of the canal.​


Answer:

a. The resultant pull is 3611.55 N

b. The angle between each rope is -86.36°

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the resultant tension or pulling force on a vessel.

To analyze the problem, I have provided a free body diagram below.

For the formula, we will be using:

[tex]F=\sqrt{(\Sigma Fx)^2+(\Sigma Fy)^2 }[/tex]

[tex]\theta =tan^{-1}(\frac{\Sigma Fy}{\Sigma Fx} )[/tex]

For the given information

F1 = 2000 N

F2 = 2400 N

θ = 70°

Solving the problem

1. First, let us solve for the horizontal components of the given tension forces, we have:

F1x = 2000 cos 55 = 1147.15 N

F2x = - 2400 cos 55 = - 1376.58 N (left side - negative)

Then, we get the summation of them, we have:

∑Fx = F1x - F2x

∑Fx = 1147.15 N - 1376.58 N = -229.43 N

2. Now, let us solve for the vertical component of each tension forces, we have:

F1y = 2000 sin 55 = 1638.30 N

F2y = 2400 sin 55 = 1965.96 N

Then, let us also solve for the summation, we have:

∑Fy = F1y + F2y

∑Fy = 1638.30 N + 1965.96 N = 3604.26 N

3. Now, let us solve for the resultant force using the formula above, we have:

[tex]F=\sqrt{(\Sigma Fx)^2+(\Sigma Fy)^2 }[/tex]

[tex]F=\sqrt{(-229.43 )^2+(3604.26 )^2 }[/tex]

F = 3611.55 N

4. Then let us solve for the angle between the resultant forces, using the formula above, we have:

[tex]\theta =tan^{-1}(\frac{\Sigma Fy}{\Sigma Fx} )[/tex]

[tex]\theta =tan^{-1}(\frac{3604.26 }{-229.43 } )[/tex]

θ = - 86.36°

GRAND CANAL OF VENICE

The Grand Canal is a passage or channel in the city of Venice, Italy. It forms one of the major water-traffic corridors in the entirety of the city.

Facts about Grand Canal

1.) The water in the Venice Grand Canal channel is 16ft deep and known by locals as the Canalazzo, it has over 170 buildings that was dated back almost 13th centuries ago and built along its two-and-a-half mile length, many of them are grand palazzos.

The majority of the structures were built in the middle of 13th and 18th century and most are open directly onto the canal, making their fronts accessible only by boat.

2.) Its most famous bridge is mentioned by the well known writer Shakespeare

Rialto is the known as the oldest of the four bridges across the Grand Canal channel, which divides the city in two. Completed in its current incarnation in 1591, the Rialto is one of Venice’s top attractions and it’s even mentioned by Shakespeare in The Merchant of Venice, when Shylock asks: “What news on the Rialto?”
