14. what is the setting story of Daedalus and Icarus


The setting of the story of Daedalus and Icarus is Crete. Crete is said to be the largest island in Greece, and it is the home of many stories in Greek Mythology. One of these stories is the Fall of Icarus where Daedalus and Icarus play the main roles. There are also other stories in Greek Mythology that happened in Crete. Here are some of them:

It was said that Zeus was born in CreteTheseus and the MinotaurTalos, the mythical guardian of Crete

Who is Daedalus?Daedalus is known for his inventions and creations. He is a renowned and skillful inventor, craftsman, and architect. Before coming to Crete, his hometown was really Athens. And there he had a nephew and apprentice named Talus. Talus showed exceptional skills as a craftsman. He was also creative and optimistic with his ideas, which enabled him to create things beyond the imagination. Although proud of his nephew at the start, Daedalus grew envious and jealous of him. Thus, he killed Talus by pushing him from the Acropolis. Because of this deed, he was banished from Athens. He went to Crete and was welcomed by King Minos with open arms because of his talents. He grew close to the royal household and even created toys for King Minos' and Queen Pasiphae's children. However, Daedalus also had a hand to the unnatural affair of the queen and the white bull. The queen had asked him to create a way for her to seduce the creature and make love to it. Unable to refuse, Daedalus created a mechanical bull where the queen can get inside. This was successful and in return, the queen gave birth to a Minotaur, a half-man half-bull creature.Because of his hand in the affair, King Minos had ordered him to create the labyrinth. Soon after the completion of the labyrinth, Daedalus and his son, Icarus were imprisoned in the labyrinth.
