What mass is C6H5NO2 was produced if 15.0g of C6 H6 was used to react with excess HNO3?2. What is the theoretical yield?3. What is the actual yield​


1. To find the mass of C6H5NO2 produced, we need to first calculate the amount of C6H6 that reacted:

Molar mass of C6H6 = 78.11 g/mol Number of moles of C6H6 = 15.0 g / 78.11 g/mol = 0.192 moles

Since the reaction has a 70% yield, we can calculate the amount of C6H5NO2 produced:

Number of moles of C6H5NO2 = 0.192 moles x 0.70 = 0.134 moles Molar mass of C6H5NO2 = 123.11 g/mol Mass of C6H5NO2 produced = 0.134 moles x 123.11 g/mol = 16.0 g

Therefore, 16.0 g of C6H5NO2 was produced.

2. The theoretical yield is the maximum amount of product that could be produced based on the amount of limiting reagent used in the reaction. In this case, the limiting reagent is C6H6, since it is used up in the reaction.

To calculate the theoretical yield of C6H5NO2, we need to first calculate the number of moles of C6H6 used:

Number of moles of C6H6 = 15.0 g / 78.11 g/mol = 0.192 moles

Since the balanced equation shows that 1 mole of C6H6 produces 1 mole of C6H5NO2, the theoretical yield of C6H5NO2 is also 0.192 moles.

To convert this to grams, we can use the molar mass of C6H5NO2:

Theoretical yield of C6H5NO2 = 0.192 moles x 123.11 g/mol = 23.6 g

Therefore, the theoretical yield of C6H5NO2 is 23.6 g.

3. The actual yield is the amount of product that was actually obtained in the experiment. In this case, the actual yield was 16.0 g (as calculated in part 1).

Therefore, the actual yield of C6H5NO2 is 16.0 g.
