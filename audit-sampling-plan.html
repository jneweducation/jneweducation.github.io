27. Which of the following procedures is an auditor least likely to perform in planning a financial statement audit?


The procedures such as communication with the previous auditor, studying about the client's internal control and performing analytical procedures are considered important for an auditor who performs an audit for a new client. The confirmation of cash balances is, however, not important when compared to the other procedures.

The formal audit procedures as stated in the auditing standards do not require the confirmation of cash balances. The cash balances are also considered immaterial unless the core business of the client is dealing with regular cash inflows and outflows such as retail business. Hence, the audit of cash balances is a negligible procedure during the audit engagement for most of the auditors who are performing the audit of a new client.
