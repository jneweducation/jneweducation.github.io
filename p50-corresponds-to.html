2. Jaime has a total of P4800 consisting of P50 and P100 bills .the number of P50 bills is 16 less than twice the number of P50 bills. how many P50 Bills does he haveWith solution please..​


Let x be the number of P100 bills that Jaime has.
Then, the number of P50 bills that Jaime has can be represented as (2x - 16).
Since Jaime has a total of P4800, the amount of money represented by the P100 bills is 100x, and the amount represented by the P50 bills is 50(2x - 16) = 100x - 800.
The total amount of money can be represented as:
100x + (100x - 800) = 4800
Simplifying this equation gives:
200x = 5600
x = 28
Therefore, Jaime has 28 P100 bills and (2x - 16) = 40 P50 bills.

Answer:

18pcs of 50 bills:

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

[tex]x[/tex] = number of 50 bills

[tex]y[/tex] = number of 20 bills

Equation:

1st equation --> [tex]x+y=48[/tex]

2nd equation --> [tex]50x+20y=1500[/tex]

Solve by substitution:

1st equation:

[tex]x+y=48\\y=48-x[/tex]

2nd equation substitute the value of y using the first equation

[tex]50x+20(48-x)=1500[/tex]

[tex]50x+960-20x=1500[/tex]

[tex]30x=540[/tex]

[tex]x=18[/tex] 50 bills

[tex]y = 48 - 18\\y = 30[/tex] 20 bills

Checking:

[tex]50(18)+20(30)=1500\\900+600=1500\\1500=1500[/tex]
