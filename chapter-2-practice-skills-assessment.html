1. career guidance program for abm students-for practical research chapter 2​


Answer:

Chapter 2 of a practical research paper typically includes the review of related literature and studies. Here is an example of a literature review for a career guidance program for ABM students:

Introduction:

Career guidance programs are crucial in helping students make informed decisions about their future career paths. For ABM students, career guidance is essential as it prepares them for the business world. In this chapter, the literature related to career guidance programs for ABM students is reviewed.

Related Literature:

A study conducted by Cacho, et al. (2017) found that career guidance programs significantly increase the career readiness of students. The study also showed that the program’s effectiveness is influenced by the methods and strategies used in its implementation.

Another study conducted by Villanueva, et al. (2018) revealed that ABM students tend to struggle with choosing a career path because of the wide range of options available. The study concluded that an effective career guidance program should provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the available career options, their requirements, and potential growth opportunities.

According to the Department of Education (DepEd) Order No. 44, s. 2015, career guidance should be integrated into the curriculum to provide students with essential skills and knowledge in navigating the world of work. The order highlights that career guidance programs should be developed with the cooperation of stakeholders, including the industry, community, and parents.

Conclusion:

Career guidance programs are vital for ABM students to help them make informed decisions about their future careers. Research suggests that effective career guidance programs should use appropriate methods and strategies, provide a comprehensive understanding of available career options, and involve stakeholders in its development. Therefore, it is crucial for schools to incorporate career guidance programs into the ABM curriculum.

4. Data visualization - Data can be presented in various formats, including graphs, charts, and tables, to help identify trends and patterns. For example, a line graph can be used to show the trend in sales over time, while a pie chart can be used to show the breakdown of expenses by category.

16. PRACTICAL RESEARCH: PART OF CHAPTER 1: THE PROBLEM, THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK ABOUT PERCEPTION OF STUDENTS TO E-COMMERCE. ​


Answer:

The Problem

The surge in e-commerce has revolutionized the way businesses operate, and it has become increasingly popular among consumers globally. In recent times, the world has experienced a significant shift towards online shopping, and this trend has been accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic. The emergence of e-commerce has brought about many benefits, including convenience, accessibility, and affordability. However, despite these benefits, there are still some reservations about using e-commerce platforms, particularly among students.

Theoretical Framework

The study is anchored on the theory of planned behavior, which posits that attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control are the main predictors of an individual's intentions to perform a specific behavior. Attitudes refer to an individual's overall evaluation of a behavior, while subjective norms refer to the perceived social pressure that an individual receives from significant others to perform a behavior. Perceived behavioral control refers to the individual's perception of the ease or difficulty of performing the behavior.

In the context of e-commerce, the theory of planned behavior suggests that students' perceptions of e-commerce are influenced by their attitudes towards the behavior, subjective norms from their peers and family, and perceived behavioral control, such as their level of computer literacy and internet access. These factors may either promote or hinder students' intentions to use e-commerce platforms, and ultimately affect their actual behavior.

The study aims to investigate the perceptions of students towards e-commerce, considering the factors that influence their attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control. By understanding these factors, the study can provide insights into how e-commerce platforms can be designed to better meet the needs and preferences of students, ultimately leading to increased adoption and usage of e-commerce among this demographic.

26. TASK 2: Directions: Explain the value and purpose of each of the five major chapters of research. Chapter 1 (The Problem and its Background) Chapter 2 ( Review of Related Literature) Chapter 3 ( Methodology) Chapter 4 ( Analysis and Interpretation of Data) Chapter 5 (Summary of Findings, Reccommendations, and Conclusions)


Answer:

Chapter 1: The Problem and its Background - This chapter introduces the research problem and provides the context for the study. It explains the significance of the problem, the purpose of the study, and the research questions or hypotheses. The chapter also includes the scope and limitations of the study, the definition of terms, and the conceptual framework that guides the study.

Chapter 2: Review of Related Literature - This chapter provides a comprehensive review of the literature related to the research problem. It summarizes the existing knowledge, identifies the gaps in the literature, and explains how the current study addresses those gaps. The review of literature also helps establish the theoretical foundation of the study, develops research hypotheses or questions, and justifies the research design.

Chapter 3: Methodology - This chapter describes the research design, procedures, and methods used in the study. It includes the population and sample, data collection instruments, data analysis techniques, and the ethical considerations that were taken into account during the study. The methodology chapter ensures the credibility and reliability of the study by explaining how the data was collected and analyzed.

Chapter 4: Analysis and Interpretation of Data - This chapter presents the results of the study, including the descriptive and inferential statistics. It also interprets the findings and relates them back to the research questions and hypotheses. The analysis and interpretation of data chapter helps answer the research questions or hypotheses and provides evidence to support the study's conclusions.

Chapter 5: Summary of Findings, Recommendations, and Conclusions - This chapter summarizes the main findings of the study, discusses their implications, and provides recommendations for future research. It also restates the purpose of the study and its significance, summarizes the major findings, and draws conclusions based on the results. The chapter helps the reader understand the practical and theoretical implications of the study and provides suggestions for further research.
