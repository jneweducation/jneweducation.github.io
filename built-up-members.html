9. A DISEASE THAT RESTRICTS BLOOD FLOW BECAUSE OF THE FATS BUILT UP ON THE ARTERY WALLS


Answer:

Atherosclerosis

Explanation:

Atherosclerosis is a specific type of arteriosclerosis. Atherosclerosis is the buildup of fats, cholesterol and other substances in and on your artery walls. This buildup is called plaque. The plaque can cause your arteries to narrow, blocking blood flow.

Answer:

Atherosclerosis

Explanation:

Atherosclerosis, sometimes called "hardening of the arteries," occurs when fat (cholesterol) and calcium build up inside the lining of the artery wall, forming a substance called plaque. Over time, the fat and calcium buildup narrows the artery and blocks blood flow through it.

12. Electrical charge built up on the surface of an object(static or current) ​


Answer:

Static

Explanation:

Static electricity is the result of an imbalance between negative and positive charges in an object. These charges can build up on the surface of an object until they find a way to be released or discharged. Rubbing certain materials against one another can transfer negative charges, or electrons.
