GAUGE PRESSURE

The piston of a hydraulic automobile lift is 0.3 meters in diameter. The gauge pressure, in Pascal, need to lift a car with a mass of 1200 kilograms is 166.4 KPa or 1.64 atm

How did we get the answer?

We are going to use the formula where force is directly proportional to the product pressure and area.

F= PA

First, let us calculate the pressure in Pascal.

P = [tex]\frac{1200(9.81)}{\frac{\pi}{4}(0.3)^2 }[/tex] = 166.4 KPa

Converting from Pa to atm:

Note: 1 atm is equal to 101,325 Pa

166.4 KPa x [tex]\frac{1 atm}{101,325Pa}[/tex] = 1.64 atm

8. A hydraulic lift used at an automotive repair shop raises a 1000-kilogram car two meters off of the ground. What is the potential energy given to the car? A 1000 J B 2000 J C 9800 J D 19,600 J


Answer:

D. 19,600 J  

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve for the potential energy of the car above the hydraulic lift. For potential energy, we will use the formula:

PE = mgh

where

PE      is the potential energy, unit is in N.m or Joules (J)

m       is the mass of the car, unit is in kg

g        is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

h         is the height lifted by the hydraulic to the car, unit is in meters (m)

For the given information

m = 1000 kg

h = 2 m

PE = ?

Solving the problem

Let us use the formula for potential energy then substitute the given information, we have:

PE = mgh

PE = (1000 kg)(9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])(2 m)

PE = 19,600 J

Therefore, the potential energy of the car is 19,600 J.

A hydraulic jacking system uses a fluid, usually oil, to transfer power from one point to another. The system consists of a hydraulic pump that applies pressure to the oil, which is then sent through a series of hoses to a hydraulic cylinder or piston. The ram is connected to a platform or hoist that needs to be raised.

When the pump applies pressure to the oil, the ram moves up and raises the platform. The power transmitted through the system depends on the size of the pump and the cylinder. The system can be controlled using valves that regulate the flow of oil through the system.

Hydraulic lifting systems are widely used in auto repair shops, manufacturing plants, and on construction sites to lift heavy equipment or machinery. They are also used in elevators, where they provide a fluid and efficient way to move people and goods between floors.

In general, the hydraulic lifting system works on the principle of power transmission through a fluid, which makes it a powerful and versatile tool for lifting heavy loads.

29. The mass of an automobile is 2800 kg. A 50 kg man drives the vehicle at the speed of 60 km/h. What is the total momentum of the automobile and the driver?


Answer:

Therefore, the total momentum of the man and the automobile together is 47, 500.95 kg·m/s.

Explanation:

Momentum, also called "mass in motion", is defined to be the quantity of motion that an object contains. It is directly proportional to the velocity of the object and mass of that certain object. Momentum is also greatly affected by force and time.

The mathematical equation of momentum in terms of velocity and mass on an object is

p = mv  →  equation 1

where

p stands for momentumm stands for mass andv stands for velocity

While the mathematical equation for momentum in terms of force exerted by the object and the time elapsed is

p = Ft  →  equation 2

where

p = momentumF = force andt = time

Analyzing the problem, we are given with the mass of the man driving an automobile and the mass of the automobile itself. The velocity is also given, hence, we will use the equation 2 for solving.

Solution:

Since we will use the equation 1, we only need one value of mass; and also because it is stated in the problem that we are looking for the total momentum.

Given:m₁ (mass of the man driving the car) = 50 kgm₂ (mass of the automobile) = 2800 kgv = 60 km/hr (convert this velocity to m/s)v = 60 km/hr = 16.667 m/sSolving:p = mvm = m₁ + m₂m = 50 kg + 2800 kgm = 2850 kgp = (2850 kg) * (16.667 m/s)p = 47,500.95 kg·m/s

Therefore, the total momentum of the man and the automobile together is 47, 500.95 kg·m/s.
