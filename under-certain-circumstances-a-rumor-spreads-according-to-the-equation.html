7. propaganda devices spread or give information,pieces of idea or rumor in order to persuada you to aririve at a certain decision in life​


Propaganda is the more or less systematic effort to manipulate other people’s beliefs, attitudes, or actions by means of symbols (words, gestures, banners, monuments, music, clothing, insignia, hairstyles, designs on coins and postage stamps, and so forth). Deliberateness and a relatively heavy emphasis on manipulation distinguish propaganda from casual conversation or the free and easy exchange of ideas. Propagandists have a specified goal or set of goals. To achieve these, they deliberately select facts, arguments, and displays of symbols and present them in ways they think will have the most effect. To maximize effect, they may omit or distort pertinent facts or simply lie, and they may try to divert the attention of the reactors (the people they are trying to sway) from everything but their own propaganda.

Which refers to the spreading of information, ideas, or rumor​s?

The transmission of information—facts, arguments, rumors, half-truths, or lies—in order to affect public opinion is known as propaganda.

Propaganda is communication aimed primarily at influencing an audience and furthering a goal. It may or may not be objective, and it may offer data selectively to encourage a specific synthesis.

Propaganda's purpose. Rather than just communicating information, the goal of propaganda is to actively influence people's ideas or behaviors.

During World War I, when posters and films were used against opponents to motivate army enrollment and attract public opinion, the term "propaganda" became widely used in the United States. Propaganda evolved into a modern political technique for generating positive sentiment among a diverse demography and winning public support.

Propaganda is a style of message presentation that is explicitly focused at influencing people's attitudes or behavior rather than presenting objective information.
