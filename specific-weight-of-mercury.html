7. Calculate the amount of heat liberated (in kJ) from 366 g of mercury when it cools from 77.0⁰C to 12.0⁰C. (specific heat of mercury = 0.139 J/g●⁰C) ​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & m = \text{366 g} \\ & c = 0.139 \: \dfrac{\text{J}}{\text{g}\cdot{}^{\circ}\text{C}} \\ & T_{\text{i}} = 77.0^{\circ}\text{C} \\ & T_{\text{f}} = 12.0^{\circ}\text{C} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the heat liberated by mercury.

[tex]\begin{aligned} q & = mc(T_{\text{f}} - T_{\text{i}}) \\ & = (\text{366 g})\left(0.139 \: \dfrac{\text{J}}{\text{g}\cdot{}^{\circ}\text{C}}\right)(12.0^{\circ}\text{C} - 77.0^{\circ}\text{C}) \\ & = -\text{3306.81 J} \\ & = -\text{3306.81 J} \times \frac{\text{1 kJ}}{\text{1000 J}} \\ & = -\text{3.30681 kJ} \\ & \approx \boxed{-\text{3.31 kJ}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the heat liberated by 366 g of mercury is 3.31 kJ.

Note: The negative sign indicates that heat is liberated or released.

15. The surface tensions of mercury and water at 60C are 0. 47 N/m and 0. 0662 N/m, respectively. What capillary-height changes will occur in these two fluids when they are in contact with air in a glass tube of radius 0. 30 mm? Use angle of contact = 130 deg. For mercury, and 0 deg. For water; specific weight of mercury is 132. 3 kN/m3 and 9. 650 kN/m3 for water.


To calculate the capillary-height change in these two fluids, we can use the following equation:

Δh = 2γcosθ / ρgr

Where:

Δh is the capillary-height change

γ is the surface tension of the fluid

θ is the angle of contact between the fluid and the glass

ρ is the density of the fluid

g is the acceleration due to gravity

r is the radius of the glass tube

For mercury, we can plug in the values given in the question to get:

Δh = 2 * 0.47 N/m * cos(130 deg) / (132.3 kN/m3 * 9.81 m/s2 * 0.30 mm)

= 4.88 x 10-6 m

For water, we can plug in the values given in the question to get:

Δh = 2 * 0.0662 N/m * cos(0 deg) / (9.650 kN/m3 * 9.81 m/s2 * 0.30 mm)

= 1.29 x 10-6 m

Therefore, the capillary-height change in mercury will be 4.88 x 10-6 m and the capillary-height change in water will be 1.29 x 10-6 m.
