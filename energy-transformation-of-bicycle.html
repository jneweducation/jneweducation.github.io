3. how does the potential energy of a bicycle transform into kinetic energy?​


Answer:

A parked bike on top of a hill has potential energy, which becomes kinetic energy once you start riding it downhill. Both of these energies are measured in joules. Energy is never destroyed or lost when changing from potential energy to kinetic energy — it is merely transformed from one energy type to another

Explanation:

An object has potential energy (stored energy) when it is not in motion. Once a force has been applied or it begins to move the potential energy changes to kinetic energy (energy of motion).

7. Bicycle has a kinetic energy of 124 J. what kinetic energy would be the bicycle have if it had the same and was moving with twice the speed?​


Answer:

KE = 496 J

Explanation:

Given:

KE = 1/2 mv^2 = 124 J

Solution

Find the mass first

When velocity is doubled v will then become v=2v, substitute it in the equation

KE = 1/2 m (2v)^2

KE = 1/2 m (4v^2)

Retain the formula for KE which is = 1/2 mv^2,

KE = 4 (1/2 mv^2)

Understanding the equation, it means that the kinetic energy increased by 4 times

To solve the kinetic energy, multiply the given value to 4

KE = 4 (124 J)

KE = 496 J
