1. Someone claims that the absolute pressure in a liquid of constant density doubles when the depth is doubled. do you agree? explain.


Answer: When the liquid's depth is increased, only the gauge pressure varies linearly with the height of the liquid and becomes doubled with the increase in the height. Thus, the absolute pressure in a liquid of constant density does not doubles when the depth is doubled; only the gauge pressure gets doubled.

6. A boiler installed where the atmospheric pressure is 752 mm hg has a pressure reading of 12 kg/cm^2. what is the absolute pressure in kpa?


The absolute pressure : 1277.056 KPa

Further explanation

Boiler is a device used to produce steam which is then used for various needs for example for electricity generation

Inside the boiler experiences pressure due to the heating (from burner) of the water inside

Absolute pressure is the total pressure resulting from the sum of the pressures in the boiler with atmospheric (air) pressure. Pressure shown on  the boiler (pressure gauge) is the pressure inside the boiler

Can be formulated:

P gauge = P absolute  - P atmosphere

P Absolute  = P gauge +  P  atmosphere

P atmospheric standard is valued at 1 atm = 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa.

A boiler installed where the atmospheric pressure is 752 mm Hg has a pressure reading of 12 kg/cm².

So :

P atm = 752 mmHg = 752 x 0,133322 KPa =100.258 KPa

Pressure reading = P gauge =  12 kg/cm² = 12 x 98,0665 KPa = 1176.798 KPa

Then

P absolute = P gauge + P atm

[tex]\rm P~abs=1176.798 +100.258 \\\\P~abs=\boxed{\bold{1277.056~KPa}}[/tex]

First, we have to convert the atmospheric pressure into psi. Where an inch of mercury is equal to about 0.491 pounds per square inch

[tex]\sf{(29.1 \: hg)( \dfrac{0.491 \: psi}{1 \: hg} )}[/tex]

17. he g A vessel has a pressure of 276 kPa. The atmospheric pressure is 11 m of water equivalent. Find the absolute pressure in meter of water.


Explanation:

Formulas:

Pa = Pg + Patm

where:

Pa = absolute pressure

Pg = gage pressure

Patm = atmospheric pressure

P = yh

where:

P = pressure

y = unit weight (9.81 kN/m³ for water)

h = height

Given:

Pg = 276 kPa

Patm = 11 m of water

Solution:

Convert Pg to m of water

P = yh

276 = (9.81)h

h = 28.13 m of water

Pa = Pg + Patm

Pa = 28.13 + 11

Pa = 39.13 m of water
