19. If 7. 03 grams of hydrogen gas (2. 02 g/mol) react with excess nitrogen gas, how many grams ammonia (17. 03 g/mol) form?​.


Answer: 39.5 grams of ammonia

Step-by-step explanation:

The balanced chemical equation for the reaction between hydrogen gas and nitrogen gas to form ammonia is:

N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3

From this equation, we can see that 3 moles of hydrogen gas react with 1 mole of nitrogen gas to form 2 moles of ammonia.

First, we need to determine how many moles of hydrogen gas we have:

7.03 g H2 x (1 mol H2 / 2.02 g H2) = 3.48 mol H2

Since there is excess nitrogen gas, we can assume that all of the hydrogen gas will react to form ammonia. Therefore, we need to determine how many moles of ammonia will form:

3.48 mol H2 x (2 mol NH3 / 3 mol H2) = 2.32 mol NH3

Finally, we can determine the mass of ammonia that will form:

2.32 mol NH3 x 17.03 g/mol NH3 = 39.5 g NH3

Therefore, 39.5 grams of ammonia will form.
