Thus

23. 3. Find the volume of the cone with a radius of 7 mm and 16 mm. Use 3.14 for the *. Note: V= (B x h) or V= (π xr²xh)​


Answer:

13,510.22 cubic millimeters.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the volume of a cone, we can use the formula:

V = (1/3) x π x r² x h

where r is the radius of the circular base of the cone, h is the height of the cone, and π is the mathematical constant pi (approximately equal to 3.14).

a) For a cone with a radius of 7 mm:

V = (1/3) x π x r² x h

V = (1/3) x 3.14 x 7² x 16

V = 615.44 mm³ (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the volume of the cone with a radius of 7 mm and height of 16 mm is approximately 615.44 cubic millimeters.

b) For a cone with a radius of 16 mm:

V = (1/3) x π x r² x h

V = (1/3) x 3.14 x 16² x 16

V = 13,510.22 mm³ (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the volume of the cone with a radius of 16 mm and height of 16 mm is approximately 13,510.22 cubic millimeters.

25. A sample of gas occupies a volume of 450.0 mL at 740 mm Hg and 16°C. Determine the volume of this sample at 760 mm Hg and 37°C.


Problem:

A sample of gas occupies a volume of 450.0 mL at 740 mm Hg and 16°C. Determine the volume of this sample at 760 mm Hg and 37°C.

Note:

To solve problem in gas law, temperature must be in kelvin unit. We have to convert degree celsius to kelvin by the formula ( Kelving = °C  +273.15).

Kelvin = 16°C + 273.15 = 289.15 K

Kelvin = 37°C + 273.15 = 310.15 K

Given Data:

P₁ (initial pressure) = 740 mmHg         P₂ (final pressure) = 760 mmHg

T₁ (initial temperature ) = 289.15 K      T₂ (final temperature)  = 310.15 K

V₁ (initial volume)  =  450 ml                 V₂ (final volume) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula  V₂  =  P₁ × T₂ × V₁  / P₂T₁

V₂  =  P₁ × T₂ × V₁  / P₂T₁

     = 740 mmHg  × 310.15 K × 450 ml  / 760 mmHg ×  289.15 K

     = 103, 279, 950 ml / 219,754

     = 469.98 ml

Answer:

Final volume is equal to  469.98 ml
