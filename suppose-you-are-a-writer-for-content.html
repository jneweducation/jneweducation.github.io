Topic: The impact of social media on mental health

Genre 1: Documentary film

Point of view: Balanced and objective, featuring interviews with experts and individuals affected by the issue

Language: Formal and informative

Length: 60-90 minutes

Mood: Thoughtful and illuminating

Genre 2: Op-ed article

Point of view: Personal and opinionated

Language: Conversational and persuasive

Length: 750-1000 words

Mood: Passionate and urgent

In the documentary film, I would aim to present a nuanced and comprehensive overview of the topic, exploring the various ways in which social media can both positively and negatively impact mental health. The film would feature interviews with psychologists, sociologists, and individuals who have experienced the effects of social media on their mental health firsthand. The length of the film would be 60-90 minutes, allowing for a thorough exploration of the topic. The overall mood of the film would be thoughtful and illuminating, encouraging viewers to consider the issue from multiple perspectives.

In the op-ed article, I would present a more personal and opinionated perspective on the topic. The language would be conversational and persuasive, using anecdotes and examples to make my case. I would aim to convince the reader of the importance of addressing the issue and suggest potential solutions. The length of the article would be 750-1000 words, allowing me to make my points concisely and effectively. The mood of the article would be passionate and urgent, reflecting the gravity of the issue and the need for immediate action.
