QUESTION:

From which of the following reactions does ∆H0rxn = ∆H0f?

a. H2(g) + S(rhombic) → H2S(g)

b. C(diamond) + O2(g) → CO2(g)

c. H2(g) +CuO(s) → H2O(l) + Cu(s)

d. O(g) + O2(g) → O3(g)

ANSWER:

a. H2(g) + S(rhombic) → H2S(g)

EXPLANATION:

The standard enthalpies of formation of H2(g), S(rhombic), and H2S(g) are 0, 0, and -20.15 kJ/mol respectively. Since the enthalpies of formation of the two reactants (H2(g) and S(rhombic)) are both zero, the ∆H0rxn of H2S(g) is equal to its ∆H0f.

21. Diamond is a natural form of pure carbon. What numbers of atoms are in 1.00 carat diamond (1.00 carat = 0.200g)? (C = 12.01 g/mol)


MASS TO ATOMS

Convert the mass from carats to grams.

1.00 carat = 0.200 g

(1.00 carat C)(0.200 g C / 1 carat C)

= 0.200 g C

Convert the mass to moles.

1 mol C = 12.01 g/mol

(0.200 g C) / 12.01 g C / 1 mol C)

= 0.017 mol C

Convert the moles to atoms.

(0.017 mol C)(6.022 x 10²³ atoms C / 1 mol C)

= 1.024 x 10²² atoms C
