7. A satellite dish is shaped like a paraboloid, with the receiver placed at the focus. It is to have a depth of 0.44 m at the vertex, with the receiver placed 0.11 m away from the vertex. What should the diameter of the satellite dish be?


let the vertex is at the origin (0,0);

distance between the vertex and focus, a = 0.11 m

depth, y = 0.44 m

find the diameter, 2x = ?

Solution:

Opening Upward

[tex]x^{2} = 4ay[/tex]

[tex]x^{2} = 4(0.11)(0.44)[/tex]

[tex]x^{2} = 0.1936[/tex]

[tex]x=\sqrt{0.1936}[/tex]

[tex]x=0.44[/tex]

diameter, 2x = 2(0.44)

diameter, 2x = 0.88 m

the diameter of satellite dish is 0.88 m.

16. A satellite dish in the shape of a paraboloid is 10 ft across, and 4 ft deep at its vertex. How far is the receiver from the vertex, if it is placed at the focus? Round off your answer to 2 decimal places. please maawa na kayo hanggang 11:59 nalang oed guys


You are trying to find 'c' -- the distance between the vertex and the focus.

Think of the equation for a vertical parabola:

y = (1/4c)(x-h)^2 + k

If we place our parabola at the center our equation becomes:

y = (1/4c)x^2

.

The problem gives you a point on the parabola: (10,4)

Plug it in and solve for 'c':

y = (1/4c)x^2

4 = (1/4c)10^2

4 = (1/4c)100

4 = (1/c)25

4c = 25

c = 25/4

c = 6.25 feet
