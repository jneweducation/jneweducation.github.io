7. 3. An aqueous solution of hydrochloric acid contains 36% HCl by mass. (a)Calculate the mole fraction of HCl in the solution. (b) Calculate themolality of HCl in the solution​


Given:

concentration = 36% by mass of aqueous HCl solution

solute: HCl

solvent: water

Required:

mole fraction

molality

Solution: (a)

Step 1: Determine the mass of the components present.

Since the given is in terms of percent by mass, we assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Therefore, 36% by mass aqueous HCl solution contains 36 g of HCl (solute) in 64 g of water (solvent).

mass of solute = 36 g

mass of solvent = 64 g

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For solute

molar mass of solute = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 36.458 g/mol

For solvent

molar mass of solvent = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solvent = 18.016 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For solute

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{36 g}}{\text{36.458 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.9874376 mol

For solvent

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{molar mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{64 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solvent = 3.5523979 mol

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{moles of solute + moles of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{0.9874376 mol}}{\text{0.9874376 mol + 3.5523979 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mole fraction} = 0.22}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Calculate the molality of solution.

Since the moles of solute is already obtained in Step 3 of Solution (a), which is 0.9874376 mol, and the mass of solvent is already given, which is 64 g or 0.064 kg, the molality of solution is

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.9874376 mol}}{\text{0.064 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 15 \: m}[/tex]

20. A 30.0% solution of HCl has a density of 1.215 g/cm3. What mass of hydrochloric acid is there in exactly 80 cm3 of this solution?​


✏️PERCENT BY MASS

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{density of solution} = \text{1.215 g/}\text{cm}^3 \\ & \text{percent by mass} = 30.0\% \\ & \text{volume of solution} = \text{80 cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of solution} & = \text{density of solution} \times \text{volume of solution} \\ & = \text{1.215 g/}\text{cm}^3 \times \text{80 cm}^3 \\ & = \text{97.2 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Solve for the mass of solute (HCl).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of solute} & = \frac{\text{percent by mass}}{100\%} \times \text{mass of solution} \\ & = \frac{30.0\%}{100\%} \times \text{97.2 g} \\ & = \boxed{\text{29.2 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 29.2 g of HCl in 30.0% HCl solution having a density of 1.215 g/cm³.

25. What is the Molarity of concentrated HCl if the solution contains 37.0% HCl by mass and if the density of the solution is 1.18 g/ml?


Answer:

The density — 1.19 g/mL — tells us that one mL of the solution has a mass of 1.19 gms. Therefore 1000 mL of the solution has a mass of 1190 grams.

Now that we know the mass of the solution we can determine the mass of the HCl in a liter. In 1190 gms of solution, 37.5 % is HCl. 1190 x 0.375 = 446.25

So, we have 446.25 gms HCl in a liter of solution. How many moles HCl do we have? The molecular weight of HCl is 36.5. Therefore, we have 446.25 / 36.5 = 12.23 moles HCl in each liter of the concentrated acid.

The molarity of the solution is 12.23. And, since HCl is a monobasic acid, the normality will also be 12.23.

26. 1.) Sixty grams of FeCl3 reacts with 50.0 grams of H2S. 2 FeCl3 (s) + 3 H2S (g) ---> 6 HCl + Fe2S3 (s) (A) What is the limiting reactant? (B) What is the mass of HCl produced?


Solution (A):

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each reactant.

For FeCl₃

molar mass = (55.85 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 162.2 g/mol

For H₂S

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 34.086 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratios needed.

Using FeCl₃

mole ratio = 2 mol FeCl₃ : 6 mol HCl

Using H₂S

mole ratio = 3 mol H₂S : 6 mol HCl

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of HCl produced by each reactant.

Using FeCl₃

[tex]\text{moles of HCl = 60.0 g FeCl₃} × \frac{\text{1 mol FeCl₃}}{\text{162.2 g FeCl₃}} × \frac{\text{6 mol HCl}}{\text{2 mol FeCl₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of HCl = 1.1097 mol}[/tex]

Using H₂S

[tex]\text{moles of HCl = 50.0 g H₂S} × \frac{\text{1 mol H₂S}}{\text{34.086 g H₂S}} × \frac{\text{6 mol HCl}}{\text{3 mol H₂S}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of HCl = 2.934 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Determine the limiting reactant.

Since FeCl₃ produces less number of moles of HCl

[tex]\boxed{\text{FeCl₃ is the limiting reactant.}}[/tex]

Solution (B)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of HCl.

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 36.458 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of HCl produced.

Since FeCl₃ is the limiting reactant

[tex]\text{mass of HCl = 1.1097 mol HCl} × \frac{\text{36.458 g HCl}}{\text{1 mol HCl}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of HCl = 40.46 g}}[/tex]
