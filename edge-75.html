3. A wooden cube of 0.2 m on one edge is submerged to 75% of its volume in water. Determine the specific gravity of the wooden cube


Answer:

The specific gravity of the wooden cube is 0.75.

Explanation:

The specific gravity of an object is the ratio of its density to the density of water. We can use the fact that the cube is submerged to find its density and then use the density of water to find its specific gravity.

The volume of the cube is (0.2 m)^3 = 0.008 m^3. If the cube is submerged to 75% of its volume, then the volume of water displaced is 0.75 * 0.008 m^3 = 0.006 m^3.

The density of water is 1000 kg/m^3 (at standard conditions), so the mass of water displaced is:

mass = density * volume = 1000 kg/m^3 * 0.006 m^3 = 6 kg

Since the cube is in equilibrium (not sinking or floating), the weight of the cube must be equal to the weight of the water displaced. The weight of the water displaced is:

weight = mass * gravity = 6 kg * 9.8 m/s^2 = 58.8 N

The weight of the cube is:

weight = mass * gravity = density * volume* gravity

We can solve for density:

density = weight / (volume * gravity) = 58.8 N / (0.008 m^3 * 9.8 m/s^2) = 750 kg/m^3
