Problem:

1. A weightlifter lifts a set of 1250kg weights a vertical distance of 2m in a weight lifting  contest. What Potential Energy do the weights now possess?​

Note:

The formula of Potential energy is ( P.E. = m × g × h )

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 1,250 kg

g ( gravity ) = 9.8 m/s²

h ( height ) = 2 m

P.E. ( Potential Energy ) = ? unknown

Solution:

we will use the formula ( P.E. = m × g × h )

P.E. = m × g × h

    = 1,250 kg × 9.8 m/s² × 2 m

     = 12,250 kg·m/s² × 2 m

     = 24,500 kg·m²/s²

     = 24,500 J

Answer:

The  Potential Energy do the weights now possess is 24,500 J  or                 24,500 kg·m²/s²

26. A weight lifter lifts a set of 1250kg weights a vertical distance of 2m in a weight lifting contest. What Potential Energy do the weights now possess?


Potential Energy is 24.5 KJ

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 1250 kg

h = 2 m

g = 9.8 m/s²

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Potential Energy

Step 3: Solve for Potential Energy

Solution:

The formula for Potential Energy is given by:

PE = mgh

where: PE - Potential Energy

m - mass

g - gravitational acceleration constant

h - height of object from the reference level

Substituting the value of m,g and h in the formula,

PE = (1250 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(2 m)

PE = 24,500 kgm²/s² or 24,500 Nm or 24,500 J or 24.5 KJ
