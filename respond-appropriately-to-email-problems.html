8. Appropriate content for email messages includes


A well-written email must be polite and concise for it to be a convenient and effective method of communication.

First, your subject should already give your recipient an idea of its content.

Next, regardless of the purpose of your email, send a greeting.

Then, let them know who you are. Now, keep in mind that this is optional, depending on your recipient. If you are sending an email to someone who recognizes you, skip to the next step. However, if you are writing to someone or someplace that has no idea who you are (a company, university, etc.), state your name and a quick description.

Proceed with your intention. Whatever your purpose is, make sure to keep things short, sweet, and professional.

If there is a need to include documents, let your recipient know that there are attachments in your email.

Finally, express your appreciation of your recipient's time and, if applicable, let your recipient know that you will be waiting for their response.

A quick tip: Before sending an email, make sure that you have your recipient's correct email address, and proofread!

Here is an example:

(An inquiry for your teacher about your grades)
Subject:
Consultation Request regarding the Scores on the Most Recent Science Project

Content:
Hello and good day, Mrs. Smith,

I am Jane Reyes, your student from Section III. I have just received my score for our most recent science project (a diorama of the human nervous system). I am a bit concerned with my score and surprised at how it turned out.

Your comment stated that my diorama "seemed to be made in a rush and incorrect." I wish to let you know that I have allotted a good amount of time to create this and have done research with help from my parents to ensure its accuracy.

I hope I can have a bit of your time as I would like to request a consultation with you when you are available.

Thank you very much and I am hoping to hear from you soon.
