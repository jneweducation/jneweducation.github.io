2. Fair discrimination definition?


Discrimination refers to the ways that show favor and prejudice over and against a person or a circumstance due to like gender, race, ethnicity, social, status, disability, belief system, culture, political views, etc.

Discrimination has two types which are fair discrimination and unfair discrimination.

Fair discrimination refers to the generally acceptable form of discrimination.  This has four respective types:

Discrimination based on affirmative action which promotes the practice of equality at work. Discrimination based on inherent requirement of a job which requires certain qualifications that are necessary in a job.  If the construction company needs a physically fit individual for the position of mason, then they will focus on hiring the best fit only. Fair compulsory discrimination by law which includes the unemployment of children who are under 15 years old. Discrimination based on productivity refers to employers who rely on an employee’s performance and productivity for his salary increase.

5. Define the term fair discrimination?


Discrimination can be observed in ways that show favor and prejudice against a person or a circumstance due to reasons like gender, race, ethnicity, social, status, disability, belief system, culture, political views, etc.

However, some discrimination are viewed as acceptable or generally allowed which is referred to as fair discrimination.

Fair discrimination has four types:

1. Discrimination based on affirmative action – This promotes the practice of equality at work.

2. Discrimination based on inherent requirement of a job – This requires certain qualifications that are necessary in a job like good eyesight for pilots and physical fitness for gym instructor.

3. Fair compulsory discrimination by law – An example is not employing children who are under 15 years old.

4. Discrimination based on productivity – Employers may rely on an employee’s performance and productivity for his salary increase.

Clarify the term fair discrimination and explain why employers often employ young females instead of males.

The easiest way to understand what fair discrimination is is to call it as legal discrimination. It serves to level the proverbial playing field in order to give others the opportunities seen as exclusively open only to a group that is blatantly protected by the system.  

Example:

In the west, some institutions may be required by law to hire a certain number of minorities and women to give others a chance.

Fair discrimination also applies to a situation wherein only a certain group can fill the position and hiring any other just won’t do.

Example:

A movie role that requires a female to play the part will never hire a male actor.

Fair discrimination may be based on many different factors such as race, gender, religion, and many others.

24. Fair discrimination definition brainly?


Fair discrimination refers to generally acceptable kinds of discrimination laws.

These are the four types of fair discrimination:

1. Discrimination based on affirmative action

This is designed to promote fairness or equity in the employment of groups or individual in terms of race, gender, and disability.

2. Discrimination based on the inherent requirements of a job

This refers to the inherent nature of the job and its corresponding qualifications.  For example, individuals who have poor eyesight are not qualified to become a pilot.

3. Fair compulsory discrimination by law

The law prohibits the employment of children who are under 15 years, and pregnant women who are near there giving of birth.

4. Discrimination based on productivity

The law allows the employer’s fair giving of performance merit based on productivity.
