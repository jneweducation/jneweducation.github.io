2. driving to the north along a highway, a driver speed up from 10 m/s per second to 25 m/s per second in 16 seconds. what is the acceleration of the automobile?​


Answer:

The acceleration of the automobile can be calculated using the formula:

acceleration = (final velocity - initial velocity) / time

Substituting the given values, we get:

acceleration = (25 m/s - 10 m/s) / 16 s

acceleration = 15 m/s / 16 s

acceleration ≈ 0.94 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the automobile is approximately 0.94 meters per second squared.


3. Calculate the values of distance, displacement, speed, and acceleration of a moving object. Question 1. While traveling to the north along a highway, a driver speeds up from 10 m/s to 25 m/s in 16s. What is the acceleration of the automobile?​


Answer:

Given:

Initial velocity, u = 10 m/s

Final velocity, v = 25 m/s

Time taken, t = 16 s

We can use the following formula to calculate the acceleration (a) of the automobile:

a = (v - u) / t

Plugging in the values we get:

a = (25 m/s - 10 m/s) / 16 s

a = 0.9375 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the automobile is 0.9375 m/s^2.

5. why do highways have speed limit and not the velocity limits ​


Answer:

Speed is a scalar quantity that attributes to "how fast an object is moving." The rate at which an object travels distance can be thought of as a speed. On the other hand, velocity is a vector quantity that pertains to "the rate at which an object changes its position." The policies that our government implies want to set a limit on how fast our vehicles are moving. In this way, they can manage to lessen the road accidents occurring. The velocity of our automobiles would not affect road accidents that much as it concerns the rate of cars moving to a different position. Therefore, we can conclude that speed limit is more helpful in our road than velocity limits.

19. while traveling along a highway a driver slows from 45 m/s to 15 m/s in 12 second. what is the automobile's accelerate


Answer:

You can easily solve for the acceleration of this vehicle by using the definition of acceleration. Acceleration is the change in velocity with respect to the elapsed time. The elapsed time is 12 seconds and the change in velocity is 15 m/s minus 45 m/s = -30 m/s.

Solving for the acceleration

a = (15 m/s - 45 m/s) / 12 s

a = -30 m/s ÷ 12 s

a = −2.5 m/s^2

The automobile's acceleration is −2.5 m/s^2.

26. The mass of an automobile is 2800 kg. A 50 kg man drives the vehicle at the speed of 60 km/h. What is the total momentum of the automobile and the driver?


Answer:

Therefore, the total momentum of the man and the automobile together is 47, 500.95 kg·m/s.

Explanation:

Momentum, also called "mass in motion", is defined to be the quantity of motion that an object contains. It is directly proportional to the velocity of the object and mass of that certain object. Momentum is also greatly affected by force and time.

The mathematical equation of momentum in terms of velocity and mass on an object is

p = mv  →  equation 1

where

p stands for momentumm stands for mass andv stands for velocity

While the mathematical equation for momentum in terms of force exerted by the object and the time elapsed is

p = Ft  →  equation 2

where

p = momentumF = force andt = time

Analyzing the problem, we are given with the mass of the man driving an automobile and the mass of the automobile itself. The velocity is also given, hence, we will use the equation 2 for solving.

Solution:

Since we will use the equation 1, we only need one value of mass; and also because it is stated in the problem that we are looking for the total momentum.

Given:m₁ (mass of the man driving the car) = 50 kgm₂ (mass of the automobile) = 2800 kgv = 60 km/hr (convert this velocity to m/s)v = 60 km/hr = 16.667 m/sSolving:p = mvm = m₁ + m₂m = 50 kg + 2800 kgm = 2850 kgp = (2850 kg) * (16.667 m/s)p = 47,500.95 kg·m/s

Therefore, the total momentum of the man and the automobile together is 47, 500.95 kg·m/s.
