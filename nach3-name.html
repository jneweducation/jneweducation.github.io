7. what kind of musical element is Verklärte Nacht


Verklärte Nacht, string sextet for two violins, two violas, and two cellos by Austrian-born American composer Arnold Schoenberg that dates to 1899, before he adopted the 12-tone method of composition that became his signature. It is a highly romantic piece and, as such, is easily the composer’s most-performed piece.

27. what is the musical style of Verklarte Nacht?​


Answer:

Verklärte Nacht is a tone poem—that is, an instrumental composition with plot content—in the tradition of Franz Liszt's Les Préludes and Richard Strauss's Don Juan. Schoenberg's piece differs from its models in that it is a chamber piece, not an orchestral work.


28. what is the harmony of Verklarte Nacht?​


Answer:

“Verklärte Nacht” does not open with strange or unexpected harmony, but with a simple six-note scale descending in D minor. It's broken into two halves, like a tense breath: three notes in, three notes out.
