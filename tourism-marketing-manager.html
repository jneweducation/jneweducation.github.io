2. Apply the concept of the four P's of marketing (marketing mix). For some decisions that you will make, as you manage a business in the tourism, hospitality and service industries.


Product/Service

What does the customer want from the product /service? What needs does it satisfy?

What features does it have to meet these needs?

Are there any features you've missed out?

Are you including costly features that the customer won't actually use?

How and where will the customer use it?

What does it look like? How will customers experience it?

What size(s), color(s), and so on, should it be?

What is it to be called?

How is it branded?

How is it differentiated versus your competitors?

What is the most it can cost to provide and still be sold sufficiently profitably? (See also Price, below.)

Place

Where do buyers look for your product or service?

If they look in a store, what kind? A specialist boutique or in a supermarket, or both? Or online? Or direct, via a catalog?

How can you access the right distribution channels?

Do you need to use a sales force? Or attend trade fairs? Or make online submissions? Or send samples to catalog companies?

What do your competitors do, and how can you learn from that and/or differentiate?

Price

What is the value of the product or service to the buyer?

Are there established price points for products or services in this area?

Is the customer price sensitive? Will a small decrease in price gain you extra market share? Or will a small increase be indiscernible, and so gain you extra profit margin?

What discounts should be offered to trade customers, or to other specific segments of your market?

How will your price compare with your competitors?

Promotion

Where and when can you get your marketing messages across to your target market?

Will you reach your audience by advertising online, in the press, on TV, on radio, or on billboards? By using direct marketing mailshots? Through PR? On the internet?

When is the best time to promote? Is there seasonality in the market? Are there any wider environmental issues that suggest or dictate the timing of your market launch or subsequent promotions?

How do your competitors do their promotions? And how does that influence your choice of promotional activity?

5. What is the difference of tourism marketing from the manufacture marketing.​


Answer:

Tourism marketing and manufacturing marketing have some key differences:

Explanation:

1. Tangibility: Manufacturing involves tangible products that can be touched, felt, and tested, whereas tourism marketing involves intangible products like experiences and destinations that cannot be physically touched.

2. Distribution: The distribution channels for manufacturing marketing are usually intermediaries like wholesalers and retailers, while tourism marketing often involves direct marketing to consumers through various channels such as travel agencies, online travel websites, and social media.

3. Seasonality: The tourism industry is seasonal and has peak and off-peak seasons, while the manufacturing industry is usually not seasonal and products can be sold throughout the year.

4. Location: Manufacturing marketing often has a fixed location, while tourism marketing can take place in various locations and destinations.

5. Promotion: Promotion in tourism marketing usually involves showcasing the unique experiences and attractions of a destination, while manufacturing marketing is focused on promoting the features and benefits of a product.

27. what is the meaning of tourism management


What is Tourism Management?

Tourism Management refers to all activities which are related to the hospitality and tourism industries. It is considered as a versatile field that prepares people with training, experience, and interest for a management position in tourism, accommodation, and food industries. It may also be related to enterprises, associations, and public authorities that includes tourism marketing for potential travellers.  

Three main areas of Tourism Management:  

Administration function in terms of business such as finance, marketing, and human resources Theories and principles about management Tourism-related topics such as environmental factors, tourism organizations, and travel motivations.
