3. In the light of researches on human development, which of the two approaches is closer to the truth traditional or life span? why?


Lifespan Approach

The lifespan method is considerably closer to the truth than the traditional approach since it states that human development is a lifelong process.

What is the difference?

The traditional perspective stresses significant change from birth to adolescence, little or no change in life, and decrease in old age. The life-span approach stresses both adult and childhood developmental progress.

Why is the life span approach closer to the truth?

From conception to death, the Lifespan Developmental Approach has provided an overall framework for understanding human development. The following are the tenets of the Lifespan Development Approach:

The development of a person's lifespan is a continual process impacted by both biology and the environment. Over the course of a lifetime, development develops as a process that encompasses both gains and losses, resulting in a multidimensional, multidirectional, and multifunctional perspective. Changes in the relationship between biological and environmental variables that determine resource allocation occur as people develop throughout their lives. Life span development is a changeable and interventional process. Age-graded, history-graded, and non-normative contexts all contain biological and environmental factors.

The Lifespan Developmental Approach, on the other hand, has not been widely disseminated or adopted across the other subspecialties of psychology. Findings from developmental research designs, for example, may be useful in other areas of research, such as the advantages of employing sequential research designs, which allow for a more exact investigation of the impacts of age, cohort, and measurement time.
