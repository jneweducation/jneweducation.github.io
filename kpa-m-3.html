12. What is the pressure at the base of an upright 15m column filled with a fluid of 1,500 kg/m^3 density? Acceleration due to gravity is at 9.81m/s^2. [Answer in kPa] ​


Pressure is 220.73 KPa

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

ρ = 1500 kg/m³

h = 15 m

g = 9.81 m/s²

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Pressure

Step 3: Solve for Pressure

Equation:

The formula for Hydrostatic pressure is given by:

P = hρg

where: P - Hydrostatic pressure, Pa

ρ - density of liquid, kg/m³

h - height of liquid, m

g - gravitational acceleration constant, 9.8 m/s²

Solution:

Substituting the value of ρ, g and h in the formula,

P = (15 m)(1500 kg/m³)(9.8 m/s²)

P = 220,725 Pa or 220.73 KPa

Water flows steadily with a velocity of 3 05 m/s in a horizontal pipe having a diameter of 15 24 cm at one section of the pipe, the temperature and pressure of the water are 21 c and 689 3 kpa, respectively at a distance of 304 8 m downstream, the pressure is 516 9 kpa what is the friction factor


Answer:

To find the friction factor in this scenario, you would need to use the Darcy-Weisbach equation which is used to calculate the friction head loss (pressure drop) due to friction in a pipe. The equation is:

hf = f * L/D * (v^2)/(2*g)

where:

hf = friction head loss (m)

f = friction factor

L = length of pipe (m)

D = diameter of pipe (m)

v = velocity of fluid (m/s)

g = acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2)

You have the information about the pressure drop (689.3 kPa - 516.9 kPa = 172.4 kPa), the distance (304.8 m), diameter (15.24 cm = 0.1524 m) and velocity (3.05 m/s)

Rearranging the equation and substituting the known values:

172.4 = f * 304.8/(0.1524 * 2 * 9.81) * (3.05)^2

Solving for f:

f = (172.4 * 0.1524 * 2 * 9.81) / (304.8 * (3.05)^2)

Without the exact value for the density, viscosity and roughness of the pipe it is impossible to give you an accurate value for the friction factor.

29. A vacuum gauge mounted on a condenser reads 0.66 m hg. what is the absolute pressure in the condenser in kpa when the atmospheric pressure is 101.3 kpa


Pressure is the perpendicular force exerted on a surface per unit area. Furthermore, there are three kinds of pressure: atmospheric pressure, gauge pressure and absolute pressure.

Atmospheric pressure is the already existing pressure within our surroundings.

Gauge pressure is the change of pressure of the atmosphere around the sensor. Take into account that the gauge sensor will refer to the existing pressure around it as 0, so it will read 1 atom of pressure inside the gauge as 0 if the pressure outside is 1 atom as well. When the pressure became 3 atoms while the outside is 1 atom the sensor will show you a pressure of 2 atoms.

Absolute pressure is the pressure measured against a vacuum, so it is the sum of the preexisting pressure in the atmosphere and the change in the pressure measured by the gauge sensor.


Understanding these concepts will us the following formula:

Absolute Pressure = Atmospheric Pressure + Gauge Pressure

Gauge Pressure =  Absolute Pressure - Atmospheric Pressure 


Going back, the problem has the following given:

Gauge Pressure = 0.66 mmHg

Atmospheric Pressure = 101.3 kpa

Absolute Pressure = x kpa


This means that the unknown value is the absolute pressure inside the condenser.


Looking at the given we notice that they have different units. Since the unknown value has a unit of kpa we will convert the other unit (mmHg) as kpa.


Conversion (mmHg to kpa):

1 mmHg = 0.133322 kpa


Computation for Gauge Pressure:

[tex]Gauge Pressure = 0.66 mmHg * \frac{0.133322 kpa}{1 mmHg} [/tex] 

[tex]Gauge Pressure = 0.66 * \frac{0.133322 kpa}{1} [/tex]

[tex]Gauge Pressure = 0.087992 kpa[/tex]


Therefore, the new given for the problem are:

Gauge Pressure = 0.087992 kpa

Atmospheric Pressure = 101.3 kpa

Absolute Pressure = x kpa


With all given having the same unit we can now compute for the unknown value, i.e. absolute pressure.


Computation: 

[tex]Absolute Pressure = Gauge Pressure + Atmospheric Pressure[/tex]

[tex]Absolute Pressure = 0.087992 kpa + 101.3 kpa[/tex]
[tex]Absolute Pressure = 101.387992 kpa[/tex]

This means that the absolute pressure inside the condenser is 101.387992 kpa or approximately 101.3880 kpa! That should be the answer.
