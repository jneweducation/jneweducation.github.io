20. A grid is superimposed on a map of the area directly surrounding the home of a suspect. Detectives want to position themselves on opposite sides of the suspect’s house. Coordinates are assigned to the suspect’s home. Unit A is positioned at (–1, 6) on the coordinate plane. Where should Unit B be located so that the suspect’s home is centered between the two units?


Answer:

(9,0)

Step-by-step explanation:

As the suspect's home is to be centered between the house and the location of the detectives, that means that the suspect's home is the midpoint. Essentially, the midpoint has coordinates which are the average of the x and y coordinates.

[tex]\text{midpoint} = (\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2})[/tex]

Since the given is the midpoint and one other point, it would be best to focus per coordinate.

[tex](\frac{-1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{6 + y_2}{2}) = (4,3)\\\\\\\frac{-1 + x_2}{2} = 4\\-1 + x_2 = 8\\x_2 = 9\\\\\\\frac{6 + y_2}{2} = 3\\6 + y_2 = 6\\y_2 = 0[/tex]

Therefore, Unit B should be located at (9, 0).

distance = sqrt((x2 - x1)^2 + (y2 - y1)^2)

distance = sqrt((10 - 4)^2 + (17 - 9)^2)

distance = sqrt(6^2 + 8^2)

distance = sqrt(100)

distance = 10

Therefore, the distance between the school library and canteen 2 is 10 meters.

To find the coordinates of the point halfway between the school gym and school library, we can use the midpoint formula:

midpoint = ((x1 + x2)/2, (y1 + y2)/2)

where (x1, y1) = (3, -3) is the location of the school gym and (x2, y2) = (9, 13) is the location of the school library. Substituting these values, we get:

midpoint = ((3 + 9)/2, (-3 + 13)/2)

midpoint = (6, 5)

(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2

(x - 1)^2 + (y - 6)^2 = (10,000)^2

x^2 - 2x + 1 + y^2 - 12y + 36 = 100,000
