2. In the SI system of measure, what is the unit of capacitance?


Answer:

In both the practical and the metre–kilogram–second scientific systems, the unit of electric charge is the coulomb and the unit of potential difference is the volt, so that the unit of capacitance—named the farad (symbolized F)—is one coulomb per volt. One farad is an extremely large capacitance.

4. used to measure capacitance in farad​


Answer:

Capacitance is measured in units called farads (abbreviated F). The definition of one farad is deceptively simple. A one-farad capacitor holds a voltage across the plates of exactly one volt when it’s charged with exactly one ampere per second of current.

14. explain the factors affecting capacitance


Answer:

There are three factors which influence capacitance: the size of the conductors, the size of the gap between them, and the material between them (the dielectric). The bigger the conductors, the bigger the capacitance. The smaller the gap, the bigger the capacitance.

24. Find the total capacitance for three capacitors connected in series, given their individual capacitances are 1.0 μF, 5.0 μF, and 8.0 μF.


Answer:

The total capacitance is 0.75μF

Explanation:

To compute for the total capacitance of capacitors connected in series, we get the reciprocal of the sum of the individual reciprocal of capacitors.

The formula is:

Ctotal = [¹/C₁ + ¹/C₂ + ¹/C₃]⁻¹

Now, let us substitute the given data to the formula

Ctotal = [¹/1μF + ¹/5μF + ¹/8μF]⁻¹

Ctotal = 0.75μF

Therefore, the total capacitance is 0.75μF.
