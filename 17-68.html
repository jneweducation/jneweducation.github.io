4. the area of parallelogram is 68 m². if the height is 17 m, what is the length of the base?​


Answer:

4 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

The formula for the area of a parallelogram is:

A = b × h

where A is the area, b is the base, and h is the height.

We know that the area of the parallelogram is 68 m² and the height is 17 m. So, we can plug these values into the formula and solve for the base:

68 = b × 17

Dividing both sides by 17, we get:

b = 68 / 17

b = 4

Therefore, the length of the base is 4 meters.

15. A triangle has an angle that measures 68°. The other two angles are in a ratio of 11:17. What are the measures of those two angles?


Answer:

11x = 44 degrees

17x = 68 degrees

Step-by-step explanation:

The sum of the three angles in a triangle is always 180 degrees.

Let x be the common ratio between the two angles.

Then, the three angles can be represented as:

68 + 11x + 17x = 180

Simplifying:

28x + 68 = 180

Subtracting 68 from both sides:

28x = 112

Dividing both sides by 28:

x = 4

So the two angles are:

11x = 44 degrees

17x = 68 degrees

Therefore, the measures of the two angles are 44 degrees and 68 degrees.
