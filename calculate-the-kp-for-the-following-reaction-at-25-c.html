1. A flask is charged with 1.500 atm of N2O4(g) and 1.00 atm NO2(g) at 25 °C, and the following equilibrium is achieved: N2O4(g) ⇌ 2NO2(g). After equilibrium is reached, the partial pressure of NO2 is 0.512 atm. a. What is the equilibrium partial pressure of N2O4? b. Calculate the value of Kp for the reaction.


a. SOLUTION:

Step 1: Summarize the partial pressures using the ICE table.

[tex]\begin{array}{lccc} & \text{N}_2\text{O}_4(g) & \rightleftharpoons & 2\text{NO}_2(g) \\ \text{Initial (atm):} & 1.500 & & 1.00 \\ \text{Change (atm):} & +x & & -2x \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium (atm):} & 1.500 + x & & 0.512 \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the value of x.

[tex]\begin{aligned} 1.00 - 2x & = 0.512 \\ 2x & = 1.00 - 0.512 \\ 2x & = 0.488 \\ x & = \frac{0.488}{2} \\ x & = \text{0.244 atm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the equilibrium partial pressure of N₂O₄.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_{\text{N}_2\text{O}_4} & = 1.500 + x \\ & = 1.500 + 0.244 \\ & = \boxed{\text{1.744 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the equilibrium partial pressure of N₂O₄ is 1.744 atm.

------------------------------------------------------------

b. SOLUTION:

[tex]\begin{aligned} K_{P} & = \frac{P^2_{\text{NO}_2}}{P_{\text{N}_2\text{O}_4}} \\ & = \frac{0.512^2}{1.744} \\ & = \boxed{0.150} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the value of Kp for the reaction is 0.150.

The reaction between gaseous sulfur dioxide and oxygen is a key step in the industrial synthesisof sulfuric acid:2S02(g)+O2(g) = 2S03(g)A mixture of SO2 and Oz was maintained at 800 K until the system reached equilibriumThe equilibrium mixture contained•5.0x10^-2 M SO3•. 3.5x10^-3 M O2 and•3.0x10^-3 M SO.Calculate Keq and Kp at this temperature.​


Answer:

Keq = 7.9 × 10⁴

Kp = 1.2 × 10³

Explanation:

Calculating the Keq

Keq = [SO3]² / [SO2]²[O2]

Keq = [5.0 × 10^(-2)]² / [(3.0 × 10^(-3)]²[3.5 × 10^(-3)]

Keq = 79365.08

Keq = 7.9 × 10⁴

Calculating the Kp

Calculating ∆n

∆n = moles of gaseous products - moles of gaseous reactants

∆n = 2 - (2 + 1)

∆n = -1

Substituting all the values

Kp = Keq(RT)^(∆n)

Kp = (79365.08)[(0.082057)(800)]^(-1)

Kp = 1.2 × 10³

22. The atmospheric oxidation of nitrogen monoxide, 2 NO (g) + O2 (g) <‐‐> 2NO2 (g), was studied at 184° C with initial pressures of 1.00 atm of NO and 1.000 atm of O2. At equilibrium Po = 0.56 atm. Calculate Kp.​


Solution:Step 1: Construct the ICE table.

[tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & 2\text{NO}(g) & + & \text{O}_{2}(g) & \rightleftharpoons & 2\text{NO}_{2}(g) \\ \text{Initial} \: (\text{atm}): & 1.00 & & 1.00 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (\text{atm}): & -2(0.28) & & -0.28 & & +2(0.28) \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (\text{atm}): & 0.44 & & 0.72 & & 0.56 \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Note: 0.28 is obtained by dividing the equilibrium pressure of NO₂ (which is 0.56) by its stoichiometric coefficient (which is 2) since the initial pressure of NO₂ is zero.

Step 2: Calculate the equilibrium constant Kp.

[tex]K_{P} = \dfrac{P^{2}_{\text{NO}_{2}}}{P^{2}_{\text{NO}}P_{\text{O}_{2}}}[/tex]

[tex]K_{P} = \dfrac{(0.56)^{2}}{(0.44)^{2}(0.72)}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{K_{P} = 2.25}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]K_{P} = 2.25[/tex]

28. Write the expression for the equilibrium constant Kp for the following reaction. CO(g) + Cl2(g) ↔ COCl2(g)


Given:

CO(g) + Cl₂(g) ↔ COCl₂(g)

Required:

[tex]K_{\text{p}}[/tex]

Solution:

Kp is an equilibrium constant in which the equilibrium quantities are expressed in terms of partial pressures of each gas instead of concentrations. The Kp expression for the given reaction is

[tex]\boxed{K_{P} = \frac{P_{\text{COCl}_{2}}}{P_{\text{CO}}P_{\text{Cl}_{2}}}}[/tex]
