Initial state:

- Pressure = 1 MPa

- The steam is dry and saturated, which means that it is in equilibrium with its liquid phase at the same temperature and pressure

- Using steam tables, we can determine that the temperature of the steam at this state is approximately 184.9°C

Final state:

- Pressure = 1 atm

- We know that the steam expands in the turbine, so its volume increases while its pressure decreases

- We can assume that the expansion is reversible and adiabatic, which means that there is no heat transfer and no entropy generation during the process

- Using the steam tables, we can determine that the specific volume of the steam at this state is approximately 26.8 m^3/kg

Next, we can use the given information about the efficiencies of the turbine and generator to calculate the overall efficiency of the system:

- Turbine efficiency = 80%, which means that 80% of the available energy is converted into mechanical work

- Generator efficiency = 95%, which means that 95% of the mechanical work is converted into electrical energy

- Overall efficiency = Turbine efficiency x Generator efficiency = 0.8 x 0.95 = 0.76, or 76%

Finally, we can use the first law of thermodynamics to calculate the amount of work output from the turbine and the electrical power output from the generator:

- The first law of thermodynamics states that the change in internal energy of a system is equal to the heat added to the system minus the work done by the system: ΔU = Q - W

- Since the process is adiabatic, there is no heat transfer: Q = 0

- Therefore, the work done by the turbine is equal to the decrease in internal energy of the steam: W_turbine = ΔU = U_final - U_initial

- Using the steam tables, we can determine the internal energy of the steam at the initial and final states: U_initial = 2840.9 kJ/kg and U_final = 2514.4 kJ/kg

- Therefore, the work done by the turbine is: W_turbine = 326.5 kJ/kg

- The electrical power output from the generator is equal to the mechanical work output from the turbine.

28. A steam turbine receives 5,000 kg/hr of steam at 5 mpa and 4000c and velocity of 25 m/sec. it leaves the turbine at 0.006 mpa and 85% quality and velocity of 20 m/sec. radiation is 10,000 kj/hr. find the kw developed.


To solve the problem, we need to use the First Law of Thermodynamics which states that the change in internal energy of a system is equal to the heat added to the system minus the work done by the system. Assuming steady state conditions and neglecting kinetic and potential energy changes, the energy balance for the steam turbine can be written as:

m_dot * (h_in - h_out) = W - Q

where m_dot is the mass flow rate of steam, h_in and h_out are the specific enthalpies at the inlet and outlet of the turbine, W is the work done by the turbine, and Q is the heat lost by radiation.

Using steam tables, we can find the specific enthalpies:

h_in = 3587 kJ/kg

h_out = 2440 kJ/kg

Substituting the given values, we get:

5000 kg/hr * (3587 - 2440) kJ/kg = W - 10000 kJ/hr

Simplifying and converting units, we get:

W = 7387 kW

Therefore, the power developed by the steam turbine is 7387 kW.
