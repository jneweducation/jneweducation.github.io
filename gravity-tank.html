6. What is the force acting on one side of the tank moving upward with an acceleration of 4.7 m/s2 when it is filled with oil whose specific gravity is 0.752 and a height of 1.3 m for the bottom of the tank?


Answer:

The force acting on one side of the tank is 36,893.13 N

Explanation:

Here, we are going to use the concept of pressure in liquids or hydrostatic equilibrium

Formula to use

1. P = F/A

2. P = γ(oil)·h(1 + a/g)

where

P         is the pressure at any point in a fluid s at rest due to the

          weight of the overlying fluid, the unit is in N/m² or Pascal

          (Pa)

F         is the weight force of the fluid normal to the surface, the

          unit is in Newton (N)

A         is the cross-sectional area of the surface, the unit is in

           m²

γ(oil)   is the specific gravity of oil

h         is the height of the tank, the unit is in meter (m)

g         is the pull of gravity, 9.8 m/s²

a         is the acceleration of the liquid, the unit is in m/s²

Given information

F = ?

a = 4.7 m/s²

γ(oil) = 0.752

h = 1.3 m

Solving the problem

Let us solve first the pressure on the liquid to move upward using the second given formula

P = γ(oil)·h(1 + a/g)

Then substitute the given data

P = 0.752(9810Pa/m)(1.3m)(1 + 4.7/ 9.8)

P = 14189.66 Pa

Take note that 9810 Pa/m is the pressure of water

Now, let us solve for the force using the first given formula

P = F/A

Derive to solve for F:

F = P x A

The area of the tank is not given here, so let us assume that the tank is rectangular with a width of 2m. And we know that the area of a rectangle is length x width.

Therefore, substitute the given data

F = (2863.97 Pa)(2m)(1.3 m)

F = 36,893.13 N

10. An unknown immiscible liquid seeps into the bottom of an open oil tank. Some measurements indicate that the depth of the unknown liquid is 1.5 m and the depth of the oil (specific weight = 8.5 KN/m^3) floating on top is 5.0 m. A pressure gage connected to the bottom of the tank reads 65 KPa. What is the specific gravity of the unknown liquid?


Answer:

The specific weight of unknown liquid is found to be 15 KN/m³

Explanation:

The total pressure in tank is measured to be 65 KPa in the tank. But, the total pressure will be equal to the sum of pressures due to both oil and unknown liquid.

Total Pressure = Pressure of oil + Pressure of unknown liquid

65 KPa = (Specific Weight of oil)(depth of oil) + (Specific Weight of unknown liquid)(depth of unknown liquid)

65 KN/m² = (8.5 KN/m³)(5 m) + (Specific Weight of Unknown Liquid)(1.5 m)

(Specific Weight of Unknown Liquid)(1.5 m) = 65 KN/m² - 42.5 KN/m²

(Specific Weight of Unknown Liquid) = (22.5 KN/m²)/1.5 m

Specific Weight of Unknown Liquid = 15 KN/m³


11. a cylindried tank can be hold 197.86 cubicseter of water. If the radius of the tank is 3meters, how high is the tank? ​


Answer:

7.04 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use the formula for the volume of a cylinder, which is:

V = πr²h

where V is the volume, r is the radius, and h is the height.

We know that the volume of the tank is 197.86 cubic meters, and the radius is 3 meters. So we can substitute these values into the formula and solve for h:

197.86 = π(3²)h

197.86 = 9πh

h = 197.86 / (9π)

h ≈ 7.04 meters

Therefore, the height of the tank is approximately 7.04 meters.
