3. Assume that a 100 bair pair dna double helix contains 45 cytosines how many adenines are there


The rules for DNA base pairing were lain down based on the experimental findings of Erwan Chargaff. Later, when Watson and Crick established the structure of DNA, the concept of base pairing was more comprehensively understood. The deductions regarding the base pairing of nucleotides in DNA molecules is as follows.

Chargaff's parity rule 1: In any given DNA sample from any species, the total percentage content of Adenine is equal to that of Thymine, and the total percentage contents of Guanine and Cytosine are the same.
%A=%T;%G=%C
Therefore it can be inferred that,
A+G=T+C

So, if there's 100 base pair and 45 are cytosine
A+45=T+45
100=A+45
A= 100-45
A=55
