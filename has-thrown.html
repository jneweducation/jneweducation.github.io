9. how much kinetic energy has a 500g soccer ball when it is thrown at a speed of 30m/s?​


✍ Calculate Use

This calculator will find the missing variable in the physics equation for Kinetic Energy of a rigid body, when two of the variables are known.

KE=½mv²

✍ Where

• KE =Kenetic Energy

• m = Mass Of A Body

• V = Velocity Of Body

✍ Kinetic Energy

Kinetic Energy is the energy an object has owing to its motion. In classical mechanics, kinetic energy (KE) is equal to half of an object's mass (1/2*m) multiplied by the velocity squared. For example, if a an object with a mass of 10 kg (m = 10 kg) is moving at a velocity of 5 meters per second (v = 5 m/s), the kinetic energy is equal to 125 Joules, or (1/2 * 10 kg) * 5 m/s2.

We use Joules, kilograms, and meters per second as our defaults, although any appropriate units for mass (grams, ounces, etc.) or velocity (miles per hour, millimeters per second, etc.) could certainly be used as well - the calculation is the same regardless.

16. why did the ball thrown upward has a zero velocity


A ball being thrown upward is the motion by which some external force is being put to the ball in order for it to move up. This motion further says that the force exerted should be enough to go against the gravity.

Let us go over with the problem.

First of, if a ball is thrown, then there is a force exerted to it which gives an INITIAL VELOCITY to the ball.

Next is that, as the ball travels up opposing the gravity, the ball's acquired initial velocity decreases and eventually reaches a stop at a certain height. This distance which the ball travels can be computed using the DISPLACEMENT FORMULA IN KINEMATICS which is

d = (Vo)(t) + (1/2)(g)(t^2)

This distance is also the highest distance that the ball could reach after being thrown.

In this distance, the  ball has already reached a velocity which is equal to zero (0). And after reaching this height, the ball will fall back to the ground from an initial velocity of zero (0) and reaches a certain FINAL VELOCITY.

That is when we can say that the ball being thrown upward will have a zero velocity when it reaches the highest height it could possibly attain.
