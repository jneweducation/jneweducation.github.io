6. agent of chemical 1 and agents of mechanical


Agents of chemical and agents of mechanical refer to the different methods of cleaning or removing dirt, stains, or other unwanted substances from surfaces or materials.

Agents of chemical cleaning involve the use of chemical substances such as detergents, solvents, acids, or alkalis to break down and dissolve dirt or stains. Examples of chemical cleaning agents include bleach, ammonia, vinegar, and various commercial cleaning products.

On the other hand, agents of mechanical cleaning involve physical actions or processes such as scrubbing, brushing, wiping, or using high-pressure water jets to remove dirt or stains. Examples of mechanical cleaning agents include sponges, brushes, towels, pressure washers, and other cleaning tools.

Both chemical and mechanical cleaning agents have their own advantages and disadvantages, and their effectiveness depends on the type of surface or material being cleaned and the nature of the dirt or stain to be removed.

9. What could be the possible reason why Microbes are considered as agent of Physical and Chemical Weathering?


Answer:

Microbial activity breaks down rock minerals by altering the rock's chemical composition, thus making it more susceptible to weathering. One example of microbial activity is lichen; lichen is fungi and algae, living together in a symbiotic relationship.

A food hazard is a biological, chemical or physical agent in a food with the potential to cause adverse health effects (Codex Alimentarius Commission, 1997; Anon., 1996).


12. it is a term used to refer to a biological,chemical or physical agent in food with the potential to cause harm?a.Hazardb.threatc.riskd.danger​


Answer:

Hazard is a term used to refer to a biological, chemical or physical agent in food with the potential to cause harm.

Explanation:

Biological hazard

Biological health hazards typically include bacteria, viruses, parasites and moulds or fungi that formed in the food. These hazards pose a threat to human safety and health either when they are inhaled, eaten or come in physical contact with our skin. Bacteria, viruses, parasites, and fungi can cause illness such as food poisoning, tetanus, respiratory infections or parasite infection.

Chemical hazard

A chemical hazard is defined as any substance usually chemicals that will cause a health problem when ingested or inhaled. Chemical hazards include toxins, dangerous chemicals, residue of excess chemicals used in processing food products.

Physical hazard

Physical hazards include glasses, plastics, packaging, jewelry, pest droppings, screws, and other things that might get into your food during packaging or processing.

Food borne illnesses

Foodborne Illnesses or Food poisoning occurs when food spoilage or contamination of food takes place. This can be caused by dirty food, pathogenic bacteria’s, viruses or parasites that contaminate food items.
