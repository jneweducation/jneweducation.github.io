16. ony has a cardboard with the length of 30 dm. He wants to cut it into three. If the length of the two strips are 9.25 dm and 8.5 dm, how long is the last strip? *


Answer:

12.25 dm

Step-by-step explanation:

The given are total length of the cardboard of 30 dm, to cut into 3 strips, length of one strip is 9.25dm and length of the second strip is 8.5 dm. We need to know how long is the last strip.

Step 1: Write the mathematical equation of the problem. Let x be the length of the third or last strip. So,

30 = 9.25 + 8.5 + x

Step 2: Rearrange the equation and equate it to x. Then, solve for x.

30 - 9.25 - 8.5 = x

x = 12.25 dm

Therefore, the length of the last strip is 12.25dm.

30 dm = 17.75 dm + x

30 dm - 17.75 dm = x

12.25 dm = x

Answer: The last strip is 12.25 dm

1. Understand the problem.

  It is important that we understand the problem and what is asked in the word problem. Specifically, we answer the following questions:

          - What question is the problem asking?

          - What information do you already have?

In the given problem, we are asked to determine the length of the last strip given the length of the other two strips and the total length of all the strips.

2. Represent unknown numbers with variables.

A variable is a symbol used to represent a number in an expression or an equation.  In the given problem, the unknown number is the length of the last strip and x is used to represent this unknown number.

3. Translate the rest of the problem into a mathematical expression.

An algebraic expression is a mathematical expression that consists of variables, numbers, and operations. Using the given problem, below is the algebraic mathematical operation:

30 dm = 9.25 dm + 8.5 dm + x

4. Solve the problem.

Simplify the equation to determine the value of the unknown, x.
