13. In 1950, the population of a city was about 800000 people. Over the next 50 years, the population increased to 1.3 million. Find the percent of increase


Increase (%) = [(New population - Original population) / Original pop] × 100

Increase (%) = [(1,300,000 - 800,000)/800,000] × 100

Increase ((%) = [500,000/800,000) x 100

Increase (%) = 0.625 × 100  ⇒  62.5%

ANSWER: 62.5%

To solve for the annual average rate of increase, use geometric progression rule:

an = a₁ (r)ⁿ⁻¹

Where:

an = 1,300,000

a₁ = 800,000

n = 50

r = ?

1,300,000 = 800,00 (r)⁵⁰⁻¹

r⁴⁹ = 1,300,000/800,000

r⁴⁹ = 1.625

⁴⁹√(r⁴⁹) = ⁴⁹√(1.625)

r = 1.0099  or  1.01  ⇒  average annual rate of increase in population

Check:

a₅₀ = 800,000(1.01)⁵⁰⁻¹

a₅₀ = 800,000 (1.628348)

COMPOUND INTEREST

Using the formula for finding the present value P of a compound interest, P=F/{1+r)^t} wherein P is the present value, F is the future value, r is the rate and t is the term, we will get P693,304.21.

P is the present value, F is the future value which is Php800,000, r is the rate which is 1.2% or 0.012, t is the term. Since their sons age are 5 years old and the future age is 17, the term is 17 minus 5 which will give 12 years. Hence, t is 12. By substituting to the formula, P=800,000/{1+0.012)^12}.
