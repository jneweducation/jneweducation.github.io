Answer:

Tangent is horizontal at (-2, 21) and (1, -6)

Explanation:

To find the points where the tangent is horizontal, we must find where the derivative is equal to 0.

First Step : First we must find the derivative of the curve #y=2x^3 + 3x^2 -12x +1#

# y'= 6x^2 +6x -12#

Second Step : Next, we must find where the derivative is equal to zero so we simplify and find the zeroes of the equation.

#y'=6x^2 +6x -12#

#y'=6(x^2 +x -2)#

#y'=6(x+2)(x-1)#

zeroes are at x= -2 and x= 1

Third Step : To find the points at which the tangent is horizontal, we have to plug in our zeroes to the original equation.

#y=2x^3 +3x^2 -12x +1#

x= -2 and x= 1

y(-2) = #2(-2)^3 +3(-2)^2 -12(-2) +1#
y(-2) = 21

(-2, 21)

y(1) = #2(1)^3 +3(1)^2 -12(1) +1#
y(1)= -6

(1,-6)

Answer: The tangent is horizontal at (-2,21) and (1,-6)
