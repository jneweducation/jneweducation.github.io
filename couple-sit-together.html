Answer:

1. A couple wants to plant some shrubs around a circular walkway. They have seven different shrubs. How many ways can the shrubs be planted?

Since the shrubs are being planted around a circular walkway, their arrangement is a circular permutation. For n distinct objects arranged in a circle, the number of circular permutations is (n-1)!. In this case, there are 7 shrubs, so the number of ways they can be planted is (7-1)! = 6!.

2. In how many ways can a family of 8 be seated at a round table?

Since the table is round, the seating arrangement is a circular permutation. The number of circular permutations of n distinct objects is (n-1)!. In this case, there are 8 family members, so the number of ways they can be seated is (8-1)! = 7!.

3. Ten students were playing spirit of the glass. In how many ways can they be arranged in a circle?

Since the students are arranged in a circle, it is a circular permutation problem. The number of circular permutations of n distinct objects is (n-1)!. In this case, there are 10 students, so the number of ways they can be arranged is (10-1)! = 9!.

4. In how many ways can 4 charms be arranged on a bracelet:

a. with clasp

b. without clasp

For both cases, the arrangement is a circular permutation because the charms are being placed on a circular bracelet. The number of circular permutations of n distinct objects is (n-1)!.

a. With clasp: There are 4 charms, so the number of ways they can be arranged is (4-1)! = 3! = 6 ways.

b. Without clasp: The arrangement is the same as a linear permutation since the bracelet does not have a clasp. Therefore, the number of ways the 4 charms can be arranged is 4!.

5. In a prayer meeting, Lina wanted her members to form a circle. Find the number of ways to arrange the 9 members.

Since the members are forming a circle, it is a circular permutation problem. The number of circular permutations of n distinct objects is (n-1)!. In this case, there are 9 members, so the number of ways they can be arranged is (9-1)! = 8!.

6. How many ways can 5 men and 5 women be seated at a round table if they must alternate the men and women?

In this case, we have two groups (men and women) that must alternate in the seating arrangement. We can fix the position of one person (let's say a man) and arrange the remaining people around him. The number of ways to arrange the remaining 4 men is 4! and the number of ways to arrange the 5 women is 5!. Therefore, the total number of ways to seat the 5 men and 5 women alternately is 4! * 5!.

Solution

2. How many ways can 3 men and 2 women sit in a row if the men and women must alternate?

Solution

M: menW: female

The sequence must be since it alternates.

M - W - M - W - M

There are 3! = 6 different arrangements for those males.There are 2! = 2 various arrangements that can be made for those women.

There are therefore a total of 6 x 2 = 12 distinct arrangements of those men and women.

3. Anna is choosing and arranging a set of 3 dresses from a set of 10 dresses. How many ways are possible?

Solution

P=n!/(n-r)!=10!/(10-3)!=10x9x8x7x6x5x4x3x2x1 / 7x6x5x4x3x2x1   cross out the common=10x9x8=720 ways
