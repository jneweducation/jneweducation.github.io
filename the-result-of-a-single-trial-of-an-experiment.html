18. It is applied when the probability of success are not the same from trial to trial. a. Discrete Probability Distribution b. Binomial Experiment c. Poisson Experiment d. Hypergeometric Experiment


Answer:

B. Binomial Experiment

Explanation:

It is applied when the probability of success is not the same from trial to trial. In a binomial experiment, there are a fixed number of trials, each trial has only two possible outcomes (success or failure), the probability of success is constant from trial to trial, and the trials are independent of each other. The binomial distribution gives the probability of getting a specific number of successes in a fixed number of independent trials, given a constant probability of success.

20. 1. a process or an action with observable results 2. a collection or set of possible outcomes of a random experiment 3. known as the results of an experiment or trial 4. the subset of the sample space5. one of the possible outcomes of the experiment A. Experiment B. Outcomes C. Sample Space D. Sample Point E. Event F. Product​


Answer:

1. A. experiment

2. C. sample space

3. B. outcome

4. E. event

5. D. sample point

Step-by-step explanation:

1. “An activity with observable results is called an experiment

2. The set of all possible outcomes is called the sample space. Thus in the context of a random experiment, the sample space is our universal set

3. Event or Outcome

The results or outcomes or observations of an experiment are called events. They are generally represented by the capital letters of English Alphabets. Though we know the possible outcomes of a random experiment, we cannot predict which of these will occur/happen in a conduct of the experiment/trial.

4. An event is a subset of the sample space of an experiment (i.e., a set of sample points). Two mutually exclusive (also known as disjoint) events have no common outcomes (i.e., the occurrence of one precludes the occurrence of the other).

5. A possible outcome of an experiment is called a sample point; the actual observations of an experiment constitute a sample.


21. WHAT IS IT?! It is an experiment, trial or observation which can result in several possible outcomes.​

A Random Experiment is an experiment, trial, or observation that can be repeated numerous times under the same conditions. The outcome of an individual random experiment must be independent and identically distributed. It must in no way be affected by any previous outcome and cannot be predicted with certainty.
