10. Fire Science: A fire department received 24 false alarms out of a total of 200 alarms received. What percent of the alarms received were false alarms?


Answer:

To calculate the percentage of false alarms received, we need to divide the number of false alarms by the total number of alarms and multiply by 100 to get the percentage.

percentage of false alarms = (number of false alarms / total number of alarms) * 100%

In this case, the number of false alarms is 24, and the total number of alarms received is 200. Therefore, the percentage of false alarms is:

percentage of false alarms = (24 / 200) * 100% = 12%

So, 12% of the alarms received were false alarms.
