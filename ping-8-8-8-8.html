15. The length of a ping-pong table is 3 feet more than twice the width the area of a ping-pong table is 90 ft.² what are the dimensions of a ping-pong table


Answer:

The length of the ping-pong table is 15 ft and the width is 6 ft.

Further Explanation

To solve the dimensions of the ping-pong table, use the concepts about the area of the rectangle. Assume a variable that represents the situation.

Let [tex]x\\[/tex] be the width, and so the length must be [tex]2x+3\\[/tex].  

The area of a rectangle formula is given by [tex]A=l\times{w}[/tex]. Substitute the expressions [tex]x=w[/tex], [tex]l=2x+3[/tex], and [tex]A=90[/tex] into the equation.

                             [tex]90=x\times{(2x+3)}[/tex]

Distribute [tex]x[/tex] to the term [tex](2x+3)[/tex] and subtract [tex]90[/tex] from both sides.

                              [tex]\begin{aligned}90&=2x^2+3x\\90-90&=2x^2+3x-90\\0&=2x^2+3x-90\\\text{or}\\&2x^2+3x-90=0\end{aligned}[/tex]

Notice that the new equation is a quadratic equation.

Some Properties of a Quadratic Equation

1. Its degree is 2.

2. The discriminant determines the number of zeros and their nature.

3. The zeros can be solved by either factoring, completing the square or using the quadratic formula.

Let's solve the equation using factoring method. Follow the steps to solve for the values of x.

Steps in Solving for x

1. Rewrite [tex]3x[/tex] as difference of [tex]15x[/tex] and [tex]12x[/tex].

2. Join together the first two terms and the last two terms.

3. Factor the GCF of the first group, [tex]x\\[/tex].

4. Factor the GCF of the second group, [tex]-6[/tex].

5. Factor the common binomial term, [tex]2x+15[/tex].

6. Equate each factor equal to zero and solve for [tex]x[/tex]

7. Take the positive sign only since length can never be negative.

                          [tex]\begin{aligned}2x^2+15x-12x-90&=0\\(2x^2+15x)+(-12x-90)&=0\\x(2x+15)-6(2x+15)&=0\\(2x+15)(x-6)&=0\\2x+15&=0\\2x&=-15\\x&=-\frac{15}{2}\\&\text{or}\\x-6&=0\\x&=6\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means that the width is [tex]x=6[/tex] ft and the length is [tex]2x+3=2(6)+3=15[/tex].

Because it's such a fast-moving sport and the players have the ability to curve the ball, unusual scoring situations arise in table tennis, also known as ping-pong or by the trademarked name Ping-Pong. The ball must bounce once on the returner's side of the table, or court, during a point, but it is possible for the server to hit the ball around the net straight onto the opponent's court without the ball ever traveling over the net.

Unusual But Legal Situations

According to the official rules by the sport's governing body, the International Table Tennis Federation, this is a legal situation—the ball does not have to travel over the net. It is also legal for the ball to travel underneath the net assembly (the part that protrudes off the table and holds the net up), as long as it lands once on the opponent's side of the table. In this situation, the ball can travel below the table surface at the side of the table, and then up onto the opponent's court.
