6. How does primary factor affecting volcanoes eruptive style affect the viscosity of magma


Answer:

The primary factor affecting the eruptive style of a volcano is the composition of the magma. The viscosity of magma, or how easily it flows, is directly related to its composition.

Magma can be broadly classified into two categories: mafic and felsic. Mafic magma has a lower silica content and is therefore less viscous, while felsic magma has a higher silica content and is more viscous. The viscosity of magma also depends on other factors such as temperature, pressure, and the presence of dissolved gases.

Volcanoes that erupt with mafic magma tend to have less explosive eruptions with gentle lava flows. This is because the low viscosity of mafic magma allows it to flow more easily and escape from the volcano before it can build up enough pressure to cause an explosive eruption.

On the other hand, volcanoes that erupt with felsic magma tend to have explosive eruptions with ash clouds, pyroclastic flows, and lava domes. The high viscosity of felsic magma makes it difficult for gas bubbles to escape, leading to the buildup of pressure and explosive eruptions.

Therefore, the primary factor affecting the eruptive style of a volcano, which is the composition of the magma, directly affects the viscosity of the magma.
