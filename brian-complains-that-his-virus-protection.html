Reasons why anti-virus software doesn't work

You should be well-protected from ransomware threats if you've installed antivirus software on all of your machines. However, tech, like protection software, isn't flawless. Your antivirus software could fail to detect a brand-new zero-day threat. It may be dormant because it needs your attention.

In reality, having two active anti-virus programs on your computer will create a conflict, leaving you vulnerable. Remove Conflicting Applications - Apart from anti-virus software, a variety of other systems have been found to create problems with antivirus software, stopping it from functioning properly or at all. For an up-to-date list of services that can create conflicts, go to AV's website (link in Resources). If you discover a program on your computer that is blocking AV from running properly, uninstall it. While we learn of Ransomware and compromised devices, there are also threats that are either focused on phishing, which helps attackers to steal users' legitimate email and device passwords, or have security flaws that are specifically abused by cybercriminals. An anti-virus solution would not be enough to shield you from this.
