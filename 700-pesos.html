13. Mang Ed plans to buy x goats and y pigs with a budget of 6000 pesos. His farm can get up to 8 goats and 7 pigs. Each goat costs 550 pesos and each pig costs 700 pesos


Linear Programming (Word Problems)

Goats and pigs should Mang Ed buy to maximize his profit are 2 goats and 7 pigs.

GIVEN

Mang Ed plans to buy x goats and y pigs with a budget of 6000 pesos. His farm can get up to 8 goats and 7 pigs. Each goat costs 550 pesos and each pig costs 700 pesos.

QUESTION

If each goat yields a profit of 120 pesos and each pig yields a profit of 200 pesos, how many goats and pigs should Mang Ed buy to maximize his profit?

Solution

Let

x = the number of goats that Mang Ed will buyy = the number of pigs that Mang Ed will buy

His farm can get up to 8 goats and 7 pigs. Then,

0 ≤ x ≤ 80 ≤ y ≤ 7

Each goat costs ⇒ 550 pesos

Each pig costs ⇒ 700 pesos

Because the budget is 6000 pesos, we get:

550x + 700y ≤ 6000

or

55x + 70y ≤ 60011x + 14y ≤ 120

Each goat yields a profit of 120 pesos

Each pig yields a profit of 200 pesos

So, the optimization equation (objective function) is

f(x, y) = (550 + 120)x + (700 + 200)yf(x, y) = 670x + 900y

First, graph the system of linear inequalities (See Fig 1)

Then, list the corner or vertex points on the region (See Fig 1), and we get,

A(2, 7)B(0, 7)C(8, 2.3)D(8, 0) E(0, 0)

Next, determine the value of the objective function at each intersection point.

f(2, 7) = 7640  (Max profit) f(0, 7) = 6300 f(8, 2.3) = 7430 f(8, 0) = 5360 f(0, 0) = 0

Therefore, goats and pigs should Mang Ed buy to maximize his profit are

2 goats7 pigs
