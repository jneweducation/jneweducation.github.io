• Air pressure (also known as barometric pressure) is the weight (or force) of the atmosphere pressing down on any given surface of the Earth. 21. A student prevents a 3 kg brick from falling by pressing it against a vertical wall. The coeefficient of static friction is 0.60. What force must she use? Is this more or less than the weight of the brick?​


Answer:

Explanation:

Let:

m = mass

W = weight

F = force

g = gravitational acceleration

μ = coefficient of static friction

f = friction force

N = normal force

Given:

m = 3 kg

g = 9.81 m/s² (constant)

μ = 0.60

Solution:

W = mg

W = (3)(9.81) = 29.43 N

N = W

N = 29.43 N

f = μN

f = (0.60)(29.43)

f = 17.658 N

since the student wants to prevent the brick to fall, he needs to apply a force F equivalent to the friction force

F = f

F = 17.658 N

F < W

17.6589 < 29.43

The force the student needs to exert is less than the weight of the brick
