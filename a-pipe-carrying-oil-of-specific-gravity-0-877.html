6. calculate the specific weight, density and specific gravity of 2.0 L of crude oil weighing 19.2 N​


Answer:

density = 0.098 kg/L or 98 kg/m³

specific weight = 960.4 N/m³

specific gravity = 0.098

Explanation:

Step 1: Solve for the density using the formula: density = mass/volume

In the given problem, the weight of the crude oil is 19.2 N. Mass is expressed in grams or kilograms so it needs to be converted first. Recall the formula for weight. Weight is mass multiplied to the acceleration due to gravity which is 9.8 m/s². This is a constant number.

weight = m x g

19.2 = m x 9.8

m = 1.95918 kg or 1,959.18 grams

density = 1.95918/20 = 0.098 kg/L

This can be converted to kg/m³.

Using the conversion factor:

1 m³ = 1,000 L

0.098 kg/L * (1,000 L/ 1 m³) = 98 kg/m³

Step 2: Solve for the specific weight by using the formula: density x acceleration due to gravity.

specific weight = 98 kg/m³ * 9.8 m/s²

specific weight = 960.4 (kg•m/s²)/m³

We know that kg•m/s² = N so:

specific weight = 960.4 N/m³

Step 3: Solve for the specific gravity.

SG = density of the substance over density of water

The density of water is 1,000 kg/m³.

SG = 98 kg/m³ / 1,000 kg/m³

SG = 0.098

SG has no unit. So we don't need to use a unit for it.

Answer:

Explanation

The weights of equal volumes of any two substances are proportional to their specific gravities.

Problem: A crystal of a chemical salt weighs 6.423 g in air and 2.873 g when immersed in an oil having a specific gravity of 0.858. what is the specific gravity of the salt?

Solution:

6.423 g – 2.873 g = 3.550 g of displaced oil.

Wt. of salt in oil/ Wt. of salt in air = Sp. gr. of oil/Sp.

Gr. of salt= (0.858 x 6.423) / 3.550 = 1.5

25. Problem 2-53 A differential manometer shown is measuring the difference in pressure two water pipes. The indicating liquid is mercury (specific gravity = 13.6), lh is 675 mm, It is 225 mm, and 12 is 300 mm. What is the pressure differential between the two pipes.​


Answer:

The pressure differential can be calculated using the following formula:

ΔP = (ρ * g * h)

where:

ΔP = pressure differential

ρ = density of the fluid

g = acceleration due to gravity

h = height difference between the two fluid columns

In this case, the specific gravity of mercury is 13.6, which means its density (ρ) is:

ρ = specific gravity * density of water

ρ = 13.6 * 1000 kg/m^3

ρ = 13600 kg/m^3

The height difference between the two fluid columns is:

ρ = specific gravity * density of water

ρ = 13.6 * 1000 kg/m^3

ρ = 13600 kg/m^3

The height difference between the two fluid columns is:

h = lh - It + 12

h = 675 mm - 225 mm + 300 mm

h = 750 mm

Converting mm to m:

h = 750 mm * (1 m / 1000 mm)

h = 0.75 m

The acceleration due to gravity (g) is 9.81 m/s^2.

Now we can calculate the pressure differential:

ΔP = (ρ * g * h)

ΔP = (13600 kg/m^3 * 9.81 m/s^2 * 0.75 m)

ΔP = 99918 Pa or 99.918 kPa

Therefore, the pressure differential between the two water pipes is approximately 99.918 kPa.
