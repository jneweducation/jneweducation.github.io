Fourth of July

The use of the word “must” intensifies the speaker’s urgency to attain those things stated in the excerpt.

The fourth of July represents the independence of America from the grasps of Britain. And just like any other form of victory, it is something that is worth teaching in history books and schools in order for the youth to remember the day where they were able to be free from a bigger country. It is a valuable day which should be retained in the minds of the people.  Their appreciation and happiness of this day by:

Doing celebrations  Enabling people to express their joy in streets Using symbolic things such as the colors of their flag

Pertaining back to the title of the excerpt, what does it even mean for the slaves? And what value is there in that certain day in July? In a common understanding of the title alone, we may assume that by the fourth day of July which means being free and independent will also mean that those slaves are now free and are able to live their life fully.

Alas, the true meaning of this excerpt is the total opposite of this. It is known to history about the White and Black race disputes where the black people are used as slaves to manual labor for the white people. They were stripped from every right and was given mistreatment all their living with their co-American people. Some were forced to be slaves to their own land and were treated as nothing more than a low human being.

The speech or the excerpt was from Frederick Douglass an orator who had given abolition towards slavery of his fellow citizens. He insisted in the speech that his presence to give a piece was unnecessary. The fourth of July may be a glorious event for the white people but it doesn’t mean anything for the slaves since their freedom was something they didn’t even have even at first.  

The speaker shows a sense of urgency in the excerpt because:

He wants to honor the founding fathers but also expose the hypocrisy behind it He says demands but not in an intimidating tone but in a matter of being dignified He speaks for his people’s struggles and doesn’t want to rejoice over something that didn’t include them as one

17. Draw a complete set of a computer. Please refer in the list: 1. Monitor 2. CPU 3. Keyboard 4. Mouse 5. Printer 6. Power Supply 7. Headset 8. Web Camera 9. Speaker 10. Scanner​


Answer:

1. Keyboard - A computer keyboard is an input device used to enter characters and functions into the computer system by pressing buttons, or keys.

2. Microphone - It is used to convert sound waves into electric waves or input the audio into computers. It captures audio by converting sound waves into an electrical signal, which may be a digital or analog signal.

3. Mouse - is a handheld hardware input device that controls a cursor in a GUI and can move and select text, icons, files, and folders.

4. Scanner - is a device that captures images from photographic prints, posters, magazine pages, and similar sources for computer editing and display.

5. Speaker - is an output hardware device that connects to a computer to generate sound. The signal used to produce the sound that comes from a computer speaker is created by the computer's sound card.

6. Headphones - are a hardware output device that either plug into a computer line out or speakers. Headphones allow you to listen to audio or watch a movie without disturbing people around you

7. Flash drive - are often used for storage, data back-up and transferring of computer files.

8. Printer - is a device that accepts text and graphic output from a computer and transfers the information to paper, usually to standard size sheets of paper.

9. Monitor - is an output device that displays information in pictorial form. A monitor usually comprises the visual display, circuitry, casing, and power supply.

10. CPU - is an important part of every computer. The CPU sends signals to control the other parts of the computer, almost like how a brain controls a body.
