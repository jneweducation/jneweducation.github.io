9. from a point outside a circle, two tangents are drawn, each 10 inches in length, the chord joining the points of tangency is 12 inches long, find the radius of the circle


First of all, there is a theorem that says that a tangent and a radius are perpendicular at the point of tangency.  This means that we can use the Pythagorean theorem, [tex]a^2 + b^2 = c^2,[/tex], where the legs are the radius, r, and the tangent segment, 10. The hypotenuse is the line segment from the point of tangency to the radius. Now, to find the length of this hypotenuse, let's step back for a bit.

Note that if we connect the points of tangency, to make a triangle with the two legs of length 10, we end up with an isosceles triangle. So, if we cut it in half with the segment from the point of tangency to the radius, these two triangles are congruent. Thus, this segment is the perpendicular bisector to the chord. Again, we can use the Pythagorean theorem.

Since it's a bisector, and we are given a chord length 12, we know that from the point of tangency to the bisector is just 12/2 = 6 inches.

Now, from this point of intersection to the point outside the circle, we just use [tex]a^2 + b^2 = c^2[/tex], where a = 6, and c = 10. Then, b = 8.

But, we still don't know the length from the center of the circle to the point of intersection (the red segment in the figure). Let's just call it x. Still, using Pythagoren theorem, to find x, we have two equations:

[tex]6^2 + x^2 = r^2[/tex][tex]10^2 + r^2 = (8 + x)^2[/tex]

Simplifying equation 2, [tex]100 + r^2 = 64 + 16x +x^2\\x^2 = 100 + r^2 - 64 - 16x[/tex]

Substituting this into equation 1,

36 + 100 + r^2 - 64 - 16x = r^2

Cancelling r^2,

72 = 16x

x = 4.5

Then, from 6^2 + x^2 = r^2,

r^2 = 36 + 20.25

r = 7.5 inches

26. 1. A 20 inches long secant intersects with a tangent at a point outside the circle. 15 inches of the secant are inside the circle. How long is the tangent?​


Tangent-Secant Theorem

Formula:BC² = BD • BA

Let:Point of Intersection = BPoint of Tangency = CA secant intersects a circle at exactly two points

Point 1 = APoint 2 = D

BD = 20 - 15BD = 5

BA = 20

BC² = 5 • 20BC² = 100BC = 10

The length of the line tangent to the circle is 10 inches
