9. Science Explain the presence of gravity and friction in the following: Swimmers used head caps and skin-tight suits.​


Gravity and friction are present in the scenario of swimmers using head caps and skin-tight suits in the following ways:

Gravity: Gravity is the force that pulls all objects with mass toward each other. In the case of swimmers, gravity is present as it affects the motion and acceleration of the swimmers as they dive, swim, and change directions in the water. The swimmer's body weight, along with the gravitational pull of the earth, can impact their performance in the water. Wearing head caps and skin-tight suits can help reduce the resistance caused by gravity by reducing the amount of drag and increasing the swimmer's speed and efficiency in the water.Friction: Friction is the force that opposes motion between two surfaces that are in contact. In the case of swimmers wearing head caps and skin-tight suits, friction is present between the surface of the suit and the water. This friction can cause drag, slowing down the swimmer's movements and making it more difficult to move through the water. By wearing head caps and skin-tight suits, swimmers can reduce the amount of friction between their bodies and the water, allowing them to move more smoothly and quickly through the water. This can help improve their performance in swimming competitions.

15. what is specific area in the laboratory is best suited in culturing microorganisms to avoid contamination?​


Answer:

The best specific area in the laboratory to culture microorganisms and avoid contamination is the laminar flow hood or biological safety cabinet. This equipment provides a sterile and controlled environment that prevents the entry of contaminants while also protecting the user and the environment from exposure to the microorganisms being cultured. It uses a HEPA filter to remove airborne particles and microorganisms, ensuring that the work area remains sterile.
