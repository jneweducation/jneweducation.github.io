6. (EVALUATE) I. Evaluating learning Answer the following questions. 1. What is Biodiversity? 2. What is carrying capacity? 3. How is population growth related to carrying capacity? 4. What is the difference between predation and competition? 5. What is the difference between density dependent and density independent limiting factors?​


Biodiversity refers to the variety of life forms in a particular ecosystem, including the number of species, genetic diversity, and ecosystem diversity.Carrying capacity is the maximum population size that an ecosystem can sustainably support over a period of time, given its available resources and environmental conditions.Population growth is related to carrying capacity in that as a population grows, it approaches the carrying capacity of its ecosystem. When a population exceeds its carrying capacity, it may experience a decline in resources and an increase in mortality rates.Predation is the interaction between two species in which one species (the predator) kills and eats the other species (the prey), while competition is the interaction between two or more species that require the same limited resource.Density-dependent limiting factors are factors that become more severe as population density increases, such as disease, predation, and competition. Density-independent limiting factors are factors that affect a population regardless of its size, such as natural disasters and climate change.

[tex] \Large \mathbb{PROBLEM:} [/tex]

[tex] \textsf{Evaluate the following limit:} [/tex]

[tex]\Large \qquad \displaystyle \sf \lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sqrt{\cos x} - \sqrt[3]{\cos x}}{\sin^{2} x} \\ \: [/tex]

[tex] \Large \mathbb{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

[tex] \quad \displaystyle \sf \lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sqrt{\cos x} - \sqrt[3]{\cos x}}{\sin^{2} x} [/tex]

[tex] \textsf{We observe that in the present form, the limit} [/tex] [tex] \textsf{becomes }\frac{0}{0}\textsf{ which is indeterminate.}[/tex]

[tex]\sf \quad Let\ \sqrt[6]{\cos x} = u \longrightarrow \cos x = u^6 [/tex]

[tex] \implies \sf \sqrt{\cos x} = \sqrt{u^6} = u^3 [/tex]

[tex] \implies \sf \sqrt[3]{\cos x} = \sqrt[3]{u^6} = u^2 [/tex]

[tex] \implies \sf \sin^2 x = 1 - \cos^2 x = 1 - u^{12} [/tex]

[tex] \textsf{As x approaches the value 0, u approaches the} [/tex] [tex] \textsf{value 1. The given limit becomes} [/tex]

[tex]\quad \displaystyle \sf \lim_{u \to 1} \dfrac{u^3 - u^2}{1 - u^{12}} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \sf = \lim_{u \to 1} \dfrac{u^2(u - 1)}{(1 - u^6)(1 + u^6)} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \sf = \lim_{u \to 1} \dfrac{u^2(u - 1)}{(1 - u^3)(1 + u^3)(1 + u^6)} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \sf = \lim_{u \to 1} \dfrac{-u^2\cancel{(1-u)}}{\cancel{(1 - u)}(1 + u + u^2)(1 + u^3)(1 + u^6)} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \sf = \lim_{u \to 1} \dfrac{-u^2}{(1 + u + u^2)(1 + u^3)(1 + u^6)} [/tex]

[tex] \sf = \dfrac{-1}{(1 + 1 + 1)(1 + 1)(1 + 1)} [/tex]

[tex] \sf = \dfrac{-1}{3(2)(2)} [/tex]

[tex] = \boxed{\sf -\dfrac{1}{12}} \Longleftarrow\textsf{Answer}[/tex]
