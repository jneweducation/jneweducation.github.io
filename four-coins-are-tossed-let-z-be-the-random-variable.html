One head and one tail
One tail and one head
Two tails
The random variable Z represents the number of heads that occur. Therefore, the values of Z can be 0, 1, or 2.

To construct the probability distribution of Z, we need to determine the probability of each value of Z occurring.

When two coins are tossed, each coin has two possible outcomes (heads or tails). Therefore, there are a total of 2 x 2 = 4 possible outcomes when two coins are tossed.

Two heads: There is only one way for both coins to land heads up, so the probability of this occurring is 1/4.

One head and one tail: There are two ways for one coin to land heads up and the other coin to land tails up (HT or TH), so the probability of this occurring is 2/4 or 1/2.

One tail and one head: This is the same as the previous outcome (HT or TH), so the probability of this occurring is also 1/2.

Two tails: There is only one way for both coins to land tails up, so the probability of this occurring is 1/4.

Therefore, the probability distribution of Z is:

Z = 0, P(Z = 0) = 1/4
