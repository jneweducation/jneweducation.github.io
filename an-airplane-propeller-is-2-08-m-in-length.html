We can then write the following equation:

(78 meters) / (x cm) = (90 meters) / (36 cm)

2880 = 90x

x = 32 cm

18. A small engine causes a 1.2 kg model airplane to acceleration at a rate of 5 m/s^2. What is the net force on the airplane?


Explanation:

To calculate the net force on the airplane, we can use Newton's second law of motion, which states that the net force acting on an object is equal to the product of its mass and acceleration.

Mathematically, this can be expressed as:

net force = mass x acceleration

In this case, the mass of the model airplane is 1.2 kg, and the acceleration is 5 m/s^2. Therefore, the net force on the airplane can be calculated as:

net force = 1.2 kg x 5 m/s^2

net force = 6 N

So, the net force acting on the airplane is 6 N.

We can notice that the differences are not constant, so the sequence is not arithmetic. However, we can look for the differences between the differences:

19 - 7 = 12

37 - 19 = 18

61 - 37 = 24

We can observe that the second differences are constant, which means that the sequence is a quadratic sequence. To find the quadratic rule, we can use the formula:

an = a1 + (n-1)d + (n-1)(n-2)/2 * c

where:

an = the nth term in the sequence

a1 = the first term in the sequence

d = the common difference between the terms (which is equal to the first difference)

c = the constant difference between the first differences (which is equal to the second difference divided by 2)

a1 = 2

d = 7

c = 6

Therefore, the rule for the sequence is:

an = 2 + (n-1)7 + (n-1)(n-2)/2 * 6

an = 3n^2 - n
