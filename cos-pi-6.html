PYTHAGOREAN IDENTITIES

If sin θ = 21/29, 0<θ<π/2 find cos(2θ)

One of the Pythagorean Identity is

sin² θ + cos² θ = 1

Derive the Identity to find the value of cos θ.

cos² θ = 1 - sin² θ

cos² θ = 1 - (21/29)²

cos² θ = 1 - (441/841)

cos² θ = 400/841

√cos² θ = √400/841

cos θ = 20/29

By using the double angle identity of cosine.

cos 2θ = cos² θ - sin² θ

cos 2θ = (20/29)² - (21/29)²

cos 2θ = (400/841) - (441/841)

cos 2θ = (400 - 441)/841

cos 2θ = -41/841
