4. The shunt field winding of a dc motor has a resistance of 240 ohms. calculate the power loss when the voltage across it is 120 vdc


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power dissipated (lost) by a resistance is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{V^2}{R}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

V = voltage

R = resistance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the power dissipated

[tex]P = \dfrac{V^2}{R}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{(\text{120 V})^2}{\text{240 ohm}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{60 W}}[/tex]

Therefore, the power loss is 60 W.

What is armature efficiency?

The ratio of the output power to the input power is the simplest definition of efficiency. Let R represent the circuit's overall resistance (including the brush contact resistance, at series winding resistance, inter-pole winding resistance and compensating winding resistance). The ratio of output power to input power is the definition of a DC motor's efficiency.

The majority of electric motors are made to operate between 50% and 100% of their rated load. Typically, maximum efficiency is within 75% of the rated load. Therefore, the allowable load range for a 10-horsepower (hp) motor is between 5 and 10 hp; its peak efficiency is at 7.5 hp. Below roughly 50% load, a motor's efficiency tends to decline significantly. The range of good efficiency, however, differs for each motor and has a tendency to cover a wider range for larger motors.

When a motor is in the range where efficiency dramatically decreases with decreasing load, it is said to be underloaded. As load diminishes, the power factor tends to decline more quickly but less abruptly than efficiency.

29. what is the general principle of dc generator? ​


Answer:

DC Generator working principal is based on Faraday's Laws of electromagnetic induction.

Explanation:

According to Faraday’s laws of electromagnetic induction, whenever a conductor is placed in a varying magnetic field (OR a conductor is moved in a magnetic field), an emf (electromotive force) gets induced in the conductor. The magnitude of induced emf can be calculated from the emf equation of dc generator. If the conductor is provided with a closed path, the induced current will circulate within the path. In a DC generator, field coils produce an electromagnetic field and the armature conductors are rotated into the field. Thus, an electromagnetically induced emf is generated in the armature conductors. The direction of induced current is given by Fleming’s right hand rule.


30. A 230 v shunt generator has an armature circuit resistance of 0.05 ohm and shunt field resistance of 57.5 ohms. when the generator operates at rated voltage it has a total core and mechanical losses of 1880 w. at what load does the generator operate so as to achieve maximum efficiency


The load at which a shunt generator with 230V, armature circuit resistance of 0.05 ohms, shunt field resistance of 57.5 ohms, and total core and mechanical losses of 1880 Watts will also achieve the maximum efficiency at the same time is 53.51 kW.

To solve this problem, we first have to find the field current.

[tex]I_{field}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{230V}{57.5 ohms}[/tex] = 4A

At maximum efficiency, the variable losses are equal to the constant losses.

[tex](I_{armature})^2R_{armature} = (I_{field})^2 R_{field}[/tex] + 1880 Watts

[tex](I_{armature})^2R_{armature} =[/tex] ( [tex]4^{2}[/tex] x 57.5 ) + 1880 Watts

[tex]I_{armature}[/tex] = 236.64 A

Then, we have to get the value of the load current.

[tex]I_{load}= I_{armature} - I_{field}[/tex] = 236.64-4 = 232.64 A

Now, we can get the load in kW by multiplying the load current by the given rated voltage.

P= 232.64 A x 230 V = 53.5072 x [tex]10^{3}[/tex] Watts
