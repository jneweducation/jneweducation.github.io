1. General arrangement drawings present the overall composition of an object such as a building.

2. A layout drawing depicts design development requirements it is similar to a detail assembly or installation drawing except that it presents pictorial notational or dimensional data to the extent necessary to convey the design solution used in preparing other engineering drawings.

3. Erection drawings are used to guide the steel erector on the construction site as to where and how to erect the fabricated steel members.

4. The purpose of an assembly drawings is item identification labeling the sequence for assembly and sometimes to even mention standard requirements.

5. To document a process for better understanding quality control and training of employees and to standardize a process for optimal efficiency and repeatability.
