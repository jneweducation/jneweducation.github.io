Answer:

The issue raised in the background of the study highlights a common problem faced by ambulant vendors in the informal economy: balancing customer demands for low-priced products with the need to maintain profitability. This is a complex issue that involves multiple factors such as production costs, market demand, and competitive pressures.

One contributing factor is the high demand for

goods, which can make it difficult for ambulant vendors to offer low prices and still make a profit. This is because they often operate on small profit margins, which can be easily eroded by the need to lower pricing to meet customer demands.

Another factor is the rise of the Veblen good, which refers to a product where demand and price are positively correlated. Customers purchasing such products do not base their decisions solely on the utility of the product but also on its perceived status value. Ambulant vendors may face increasing difficulty in pricing these products because of this phenomenon.

To address this issue, ambulant vendors may need to employ creative pricing strategies such as volume discounts or bundling of products. They may also need to consider ways to reduce their production costs while maintaining product quality. Moreover, customers could be educated about the true costs of production, which can help them understand why prices may not always be as low as they desire.

The issue raised in the background of the study highlights the need for sustainable and ethical pricing practices that balance the needs of both vendors and customers. By addressing this issue, we can help support the informal economy and promote economic stability for all stakeholders involved.
