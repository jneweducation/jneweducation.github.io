3. "What copper-cored cable is surrounded by a heavy shielding? a. coaxial cable c. optical fiber b. fiber optics d. twisted pair"


The copper-cored cable surrounded by a heavy shielding is Coaxial Cable.  

What is a Coaxial Cable?

A Coaxial cable or coax is a type of electrical cable that has an inner conductor surrounded by a tubular insulating layer and a tubular conducting shield. It also conducts electrical signals using an inner conductor surrounded by an insulating layer and all enclosed by a shield, most likely one to four layers of woven metallic braid and metallic tape.  

What it is used for?

Coaxial cable is a versatile transmission medium, used in a wide variety of applications. It is used to transmit lines for radio frequency, long-distance telephone transmission, local area network, and cable television distribution.

11. copper cabling used for telephone communication?


The Copper cabling used for telephone communication is called Twisted Pair Cables.

Twisted Pair cables are type of cable that is composed of two insulated copper wires which are twisted to each other. The reason why they are like that is to minimize electromagnetic induction between them. These cables are also used in home networks. Twisted pair cables are also cheaper than other cable types and easier to set up.  

There are two types of Twisted Pair Cables such as :

Shielded Twisted Pair - STP or Shielded Twisted Pair are types of Twisted Pair Cables that has covers. STPs are common in large network that is used in a business area.  Unshielded Twisted Pair - is the type of Twisted pair cables which has no coverings.
