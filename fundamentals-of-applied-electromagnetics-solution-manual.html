5. how to apply electromagnetic waves in medicine​


Answer:

There are several ways in which electromagnetic waves can be used in medicine. Low-energy electromagnetic waves, such as magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), can be used to create images of internal structures of the body. Pulsed electromagnetic field (PEMF) therapy is used to stimulate tissue healing and reduce inflammation. High-frequency diathermy uses microwaves to generate heat, which is then used to help reduce swelling and kill cancerous cells. Electromagnetic radiation can also be used to treat conditions such as incontinence and depression, since some frequencies can interact with brain activity. In addition, therapeutic ultrasound is used to treat musculoskeletal issues such as tendinitis and bursitis.

Answer:

Electromagnetic waves have a variety of applications in medicine,

including:

X-rays: X-rays use electromagnetic waves to produce images of bones and tissues. X-rays can help diagnose bone fractures, tumors, and other conditions.

Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): MRI uses strong magnetic fields and electromagnetic waves to produce images of internal organs and tissues. MRI can help diagnose conditions such as tumors, strokes, and multiple sclerosis.

Ultrasound: Ultrasound uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of internal organs and tissues. Ultrasound is commonly used to monitor fetal development during pregnancy.

Radiation therapy: Radiation therapy uses high-energy electromagnetic waves to destroy cancer cells. Radiation therapy can help shrink tumors and kill cancer cells.

Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT): ECT uses electromagnetic waves to induce seizures in the brain. ECT is used to treat severe depression and other psychiatric conditions.

Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS): TMS uses electromagnetic waves to stimulate nerve cells in the brain. TMS is used to treat depression, migraines, and other conditions.

Electromagnetic therapy: Electromagnetic therapy uses electromagnetic fields to stimulate healing in tissues and bones. Electromagnetic therapy can help heal fractures, reduce inflammation, and relieve pain.

16. how does electromagnetism is applied in refrigerator?


When a wire carries an electrical current, a magnetic field forms around the wire, like a hula hoop around a waist. The field reverses when the current reverses. When the wire is turned into a coil, the fields of all the turns add up to form one big, combined magnetic field. This charged coil is called a "solenoid," though usually solenoid refers to a mechanical part that has such a coiled wire.

When an iron core is inside the coil of wire, the coil's magnetic field aligns the spinning electrons of the iron atoms, greatly increasing the strength of the field. When the core itself is a permanent magnet, the coil's field can either attract or repel it, depending on the direction of current in the coil.

Explanation:

Electromagnetism plays a crucial role in the functioning of a television. Television sets use a combination of electromagnetic waves and electronic circuits to produce and display images.

Firstly, the television set uses an electromagnetic wave to receive a broadcast signal. The signal is transmitted as an electromagnetic wave through the air and is picked up by the antenna of the television. The antenna then converts the electromagnetic waves into electrical signals that are transmitted to the television set.

Inside the television set, the electrical signals are amplified and converted into a form that can be used to create an image. This is done by using an electron gun to shoot a beam of electrons onto a screen coated with phosphorescent material. As the beam hits the screen, it causes the phosphorescent material to emit light, creating the image that we see on the screen.

The electron gun is powered by an electromagnetic coil that generates a magnetic field. This magnetic field controls the path of the electrons, ensuring that they hit the correct spots on the screen to create the image.

Additionally, the television set uses electromagnets to control the movement of the electron beam. These electromagnets are located near the electron gun and create a magnetic field that controls the movement of the electrons. By adjusting the strength of the magnetic field, the television set can control the brightness and color of the image.

In summary, electromagnetism plays a critical role in the functioning of a television set. From receiving the broadcast signal to creating the image, electromagnetism is used at every step of the process.

20. how to apply electromagnetic waves in transportation ​


Answer:

Electromagnetic waves have been used in various transportation technologies, such as in wireless communication, radar systems, and navigation systems. Here are some specific examples of how electromagnetic waves are applied in transportation:

1.)Wireless communication: Electromagnetic waves, specifically radio waves, are used in wireless communication technologies such as cellular networks, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth. These technologies allow communication between transportation systems, such as communication between trains and control centers, or communication between vehicles on the road.

2.)Radar systems: Radar systems use electromagnetic waves to detect and locate objects, such as other vehicles or obstacles, in the path of a transportation system. This technology is widely used in aviation and marine transportation for navigation and collision avoidance.

3.)Navigation systems: Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNSS), such as GPS (Global Positioning System), use electromagnetic waves to determine the location of a vehicle. This technology is widely used in aviation, marine, and ground transportation for navigation.

4.)Electromagnetic propulsion: Electromagnetic waves can also be used for propulsion in transportation. Electromagnetic propulsion technology uses magnetic fields to propel a vehicle, such as in the case of maglev (magnetic levitation) trains.

Overall, electromagnetic waves have a significant impact on transportation by providing efficient communication, navigation, and propulsion solutions that enhance the safety and speed of transportation systems.

Answer:

Electromagnetic waves have a variety of applications in transportation,

including:

Radar: Radar uses electromagnetic waves to detect objects and determine their distance and speed. Radar is used in aviation, marine navigation, and automotive collision avoidance systems.

GPS: Global Positioning System (GPS) technology uses a network of satellites that emit electromagnetic signals to determine the position of vehicles. GPS is commonly used in navigation systems in cars, boats, and airplanes.

Wireless communication: Electromagnetic waves are used to transmit data wirelessly between vehicles, such as in car-to-car communication systems. This technology can be used to provide warnings about accidents, road hazards, or traffic jams.

Electric and magnetic levitation: Electromagnetic fields are used in maglev trains to lift the train off the tracks and propel it forward. This technology is used in high-speed trains in Japan and China.

Radio frequency identification (RFID): RFID technology uses electromagnetic waves to transmit data wirelessly between a tag and a reader. RFID tags can be attached to vehicles to track their movement and provide information about their location, speed, and maintenance needs.

Electric vehicles: Electric vehicles use electromagnetic fields to charge their batteries wirelessly through a process called inductive charging. The vehicle is parked over a charging pad, which generates an electromagnetic field that is picked up by a coil in the vehicle and converted into electrical energy.
