2. A flagpole is at the top of a building. From a point 420 ft from the base of the building, an engineer finds that the angles of elevation on the top of the building and the top of the flagpole are 18° and 25°, respectively. Calculate the height of the flagpole.


Answer:

h≈59.38ft

Step-by-step explanation:

The height of the building (x) is:

tan(18°)=x/420

x=420*tan(18°)

x≈136.47 ft

The height of the building plus height of the flagpole is:

tan(25°)=y/420

y=420*tan(25°)

y≈195.8 ft

Hence, the height of the flagpole is:

h=y-x

h=195.85-136.47

h≈59.38ft

7. A flagpole stands on the edge of the top of the building. At a horizontal distance of 120 meters, the angle of elevation of the top of the pole is 47°12′ and that of the foot of the pole is 39°18′. Find the height of the pole to the nearest tenth of a meter.


Answer:  ≈31.4 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

It helps to draw a basic diagram of the situation (see picture). Then do the following...

1. Find the decimal equivalents for the angle measures.

  12/60=0.2, so 47°12'=47.2°

  18/60=0.3, so 39°18′=39.3°

2. Set up and solve an equation to find the height of the building (b).

    tan(39.3°)=b/120 ⇒ b=120*tan(39.3°)≈98.22m (since final answer should be rounded to nearest tenth, best to only round to hundredths while still calculating)

3. Set up an equation that represents the total height of the building and flagpole (f), and solve for f.

   tan(47.2°)=(b+f)/120 ⇒ f=120*tan(47.2°)-b

                                        f≈129.59-98.22=31.37m

  You can also use the exact expressions to calculate:

     f=120*tan(47.2°)-120*tan(39.3°)≈31.37m

4. Round answer to nearest tenth: 31.37m≈31.4m

The Angle of Elevation is the angle between a horizontal line and the line joining the observer's eye to some object above the horizontal line.

28. 1. A student standing 100 meters from the base of a flagpole observed that the angle of elevation of the top of the flagpole is 60°, how high up the building will it reach? 2 A stone is placed 50 feet from​


Answer:

To solve the problem, I can use the trigonometric function tangent, which relates the opposite side to the adjacent side of a right triangle. In this case, we can let x be the height of the flagpole, and 100 be the adjacent side (the distance from the student to the base of the flagpole).

We know that the angle of elevation from the student to the top of the flagpole is 60 degrees. This means that the tangent of 60 degrees is equal to the height of the flagpole divided by the distance from the student to the flagpole:

tan(60) = x/100

To solve for x, we can multiply both sides by 100:

100 tan(60) = x

Using a calculator, we can find that tan(60) is approximately 1.732, so:

100 × 1.732 ≈ 173.2

Therefore, the height of the flagpole is approximately 173.2 meters
