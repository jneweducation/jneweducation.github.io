22. 1. Based on the accomplished chart diagram above, what is the importance of knowing the location of faults and plate boundaries?​


Answer:

Earth’s Tectonic Plates

Seafloor and continents move around on Earth’s surface, but what is actually moving? What portion of the Earth makes up the “plates” in plate tectonics? This question was also answered because of technology developed during war times – in this case, the Cold War. The plates are made up of the lithosphere.

During the 1950s and early 1960s, scientists set up seismograph networks to see if enemy nations were testing atomic bombs. These seismographs also recorded all of the earthquakes around the planet. The seismic records could be used to locate an earthquake’s epicenter, the point on Earth’s surface directly above the place where the earthquake occurs.

Earthquake epicenters outline the plates. Mid-ocean ridges, trenches, and large faults mark the edges of the plates, and this is where earthquakes occur (figure 1).

The lithosphere is divided into a dozen major and several minor plates (figure 2). The plates’ edges can be drawn by connecting the dots that mark earthquakes’ epicenters. A single plate can be made of all oceanic lithosphere or all continental lithosphere, but nearly all plates are made of a combination of both.

25. V. THE COORDINATE SYSTEM: Using the graphic organizer 'Sequence Chart', identify the sequence on how to locate a certain location with the use of coordinates. Locating Places Using Coordinates L. THE PHILIPPINE MAP PHILIPPINES ** MAD T 130/1​


Sequence Chart:

Step 1: Determine the coordinates of the location you want to locate.

Step 2: Locate the vertical line or longitude of the location on the map.

Step 3: Trace the horizontal line or latitude of the location on the map.

Step 4: Find the point where the vertical line and the horizontal line intersect.

Step 5: Identify the name of the location on the map based on its proximity to the point of intersection.

Example: If the coordinates of the location you want to locate are 14° 35' N latitude and 121° 0' E longitude, you can follow these steps to locate it on the Philippine map:

Step 1: Determine the coordinates of the location - 14° 35' N latitude and 121° 0' E longitude.

Step 2: Locate the vertical line or longitude of the location on the map, which is 121° E.

Step 3: Trace the horizontal line or latitude of the location on the map, which is 14° 35' N.

Step 4: Find the point where the vertical line and the horizontal line intersect.

Step 5: Identify the name of the location on the map based on its proximity to the point of intersection, which is Manila, the capital city of the Philippines.
