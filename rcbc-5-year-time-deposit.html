General Annuity

In a general annuity, payments are made at the start of the payment intervals. The payment and compounding frequencies are not the same. The initial payment occurs on the same date at the start of the annuity. The last payment occurs one payment interval before the end of the annuity. Thus, a general annuity is where the payments do not coincide with the interest periods

About bank loan interest

it is important for you to understand in advance what are the types of loan interest rates. This is because each type of credit interest has a different calculation mechanism.

Fixed Interest

As the name implies, fixed interest is a type of loan interest rate whose percentage value is fixed. In other words, the amount of interest will not change during the loan period even until the maturity date. Generally, the bank will provide information about this at the time of making the agreement.

For example, if the interest set by the Bank is 10%. So, during the installment period, how to calculate the loan interest per month will remain 10%. The advantage is that you don't have to worry about increasing interest rates in the future.

Because, you will only carry out installment payment obligations of the same value. However, if interest rates decrease on the market, then inevitably you still have to pay off the installments even though the amount is higher.

Floating Flowers

Then, there are also types of floating or floating interest rates. If the fixed interest value will not change, then the floating interest type is the opposite. Yes, the amount of this interest percentage will continue to change during the installment payment period. This is influenced by the dynamics of interest rates on the market.

So, if the value of interest rates on the market is rising, then your loan interest will also increase as well. And vice versa, if the value of interest rates on the market is falling, then your loan interest will also fall. That way, you don't have to worry about the risk of interest being too high or too low.

✏️PRESENT VALUE===============================

Problem: Mr. Quijano decided to sell their farm and to deposit the fund in a bank. After computing the interest, they learned that they may withdraw P480,000 yearly for 8 years starting at the end of 6 years when it is time for him to retire. How much is the fund deposited if the interest rate is 5% converted annually?

Solution: Using the Present Value of Deferred Annuity Formula to find the present value if he sell their farm and he deposit it the fund into the bank.

Given:

[tex]R = ₱480\text,000[/tex][tex]r = 5\% = 0.05[/tex][tex]t = 8[/tex][tex]m = 1[/tex][tex]i = \frac{0.05}{1} = 0.05 \\ [/tex][tex]n = (1)(8) = 8[/tex][tex]k = 5[/tex][tex]P = \:? [/tex]

[tex] \begin{aligned}& \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\& \small\boxed{PV = R\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + i)^{ \text -(k + n)} }{i} \bigg] -R\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + i)^{ \text -k} }{i} \bigg] } \end{aligned}[/tex]

- Use the scientific calculator to solve this.

[tex] \begin{aligned}\small{PV = 480\text, 000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + 0.05)^{ \text -(5 + 8)} }{0.05} \bigg] -480\text,000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + 0.05)^{ \text -5} }{0.05} \bigg] } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{aligned}\small{PV = 480\text, 000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1.05)^{ \text -13} }{0.05} \bigg] -480\text,000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1.05)^{ \text -5} }{0.05} \bigg] } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]PV = 2\text,430\text,766.232[/tex]

- Therefore, the present value of fund deposited is:

[tex] \large \rm Present \: Value = \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: ₱2\text,430\text,766.23 \: }}[/tex]===============================
