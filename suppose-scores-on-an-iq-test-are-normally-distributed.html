Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the properties of the normal distribution and the standard normal distribution. The standard normal distribution is a normal distribution with a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1.

Let X be the IQ scores of the applicants. Then X ~ N(110, 10^2).

To find the probability that the applicant will score between 100 and 120, we need to standardize the values using the formula:

Z = (X - μ) / σ

where μ is the mean and σ is the standard deviation. So, we have:

Z1 = (100 - 110) / 10 = -1

Z2 = (120 - 110) / 10 = 1

Now, we can find the probability using a standard normal distribution table or calculator. Using a table, we get:

P(-1 < Z < 1) = 0.6827

Therefore, the probability that the applicant will score between 100 and 120 is 0.6827.

To find the percentage of applicants getting scores less than 90, we need to standardize the value of 90 using the formula above:

Z = (90 - 110) / 10 = -2

Using a standard normal distribution table, we get:

P(Z < -2) = 0.0228

Therefore, the percentage of the applicants getting scores less than 90 is 2.28%.

To find the scores obtained by the middle 60% of the applicants, we need to find the Z-scores corresponding to the lower and upper percentiles. The lower percentile is (100 - 60) / 2 = 20, and the upper percentile is 100 - 20 = 80.

Using a standard normal distribution table, we find that the Z-score corresponding to the 20th percentile is -0.84, and the Z-score corresponding to the 80th percentile is 0.

One such test is the Stanford-Binet Intelligence Scales (SBIS), which assumes that IQ scores are normally distributed with a mean of 100 and a standard deviation of 16. A. Find the proportion of people in the general population with IQs between 90 to 110. • B. What is the lowest IQ score in the SBIS that would qualify you for MENSA membership? • C. Leonor has an IQ score of 150. What proportion of the general population have IQ scores above Leonor's IQ score?


Step-by-step explanation:

A. To find the proportion of people in the general population with IQs between 90 to 110, we need to standardize the IQ scores using the formula:

z = (x - μ) / σ

where:

x = the IQ score

μ = the mean IQ score (100)

σ = the standard deviation (16)

For x = 90:

z = (90 - 100) / 16 = -0.625

For x = 110:

z = (110 - 100) / 16 = 0.625

Using a standard normal distribution table or calculator, we can find the proportion of people with IQs between -0.625 and 0.625:

P(-0.625 < z < 0.625) = 0.4129 - 0.2334 = 0.1795

Therefore, about 17.95% of the general population have IQs between 90 to 110.

B. To qualify for MENSA membership, one needs to have an IQ in the top 2%. We can find the IQ score that corresponds to the top 2% by using the inverse normal distribution (z-score) table.

The z-score that corresponds to the top 2% is approximately 2.05. Therefore, we can solve for x in the following equation:

2.05 = (x - 100) / 16

x - 100 = 2.05 * 16

x = 132.8

So, the lowest IQ score in the SBIS that would qualify for MENSA membership is approximately 133.

C. To find the proportion of the general population with IQ scores above Leonor's IQ score of 150, we can again standardize the IQ score:

z = (150 - 100) / 16 = 3.125

Using a standard normal distribution table or calculator, we can find the proportion of people with IQs above 3.125:

P(z > 3.125) = 1 - 0.9992 = 0.0008

Therefore, only about 0.08% of the general population have IQ scores above Leonor's IQ

29. ACTIVITY 5. Read the following problems carefully. Use empirical rule to answer each question. 1. IQ scores of the ALS students in the Division of Quezon are normally distributed with a mean of 110 and a standard deviation of 10. What percent of the distribution falls within the IQ scores of 100 to 130?​


Answer:

81.85%

Step-by-step explanation:

The empirical rule (also known as the 68-95-99.7 rule) states that for a normal distribution:

Approximately 68% of the data falls within one standard deviation of the mean.

Approximately 95% of the data falls within two standard deviations of the mean.

Approximately 99.7% of the data falls within three standard deviations of the mean.

To find the percentage of the distribution that falls within the IQ scores of 100 to 130, we first need to calculate the z-scores for these values using the formula:

z = (x - μ) / σ

where x is the IQ score, μ is the mean, and σ is the standard deviation.

For an IQ score of 100:

z = (100 - 110) / 10 = -1

For an IQ score of 130:

z = (130 - 110) / 10 = 2

We can then use a z-table or calculator to find the percentage of the distribution that falls between these two z-scores.

Using a standard normal distribution table, the percentage of the distribution that falls between z = -1 and z = 2 is approximately 81.85%.

Therefore, we can say that approximately 81.85% of the ALS students in the Division of Quezon have IQ scores between 100 and 130.
