1. What is the primary source of revenues of service companies? a. Performance of service b. Sale of equipment c. Sale of finished goods d. Sale of component parts


Answer: The answer is letter a. Performance of service

Explanation:

As a service company they provide services from the customers. Performing services is their main source of income or revenue.

Performance of service is frequently offer healthy profit margins that help compensate for the declining revenues and profitability in equipment sales. Moreover, services might stabilize cash flows and provide increased visibility in revenue streams, a key benefit in economic slumps. Extending the service business thus promises greater firm revenues and profits.

Service companies must create customers’ willingness to pay for their service offerings. Second, they must manage the costs of the service provision to turn service revenues into profits. Revenues and profits are interdependent, but they do not necessarily evolve in the same direction.

Moving into services typically represents an investment, which initially might lead to lower profitability levels for companies focusing on services. Over time though, service companies might increase the rate at which profits grow, causing companies with extensive service companies offerings to realize financial advantages.

When a company makes revenues from its operations, it must be recorded in the general ledger and then reported on the income statement every reporting period. According to generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP), there are two criteria the company must meet before it can record revenue on its books. The first criterion must be a critical event that triggered the transaction process, and the amount that is to be collected from that transaction is measurable within a certain degree of reliability. 27. Statement I – Like merchandising companies, manufacturers earn sales revenues. Statement II – The primary source of revenues of manufacturing companies is the production of goods. a. Both statements are true. b. Only Statement I is true. c. Only Statement II is true. d. Both statements are false.


The correct answer to your question is a. Both statements are true.

By looking into the activity both types of business are engaged in, we can correctly determine the validity of the two statements. Now, let's take a closer look at why these statements are correct by defining these two types of business.

Manufacturing Business

This business is mainly involved in the production of goods or products. To produce these products, they need the following:

raw materialsother goods (also known as producer goods)labor (may either come from people or machines)factory overhead such as electricity, water, rent

They product they produce may either be producer goods or consumer goods.

Producer goods are products that are used to create another product. Examples are be nails, glue, paint, fabric, thread, and cardboard.Consumer goods are products that are bought by consumers and not makers of other products. Examples are clothes, hamburgers, cellphones, bags, and books.

Manufacturers not only create products, they also have to make sure that their products are sold. To sell these products, they may go through one several different channels of distribution.

Some manufacturers also offer services. Usually these service are provided in conjunction or after the sale of a product. For example, a sewing machine manufacturer may provide services to their customers because when these machines break down or malfunction, they are the ones who know best how to troubleshoot and repair it. But do remember, services are not their primary source of revenue.
