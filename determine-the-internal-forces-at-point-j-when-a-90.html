8. the internal energy of a system is initially 35 j the sift in the certificate of work what is assistant final internal energy when the total of 26 j of energy is added to the system by heat​


Answer:

The system's final internal energy : 47 J

Further explanation

The laws of thermodynamics 1 state that: energy can be changed but cannot be destroyed or created

Energy owned by the system is expressed as internal energy (U)

The equation is:

\begin{gathered}\rm \boxed{\bold{\Delta U=Q+W}}\\\\\Delta U=Uf-Ui(f=final,i=initial)\end{gathered}

ΔU=Q+W

ΔU=Uf−Ui(f=final,i=initial)

This internal energy can change if it absorbs heat Q (U> 0), or releases heat (U <0). Or the internal energy can change if the system does work or accepts work (W)

The sign rules for heat and work are set as follows:

The system receives heat, Q +

The system releases heat, Q -

The system does work, W -

The system accepts work, W +

Heat can be calculated using the formula:

\rm Q=m\times c\times \Delta TQ=m×c×ΔT

The internal energy of a system is initially 35 J(Ui). The system does 34 J of work. 46 J of energy is added to the system by heat, then

The system receives heat = 46 J

The system does work, W = -34 J

the change in the internal energy, ∆E/∆U, of the system

\begin{gathered}\rm \Delta E/\Delta U=Q+W\\\\Uf-Ui=46 -34\\\\Uf-35 = 12\\\\Uf = 35 + 12\\\\Uf = \boxed{47\:J}\end{gathered}

ΔE/ΔU=Q+W

Uf−Ui=46−34

Uf−35=12

Uf=35+12

Uf=

47J

Publisher Summary

By evaluating the effect of erosion factors on the erosion processes, the removal and transport of eroded material by exogenous forces can be quantified. Both theoretical and empirical methods are used to specify each factor's contribution and describe the course of the erosion processes. The problem studied is very complex and different methods afford different levels of accuracy. The methods used include, in essence, the mathematic expression of the erosion process, the application of empirical formulae and the simulation of erosion. Water erosion takes place during surface runoff, including both the runoff down the soil surface and runoff concentrated in a river network. Areal surface runoff starts as a coherent layer of water moving down the soil surface. Exposed to the unevenness of the soil surface, this layer breaks into separate runoff lines at a certain distance from the place where the runoff started. In analyzing the areal runoff, two assumptions are used: first, the layer of water running down the surface is coherent and second, its thickness increases with the distance from the start of the runoff. The erosion processes can be studied on the basis of the dependence of velocity or tangential stress on the distance from the origin of runoff.

18. The internalize energy of a system is initially 35 J. The system does 34 J of work whats is the system final internal energy when a total of 46 J of energy is added to the system by heat?

Problem:

The internalize energy of a system is initially 35 J. The system does 34 J of work whats is the system final internal energy when a total of 46 J of energy is added to the system by heat?

Given Data:

Q (heat added) = 46 J

W (work done) = 34 J

ΔU (change in the internal energy ) = ? unknown

internal energy initial = 35 J

internal energy final = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve this problem using the formula ΔU = Q - W

ΔU = Q - W

     = 46 J - 34 J

ΔU = 12 J

Final internal energy = 35 J + 12 J

                                    = 47 J

Answer:

The final internal energy is 47 J
