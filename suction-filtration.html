17. what is slab suction?


Answer:

Slab suction forces are one of the major plate tectonic driving forces. Slab suction occurs when a subducting slab drives flow in the nearby mantle. This flow then exerts shear tractions on nearby plates. This driving force is important when the slabs (or portions thereof) are not strongly attached to the rest of their respective tectonic plate. They cause both the subducting and overriding plate to move in the direction of the subduction zone. Slab suction is the weakest of the three major forces involved in plate motion, the others being slab pull, the strongest, and ridge push.

28. Honors angela was assigned to a client who is scheduled for suctioning every 4 hours


If nurse Angela is assigned to client Mikaela, who has suctioning every 4 hours and oxygen via nasal cannula hooked up at 2-3 pm, nurse Angela's priority should be to hyperoxygenate the client before suctioning.

Hyperoxygenation is the temporary administration of a higher concentration of oxygen than usual in order to reduce the risk of hypoxia and other suctioning complications.

Hyperoxygenation poses few dangers.

Conduct an assessment to determine whether suctioning is required before oxygenating the patient. To prevent oxygen desaturation in mechanically ventilated patients, hyperoxygenation should be used both before and after suctioning.
