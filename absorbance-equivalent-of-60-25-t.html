2. 3. How much heat energy is absorbed by 70 g of water if the temperature rises from 25 C to 60 C. The specific heat of water is 4.184 g/g C?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & m = \text{70 g} \\ & c = 4.184 \: \dfrac{\text{J}}{\text{g}\cdot{}^{\circ}\text{C}} \\ & T_{\text{i}} = 25^{\circ}\text{C} \\ & T_{\text{f}} = 60^{\circ}\text{C} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the heat absorbed by water.

[tex]\begin{aligned} q & = mc(T_{\text{f}} - T_{\text{i}}) \\ & = (\text{70 g})\left(4.184 \: \dfrac{\text{J}}{\text{g}\cdot{}^{\circ}\text{C}}\right)(60^{\circ}\text{C} - 25^{\circ}\text{C}) \\ & = \boxed{\text{10,250.8 J}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the heat absorbed by 70 g of water is 10,250.8 J.

8. What is the difference between %t and absorbance?


Answer:

Transmittance (T) is the fraction of incident light which is transmitted. In other words, it's the amount of light that “successfully” passes through the substance and comes out the other side. ... Absorbance (A) is the flip-side of transmittance and states how much of the light the sample absorbed.


9. it must be absorbed by 375 g of water to raise its temperature at 25 degrees celsius ?pasagot po ​


Heat that must be absorbed is 39.24 KJ

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 375 g

∆T = 25°C

cp of water = 4.186 J/g°C

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Energy

Step 3: Solve for Energy

Equation:

The formula for Heat Energy is given by:

Q = mcp∆T

where: Q - Heat Energy

m - mass

cp - specific heat

∆T = change in temperature, T2 - T1

Solution:

Substituting the value of m, cp and ∆T in the formula,

Q = (375 g)(4.186 J/g°C)(25°C)

Q = 39,243.75 J or 39.24 KJ

Heat can be calculated using the formula:  

Q = mc∆T  

Q = heat, J  

m = mass, g  

c = specific heat, joules / g ° C  

∆T = temperature difference, ° C / K  

Given

mass of water = 550 g

∆T = temperature difference = 25°C to 95°C

Required

Heat absorbed

Solution

c = specific heat for water = 4.18 J/ g ° C

Input the value :

Q = 550 g x 4.18 J/ g ° C x ( 95 - 25 ° C)

Q = 172,425 J
