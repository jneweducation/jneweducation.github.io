13. 1 point 22. Vincent works three days a week as a gasoline boy. If he works 4 1/4 hours on the first day, 3 1/2 hours on the second day and 4 1/3 hours on the third day, how many hours did he work in three days? O A. 111/12 hours O B. 12 1/12 hours O C. 13 1/12 hours O D. 14 1/12 hours​


Answer:

A. 11 [tex]\frac{1}{12}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Addition of Mixed Numbers

The given are Vincent's work hours, 4 1/4 on the first day, 3 1/2 on the second day, and 4 1/3 on the third day. We need to know the total hours that he worked.

Step 1: Determine the working equation of the problem.

4 [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] + 3 [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] + 4 [tex]\frac{1}{3}[/tex] = ?

Step 2: Notice that we have mixed numbers to add. Change the mixed numbers into an improper fraction by multiplying the whole number by the denominator and adding the answer to the numerator. So,

[tex]\frac{(4 x 4 ) + 1}{4}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{(2 x 3 ) + 1}{2}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{(4 x 3 ) + 1}{3}[/tex] = ?

[tex]\frac{13}{4}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{7}{2}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{13}{3}[/tex] = ?

Step 3: Get the least common denominator of the resulting improper fractions. In this problem, the LCD is 12.

[tex]\frac{(3 x 13) + ( 6 x 7) + ( 4 x 13) }{12}[/tex] =?

[tex]\frac{39 + 42 + 52}{12}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{133}{12}[/tex]

Step 4: We can further simplify the resulting fraction into a mixed number by dividing the numerator by the denominator. Let the quotient be the whole number and the remainder be the new numerator and copy the denominator.

[tex]\frac{133}{12}[/tex] = 11 [tex]\frac{1}{12}[/tex]

Therefore, Vincent works a total of 11 [tex]\frac{1}{12}[/tex] hours in three days.
