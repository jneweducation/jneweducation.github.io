3. How do I solve (-2, 2), perp. to y=2/3x-2?​


Answer:

your line is already in y-intercept format

y = Mx + b

y = (2/3) x + 2

The slope of a perpendicular line relative to the slope of the line it is perpendicular to

m = -1/M

m = -1/(2/3) = -3/2

Find a point on your line. f(0) is easiest, This just means when x is zero, what is y?

y = -3/2 * 0 + 2 = 2

p(0, 2)

Then use definition of the slope

(y - 2)/(x - 0) = -3/2

(y - 2)/x = -3/2

y - 2 = (-3/2) x

y = (-3/2) x + 2

But this is only the equation of the line perpendicular to your line through one point. It could be through any point: Therefore its slope will always be -3/2, but it could have any y-intercept:

y = (-3/2) x + b
