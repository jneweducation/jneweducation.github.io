12. kathy use a survey to determine how many of her classmates were left-handed and how many were right-handedA. recognize a problem B. form a hypothesis C. test the hypothesis with an experiment D. draw conclusions ​


Investigations in IPA help investigate a problem. This investigation is needed to prove a problem. With investigation, all can be proven. An investigation is beneficial in making scientific papers. In scientific work, scientific research methods are needed. Scientific research must be carried out systematically and objectively by carrying out the following steps, namely:

Recognize the problem
Formulating the problem is the first step in any research. The formulation of the problem is in the form of a scientific question. The problems posed can be discussed together to get the correct answer.Collecting data and information
Collecting data about the object to be observed is very important. Collecting data can be done orally or in writing. If the object data has been collected, it must be given a data unit.Form a hypothesis
After collecting data, it is possible to make a hypothesis. The hypothesis must be based on the provisional data collected. This hypothesis had not been calculated for sure before the research.Hypothesis test with an experiment.
This experiment aims to prove the results of the hypothesis. This experiment must be carried out carefully and thoroughly because the results of these observations will be used as conclusions later.Collecting experimental data
After experimenting, the experimental data must be collected. After that, the data will be combined.Processing data
The next thing is to process the experimental data that has been combined. Processing this data aims to get an accurate conclusion.Draw a conclusion
After processing the data, conclusions can be drawn from the data. This conclusion is an accurate conclusion to the hypothesis.Communicating
After getting a conclusion, then the conclusion must be published. It is intended that other people also know the results of our research.

Based on the questions above, the survey conducted by Kathy to determine the number of right-handed and left-handed in her class is included in the hypothesis test with an experiment step because the survey can prove the actual amount of data from right-handed and left-handed (option C).

Option A is incorrect because Kathy did not recognize the problem to get the correct answer.

Option B is incorrect because Kathy did not form a hypothesis from the problems found in her class but proved it by making a survey.

Option C is incorrect because the conclusion will be obtained if the survey data conducted by Kathy has been processed and shows the exact number of right-handed and left-handed classmates.

So, the scientific method that Kathy uses a survey is to test the hypothesis with an experiment.

General advice on staying safe

be aware of what’s going on around you

Self defence

If you see a crime is about to take place or you think that someone is going to commit a crime against you or your home, you should always try to contact the police or call for help before confronting any attacker or person committing a crime.

Using reasonable force

The law says you can use reasonable force to:

protect yourself

protect another person

prevent a crime

But there's no clear definition of what reasonable force is. A court judges the individual circumstances. But if you use force, it's likely the police will investigate your actions.

Out and about at night

If you’re out at night, don’t put yourself at risk by drinking too much, going off alone, or leaving with someone you don’t know.

Getting about

If you are travelling by yourself, getting a taxi or using public transport is much safer than walking. You should use your common sense to stay safe.

Walking

Whenever it’s possible, try and walk with someone else or a group of friends. You're less likely to be attacked or mugged if you're with other people.

If you are walking alone, always be aware of what's going on around you. Stay in busy areas with plenty of street lights and always avoid alleys or shortcuts.

Stay alert if listening to headphones, especially at night. As well as being distracting, headphones could be valuable and worth stealing. Make sure you keep any valuables in your coat or bag.

Travelling on public transport

Make sure that you get a bus at a stop where other people are waiting. If you are getting a train, wait in an area of the platform with plenty of light where you can see if anyone is approaching you.

Once you’re on board, sit where there are plenty of people or next to the driver. If you feel uncomfortable or afraid, speak to the driver. Most trains have emergency alarms. If you get into trouble, don’t be afraid to use them.
