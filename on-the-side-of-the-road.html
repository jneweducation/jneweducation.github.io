Answer is 8 ft, via pythagorean theorem
Pythagorean Theorem Formula: c^2 = a^2 + b^2
Let:
a = length of the wall
b = width of the road
c = length of the flag pole
17^2 = 15^2 + b^2
289 = 225 + b^2
289 - 225 = 225 + b^2 - 225
64 = b^2 ---extract the square roots

+-8 = b
The negative is irrelevant in this case so get only the positive which is 8.

9. A car takes 2¼ hours to travel 200 km along a main road and ¾ hour to travel 25 km along a side road. What is the average speed of the car for the whole journey?​


Answer:

To find the average speed of the car for the whole journey, we need to calculate the total distance traveled and the total time taken.

Total distance = 200 km + 25 km = 225 km

Total time = 2¼ hours + ¾ hour = 3 hours

Average speed = Total distance ÷ Total time

= 225 km ÷ 3 hours

= 75 km/hour

Therefore, the average speed of the car for the whole journey is 75 km/hour.

Answer:

To find the average speed of the car for the whole journey, we need to use the formula:

Average speed = Total distance ÷ Total time

First, let's calculate the total distance traveled by the car:

Total distance = Distance traveled along the main road + Distance traveled along the side road Total distance = 200 km + 25 km Total distance = 225 km

Next, let's calculate the total time taken by the car:

Total time = Time taken along the main road + Time taken along the side road Total time = 2¼ hours + ¾ hour Total time = 3 hours

Now, we can use the formula to find the average speed:

Average speed = Total distance ÷ Total time Average speed = 225 km ÷ 3 hours Average speed = 75 km/h

Therefore, the average speed of the car for the whole journey is 75 km/h.

15. A bridge that was 8.3 m long has been washed out by the rain several days ago. How fast must a car be going to successfully jump the stream? Although the road is level on both sides of the bridge, the road on the far side is 2.0 m lower than the road on this side.


Answer:

The speed of the car should be 12.57 m/s.

Explanation:

In our case, we are going to solve for the velocity of the car to jump the stream which is also the length of the bridge. Also the far side of the road was lowered by 2.0 m. The best way to analyze this is to draw a diagram.

Here, I provided an illustration of the problem below.

Since, we are asked to solve for the initial velocity of the car to jump the stream, we will use the concept of Projectile Motion.

For the formula, we will use

1. The formula for the range which is  [tex]x=v_{0x} t[/tex]

where x is the range or the length of the stream in this problem

          [tex]v_{0x}[/tex] is the initial horizontal velocity, unit is in m/s

           [tex]t[/tex] is the total time of flight of the car to both ends of the stream

2. We will use also the formula [tex]v=v_{0} +gt[/tex]

where [tex]v[/tex] is the final velocity at the maximum height the car will reach

           [tex]v_{0}[/tex] is the initial velocity

           [tex]g[/tex] is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

           [tex]t[/tex] is the time at maximum height the car will reach

For the given information

d = 8.3 m      this is the length of the bridge

[tex]y_{0} =2.0m[/tex]      this is the initial vertical distance at the far end of the road

Solving the problem

1. First is to solve for the length of the stream. Using Pythagorean theorem, we have: (See the attached illustration to analyze.)

[tex]x=\sqrt{d^2-2^2}[/tex]

[tex]x=\sqrt{(8.3)^2-2^2}[/tex]

x = 8.06 m

2. After solving for the length of the stream, let us solve for the initial velocity. Let us use first the formula for range, we have:

[tex]x=v_{0x} t[/tex]

Then substitute the given information, we have:

[tex]8.06=v_{0x} t[/tex]  derive the equation equal to time, we have:

[tex]t =\frac{8.06}{v_{0x} }[/tex]   use this as equation 1

Take note that the time here is the total time of flight for the car to jump over to the other side of the stream.

Then let us use the other formula

[tex]v=v_{0} +gt[/tex]

[tex]0=v_{0} +(-9.8)t[/tex]     then transpose the right side member of the equation to the left, we have:

[tex]v_{0} =9.8t[/tex]    

Take note that the [tex]t[/tex]  here is the time for the car to reach the maximum height, therefore we will multiply this by two (2) to cover the total time of flight and the final velocity at this point is zero.

Therefore, the equation will become:

[tex]v_{0} =9.8(2t)[/tex]

[tex]v_{0} =19.6t[/tex]   let us use this as equation 2

Now, we are ready to solve for the initial velocity.

Let us substitute equation 1 to equation 2, we have:

[tex]v_{0} =19.6(\frac{8.06}{v_{0x} } )[/tex]

[tex](v_{0})^2 =19.6(8.06)[/tex]

[tex]v_{0} = 12.57m/s[/tex]

Therefore, the initial velocity of the car to jump the stream is 12.57 m/s

23. A side from the given examples give atleast 5 tips to stay safe on the road


Answer:

In addition to the given examples, here are 5 more tips to stay safe on the road:

Keep a safe distance: Maintain a safe distance from the vehicle in front of you, especially during adverse weather conditions such as rain or snow. This will give you enough time to react and brake if the vehicle in front of you suddenly stops.

Avoid distractions: Avoid distractions such as texting or talking on the phone while driving, eating, or applying makeup. These activities can take your attention away from the road and increase your risk of an accident.

Check blind spots: Before changing lanes or turning, always check your blind spots to make sure there are no vehicles or pedestrians in your path. Use your mirrors and turn your head to look over your shoulder if necessary.

Use turn signals: Always use your turn signals when changing lanes or turning to indicate your intentions to other drivers. This can help prevent accidents and promote safe driving practices.

Observe speed limits: Observe posted speed limits and adjust your speed to match the road and weather conditions. Driving too fast for the conditions can increase your risk of an accident and reduce your ability to react to unexpected situations.
