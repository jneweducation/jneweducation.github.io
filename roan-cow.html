23. What is the phenotype ratio expected if a roan cow and a roan steer mate together?


✏Answer✏

Roan coat color in cows and steers is determined by an incomplete dominance pattern of inheritance, where the heterozygous genotype (Rr) results in a mixture of red and white hairs, creating the roan phenotype. When a roan cow (Rr) and a roan steer (Rr) mate, they can produce four possible genotypes in their offspring: RR, Rr, rR, and rr.

Assuming random segregation of alleles during gamete formation and random mating, the expected phenotype ratio in the offspring would be as follows:

Red (RR) phenotype: 25%

Roan (Rr) phenotype: 50%

White (rr) phenotype: 25%

So the expected phenotype ratio would be 25% red, 50% roan, and 25% white. This assumes that the roan cow and roan steer are both heterozygous (Rr) for the roan coat color gene. If one of them is homozygous dominant (RR) and the other is heterozygous (Rr), then the expected phenotype ratio may differ. It's important to note that this is a predicted ratio based on Mendelian genetics and actual ratios in offspring may vary due to other factors such as genetic recombination, mutations, and environmental influences.
