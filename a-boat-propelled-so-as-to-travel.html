7. One boat travels 10 kph faster than another. While one boat travels 125 km, the other travels 155 km. Find their speeds.


One boat travels 10 kph faster than another. While one boat travels 125 km, the other travels 155 km. Find their speeds.

Let x be the speed of the slower boat.

The faster boat is x + 10 kph.

We know from the problem that:

x + (x + 10) = 125 + 155

2x + 10 = 280

2x = 270

x = 135 kph

So the slower boat is traveling at 135 kph and the faster boat is traveling at 135 + 10 = 145 kph.

Answer:

Explanation:

Use Kinematic Equation

Δx = Vit+½at²

where:

Δx is the total distance traveled

Vi is the initial velocity = 4.0m/s

t is time = 6.0 s

a is acceleration = 1.0m/s²

Δx = Vit+½at²

Δx = (4.0m/s) (6.0s) + ½(1.0m/s²) (6.0s)²

Δx = 24m +½ (1.0m/s²) (36s²)

Δx = 24m + ½ (36m)

Δx = 24m + 18m

Δx = 42 m

The total distance traveled by the boat is 42 m.

(Note: The i in Vi is a subset. Also, take note how the time quantity (expressed in second) was cancelled.)
