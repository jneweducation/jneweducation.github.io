13. when making the chapter one for a qualitative research, do you need to include a solution to your introduction?​


In qualitative research, the purpose of the introduction is to provide background information on the research topic, explain the importance of the research, and identify the research questions or objectives. It is not necessary to include a solution to the research problem in the introduction.

Qualitative research often focuses on exploring and understanding complex phenomena and experiences, rather than providing a specific solution to a problem. Therefore, the research questions or objectives are typically framed to guide the research process and to help the researcher gain a deeper understanding of the topic under investigation.

However, in some cases, qualitative research may aim to develop or test a theoretical framework, or to inform the development of interventions or policies. In these cases, the research may have a more practical or applied focus, and the introduction may include some discussion of potential solutions or implications of the research.

Overall, the content and structure of the introduction in a qualitative research study will depend on the specific research questions, objectives, and context of the study.

17. TASK 2: Directions: Explain the value and purpose of each of the five major chapters of research. Chapter 1 (The Problem and its Background) Chapter 2 ( Review of Related Literature) Chapter 3 ( Methodology) Chapter 4 ( Analysis and Interpretation of Data) Chapter 5 (Summary of Findings, Reccommendations, and Conclusions)


Answer:

Chapter 1: The Problem and its Background - This chapter introduces the research problem and provides the context for the study. It explains the significance of the problem, the purpose of the study, and the research questions or hypotheses. The chapter also includes the scope and limitations of the study, the definition of terms, and the conceptual framework that guides the study.

Chapter 2: Review of Related Literature - This chapter provides a comprehensive review of the literature related to the research problem. It summarizes the existing knowledge, identifies the gaps in the literature, and explains how the current study addresses those gaps. The review of literature also helps establish the theoretical foundation of the study, develops research hypotheses or questions, and justifies the research design.

Chapter 3: Methodology - This chapter describes the research design, procedures, and methods used in the study. It includes the population and sample, data collection instruments, data analysis techniques, and the ethical considerations that were taken into account during the study. The methodology chapter ensures the credibility and reliability of the study by explaining how the data was collected and analyzed.

Chapter 4: Analysis and Interpretation of Data - This chapter presents the results of the study, including the descriptive and inferential statistics. It also interprets the findings and relates them back to the research questions and hypotheses. The analysis and interpretation of data chapter helps answer the research questions or hypotheses and provides evidence to support the study's conclusions.

Chapter 5: Summary of Findings, Recommendations, and Conclusions - This chapter summarizes the main findings of the study, discusses their implications, and provides recommendations for future research. It also restates the purpose of the study and its significance, summarizes the major findings, and draws conclusions based on the results. The chapter helps the reader understand the practical and theoretical implications of the study and provides suggestions for further research.


18. Define all of the key terms of chapter 7:Key Terms Accounting Cost Average Fixed CostAverage Variable CostBusiness ProfitEconomic CostEconomic ProfitExplicit CostFixed CostImplicit CostMarginal CostOpportunity CostRevenueVariable Cost ​


Answer:

Accounting Cost - The actual monetary expense incurred by a business for the production of goods or services. It includes explicit costs, such as wages, rent, and utilities, that can be easily tracked and accounted for.

Average Fixed Cost - The total fixed cost divided by the quantity of output produced. It represents the per-unit fixed cost.

Average Variable Cost - The total variable cost divided by the quantity of output produced. It represents the per-unit variable cost.

Business Profit - The difference between total revenue and total cost.

Economic Cost - The total cost of resources used in the production of goods or services, including both explicit and implicit costs.

Economic Profit - The difference between total revenue and total economic cost, including both explicit and implicit costs.

Explicit Cost - The actual out-of-pocket expenses incurred by a business for the production of goods or services, such as wages, rent, and utilities.

Fixed Cost - The cost of production that remains constant regardless of the quantity of output produced.

Implicit Cost - The opportunity cost of resources used in the production of goods or services that do not have a direct monetary value, such as the cost of using the owner's time and resources.

Marginal Cost - The additional cost incurred by producing one additional unit of output.

Opportunity Cost - The cost of forgoing the best alternative opportunity in order to pursue a certain action or decision.

Revenue - The total amount of income earned by a business through the sale of goods or services.

Variable Cost - The cost of production that changes with the quantity of output produced.
