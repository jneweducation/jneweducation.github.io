7. 3. A kinder teacher developed a coloring worksheet for her pupils. Using this worksheet, the pupils' performance has a mean score of 90 and a standard deviation of 10. Fifty kinder pupils from a certain barangay were asked to answer the said worksheet and found that their mean score was 95 with a standard deviation of 5. Test the hypothesis at 1% significance level.​


hope it help

Step-by-step explanation:

To test the hypothesis, we need to perform a hypothesis test using the following steps:

Step 1: State the null and alternative hypotheses.

The null hypothesis (H0) is that there is no significant difference between the mean score of the pupils who used the worksheet (μ) and the mean score of the entire population (μ0).

H0: μ = μ0

The alternative hypothesis (Ha) is that there is a significant difference between the mean score of the pupils who used the worksheet (μ) and the mean score of the entire population (μ0).

Ha: μ ≠ μ0

Step 2: Determine the level of significance (α).

The level of significance is given as 1% or 0.01.

Step 3: Determine the test statistic.

We can use the z-test to compare the means of two populations. The test statistic can be computed using the formula:

z = (x̄ - μ0) / (σ / √n)

where x̄ is the sample mean, μ0 is the population mean, σ is the population standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

Substituting the given values, we get:

z = (95 - 90) / (10 / √50)

z = 7.07

Step 4: Determine the critical value.

Since the level of significance is 1%, the critical value for a two-tailed test is ±2.58.

Step 5: Make a decision.

Since the calculated test statistic (z = 7.07) is greater than the critical value (±2.58), we can reject the null hypothesis.

Step 6: Interpret the result.

The result of the hypothesis test indicates that there is a significant difference between the mean score of the pupils who used the worksheet and the mean score of the entire population. Therefore, we can conclude that the worksheet developed by the teacher has a positive effect on the pupils' performance.
