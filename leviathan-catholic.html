Answer:

Hobbes contends that a state of nature and a state of war are the exact same thing. As a state of war is anything that includes even the intention or desire for battle, and there is always the intention or desire for battle in nature, nature is in a continuous state of war.

Also in chapter 13, the condition of man is a condition of war of everyone against everyone, so that everyone is governed by his own reason and can make use of anything he likes that might help him to preserve his life against his enemies.
