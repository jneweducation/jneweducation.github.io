4. how to get the production input and outputs of the given raw materials and Ingredients?​


Answer:

There are two basic processes for converting inputs into outputs. In process manufacturing, the basic inputs (natural resources, raw materials) are broken down into one or more outputs (products). For instance, bauxite (the input) is processed to extract aluminum (the output). The assembly process is just the opposite. The basic inputs, like natural resources, raw materials, or human resources, are either combined to create the output or transformed into the output. An airplane, for example, is created by assembling thousands of parts, which are its raw material inputs. Steel manufacturers use heat to transform iron and other materials into steel. In services, customers may play a role in the transformation process. For example, a tax preparation service combines the knowledge of the tax preparer with the client’s information about personal finances in order to complete the tax return.

13. Which of the following resources is/are used as major inputs of manufacturing companies? a. Raw materials b. Labor c. Overhead d. All of the above


Which of the following resources is/are used as major inputs of manufacturing companies? a. Raw materials b. Labor c. Overhead d. All of the above

The answer is letter D.   A raw material, also known as a feedstock, unprocessed material, or primary commodity, is a base material used to make commodities, finished products, energy, or intermediate materials used as feedstock for potential finished products. As feedstock, the term means that these commodities are bottleneck properties that must be produced in order to manufacture other products.

Raw latex, crude oil, cotton, coal, raw biomass, iron ore, air, logs, water, or "any commodity of agriculture, forestry, fishing, or mineral in its natural form or which has undergone the transformation needed to prepare it for foreign selling in significant quantities" are examples of raw materials. The word secondary raw material waste material that has been recycled and reintroduced into the production process.

What Is Manufacturing Production?

Beyond a bill of materials, manufacturing production refers to the practice of how to more successfully manufacture and deliver products for sale. Manufacturing processing processes are classified into three types: make to stock (MTS), make to order (MTO), and make to assemble (MTA). Such methods have benefits and drawbacks in terms of labour costs, inventory management, overhead, customization, and the speed at which orders are produced and fulfilled.

Understanding Manufacturing Production

Manufacturing is the large-scale production and assembly of parts and finished goods for sale. It may use a variety of technologies, including human and machine labour, as well as biological and chemical processes, to convert raw materials into finished goods. Production is similar to manufacturing, but it encompasses a wider variety of operations. It refers to the methods and procedures used to transform raw materials or semi-finished goods into finished products or facilities, whether or not equipment is used. Manufacturers must match their manufacturing strategies to the market's demands and preferences, available capital, order volume and scale, seasonal fluctuations in demand, operating costs (such as labour and inventory), and a variety of other variables.
