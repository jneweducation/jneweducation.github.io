9. 1. A force of 10 N acts on a body for 2 seconds.a. what is the change in momentum of the body?b. if the body has a mass of 5200 grams, what is the velocity?2. what is the change in velocity of a 350g air track cart if you exert a force of 1.2N on it for 3 seconds?​


Explanation:

1.

a. The change in momentum of the body is equal to the force multiplied by the time:

Δp = FΔt = 10 N * 2 s = 20 Ns

b. We can use the formula for momentum:

p = mv

where p is momentum, m is mass, and v is velocity. Rearranging the formula, we get:

v = p/m

We can substitute the values we know:

p = 20 Ns

m = 5200 g = 5.2 kg

We need to convert the mass to kilograms before we can use the formula. Now, we can solve for v:

v = p/m = 20 Ns / 5.2 kg = 3.85 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the body is 3.85 m/s.

2.

We can use the formula for impulse:

J = FΔt = mΔv

where J is impulse, F is force, Δt is time, m is mass, and Δv is the change in velocity.

We are given:

m = 350 g = 0.35 kg

F = 1.2 N

Δt = 3 s

We need to convert the mass to kilograms before we can use the formula. Now, we can solve for Δv:

Δv = J/m = FΔt/m = 1.2 N * 3 s / 0.35 kg = 10.29 m/s

Therefore, the change in velocity of the air track cart is 10.29 m/s.
