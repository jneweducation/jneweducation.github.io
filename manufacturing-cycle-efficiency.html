Answer: Favorable.

Explanation:

Since Variable Manufacturing Overhead uses direct labor hours as its basis for application, therefore, an unfavorable variance in direct labor efficiency would mean that the company used more direct labor hours than should be rendering it inefficient. Consequently, the direct labor hours to be used as divisor would be higher thereby spreading the variable manufacturing overhead cost among units resulting to a lower per unit VOH cost.

24. A car manufacturer claims that its latest car model has a combustion engine with 40% efficiency. If the said engine is supplied with 10 000 J of heat in a cycle, how much heat will be discharged by the engine through its exhaust?​


Answer:

Waste thermal energy, that is not converted to kinetic energy in the engine, will be roughly split between exhaust and cooling system (say 3000 j. in the exhaust, in your hypthetical situation). This will vary continuously as engine speed and load vary. - That advertised 40% efficiency represents a ‘peak’ that occurs only under ideal conditions and does not represent the average efficiency in normal driving, which may do well to exceed 10%.

27. a car manufacturer claims that its latest car model has a combustion engine with 40% efficiency. If she said engine is supplied with 10 000J of heat in cycle, how much heat will be discharged by the engine through its exhaust​


Answer:

If the engine has 40% efficiency, this means that only 40% of the supplied heat will be converted into useful work. The remaining 60% will be lost as heat energy through the exhaust.

To find out how much heat will be discharged through the exhaust, we need to calculate the amount of heat energy that will be converted into useful work by the engine:

Useful work = 40% x 10,000 J

Useful work = 0.4 x 10,000 J

Useful work = 4,000 J

The remaining heat energy that will be lost through the exhaust can be calculated as:

Lost heat energy = Supplied heat energy - Useful work

Lost heat energy = 10,000 J - 4,000 J

Lost heat energy = 6,000 J

Therefore, the engine will discharge 6,000 J of heat energy through its exhaust.
