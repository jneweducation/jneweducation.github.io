5. Find the 20, 22, 25, 29, 31 interquartile range of the data​


To find the interquartile range (IQR), we need to first find the first quartile (Q1) and the third quartile (Q3).

To find Q1, we need to find the median of the lower half of the data (the data points less than the median).

First, let's arrange the data in order:

20, 22, 25, 29, 31

The median is 25.

The lower half of the data is: 20, 22, 25.

The median of the lower half of the data is (20 + 22) / 2 = 21.

Therefore, Q1 = 21.

To find Q3, we need to find the median of the upper half of the data (the data points greater than the median).

The upper half of the data is: 29, 31.

The median of the upper half of the data is (29 + 31) / 2 = 30.

Therefore, Q3 = 30.

Now we can find the IQR:

IQR = Q3 - Q1 = 30 - 21 = 9.

Therefore, the interquartile range of the data is 9.

23. Are Median and Interquartile Range related? How?


Answer:

Yes, the Median and Inter-quartile range (IQR) are related.

Explanation:

The median and inter-quartile range are related in terms of getting the middle value of an ordered data, especially for ungrouped data. Both of them are arranged from lowest to highest order then getting the middle value of it.

What differs in each process is the next step. For the IQR, with even number ascending order data, the middle value is disregarded while the left and right side data are considered in taking the difference of their middle values.

While in the median, after listing an ascending order for the values, we only consider the middle value. To solve for the median of even numbers, we get the sum of the two middle numbers and divide by two. For odd numbers, we just get the middle number and that is the median of the data.

24. Interquartile range of 250,150,100,200,225,100,100,175,225,250​


Answer:

112.5

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the interquartile range, we need to first find the first quartile (Q1) and third quartile (Q3) of the data set.

Step 1: Put the data in order from least to greatest:

100, 100, 100, 150, 175, 200, 225, 225, 250, 250

Step 2: Find the median (Q2) of the entire data set:

Q2 = median = (200 + 175) / 2 = 187.5

Step 3: Find the median (Q1) of the lower half of the data set:

Lower half: 100, 100, 100, 150, 175

Q1 = median = (100 + 150) / 2 = 125

Step 4: Find the median (Q3) of the upper half of the data set:

Upper half: 200, 225, 225, 250, 250

Q3 = median = (225 + 250) / 2 = 237.5

Step 5: Calculate the interquartile range (IQR):

IQR = Q3 - Q1 = 237.5 - 125 = 112.5

Therefore, the interquartile range of the data is 112.5.
