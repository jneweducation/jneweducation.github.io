1. What is the greenhouse effect and enhanced greenhouse effect?​


GREENHOUSE EFFECT:The trapping of the sun's warmth in a planet's lower atmosphere, due to the greater transparency of the atmosphere to visible radiation from the sun than to infrared radiation emitted from the planet's surface.ENHANCED GREENHOUSE EFFECT:The disruption to Earth's climate equilibrium caused by the increased concentrations of greenhouse gases has led to an increase in the global average surface temperatures.

Answer:

What are the greenhouse gases?

The greenhouse gases are gases in the Earth's atmosphere that trap heat from the sun and contribute to the greenhouse effect. Examples of greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide, methane, and water vapor.

What do greenhouse gases do?

Greenhouse gases absorb and trap radiation from the sun that is reflected off the Earth's surface. This process keeps the Earth warm and habitable for life.

What is the greenhouse effect?

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that occurs when greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere trap heat from the sun, warming theEarth's surface and atmosphere.

How does the greenhouse effect work?

The greenhouse effect works by trapping incoming solar radiation from the sun in the Earth's atmosphere. This radiation is absorbed by greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide and water vapor, and is then radiated back to the Earth's surface as heat. This process warms the Earth's surface and atmosphere, much like a blanket insulates and warms the human body.

Is the greenhouse effect a good thing?

Yes, the greenhouse effect is a good thing. It is a natural process that keeps the Earth warm and habitable for life.

So why is the greenhouse effect considered a bad thing and is linked to global warming?

The greenhouse effect is considered a bad thing because human activities, such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation, have increased the amount of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. This has caused an imbalance in the natural greenhouse effect, leading to an increase in the Earth's average temperature, also known as global warming. This increase in temperature can have negative impacts on the environment, such as melting glaciers, rising sea levels, and more extreme weather events.
