9. A sphere has a diameter of 2ft. If it is cut into an equal pieces find the volume of each portion in cubic inches.​


Answer:

To find the volume of each portion after cutting a sphere into equal pieces, we first need to determine the radius of the sphere. The radius is half the diameter.

Given:

Diameter of the sphere = 2 ft

Radius of the sphere = Diameter / 2 = 2 ft / 2 = 1 ft

We know that the volume of a sphere can be calculated using the formula:

Volume of a sphere = (4/3) * π * (radius)^3

Plugging in the values:

Volume of a sphere = (4/3) * π * (1 ft)^3

Now, we need to convert the volume from cubic feet to cubic inches. Since 1 ft is equal to 12 inches, we use the conversion factor of 1 ft^3 = 12^3 in^3.

Volume of a sphere = (4/3) * π * (1 ft)^3 * (12 in/ft)^3

Calculating the volume:

Volume of a sphere = (4/3) * 3.14159 * (1 ft)^3 * (12 in/ft)^3

= (4/3) * 3.14159 * 1^3 * 12^3 in^3

= (4/3) * 3.14159 * 1 * 12 * 12 * 12 in^3

≈ 7238.23 in^3

So, each portion of the sphere, when cut into equal pieces, will have a volume of approximately 7238.23 cubic inches.
