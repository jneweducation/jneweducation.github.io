11. fast pls A famous artist wishes to insure his dog for half a million pesos. The insurance company estimates that a total loss will occur with probability of 0.003, a 50% loss with probability of 0.02, and 25% loss with probability 0.1. What premium should the insurance company charge each year to realize an average profit of 25, 000? Note: Express your answer in the nearest whole number. Also do not include the currency symbol for your answers.


Considering;

A private pilot wants to get $500,000 worth of insurance for his plane. The insurance company thinks that there is a 0.007% chance of a total loss, a 0.056% chance of a 50% loss, and a 0.16% chance of a 25% loss. Putting aside all other losses.

Answer:

Given,

A well-known artist wants to pay 500,000 pesos to insure his dog.

Let us assume total premium = x

Let,

[tex]x_{1}[/tex] = Total amount of 50% loss = -(50% of x)

[tex]= - (\frac{50}{100} x)[/tex]

(Minus sign indicates loss)

= -0.5x

[tex]x_{2}[/tex] = Total amount of 25% loss = -(25% of x)

[tex]= - (\frac{25}{100} x)[/tex]

=-0.25x

[tex]x_{3}[/tex] = Total amount of loss = -x[tex]x_{4}[/tex] = Total amount of profit = x

∴ [tex]P(x_{1} = -0.5x) = 0.02[/tex]

[tex]P(x_{2} = -0.25x) = 0.1[/tex]

[tex]P (x_{3} = -x) = 0.003[/tex]

[tex]P(x_{4} = x) = 1 - [P(x_{1} ) + P(x_{2} ) + P(x_{3} ) + P(x_{4} )][/tex]

[tex]P(x_{4} = x) = 1 - [0.02 + 0.1 + 0.003][/tex]

[tex]P(x_{4} = x) = 1 - 0.123[/tex]

[tex]P(x_{4} = x) = 0.877[/tex]

Given that expected profit = 25,000

Formula,Expected Profit = ∑[tex]x_{i} P(x_{i} )[/tex]

25,000 = (-0.5x)(0.02) + (-0.25x)(0.1) + (-x)(0.003) + (x)(0.877)

25,000 = -0.01x - 0.025x - 0.003x + 0.877x

25,000 = 0.839x

x = 25,000/0.839

x = 29,797.4

x = 29,797

As a result, the entire amount of premium is 29,797.

27. A pilot wishes to fly an airplane due East , but a strong wind blowing Southeast at 100km/h keeps blowing the airplane off-course. If the airplane has a cruising speed of 450km/h, in what direction should the pilot fly to reach the destination?​


Answer:

First thing to note is that wind, unlike just about everything else, is described by the direction it is coming from, not the direction it is heading towards. It’s vector direction is the opposite, i.e. NW.

We’re going to construct a vector triangle, using the information we have.

Step-by-step explanation:

The line W ← → E is the desired course flown. AB represents the plane’s velocity, at an unknown angle θ south of east, and BC is the wind’s velocity. The addition of these vectors gives the resultant AC which is the velocity made good.

So, now, all we need to do is solve for θ. In dotted lines I have shown the line BD which is perpendicular to the W - E line.

In the ΔBCD, BD/BC = sin45° → BD = BC.sin45° = 100.sin45°.

In ΔABD, sinθ = BD/AB → θ = arcsin(100.sin45°/450)

θ = 9.04°, so the plane needs to fly 9° South of East (099°T).

The length AC is the speed at which the plane is travelling over the ground. You can use the same two triangles to find AC. (Hint, find AD using cosθ, then subtract CD using cos45°).
