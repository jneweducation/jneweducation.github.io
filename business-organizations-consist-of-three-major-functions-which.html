1. Identify the three major functional areas of business organizations and briefly describe how they interrelate


The following are the three major functional areas of a business:Management Production/Operations Marketing

One motivation for segmenting business processes into functional areas is to enable each to operate within its area of competence, increasing overall efficiency and effectiveness.

Management's significant responsibility in business is to supervise the performance of others. The majority of managerial activities can be classified as follows:

Planning: Managers plan by establishing long-term goals for the firm and the short-term strategies necessary to achieve those goals.Arranging: Managers are responsible for organizing an organization's operations in the most efficient manner possible, allowing the business to make the best use of its resources.Managing:  A considerable portion of a manager's time is spent monitoring the business's actions to ensure that it is on the path to meeting its objectives. When individuals or systems deviate from the course, managers are frequently the first to detect and correct.Leading: Managers serve as organizational leaders in both practical and symbolic ways. The manager may be responsible for leading work teams or groups by implementing a new process or developing a new product.

Operations are converting inputs (factors of production) to outputs (goods and services). Processes function similarly to the heart of a firm, pumping forth goods and services in sufficient numbers and of a high enough quality to suit clients' expectations. The operations manager is in charge of the business's day-to-day operations, which might range from ordering raw supplies to scheduling personnel to generate tangible things.

Marketing encompasses everything a business does to ascertain its consumers' needs and produce products and services to fulfill those demands. Additionally, marketing is responsible for advertising goods and services, establishing how goods and services will be delivered, and developing a price strategy to acquire market share while remaining competitive.

8. Can you describe the roles and functions of major organs of the human body? Which organs work together?​


Answer and Explanation

There are five major organs in our bodies that must function in order to keep us alive. These are the brain, heart, lungs, liver and kidneys. If any of these organs stop working, our bodies cannot stay alive. In addition, there are other major organs that play very important roles in our bodies. These are the skin, stomach, intestines and bladder. Each organ is part of an organ system, and has a specific function that it must perform for the overall health of the body.

Brain: The brain is the command center of the entire body and directs all functions of the body, both voluntary and involuntary. We are able to move and our internal organs work because of the brain. It connects and communicates with nerves all throughout the body and with the spinal cord as well.

Heart: The heart is the powerful muscle that pumps our blood throughout our bodies within blood vessels. Blood is a system of transport for the body, moving vital molecules and transporting cells such as those of the immune system.

Lungs: The lungs allow us to take in oxygen, a gas necessary for life, and release carbon dioxide, a waste product. We breathe air into our lungs and oxygen is exchanged between tiny structures called alveoli and blood vessels called capillaries. Carbon dioxide follows the opposite path.

Liver: The liver is an organ that filters our blood and removes toxins from our bodies. It also secretes a fluid called bile that is needed for digestion, and produces important proteins for the body.

Kidneys: The kidneys remove waste products from our blood, moving them into urine, which will be excreted from the body. We have two kidneys, but can live with just one.

Skin: The skin is the largest organ of the body and serves as a protective barrier between our inside environment and the outside world. It keeps fluids in and helps keep potential pathogens out.

Stomach: The stomach is the organ into which our food moves after we chew and swallow, and it produces strong digestive enzymes to break down our food.

Intestines: The small intestine receives the partially digested food matter from the stomach and continues digestion. Nutrients are absorbed into the blood stream through the walls of the small intestine. The large intestine absorbs water and prepares feces to be excreted from the body.

Bladder: The bladder is a small sac that collects the urine that will be excreted from the body.

18. give the system,major structures,and functions of the "MAJOR ORGANS AND THEIR FUNCTION"​


The brain

Its functions include muscle control and coordination, sensory reception and integration, speech production, memory storage, and the elaboration of thought and emotion.

The lungs

Their essential function is to provide oxygen from inhaled air to the bloodstream and to exhale carbon dioxide.

The liver

Its main function is to process the contents of the blood to ensure composition remains the same.

The bladder

The bladder is a muscular organ located in the pelvic cavity. It stretches to store urine and contracts to release urine.

The kidneys

Their function is to maintain the body’s chemical balance by excreting waste products and excess fluid in the form of urine.

The heart

The heart is a hollow, muscular organ that pumps blood through the blood vessels by repeated, rhythmic contractions.

The stomach

Its main purpose is digestion of food through production of gastric juices which break down, mix and churn the food into a thin liquid.

The intestines

The function of the small intestine is to absorb most ingested food. The large intestine is responsible for absorption of water and excretion of solid waste material.
