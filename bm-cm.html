1. Given: AD = AN Prove: BD/CN=BM/CM


Refer to attached photo.

Construct [tex]DH[/tex] such that [tex]DH[/tex] is parallel to [tex]CN.[/tex] Clearly,

[tex]\angle DMH=\angle CMN=\gamma[/tex]

By corresponding angles theorem, we can get that

[tex]\begin{cases} \angle MDH=\angle MNC=\alpha \\ \angle DHM=\angle MCN=\beta \end{cases}[/tex]

Thus, using the results, we find that [tex]\triangle CMN[/tex] is similar to [tex]\triangle MDH,[/tex] and it follows that

[tex]\displaystyle \frac{DH}{HM}=\frac{CN}{CM} \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad\quad\quad (1)[/tex]

Given that [tex]AD=AN,[/tex] then [tex]\angle ADN=\angle AND,[/tex] so that by vertical angles theorem,

[tex]\displaystyle \angle AND=\angle MNC=\alpha[/tex]

Which implies that

[tex]\angle ADN=\angle MDH=\alpha[/tex]

Notice that [tex]\angle ADN[/tex] and [tex]\angle MDH[/tex] form the exterior angle of [tex]\angle BDH[/tex]wherein [tex]DM[/tex] bisects the exterior angle. Therefore, by exterior angle bisector theorem, we can conclude that

[tex]\displaystyle \frac{BD}{BM}=\frac{DH}{HM}[/tex]

From (1), we get

[tex]\displaystyle \frac{BD}{BM}=\frac{CN}{CM} \implies \boxed{\frac{BD}{CN}=\frac{BM}{CM}}[/tex]

22. Given that point M is the midpoint of line segment AB, by the definition of mid-point, the measurement of AM and BM are equal . Since AM ≅BM, then by definition of congruent segments, prove that line segment AM is congruent to line segment BM​


Proof:

Given: Point M is the midpoint of line segment AB, and AM ≅BM

To prove: Line segment AM is congruent to line segment BM

Proof:

Since Point M is the midpoint of line segment AB, then by definition of midpoint, the measurement of AM and BM are equal.

Since AM ≅BM, then by definition of congruent segments, line segment AM is congruent to line segment BM.

Therefore, line segment AM is congruent to line segment BM.
