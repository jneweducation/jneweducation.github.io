9. Statement I – Manufacturers provide tangible products. Statement II – Manufacturing companies use raw materials, components, or parts which are processed using machines, computers, and labor to produce finished goods. a. Both statements are true. b. Only Statement I is true. c. Only Statement II is true. d. Both statements are false.


Great question! Now allow me to give you a great answer that will help you understand the core concept of a manufacturing business.

The correct answer is a. Both statements are true.

Why do I say so? Well, Let's take a look at what a manufacturer is.

Manufacturing Company

A manufacturing company or a manufacturer is mainly involved in the production of physical products. Physical products are things that we can hold, use, see, and consume. Physical products are also called tangible products.

Remember, physical or tangible products are different from services. Services are a series of activities, a software, or an application that allows one to finish or carry out a job.

To create a product, several components are needed.

Components of a Product

Raw Materials

These are materials that are directly sourced from nature or materials that cannot be used alone.

Examples: vegetables, sand, leaves

Producer Goods or Other Materials

These are made from raw materials but are not necessarily final products on its own because they are used to create another product.

Examples: cement, paint, screws

Labor

This is the work that takes the materials together to create a product.

Examples: manual labor from people, machines, computer software or applications

Overhead

These are materials or things that are needed to make the product but they are not seen in the final product itself.

Examples: electricity, water, gas, rent

Statement II – The products of manufacturing companies can be sold directly to consumers only. a. Both statements are true. b. Only Statement I is true. c. Only Statement II is true. d. Both statements are false.


Hello! I'm so glad you asked this question on Brainly because other students are also wondering about this. I'm here to provide you an answer and a corresponding explanation.

The correct answer is d. Both statements are false.

Statement: Manufacturers typically employ large-scale production which is done in  manufacturing plants.Validity: FalseReason

Manufacturers are makers of goods and products. The production of goods may either be large-scale or small-scale depending on the available capital, demand, the complexity of the product, and the skill required for production.

This means that a person or a small group of people creating a product can be called manufacturers themselves. When we say manufacturing or production, it does not necessarily refer to large factories or big corporations. It can also be small or medium-sized enterprises.

Statement: The products of manufacturing companies can be sold directly to  consumers only.Validity: FalseReason

Companies operate differently from one another. They create different products that have varying shelf life and demand. Some products have to handled delicately while others can take on more beating.

Some manufacturers know the exact people who need their products and have the ability to directly contact them while other companies focus their efforts on production and will leave distribution to a different team or company. There are also manufacturers that prefer to tie themselves under exclusivity clauses which guarantees them a client while others will have clients no matter what.

This is why manufacturing companies have different distribution channels. Not all manufacturers choose to sell directly to their final consumers while others may choose to have several levels of distribution channels.

The correct answer to your question is a. Both statements are true.

By looking into the activity both types of business are engaged in, we can correctly determine the validity of the two statements. Now, let's take a closer look at why these statements are correct by defining these two types of business.

Manufacturing Business

This business is mainly involved in the production of goods or products. To produce these products, they need the following:

raw materialsother goods (also known as producer goods)labor (may either come from people or machines)factory overhead such as electricity, water, rent

They product they produce may either be producer goods or consumer goods.

Producer goods are products that are used to create another product. Examples are be nails, glue, paint, fabric, thread, and cardboard.Consumer goods are products that are bought by consumers and not makers of other products. Examples are clothes, hamburgers, cellphones, bags, and books.

Manufacturers not only create products, they also have to make sure that their products are sold. To sell these products, they may go through one several different channels of distribution.

Some manufacturers also offer services. Usually these service are provided in conjunction or after the sale of a product. For example, a sewing machine manufacturer may provide services to their customers because when these machines break down or malfunction, they are the ones who know best how to troubleshoot and repair it. But do remember, services are not their primary source of revenue.
