27. patricia of the green hills moral lesson​


Answer:

The story is a reminder that we should be reflexive of other cultures and way of life.Not because we find that this particular way of life serve us well it will automatically be case for others .Happiness is arbitrary as the saying goes " one Man's poison is another Man's meat." we cannot appropriate what is good for other people other than that of ourselves. For only you know what your heart truly desires.It is also a call for us, who are too much preoccupied with the superficialities of urbanlife, that there is more to life than the comforts and ease of the city. Humanity often mistakesitself as the creator of truths, the center of all things and the benefactor of everything making hima threat to nature, the species, and ultimately to himself. We should remember that we are only apart of a whole with that whole being the world wherein we dwell. Embrace nature as it is and letit be.We can see in the character of Patricia how one can be freed from the very fast mode of existence that is common in the life of the modern man and how one can be contented by merelybeing able to experience life as it is without the complexities of contemporary life. Life is aprecious gift and we should celebrate it as slowly as we possibly can.
