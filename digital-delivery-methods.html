1. differences of four methods of speech delivery​


Impromptu speech is the delivery without prior planning of a short message. Impromptu speeches also happen when someone is asked on a special occasion to "say a few words" or toast for someone.

Extemporary speech is the delivery of a carefully designed and scripted voice, spoken with short notes in a conversational manner.

The word-for-word version of a written note is manuscript speech. In a manuscript speech, except when using reference material, the speaker retains his or her attention on the printed page.

Memorized speech is the rote recitation of a written message engaged to memory by the speaker. Memorization can be effective when it comes to speeches when the message has to be precise and the speaker doesn't want to be limited to notes.

13. What are the different methods of speech delivery? Describe each one.​


Answer:

Different methods of speech delivery:

•Manuscript

A manuscript speech is when the speaker writes down every word they will speak dur ing the speech. When they deliver the speech, they have each word planned and in front of them on the page, much like a newscaster who reads from a teleprompter.

•Memorized

A memorized speech is also fully prepared in advance and one in which the speaker does not use any notes. In the case of an occasion speech like a quick toast, a brief dedication, or a short eulogy, word-for word memorization might make sense.

•Impromptu

An impromptu speech is one for which there is little to no preparation. There is of ten not a warning even that the person may be asked to speak. For example, your speech teacher may ask you to deliver a speech on your worst pet peeve. You may or may not be given a few minutes to orga nize your thoughts.

•Extemporaneous

The last method of delivery we'll look at is extemporaneous. When speaking extemporaneously, speakers prepare some notes in advance that help trigger their memory of what they planned to say. These notes are often placed on notecards.
