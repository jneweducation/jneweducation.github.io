7. Through a refinery, fuel ethanol is flowing in a pipe at a velocity of 1 m/s and a pressure of 101300 Pa. The refinery needs the ethanol to be at a pressure of 2 atm (202600 Pa) on a lower level. How far must the pipe drop in height in order to achieve this pressure? Assume the velocity does not change. (Hint: Use the Bernoulli equation. The density of ethanol is 789 kg/m3 and gravity g is 9.8 m/s2. Pay attention to units!)


The height must be 13,101 m lower

Further explanation

Bernoulli stated :

The sum of the pressure, kinetic energy, and potential energy per volume at any point along the fluid flow is the same.

[tex]\tt P+\dfrac{1}{2}\rho v^2+\rho gh=constant[/tex]

Given

v = 1 m/s ⇒ constant

P₁ = 101300 Pa (1 atm)

P₂ = 202600 Pa (2 atm)

ρ ethanol = 789 kg/m3

g = 9.8 m/s2

Required

The height(lower level)

Solution

Because v₁=v₂ constant, the Bernoulli equation becomes :

ρgh₁+P₁=ρgh₂+P₂

Solve for the value (height difference):

P₁-P₂=ρgh₂-ρgh₁

P₁-P₂=ρg(h₂-h₁)

(P₁-P₂)/(ρg) = Δh

[tex]\tt \dfrac{-101300~kg/ms^2}{789~kg/m^3\times 9.8~m/s^2}=\Delta h\\\\\Delta h=-13.101~m[/tex]
