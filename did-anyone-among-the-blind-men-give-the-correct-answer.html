7. Did anyone among the blind men give the correct answer? Why or why not


No one among the six blind men were able to give the correct answer. If we recall, the story talks about six blind men hearing about the elephant for the first time. Then then set out to discover what it is. They touched various parts of the elephant and came to six different conclusions of what an elephant is. They even argued and thought that each other was lying. The versions differ but the lesson remains primarily the same

The Lesson  in the Parable

This tells us that we are limited by what we know, unless we see the bigger picture.  Or someone points it out for us. Sometimes people focus only on their own experience without regard for the other experiences. In other versions of the parable, it was said that they talked among themselves so that they could get a holistic picture of the elephant. And then a wise man stepped up to tell then they did not get it correct.

So it could be viewed as a lesson on conflict management as well since the men had to take a step back, and then work out their differences - a tactic used in conflict management. A wise man can also be likened to a leader who is always ready to provide guidance in case the men are lost.

25. HOW DID THE ELEPHANT EXPERIENCE OF THE BLIND MEN END?


Answer:

The elephant experience of the blind men ended up with the men getting confused as to the exact nature of the animal. There are two reasons why this happened. First, the men unfortunately touched and felt different parts of the elephant. This resulted to the men thinking that the part that they touched is the elephant already as a whole. Second, the men were insistent that the animal part they touched is the entirety already of the animal. They failed to listen to each other and to work together in order to paint a better picture and then get a better overview of the elephant.
