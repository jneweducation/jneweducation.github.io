FINDING THE LCM

PROBLEM:

What is the Least Common Multiple of 28 and 58?

The LCM or Least Common Multiple of 28 and 58 is 812.

STEP-BY-STEP EXPLANATION:

Let's use to solve both numbers by their prime factorization.

28 = 2 × 2 × 758 = 2 × 29

Therefore, let's multiply their prime factorization to find the LCM or Least Common Denominator.

2 × 2 × 7 × 29 = 812
