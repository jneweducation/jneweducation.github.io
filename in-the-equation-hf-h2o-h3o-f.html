25. 6.) Calculate the standard enthalpy change, ∆H° , for the following reaction:2CH3OH (l) + 3O2 (g) --› 2CO2 (g) + 4H2O (l)Given:CH3OH (l) ∆Hf° = -238.7kJ/molCO2 (g) ∆Hf° = -393.5kJ/molH2O (l) ∆Hf° = -285.8kJ/molanswer plssss...​


Strategy:

The general formula for the standard enthalpy change of reaction is

[tex]\Delta H_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = \sum n \Delta H_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{products}) - \sum m \Delta H_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{reactants})[/tex]

where:

[tex]\Delta H_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ}[/tex] = standard enthalpy change of reaction

n = stoichiometric coefficients of each product

m = stoichiometric coefficients of each reactant

[tex]\Delta H_{\text{f}}^{\circ}[/tex] = standard enthalpy change of formation

[tex]\sum[/tex] = "the sum of"

Solution:

Note: The standard enthalpy change of formation of any element in its most stable form is zero.

In the given balanced chemical equation, molecular oxygen (O₂) is one of the reactants. Since molecular oxygen is the most stable allotrope of oxygen, its standard enthalpy change of formation is zero.

Therefore, the standard enthalpy change of reaction as described by balanced chemical equation is

[tex]\Delta H_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = [2\Delta H_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{CO}_{2}) + 4\Delta H_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{H}_{2}\text{O})] - [2\Delta H_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{CH}_{3}\text{OH}) + 3\Delta H_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{O}_{2})][/tex]

[tex]\Delta H_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = [2(-\text{393.5 kJ/mol}) + 4(-\text{285.8 kJ/mol})] - [2(-\text{238.7 kJ/mol}) + 3(0)][/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\Delta H_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = -\text{1452.8 kJ/mol}}[/tex]

The rate determining step for any reaction mechanism is the slowest step of the proposed mechanism.
