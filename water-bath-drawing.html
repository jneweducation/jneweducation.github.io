why do you need to use hot oil bath instead of water bath?

What is the purpose of oil bath?

An oil bath is a type of heated bath used in a laboratory, most commonly used to heat up chemical reactions. It's essentially a container of oil that is heated by a hot plate or (in rare cases) a Bunsen burner.

8. which of the following ailments. will you acquired in bathing in a dirty water​


Answer:

ALLERGY

Explanation:

Bathing in contaminated water can cause certain health problems: Swimmer's itch (skin irritation caused by contact with small larvae called 'cercarias', which are found in some lakes) Gastroenteritis. Eye, ear and throat infections.

Description

Bathing waters include beaches, lakes, rivers and artificial pools such as indoor and outdoor pools and spas. Bathing waters may contain microorganisms (blue-green algae and other bacteria, viruses, parasites) or other pollutants. People can also contaminate bathing waters with their secretions, urine, feces or products such as sunscreen.

Chlorine is used for disinfection of water in pools and spas to prevent contamination with disease-causing microorganisms. However, chlorine can react with organic matter in water to produce toxic substances called ‘chloramines’. These chloramines spread in the air and give the characteristic chlorine smell of indoor pools. Chloramines can also cause irritation of the respiratory tract.

Bathing in contaminated water can cause certain health problems:

•Swimmer's itch (skin irritation caused by contact with small larvae called ‘cercarias’, which are found in some lakes)

•Eye, ear and throat infections

•Health problems caused by blue-green algae blooms

•Legionellosis (an infection caused by the bacterium Legionella, which is especially prevalent in spas)

Heat and Lysis (lysis in biology generally means "cutting" - and hydrolysis is "cutting with water") help to make sure the DNA is fully removed from the cell by getting rid of all the parts of the cell that keep it intact.

Answer:

The answer is most likely that the Lysis solution helps in the breakdown of any membranes that DNA may be trapped in. By removing all the components of the cell that keep the DNA intact, heat and lysis aid in ensuring that the DNA is completely eliminated from the cell.

Explanation:

In procedures like DNA extraction, cells are split open using lysis solutions, which are buffer solutions. Ionic salts and buffer salts make up lysis solutions. These salts control the lysate's pH and osmolarity. The components of the lysed cells are kept separate by the buffer present in the lysis solution. Usually, more than one salt is included in a lysis solution. The salts in the lysis solution give the solution more ionic strength.

The proteins on the cell membrane are helped to denature by heating the onion cells in a water bath. DNA may be extracted from certain onion cell locations with the aid of heating. The membrane proteins are denatured while the onion cells are heated, which speeds up the lysis solution's process. Additionally, it aids in the deactivation of inhibitors of enzymatic reactions.

28. Water from a tap flows into a bath at a rate of 750 ml/s. How long does it take to fill a bath with 180 ℓ of water?


LITERS==============================

» Water flows at the rate of 750 milliliters per second. How much time does it take to fill a 180 Liters of water?

» First is we need to convert milliliters to liters

[tex] \sf \large 1 \: ml = 0.001 \: L \\ \sf \large1 \times 750 \: ml = 0.001 \times 750 \: L \\ \sf \large 750 \: ml = \boxed{\sf \: 0.75 \: L \: } \: \: [/tex]

» Now, if the water runs 0.75 Liters per second. How much time does it take to fill a 180 Liters of water?

» Let (s) as the seconds which will take 180 Liters to fill. Use Proportion. Or if you wanted to make it easier, just divide 180 into 0.75

[tex] \implies \sf \large \frac{0.75 \: liter}{1 \: sec.} = \frac{180 \: liters}{(s) \: sec.} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \implies \sf \large 0.75s = 180[/tex]

[tex] \implies \sf \large \frac{ \cancel{0.75} s}{ \cancel{0.75}} = \frac{180}{0.75} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \implies \sf \large (s) \: sec = 240[/tex]

» So it will only take 240 seconds to fill a 180 Liters bathtub. If we convert that into minutes, it will only exactly take...

Final Answer:
