3. The radioactive decay of Tl-206 to Pb-206 has a half-life of 4.20 min. Starting with 5.00 x 1022 atoms of Tl-206, calculate the number of such atoms left after 42.0 min.


Answer:

[tex]n(t) = n {e}^{ -lambda \times \: t} [/tex]

lambda = ln 2 / half-life

= ln 2 / 4.20 min

= 0.165035043 min^-1

n(t) = 5.00 × 10^22e^(-0.165035043min-1 × 42.0 min)

= 4.8828125 × 10^19

# of atoms after 42 mins: 4.88 × 10^19 atoms
