A tangent segment is a segment that has one endpoint at the point of tangency and another endpoint on the tangent line (which is perpendicular to the radius of the circle that has the point of tangency as its endpoint).

A secant line is a line that intersects a circle at two points. It is derived from the Latin word secare, which means to cut. In other words, the secant line is the extension of the chord of a circle that goes beyond the circle.

30. 2. Which of the following theorems states that when the chords intersects with each other inside the circle, the products of their segments are equal. A. Two intersecting chords B. Secant-secant Segments C. Tangent-secant Segments D. Two intersecting Secant


Answer:

A. TWO INTERSECTING CHORDS

Step-by-step explanation:

The intersecting chords theorem or just The chord theorem is a statement in elementary geometry that describes a relation of the four line segments created by two intersecting chords within a circle. It states that the products of the lengths of the line segments on each chord are equal.
