Answer:

Angiosperms

Explanation:

Angiosperms or flowering plants store carbohydrates and sugars in their taproots. A taproot is a dominant and central root where other roots sprout from laterally. Usually, a taproot is straight and thick and grows downwards.

6. what enzyme breaks the carbohydrates into sugar​


Answer:

When people eat a food containing carbohydrates, the digestive system breaks down the digestible ones into sugar, which enters the blood. As blood sugar levels rise, the pancreas produces insulin, a hormone that prompts cells to absorb blood sugar for energy or storage.

22. what is the food nutrient in carbohydrate sugars?​


Answer:

Carbohydrates, or carbs, are sugar molecules. Along with proteins and fats, carbohydrates are one of three main nutrients found in foods and drinks. Your body breaks down carbohydrates into glucose. Glucose, or blood sugar, is the main source of energy for your body's cells, tissues, and organs.
