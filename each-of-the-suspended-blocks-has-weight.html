To solve this problem analytically, we can start by drawing a free-body diagram of the block and string. The weight of the block acts downward, and the tension in the string acts upward. The horizontal force of 100 lbs can be resolved into components parallel and perpendicular to the string.

Using trigonometry, we can find that the force parallel to the string is 100cos(60) = 50 lbs, and the force perpendicular to the string is 100sin(60) = 86.6 lbs.

To find the weight of the block, we can use the fact that the block is in equilibrium, so the net force acting on it must be zero. This means that the weight of the block must be equal and opposite to the force parallel to the string, or W = 50 lbs.

To find the force on the upper portion of the string, we can use the fact that the tension in the string is the same throughout. The tension can be found by resolving the forces vertically, and we get T*cos(60) = W = 50 lbs. Therefore, T = 100 lbs.

To check our answers by graphical method, we can draw a vector diagram using the same free-body diagram. We draw the weight vector downward, the tension vector upward, and the horizontal force vector to the right. The vectors should form a closed triangle, which confirms that the block is in equilibrium. We can measure the lengths of the vectors and use trigonometry to find the same values we calculated analytically.

In summary, the weight of the block is 50 lbs, and the force on the upper portion of the string is 100 lbs. These values can be found analytically using trigonometry and checked graphically by drawing a vector diagram.

A small wooden block with mass 0.800 kg is suspended from the lower end of a light cord that is 1.60 m long. The block is initially at rest. A bullet with mass 12.0 g is fired at the block with a horizontal velocity [tex]v_{0} [/tex]. The bullet strikes the block and becomes embedded in it. After the collision, the combined object swings on the end of the cord. When the block has risen a vertical height of 0.800 m, the tension in the cord is 4.80 N. What was the initial speed [tex]v_{0} [/tex] of the bullet?

Solution:

The question clearly shows an example of inelastic collision since the objects stick together after the collision. Two types of "conservation" are also shown: (1) conservation of momentum during the collision and (2) conservation of mechanical energy after the collision.

Also, take note that Newton's second law of motion, F = ma, will relate tension in the cord to the speed of the block while it is swinging.

(F = force; m= mass; and a = acceleration)

Step 1: Finding the angle the cord makes with the vertical

cos θ = [tex] \frac{0.8 m}{1.6 m} = 0.50[/tex]

θ = 60.0 degrees

Step 2: Finding the speed (v) of the block considering Newton's 2nd Law of Motion (NOTE: Force components were taken toward the center of the circle.)

T - mgcosθ = m[tex] \frac{v^{2} }{R} [/tex]

Therefore,

v = [tex] \sqrt{ \frac{R}{m} (T-mgcos (theta)[/tex]

(NOTE: theta = θ. I can't put the symbol inside.The value of mass (m) should be the combined mass of the wooden block (0.800 kg) and the mass of the bullet (12 g = 0.012 kg).)

v = [tex] \sqrt{ \frac{1.6 m}{0.812 kg}(4.80 N - 3.979 N) } [/tex]

v = 1.272 m/s


Step 3: Apply the conservation of energy to find the velocity of the combined object

[tex] \frac{1}{2} m V^{2} = mgh + \frac{1}{2} m v^{2} \\ V = \sqrt{2gh + v^{2} } \\ V = \sqrt{2(9.8 m/s^{2})(0.8 m) + (1.272 m/s)^{2}} \\ V = 4.159 m/s[/tex]


Step 4: Apply the conservation of momentum

[tex](0.012 kg) v_{0} = (0.812 kg)(4.159 m/s) \\ v_{0} = 281 m/s[/tex]

28. Explain the impact of the suspending of the Brezhnev Doctrine has on Eastern Europe.


Answer

The Brezhnev Doctrine was implemented from 1968 until the death of Leonid Brezhnev in 1982. It was a foreign policy that allowed the Soviet Union to intervene Easter European countries with Socialist government whenever Socialism was under 'threat'. The Brezhnev Doctrine also included intervention with military affairs.  

The Brezhnev Doctrine was still in effect in Eastern Europe until Mikhail Gorbachev became the Soviet Union leader in 1985. Because of Mikhail's political view leaning towards democracy, he rejected the Brezhnev Doctrine. Mikhail Gorbachev favored the democratic political system and wanted a decentralized free economy for Russia. He didn't intervene with other Eastern European governments switching to Democratic Policies. This decision of Mikhail Gorbachev led to the end of the Socialist government of the Soviet Union on December 26, 1991.
