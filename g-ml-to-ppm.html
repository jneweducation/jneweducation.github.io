4. How many grams of NACl required to prepare each of the following solutions of 2500 ppm(w/v)250 ml solution ​


To calculate the number of grams of NaCl required to prepare a 2500 ppm(w/v) solution with a volume of 250 ml

we need to use the following formula:

mass (in grams) = (ppm x volume x molecular weight) / 10^6

where:

ppm = parts per million

volume = volume of solution (in mL)

molecular weight = molecular weight of the compound

For NaCl, the molecular weight is 58.44 g/mol.

Substituting the given values into the formula, we get:

mass (in grams) = (2500 ppm x 250 mL x 58.44 g/mol) / 10^6

mass (in grams) = 36.525 g

Therefore, we need 36.525 grams of NaCl to prepare a 2500 ppm(w/v) solution with a volume of 250 mL.

20. A solution is prepared by dissolving 0.50 g of Na2HPO4 in 500.0 mL of water. The density of the solution is 1.00 g/mL. Express the solution concentration in (1) molarity, (2) molality, (3) ppm and (4) % (m/m).​


To solve for the solution concentration in different units, we need to use the following formulas:

Molarity (M) = moles of solute / liters of solutionMolality (m) = moles of solute / mass of solvent in kgParts per million (ppm) = (mass of solute / mass of solution) x 10^6Percent (m/m) = (mass of solute / mass of solution) x 100%

Given:

Mass of Na2HPO4 = 0.50 g

Volume of water = 500.0 mL

Density of solution = 1.00 g/mL

To calculate the number of moles of Na2HPO4, we need to divide the mass by the molar mass:

Molar mass of Na2HPO4 = 2(23.0 g/mol) + 1(31.0 g/mol) + 4(16.0 g/mol) = 142.0 g/mol

Moles of Na2HPO4 = 0.50 g / 142.0 g/mol = 0.0035 mol

Molarity (M) = moles of solute / liters of solutionMolarity = 0.0035 mol / 0.500 L = 0.0070 M

Molality (m) = moles of solute / mass of solvent in kgMass of water = volume x density = 500.0 mL x 1.00 g/mL = 500.0 gMolality = 0.0035 mol / 0.500 kg = 0.0070 m

Parts per million (ppm) = (mass of solute / mass of solution) x 10^6Mass of solution = mass of solute + mass of solvent = 0.50 g + 500.0 g = 500.5 gPPM = (0.50 g / 500.5 g) x 10^6 = 999 ppm

Percent (m/m) = (mass of solute / mass of solution) x 100%Percent (m/m) = (0.50 g / 500.5 g) x 100% = 0.0999% (rounded to 3 decimal places)

25. If 1000 g of water from Luboc River contains 0.0075 g of Ca ion, how many ppm by mass of Ca is present in the River?


Parts per Million by Mass (ppm)Discussion:

Parts per million (ppm) by mass is the ratio of the mass of a solute to the mass of the solution, multiplied by 1 million or 10⁶. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} × 10^{6}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 10^{6}}[/tex]

The parts per million by mass is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

If 1000 g of water from Luboc River contains 0.0075 g of Ca ion, how many ppm by mass of Ca is present in the River?

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 0.0075 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent = 1000 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute} = \text{Ca ion}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solvent} = \text{H}_{2}\text{O}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{parts per million by mass}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 10^{6}[/tex]

[tex]\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{0.0075 g}}{\text{0.0075 g + 1000 g}} × 10^{6}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{parts per million by mass = 7.5 ppm}}[/tex]

26. Calculate the percentage by mass of of Na+ in a 100 mL NaCl-water solution (density = 1.03 g/mL) with a concentration of 35000 ppm (m /v).


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_{\text{solution}} = \text{100 mL} \\ & d_{\text{solution}} = \text{1.03 g/mL} \\ & ppm(m/v) = \text{35,000 ppm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solute (Na⁺).

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{ppm(m/v)}{10^6 \: \text{ppm}} \times V_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{35,000 ppm}}{10^6 \: \text{ppm}} \times \text{100 mL} \\ & = \text{3.5 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solution}} & = d_{\text{solution}}V_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \text{(1.03 g/mL)(100 mL)} \\ & = \text{103 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the percent by mass of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%(m/m) & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{mass_{\text{solution}}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{3.5 g}}{\text{103 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{3.4\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the percentage by mass of Na⁺ in a 100 mL solution is 3.4%.

Sure, here are the solutions for each question:

A) A sample of solution is prepared by dissolving 15.0 grams of NaCl in 70.0 grams of water.

a. What is the mass of solution in the sample?

The mass of the solution is calculated by adding the masses of the two components:

Mass of solution = Mass of NaCl + Mass of water

= 15.0 g + 70.0 g = 85.0 g

Therefore, the mass of the solution is 85.0 grams.

b. What is the mass percent of solute in the solution?

The mass percent of solute is calculated by dividing the mass of the solute by the mass of the solution and then multiplying by 100:

Mass % solute = (Mass of NaCl / Mass of solution) * 100%

= (15.0 g/1000 g) * 100%
