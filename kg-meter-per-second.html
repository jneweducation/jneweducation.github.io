2. If a net force of 50 Newtons was applied to an object with a mass of 5 kg, what will the object's acceleration be? a. 10 meter per second squarecb. 100 meter per second squaredc. 1 meter per second squaredd. 1000 meter per second squared​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The acceleration of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{a = \frac{F}{m}}[/tex]

where:

a = acceleration

F = force

m = mass

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the acceleration

[tex]a = \dfrac{F}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{50 N}}{\text{5 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{10 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is a. 10 meter per second squared.

7. What is the kinetic energy of a 10 kg bicycle moving at 2 meters per second?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for KE

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{10 kg})(\text{2 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{20 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the bicycle is 20 J.

[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \frac{F}{a}}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the mass

[tex]m = \dfrac{F}{a}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{24 N}}{\text{3 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{8 kg}}[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is B. 8 kg.

The value of "g" or acceleration due to gravity on Earth is approximately 9.81 meters per second squared.

If the weight of a table is 150 N, its mass would be approximately 15.3 kg, calculated as follows:

Weight = Mass x Acceleration due to gravity (W = m x g)

150 N = m x 9.81 m/s^2

m = 150 N / 9.81 m/s^2

m ≈ 15.3 kg

14. A boy weighs 50 kg and is running 3 meters per second, what is the kinetic energy?


• Question:

A boy weighs 50 kg and is running 3 meters per second, what is the kinetic energy?

• Given:

[tex] \tt mass \: (m)= 50 \: kg [/tex]

[tex] \tt velocity\: (v)= 3 \: m/s[/tex]

• Solution:

✓You need to use the formula to find the kinetic energy of the boy.

[tex] \tt KE= \frac{1}{2} mv {}^{2} \\ [/tex]

✓Substitute the given values to the formula and then solve.

[tex] \tt KE= \frac{1}{2} mv {}^{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt KE= \frac{1}{2} (50kg )(3m/s) {}^{2} [/tex]

[tex] \tt KE= \frac{1}{2}(50kg)(9m {}^{2}/s {}^{2} )[/tex]

[tex] \tt KE= \frac{50 kg \times 9m {}^{2} }{2s {}^{2} } \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt KE= \frac{450 \: kgm {}^{2} }{2s {}^{2} } \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt KE=225 \frac{kgm {}^{2} }{s {}^{2} } \\ [/tex]

• Answer:

✓The quantity 225 kgm²/s² is equal to 225 J. Therefore, the kinetic energy of the boy is 225 J.
