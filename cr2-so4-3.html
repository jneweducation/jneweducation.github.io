6. fe + h2S04 = fe2(SO4)3 + h2 =​


Good Day

Given:

                       Fe + H₂S0₄  ⇒ Fe₂(SO4)₃  + H₂

The type of reaction is single replacement in which Fe (iron) is more active that H (hydrogen) thus, it replaces the place of Hydrogen. Fe can only replace Hydrogen since they have the same charge. Fe cannot replace the place of SO₄ since they have different charge.

To Balance the equation:

                          2Fe + 3H₂S0₄  ⇒ Fe₂(SO4)₃  + 3H₂                                       ↓                          ↓                                Reactant              Product                                  Fe = 2                    Fe = 2                                  H = 6                     H = 6                                       S0₄ = 3                   S0₄ = 3

We balance the chemical equation since atoms are not destroyed in the process and the mass is conserved. The mass of the reactant is equal to the mass of the product which also means, the number of atoms of elements in both the product and reactant are equal.

In balancing the chemical equation, we need to follow the law of conservation of mass. The mass of the reactant is equal to the mass of the product. Which means, the number of atoms in the reactant is equal to the number of atoms in the product.

                     Fe  = 1                 Fe = 1

8. What is the percent composistion of Al2(SO4)3? ​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Al₂(SO₄)₃.

molar mass = (26.98 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 3) + (16.00 g/mol × 12)

molar mass = 342.17 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the percentage composition of Al₂(SO₄)₃.

General Formula:

[tex]\text{percent composition} = \frac{\text{atomic mass of an element × number of atoms in a compound}}{\text{molar mass of compound}} × 100[/tex]

For %Al

[tex]\text{\%Al} = \frac{\text{26.98 g/mol × 2}}{\text{342.17 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{\%Al = 15.77\%}}[/tex]

For %S

[tex]\text{\%S} = \frac{\text{32.07 g/mol × 3}}{\text{342.17 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{\%S = 28.12\%}}[/tex]

For %O

[tex]\text{\%O} = \frac{\text{16.00 g/mol × 12}}{\text{342.17 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{\%O = 56.11\%}}[/tex]

22. How many moles of Fe2(SO4)3 are present in 300 grams of Fe2(SO4)3 ?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Fe₂(SO₄)₃.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass} & = \text{2(55.85 g) + 3(32.07 g) + 12(16.00 g)} \\ & = \text{399.9 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Fe₂(SO₄)₃ by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3 & = \text{300 g} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3}{\text{399.9 g} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.750 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there is 0.750 mol of Fe₂(SO₄)₃ in 300 g of Fe₂(SO₄)₃.
