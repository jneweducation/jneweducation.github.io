1. What is a screen tip?​


Answer:

ScreenTip

By Vangie Beal

A graphical user interface (GUI) feature in which a small text box appears when a mouse pointer is hovered over an icon or button. The pop-up window will provide details that explain the icon's or button's function. In some instances, though, the ScreenTip will display only the item's name. When the mouse is moved away from the icon or button, the ScreenTip will disappear from view.

A ScreenTip is also called a ToolTip.

15. 1. what is screen painting 2.what materials can be used to make a screen print?


Answer:

what is screen painting?

Screen printing is a printing technique where a mesh is used to transfer ink onto a substrate, except in areas made impermeable to the ink by a blocking stencil. A blade or squeegee is moved across the screen to fill the open mesh apertures with ink, and a reverse stroke then causes the screen to touch the substrate momentarily along a line of contact. This causes the ink to wet the substrate and be pulled out of the mesh apertures as the screen springs back after the blade has passed. One colour is printed at a time, so several screens can be used to produce a multi-coloured image or design.

Definitions of Point, Line and Plane

The point, line and plane are some of the basic concepts that you are going to encounter in studying Geometry. You can find each of them everywhere around you since different objects can represent point, line and plane. Let us define each of them to understand them better.

Point

It is the most basic building block of Geometry. A point in Geometry refers to a location.It has no size and shape.It is represented by a small dot.It is named with a capital letter.Some examples of objects that represent a point are the tip of a pen, a mole and a grain of salt.

Line

A line is a straight and continuous arrangement of infinitely many points. It extends in both directions.It has infinite length but no thickness.It is represented by a straight line with two arrowheads. The two points on the line are used in naming a line.Some examples of objects that represent a line are the edge of a wall, thread and strand of hair.

Plane

It is a flat two-dimensional surface that contains points, lines and segments.It has length and width but no thickness.It extends infinitely along its length and width. A plane is named either by a script letter or three non-collinear points. Some examples of objects that represent a plane are the table cloth, bondpaper and TV screen.

24. Answer the following questions. 1. What is screen printing? 2. What materials can be used to make a screen print? 3. What are the steps in screen printing?​


Answer:

1. Screen printing is a printing technique where a mesh is used to transfer ink onto a substrate2. Fabric and paper3. Step 1: The design is created

To start, the printer takes the design they want to create on the finished product, and prints it out onto a transparent acetate film.

Step 2: The screen is prepfilm

Next, the printer will choose a mesh screen to suit the complexity of the design, and the texture of the fabric being printed.

Step 3: The emulsion is exposed

The acetate sheet featuring the design is then laid onto the emulsion-coated screen, and the whole thing is exposed to a very bright light.

Step 4: The emulsion is washed off, creating the stencil

After the screen has been exposed for a set time, the areas of the screen not covered by the design will have turned hard

Step 5: The Item Is Prepared For Phard

The screen is then placed on the printing press. The item or garment being printed is laid down flat onto the printing board, underneath the screen.

Step 6: The ink is pressed through the screen onto the item

The screen is lowered down onto the printing board. Ink is added to the top end of the screen, and a squeegee isused to pull the ink along the full length of the screen.

Step 7: The product is dried, checked and finished

The printed product then passes through a dryer, which 'cures' the ink and creates a smooth, colourfast finish. The final product will be checked and washed thoroughly to remove any residue, before being passed on to its new owner.

Explanation:
