8. what is the elements of story the "the comedian" by yoko morgenstern​


Answer:

The elements of a story typically include:

Characters: The people or creatures who drive the action in the story. In "The Comedian", the main characters are Nina, Chloe, Granny, Linda, and the comedian.

Plot: The sequence of events that make up the story. In "The Comedian", the plot follows Nina and Chloe as they perform at a gig, and the events that happen afterwards.

Setting: The time and place in which the story takes place. In "The Comedian", the story takes place in a small town called Kitchener, Canada.

Conflict: The struggle or problem that the characters face. In "The Comedian", the conflict is Nina's internal struggle with her identity and feeling of being stuck in a small town, and Chloe's struggle with her own choices and the temptation to leave the small town with a stranger.

Theme: The underlying message or idea that the story explores. In "The Comedian", the theme is the struggle of finding one's identity, the disappointment of unfulfilled dreams, and the feeling of being stuck in a small town.

Point of View: The perspective from which the story is told. In "The Comedian", the story is told from the third-person point of view, focusing on the thoughts and actions of the main character, Nina.

Style: The way in which the story is written, including the tone, language, and structure. In "The Comedian", the style is introspective and contemplative, using descriptive language to create vivid imagery and a sense of nostalgia.

Explanation:

The story "The Comedian" is a flash fiction about a young woman named Nina, who is a dancer, and her journey with her friend Chloe at their gig in a restaurant. The story follows Nina as she reflects on her life choices and her relationship with her grandmother. It also introduces a new character, the comedian, who offers to give her a ride home and starts a conversation with her. The story touches on themes of identity, disappointment, and the feeling of being stuck in a small town. It also examines the relationship between the characters and their personal struggles.
