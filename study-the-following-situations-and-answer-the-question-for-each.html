23. Learning task 6: Study the following situations and answer the question for each. Write your explanations on your answer sheet. 1. A book is placed on top of a table. Why does the book stay in place and not fall? 2. A rolling marble on top of a leveled surface gradually stops. How did it happen? 3. A block of wood was pushed and slid on the plank. How did gravity and friction affect the movement of the woof?


Answer:

1. The book stays in place and does not fall because of the force of gravity. The Earth's gravity pulls the book downwards towards the ground, but the table is pushing up on the book with an equal and opposite force, called the normal force. These two forces balance each other out, which keeps the book in place.

2. The rolling marble on a leveled surface gradually stops because of friction. Friction is the force that opposes the motion of the marble. As the marble rolls, it rubs against the surface of the table, which causes friction. This friction gradually slows down the marble until it stops.

3. When the block of wood is pushed and slid on the plank, gravity and friction affect its movement. Gravity pulls the block down towards the ground, while friction opposes the motion of the block on the plank. The amount of friction depends on the roughness of the surface of the plank and the weight of the block. If the surface of the plank is smooth and the block is light, there will be less friction, and the block will slide more easily. If the surface of the plank is rough and the block is heavy, there will be more friction, and the block will slide more slowly or not at all.
