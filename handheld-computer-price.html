16. Handheld; portable computers: mobile device that has bigger screen and can be use by both hands or a dedicated pen/stylus.​


Answer:

Handheld or portable computers are mobile devices that are designed to be easily carried and used on the go. These devices typically have a larger screen than smartphones, and they can be operated using both hands or a dedicated pen/stylus. Some examples of handheld computers include tablets, e-readers, and 2-in-1 laptops.

Tablets are a type of handheld computer that usually have a screen size between 7 and 13 inches. They are lightweight and slim, making them easy to carry around. Tablets can be used for a variety of tasks, including web browsing, watching videos, playing games, and reading e-books.

E-readers, on the other hand, are specialized handheld devices designed primarily for reading e-books. They typically have a larger screen than smartphones, but a smaller screen than tablets. E-readers use e-ink technology, which mimics the look of ink on paper and reduces eye strain.

2-in-1 laptops are a newer type of handheld computer that can be used as both a laptop and a tablet. These devices typically have a detachable keyboard or a 360-degree hinge that allows the screen to be rotated and used as a tablet.

Overall, handheld or portable computers provide users with the flexibility to work or entertain themselves while on the move, without being tied to a desktop computer or laptop.
