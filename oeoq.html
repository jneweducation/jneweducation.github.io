Answer:

EOQ = sqrt((2 * 1000 * 2) / 5) = sqrt(4000/5) = sqrt(800) = 28.3

11. Business Finance 4. A firm's annual usage of flour is 10 000 kilograms. The carrying cost is P5.00 per kilogram while the ordering cost is P100 every time an order is placed. If the firm's safety stock requirement is 100 kilograms, compute for the following (assume that there are 50 working weeks in a year): a. EOQ b. Number of times that orders should be placed in a year c. Reorder Point (ROP) 5. Assume that the beginning balance for inventory is 7000, the ending inventory is 9 000, and the cost of inventory per unit is P15.00, compute the investment in inventory. 6. Assume that your answer in number 9 is the amount of investment every month and for that amount, the firm will get 3% discount. What would be the discounted amount for inventory? What would be the total discount for one year?​


Answer:

4. To determine the Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) for the firm, you can use the following formula:

EOQ = √(2DS/H)

Where D is the annual demand for the product (in this case, flour), S is the ordering cost per unit, and H is the carrying cost per unit.

Plugging in the values given in the problem, you get:

EOQ = √(2(10 000 kg)(100)/5.00)

= √(200 000/5.00)

= √(40 000)

= 200

Therefore, the EOQ for the firm is 200 kilograms.

To determine the number of times that orders should be placed in a year, you can divide the annual demand for flour by the EOQ:

(10 000 kg) / (200 kg/order) = 50 orders/year

To determine the Reorder Point (ROP) for the firm, you can use the following formula:

ROP = Lead Time Demand + Safety Stock

Where Lead Time Demand is the amount of product needed during the lead time, and Safety Stock is the extra amount of product kept on hand to account for uncertainty in demand and lead time.

In this case, the lead time demand is 50 weeks (since there are 50 working weeks in a year) and the safety stock requirement is 100 kilograms, so the ROP is:

ROP = (50 weeks)(100 kg/week) + 100 kg = 5100 kg

5. To determine the investment in inventory for the firm, you need to calculate the total cost of the inventory. You can do this by multiplying the number of units of inventory by the cost per unit:

(9 000 units - 7000 units) x (15.00/unit) = (2 000 units) x (15.00/unit) = 30 000.00

Therefore, the investment in inventory is P30 000.00.

6. To determine the discounted amount for inventory, you can multiply the investment in inventory by the discount rate:

30 000.00 x (1 - 0.03) = 30 000.00 x 0.97 = 29 100.00

Therefore, the discounted amount for inventory is P29 100.00.

To determine the total discount for one year, you can subtract the discounted amount from the original investment:

30 000.00 - 29 100.00 = 900.00

Therefore, the total discount for one year is P900.00.
