5. Hydrogen gas reacts with oxygen gas to produce water. If a student fills a 250-mL flask with hydrogen at STP, what volume of oxygen is necessary for the reaction?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Step 2: Calculate the volume of oxygen necessary for the reaction.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 liters of H₂ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 liter of O₂.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of} \: \text{O}_2 & = \text{250 mL} \: \text{H}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 L} \: \text{O}_2}{\text{2 L} \: \text{H}_2} \\ & = \boxed{\text{125 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 125 mL of O₂ is necessary for the reaction.

28. Ammonium perchlorate is the solid rocket fuel used by the U.S. Space Shuttle. It reacts with itself to produce nitrogen gas , chlorine gas , oxygen gas , water , and a great deal of energy. What mass of nitrogen gas is produced by the reaction of 27g of ammonium perchlorate?


Answer:

2NH4ClO4----> N2 + Cl2 + 2O2 + 4H2O

From the balanced equation above,

2 moles of NH4ClO4 produces 1mol of N2

0.800mol will produce 1/2 x 0.800 = 0.400mol of N2

SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

2NH₄ClO₄ → N₂ + Cl₂ + 2O₂ + 4H₂O

Step 2: Calculate the mass of N₂ produced by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of NH₄ClO₄ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of N₂.

The molar masses of NH₄ClO₄ and N₂ are 117.49 g and 28.013 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{N}_2 & = \text{27 g} \: \text{NH}_4\text{ClO}_4 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{NH}_4\text{ClO}_4}{\text{117.49 g} \: \text{NH}_4\text{ClO}_4} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{N}_2}{\text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_4\text{ClO}_4} \times \frac{\text{28.013 g} \: \text{N}_2}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{N}_2} \\ & = \boxed{\text{3.2 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 3.2 g of N₂ is produced by the reaction of 27 g of NH₄ClO₄.
