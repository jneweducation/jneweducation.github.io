28. A van of mass 2000 kg is travelling at 10 m/s.(A) Calculate its kinetic energy.(B) if its speed increases to 20 m/s,by how much does its kinetic energy increase?​


A.

Given: m = 2000kg; v = 10m/s

Find: KE = ?

Formula: KE = 1/2mv^2

Solution:

KE = 1/2b(2000kg) (10m/s)^2

KE = 1000kg (100m^2/s^2)

KE = 100,000kgm^2/s^2

Thus, the KE of the van is 100,000J

B.

Given: m = 2000kg; v = 20m/s

Find: KE = ?

Formula: KE = 1/2mv^2

Solution:

KE = 1/2 (2000kg) (20m/s)^2

KE = 1000kg (400m^2/s^2)

KE = 400,000kgm^2/s^2

Thus, the KE of the van with speed of 20m/s is 400,000J

Given: KE1 = 100,000J; KE2 = 400,000J

Find: ∆KE = ?

Formula: ∆KE = KE2 - KE1

Solution:

∆KE = 400,000J - 100,000J

∆KE = 300,000J

Thus, the van's kinetic energy would increase by 300,000J if the speed increased from 10m/s to 20m/s
