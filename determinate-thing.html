25. 2. The primary purpose of conducting survey is toa. find cause and effect relationshipsb. compare groups to determine differencesc. determine and describe the way things ared. predict the way things will be based on the way things are​


The primary purpose of conducting survey is to find cause and effect relationships

Survey research is quantitative research

In survey research, the researcher asks several people (respondents) about past or present beliefs, opinions, characteristics of an object and behavior. Survey research methods are concerned with questions about one's own beliefs and behavior.

All sample members or respondents in survey research answer the same questions. Survey research measures the value of several variables, testing several hypotheses about the behavior, experiences and characteristics of an object. Survey research in general is correlation research.

Quantitative survey research departs from the problem, which consists of problem background, problem identification, and problem formulation. These problems are then explained and answered with theory. The answer to the new problem formulation using theory is called a hypothesis. The hypothesis will be proven true in the field. Therefore, researchers determine a particular population. If the population is large, the researcher uses the sample as a source of research data. If the researcher intends to make generalizations about the results of the sample research, then the samples are taken randomly.

Researchers in collecting data need to develop instruments. So that the data obtained is valid and reliable, the research instrument before being used for data collection is tested for validity and reliability first. After the sample is determined and the instrument has been tested for validity and reliability, the next step is to collect data on the sample that has been determined. After the data is collected then the data is analyzed. Analysis is directed to answer the formulation of the problem and hypotheses that have been formulated. The last research activity is to make research reports which are manifested in the form of theses, theses, and dissertations.
