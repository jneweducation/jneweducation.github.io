2. A 6 kg bowling ball collides with a 1.6 kg bowling pin at rest. The pin flies forward witha speed of 4m/s. If the balls continues to move forward with a speed of 1.8m/s , what isthe speed of the ball before collision?​


Answer:

2.87 m/s

Explanation:

m1 = 6 kg

v1i = ?

v1f = 1.8 m/s

m2= 1.6 kg

v2i = 0 m/s

v2f = 4 m/s  

Use formula for elastic collision:

m1v1i + m2v2i = m1v1f + m2v2f

m1v1i = m1v1f + m2v2f - m2v2i

m1v1i/ m1 = (m1v1f + m2v2f - m2v2i) / m1

v1i = (m1v1f + m2v2f - m2v2i) / m1

Plugging in the values, we get: v1i = 2.87 m/s

4. A constant force of 20N applied to an object causes it to move at a constant speed of 4m/s. How much work is done on the object? If this work is being applied for 6 seconds,how much power is expended?​


WORK & POWERMECHANICS

GIVEN:

F = 20 N

v = 4 m/s

t = 6 s

θ = 0°

FORMULA:

For the Work

Work = Force × displacement × cos θ

For the Power

Power = Work ÷ time

EQUATION:

For the Work

Work = Force × Velocity × time     (1)

For the Power

Power = Work ÷ time     (2)

SOLUTION:

For the Work

Work = Force × Velocity × time

W = (20 N)(4 m/s)(6 s)

W = 480 Nm

W = 480 J

For the Power

Power = Work ÷ time

P = 480 J ÷ 6s

P = 80 watts

ANSWER:

W = 480 J

P = 80 watts

Problem:

A car start and accelerate at a constant 4m/s in second.Determine speed and distance after 10 second?

Given Data:

Vi (initial velocity) = 0 m/s

Vf (final velocity) = ? unknown

a (acceleration)  =  4 m/s²

t (time)  =  10 seconds

Solution:

We can solve this problem using the formula a = Vf-Vi/t and to vf, we can use the formula Vf = at since Vi = 0 m/s.

Vf = at

   = 4 m/s² × 10 s

   = 40 m/s

Solving for distance:

d = 1/2 at²

   = 1/2 (4 m/s²)(10 s)²

   = 2 m/s² (100 s²)

   =  200 m

The speed is 40 m/s and the distance is 200 m

time = 200 seconds
