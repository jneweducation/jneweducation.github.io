Electric field and magnetic field patterns

Magnetic fields and  electric fields: Similarities:  

Two forms of charge,  positive and negative, create electrical fields.  Magnetic fields, while generated by charges (but moving charges),  are associated with two magnet poles north and south.  Like repeling  sticks, unlike attracting pin.  The electrical field points towards  the positive charge of the body.  The magnetic field points towards  the power of the north pole.

Magnetic and  electrical field differences:  

There could be  different positive and negative charges.  The poles north and  south come together also.  Theoretically, individual magnetic poles known as magnetic monopolies  were suggested, but no magnetic monopoly was observed.   There are distinct beginning and  stopping points on electric field lines.  Continuous loops are  magnetic field lines.  The field from the north pole to the  south pole is directed from outside a magnet.  The current flows from South  to North through a magnet.
