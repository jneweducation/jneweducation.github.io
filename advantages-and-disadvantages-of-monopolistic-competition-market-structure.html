9. List down some advantages and disadvantages of market competition​


Explanation:

ADVANTAGE:The advantage of having market competition is that companies are always adding value to their product. They can either increase the quality of the product, or they can decrease the prices. 10. B. Define the following terms in your own words, 1. Homogenous 2. Pure Competition3. Monopoly4. Monopolistic5. Perfect Competition6. Pure Competition 7. Imperfect competition 8. Oligopoly 9. Market 10. Market Structure​


1. Homogeneous: of the same or a similar kind or nature

2. Pure competition: a perfect market, also known as an atomistic market, is defined by several idealizing conditions, collectively called perfect competition, or atomistic competition.

3. Monopoly: the exclusive possession or control of the supply of or trade in a commodity or service.

4. Monopolistic: relating to a person or business that has exclusive possession or control of the supply of or trade in a commodity or service.

5. Perfect competition: the situation prevailing in a market in which buyers and sellers are so numerous and well informed that all elements of monopoly are absent and the market price of a commodity is beyond the control of individual buyers and sellers.

6. ITS SAME AS NUMBER 2.

7. Imperfect competition: the situation prevailing in a market in which elements of monopoly allow individual producers or consumers to exercise some control over market prices.

8. Oligopoly: a state of limited competition, in which a market is shared by a small number of producers or sellers.

9. Market: advertise or promote (something).

10. Market structure: Market structure, in economics, depicts how firms are differentiated and categorised based on the types of goods they sell (homogeneous/heterogeneous) and how their operations are affected by external factors and elements.

24. Why do we consider monopolistic market a competitive market​


Answer:

In monopolistic competition there are no barriers to entry. Therefore in long run, the market will be competitive, with firms making normal profit. In Monopolistic competition, firms do produce differentiated products, therefore, they are not price takers (perfectly elastic demand).

Essentially a monopolistic competitive market is one with freedom of entry and exit, but firms can differentiate their products. Therefore, they have an inelastic demand curve and so they can set prices. ... Firms make normal profits in the long run but could make supernormal profits in the short term

What is the role of an Organizational structure into a business..?

Organizational structure refers to the framework that defines how tasks are divided, how responsibilities are assigned, and how communication flows within a company or organization. The role of an organizational structure in a business is to provide a clear and defined hierarchy of authority, roles, and responsibilities, and to establish a formal system of communication and decision-making.

Here are some specific ways that an organizational structure can impact a business:

Clarify roles and responsibilities: An organizational structure helps to clearly define the roles and responsibilities of different employees and teams within the company. This can help to minimize confusion, improve efficiency, and ensure that everyone is working towards the same goals.

Increase efficiency: By defining a clear chain of command and decision-making process, an organizational structure can help to minimize delays and ensure that tasks are completed in a timely and efficient manner.

Improve communication: A formal organizational structure can help to establish clear lines of communication between different departments and employees, which can improve collaboration and decision-making.

Facilitate growth: As a business grows, a formal organizational structure can help to ensure that the company is able to scale effectively by providing a framework for hiring new employees and delegating responsibilities.

Enhance accountability: By clearly defining roles and responsibilities, an organizational structure can help to enhance accountability and ensure that employees are held responsible for their actions and decisions.

Overall, the role of an organizational structure is to provide a clear and defined framework for how a business operates, with the ultimate goal of improving efficiency, communication, and overall performance.
