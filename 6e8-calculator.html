17. calculate the velocity of a train heading 1857km 68°SW of bicol in 2hrs.​


Answer:

velocity_SW = (1857 km / 2 hours) x cos(112°) = - 926.2 km/h

Explanation:

To calculate the velocity of the train, we need to use the formula:

velocity = distance / time

where distance is the distance traveled by the train and time is the time taken to travel that distance.

In this case, the distance traveled by the train is 1857 km and the time taken is 2 hours.

To find the component of the velocity in the south-west direction, we need to multiply the velocity by the cosine of the angle between the velocity vector and the south-west direction. The angle between the velocity vector and the south-west direction is 180° - 68° = 112°.

So, the velocity of the train in the south-west direction is:

velocity_SW = (1857 km / 2 hours) x cos(112°) = - 926.2 km/h

Note that the negative sign indicates that the velocity is in the opposite direction to the south-west direction
