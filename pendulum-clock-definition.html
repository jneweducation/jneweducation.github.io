7. at what location along the path of the pendulum is the pendulum's kinetic energy the greatest?​


Answer:

Potential energy is converted to kinetic energy, which is the energy exerted by a moving object. An active pendulum has the most kinetic energy at the lowest point of its swing when the weight is moving fastest.

18. how is the upside down pendulum of a metronome different from a conventional pendulum?​


Answer:

A mechanical metronome uses a movable weight on the end of an inverted pendulum rod to alter the tempo. A second, fixed weight is on the base of the pendulum pivot, hidden within the metronome case. That is why a mechanical metronome is also known as a double-weighted pendulum.
