16. when you toss a ball into the air expecting to hit the ground later,when will the ball achieve it's highest velocity? highest acceleration​

When the ball is at its highest point, its speed is zero. Thus, no air resistance is working on it. What forces are working on it? The force of gravity, and that one alone. How much is the acceleration that the force of gravity creates?

Some other answerers have said the acceleration would be zero. This is incorrect. The velocity is zero. The juggler catches the ball when it falls back to a height of 3 feet. How long is ball in air? 2. If a ball is rolled off a cliff that is 50 meters high. If the ball's initial velocity is 3 m/s, determine the following; • how long before it hits the ground • how far from the base of the cliff will it hit the ground and • its velocity just before it hits the ground.


Answer:
1.The ball was in the air for 4 seconds.

2.

- The ball will hit the ground after 2.05 seconds.

- The ball will hit the ground 30.75 meters from the base of the cliff.

- The ball's velocity just before hitting the ground will be 20.1 m/s.

Explanation:

To solve for the time the ball was in the air, we can use the equation:

h = vit + 1/2a*t^2

where h is the change in height (3 ft - 4 ft = -1 ft), vi is the initial vertical velocity (40 ft/s), a is the acceleration due to gravity (-32 ft/s^2), and t is the time the ball was in the air.

Substituting the values, we get:

-1 = 40t + 1/2*(-32)t^2

Simplifying, we get:

16t^2 - 40t - 1 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, we get:

t = [40 ± sqrt(40^2 - 416(-1))] / (2*16)

t ≈ 2.51 seconds

Therefore, the ball was in the air for approximately 2.51 seconds.

To solve for the time it takes for the ball to hit the ground, we can use the equation:

h = vit + 1/2a*t^2

where h is the height of the cliff (50 m), vi is the initial velocity (3 m/s), a is the acceleration due to gravity (-9.81 m/s^2), and t is the time it takes for the ball to hit the ground.

Substituting the values, we get:

50 = 3t + 1/2*(-9.81)t^2

Simplifying, we get:

4.905t^2 + 3t - 50 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, we get:

t = [-3 ± sqrt(3^2 - 44.905(-50))] / (2*4.905)

t ≈ 3.26 seconds (using the positive root)

Therefore, it will take approximately 3.26 seconds for the ball to hit the ground.

To solve for the horizontal distance the ball will travel, we can use the equation:

d = vi*t

where d is the horizontal distance, vi is the initial velocity (3 m/s), and t is the time it takes for the ball to hit the ground (3.26 seconds).

Substituting the values, we get:

d = 3*3.26

d ≈ 9.78 meters

Therefore, the ball will hit the ground approximately 9.78 meters from the base of the cliff.

To solve for the velocity of the ball just before it hits the ground, we can use the equation:

vf^2 = vi^2 + 2ah

where vf is the final velocity, vi is the initial velocity (3 m/s), a is the acceleration due to gravity (-9.81 m/s^2), and h is the height of the cliff (50 m).

Substituting the values, we get:

vf^2 = 3^2 + 2*(-9.81)*50

vf ≈ -31.32 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the ball just before it hits the ground is approximately 31.32 m/s downward.
