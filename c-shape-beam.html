21. A 54 kg girl is walking up a 22 kg uniform beam. The coefficient of friction is 0.2 at all surfaces. A) What is the reaction at A? B) What is the reaction at B? C) Determine the distance "x" up the beam can the girl walk, before the beam starts to slip.


Answer:

A) The reaction at A is equal to the weight of the beam, which is 22 kg.

B) The reaction at B is equal to the weight of the girl, which is 54 kg.

C) To determine the distance "x" up the beam the girl can walk before the beam starts to slip, we can use the equation for static friction:

μ * F_N = F_f

Where μ is the coefficient of friction, F_N is the normal force, and F_f is the force of friction.

The normal force is equal to the weight of the girl, which is 54 kg.

The force of friction is equal to the weight of the beam, which is 22 kg.

Substituting these values into the equation, we get:

0.2 * 54 kg = 22 kg

Solving for x, we get:

x = 110 kg

This means the girl can walk a distance of 110 kg up the beam before it starts to slip.
