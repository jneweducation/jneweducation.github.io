1. what is the difference between a storm surge and a tsunami​


Answer:

A tsunami is caused by an undersea earthquake or tremor. A storm surge is caused by the combined action of wind and low barometric pressure (often but not necessarily at high tide). Storm surge is the forcing of water from a large ocean or body of water toward the shore when the wind blows for hundred of miles

2. What is diffirentiate between tsunami and storm surge?​


Answer:

a tsunami is caused by oceanic earthquake and storm surge is caused by typhoon/hurricane

Answer:

A storm surge is a water pushed inland by very strong winds usually during the event of very strong typhoons. Meanwhile, a tsunami is water displaced by an earthquake.

Explanation:

Storm surge and tsunami are caused by different events but both can result in massive destruction to life and property. Dr. Mahar Lagmay, a director of the National Operational Assessment of Hazards (Project NOAH) said, "Equating storm surge to a tsunami, or saying it is tsunami-like is inaccurate, dangerous and responsible."  Their unique description can't be disregarded for the sake of accurate visualization, said Lagmay, and could be fatal once misunderstood and conveyed erroneously.

A tsunami is a large ocean wave caused by the sudden movement of the ocean floor. This sudden motion could be an earthquake, a powerful volcanic eruption, or an underwater landslide.  These deadly waves travel across the open ocean at great speed. An evacuation alert after such an event will approximately give 10 to 15 minutes allowance for the people to save their lives by moving onto higher grounds. The people's reaction to such news is portrayed perfectly by every disaster movie we've seen. The community is big. People will surely be in the midst of panic and disorientation which can cause a stampede.

On the other hand, storm surge is the abnormal rise in seawater level during a storm, measured as the height of the water above the normal predicted astronomical tide. The surge is caused primarily by the storm's winds pushing water onshore. But unlike a tsunami, the storm surge provides an ample amount of time to prepare an evacuation plan essential for survival. People in authority can utilize the nowadays advanced technology because storm surge predictions can be made as early as two days before typhoon landfall on a certain locality.

8. How are storm surge distinguished from tsunamis?


Tsunamis and storm surges are caused by different events but both result in flooding and damage to coastal areas. tsunamis are immense sea waves (10 metres or more) which are produced by underwater events such as earthquakes,mudslides and volcanic eruption. while, storm surges are elevated sea levels produced by intense marine low pressure systems.
