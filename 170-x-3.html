[tex] \qquad \Large \: \rm 2) \; x = 85\degree \:, \: y = 190\degree [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \: \rm 3) \; x = 130\degree \:, \: y = 230\degree [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \: \rm 4) \; x = 13 [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \: \rm 5) \; m = 13 [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Number 1:A radius that is drawn to the point of tangency is perpendicular to the tangent line. Therefore, x = 90°

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

Number 2:The measure of an angle created on the point of tangency is half the measure of its intercepted arc. Thus, x is half of 170° which is 85°A full circle measures 360°, which is the sum of 170° and y. If we subtract 170° from 360°, we will get the measure of y as 190°

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

Number 3:Conversely, the measure of an intercepted arc is twice the measure of an angle created on the point of tangency. Thus, y is twice of 115° which is 230°A full circle measures 360°, which is the sum of x and y. If we subtract y which is 230° from 360°, we will get the measure of x as 130°

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

Number 4:

Two external tangent segments that intersects the same circle are equal in measure we can say that.

[tex] x - 2 = 11 [/tex]

[tex] x = 11 + 2 [/tex]

[tex] x = 13[/tex]

Therefore, the value of x is 13

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

Number 5:

Two external tangent segments that intersects the same circle are equal in measure we can say that.

[tex] 3m - 2 = m + 24 [/tex]

[tex] 3m - m = 24 + 2 [/tex]

[tex] 2m = 26 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{2m}2 = \frac{26}2 \\ [/tex]

[tex] m = 13 [/tex]

Therefore, the value of m is also 13
