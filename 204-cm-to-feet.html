204 = 2(2*w + 6) + 2*w

204 = 4*w + 12 + 2*w

204 = 6*w + 12

204 - 12 = 6*w

192 = 6*w

32 = w

29. The length of a rectangle is 9 cm less than twice its width and the perimeter of the rectangle is 204 cm. Let w be the width of a rectangle. 14. Represent the length of the rectangle in terms of w. 15. Represent the perimeter of the rectangle in terms of w. ​


Answer:

14. length of the rectangle is 65 cm.

15. the perimeter of the rectangle in terms of its width w is given by the expression 6w - 18.

Step-by-step explanation:

14. Let's start by using algebra to represent the given information:

Let w be the width of the rectangle.

The length of the rectangle is 9 cm less than twice its width: L = 2w - 9.

The perimeter of the rectangle is 204 cm: P = 2L + 2w = 204.

Now we can use the second equation to solve for L in terms of w:

2L + 2w = 204

2(2w - 9) + 2w = 204 (substitute L = 2w - 9)

4w - 18 + 2w = 204

6w - 18 = 204

6w = 222

w = 37

So the width of the rectangle is 37 cm. Now we can use the expression for L in terms of w to find the length:

L = 2w - 9

L = 2(37) - 9

L = 65

So the length of the rectangle is 65 cm.

15. Let's start by using the given information to write an equation for the length of the rectangle in terms of its width w.

We are told that the length is 9 cm less than twice the width, so we can write:

length = 2w - 9

Next, we can use the formula for the perimeter of a rectangle, which is:

perimeter = 2(length + width)

Substituting our expression for length into this formula, we get:

perimeter = 2((2w - 9) + w)

Simplifying this expression, we get:

perimeter = 2(3w - 9)

perimeter = 6w - 18

Therefore, the perimeter of the rectangle in terms of its width w is given by the expression 6w - 18.
