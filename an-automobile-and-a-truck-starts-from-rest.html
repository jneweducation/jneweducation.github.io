v² = u² + 2as

Given that the truck accelerates to a velocity of 25 m/s in 12 seconds, we can calculate the acceleration as follows:

v = 25 m/su = 0 m/st = 12 ss = 125 m

Substituting these values into the kinematic equation, we get:

25² = 0² + 2a(125)

a = 625/250

a = 2.5 m/s²

Therefore, the acceleration of the truck over 125 meters is 2.5 m/s².

30. 3. A truck starting from rest accelerates uniformly at 2 m/s². a. What is its speed after 20 s? b. How far has it traveled at 15 s? c. After how long will the truck be 500 m from its starting point?Not A multiple choiceremove mali sagot​


Answer:

a. The speed of the truck can be calculated using the formula: v = u + at, where v is the final velocity, u is the initial velocity (which is 0), a is the acceleration (which is 2 m/s^2), and t is the time taken (which is 20 s).

v = 0 + 2 * 20 = 40 m/s

b. The distance traveled by the truck can be calculated using the formula: s = ut + (1/2)at^2, where s is the distance traveled, u is the initial velocity (which is 0), a is the acceleration (which is 2 m/s^2), and t is the time taken (which is 15 s).

s = 0 + (1/2) * 2 * 15^2 = 225 meters

c. The distance traveled by the truck can also be calculated using the formula: s = ut + (1/2)at^2. Rearranging this formula, we get: t = sqrt(2s/a), where s is the distance traveled and a is the acceleration.

So, t = sqrt(2*500/2) = 15.8 seconds (rounded to one decimal place).
