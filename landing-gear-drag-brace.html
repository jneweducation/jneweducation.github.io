1. why do airplanes full up their landing gears after takeoff​


Answer:

Landing gears

Landing gearsIf you don't know why, apart from being a matter of aerodynamics of course, by retracting the undercarriage, the aircraft reduces its drag, consumes less fuel and can even fly faster. In addition, quickly retracting the aircraft landing gear is also for safety reasons.


2. why do airplanes pull up their landing gears the after takeoff​


Answer:

When you retract it the speed will increase at a larger rate. This means that the landing gear creates a drag which reduces the aircraft speed. If the drag increases then the fuel consumption will increase. Moreover the gear might get damaged at high speed.

Why do pilots tap brakes after takeoff?

It's strictly done to stop the wheels from vibrating as they wind down, which can scare the pax and sometimes the pilot at first.

Explanation:

When would you retract the landing gear after takeoff?

Normally, the landing gear is retracted after lift-off when the airplane has reached an altitude where, in the event of an engine failure or other emergency requiring an aborted takeoff, the airplane could no longer be landed on the runway.

15. discuss and differentiate passive gears and active gears​


Answer:

With passive gears, the capture of fish is generally based on movement of the target species towards the gear (e.g. traps), while with active gears capture is generally based on an aimed chase of the target species (e.g. trawls, dredges).
