Answer:

1. Latitude is the distance of a location on Earth from the equator and is measured in degrees using lines that run east to west around the globe.

2. The greatest possible latitude is 90 degrees and it is located at the North and South Poles.

3. Latitudes are identified on maps or globes by using lines called parallels, which are numbered in degrees.

4. The equator is the line of latitude that circles the Earth's midsection and is located at 0 degrees latitude. It serves as a reference point for measuring latitude.

5. The special names of the five lines of latitude are the equator, Tropic of Cancer, Tropic of Capricorn, Arctic Circle, and Antarctic Circle.

6. The temperature range in the tropics is usually hot and includes countries such as Mexico, Brazil, India, and Indonesia.

7. The Northern and Southern Temperate Zones are located between the Tropic of Cancer and the Arctic Circle in the north and between the Tropic of Capricorn and the Antarctic Circle in the south.

8. The Polar regions, also known as frigid zones, are located around the North and South Poles and have very cold temperatures, often below freezing.

9. The Earth's grid system helps us navigate and understand the planet by providing a standardized way of measuring location and using coordinates to locate specific points on the globe.

10. Other geographical features or phenomena influenced by latitude include climate, vegetation, and animal species distribution.
