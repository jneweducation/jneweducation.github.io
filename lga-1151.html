22. Philipine Environmental Policy PD NO.1151 section 1-4​


PD NO. 1151 is the Philippine Environmental Policy, which was enacted in 1977 to provide the legal framework for the protection and conservation of the country's natural resources and the promotion of sustainable development. The policy covers a wide range of environmental issues, including air, water, land, and biodiversity conservation.

Here are sections 1-4 of PD NO. 1151:

Section 1: Declaration of Policy

The State recognizes that the rational and sustainable use, development, and conservation of the country's natural resources are vital to the nation's economic and social development. It is the policy of the State to ensure the protection and improvement of the quality of the environment and the natural resources, and to promote the health and welfare of the people.

Section 2: Definition of Terms

This section defines various terms used in the policy, such as "environment," "natural resources," "pollution," "conservation," and "sustainable development."

Section 3: Environmental Management

This section mandates the government to adopt a comprehensive and integrated approach to environmental management, which includes the establishment of a national environmental policy, the creation of an environmental impact assessment system, the implementation of pollution control measures, and the promotion of environmental education and research.

Section 4: Environmental Impact Assessment System

This section requires all public and private projects, programs, and activities that are likely to have significant environmental impacts to undergo an environmental impact assessment (EIA). The EIA process involves the identification, prediction, and evaluation of potential environmental impacts of the proposed project or activity, as well as the development and implementation of measures to mitigate or prevent those impacts. The EIA is a critical tool in ensuring that development activities are environmentally sustainable and socially responsible.

24. P.D No. 1151 section 1,2,3,4 summarize​


Presidential Decree No. 1151 is a law in the Philippines that declares the policy of the state to promote and develop tourism. The following is a summary of its key sections:

Section 1: The policy of the state is to promote and develop tourism while preserving and enhancing the country's natural and cultural resources.

Section 2: The government shall encourage the establishment and operation of tourism enterprises that are economically viable and socially acceptable, and that preserve the environment and promote Filipino culture.

Section 3: The Department of Tourism shall be the primary government agency responsible for the implementation of the policy and the coordination of all activities related to tourism development.

Section 4: The government shall prioritize the development of tourism in areas that have the potential for growth, including but not limited to, areas with natural attractions, historic landmarks, cultural and artistic treasures, and areas with potential for sports and adventure activities. The government shall also provide incentives for the development of tourism in these areas.

26. philippine environmental policy PD NO.1151 Section 1 to 4​


Answer:

Section 1 states that it is the policy of the State to "protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature". Section 2 declares that it is also the policy of the State to "attain and maintain a rational and orderly balance between socio-economic growth and environmental protection". Section 3 provides for the creation of an Environmental Impact Statement System which requires all agencies or instrumentalities of the government or any private entity intending to undertake projects or activities which may significantly affect the quality of the environment to file an environmental impact statement. Section 4 provides for penalties for violations of any provisions of this decree.

29. 1. isa isahin ang mga layunin ng Espanya sa Pilipinas? 2. Paano isinasagawa ang lga layuning ito? ​


Answer:

Ang mga layunin ng Espanya sa Pilipinas ay upang ipamahagi ang kanilang kultura, relihiyon, at ideolohiya. Upang maisakatuparan ang mga layunin na ito, ang Espanya ay nagdagdag ng mga institusyon tulad ng Simbahan, mga eskwelahan, at ang mga kolonyal na pamahalaan, na nagbibigay sa kanila ng kontrol sa mga Pilipino. Ang mga Espanyol din ay nagpatupad ng mga batas upang mabigyan ng kontrol ang kanilang pananakop sa Pilipinas.
