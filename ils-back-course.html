20. 43. Given: HP> PI, HL bisects ZH, and IL bisects ZI. Prove: HL > IL H​


Step-by-step explanation:

To prove: HL > IL

Proof:

Given HP > PI, we can say that triangle HPI is not an isosceles triangle.

Since HL bisects angle ZH, we can say that angles HLZ and HLI are equal.

Similarly, since IL bisects angle ZI, we can say that angles ILZ and IHL are equal.

Now, consider triangle HLI and triangle HLZ.

Both these triangles have the same hypotenuse HL and share an angle (angle HLZ = angle HLI).

Therefore, by the hypotenuse-leg theorem, triangle HLI is congruent to triangle HLZ.

Thus, the remaining sides of these two triangles must be equal. That is, IL = LZ and HL = ZI.

Since HP > PI, we can say that ZI > LZ.

Therefore, we can conclude that HL > IL.

Hence, proved.
