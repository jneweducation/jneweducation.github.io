1. advantage of coil spring​


1. They are durable and reliable. Coil springs are robust and reliable, able to withstand sustaining shock loads and providing long-lasting performance. Because of the strong construction and durable material, a coil spring can often outlast most other suspension components.

2. They are cost effective. Coil springs are relatively inexpensive when compared to other suspension options. Additionally, the cost of the initial replacement can be offset by their durability and long lifespan.

3. They are adjustable. Coil springs can be easily altered to provide different levels of support for different applications. This can include different wheel rates through progressive windings and adjustable height requirements. This can help customize ride characteristics and preserve handling performance.

4. They allow for articulation. Coil springs provide more wheel movement and flexibility than leaf springs and other suspension components. This is important for optimizing wheel articulation off-road and increasing traction in difficult conditions.

6. if the amplitude of longitudinal waves traveling along the spring increases, how does the spacing between coils of a spring change?


Answer:

The spacing between the coils of a spring is directly related to the amplitude of the longitudinal waves traveling along the spring.

Explanation:

When the amplitude of longitudinal waves traveling along the spring increases, the spacing between the coils of the spring also increases. This is because longitudinal waves are compressional waves that cause the particles in the spring to vibrate back and forth in the same direction as the wave is traveling. As the amplitude of the wave increases, the particles in the spring are compressed and expanded to a greater degree, causing them to move over a larger distance.

As the particles move over a larger distance, the spacing between the coils of the spring increases. This is because the coils are made up of particles that are connected to each other, and when the particles move further apart, the coils also move further apart. Conversely, when the amplitude of the wave decreases, the particles move over a smaller distance, causing the spacing between the coils to decrease. Therefore, the spacing between the coils of a spring is directly related to the amplitude of the longitudinal waves traveling along the spring.

19. what are the 2 basic designs of coil spring? cite there differences​


Answer:

A coil spring is a mechanical device which is typically used to store energy and subsequently release it, to absorb shock, or to maintain a force between contacting surfaces. They are made of an elastic material formed into the shape of a helix which returns to its natural length when unloaded.

A tension coil spring

A selection of conical coil springs

Under tension or compression, the material (wire) of a coil spring undergoes torsion. The spring characteristics therefore depend on the shear modulus, not Young's Modulus.

A coil spring may also be used as a torsion spring: in this case the spring as a whole is subjected to torsion about its helical axis. The material of the spring is thereby subjected to a bending moment, either reducing or increasing the helical radius. In this mode, it is the Young's Modulus of the material that determines the spring characteristics.

Metal coil springs are made by winding a wire around a shaped former - a cylinder is used to form cylindrical coil springs.

Illustration of various arc springs and arc spring systems (systems consisting of inner and outer arc springs).

Coil springs for vehicles are typically made of hardened steel. A machine called an auto-coiler takes spring wire that has been heated so it can easily be shaped. It is then fed onto a lathe that has a metal rod with the desired coil spring size. The machine takes the wire and guides it onto the spinning rod as well as pushing it across the rod to form multiple coils

20. A coiled spring in a waist trimming exerciser requires a force of 35 N to compress it by 0.30 m. (a) Find the force constant of the spring. (b) How much force is needed to compress the same spring by 0.70 m? (c) What is the PEe when compressed by 0.5 m?


Answer:

(a)

F = kx

where F = force applied on a spring, k = spring constant, x = extension or compression of the spring.

Given F = 35 N, x = 0.30 m

k = F/x = 35 N / 0.30 m = 116.67 N/m

(b)

Since k = 116.67 N/m from our solution and given x = 0.70 m

F = (116.67 N/m)(0.70 m) = 81.67 N

(c)

Since k = 116.67 N/m from our solution and given x = 0.5 m

PE = 1/2 kx² = (1/2)(116.67 N/m)(0.5 m)² = 14.58 J
