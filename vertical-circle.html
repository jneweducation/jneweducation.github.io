20. Vertical angles always intersect at the center of the circle.* True False ​


Answer:

False.

Step-by-step explanation:

Vertical angles are formed by intersecting lines or rays, and they are always congruent (equal in measure) to each other.

On the other hand, a circle is a round shape with no corners or edges, and it does not have angles. Therefore, vertical angles cannot intersect at the center of a circle.

Answer:

False.

Vertical angles are a pair of non-adjacent angles formed by intersecting lines. The concept of the center of a circle is not related to vertical angles. Therefore, vertical angles do not always intersect at the center of the circle.

Given that, area of the circle =
[tex]1256 \: cm {}^{2} [/tex]
[tex]\pi \: r {}^{2} = 1256 \\ \: \: \: \: r {}^{2} = \frac{1256}{\pi} = \frac{1256}{3.14} = 400 \\ \: \: \: \: r {}^{2} = (20) {}^{2} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: r = 20 \: cm[/tex]
So, the radius of circle is 20 cm.
=> Diameter of circle = 2 × Radius
= 2 × 20 = 40 cm

Since, all the vertices of a rhombus lie on a circle that means each diagonal of a rhombus must pass through the centre of a circle that is why both diagonals are equal and same as the diagonals of the rhombus.

Let d1 and d2 be the diagonals of the rhombus.
Therefore, d1 = d2 = Diameter of circle = 40 cm
So, area of rhombus
[tex] = \frac{1}{2} \times d1 \times d2 \\ = \frac{1}{2} \times 40 \times 40 = 20 \times 40 = 800 \: cm {}^{2} [/tex]
