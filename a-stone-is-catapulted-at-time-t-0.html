2. In a historical movie, you observed a catapult being used by an army. It hurled a stone with a velocity of 55 m/s at an angle of 32° from the horizontal. How far did the stone travel horizontally? ​


Answer:

To find the horizontal distance traveled by the stone, we can use the horizontal component of the velocity and the time of flight of the projectile.

Given:

Initial velocity (v) = 55 m/s

Launch angle (θ) = 32°

First, we need to find the time of flight of the projectile. We can use the following equation for the vertical motion:

Vertical displacement (Δy) = v*sin(θ)*t - (1/2)*g*t^2

At the highest point of the trajectory, the vertical displacement is zero, so we have:

0 = v*sin(θ)*t - (1/2)*g*t^2

Simplifying the equation, we get:

(1/2)*g*t^2 = v*sin(θ)*t

t = 2*v*sin(θ)/g

Substituting the given values, we have:

t = 2*(55 m/s)*sin(32°)/(9.8 m/s^2)

t ≈ 5.93 s

Now that we have the time of flight, we can find the horizontal distance using the equation:

Horizontal distance (D) = v*cos(θ)*t

D = (55 m/s)*cos(32°)*(5.93 s)

D ≈ 255.78 m

Therefore, the stone traveled approximately 255.78 meters horizontally.

The number of rubber bands is directly correlated to the distance the ping pong balls would travel.

Explanation:

When several rubber bands are bunched together, it essentially gets stronger when we look at it as if the bunched up rubber bands create one thick rubber band. One rubber band is easier to snap than fifteen put together. This essentially shows that with the number of rubber bands increasing, its tensile strength increases as well; with the tensile strength increasing, its capacity for potential energy increases also. The number of rubber bands clustered together within a catapult's launching system is directly correlated with its amount of potential energy. With its potential energy increased, the force that will be exerted by the catapult will be increased as well. The force of the catapult will be exerted on its load, in this case, it's ping pong balls, and now the ball has kinetic energy. If we compare the distance traveled by ping pong balls when it's blown on to thrown ones, the thrown ping pong balls would've traveled farther than the ones blown, therefore force must be directly correlated with the distance traveled by a mass.

.

X=S(Tensile Strength)=PE(Potential Energy)= KE(Kinetic Energy/Force)= D(Distance Travelled)

X=D

The number of rubber bands is directly related to the distance traveled by the ping pong balls.

14. A cannonball is catapulted toward a castle. The cannon ball velocity when it leaves a catapult is 40 m/s at an angle of 37° with respect to the horizontal and the cannonball is 7.0 m from the ground at this time. (a) What is the maximum height above the ground reach by the cannon ball? (b) What horizontal Distance from the release point will it land?


Answer:

a. The maximum height is 36.57m

b. The horizontal distance from the release point is 156.94m

Explanation:

In this problem, we will apply the concept of projectile motion to solve the following requirements.

Formula to use

a. h(max) = V₀²sin²θ / 2g

b. R = V₀²sin(2θ) /g

where

h(max)     is the maximum height the ball can reach, the unit is in meter

V₀            is the initial velocity, the unit is in m/s

θ              is the angle

g              is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/s²

R              is the range or maximum horizontal distance the ball can reach,

                the unit is also in meter

Given information

V₀ = 40 m/s

θ = 37°

y₀ = 7 m (initial height of the cannonball)

a. h(tmax) = ? (total maximum height)

b. R = ?

Solving the problem

a. First. let us solve the maximum height the cannonball can reach. Let us use the first given formula.

h(max) = V₀²sin²θ / 2g

Then, substitute the given data

h(max) = (40 m/s)²(sin 37°)² / 2(9.8 m/s²)

h(max) = 29.57m

Now, since we have a given initial height of 7.0m, we will add this to the maximum height, we have:

h(tmax) = 7m + 29.57m

h(tmax) = 36.57m

Therefore, the total maximum height the cannonball can reach is 36.57 meters.

b. Finally, let us solve for the horizontal distance. Let us use the second formula

R = V₀²sin(2θ) /g

Again, let us substitute the given data

R = [(40 m/s)²sin2(37°)] / 9.8 m/s²

R = 156.94m

Therefore, the range or horizontal distance is 156.94 meters.
