29. Advice to study a job, carefully train workers to do that job, and link financial incentives to job performance would most likely come from ______.


Scientific Management

The scientific management theory offers a strategy for increasing productivity within an organization's workforce. This management theory bases its evaluation of work processes on scientific methodology, as the name of the theory suggests.

Taylor's Principles of Scientific Management consists of the following chapters:

- Science, rather than relying on the Rule of Thumb.

- The Mental Revolution Towards Harmony, Not Discord.

- Collaboration, rather than a focus on the individual.

- The Growth of Each Individual to Their Fullest Potential in Every Way

The importance of scientific management lies in that it assists businesses in effectively allocating their resources, enabling these businesses to optimize their earnings. It enables quality management, which in turn assists businesses in further helping them develop their relationship with their staff.

Productivity enhancement is one of the modern organizations' most significant benefits from adopting scientific management practices. A scientific approach to management emphasizes the tasks carried out by employees inside a company. The significance of this was the fact that scientific management increases the productivity of the workers or employees.
