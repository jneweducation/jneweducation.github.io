21. 5. Let M, A, T, and H be the events of a sample space. M = {f. j. 1, 2, a, 9, 5, h) A = (3, 6, 9, a, b, d) T = {o, a, k, h, 0, 9) H = (a, g, 9, 1, f, j, 5, h} What is the intersection of these events?​


PROBLEM:

Let M, A, T, and H be the events of a sample space. What is the intersection of these events?

M = { f, j, 1, 2, a, 9, 5, h }A = { 3, 6, 9, a, b, d }T = { o, a, k, h, 0, 9 }H = { a, g, 9, 1, f, j, 5, h }ANSWER:

The intersection of two events which is denoted as A ∩ B, is the event that contains exactly the outcomes that are in both A and B.

In the given problem, we have M, A, T and H as the events. The intersection of the given events are the elements (outcomes) that are visible and used in every events just like a and 9.

[tex]\huge\boxed{\large\rm M ∩ A ∩ T ∩ H = \{ \: a, 9 \: \}}[/tex]
