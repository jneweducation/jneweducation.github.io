2. 8. If I drive from Bunawan to Toril at 40 miles per hour and then from Toril to Bunawan at 60 miles per hour, what is my average speed for the whole journey?​


Answer:

We can start by using the formula:

average speed = total distance ÷ total time

Let's assume the distance from Bunawan to Toril is the same as the distance from Toril to Bunawan (a round trip). Let's also assume the distance is d miles.

For the first leg of the trip, traveling at 40 miles per hour, the time it takes is:

time = distance ÷ speed

time = d ÷ 40

For the second leg of the trip, traveling at 60 miles per hour, the time it takes is:

time = distance ÷ speed

time = d ÷ 60

The total time for the round trip is:

total time = d/40 + d/60

We can simplify this expression by finding the least common multiple of 40 and 60, which is 120.

total time = d/40 + d/60 = (3d + 2d)/120 = 5d/120

Now we can substitute the total distance and total time into the formula for average speed:

average speed = total distance ÷ total time

average speed = 2d ÷ (5d/120) = 48 miles per hour

Therefore, the average speed for the whole journey is 48 miles per hour.
