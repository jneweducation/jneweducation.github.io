Biology: Why is biodiversity so important?

Biodiversity or biological diversity is a term that describes the variety of living beings on earth.

Biodiversity also refers to number of different species living in a particular region. It represents the wealth of biological resources available to us.

Biodiversity is unevenly distributed. It varies globally with in the regions. The various factors that influence biodiversity include- temperature, soils, precipitations, altitude & their relation with other species.

Why is Biodiversity important?

Maintaining balance of ecosystem: recyling & storage of nutrients, combating pollution & stabilizing climate, protecting water resources & protecting soil & maintaining ecobalance.

Provision of biological resources:

Provision of medicines & pharmaceuticals, food for the human population & animals, ornamental plants, wood products, breeding stock & diversity of species, ecosystem & genes.

The organizational structure

Yes. Because Organizational functions provide direction and rules. As well as the division of labor regarding what should and should not be done by members in the organization.

The organizational structure also functions as an explanation of the position and position of each member of the organization. And so that each team can work according to their respective duties and expertise so that organizational goals can be achieved.

Organizing is a set of relationships. Defines vertical and horizontal relationships between people performing organizational tasks. Organizational tasks are divided into several units, personnel in each unit are assigned specific tasks.

The relationship aims to maximize organizational and individual well-being. This structure determines the division of labor and shows how different organizational functions or activities are related.

Basically, the organizational structure is a diagram that shows the chain of command, work relationships, responsibilities, spans of control and organizational leadership functions as a framework and work tasks that are formally divided, grouped and coordinated.

Through the organizational structure, you can see a flow of orders indicating the job positions that must be accounted for by each member of the organization.

By drawing up an organizational structure, you can see clearly how the work is divided and how the different functions and activities can be well coordinated.

You can also find out the specialization of a position, command channels, to submit reports.

Apart from that, you can also see that there is no overlap in positions, authorities, or responsibilities carried out by each member of the organization.
