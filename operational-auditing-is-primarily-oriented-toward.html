An operational audit refers to the process of evaluating the company's operating activities. Operational audits has the objective to focus on effectiveness, productivity, and cost efficiency of the operation of the business. Operational audits main purpose is to make improvements in the upcoming business process in the future, this helps the company to overlook the mistake in the past and become better in the business process. An operational audit can be completed in optimal time when there are no errors in the process, and all resources are used efficiently.

Advantages of the operational audit process, the advantages of an operational audit process are :

Improving departmental effectiveness : During the process of the operational audit, the management team can compare and rank the effectiveness of different departments in the organization. This way they can find the best way to complete the process and avoiding methods that are less effective in the future. The management team see's every team performance and then select the best team that can perform the process more effectively.Discovering opportunities for improvement : Operational audit doesn't not focus on seeing how things are but they focus on how things can be in the future, meaning that in an operational audit the auditors do not only audit the performance of the organization but they look for better ways of accomplishing the same task better in the future.Increasing the efficiency of operational audits : To make the process of the auditing system more efficiency and does not take up too much of the team's time and cost they should always have a good auditing management team and a proper process with no errors in order to achieve efficient results.

The primary responsibility of an internal audit team is to spot issues and get them fixed before they are found during an external audit by a third party company or regulatory body, like the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). An organization's risk management, governance, and internal control mechanisms must all be functioning properly, and internal audit's job is to independently certify this. A company's survival and profitability are directly impacted by the challenges that internal auditors deal with.

Internal auditors often carry out a wide range of duties to accomplish this purpose, including reviewing financial statements, expense reports, inventories, financial data, budgeting, and accounting procedures, as well as developing risk assessments for each department. To weed out potentially harmful errors or falsehoods and discover ways to increase productivity, thorough notes are collected, interviews with employees are done, work schedules are monitored, physical assets are confirmed, and financial accounts are closely examined.
