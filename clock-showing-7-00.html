18. certain loses 15 minutes every hour if the clock is set to the correct time at 9:00 a.m. that what will the correct time be when the show slower clock shows 9:00 a.m. the next dayplss i need answerrrr guys​


Answer:

If a clock loses 15 minutes every hour, it means that for every 60 minutes that pass in real time, the clock only shows 45 minutes have passed. Therefore, in one hour of real time, the clock loses 15 minutes.

If the clock is set to the correct time at 9:00 a.m., and it loses 15 minutes every hour, then at 10:00 a.m. in real time, the clock will only show 9:45 a.m. At 11:00 a.m. in real time, the clock will only show 10:30 a.m., and so on.

Therefore, when the clock shows 9:00 a.m. the next day, it will have lost 15 minutes every hour for 24 hours, which is a total of 360 minutes, or 6 hours. This means that the actual time will be 6 hours later than the time shown on the clock.

So, if the clock shows 9:00 a.m. the next day, the correct time will be 3:00 p.m.
