7. determine the type of chemical bond by solving ∆EN of the compound (H2) H= 2.1 H= 2.1 ∆EN


Answer:

In order to determine the type of chemical bond in a compound using electronegativity difference (∆EN), we can use the following guidelines:

If the ∆EN is less than 0.5, the bond is nonpolar covalent.If the ∆EN is between 0.5 and 1.7, the bond is polar covalent.If the ∆EN is greater than 1.7, the bond is ionic.In the case of (H2), both atoms are hydrogen, which have the same electronegativity value of 2.1. Therefore, the ∆EN is:

∆EN = |2.1 - 2.1| = 0

Since the ∆EN is less than 0.5, the bond in (H2) is nonpolar covalent. This means that the electrons in the bond are shared equally between the two hydrogen atoms, resulting in a molecule with no overall dipole moment.

11. Polarity of molecules and types of intermolecular forces of:1.H² and Br²2.Na²SO⁴ and H²O3.CH³OH and H²O4. Cl–¹ and C⁶H¹⁴​


Answer:

1. H₂ and Br₂:

Both H₂ and Br₂ are nonpolar molecules with covalent bonds between atoms of the same element. The only intermolecular force present is London dispersion force, which is the weakest type of intermolecular force.

2. Na₂SO₄ and H₂O:

Na₂SO₄ is an ionic compound, while H₂O is a polar molecule. The ions of Na₂SO₄ interact with the partial charges of H₂O through ion-dipole interactions, which are stronger than London dispersion forces.

3. CH₃OH and H₂O:

Both CH₃OH and H₂O are polar molecules. They can interact through dipole-dipole interactions, as well as hydrogen bonding due to the presence of hydrogen atoms bonded to electronegative atoms (oxygen in H₂O and oxygen and nitrogen in CH₃OH). Hydrogen bonding is a stronger type of intermolecular force than dipole-dipole interactions.

4. Cl⁻ and C₆H₁₄:

Cl⁻ is an anion with a negative charge, while C₆H₁₄ is a nonpolar molecule. Therefore, the only intermolecular force between them would be ion-induced dipole interactions, which are weaker than dipole-dipole interactions and hydrogen bonding.

14. balance and identify the type of reactionpb +H³(90⁴) --> H² + pb³(PO⁴)​


Answer:

The given chemical equation represents a redox reaction, in which there is an exchange of electrons between the reactants.

Pb (lead) is oxidized from its zero oxidation state to +4 oxidation state, while H3(90⁴) (sulfuric acid) is reduced from +6 oxidation state to +4 oxidation state.

The balanced chemical equation for the reaction is:

Pb + 2H3(90⁴) → H2 + Pb3(PO4)2

In this equation, one atom of Pb reacts with two molecules of H3(90⁴) to form one molecule of H2 gas and one molecule of Pb3(PO4)2, which is a white precipitate.

Therefore, the type of reaction is a redox reaction, specifically an oxidation-reduction reaction.
