5. Skills and Competencies of ABM Students/Graduates​


ABM stands for Accountancy, Business, and Management, a strand in the senior high school curriculum in the Philippines that prepares students for careers in finance, accounting, marketing, and other business-related fields. Here are some of the key skills and competencies that ABM students/graduates should possess:

1. Financial literacy: ABM students should be knowledgeable about basic accounting principles, financial management, budgeting, and financial analysis.

2. Business acumen: ABM students should have a solid understanding of business operations, including marketing, sales, human resources, and operations management.

3. Analytical thinking: ABM students should be able to analyze and interpret data, identify patterns, and make informed decisions based on evidence.

4. Communication skills: ABM students should be able to communicate effectively both orally and in writing, and have the ability to convey complex information to diverse audiences.

5. Leadership skills: ABM students should possess the ability to work collaboratively, manage teams, and lead others effectively.

6. Critical thinking: ABM students should be able to evaluate information, identify problems, and propose effective solutions.

7. Ethical awareness: ABM students should be aware of ethical issues in business and finance, and be able to make ethical decisions in complex situations.

8. Technological proficiency: ABM students should be comfortable using technology and software commonly used in the business world, such as accounting software, data analysis tools, and presentation software.

9. Adaptability: ABM students should possess the ability to adapt to changing circumstances and be willing to learn and apply new skills as needed.

10. Entrepreneurial mindset: ABM students should have an entrepreneurial mindset and possess the creativity and innovation needed to start and run successful businesses.
