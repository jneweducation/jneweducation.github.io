6. example with given variables,sulotion and answer for charles law


Good Day

Charles' Law

states that volume is directly proportional to kelvin temperature of gases if amount of gas and pressure are constant. If temperature decreases, volume decreases.

Sample Problem:

An oxygen gas occupies a volume of 50 L at a temperature of 200 Kelvin. What will be the resulting temperature if temperature is reduced to 1/2.

T₂ (temperature kelvin) = 200 Kelvin reduced 1/2 = 100 Kelvin

Given:

V₁ (initial volume) = 50 L                    V₂  (final volume) = ? unknown

T₁ (initial temperature = 200 Kelvin   T₂  (final temperature) = 100 Kelvin

Solution:

We can solve the problem using the formula V₂  = V₁T₂  / T₁

V₂  = V₁T₂  / T₁

      = 50 L × 100 Kelvin / 200 Kelvin   (cancel unit kelvin)

      = 5000 L / 200

V₂  =  25 L

7. INTRODUCTION TO CHARLES' LAW Charles' Law In 1787, French physicists Jacques Charles, discovered the correlation between Temperature (T) and Volume(V) (assuming Pressure (P) and Amount of Gas(n) remain constant): VxT-V=yT​


Answer:

The three fundamental gas laws discover the relationship of pressure, temperature, volume and amount of gas. Boyle's Law tells us that the volume of gas increases as the pressure decreases. Charles' Law tells us that the volume of gas increases as the temperature increases. And Avogadro's Law tell us that the volume of gas increases as the amount of gas increases. The ideal gas law is the combination of the three simple gas laws.

2. What are the components used is Charles's law?
Charles's law—named for J. -A. -C. Charles (1746–1823)—states that, at constant pressure, the volume V of a gas is directly proportional to its absolute (Kelvin) temperature T, or V/T = k.

and Constants: Mass (m) and pressure (P) of the gas.
