1. What is meaning of acumen​


1acumen

nounacu·men\ə-ˈkyü-mən, ˈa-kyə-mən\

: the ability to think clearly and make good decisions

Full Definition

: keenness and depth of perception, discernment, or discrimination especially in practical matters

Examples

Her political acumen won her the election.

a lack of business acumen

OriginLatin acumin-, acumen, literally, point, from acuere see acute.

First known use: circa 1579

Synonyms: astuteness, caginess (also cageyness), canniness, clear-sightedness, foxiness, hardheadedness, intelligence, keenness, knowingness, sharpness, shrewdness, wit

the meaning of acumen is keen insight or shrewdness :)

9. Section: Editorial Subject: 1. the introduction or the news peg 2 the body 3 the conchlusion 4. Opionion at the editor abonto the cument issues.​


The introduction or news peg of an editorial is the opening paragraph or two that sets the context for the rest of the piece. It typically includes the topic or issue being discussed, and may include any relevant background information or recent developments that help to explain the importance of the issue.

The body of the editorial is the main portion of the piece, where the writer presents their arguments and evidence to support their position on the issue. This section may include research, statistics, examples, and other supporting materials to help make the case for the writer's point of view.

The conclusion of the editorial is the final paragraph or two, where the writer summarizes their main points and restates their position on the issue. The conclusion may also include a call to action, asking readers to take some kind of action in response to the issue discussed in the editorial.

An opinion at the end of an editorial is a statement of the writer's personal view on the issue. This may be a summary of the arguments made in the editorial, or it may be a more personal statement of the writer's beliefs and values. Some editorial writers may also include a disclaimer at the end of their piece, stating that the opinions expressed are their own and do not necessarily reflect the views of the publication or its editorial board.
