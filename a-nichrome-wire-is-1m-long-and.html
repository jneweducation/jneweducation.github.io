R = (ρ * L) / A

Where:

R = Resistance of the wire

L = Length of the wire (in meters)

A = Cross-sectional area of the wire (in square meters)

Where:

ρ2 = Resistivity at the operating temperature (in ohm-meters)

ρ1 = Resistivity at room temperature (in ohm-meters)

α = Temperature coefficient of resistivity (in per degree Celsius)

T2 = Operating temperature (in degrees Celsius)

T1 = Room temperature (in degrees Celsius)

The temperature coefficient of resistivity for nichrome is approximately 0.0004 per degree Celsius.

Using these values, we can calculate the resistivity at the operating temperature:

ρ2 = 1.10 x 10^-6 * (1 + 0.0004 * (300 - 25))

A = π * (d/2)^2

Where:

d = Diameter of the wire (in meters)

The wire resistivity of a 5-meter long nichrome wire used as the heating element in an oven toaster can be calculated using the formula:

ρ = (R x A) / L

where ρ is the resistivity, R is the resistance, A is the cross-sectional area, and L is the length of the wire.

Assuming the wire has a diameter of 1 mm, its cross-sectional area can be calculated as:

A = π x (d/2)^2 = 0.785 mm^2

The resistance can be calculated using the formula:

R = V / I

where V is the voltage and I is the current. Since the current is not given, let's assume it is 10 A (which is a typical current for an oven toaster).

Using these values and the given temperature of 300 degrees Celsius, we can look up the resistivity of nichrome wire at that temperature from a table or use an online calculator. Let's assume the resistivity is 1.11 x 10^-6 ohm-meter.

Substituting these values in the formula for resistivity, we get:

ρ = (R x A) / L = (V/I x A) / L = (270 V / 10 A x 0.785 mm^2) / 5 m = 3.4 x 10^-6 ohm-meter

Therefore, the resistivity of the nichrome wire at an operating temperature of 300 degrees Celsius is approximately 3.4 x 10^-6 ohm-meter.
