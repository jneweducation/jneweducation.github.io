5. the unit weight of soil is 15.10 kN/m3. The moisture content of this soil is 17% when the degree of saturation is 60%. determine the following: a)Void Ratio b)Specific Gravity of Soil c)Saturated Unit Weight


Answer:

1.) 0.59 2.) 2.08 3.) 16.47 kN/m³

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

[tex]\mathsf{\gamma_{moist}=15.10 \ kN/m^3}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\omega = 17\%}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{S=60\%}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Formulas:

[tex]\mathsf{\gamma_{moist}=\dfrac{(G_s+Se)\gamma_w}{1+e}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{Se=G_s\omega}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\gamma_{sat}=\dfrac{(G_s+e)\gamma_w}{1+e}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solution:

1. ) Void ratio, e

[tex]\mathsf{\gamma_{moist}=\dfrac{(G_s+Se)\gamma_w}{1+e}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{15.10=\dfrac{(G_s+0.60e)(9.81)}{1+e}} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 1}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{Se=G_s\omega}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{0.60e=G_s(0.17)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{G_s=\dfrac{60}{17}e} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 2}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute equation 2 in equation 1:

[tex]\mathsf{15.10=\dfrac{\left(\dfrac{60}{17}e+0.60e\right)(9.81)}{1+e}}[/tex]

Solving for e,

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{e=0.59}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

2.) Specific gravity of soil solids, [tex]\mathsf{G_s}[/tex]:

From equation 2:

[tex]\mathsf{G_s=\dfrac{60}{17}e}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{G_s=\dfrac{60}{17}(0.59)}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{G_s=2.08}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

3.) Saturated unit weight, [tex]\mathsf{\gamma_{sat}}[/tex]:

[tex]\mathsf{\gamma_{sat}=\dfrac{(G_s+e)\gamma_w}{1+e}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\gamma_{sat}=\dfrac{(2.08+0.59)(9.81)}{1+0.59}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{\gamma_{sat}=16.47 \ kN/m^3}}[/tex]

29. The surface tensions of mercury and water at 60C are 0. 47 N/m and 0. 0662 N/m, respectively. What capillary-height changes will occur in these two fluids when they are in contact with air in a glass tube of radius 0. 30 mm? Use angle of contact = 130 deg. For mercury, and 0 deg. For water; specific weight of mercury is 132. 3 kN/m3 and 9. 650 kN/m3 for water.


To calculate the capillary-height change in these two fluids, we can use the following equation:

Δh = 2γcosθ / ρgr

Where:

Δh is the capillary-height change

γ is the surface tension of the fluid

θ is the angle of contact between the fluid and the glass

ρ is the density of the fluid

g is the acceleration due to gravity

r is the radius of the glass tube

For mercury, we can plug in the values given in the question to get:

Δh = 2 * 0.47 N/m * cos(130 deg) / (132.3 kN/m3 * 9.81 m/s2 * 0.30 mm)

= 4.88 x 10-6 m

For water, we can plug in the values given in the question to get:

Δh = 2 * 0.0662 N/m * cos(0 deg) / (9.650 kN/m3 * 9.81 m/s2 * 0.30 mm)

= 1.29 x 10-6 m

Therefore, the capillary-height change in mercury will be 4.88 x 10-6 m and the capillary-height change in water will be 1.29 x 10-6 m.
