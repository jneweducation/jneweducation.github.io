18. it was used for calligraphy and for making festive temple decoration umbrellas, fans, and kites​


ANSWER

The Paper of Laos is a natural paper hand made from the inner bark of a Saa tree (a.k.a Mulberry tree). The use of the Mulberry tree for papermaking has been a tradition for around 700years and traces its origin from China.

The Laos paper is strong and sturdy enough to be used for crafting functional items like boxes, lamps, festive temple decorations, umbrellas, fans, kites​, lanterns, and so on. It can be waterproof to make it suitable for weather protection and outdoor usage. The Laos paper is also used for ceremonial and religious purposes.
