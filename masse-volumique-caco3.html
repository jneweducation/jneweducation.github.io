Step 1: Determine the mole ratios needed.

Using CaO

mole ratio = 1 mol CaO : 1 mol CaCO₃

Using CO₂

mole ratio = 1 mol CO₂ : 1 mol CaCO₃

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of CaCO₃ produced by each reactant.

Using CaO

[tex]\text{moles of CaCO₃ = 15.27 g CaO} × \frac{\text{1 mol CaO}}{\text{56.08 g CaO}} × \frac{\text{1 mol CaCO₃}}{\text{1 mol CaO}}[/tex]

Using CO₂

[tex]\text{moles of CaCO₃ = 18.06 g CO₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol CO₂}}{\text{44.01 g CO₂}} × \frac{\text{1 mol CaCO₃}}{\text{1 mol CO₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of CaCO₃ = 0.4104 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Determine the limiting reactant.

Since CaO produces less number of moles of CaCO₃

[tex]\boxed{\text{CaO is the limiting reactant.}}[/tex]

Solution (Theoretical Yield):

Since CaO is the limiting reactant

[tex]\text{theoretical yield = 0.2722896 mol CaCO₃} × \frac{\text{100.09 g CaCO₃}}{\text{1 mol CaCO₃}}[/tex].

23. Calcium carbonate (CaCO3), also called calcite. a. Calculate the molar mass of calcium carbonate. b. A certain sample of calcium carbonate contains 4.86 moles. What is the mass in grams of this sample? What is the mass of the CO3-2 ions present?


Answer:

a. To calculate the molar mass of calcium carbonate, we need to determine the number of grams per mole for each element present in the compound. The molar mass of calcium carbonate can be calculated using the following formula:

Molar mass (g/mol) = (Mass of Ca (g/mol)) + (Mass of C (g/mol)) + (Mass of O (g/mol))

The atomic weights of Ca, C, and O are 40.08 g/mol, 12.01 g/mol, and 16.00 g/mol, respectively. Using these values, the molar mass of calcium carbonate can be calculated as follows:

Molar mass of CaCO3 = (40.08 g/mol) + (12.01 g/mol) + (3 * 16.00 g/mol)

= 40.08 g/mol + 12.01 g/mol + 48.00 g/mol

= 100.09 g/mol

Thus, the molar mass of calcium carbonate is 100.09 g/mol.

b. If a certain sample of calcium carbonate contains 4.86 moles, the mass in grams of this sample can be calculated using the molar mass of calcium carbonate:

Mass (g) = (Number of moles) * (Molar mass)

= (4.86 moles) * (100.09 g/mol)

= 486.0 g

The mass of the CO3-2 ions present in this sample can be calculated using the molar mass of CO3-2, which is 60.01 g/mol:

Mass of CO3-2 ions = (Number of moles of CO3-2) * (Molar mass of CO3-2)

= (4.86 moles) * (60.01 g/mol)

= 291.6 g

Thus, the mass of the CO3-2 ions present in the sample of calcium carbonate is 291.6 g.

Step 5: Calculate the mass of CaCO₃ needed using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of CaCO₃ = 0.133845 mol CO₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol CaCO₃}}{\text{1 mol CO₂}} × \frac{\text{100.09 g CaCO₃}}{\text{1 mol CaCO₃}}[/tex]
