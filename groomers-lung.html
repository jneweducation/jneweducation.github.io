A. Write a piecewise function that describes what your dog groomer charges.

[tex]\sf f(x)= \begin{cases}\sf 300, &\sf if \: x \leq 15 \\\sf 400, &\sf if \: 15<x \leq 40 \\\sf 20x-400, &\sf if \: x > 40 \end{cases}[/tex]

B. What would the groomer charge if your bulldog weighs 60 pounds?

x=60, 60>40

[tex]\begin{aligned} &\sf f(x)=20x-400 \\&\sf f(60)=20(60)-400 \\&\sf f(60)=1200-400 \\&\sf f(60)=800 \end{aligned} [/tex]

The groomer would charge 800 pesos if your bulldog weighs 60 pounds.

6. solve the given problem: your dog groomer charges according to the weight of the dog. The minimum charge for 3kilos is php 300 and php 48 for every additional weight (kilo). How much are you going to pay if your dog weights 18 kilos?​


Answer:

You will pay PHP 1020 to groom your 18-kilo dog, based on the given pricing scheme.

Step-by-step explanation:

We can calculate the cost of grooming the dog that weighs 18 kilos as follows:

First, we subtract the minimum weight of 3 kilos from the actual weight of the dog:

18 kg - 3 kg = 15 kg

Next, we calculate the additional charge for the weight over the minimum:

15 kg * PHP 48/kg = PHP 720

Finally, we add the additional charge to the minimum charge:

PHP 300 (minimum charge) + PHP 720 (additional charge) = PHP 1020

Therefore, you will pay PHP 1020 to groom your 18-kilo dog, based on the given pricing scheme.
