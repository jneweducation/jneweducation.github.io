15. Statement I – Manufacturers typically employ large-scale production which is done in manufacturing plants. Statement II – The products of manufacturing companies can be sold directly to consumers only. a. Both statements are true. b. Only Statement I is true. c. Only Statement II is true. d. Both statements are false.


Hello! I'm so glad you asked this question on Brainly because other students are also wondering about this. I'm here to provide you an answer and a corresponding explanation.

The correct answer is d. Both statements are false.

Statement: Manufacturers typically employ large-scale production which is done in  manufacturing plants.Validity: FalseReason

Manufacturers are makers of goods and products. The production of goods may either be large-scale or small-scale depending on the available capital, demand, the complexity of the product, and the skill required for production.

This means that a person or a small group of people creating a product can be called manufacturers themselves. When we say manufacturing or production, it does not necessarily refer to large factories or big corporations. It can also be small or medium-sized enterprises.

Statement: The products of manufacturing companies can be sold directly to  consumers only.Validity: FalseReason

Companies operate differently from one another. They create different products that have varying shelf life and demand. Some products have to handled delicately while others can take on more beating.

Some manufacturers know the exact people who need their products and have the ability to directly contact them while other companies focus their efforts on production and will leave distribution to a different team or company. There are also manufacturers that prefer to tie themselves under exclusivity clauses which guarantees them a client while others will have clients no matter what.

This is why manufacturing companies have different distribution channels. Not all manufacturers choose to sell directly to their final consumers while others may choose to have several levels of distribution channels.

19. A soap manufacturing company wants to design a packaging for one of their products. The length of the packaging should be 5 cm longer than its width and its height should be 5 cm. It should be able to hold 750 cu cm. What are the dimensions of the packaging? Show the solutions please.


Problem:

A soap manufacturing company wants to design a packaging for one of their products. The length of the packaging should be 5 cm longer than its width and its height should be 5 cm. It should be able to hold 750 cu cm. What are the dimensions of the packaging?

Given:

Let the width be W

length be 5 cm + W

height be 5 cm

volume be 750 cu cm

Base from the given, we can assume that the shape of soap packaging is prism.

Formula: Volume of Prism

V = length × width × height

Solution:

Substitute the given values.

750 = (5 + W) × (W) × (5)

750 = 5W(5 + W)

750 = 25W + 5W^2

Arrange. Transpose 750 to the other side of the equation to make a quadratic equation.

5W^2 + 25W - 750 = 0

Find W. Use quadratic formula.

[tex] w = \frac{ - b \: \frac{ + }{} \sqrt{ {b}^{2} - 4ac} }{2a} \\ [/tex]

a = 5, b = 25, c = -750

[tex] = \frac{ - 25 \frac{ + }{} \sqrt{ {25}^{2} - 4(5)( - 750) } }{2(5)}[/tex]

[tex] = \frac{ - 25 \frac{ + }{} \sqrt{625 + 15000} }{10} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{ - 25 \frac{ + }{} \sqrt{15625} }{10} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{ - 25 \frac{ + }{}125 }{10} [/tex]

Simplify

[tex] = \frac{ - 5 \frac{ + }{} 25}{2} [/tex]

Two possible values of W:

[tex]w1 = \frac{ - 5 + 25}{2} = \frac{20}{2} = 10[/tex]

[tex]w2 = \frac{ - 5 - 25}{2} = \frac{ - 30}{2} = - 15[/tex]

However, the value we need to solve the problem must be a positive integer. Therefore, the value of W is 10.

Dimension of the soap packaging:

L × W × H

Substitute.

L = 5 cm + W

= 5 cm + 10

= 15 cm

W = W

= 10 cm

H = 5 cm

Final Answer:

The dimension of the soap packaging is 15 cm by 10 cm by 5 cm.
