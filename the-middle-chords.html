1. . A special type of broken chord accompaniment where the chord is played in this order: lower note- highest note-middle note then repeats the pattern to create a smooth, sustained, flowing sound


Answer:

"ALBERTI"

Explanation:

Alberti bass is a kind of broken chord or arpeggiated accompaniment, where the notes of the chord are presented in the order lowest, highest, middle, highest. This pattern is then repeated several times throughout the music. The broken chord pattern helps to create a smooth, sustained, flowing sound on the piano.


2. a gameplay on the flat table divided into two equal chords by a net fixed across its width at the middle​


Answer:

1. Table Tennis

-Is a sport in which two or four players hit a lightweight ball, also known as the ping-pong ball, back and forth across a table using small rackets. The game takes place on a hard table divided by a net. Except for the initial serve, the rules are generally as follows: players must allow a ball played toward them to bounce one time on their side of the table and must return it so that it bounces on the opposite side at least once. A point is scored when a player fails to return the ball within the rules. Play is fast and demands quick reactions. Spinning the ball alters its trajectory and limits an opponent's options, giving the hitter a great advantage.
