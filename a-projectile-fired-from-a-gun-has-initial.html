Answer:

To calculate the horizontal range of the projectile, we can use the following formula:

Range = (initial velocity^2/g) x sin(2θ)

Where:

initial velocity is the velocity at which the projectile is launched, which is 350 m/s in this case

g is the acceleration due to gravity, which is 9.8 m/s^2

θ is the angle of elevation, which is 25 degrees in this case

Substituting the values:

Range = (350^2/9.8) x sin(2 x 25)

Range = (122500/9.8) x sin(50)

Range = 12449.49 meters (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the horizontal range of the projectile is approximately 12449.49 meters.

20. a projectile is fired fron a cliff 300 m high with an initial velocity of 400m/s. if the firing angle is 30 degrees from the horizontal, compute the horizontal range of the projectile mo​


Answer:

7000.05 m

Explanation:

Using the equations of motion for projectile motion, we can find the horizontal range of the projectile as follows:

The initial vertical velocity, Vy = V * sin(theta) = 400 * sin(30) = 200 m/s

The initial horizontal velocity, Vx = V * cos(theta) = 400 * cos(30) = 346.4 m/s

The time taken for the projectile to hit the ground can be found using the formula:

h = Vy * t + 0.5 * g * t^2

where h = 300 m (height of cliff) and g = 9.8 m/s^2 (acceleration due to gravity)

Substituting the values, we get:

300 = 200 * t + 0.5 * 9.8 * t^2

Solving for t, we get t = 20.204 seconds (approx.)

The horizontal range, R, can be found using the formula:

R = Vx * t

Substituting the values, we get:

R = 346.4 * 20.204 = 7000.05 m (approx.)

Therefore, the horizontal range of the projectile is approximately 7000.05 m.
