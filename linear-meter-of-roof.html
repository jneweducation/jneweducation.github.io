1. 1. The roof has a slant height of 13 meters and the span is 24 meters. what is the vertical height of the roof?​


Answer:

5 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's call the vertical height of the roof "h".

Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can relate the slant height, span, and vertical height of the roof:

h^2 + (24/2)^2 = 13^2

h^2 + 144 = 169

h^2 = 169 - 144

h^2 = 25

Taking the square root of both sides, we get:

h = 5

Therefore, the vertical height of the roof is 5 meters.

Let: a = vertical height of the roof

c = slant height

b = span

Solution:

b = 24/2 = 12

c² = a² + b²

(13)² = a² + (12)²

169 = a² + 144

a² = 169 - 144

a² = 25

√a² = √25

a = 5

The vertical height of the roof is 5 meters

6. A tennis ball is dropped from a roof 16 meters from the ground. How long does it take for the ball to reach the ground?​


Answer: 1.807 seconds

Step-by-step explanation:

We can solve this problem using the kinematic equation:

[tex]\sf\qquad\dashrightarrow h = \dfrac{1}{2} * g * t^2 + v_0 * t + h_0[/tex]

where:

h is the height of the ball above the groundg is the acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2)t is the time taken for the ball to reach the groundv0 is the initial velocity of the ball (which is 0 since it is dropped)h0 is the initial height of the ball (which is 16 meters)

At the moment the ball hits the ground, its height is zero. Therefore, we can set h = 0 and solve for t:

[tex]\sf:\implies 0 = \dfrac{1}{2} * 9.81\: m/s^2 * t^2 + 0 * t + 16\: m[/tex]

Simplifying and solving for t, we get:

[tex]\sf:\implies t = \sqrt{\bigg(\dfrac{32}{9.81}\bigg)} \approx \boxed{\bold{\:\:1.807\: seconds\:\:}}\:\:\:\green{\checkmark}[/tex]

Therefore, it takes approximately 1.807 seconds for the tennis ball to reach the ground when dropped from a roof 16 meters high.

16. A tennis ball is dropped from a roof 16 meters from the ground. how long does it take for the ball to reach the ground?


Answer:

To find out how long it takes for the ball to reach the ground, you can use the equation for the motion of an object under the influence of gravity:

d = 1/2 * a * t^2 + v * t + d

Where:

d is the distance traveled, measured in metersa is the acceleration due to gravity, which is 9.8 m/s^2t is the time it takes for the ball to fall, measured in secondsv is the initial velocity of the ball, which is zero in this case since the ball is dropped from rest

In this case, we know that the distance traveled (d) is 16 meters and the initial velocity (v) is zero, so we can rearrange the equation to solve for t:

t = sqrt((2 * d) / a)

Plugging in the values, we get:

t = sqrt((2 * 16) / 9.8) = sqrt(32 / 9.8) = sqrt(3.26530612244898) = 1.8013271108436504 seconds

So it would take about 1.8 seconds for the ball to reach the ground.

29. Luke drop a pile of roof shingles from the top of a roof located 8.52 meters above the ground. Determine the time required for the shingles to reach the ground.​


Answer:

We can solve this problem using the kinematic equation:

d = 0.5 * g * t^2

where d is the distance traveled (which is equal to the height of the roof), g is the acceleration due to gravity (which is approximately 9.81 m/s^2), and t is the time it takes for the shingles to reach the ground.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

8.52 = 0.5 * 9.81 * t^2

Simplifying and solving for t, we get:

t = sqrt(8.52 / (0.5 * 9.81)) = 1.22 seconds

Therefore, it takes approximately 1.22 seconds for the shingles to reach the ground.
