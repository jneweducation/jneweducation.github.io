Answer:

Formula for the surface area of a pyramid: SA = A + (1/2)Pl

- S = 8cm (length of one side of the square base)

- A = S^2 (formula for finding the area of a square)

- A = 8^2 = 64cm^2

- P = 4S (perimeter of the square base)

- P = 4(8) = 32cm

- l = sqrt(S^2 + (1/2P)^2) (formula for finding the slant height)

- l = sqrt(8^2 + (1/2(32))^2) = sqrt(64 + 64) = sqrt(128) = 8sqrt(2)

- A = (1/2)Sl (formula for finding the area of a triangular face)

- A = (1/2)(8)(8sqrt(2)) = 32sqrt(2)cm^2

- SA = A + (1/2)Pl

- SA = 64cm^2 + (1/2)(32cm)(8sqrt(2)) = 64cm^2 + 128sqrt(2)cm^2

- Therefore, the surface area of the pyramid is 64cm^2 + 128sqrt(2)cm^2.
