1. A 700-N rightward net force is being applied to a 10-kg object. What is the magnitude of the rightward acceleration on the object in m/s²?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F = ma}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for a

F = ma

[tex]\dfrac{F}{m} = \dfrac{ma}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{F}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{700 N}}{\text{10 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{70 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the magnitude of the rightward acceleration of the object is 70 m/s².

3. Suppose a rightward force of 64 N is applied on a box thathas a mass of 5.5 kg. If the box moved 16 m at a constantspeed. How much work is done on the object by the 64 Nforce?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the work

W = Fd

W = (64 N)(16 m)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{1,024 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 1,024 J.

14. Applied Eco1The price of 1 kg apples, which was $5 last month, is $6 today. True of False: The demand curve for apples must have shifted rightward between last month and today. Explain your answer.​


Answer:

False. The increase in price alone does not necessarily imply a rightward shift in the demand curve for apples.

The demand curve for apples represents the relationship between the quantity of apples consumers are willing and able to buy and the price of apples, while holding other factors constant. A rightward shift in the demand curve would mean that at each price level, consumers are willing and able to purchase a greater quantity of apples compared to the previous period.

However, it is possible that the increase in price from $5 to $6 has occurred due to other factors such as a decrease in the supply of apples. For example, if there was a drought or other weather-related issues that affected the harvest of apples, the supply of apples would decrease, causing the price to rise. In this case, the demand curve for apples would not have shifted, but rather the equilibrium point (where supply equals demand) would have shifted, resulting in a higher price for apples.
