5. A rigid tank of 1 m3 contains air at 600 kPa, 400 K. By mistake, someone lets 0.5 kg flow out. If the final temperature is 375 K a. Determine is the final pressure. b. Determine the mass of the gas remained in the tank.


Formula:

Ideal gas law is given by

[tex]PV=mRT[/tex]

where P is pressure, V is volume, m is mass, R is ideal gas constant, T is temperature.

Solution:

At the initial state of the tank, we have

[tex]P_{1}V=m_{1}RT_{1}[/tex]

For air, the ideal gas constant is

[tex]R=287\text{ J/kg-K}[/tex]

The initial mass is

[tex]m_1=\dfrac{P_{1}V}{RT_{1}} \\\\m_1=\dfrac{(600\times10^3\text{ Pa})(1\text{ m}^3)}{(287\text{ J/kg-K})(400\text{ K})} \\\\m_1=5.23\text{ kg}[/tex]

After the process, the mass of the gas remained in the tank is

[tex]m_{2}=5.23\text{ kg}-0.5\text{ kg}=4.73\text{ kg}[/tex]

At the final state of the tank, we have

[tex]P_{2}V=m_{2}RT_{2}[/tex]

The final pressure is

[tex]P_{2}=\dfrac{m_2RT_2}{V}\\\\P_{2}=\dfrac{(4.73\text{ kg})(287\text{ J/kg-K})(375\text{ K})}{1\text{ m}^3}\\\\P_{2}=509\times10^3\text{ Pa}=509\text{ kPa}[/tex]

Answers:

Problem:

Calculate the volume of gas in liter at a pressure of 100 kpa if its volume at 120 kpa is 1500 ml.

Note:

we will convert first 1500 ml to liter

1500 ml × 1 L / 1000 ml = 1.5 L

Given Data:

V₁ (initial volume) =  unknown

P₁  (initial pressure) = 100 kpa

V₂ (final volume) = 1500 ml or 1.5L

P₂ (final pressure) = 120 kpa

Solution:

We can solve this problem using Boyle's law formula ( P₁V₁ = P₂V₂)

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

   V₁  = 120 kpa × 1.5 L  /  100 kpa

   V₁  = 180 kpa.L / 100 kpa

   V₁  = 1.8 L

Answer:

the volume of gas in liter at 100 kpa is 1.8 L

Boyle's Law states that volume and pressure are inversely proportional when the temperature and the amount of gas is constant. When volume decreases, the pressure increases.

R' is the specific gas constant of air, it is:
