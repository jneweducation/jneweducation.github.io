2. A flagpole stands on the edge of the top of the building. At a horizontal distance of 120 meters, the angle of elevation of the top of the pole is 47°12′ and that of the foot of the pole is 39°18′. Find the height of the pole to the nearest tenth of a meter.


Answer:  ≈31.4 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

It helps to draw a basic diagram of the situation (see picture). Then do the following...

1. Find the decimal equivalents for the angle measures.

  12/60=0.2, so 47°12'=47.2°

  18/60=0.3, so 39°18′=39.3°

2. Set up and solve an equation to find the height of the building (b).

    tan(39.3°)=b/120 ⇒ b=120*tan(39.3°)≈98.22m (since final answer should be rounded to nearest tenth, best to only round to hundredths while still calculating)

3. Set up an equation that represents the total height of the building and flagpole (f), and solve for f.

   tan(47.2°)=(b+f)/120 ⇒ f=120*tan(47.2°)-b

                                        f≈129.59-98.22=31.37m

  You can also use the exact expressions to calculate:

     f=120*tan(47.2°)-120*tan(39.3°)≈31.37m

4. Round answer to nearest tenth: 31.37m≈31.4m
