Problem-Solving

Since the number of items in your post exceeded the maximum number of items per question (which is 3 items/question), I'll just provide answers and explanations for the odd-numbered items.

1. [tex]\dfrac{200}{3}[/tex] or 66.67

PROBLEM: If 200 is divided in the ratio 1:2:3, what is the middle part?

SOLUTION: Get the total number of divisions in the ratio 1:2:3. Let the total be [tex]t[/tex]. Then,

[tex]t=1+2+3=\bold{6}[/tex]

Divide 200 by [tex]t[/tex] to determine the value of one part.

[tex]\dfrac{200}{t}=\dfrac{200}{6}=\bold{\dfrac{100}{3}}[/tex]

Since the middle part has 2 parts, multiply [tex]\frac{100}{3}[/tex] by 2.

[tex]2\left(\dfrac{100}{3}\right)=\boxed{\bold{\dfrac{200}{3}}}[/tex]

3. 12 years old

PROBLEM: Erl is 3 years older than Dhang. Together, their ages total 21 years. How old is Erl?

SOLUTION: Let [tex]e[/tex] be the age of Erl and [tex]d[/tex] be the age of Dhang. Then,

[tex]e+d=21\ \ \ \ \ ...(1)[/tex]

If Erl is 3 years older than Dhang, this means that

[tex]e=d+3\ \ \ \ \ ...(2)[/tex]

Substituting eq. (2) into (1),

[tex](d+3)+d=21\\2d+3=21\\2d=21-3\\2d=18\\\bold{d=9}[/tex]

Since [tex]e[/tex] represents Erl's age, substitute [tex]d=9[/tex] into eq. (2).

[tex]e=9+3=\bold{12}[/tex]

Thus, Erl's age is 12 years old.

5. Php 217.87

PROBLEM: A restaurant charges a 3.5% service tax. If your order amounted to P 210.50, how much in all did you pay?

SOLUTION: Let [tex]T[/tex] be the total amount you need to pay;

[tex]o[/tex] be the order amount; and

[tex]p[/tex] be the service fee amount.

Then, [tex]T=o+p\ \ \ \ ...(1)[/tex]

The service fee is equal to

[tex]p=(3.5\%)(o)\\\text{Change 3.5\% to decimal form.}\\p=(0.035)(210.50)=\bold{7.3675}[/tex]

Substituting the value of [tex]p[/tex] into eq. (1),

[tex]T=210.50+7.3675=217.8675\approx \boxed{\bold{217.87}}[/tex]

7. 450 dm

PROBLEM: Find the perimeter of a regular pentagon with sides that are 90 dm long.

SOLUTION: A regular pentagon has 5 equal sides. If the perimeter stands for the sum of all sides of a geometric shape, then, the perimeter of a pentagon is the sum of all of its 5 sides.

Thus,

[tex]P_{pentagon}=s+s+s+s+s=5s\\P_{pentagon}=90+90+90+90+90=5(90)=\boxed{\bold{450\ dm}}[/tex]
