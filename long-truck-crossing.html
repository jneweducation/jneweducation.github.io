Problem:

» How long does it take a truck traveling at a velocity of 90 km/hr to travel 990 km?

Formula:[tex] \underline{ \boxed{ \bold{ \sf time= \frac{distance}{speed} }}} [/tex]

— To calculate the time, divide the given distance (in km) by the given speed (in km/h). The quotient of it is the time, should be measured in hours.

Solution:

The givens are speed of 90 km/hr and distance of 990 km ... So,

[tex]\sf time= \frac{distance}{speed} \\[/tex]

[tex]\sf time= \frac{990 \: km}{90 \: km/h} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\sf time = \underline{ \boxed{ \purple{ \sf 11 \: h}}}[/tex]

Therefore, it will take 11 hours.

24. an owner type jeepney 3m long overtakes a truck 30m long which is travelling at the rate of 20kmh. how fast must the car travel to pass the truck in 30 seconds?​


[tex] \Large \mathbb{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

Since the car and the truck are traveling in the same direction, the car will cover its own length + the length of the truck to completely pass or cross the moving truck. Note that the relative speed of 2 bodies with speeds [tex]S_1[/tex] and [tex]S_2,[/tex] where [tex] S_1 > S_2, [/tex] moving in same direction is [tex]S_1 - S_2. [/tex]

[tex] \bold{Given:}\: \begin{cases}\: L_1 = 3\ \sf m \\ \: L_2 = 30\ \sf m \\ \: S_2 = 20\ \sf kph \\ \: t = 30\ \sf seconds \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \bold{Required:}\: S_1 [/tex]

We know that [tex] \textsf{Distance} = \sf Speed \times Time. [/tex] So the formula will be

[tex] L_1 + L_2 = (S_1 - S_2)t, [/tex] where [tex] t [/tex] is the elapsed time.

Now substitute the given to the formula then solve for [tex] S_1 [/tex] but you need to multiply the speed by [tex] \frac{1000}{3600} [/tex] to convert [tex] \sf kph [/tex] into [tex] \sf m/s. [/tex]

[tex] 3 + 30 = \left(S_1 - 20\right)\left(\dfrac{1000}{3600}\right)(30) [/tex]

[tex] 33 = \dfrac{25}{3}(S_1 - 20) [/tex]

[tex] 33\left(\dfrac{3}{25}\right) = S_1 - 20 [/tex]

[tex] \dfrac{99}{25} = S_1 - 20 [/tex]

[tex] 3.96 = S_1 - 20 [/tex]

[tex] S_1 = \boxed{23.96\ \sf kph} [/tex]
