27. The time taken to assemble a car in a certain plant is a random variable having a normal distribution of 20 hours and a standard deviation of 2 hours. What is the probability that a car can be assembled at this plant in a period of time less than 19.5 hours between 20 and 22 hours? ​


There is 0.3413 probability that a car can be assembled at this plant in a period of time less than 19.5 hours between 20 and 22 hours.

The time required to assemble follows a normal distribution.

Given a mean  i.e. μ of = 20 hoursGiven the standard deviation i.e. σ, which is = 2 hours

A standard score or z-score is the normal random variable of a standard normal distribution. The following equation can be used to convert any normal random variable X into a z score:

z = (X − μ) / σ

where X is a normal random variable, is its mean, and is its standard deviation.

For X = 22

Z = (22 − 20) / 2 = 1

For X = 20

Z = (20 − 20) / 2 = 0

P(a < X<b) = P(X < b) – P(X < a)  as understood from diagram

P(20 < X < 22) = P(X < 22) − P(X < 20)

                   = P(Z < 1) − P(Z < 0)

                    = 0.3413

What exactly is Standard Deviation?

The standard deviation is similar to the "mean of the mean," and it can often assist you in determining the story behind the data. It can help to understand this concept by learning about what statisticians call "normal distribution" of data.

A normal distribution of data means that the majority of examples in a set of data are close to the "average," with only a few examples tending to one extreme or the other.

Terms to be familiar withx = a single value in your data setavg (x) = the mean (average) of all x values in your dataset.n = the number of x values in your data set

Looking at the standard deviation can help point you in the right direction when trying to figure out why information is the way it is.
