10. JJ's training program mostly consists of squats, push-ups and pull-ups that will help increase the amount of force that he will exert. Which of the following components of physical fitness he'll most likely going to develop?


Answer:

When going through JJ's training program which mostly consists of doing squats, push-ups, and pull-ups. Which component of physical fitness will he develop the most?

By doing squats, pushups, and pullups, JJ’s training program is developing muscular strength.

Explanation:

Depending on the number of sets and repetitions of the exercises mentioned, the other components of physical fitness can also be worked on. But they mostly develop the strength of those body parts. They usually target the large muscles of the limbs and the lats, in order to get that muscular form that shows strength.  

Squats are done by standing up with the somewhat apart from each other and going down to an almost sitting position, and repeating this for a certain number of times, it depends on how advanced the person is in his or her training. The back would have to be straightened throughout the exercise, this will develop the size and the strength of the thighs, and the glute muscles.  

Reminders:

Avoid arching the back forward, only use the legs to lift the upper body. Anyone doing squats should avoid locking the knees after standing up because it can ruin the cartilage between the Femur and Tibia.

For Push-ups, one has to start with his entire front on the floor, with a straight stiff back. Then you have to push down and forward with your hands and arms and stopping right before the elbows lock. The pushing motion develops the upper body, shoulders, and arms.

To get that familiar V-shaped upper body everyone likes, Pull-ups are needed.

What you need to have in order to do pull-ups:

Any strong cylindrical material that is wide enough for you to safely grip with your hand that is horizontally positioned.  It has to be at least a foot above your upwardly extended arms. It has to be able to carry your weight.

So you would have to hold with a firm grip on let’s say a horizontal metal pipe or bar, and pull yourself up and stopping when your chin is at the same level as the pipe or bar. Then you slowly go back down without locking the elbows. The number of reps and sets will again depend on the person. Pull-ups develop the lateral muscles (Latissimus Dorsi), Deltoids and the Biceps.
