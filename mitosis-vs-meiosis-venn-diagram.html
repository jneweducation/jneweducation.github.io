14. Activity 2 Mitosis vs Meiosis please pa sagot po​


Answer:

Mitosis and meiosis are two types of cell division processes that occur in eukaryotic cells. Though they have some similarities, they are fundamentally different from each other in terms of their purpose, results, and the number of times the cell divides. Here are the key differences between mitosis and meiosis:

1. Purpose:

The primary purpose of mitosis is to generate two identical daughter cells from a single parent cell. Each of the daughter cells contains a complete set of identical chromosomes as the parent cell. On the other hand, the primary purpose of meiosis is to generate gametes such as sperm and eggs that are genetically diverse from the parent cell. This is achieved with the help of genetic recombination and independent assortment.

2. Number of Divisions:

Mitosis involves only one division and results in two identical daughter cells, while meiosis involves two divisions: Meiosis I and Meiosis II. Meiosis I results in two haploid daughter cells or secondary spermatocytes, each with half the number of chromosomes as the parent cell. Meiosis II then separates the sister chromatids into four haploid cells or spermatids.

3. Chromosome Distribution:

In mitosis, the chromosome distribution is symmetrical between the two daughter cells. This means that each daughter cell gets an identical set of chromosomes. However, meiosis is an asymmetrical process, and each daughter cell ends up with a distinct set of chromosomes that are different from the others. This is because during prophase I of meiosis, the homologous chromosomes pair up and exchange genetic information by crossing over.

4. Genetic Diversity:

Another key difference between mitosis and meiosis is the level of genetic diversity present in the daughter cells. In mitosis, the genetic material of the parent cell is replicated and divided equally between the two daughter cells. This leads to the production of identical copies of the original cell, with no genetic diversity. In meiosis, genetic recombination and independent assortment lead to the production of gametes that are genetically diverse and could have different combinations of alleles.

In summary, mitosis is a process of cell division that results in the production of two identical daughter cells with a complete set of chromosomes. While meiosis is a process of cell division that results in the production of four genetically diverse haploid cells or gametes.

26. Compare the number and type of cells that result from meiosis vs. mitosis.


Comparison of the processes of mitosis and meiosis. Mitosis produces two diploid (2n) somatic cells that are genetically identical to each other and the original parent cell, whereas meiosis produces four haploid (n) gametes that are genetically unique from each other and the original parent (germ) cell.

Which type and number of cells result from meiosis?

four

Meiosis is a type of cell division that reduces the number of chromosomes in the parent cell by half and produces four gamete cells. This process is required to produce egg and sperm cells for sexual reproduction.

What type of cells result from mitosis?

Mitosis results in two cells that are identical to the cell you started with. Mitosis, unlike meiosis, occurs in somatic cells and creates daughter cells with a full set of chromosomes. So, 2 diploid somatic cells (that are exact copies of the parent cell) result from mitosis.

How many cells are produced in mitosis and meiosis?

Meiosis produces 4 haploid cells. Mitosis produces 2 diploid cells. The old name for meiosis was reduction/ division. Meiosis I reduces the ploidy level from 2n to n (reduction) while Meiosis II divides the remaining set of chromosomes in a mitosis-like process (division).
