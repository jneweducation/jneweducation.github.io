6. The ratio of Faye's savings to Mina's savings is 3 : 7. If Faye spends 4/9 of her money, she will have $112 less than Mina. How much is Mina's savings?


Answer:

We can start by using algebra to set up and solve the problem. Let x be the amount of Mina's savings in dollars. Then, based on the given ratio, we know that Faye's savings is 3/10 of x. We can express this as:

Faye's savings = (3/10)x

We also know that if Faye spends 4/9 of her money, she will have $112 less than Mina. So we can write an equation using the information given:

(3/10)x - (4/9)(3/10)x = x - 112

Simplifying the equation gives:

(3/10)x - (4/9)(3/10)x = x - 112

Solving for x, we get:

x = 560

Therefore, Mina's savings is $560.

✏️AGES===============================

Problem: Mina is Sana's Mother. Mina is 25 years old. Four years ago. Mina was three times as old as Sana was then. How old is Sana now?

Solution: Represent x and y as the ages of Mina and Sana respectively. Make two equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25& \green{(eq. \: 1)} \\ (x - 4) = 3(y - 4)& \green{(eq. \: 2)} \end{cases}[/tex]

- Find the age of Sana by substituting x in the second equation in terms of y.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ (25 - 4) = 3(y - 4) \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 21 = 3(y - 4) \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 21 = 3y - 12 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 21 + 12 = 3y \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 33 = 3y \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ \begin{gathered} \frac{33}{3} = \frac{ \cancel3y}{ \cancel3} \end{gathered} \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\y = 11 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \therefore[/tex] Sana's age is:

[tex] \large \rm Sana = \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 11 \: years \: old \: }}[/tex]===============================

14. if 8 moles of potassium superoxide reacts with 5 moles of water how many moles will remain underacted? A. 1 mole KO2 B. 3.0 moles KO2 C. 1.0 mole H2O D. 3.0 moles H2O


Number of unreacted moles : B. 3.0 moles KO₂

Further explanation

A reaction coefficient is a number in the chemical formula of a substance involved in the reaction equation. The reaction coefficient is useful for equalizing reagents and products.

Limiting reagents are reactants that limit the outcome of the reaction. Where if two substances are reacted there is a possibility that both substances are used up or one is used up and the other is left.

The method that can be used to find limiting reagents is to divide the number of moles of known substances by their respective coefficients, and the small quotient will be used up or as a limiting reagent

Reaction

2 KO₂ + 2 H₂O ⇒ 2 KOH + H₂O₂ + O₂

8 moles of potassium superoxide reacts with 5 moles of water, then :

Comparison of both moles:

KO₂ : H₂O = 8 : 5

Comparison of both moles with coefficients :

[tex]\rm KO_2=\dfrac{8}{2}=4\\\\H_2O=\dfrac{5}{2}=2.5[/tex]

So H₂O : limiting reactants

mol KO₂  that reacts :

[tex]\rm mol~KO_2 = \dfrac{coefficient~KO_2}{coefficient~H_2O}\times mol~H_2O\\\\mol~KO_2=\dfrac{2}{2}\times 5\\\\mol~KO_2~=5[/tex]

reaction       5             5           5           2.5      2.5
