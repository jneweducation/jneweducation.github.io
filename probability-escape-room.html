14. A farm has 5 cows and 10 white cows. A fence is open and two cows escape. What is the probability that it will be a brown cow,then a white cow?


Answer:

The probability of selecting a brown cow first is 5/(5+10) = 1/3, since there are 5 brown cows out of 15 total cows.

After one brown cow has escaped, there are 4 brown cows and 10 white cows left. So the probability of selecting a white cow second is 10/(4+10) = 5/7.

Therefore, the probability of selecting a brown cow first and a white cow second is:

(1/3) * (5/7) = 5/21, or approximately 0.238.

Answer:

The probability of the first cow being brown is 5/15 (since there are a total of 5 brown cows and 10 white cows, for a total of 15 cows).

After the first cow escapes, there are 4 brown cows and 10 white cows left on the farm. The probability of the second cow being white, given that the first cow was brown, is 10/14 (since there are now 14 cows left on the farm, of which 10 are white).

Therefore, the probability of both events happening in sequence (brown cow, then white cow) is:

(5/15) x (10/14) = 1/3 x 5/7 = 5/21

So the probability that it will be a brown cow, then a white cow, is 5/21.
