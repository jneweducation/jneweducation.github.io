3. An arc in the form of parabola is 60 m across the bottom. The highest point is 16 m above the horizontal base. What is the length of the beam placed horizontally across the arc 3m below the top?


SOLUTION:

Let:

(0, 16) be the vertex or the highest point of the parabolic arc(-30, 0) and (30, 0) be the endpoints of the horizontal base (60 m across)

Using the three points, we can solve for the equation of the parabola.

Vertex: [tex](16, 0)[/tex]

[tex] y = a(x - h)^2 + k [/tex]

[tex] y = a(x - 0)^2 + 16 [/tex]

[tex] y = ax^2 + 16 [/tex]

We can use either of the two endpoints to solve for [tex]a. [/tex]

At [tex] x = 30, [/tex]

[tex] y = a(30)^2 + 16 = 0 [/tex]

[tex] 900a = -16 [/tex]

[tex] a = -\dfrac{16}{900} [/tex]

[tex] a = - \dfrac{4}{225} [/tex]

Equation of the parabola:

[tex] y = -\dfrac{4}{225}x^2 + 16 [/tex]

Now at [tex] y = 13 [/tex] (3 units below the vertex),

[tex] 13 = -\dfrac{4}{225}x^2 + 16 [/tex]

[tex] \dfrac{4}{225}x^2 = 16 - 13 [/tex]

[tex] \dfrac{4}{225}x^2 = 3 [/tex]

[tex] x^2 = 3\left(\dfrac{225}{4}\right) [/tex]

[tex] x = \pm \dfrac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} [/tex]

The endpoints of the segment 3 m below the highest point will be at points [tex] \left(-\dfrac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}, 13\right)[/tex] and [tex] \left(\dfrac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}, 13\right)[/tex] with length equal to [tex] 2\left(\dfrac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) = 15\sqrt{3}[/tex] units.

Thus, the length of the horizontal beam is [tex] \boxed{15\sqrt{3}\:\: m}. [/tex]
