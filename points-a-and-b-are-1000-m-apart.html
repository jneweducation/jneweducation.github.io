Sin(Opposite/Adjacent) Cos(Adjacent/Hypotenuse) Tan(Opposite/Adjacent)

3. Two vertical poles 20 m and 30 m high, are 60 m apart. A rope is to be attached from the top of one pole to a point on the ground halfway between the poles, and then to the top of the other pole. How long a rope is needed?

┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄

Explanation:

The description forms two right triangle. Half the distance between the two poles represents its bases. The rope from the top of each poles connecting to the ground represents the hypotenuse of the triangles.

1st Triangle:

Base = 30mLeg = 20mHypotenuse = c

2nd Triangle:

Base = 30mLeg = 30mHypotenuse = c

┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄

Formula:

[tex]\Large \tt \green{a^2 + b^2 = c^2}[/tex]

┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄

Find the length of the hypotenuse of the 1st Triangle

[tex]\big \begin{aligned}\green{\sf c^2} &\green{=\sf a^2 + b^2} \\\sf c^2 &=\sf 30^2 + 20^2 \\\sf c^2 &=\sf 900+400 \\\sf c^2 &=\sf 1300 \\\sf \sqrt{c^2}&=\sf \sqrt{1300} \\\sf \purple{c}&\purple{=\sf 10 \sqrt{13}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄

Find the length of the hypotenuse of the 2nd Triangle

[tex]\big \begin{aligned}\green{\sf c^2} &\green{=\sf a^2 + b^2} \\\sf c^2 &=\sf 30^2 + 30^2 \\\sf c^2 &=\sf 2 \times 30^2 \\\sf c^2 &=\sf 2 \times 900 \\\sf c^2 &=\sf 1800 \\\sf \sqrt{c^2}&=\sf \sqrt{1800} \\\sf \purple{c}&\purple{=\sf 30 \sqrt{2}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄┄

Add the lengths of the rope

[tex]\big \sf 10\sqrt{13}+30\sqrt{2}=\purple{78.48192}[/tex]

26. A car travels from point A to B in 3 hours and returns back to point A in 5 hours.Point A and B are 1500km apart along a straight highway.What is the speed of the car?


Answer:

The speed of the car from point A to B is 500 km/h

The speed of the car from point B to A is 300 km/h

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve the speed of a car with a constant distance but different time of travel. Here, we are going to solve each case using the same formula and this is:

s = d/t

where

s     is the speed of the car traveling from point A to B and vice

      versa, unit is in km/h

d     is the distance between the two points, unit is in km

t      is the time traveled by the car, unit is in seconds

For the given information

A to B:

t = 3 hours

d = 1500 km

s = ?

B to A:

t = 5 hours

d = 1500 km

s = ?

Solving the problem

A to B:

s = d/t

s = 1300 km/ 3 hours

s = 500 km/h

A to B:

s = d/t

s = 1300 km/ 5 hours

s = 300 km/h

Therefore, the speed of the car from point A to B is 500 km/h while its speed in returning back is 300 km/h.
