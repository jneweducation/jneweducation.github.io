7. Write 5 examples of each common stereotypes Age stereotype Racial StereotypesGender StereotypesSocial Class Stereotypes​


Answer:

Age stereotypes:

1. Older people are forgetful and unable to learn new things.

2. Teenagers are rebellious and irresponsible.

3. Children are naive and innocent.

4. Middle-aged people are boring and unadventurous.

5. Young people are entitled and self-absorbed.

Racial stereotypes:

1. African Americans are lazy and dishonest.

2. Asian Americans are good at math and science.

3. Latinx people are passionate and fiery.

4. Native Americans are lazy and dependent on government assistance.

5. White people are privileged and entitled.

Gender stereotypes:

1. Men are not good at household tasks.

2. Women are emotional and irrational.

3. Girls are passive and delicate.

4. Women are not good at math and science.

5. Men are strong and capable, while women are weak and vulnerable.

Social class stereotypes:

1. Poor people are lazy and do not want to work.

2. Rich people are greedy and selfish.

3. Upper class people are snobbish and pretentious.

4. Working class people are uneducated and uncultured.

5. Middle class people are boring and conformist.

It is important to note that these stereotypes are harmful and not based in reality. Every individual is unique and cannot be accurately judged or categorized based on their age, race, gender, or social class.

19. Marching Band Common Stereotype?


Answer:

The inter-group perception was examined in a context characterized by positive interdependence and extensive contact across group boundaries. The attitudes and beliefs of the woodwind, brass, and percussion sections of a university marching band were examined using measures of ingroup favoritism, outgroup homogeneity, and 12 scales assessing adult temperament. Although there was no evidence of ingroup favoritism or outgroup homogeneity, and few actual temperament differences across groups, stereotypic expectations based on temperament characteristics were strong. For each target group, the strength of stereotype was accentuated by ingroup judges and, most strongly, by outgroup judges.

28. DIRECTIONS: common types of stereotypes and give 5 example in every type​


1. Ethnic stereotypes: Examples include the belief that all Asians are good at math, that all African Americans are good at sports, that all Mexicans are lazy, that all Arabs are terrorists, and that all Jews are greedy.

2. Gender stereotypes: Examples include the belief that men are more assertive and aggressive, while women are more emotional and passive. That women are not good at science and men are not good at cooking and cleaning. That men are not good at expressing their emotions and women are not good at making decisions. That women are not good at leadership and men are not good at raising children.

3. Age stereotypes: Examples include the belief that older people are set in their ways, that young people are irresponsible, that teenagers are lazy, that middle-aged people are boring, and that people over a certain age are no longer capable of learning new things.

4. Sexual orientation stereotypes: Examples include the belief that all gay men are effeminate, that all lesbians are man-hating, that all bisexuals are promiscuous, that all transgender individuals are mentally ill, and that all asexual individuals are lacking in social skills.
