4. How do evaporation and boiling differ?


Evaporation refers to the transformation of a liquid into a gas without the process of boiling. It does not form any bubbles and evaporation will happen only in the exposed surface. On the other hand, boiling is simply the process of transforming liquid to gas with the production of bubbles. Also it happens through the entire liquid unlike evaporation.
