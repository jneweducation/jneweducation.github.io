The main types of chemical reactions

A chemical reaction is a process that occurs when matter undergoes rearrangement of the molecular or ionic composition and structure. It is divided into different types

1.) Synthesis – A reaction in which two or more elements combine chemically to form compounds

A +B --> AB

2.) Decomposition- A reaction in which a compound is broken down into simpler substances

AB --> A + B

3.) Single Replacement – a reaction wherein a monoatomic or polyatomic ion is being replaced with another monoatomic or polyatomic ion

AB + C --> AC + B

4.) Double Displacement – A reaction wherein a two compounds are exchanging their monoatomic or polyatomic ions to form two new compounds.

AB + CD --> AC + BD

5.) Neutralization – A type of double displacement reaction wherein an acid and a base reacts to form water and salt.

Acid  + Base --> Salt + water

6.) RedOx Reaction – A reaction where electron transfer occurs from a reducing agent to an oxidizing agent.

7.) Combustion  reaction - a reaction that occurs when an organic compound is burned in the presence of oxygen gas, forming carbon dioxide and water.

30. classify which reaction produces a precipitate​


Answer:

A double replacement reaction is specifically classified as a precipitation reaction when the chemical equation in question occurs in aqueous solution and one of the of the products formed is insoluble. An example of a precipitation reaction is given below: CdSO4 (aq) + K2S (aq) → CdS (s) + K2SO4 (aq)
