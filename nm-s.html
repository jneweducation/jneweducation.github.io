12. A force of 25 newtons moves a box a distance of 4 meters in 5 seconds. The work done on the box is Nm, and the power is Nm/s.


ANSWER:

W = 100 JP = 20 W

The work done is 100 J, The power is 20 W

EXPLANATION:

Given :

F = 25 N

d = 4 m

t = 5 s

Finding for Work :

The amount of work done on an object is the product of the force which is the 25 newtons and the box's displacement which is the 4 meters so:

Work = force × displacement

W = Fd

W = (25 newtons) ( 4 meters)

W = (25 N ) (4 m)

W = 100 N.m

W = 100 J

Finding for Power :

Power is the amount of work done which is 100 J in a unit of time which is the 5 seconds so:

Power = [tex]\frac{work}{time} \\[/tex]

P = [tex]\frac{W}{t}[/tex]

P = [tex]\frac{100 joules}{5 seconds}[/tex]

P = [tex]\frac{100 J}{5 s}[/tex]

P = 20 [tex]\frac{J}{s}[/tex]

P = 20 W

Therefore the work done on the box with a force of 25 newtons that moves a box a distance of 4 meters in 5 seconds is 100 Nm or 100 J, and the power is 20 Nm/s or 20 J/s or 20 J
