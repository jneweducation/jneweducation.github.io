1. The regions of the electromagnetic spectrum​


Answer:

The EM spectrum is generally divided into seven regions, in order of decreasing wavelength and increasing energy and frequency. The common designations are: radio waves, microwaves, infrared (IR), visible light, ultraviolet (UV), X-rays and gamma rays

5. describe the regions of the electromagnetic spectrum their properties and uses​


Explanation:

The electromagnetic spectrum is broadly classified into different named categories based on the wavelength and characteristics of the energy. The names like "microwave" or "infrared" were developed for convenience to describe electromagnetic radiation with similar characteristics, but there are no definitive dividing lines between one spectral region or the next. The only region in the electromagnetic spectrum that is relatively consistent in the wavelength definition is the visible spectrum, as it corresponds directly with to wavelengths that human eyes are sensitive to. The visible spectrum is a small window of the entire electromagnetic spectrum.

23. What are the different regions in the electromagnetic spectrum?​


The different regions in the electromagnetic spectrum are radio wave, microwave, infrared, visible light, UV light, XRay, Gamma Ray.

Electromagnetic wave types

Radio Wave

Radio waves are a form of electromagnetic radiation. These waves are formed when sound or audio turns into an electrical signal and through an oscillator wave (carrier wave) experiences modulation at a frequency contained in the radio wave frequency.

2. Microwaves

Microwaves (Microwave) are electromagnetic waves with a super high frequency (Super High Frequency), which is above 3 GHz (3×109 Hz). If microwaves are absorbed by an object, the molecules and atoms in the object will vibrate, causing a heating effect.

3. Infrared waves (Infra Red)

Infrared is an electromagnetic wave that has a longer wavelength than visible light, but shorter than radio waves. Infrared light is produced by electrons in molecular vibrating and then breaking down into different molecules as the object is heated.

4. Visible Light (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and purple)

Visible light waves are electromagnetic waves that can be seen by the human sense of sight. Visible light waves consist of red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet.

5. Ultraviolet Waves (Ultra purple)

Ultra violet light is part of the color spectrum, but the human sense of sight cannot see it. Ultra violet light has a very short wavelength compared to other colors. This causes ultra violet light to have a much stronger penetrating power than other colors. Ultra violet light is able to penetrate human skin and can even change cell structure.

6. X-rays

X-rays have a stronger penetrating power than ultraviolet light. X-rays are able to penetrate soft structures such as flesh and wood, except for solid structures such as bone. With its ability to penetrate flesh, the bone/skeletal structure can be seen on the film screen.

7. Gamma rays

Gamma-ray waves are the strongest electromagnetic waves among other electromagnetic waves.
