#BrainlyFast

Problem:

Osmium is the densest metal on Earth. What is the weight of a 2.15 kg osmium on  Earth?

Note:

The gravitation acceleration of Earth is equal to 9.8 m/s²Weight is equal to Gravitational forceThe Formula for Force is ( F = m × a ) and weight

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 2.15 kg

a ( gravitation acceleration ) = 9.8 m/s²

F ( Force ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve the problem using the formula of Force which is ( F = m × a ).

F = m × a

   = 2.15 kg ×  9.8 m/s²

   = 21.07 kg·m/s²

   = 21.07 N

Answer:

The weight of 2.15 kg osmium on Earth is = 21.07 N or  21.07 kg·m/s²

Solution:

Given Details:

Mass: 2.72 gramsVolume: 0.121 cm^3

(Volume is commonly measured in cubic units because volume is a measure of an object's length, width and height)

Formula for density:

Density = mass/volume

(Or as shown in the picture)

ρ = 2.72g/0.121 cm^3

=22.47 g/cm^3

Hence, the density of osmium is 22.47 g/cm^3 when its mass is 2.72g and its when volume is 0.121 cm.
