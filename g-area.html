5. polygon H is a scaled copy of polygon G using factor of 1/4.Polygon H's area is what fraction of polygonG's area​


Answer:

If polygon H is a scaled copy of polygon G using a scale factor of 1/4, then the area of polygon H will be 1/16 of the area of polygon G.

This is because the area of a polygon is proportional to the square of its scale factor. When the scale factor is 1/4, the area will be (1/4)^2 = 1/16 of the original area.

Therefore, polygon H's area is 1/16 or 0.0625 (as a decimal) of polygon G's area.

20. polygon H is scaled copy of polygon G using the scaled factor of 1/4. Polygon H area is what factor of polygon G area?​


Answer:

1/16 of the area of polygon G

Step-by-step explanation:

When a polygon is scaled down by a factor of $k$, its area becomes $k^2$ times smaller than the original polygon.

Since polygon H is a scaled copy of polygon G using a scale factor of 1/4, its area would be $\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{16}$ times the area of polygon G.

Therefore, the area of polygon H is 1/16 of the area of polygon G, or we can also say that the area of polygon G is 16 times larger than the area of polygon H.
