Jerry used 2 Bicycle Wheels for Manuel and Manuel used 3 of the upgraded car wheels.

Explanation:

Jerry had 6 Bicycle Wheels.

He upgraded 4 of them to car wheels, so he has 2 Bicycle Wheels and 4 car wheels.

Jerry used 3 of the car wheels to fix the wheels of his car, so he has 1 Bicycle Wheel and 1 car wheel left.

Jerry gave 1 Bicycle Wheel to Manuel, who already had 7 Bicycle Wheels. So, Manuel has 8 Bicycle Wheels now.

Manuel sold 4 of his Bicycle Wheels, so he has 4 Bicycle Wheels left.

Manuel upgraded the rest to car wheels, which means he upgraded 4 - 4 = 0 Bicycle Wheels to car wheels.

Therefore, Jerry used 2 Bicycle Wheels for Manuel and Manuel used 3 of the upgraded car wheels (the underlined digits are 2 and 3, respectively).

11. The Diameter of a bicycle wheel is 65 cm. How does the bicycle travel when the wheel turns one revolution ​


Answer:

When the bicycle wheel turns one full revolution, it covers a distance equal to the circumference of the wheel.

The circumference of a circle is given by the formula:

Circumference = 2 * pi * radius

where pi (π) is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 3.14, and the radius is half the diameter of the circle.

So, in this case, the circumference of the bicycle wheel can be calculated as:

Circumference = 2 * pi * (diameter/2)

= pi * diameter

= 3.14 * 65 cm

= 204.1 cm (rounded to one decimal place)

Therefore, when the bicycle wheel turns one full revolution, it covers a distance of 204.1 cm or approximately 2.04 meters (rounded to two decimal places).
