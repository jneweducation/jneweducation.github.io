Answer:

Signed electronically, printed on paper, or saved as PDF files.

Explanation:

Portable Document Format (PDF) is a standardized format developed by Adobe in 1992. It was created to allow presentation of documents as well as the document' text formatting ang its included images.

By using Adobe Reader, users can have PDF files signed electronically. This is done through the use of Adobe Reader's Fill and Sign feature, which allows users to fill and complete forms or send the forms to other users to be filled and completed. Users can then add their initials, text such as full names and dates, or attach a photo of their signature to electronically sign the document.

PDF files can also be printed on paper or stored in the computer or in the cloud as a .pdf file.
