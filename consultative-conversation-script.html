Answer:

Language register is the level of formality with which you speak. Different situations and people call for different registers. This is a concept I was talking about recently with some of my graduate students who dislike the idea of writing with a formal tone. They were commenting that they preferred to write in a conversational tone. Some of the biggest problems that I see with massage therapists have to do with knowing what to say to clients and potential clients, and knowing when and how to say it.

I often get asked questions such as, “I want to go out and market myself, but what should I say?”; or “How can I ask clients to reschedule without feeling awkward?”

Creating and using scripts can help with this sort of situation—and a lot more. Things like: exactly what you should say to rebook that client; how to explain the benefits of a retail product; and how to ask for a sale. Scripts can help you to figure out what to say, remember to say it, and not leave out any important info.

Scripts are a great business-building tool to help with communication with clients—until that communication becomes second nature. This does not mean we want to sound like massage robots. 12. Informal feedback conversation/script for 4 people


Informal feedback conversation/script for 4 people

Here's a possible script for an informal feedback conversation among four people:

Participant 1: Hi everyone, thanks for joining me for this feedback conversation. I wanted to start by saying that I really appreciate all of you and the work we've been doing together.

Participant 2: That's great to hear! I feel the same way.

Participant 3: Yeah, I agree. It's been a pleasure working with all of you.

Participant 4: Absolutely. I think we've accomplished a lot together so far.

Participant 1: That's awesome to hear. Now, I wanted to start by asking each of you for some feedback on how I've been doing in my role. Is there anything I could improve on or do differently?

Participant 2: Honestly, I think you've been doing a great job so far. You're always very organized and detail-oriented, which makes it easy to work with you.

Participant 3: I agree with that. The only thing I could suggest is maybe trying to be a bit more assertive in meetings. I feel like sometimes you let others dominate the conversation.

Participant 4: Yeah, I've noticed that too. But overall, I think you've been doing really well.

Participant 1: Thank you for that feedback. I appreciate it. Now, I wanted to turn the conversation over to each of you. Is there anything you'd like to ask for feedback on, or anything you'd like to discuss?

Participant 2: Actually, I was hoping to get some feedback on a project I've been working on. Do you guys think I'm on the right track with it?

Participant 3: Yeah, I've seen some of your work on that project and I think you're doing great.

Participant 4: Same here. I think it's coming along really well.

Participant 1: That's great to hear. Is there anything specific you'd like feedback on, or any areas where you're feeling stuck?

Participant 2: Actually, now that you mention it, there is one area where I could use some input.

And so on. The conversation could continue like this, with each person taking turns asking for feedback and offering input on others' work. The goal is to create a supportive and constructive environment where everyone feels comfortable giving and receiving feedback.

22. Teacher and student conversation, client and doctor consultation, employer and employee conversation


Teacher and student conversation:

Student: Good morning teacher!

Teacher: Good morning! How are you today?

Student: I am doing fine! Also, may I ask you a question?

Teacher: Yes, you may.

Student: I can't understand what this topic is. Everything written here is just too difficult to understand!

Teacher: Don't worry! I will explain everything about this topic.

Student: Thank you so much!

Teacher: You're welcome.

Client and doctor consultation:

Client: Doctor, I have had a stomach ache since yesterday.

Doctor: What did you eat yesterday?

Client: Some street food.

Doctor: You may have eaten contaminated food.

Client: What should I do?

Doctor: Drink plenty of water every day. I will also prescribe you some medicine to control diarrhea.

Client: Thank you, doctor.

Employer and employee conversation:

Employee: Good day, sir. May I speak with you?

Employer: Yes, you may.

Employee: I want to talk to you about something.

Employer: Okay, please take a seat. Now, what would you like to talk about?

Employee: I have been working here for the past 5 years and have been loyal to this company ever since, so may I get my salary raised?

Employer: If this is the matter, how much?

Employee: Php 30,550 to Php 45,000 is good for me.

Employer: If that's the case then I will accept. I will add a few responsibilities for your salary raise, is that alright?

Employee: Yes, sir! Thank you very much!

24. Informal feedback conversation script example


Sure, here's an example script for an informal feedback conversation:

Employee: Hi, boss. Thanks for taking the time to meet with me today.

Manager: Of course, what's on your mind?

Employee: I just wanted to get some feedback from you on my performance over the past few months. Do you have any thoughts or suggestions?

Manager: Sure, I'm happy to give you some feedback. Overall, I think you've been doing a great job. You've been meeting your goals and your work has been of high quality.

Employee: That's good to hear. Is there anything specific you think I could improve on?

Manager: Well, one thing I've noticed is that you sometimes struggle to prioritize your tasks effectively. You may want to work on organizing your to-do list and figuring out which tasks are most important to complete first.

Employee: Okay, I appreciate that feedback. I'll definitely work on that. Is there anything else you wanted to mention?

Manager: No, that's really the only thing that comes to mind. But if you have any other questions or concerns, please don't hesitate to reach out to me.

Employee: Sounds good, thanks again for the feedback.
