Answer:

P140,000

Let's identify the given first and give substitution on variables for simplicity.

B = Beginning Inventory  

N = Net Cost of Purchase

E = Ending Inventory

S = Cost of Goods Sold

The Values of our variables are this:

B = P50,000

N = P125,000

E = P35,000

S = ?

Our problem is to find "S", the Cost of Goods Sold.

S = (B+N) - E

Subtitute the Values

S = (P50,000 + P125,000) - P35,000

Add first the values inside the clause.

S = P175,000 - P35, 000

Proceed on Subtracting

S = P140,000

So the total value of Cost of Goods that were sold is P140,000.

25. Based on the following information:Net income for period P18,000.; Drawing, P56,000. Capital (January 31, 2021), P20,000. Find the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020.​


Answer:

To find the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020, you can use the following formula:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = Capital (January 31, 2021) + Net Income - Drawing

Plugging in the given values, we get:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = P20,000 + P18,000 - P56,000

Simplifying, we get:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = P20,000 + P18,000 - P56,000 = P-18,000

So the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020 is P-18,000.

This means that the owner has a negative net worth of P18,000 on December 31, 2020.
