Write the rate law expected for this mechanism. What is the overall balanced equation for the reaction? What are the intermediates in the proposed mechanism?

Rate-Determining Step and Intermediates:

The rate determining step for any reaction mechanism is the slowest step of the proposed mechanism. The intermediates formed in the different steps are species in the mechanism that do not appear in the overall reaction equation and must be substituted with species that do show in the final overall reaction equation.

Answer:

The empirical formula for C8H16O2 is C4H8O2, which is a ratio of the relative numbers of atoms of each element present in a compound. The empirical formula is the simplest whole-number ratio of atoms of each element present in a molecule of the compound. To determine the empirical formula, we must first calculate the molar masses of each element. The molar mass of C is 12.01 g/mol, the molar mass of H is 1.008 g/mol, and the molar mass of O is 16.00 g/mol.

We can now calculate the molar mass of C8H16O2 as 8 x 12.01 + 16 x 1.008 + 2 x 16.00, which is 192.32 g/mol.

Next, we must divide this molar mass by the molar mass of the empirical formula to determine the number of empirical formula units per molecule. The molar mass of the empirical formula C4H8O2 is 88.11 g/mol.

So, we divide 192.32 g/mol by 88.11 g/mol to get 2.17, which is the number of empirical formula units per molecule of C8H16O2.

Therefore, the empirical formula for C8H16O2 is C4H8O2


8. Does the compound below undergo saponification reaction? Why? C4H7O2


Answer:

The compound in question is an ester (R-C=O-O-R'), a reactant in the saponification process. The saponification reaction in layman's terms is the "soap-making process". The general process of saponification is:

ester (R-C=O-O-R') + water (H₂O) + sodium hydroxide (NaOH)  ⇒ carboxylate (R-C=O-O⁻) + alcohol (OH-R')

As we can see, the compound has a hydroxide (OH⁻) atom attached to it. We can say that a compound undergoes a saponification process if it does not have a hydroxide atom attached to it.

16. how many moles are there in 25g of C5H8O2​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of C₅H₈O₂.

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 5) + (1.008 g/mol × 8) + (16.00 g/mol × 2)

molar mass = 100.114 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of C₅H₈O₂.

[tex]\text{moles of C₅H₈O₂ = 25 g C₅H₈O₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₅H₈O₂}}{\text{100.114 g C₅H₈O₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of C₅H₈O₂ = 0.25 mol}}[/tex]
