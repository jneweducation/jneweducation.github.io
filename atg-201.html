15. Thallium-201 has a half-life of 73.0 hr. If 4.00 mg of thallium-201 disintegrates over a period of 6.00 days and 2.00 hr, how many milligrams of thallium-201 will remain?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & t = \text{6.00 days and 2.00 hr = 146 hr} \\ & m_0 = \text{4.00 mg} \\ & t_{\frac{1}{2}} = \text{73.0 hr} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the rate constant.

[tex]\begin{aligned} k & = \frac{\text{ln 2}}{t_{\frac{1}{2}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{ln 2}}{\text{73.0 hr}} \\ & = \text{0.0094952 /hr} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the remaining mass of the sample.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = m_{0}e^{-kt} \\ & = (\text{4.00 mg})[e^{-(\text{0.0094952 /hr})(\text{146 hr})}] \\ & = \boxed{\text{1.00 mg}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 1.00 mg of thallium-201 will remain.
