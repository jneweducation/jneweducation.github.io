5. A 10 kn car is to be lifted by a hydraulic jack. if 625 n force is applied in a 25-mm diameter plunger, find the required diameter of the piston in mm.


Hydraulic Pascal's Law Problem

The answer is 126 mm.

A mechanical process known as hydraulic uses the force of liquid pressure to operate. In hydraulics-based systems, confined, pumped liquid moves pistons, often through hydraulic cylinders, to create mechanical movement.

Formula:

F1 / A1 = F2 / A2

Where,

• F1 is the force applied to the plunger

• A1 is the area of the plunger

• F2 is the force exerted by the piston

•[tex]A2 = 625 N * (π * (0.025 m)^2) / 10 kN= 0.0039 m^2[/tex] A2 is the area of the piston

We can rearrange the formula to solve for the area of the piston:

A2 = F1 * A2 / F2

Solution:

Substituting the given values:

[tex]A2 = 625 N * (\pi * (0.025 m)^2) / 10 kN= 0.0039 m^2[/tex]

The area of the piston is 0.0039 square meters. To find the diameter of the piston in millimeters, we can convert the area to diameter using the formula for the area of a circle:

[tex]A=\pi * r^{2}[/tex]

Solving for the radius r:

√[tex](A/\pi )[/tex]

Substituting the values:

r[tex]=[/tex]√[tex](0.0039 m^2 / \pi )[/tex]

[tex]= 0.063 m[/tex]

The radius of the piston is 0.063 meters. To find the diameter, we can multiply the radius by 2, which gives us a diameter of:

[tex]diameter = 2 * 0.063 m\\= 0.126 m[/tex]

Converting to millimeters:

diameter = 0.126 m * 1000 mm/m

= 126 mm

16. what would enable you to hear the sound of the jack hammer shown in the picture or the humming of the bird​


Answer:

To hear the sound of the jackhammer shown in the picture or the humming of a bird, you would need to have a functioning auditory system, which includes the ears, auditory nerve, and brain.

When sound waves enter the ear, they cause the eardrum to vibrate, which in turn causes tiny bones in the middle ear to vibrate as well. These vibrations are then transmitted to the inner ear, where they stimulate hair cells that convert the mechanical vibrations into electrical signals. The electrical signals are then sent through the auditory nerve to the brain, where they are processed and interpreted as sound.

Therefore, having a healthy auditory system is necessary to perceive sound, whether it's the sound of a jackhammer or the humming of a bird.
