13. An object is kept at a distance of 150 mm from a concave mirror with a radius of curvature of 600 mm. Find the image distance.a. -150 mmb. 150 mmc. -300 mmd. 300 mm


Subject Science

An object is kept at a distance of 150 mm from a concave mirror with a radius of curvature of 600 mm. Find the image distance.

a. -150 mm

b. 150 mm

c. -300 mm

d. 300 mm

Answer

The correct answer among the choices is letter c . Image distance of the convex mirror is negative. It means that the image is behind the mirror, so the image is virtual and upright.. The mirror equation is the solution to solve the problem. It expresses the quantitative relationship between the object distance (p), the image distance (q), and the focal length (f). The equation is stated as follows:

                   

1/f = 1/p + 1/ q

f means focal lengthp means object distance q means image distance

Problem GivenR ( radius of curvature) = 600mmp = 150mm

Missing to solve - f and q

Solution

First, know the equation for the solution of focal point. f is important figure in the mirror equation.

f = radius of curvature(R)/2

Therefore,

f = 600/2

f = 300

To solve the problem, substitute the given figure to the mirror equation in order to solve the image distance. Therefore,

1/f = 1/p + 1/ q

1/ 300mm = 1/150mm + 1/ q

1/q = 1/300 mm - 1/150 mm

1/q = 150mm- 300 mm/ (300mm)(150mm)

1/q = -150 mm/45,000 mm

q =45,000 mm/ -150mm

q = -300 mm

30. An object is kept at a distance of 150 mm from a concave mirror with a radius of curvature of 600 mm. Find the image distance. a. -150 mm b. 150 mm c. -300 mm d. 300 mm


[tex]\Large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

Problem: An object is kept at a distance of 150 mm from a concave mirror with a radius of curvature of 600 mm. Whats the image distance?

Given values:

p = 150 mmR = 600 mm

By finding the image distance, we must find first the focal length using the formula below:

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \rm \: \: \: f = \dfrac{R}{2} \: \: \: }[/tex]

where:

f is the focal lengthR is the radius of curvature

Substitute the given values into the above formula and solve for f:

[tex]\sf:\implies{f = \dfrac{R}{2} = \dfrac{600 \:mm}{2} = \pmb{300 \: mm}}[/tex]

Therefore, the focal length is 300 mm.

Now, we haved find the focal length we can now find the image distance so let us proceed.

To find the image distance, apply the mirror equation:

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \rm \: \: \: \dfrac{1}{f} = \dfrac{1}{p} + \dfrac{1}{q} \: \: \: }[/tex]

where:

f is the focal lengthp is the distance of objectq is the distance of image

Substitute the given value and found value into the mirror equation and solve for q:

[tex]\sf: \implies \: \dfrac{1}{f} =\dfrac{1}{p} +\dfrac{1}{q}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies \: \dfrac{1}{300 \: mm} =\dfrac{1}{150 \: mm} +\dfrac{1}{q}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies \: \dfrac{1}{300 \: mm} - \dfrac{1}{150 \: mm} = \dfrac{1}{q}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies \: \dfrac{150 \: mm - 300 \: mm}{(300 \: mm)(150 \: mm)} = \dfrac{1}{q}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies \: \dfrac{ - 150 \: mm}{45000 \: mm^{2} } = \dfrac{1}{q}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies \: \dfrac{ \cancel{ - 150 \: mm}(q)}{ \cancel{ - 150 \: mm}} = \dfrac{45000 \: mm^{ \cancel{2}} }{ -150 \: \cancel{mm}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf: \implies{q = - 300 \: mm} \\[/tex]

Therefore, the image distance is -300 mm (negative).
