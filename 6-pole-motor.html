15. A 3-0.6 pole 50 Hz induction motor is running at 5% slip. What is the speed of motor? a. 950 rpm b. 850 rpm c. 900 rpm d. 1000 rpm​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The rotor speed of a motor is

[tex]\boxed{N_{r} = \frac{120f}{P}(1 - s)}[/tex]

where:

Nr = rotor speed

f = frequency

P = number of poles

s = slip

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Listing the given values

f = 50 Hz

P = 6

s = 5% = 0.05

Solving for Nr

[tex]N_{r} = \dfrac{120f}{P}(1 - s)[/tex]

[tex]N_{r} = \dfrac{120(50)}{6}(1 - 0.05)[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{N_{r} = \text{950 rpm}}[/tex]

Therefore, the rotor speed of the motor is 950 rpm

Answer:

a. 950 rpm

29. A 6 -m bamboo pole is leaning against a coconut tree. The foot of the pole is 3 m from the base of the tree. The angle does the pole make with the tree if the pole reaches up to the top of the tree?


Answer:

The angle the pole make with the tree is 30°.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Length of bamboo pole:  6 m

Distance between the foot of the pole and the base of the tree:  3 m

Angle the pole make with the tree: Unknown

Where:

hypotenuse ⇒ bamboo poleopposite of the angle ⇒ distance between pole foot and tree baseAngle ⇒ Sin θ

Trigo function:

Sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse

Sin θ = (3 m) / (6 m)

Sin θ = 0.5

θ = arcsin (0.5)

Sin⁻¹ = 0.5

θ = 30°

The angle the pole make with the tree is 30°.

30. 18. A 9-meter pole is driven into the ground so that 5/6 of the pole is above the ground. How long is the part of the pole driven into the ground?​


Answer:

The length of the part of the pole driven into the ground can be found by subtracting the length of the part above the ground from the total length of the pole.

Since 5/6 of the pole is above the ground, then 1/6 of the pole is below the ground.

The length below the ground is 9 meters x (1/6) = 9/6 = 1.5 meters.
