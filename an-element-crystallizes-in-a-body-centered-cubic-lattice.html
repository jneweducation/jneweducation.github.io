1. An element crystallizes in a body-centered cubic lattice. The edge of the unit cell is 0.286 nm, and the density of the crystal is 7.92 g/cm3 . Calculate the atomic weight of the element.


Unit cell

A crystalline solid consists of repeating patterns (a crystal lattice) called a unit cell. There are seven kinds of unit cells, which differ in the lengths of the edges and the angles between them for example tetragonal, hexagonal, orthorhombic, triclinic, monoclinic, rhombohedral, and cubic. Each unit cell has six sides of a parallelogram. In the cubic unit cells, all sides have the same length and all angles are 90°. From the formula, the atomic weight of the element is 55.796 ≈ 55.8 g/mol.

Further explanation

There are 3 main structures of the unit cell:  

1. Body-Centered Cubic (BCC),  

2. Face Centered Cubic (FCC).  

3. Hexagonal Close Packed (HCP).  

If the cubic unit cell consists of 8 atoms, molecules, or ions located at the corners of the cube and also contains an identical component in the center of the cube, then it is BCC.

For all unit cells (except hexagonal), atoms on the faces contribute 1/2 atom to each unit cell, atoms on the edges contribute 1/4 atom, and atoms on the corners contribute 1/8 atom.

For example, metallic iron has a BCC unit cell, so there are 2 Fe atoms in each unit cell ( 8 x 1/8 the corners of the cube + 1 atom in the centre).

The density of a unit cell can be formulated:

[tex]\tt d=\dfrac{Z\times M}{a^3\times No}[/tex]

Z = atom per unit cell (2 for BCC lattice)

M = atomic weight

a = length of edge

No = Avogadro’s = 6.023 x 10²³

Given

The edge of the unit cell is 0.286 nm (a) = 2.86 x 10⁻⁸ m

density (d) = 7.92 g/cm³

Required

the atomic weight of the element.

Solution

[tex]\tt M=\dfrac{d\times a^3\times No}{Z}\\\\M=\dfrac{7.92\times (2.86\times 10^{-8})^3\times 6.023\times 10^{23}}{2}\\\\M=\boxed{\bold{55.796}}[/tex]

6. Copper crystallizes in a face-centered cubic unit cell. The density of copper is 8.94 g/cm3. Calculate the length of the edge of the unit in pm.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the volume of the unit cell by using dimensional analysis

In a face-centered cubic crystal structure, there are 4 atoms in a unit cell. The molar mass of copper is 63.546 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V & = \text{4 atoms} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{6.0221 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}} \times \frac{\text{63.546 g}}{\text{1 mol}} \times \frac{\text{1 cm}^3}{\text{8.94 g}} \\ & = 4.7214 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the edge length of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = \sqrt[3]{V} \\ & = \sqrt[3]{4.7214 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3} \\ & = 3.61 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \\ & = 3.61 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \times \frac{\text{1 m}}{\text{100 cm}} \times \frac{\text{1 pm}}{1 \times 10^{-12} \: \text{m}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{361 pm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the length of the edge of the unit cell is 361 pm.

11. The substance rubidium is found to crystallize in a cubic lattice, with an edge length of 562.0 pm. if the density of solid rubidium is 1.599 g/cm3, how many rb atoms are there per unit cell


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Convert the given edge length from picometer (pm) to centimeter (cm).

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = \text{562.0 pm} \times \frac{1 \times 10^{-12} \: \text{m}}{\text{1 pm}} \times \frac{\text{100 cm}}{\text{1 m}} \\ & = 5.620 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V & = a^3 \\ & = (5.620 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm})^3 \\ & = 1.775 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of atoms within a unit cell by using dimensional analysis.

The molar mass of rubidium is 85.468 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = 1.775 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{cm}^3 \times \frac{\text{1.599 g}}{\text{1 cm}^3} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{85.468 g}} \times \frac{6.0221 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}}{\text{1 mol}} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{2 atoms}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 2 rubidium atoms are there per unit cell.

16. Calculate the number of unit cells in 0.5 cm3 of iron solid sample if iron has an atomic diameter of 252 pm and crystallizes with a body-centered cubic structure.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Determine the atomic radius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} r & = \frac{d}{2} \\ & = \frac{\text{252 pm}}{2} \\ & = \text{126 pm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the edge length of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}r \\ & = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}(\text{126 pm}) \\ & = \text{290.98 pm} \\ & = \text{290.98 pm} \times \frac{1 \times 10^{-12} \: \text{m}}{\text{1 pm}} \times \frac{\text{100 cm}}{\text{1 m}} \\ & = 2.9098 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the volume of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{unit cell}} & = a^3 \\ & = (2.9098 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm})^3 \\ & = 2.4637 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the number of unit cells.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{no. of unit cells} & = \frac{V_{\text{sample}}}{V_{\text{unit cell}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.5 cm}^3}{2.4637 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3} \\ & = \boxed{2.03 \times 10^{22} \: \text{unit cells}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 2.03 × 10²² unit cells in 0.5 cm³ of solid iron sample.

23. Metal Y crystallizes in a body-centered cubic structure with a density of 10.28 g⁄cm3. Calculate the radius (in pm) of one atom of Metal Y if the molar mass is 95.96 g⁄mol?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the volume of the unit cell by using dimensional analysis

In a body-centered cubic crystal structure, there are 2 atoms in a unit cell

[tex]\begin{aligned} V & = \text{2 atoms} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{6.0221 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}} \times \frac{\text{95.96 g}}{\text{1 mol}} \times \frac{\text{1 cm}^3}{\text{10.28 g}} \\ & = 3.10012 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the edge length of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = \sqrt[3]{V} \\ & = \sqrt[3]{3.10012 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3} \\ & = 3.14142 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the atomic radius of Metal Y.

[tex]\begin{aligned} r & = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}a \\ & = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(3.14142 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm}) \\ & = 1.36 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \\ & = 1.36 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \times \frac{\text{1 m}}{\text{100 cm}} \times \frac{\text{1 pm}}{1 \times 10^{-12} \: \text{m}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{136 pm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the atomic radius of Metal Y is 136 pm.


24. Sodium crystallizes in the body-centered cubic structure with a=0.424 nm. Calculate the theoretical density of Na in kg/m3 ​


The theoretical density of Na : 1.002 x 10⁹ kg/m³

Further explanation

There are 3 forms of cubic crystal:

the simple/primitive cubic body-centered cubic, and the face-centered cubic

In a body-centered cubic, there are 2 atoms in each unit cell,

1 atom at the center of the unit cell 1/8 atom on 8 corners of the cube = 1 atom

Examples of metals that have a BCC crystal structure are Fe, Cr, and Mo

The density of the crystal structure can be formulated:

[tex]\tt \rho=\dfrac{Z\times M}{a^3\times N}[/tex]

ρ = density

Z = number of atoms

M = atomic mass

N = Avogadro's number

For Sodium (Na) :

M = 22,989 g/mol

a = 0.424 nm = 4.24 x 10⁻¹⁰ m

Then the density :

[tex]\tt \rho=\dfrac{2\times 22.989}{(4.24\times 10^{-10})^3\times 6.02\times 10^{23}}\\\\\rho=1.002\times 10^6~g/cm^3=\boxed{\bold{1.002\times 10^9~kg/m^3}}[/tex]

25. Solve each of the following problems.1) Chromium with an atomic mass of 51.996 g/mol crystallizes with a body-centered cubic unit cell.The radius of a chromium atom is 128 pm. Calculate the density of solid crystalline chromium ingrams per cubic centimeter.​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the edge length of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \times r \\ & = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \times \text{128 pm} \\ & = \text{295.6 pm} \\ & = \text{295.6 pm} \times \frac{1 \times 10^{-12} \: \text{m}}{\text{1 pm}} \times \frac{\text{100 cm}}{\text{1 m}} \\ & = 2.956 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V & = a^3 \\ & = (2.956 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm})^3 \\ & = 2.5829 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass the unit cell (m).

Note that in a body-centered cubic crystal structure, there are 2 atoms in a unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \text{2 atoms} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{6.0221 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}} \times \frac{\text{51.996 g}}{\text{1 mol}} \\ & = 1.7268 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the density of rhodium.

[tex]\begin{aligned} d & = \frac{m}{V} \\ & = \frac{1.7268 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{g}}{2.5829 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{6.69 g/cm}^3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the density of chromium is 6.69 g/cm³.

27. When silver crystallizes, it forms face-centered cubic cells. The unit cell edge length is 4.087 Å. Calculate the density of silver.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Convert the given edge length from angstrom unit (Å) to centimeter (cm).

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = 4.087 \: \AA \times \frac{1 \times 10^{-10} \: \text{m}}{1 \: \AA} \times \frac{\text{100 cm}}{\text{1 m}} \\ & = 4.087 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V & = a^3 \\ & = (4.087 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm})^3 \\ & = 6.8267 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass the unit cell.

In a face-centered cubic crystal structure, there are 4 atoms in a unit cell.

The molar mass of silver is 107.87 g/mol

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \text{4 atoms} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{6.0221 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}} \times \frac{\text{107.87 g}}{\text{1 mol}} \\ & = 7.1649 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the density of silver.

[tex]\begin{aligned} d & = \frac{m}{V} \\ & = \frac{7.1649 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{g}}{6.8267 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{10.50 g/cm}^3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the density of silver is 10.50 g/cm³.
