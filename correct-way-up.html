Four Basic Ways to Avoid Communication Barriers

There are a lot of ways to avoid communication barriers. Basically, here are some ways to avoid communication barriers.

Talk slowly and clearly

Make sure to properly enunciate words and if there is a need to slow down your speech then do it, so that you can be more understandable. If there would be a time pressure do not ever rush through your communication. It can somehow take more time but being understood and avoid miscommunication is much better to avoid any confusion on either the speaker or listener.

Learn to ask for clarification from your speaker or your listener

If you yourself are having difficulty understanding what the speaker is telling you, you may politely ask for clarification. It is better to ask than to assume that you have understood what has been said. This would indicate that you and the speaker would be on the same pace and understanding of the topic.

Always check for understanding

The proper use of open-ended questions to check other people’s understanding will definitely help. You may also practice talking in front of a mirror and be reflective listening for you to check your own understanding of what you would want to share or say. Avoid asking “Is that clear?” instead ask “What would be your understanding about the process that I have just explained”.

Avoid using idioms

Most of the idioms are culture specific. If you are talking to a big crowd or a group of people that you are not sure of their culture, there would be high chances that they will not understand your point. It will be more difficult to understand and will make the communication poor.

Be mindful of using jargons

Make sure to analyze the type of people that you will be talking to. There would be a need for you to state your speech in a layman’s term if you do not know who your listeners are. For an example, a doctor. Not everyone can understand medical terms. Terms that you often use in the hospital and only people with medical background can get it.  

Be a good listener and avoid interrupting if someone is talking

Do not just interrupt a speaker if he or she is talking. It would distract his or her concentration on the topic being discussed. If there is a need to but in, make sure to do it in a politely manner or wait until the speaker has finished his or her speech.  

Pay attention to non-verbal cues and keep emotions.  

Non-verbal cues would be eye contact, tone of voice or body movements. By paying closer attention to non-verbal cues can enhance your ability to communicate nonverbally.

Assert yourself

Being able to express positively or assert yourself would result to effective communication. It is a key to build good conversation and good working relationship. Assert yourself is by engaging and interactive learning experience can help us to develop our skills and confidence.

The answer to the question would be: D - Both Practice active listening, Keep emotions, Pay attention to non-verbal cues and Assert yourself are correct.
