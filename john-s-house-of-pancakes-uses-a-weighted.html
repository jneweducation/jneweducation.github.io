22. what technique is used in making pancake​


Answer:

using pan

Explanation:

non-stick pan para maperfect ung pancake sana tama not sure

Answer:

Follow these tips, and you'll know how to make perfect pancakes every time.

Dos for Making Perfect Pancakes

-Even though it's a pain, it's important to mix the dry ingredients and the wet ingredients in separate bowls before combining them.

-A griddle with temperature control is the best tool for cooking pancakes. We like to use an electric skillet because as Alton Brown says, it's not a uni-tasker (one-trick pony kitchen tool). Set the temperature to 375 F when preheating, then turn it down to 370 or 360 F once you start to cook the pancakes.

-If you don't have an electric skillet or griddle, use a heavy cast iron or nonstick skillet over medium heat.

-Let the batter rest 5 to 15 minutes before cooking.

-For really light and fluffy pancakes, separate the eggs. Add the egg yolk(s) where the recipe calls for eggs to be added in. Beat the egg white(s) separately, and fold in at the very end.

-Use a 1/4 cup measuring cup to make nice-sized pancakes that are consistent.

-Flip at the right time: Look for bubbles on top and light browning around the edges.

-It's okay to peek to see if they are browned on the bottom. If the pancakes are too floppy to flip, they aren't cooked enough. If they are getting brown before they're ready to flip, turn down the heat.

-Try making one test pancake first to see if the griddle or skillet is the right temperature. Invariably, the first pancake ends up being the worst one anyway.

Don'ts for Making Perfect Pancakes

-Don't overmix the batter. This will cause the pancakes to turn out tough.

-Don't let the griddle or skillet get too hot! This will cause the pancakes to burn before they're cooked through.

-Don't add the butter or shortening until after the griddle or skillet has been preheated.

-Don't go overboard with butter or shortening. One tablespoon is enough for 12 to 16 pancakes, or leave it out entirely.

-Don't press down on the pancakes while they're cooking.

-Don't overflip. If you let the pancakes cook until golden on the first side, there's no need to flip multiple times.

29. PROBLEM#1. JOHNNY COMBINED 7/4 CUPS OF PANCAKE MIX AND 3/4 CUP OF WATER TO MAKE PANCAKE BATTER. IF HE USES 1/4 OF A CUP OF BATTER TO MAKE EACH PANCAKE HOW MANY PANCAKES CAN JOHNNY MAKE FROM HIS BATTER


10 pancakes

Johnny can make 10 pancakes from his batter.

Step-by-step explanation:

Two-Step Word Problem

The problem is asking for the number of pancakes that Johnny can make from his batter. There are two operations involve in finding the answer to the problem. They are addition and division.

Find the total cups of batter that Johnny have by adding the cups of pancake mix and water.Find the number of pancakes that he can make by dividing the total cups of batter by a cup of batter for each pancake.

Given:

cups of pancake mix - ‌7⁄4‌

cup of water - ¾

Solution:

Step 1: Add

Since the fractions are similar, just add the numerators and copy the denominator.

‌7⁄4‌ + ¾ = ‌10⁄4‌

Step 2: Divide

To divide, change the sign to multiplication and reciprocate the second fraction.

10⁄4‌ ÷ 1⁄4‌

= 10⁄4‌ × 4⁄1

= 40⁄4‌ or 10

Final Answer:

10 pancakes

30. You know that to make 20 pancakes you have to use 2 eggs. How many eggs are needed to make 100 pancakes?


Answer:

10 eggs

Step-by-step explanation:

You know that to make 20 pancakes you have to use 2 eggs. How many eggs are needed to make 100 pancakes?

Let x represents the number of eggs needed to make 100 pancakes.

[tex] \bm{20:2=100:x} \\ \\ \\ \bm{ \frac{20}{2} = \frac{100}{x} } \\ \\ \\ \bm{(20)(x) = (100)(2)} \\ \\ \\ \bm{20x = 200} \\ \\ \\ \\ \bm{ \frac{20x}{20} = \frac{200}{20} } \\ \\ \\ \bm{x = 10}[/tex]

__________

To make 100 pancakes, 10 eggs are needed.
