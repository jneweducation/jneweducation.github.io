17. Your friend is looking for the best spot for his mobile data.He walks 5 north,2 meters east,5 meters south,then lasty,2 meters west.determine your friend's total distance and placement​


DISTANCE & DISPLACEMENT

Problem:

Your friend is looking for the best spot for his mobile data. He walks 5 meters north, 2 meters east, 5 meters south, then lastly, 2 meters west. Determine your friend's total distance and displacement​.

Answer:

His total distance traveled is 14 meters and his displacement is equal to zero.

Explanation:

When we talk about total distance traveled, it refers to the summation of all the distances that a certain body had traveled. In this problem, in order to obtain it, just simply add all the given distances that my friend had walked.

So: 5 meters north plus 2 meters south is 7 meters. 7 meters plus 5 meters south is 12 meters. 12 meters plus 2 meters west is 14 meters.

In a simple equation: 5 + 2 + 5 + 2 = 14 meters

Therefore, my friend walked a total of 14 meters.

On the other hand, displacement refers to the distance of the body from the reference point to his current position. The distance traveled may be equal to displacement but not all the time. Sometimes, displacement can also be equal to zero when the current position of the body is also at the reference point.

In this problem, if we trace the path of my friend, it turns out that he will still end up on the reference point. Therefore, his displacement is zero.
