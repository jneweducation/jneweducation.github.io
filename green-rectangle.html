2. A big rectangle has 9 rows of smaller rectangle with 9 squares in each row. One third of the rectangle are colored red, One ninth is colored green, 2/3 of the remainder are blue, and the rest are yellow. How many small rectangles are red? Green ? Blue? And yellow​


Answer:

there are 27 small rectangles that are colored red, 9 small rectangles that are colored green, 36 small rectangles that are colored blue, and 18 small rectangles that are colored yellow.

Step-by-step explanation:

The big rectangle has a total of 9 x 9 = 81 smaller rectangles.

One third of the rectangle are colored red, which is (1/3) x 81 = 27 small rectangles.

One ninth is colored green, which is (1/9) x 81 = 9 small rectangles.

The remainder of the rectangle that is not red is (81 - 27) = 54 small rectangles.

Two-thirds of the remainder are blue, which is (2/3) x 54 = 36 small rectangles.

The remaining small rectangles are yellow, which is (54 - 36) = 18 small rectangles.

Therefore, there are 27 small rectangles that are colored red, 9 small rectangles that are colored green, 36 small rectangles that are colored blue, and 18 small rectangles that are colored yellow.

The big rectangle has a total of 9 x 9 = 81 small rectangles.

One third of the rectangle are colored red, which is:

(1/3) x 81 = 27

So, there are 27 small rectangles that are red.

One ninth is colored green, which is:

(1/9) x 81 = 9

So, there are 9 small rectangles that are green.

The remainder after the red and green rectangles have been counted is:

81 - 27 - 9 = 45

2/3 of the remainder are blue, which is:

(2/3) x 45 = 30

So, there are 30 small rectangles that are blue.

The rest of the rectangles are yellow, which is:

45 - 30 = 15

So, there are 15 small rectangles that are yellow.

Therefore, there are 27 small rectangles that are red, 9 that are green, 30 that are blue, and 15 that are yellow.
