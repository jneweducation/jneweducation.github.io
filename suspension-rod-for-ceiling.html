9. A Piece of iron rod has a volume of 4.6cm^3 if the density of the iron rod is 7.5g/cm^3 what is the Mass in kilogram of that iron rod


Mass divided by Volume gives Density.

[tex]\frac{M}{V} = D[/tex]

so it only follows that

[tex]M = VD[/tex]

Therefore multiply the Volume by Density and you'll get the mass.

4.6 x 7.5 = 34.5g

However, the mass is needed in KILOGRAMS (kg) so just divide it by 1000

And you'll get 

34.5g = 0.034kg

[tex] \large \rm 5 \times 12 = 60 \: inches[/tex]

☞ Then solve for the area using the formula.

[tex]\large\rm A=πr^2[/tex]

[tex]\large\rm A=(3.14)(60 \: in)^2[/tex]

[tex]\large\rm A=(3.14)(3 \: 600 \: in {}^{2} )[/tex]

[tex]\large\rm A=11 \: 304 \: in {}^{2}[/tex]

The area of the ceiling is approximately 11, 304 square inches.

A. Rubbing styrofoam with paper.

Explanation:

When rubbing Styrofoam with a paper, some electrons from the paper get transferred to the Styrofoam. Therefore, the Styrofoam acquires a net negative charge because it gains an excess of negatively charged particles, and the paper acquires a positive charge.
