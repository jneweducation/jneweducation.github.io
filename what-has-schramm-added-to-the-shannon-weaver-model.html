Differences between Shannon-Weaver Model, Schramm Model, and Transaction Modelâ€‹

The Shannon weaver model consists of fundamental components like a source, an encoder, a medium, a decoder, a receiver, and noise. It explains how information travels from a sender to a receiver. A transaction, on the other hand, is a model that describes the sender and the receiver engaging at the same time. The Schramm model is a form of circular communication that can provide both sides the chance to express their thoughts.

Further explanation:1. Linear Communication Models

The one-way interaction of the linear communication model lacks feedback. The transactional model is derived from the linear model, whereas linear is the basic communication paradigm. Without getting a response, the sender, and receiver converse. Additionally, it symbolizes the one-way nature of communication.

Aristotle's, Shannon-Weaver's, Lasswell's, and Berlo's SMCR Model of Communication are just a few examples of the many linear communication models that have been developed by academics.

2. Interactive Communication Models

The two-way approach to communication with feedback is referred to as the interactive communication model. Feedback is gradual and indirect, while it is not simultaneous. In some cases, when recipients do not respond to senders, communication might become linear. Internet-based and mediated communication is indicated by the interactive communication model.

Osgood-Schramm and Westley, as well as Maclean's models, are two examples of interactive communication models.

3. Transactional Communication Models

A two-way communication process with immediate response appears to be the transitional communication paradigm. The foundational element of transitional models of communication is simultaneous feedback. Therefore, without feedback, the communication process won't turn transactional. The response is immediate and quick. The recipient is forced to respond right away. Indirect and direct feedback are the main distinctions between the interactive and transactional approaches.

Transitional communication models include, for instance, Wilbur Schramm's model of communication, Barnlund's transactional model of communication, Dance's helical model of communication, and Eugene White's model of communication.
