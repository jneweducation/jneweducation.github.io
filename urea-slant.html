Urease broth is a differential medium that tests the ability of an organism to produce an exoenzyme, called urease, that hydrolyzes urea to ammonia and carbon dioxide. The broth contains two pH buffers, urea, a very small amount of nutrients for the bacteria, and the pH indicator phenol red. Phenol red turns yellow in an acidic environment and fuchsia in an alkaline environment. If the urea in the broth is degraded and ammonia is produced, an alkaline environment is created, and the media turns pink.

Many enterics can hydrolyze urea; however, only a few can degrade urea rapidly. These are known as “rapid urease-positive” organisms. Members of the genus Proteus are included among these organisms.

Urea broth is formulated to test for rapid urease-positive organisms. The restrictive amount of nutrients coupled with the use of pH buffers prevent all but rapid urease-positive organisms from producing enough ammonia to turn the phenol red pink

12. What is the mole fraction of urea, CO(NH2)2, in a solution prepared by dissolving 4.8g of urea in 30.3g of methanol, CH3OH?


The mole fraction of urea : 0.078

Further explanation

The mole fraction shows the mole ratio of a substance to the mole of solution /mixture

[tex]\rm xa=\dfrac{na}{na+nb}\\\\na+nb=1[/tex]

Urea (CO(NH₂)₂)

molar mass of urea :

molar mass = Ar C+Ar O + 2.Ar N + 4.Ar H

[tex]\rm =12+16+2.14+4.1\\\\= 60~g/mol[/tex]

mol of urea :

[tex]\rm mol~urea=\dfrac{mass}{molar~mass}\\\\mol~urea=\dfrac{4.8}{60}\\\\mol~urea=0.08[/tex]

Methanol, CH₃OH

molar mass of methanol :

molar mass = Ar C+4.Ar H+ Ar O

[tex]\rm molar~mass=12+4.1+16\\\\molar~mass=32~g/mol[/tex]

mol of methanol :

[tex]\rm mol~methanol=\dfrac{mass}{molar~mass}\\\\mol~methanol=\dfrac{30.3}{32}\\\\mol~methanol=0.947[/tex]

mol of solution = mol urea + mol methanol

mol of solution = 0.08 + 0.947

mol of solution = 1.027

then the mole fraction of urea,

[tex]\rm mole~fraction~urea=\dfrac{mol~urea}{mol~solution}\\\\mol~fraction~urea=\dfrac{0.08}{1.027}=\boxed{\bold{0.078}}[/tex]

29. If an aqueous solution of urea n2h4co is 26% by mass and has density of 1.07 g/ml, calculate the molality of urea in the soln


Answer:

The molality of the urea in the solution is 5.85 mol/kg.

Explanation:

Molality

It is defined as  a property of a solution which involves the number of moles of solute to the weight (in kilogram) of solvent. The SI unit for molality is mol of solute/ kg of solvent.

We know that solute is the substance that is to be dissolved in the solvent, while the solvent is the substance that will be used to dissolve the solute.

Given:

Aqueous solution of Urea (N₂H₄CO) = 26% by massDensity of Urea = 1.07 g/mL

Required:

Molality (m) of the urea solution

Solution:

Let us assume 100 g mass of solution. If there is 26% by mass of urea in the solution, hence, the solvent (water) will be 74% by mass.

mass of urea = 0.26 * 100 = 26 grams

mass of water = 0.74 * 100 = 74 grams

Keep in mind the unit of molality; mole of solute per kilogram of solvent.

We now have the mass of solvent, we just need to convert the grams to kilograms.

The only thing to solve now is the number of moles of solute (urea).

Solving the moles of solute, we have:

(26 grams urea) * [tex](\frac{1 mol urea}{60.06 grams urea} )[/tex] = 0.4329 moles urea

Converting grams to kilograms (of solvent):

74 grams * [tex](\frac{1 kg}{1000 grams})[/tex] = 0.074 kg solvent

Solving for the molality (m):

m = [tex]\frac{moles solute}{kg solvent}[/tex]m = [tex]\frac{0.4329 mol urea}{0.074 kg solvent}[/tex]m = 5.85 mol/kg

Therefore, the molality of the urea in the solution is 5.85 mol/kg.
