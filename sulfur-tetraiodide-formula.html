15. A compound contains sulfur, oxygen, and chlorine. Analysis shows that it contains by mass 26.95% sulfur and 59.61% chlorine. What is the simplest formula for this compound?


Answer:

SOCl2

Explanation:

26.95% and 59.61% do not equal 100%, so there must be 13.44% oxygen in the sample

For sulfur, 26.95g × 1 mol/32.07 g = 0.8403 mol

For chlorine, 59.61g × 1 mol/35.45 g = 1.682 mol

For oxygen, 13.44g × 1 mol/16.00 g = 0.8400 mol

The ratio of sulfur to oxygen to chlorine is 1 to 1 to 2. The empirical formula is SOCl2

21. what is the empirical formula of the compound that contain 40.05% sulfur and 59.95% oxygen?​


Answer: SO3 (sulfur trioxide)

Explanation:

Assuming a 100 g sample,

40.05% S = 40.05 g S

59.95% O = 59.95 g O

40.05 g S / (1 mole S / 32.06 g S)

= 1.25 mole S

59.95 g O / (1 mole O / 16.00 g O)

= 3.75 mole O

1.25 mole S / 1.25 = 1 mole S

3.75 mole O/ 1.25 = 3 moles O

E.F. = SO3

25. Determine the empirical formula for a compound that contains 35.98% aluminun and 64.02% sulfur​


Answer:

empirical formula = Al₂S₃

Explanation:

Assuming that the mass of a compound is 100 g

mass Al = 35.98 g

mass S = 64.02 g

Calculating the number of moles of each element

n Al = 35.98 g × (1 mol / 26.9815 g) = 1.33351 mol

n S = 64.02 g × (1 mol / 32.0648 g) = 1.99658 mol

Determining the empirical formula

Representing empirical formula

empirical formula = AlxSy

Dividing the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles

x = 1.33351 mol / 1.33351 mol = 1 × 2 = 2

y = 1.99658 mol / 1.33351 mol = 1.5 × 2 = 3

Writing the empirical formula

empirical formula = Al₂S₃

29. A compound composed of sulfur and flourine is found to contain 25.24% by mass of sulfur. If the molar mass of the compound is 254.11 g/mol, what is the molecular formula?​


Answer:You’ve already got two perfectly good answers, both arrived at by the expected systematic mathematical approach. But really, do we need to go through all that?

molar mass of S = 32.06

molar mass of F = 18.99

And we’re told that the molar mass of the unknown is 254.11.

If there is one atom of S in the molecule,

254.11−32.06/18.99=11.69 atoms of F

This isn’t good because we can’t have fractions of an atom in a molecule. Moving on, if there are two atoms of S in the molecule,

254.11−2×32.06/18.99=10.00 atoms of F

and we are on to something.

You can check a few more cases with 3 or 4 atoms of S per molecule, but you’re going to find none of them give a whole number of F atoms.

QED, the unknown is S2F10 . If you want to back-calculate the percent S by mass to check the answer…

64.12 g S/254.11 g = 25.23% S
