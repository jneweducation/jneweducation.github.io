Answer:

Pita and lavash are both types of flatbreads that are commonly used in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine. Pita is made from a yeast-leavened dough, while lavash is made from an unleavened dough. Pita has a thicker, softer texture with a slightly crispy exterior, while lavash is thinner and crispier. Pita is traditionally cooked in an oven, while lavash is cooked on a griddle or in a tandoor oven. Pita is usually round with a pocket in the center for stuffing, while lavash is rectangular and can be used for wrapping around fillings. Both flatbreads have similar ingredients, uses, and versatility, but the differences in dough, texture, cooking method, and shape make them unique.

Reviewing the Cartesian Coordinate Plane

Imagine a plane without anything on it but point.One will find it difficult to describe the location of the point on the plane. But if marked lines are drawn, the location of the point can be easily described as to whether it is to the left or to the right, or above or below as specific line. The horizontal number line is called x-axis and the vertical line is called the y-axis. These axis , intersecting at a point called origin, form four regions or quadrants.The system established one-to-one correspondence between a point on the plane and an ordered pair of real numbers. That is, for every point on the plane, there corresponds an ordered pair of real numbers , and for every ordered pair of real numbers, there corresponds a point on the plane .The idea of describing a point in the plane was systematized by Rene Descartes, a seventeenth century French mathematician and philosopher, when he invented a system that relates the correspondence between points on a plane to a pair of real numbers. This system is called " the rectangular coordinate system" .________________________
