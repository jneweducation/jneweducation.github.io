Upland and lowland are portions of plain that are conditionally categorized by their elevation above the sea level. Lowlands are usually no higher than 200 m (660 ft), while uplands are somewhere around 200 m (660 ft) to 500 m (1,600 ft). On unusual occasions, certain lowlands such as the Caspian Depression lie below sea level.

Upland habitats are cold, clear and rocky whose rivers are fast-flowing in mountainous areas; lowland habitats are warm with slow-flowing rivers found in relatively flat lowland areas, with water that is frequently colored by sediment and organic matter.

These classifications overlap with the geological definitions of "upland" and "lowland". In geology an "upland" is generally considered to be land that is at a higher elevation than the alluvial plain or stream terrace, which are considered to be "lowlands". The term "bottomland" refers to low-lying alluvial land near a river.

22. Are there similarities in the different musical forms of the lowlands of Luzon?s of the lowlands of Luzon?


Music from the lowlands of Luzon is famous for its bamboo ensembles.

The lowlands of Luzon are also known for their art of music called kundiman. Kundiman came from the Tagalog phrase “Kung hindi man,” which translates to “If not possible,” An example of a kundiman is the song  Mutya ng Pasig by Nicanor Abelardo. The song explores themes that are common in kundiman we can see it just from its lyrics:

“Ang lakas ko ay nalipat

Sa puso’t dibdib ng lahat;

Kung nais ninyong ako'y mabuhay

Pag-ibig ko'y inyong ibigay”

The bamboo ensembles from the lowland of Luzon are called Rondalla and Pangkat Kawayan.

The rondalla orchestra is composed of the bandurria, the laud, the octavina, the guitar, and the bajo de unas. They tend to accompany folk music.

The Pangkat Kawayan is an orchestral group that originated from Quezon City and students from Manila. The group uses bamboo instruments like the Angklung, Bumbong, Kalatok, Talunggating, Kalagong, and many more.
