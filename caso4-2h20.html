5. find the percent composition of the following (with solution):a. CaSO4b. SO2c. H2S​

%S = 23.55%

c) %H = 5.914%

%S = 94.07%

Explanation:

a.CaSO4

Calculating the molar mass of CaSO4

molar mass = (40.08 g × 1) + (32.07 g × 1) + (16.00 g × 4)

molar mass = 136.15 g

Calculating the percent composition of each elements

For Ca

%Ca = [(40.08 g × 1) / 136.15 g] × 100

%Ca = 29.44%

For S

%S = [(32.07 g × 1) / 136.15 g] × 100

%S = 23.55%

For O

%O = [(16.00 g × 4) / 136.15 g] × 100

%O = 47.01%

b. SO2

molar mass = (32.07 g × 1) + (16.00 g × 2)

molar mass = 64.07 g

Calculating the percent composition of each elements

For S

%S = [(32.07 g × 1) / 64.07 g] × 100

%S = 50.05%

For O

%O = [(16.00 g × 2) / 64.07 g] × 100

%O = 49.95%

c. H2S

Calculating the molar mass of H2S

molar mass = (1.008 g × 2) + (32.07 g × 1)

molar mass = 34.09 g

Calculating the percent composition of each elements

For H

%H = [(1.008 g × 2) / 34.09 g] × 100

%H = 5.914%

For S

%S = [(32.07 g × 1) / 34.09 g] × 100

17. A dying agent has the chemical composition CaSO4 Calculate AG at 25 °C, for the following reaction, which has a AS value of -139.7 J/K CaSO4 ---> Ca2 (aq) + SO4 (aq) AH=-18.0 kJ​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \Delta S = -\text{139.7 J/K} = - \text{0.1397 kJ/K} \\ & \Delta H = -\text{18.0 kJ} \\ & T = 25^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{298 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate ∆G by using the formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \Delta G & = \Delta H - T\Delta S \\ & = -\text{18.0 kJ} - (\text{298 K})(-\text{0.1397 kJ/K}) \\ & = -\text{18.0 kJ} + \text{41.6 kJ} \\ & = \boxed{\text{23.6 kJ}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the ∆G of the reaction at 25°C is 23.6 kJ.
