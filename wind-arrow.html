The arrow shows the direction that the wind is blowing, and the letter represents the direction the wind is blowing from (on a standard 16-point compass rose). To insure the most accurate solution, this problem is best solved using a calculator and trigonometric principles. The first step is to determine the sum of all the horizontal (east-west) displacements and the sum of all the vertical (north-south) displacements.

Horizontal: 2.0 meters, West + 31.0 meters, West + 3.0 meters, East = 30.0 meters, West

Vertical: 12.0 meters, North + 8.0 meters, South = 4.0 meters, North

The series of five displacements is equivalent to two displacements of 30 meters, West and 4 meters, North. The resultant of these two displacements can be found using the Pythagorean theorem (for the magnitude) and the tangent function (for the direction). A non-scaled sketch is useful for visualizing the situation.

Applying the Pythagorean theorem leads to the magnitude of the resultant (R).

R2 = (30.0 m)2 + (4.0 m)2 = 916 m2

R = Sqrt(916 m2)

R = 30.3 meters

The angle theta in the diagram above can be found using the tangent function.

tangent(theta) = opposite/adjacent = (4.0 m) / (30.0 m)

tangent(theta) = 0.1333

theta = invtan(0.1333)

theta = 7.59 degrees

This angle theta is the angle between west and the resultant. Directions of vectors are expressed as the counterclockwise angle of rotation relative to east. So the direction is 7.59 degrees short of 180 degrees. That is, the direction is ~172 degrees.


21. 3. Refer to the illustration below. Illustrate with the arrows the movement of the wind between the land and the water during daytime. land a. Where does the updraft take place? b. Describe the direction of wind blow What type of breeze is this?​


Answer:

During the daytime, the land heats up faster than the water, causing the air above the land to warm and rise. This creates a low-pressure zone over the land. Meanwhile, the cooler air over the water sinks, creating a high-pressure zone. The air then flows from the high-pressure zone over the water to the low-pressure zone over the land. This movement of air is known as a sea breeze, and it usually blows from the water towards the land.

As for your questions:

a. The updraft takes place over the land, as the warm air rises from the heated surface.

b. The direction of wind blow is from the water towards the land, as cooler air sinks over the water and flows towards the low-pressure zone created by the updraft over the land.

The greater the difference between the high and low pressure and the nearer they are to each other or the shorter the distance between the high and low pressure areas, the faster the wind will blow.

So the fastest wind can be found in between the high and low pressure area, at the lower left of the provided image.

The winds moving in between the low and high pressure area, are going to opposite sides, because air at the lower atmosphere is more dense than air higher, and air pressure down low is greater than air pressure higher up.

Pressure gradient refers to the physical quantity that describes in which direction and at what rate a certain pressure increases the most rapidly around a specific location.

LAND AND SEA BREEZE

Breeze is the movement of air from the land to the sea and vice versa.

The land breeze is known as the air that is moving towards the surface of the sea.

On the other side, the sea breeze is defined as the air that moves towards the landmass from the sea.

Land breeze and sea breezes happen most of the time in a location that is close to wide bodies of water.

The main difference between the 2 is the types of water to preserve and heat up longer. These kinds of variations in the temperature of land and water causes modifications to the densities of the air above them.

The result of low pressures has an effect of alternating movement of the air which can be manifested as breezes.

In any kind of breeze, they serve as an instrumental in humidity and temperature levels, and precipitation rates.

Breeze or gentle blow of air has a direct result in setting the humidity levels, (it can be high or low), which is the high precipitation, and temperature level moderation in areas near the coast.
