Explanation:______________________________________________________________________________________ ______________2. In angle-launched projectile, the vertical velocity (vy) decreases as the projectile rises. Explanation:______________________________________________________________________________________ ______________3. In angle-launched projectile, the projectile’s vertical velocity becomes zero at its maximum height. Explanation:______________________________________________________________________________________ ______________4. The vertical velocity (vy) of a projectile that descends, decreases in angle-launched projectiles. Explanation:______________________________________________________________________________________


1. The horizontal velocity (vx) in horizontally launched projectiles is always constant because there is no force acting on the object in the horizontal direction. Therefore, it maintains a constant velocity throughout its trajectory.

2. In angle-launched projectile, the vertical velocity (vy) decreases as the projectile rises because of the force of gravity acting on it. As the projectile moves upwards, gravity pulls it downwards, reducing its vertical velocity.

3. In angle-launched projectile, the projectile's vertical velocity becomes zero at its maximum height because the projectile reaches the highest point of its trajectory, where its vertical velocity equals zero. At this point, the projectile starts to descend, and gravity begins to increase its vertical velocity in the downward direction.

4. The vertical velocity (vy) of a projectile that descends, decreases in angle-launched projectiles because gravity continues to act on the object, pulling it downwards. The vertical velocity decreases until the projectile reaches its final destination, where its vertical velocity becomes zero again.

20. Arubber ball was launched at a velocity of 5m/s at an angle where its range is at a maximum. A target is located exactly 2 meters from where the ball was launched. What is the velocity of the ball as soon it hits the ground?


Answer:

The velocity of the rubber ball is 5 m/s as it hits the ground.

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the velocity of the ball at the total time of its flight to hit the ground. We will use the concept of projectile motion in this problem.

For the formula, we will use the following:

1. To solve for the total time of flight

   Vy = Voy - gt      

   where

   Vy     is the vertical velocity at any given time

   Voy   is the initial vertical velocity, Voy = Vo sinθ

   g        is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/s²

    t        is the time covered in launching the ball  

2. To solve for the horizontal velocity

   Vx = Vox

   where

   Vx      is the horizontal velocity

   Vox    is the initial horizontal velocity, Vox = Vo cosθ

3. To solve for the velocity as soon as the ball hits the ground

   [tex]v=\sqrt{(Vx)^2+(Vy)^2}[/tex]

   where

    v     is the magnitude of the velocity of the ball at any given time

For the given information

Vo = 5 m/s

θ = 45°   this is the angle where its range is at maximum

xo = 2 m

v = ?

Solving the problem

1. First, let us solve for the total time of flight of the ball. Let us use the first formula, we have:

Vy = Voy - gt

0 = Vo sin θ - gt

0 = 5 sin 45 - 9.8 t

Here, we use the consider the ball at maximum height where Vy = 0

Do some transposition, we have:

9.8 t = 5 sin 45

t = 5 sin 45 / 9.8

t = 0.36 s

Then, multiplying this by to solve for the total time of flight, we have:

t = 2 x 0.36 s

t = 0.72 s

2. Then, let us solve for Vx and Vy using the formula above, we have:

Vox = Vo cos θ

Vox = 5 cos 45 = Vx

Vx = 3.54 m/s

Voy = Vo sin θ

Voy = 5 sin 45 = 3.54 m/s

Vy = Voy - gt

Vy = 3.54 m/s - (9.8 m/s²)(0.72s)

Vy = - 3.54 m/s

3. Now, we are ready to solve for the velocity to hit the ground. Let us sue the third formula, we have:

[tex]v=\sqrt{(Vx)^2+(Vy)^2}[/tex]

[tex]v=\sqrt{(3.54m/s)^2+(-3.54m/s)^2}[/tex]

v = 5 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the rubber to hit the ground is 5 m/s.

Vertical motion is symmetrical - the distance and time are the same in the rise as in the fall; the final velocity will have the same magnitude as the initial velocity but in the opposite direction. Horizontal components will never be effected by gravity; it is constant velocity motion.
