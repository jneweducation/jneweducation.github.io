5. PAHELPWhat is the wavelength of a certain electromagnetic wave that is traveling in a vacuum and has a frequency of 4 x 10⁸ Hz? ​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The wavelength of an electromagnetic (EM) wave is given by

[tex]\boxed{\lambda = \frac{c}{f}}[/tex]

where:

λ = wavelength

c = speed of light in vacuum = 3 × 10⁸ m/s

f = frequency

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the wavelength

[tex]\lambda = \dfrac{c}{f}[/tex]

[tex]\lambda = \dfrac{3 \times 10^{8} \: \text{m/s}}{4 \times 10^8 \: \text{Hz}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\lambda = \text{0.75 m}}[/tex]

Therefore, the wavelength is 0.75 m.

8. A certain electromagnetic wave has a wavelength of 6.25 x 10-7m. What is the energy of wave ?


Answer:

The frequency of the electromagnetic wave is 4.8 x 10¹⁴s⁻¹

In an Electromagnetic wave, The frequency , speed and wavelength of an are related by the formula

λ= c/f

where

λ = wavelength in metres, m.

c= speed of light in metres per sec, m/s

f= frequency in per seconds or hertz,( s⁻¹ , Hz)

Given that Wavelength of the electromagnetic wave is 6.25 x 10-7m.

We know that speed of light is 3 x 10⁸ m/s

Substituting the values into the equation becomes

f = c/ λ

f= 3 x 10 ⁸m /s / 6.25 x 10⁻⁷ m = 4.8 x 10¹⁴s⁻¹

Hence, The frequency of the electromagnetic wave is 4.8 x 10¹⁴s⁻¹

11. using the speed of light calculate for the frequency of an electromagnetic wave with a wavelength of 835 nm.


pa brainliest po :D

The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 299,792,458 meters per second. To calculate the frequency of an electromagnetic wave with a wavelength of 835 nm (nanometers), we can use the formula:

frequency = speed of light / wavelength

Converting the wavelength to meters:

835 nm = 835 x 10^-9 m

Plugging in the values:

frequency = 299,792,458 m/s / 835 x 10^-9 m

frequency = 3.59 x 10^14 Hz

Therefore, the frequency of an electromagnetic wave with a wavelength of 835 nm is approximately 3.59 x 10^14 Hz.
