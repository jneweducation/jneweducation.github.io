21. Write balanced chemical equations corresponding to the description: when hydrogen gas is passed over solid hot iron (iii) oxide, the resulting reaction produces iron and gaseous water


The balanced chemical equation for the reaction of hydrogen gas with solid hot iron (III) oxide to produce iron and gaseous water is:

2H2(g) + Fe2O3(s) --> 2Fe(s) + 3H2O(g)

This is a reduction-oxidation (redox) reaction, in which hydrogen acts as the reducing agent and iron(III) oxide acts as the oxidizing agent. In the process, hydrogen loses electrons to iron (III) oxide, and iron (III) oxide gains electrons from hydrogen. The electrons are transferred from the H atoms in hydrogen gas to the oxygen atoms in iron(III) oxide. As a result, hydrogen is oxidized to form water, and iron(III) oxide is reduced to form iron.

Fe2O3(s) + 3H2(g) --> 2Fe(s) + 3H2O(g)

It is also worth noting that this reaction is exothermic, meaning that it releases heat energy. This is why the iron(III) oxide must be heated in order for the reaction to proceed.

ANSWER:

Hydrogen chloride is a gas in room temperature because the molecules of hydrogen chloride have a strong bond between the hydrogen and chlorine atoms, but this bond is still relatively weak compared to other bonds, meaning that the molecules are able to move relatively freely and can quickly break apart and evaporate into a gas.

Nitrogen is a gas in room temperature because the nitrogen molecules are composed of two nitrogen atoms that are held together by a relatively weak bond. Since this bond is relatively weak, the molecules are able to move relatively freely and can quickly break apart and evaporate into a gas.

30. Which of the following shows a chemical reaction in which magnesium and hydrogen sulfide are the reactants and magnesium sulfide and hydrogen are the products? A. magnesium sulfide + hydrogen → magnesium + hydrogen sulfide B. magnesium + magnesium sulfide → hydrogen sulfide + hydrogen C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen D. magnesium + hydrogen → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen sulfide


Answer:  C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen

In chemical reactions, it always involve both reactants and products. Reactants are the substances that start a chemical reaction, and products are substances that are produced in the reaction. Reactants are usually at the left of the tail of the arrow (→ ) and products are at the right or tip of the arrow (→) if it is forward reaction. And if it is backward reaction, it is the reverse of the forward reaction.

Given: Reactants: magnesium, hydrogen sulfide

           Products: magnesium sulfide, hydrogen

Answer: magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen
