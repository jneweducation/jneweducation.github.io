Circumference = π × diameter

166.4 = 3.14 × diameter

Solving for diameter, we get:

diameter ≈ 53 cm

Therefore, the answer is option (d), 53cm.

VOLUME

=======================================

NUMBER NINE

The volume of a pyramid is_____if it has the length and width of 4cm and the height of 6cm.

CHOICES:

A. 31 cm³

B. 32 cm³

C. 33 cm³

D. 34 cm³

SOLUTION:

» We will use the formula [tex]\large\sf\purple{} V=\frac{lwh}{3} [/tex] since we are finding the volume of a pyramid.

» To find the volume of a pyramid, multiply the length, width and the height then divide the product by three.

[tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{lwh}{3} [/tex][tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{(4\:cm)(4\:cm)(6\:cm)}{3} [/tex][tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{96\:cm³}{3} [/tex][tex]\large\sf\bold \purple{V=32 \: cm {}^{3}}[/tex]

⟩ Therefore, the volume of the pyramid is 32 cm³. (Letter B. 32 cm³)

NUMBER TEN

The volume of a sphere is ____ if it has the radius of 5 in.

CHOICES:

A. 523.6 in³B. 525.3 in³C. 530.5 in³D. 523.5 in³

SOLUTION:

» We will use the formula [tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{4πr³}{3}[/tex] since we are finding the volume of a sphere.

» To find the volume of a sphere, multiply 4, pi (3.14) and the radius by itself three times then divide the product by three.

[tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{4πr³}{3}[/tex][tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{4(3.14)(5\:in)³}{3}[/tex][tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{4(3.14)(125\:in³)}{3}[/tex][tex]\large\sf\purple{}V= \frac{1,570\:in³}{3}[/tex][tex]\large\sf\bold \purple{ V=523.3\: in {}^{3}}[/tex]

⟩ Therefore, the volume of the sphere is 523.3 in³. (The answer can be 523.3 in³ or 523.6 in³, because it differs from the value of pi.)
