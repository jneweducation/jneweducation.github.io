17. 1. A bird flies a distance of 50 km in 10 minutes. What is the speed of the bird? 2. An elephant covers 21 km in 3 hours. What is the speed of the elephant? 3. A car travels 81 km in 9 hours going southwest. What is its velocity?​


Answer:

1.To find the speed of the bird, we need to divide the distance it traveled by the time it took:

Speed = Distance / Time

The distance traveled is 50 km and the time taken is 10 minutes, or 1/6 hours (since there are 60 minutes in an hour and 10 minutes is 1/6 of an hour). So we have:

Speed = 50 km / (1/6) hours

Speed = 300 km/hour

Therefore, the speed of the bird is 300 km/hour.

2.To find the speed of the elephant, we again use the formula:

Speed = Distance / Time

The distance traveled is 21 km and the time taken is 3 hours, so we have:

Speed = 21 km / 3 hours

Speed = 7 km/hour

Therefore, the speed of the elephant is 7 km/hour.

3.Velocity is a vector quantity that takes into account both speed and direction. If the car is traveling southwest, we need to specify the direction as well as the speed. We can use the formula:

Velocity = Displacement / Time

where displacement is the change in position of the car over the 9-hour period. If we assume that the car started at point A and ended at point B, then the displacement would be the distance from A to B in a straight line (since the car is traveling southwest, we can assume that it is moving diagonally between these two points). Let's say that the distance from A to B is 90 km (we can use the Pythagorean theorem to calculate this, since we know that the car has traveled 81 km southwest, which is the hypotenuse of a right triangle). Then we have:

Velocity = 90 km / 9 hours

Velocity = 10 km/hour

So the velocity of the car is 10 km/hour southwest.

Solution:
