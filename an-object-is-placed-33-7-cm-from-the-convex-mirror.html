9. what is the focal point distance of a convex mirror if the object located 10 cm away from the mirror forms a virtual image which is 30 cm away from the mirror?​


The focal point distance (f) of a convex mirror can be found by using the mirror formula: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v, where u is the distance of the object from the mirror and v is the distance of the image from the mirror.

Given that the object is located 10 cm away from the mirror and forms a virtual image which is 30 cm away from the mirror:

1/f = 1/10 + 1/ (-30)

1/f = 0.1 - 0.033

1/f = 0.067

f = 1/0.067

f = 14.9 cm

The focal point distance of the convex mirror is 14.9 cm. This is the point where all the light rays that are parallel to the principal axis of the mirror will converge to after reflection by the mirror.

12. an object 4.5 cm high is placed 50cm in front of a convex mirror with a radius of curvature of 20 cm. what is the height of the image?​


To find the height of the image, we can use the mirror formula:

1/f = 1/do + 1/di

where f is the focal length of the mirror, do is the object distance from the mirror, and di is the image distance from the mirror.

Since the mirror is a convex mirror, the focal length is positive and equal to the radius of curvature, f = R = 20 cm.

Substituting the values:

1/20 = 1/50 + 1/di

Multiplying both sides by 20:

1 = -2 + 20/di

Adding 2 to both sides:

3 = 20/di

Dividing both sides by 20:

1/6 = 1/di

The image distance di is 6 cm, so the height of the image can be found using the magnification formula:

hi/ho = -di/do

where hi is the height of the image and ho is the height of the object.

Substituting the values:

hi/4.5 = -6/50

Multiplying both sides by 50:

hi = -6 * 4.5 / 50

= -0.54 cm

So the height of the image is -0.54 cm. This means that the image is upside down and has a height of 0.54 cm.

24. What is the focal length of a convex mirror that produces an image that appears 15.0 cm behind the mirror when the object is 27.5 cm from mirror?


Answer:

The answer is 33 cm.

Explanation:

We are asked to get the focal length of the convex mirror. We can do this using the mirror equation:

[tex]\frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{d_{o}}+\frac{1}{d_{i}}[/tex].

In this particular problem

[tex]{d_{i}} = -15 cm[/tex] (distance of the image)

[tex]{d_{o}} = 27.5 cm[/tex] (distance of the object)

note that we used the sign convention for this particular equation.

[tex]{d_{i}} [/tex] is negative (-) if the image is formed behind the mirror.

Now, substituting the values that we have, we get:

[tex]\frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{d_{o}}+\frac{1}{d_{i}}[/tex].

[tex]\frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{27.5 cm}+\frac{1}{-15 cm}[/tex].

[tex]\frac{1}{f}=-\frac{1}{33 cm}[/tex].

[tex]f = -33 cm[/tex]

The sign just signifies that it is, in fact, a convex mirror.

Therefore, the focal length of the convex mirror used is 33 cm.
