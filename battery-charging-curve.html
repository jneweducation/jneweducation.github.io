13. Mr. Aguban is checking the difference in energy between a car battery and a motorcycle battery in moving a certain amount of charge. A 12 V motorcycle battery can move 6,000 C of charge, and a 12 V car battery can move 80,000 C of charge. How much energy does each deliver?​


Answer: For motorcycle 7.2*10^4 J and for the car, 9.6*10^5 J

Explanation: To say we have a 12.0 V battery means that its terminals have a 12.0 V potential difference. When such a battery moves charge, it puts the charge through a potential difference of 12.0 V, and the charge is given a change in potential energy equal to  ΔPE=qΔV .

So to find the energy output, we multiply the charge moved by the potential difference.

ΔPEcycle=(6000C)(12.0V)

= (6000C)(12C/J)

= 7.2x10^4J

ΔPEcar=(80,000C)(12.0V)

=(80,000C)(12.0C/J)

= 9.6x10^5 J

14. A 12V battery is able to move a charge of 30C in a given unit of time. Solve for the energy stored in the battery


Answer:

The energy stored in a battery can be calculated using the formula: Energy = 1/2 * C * V^2, where C is the charge stored in the battery and V is the voltage of the battery.

Given, C = 30C and V = 12V.

Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

Energy = 1/2 * 30 * 12^2 = 1/2 * 30 * 144 = 8640 J.

So, the energy stored in the 12V battery is 8640 J.
