19. A bird drops an egg from the top of a tree located 10.30 meters above the ground. Determine the time required for the egg to reach the ground.


Answer:

1.45 seconds

Explanation:

Formula:

[tex]h=\dfrac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

where:

h = height

g = gravitational acceleration (constant)

t = time

Given:

h = 10.30 meters

g = 9.81 m/s²

Solution:

[tex]h=\dfrac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

[tex]10.30=\dfrac{1}{2}(9.81)t^2[/tex]

[tex]t^2=\dfrac{2(10.30)}{9.81}[/tex]

[tex]t^2=2.1[/tex]

[tex]t=\sqrt{2.1}[/tex]

t = 1.45 seconds

29. Raymund sees a bird sitting on the top of a mango tree that is 8 m tall. If he is standing 15 m from the tree, what is the distance of the line of sight from Raymund to the bird?


Answer:

Square root 161

Step-by-step explanation:

They form a right triangle therefore we can use the pythagorean theorem

Then 8²+ b²= 15² 64+ b²= 225 b²= 225-64 b²=161

Get the Square root of both sides b= square root of 161

[tex]\\[/tex]
