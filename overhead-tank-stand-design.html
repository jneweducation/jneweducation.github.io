19. On a trip, Ana is standing at point O about 8 feet from a circular aquarium tank.The distance from Ana to a point of tangency on the tank is about 16 ft. Solve forthe radius of the tank.​


✒️POWER THEOREM

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \:\: \rm 12 \: feet [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

We can use the Tangent-Secant Power Theorem to determine the radius.

[tex] (8)(8 + r + r) = (16)^2 [/tex]

[tex] (8)(8 + 2r) = (16)^2 [/tex]

[tex] 64 + 16r = 256 [/tex]

[tex] 16r = 256 - 64 [/tex]

[tex] 16r = 192 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{16r}{16} = \frac{192}{16}\\ [/tex]

[tex] r = 12 [/tex]

Therefore, the length of the radius of the circular aquarium tank is 12 feet.

Find the diameter of the tank. Step 1:Read and understand the problem. Step 2:Devise a plan by illustrating the given problem and putting the appropriate labelling. Step 3:Carry out the plan by solving for the unknown. Step 4:make a conclusion Send rin po yung illustration thanks​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can follow these steps:

Step 1: Read and understand the problem.

The problem gives us the distances of Cardo and Alyana from a giant circular water tank, and the distance between them. We are asked to find the diameter of the tank.

Step 2: Devise a plan by illustrating the given problem and putting the appropriate labelling.

[asy]

pair A, C, O;

A = (0,0);

C = (16,0);

O = (8,9);

draw(O--A--C--cycle);

draw(Circle(O,9));

label("Alyana",A,S);

label("Cardo",C,S);

label("O",O,N);

label("16 ft",(O+C)/2,NE);

label("27 ft",(A+C)/2,S);

label("9 ft",(O+A)/2,W);

[/asy]

We can label the points as follows:

A for Alyana

C for Cardo

O for the center of the circular water tank

Step 3: Carry out the plan by solving for the unknown.

We can use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the diameter of the tank. We have:

AC^2 = AO^2 + CO^2

Substituting the given values, we get:

27^2 = AO^2 + 16^2

Solving for AO, we get:

AO = sqrt(27^2 - 16^2) = sqrt(729 - 256) = sqrt(473) = 21.57

Since the diameter of the tank is twice the distance from the center to the edge, the diameter is 2 * 21.57 = 43.14 feet.

Step 4: Make a conclusion.

The diameter of the circular water tank is 43.14 feet.

6. Forgetting to Care for Your Tank’s Drain Field

When talking about a septic system, most people focus on the drains and the tank. However, the drain field is equally important. If there is too much water in the drainage area, the tank won’t drain properly. When there is nowhere for the waste to go, your septic tank will stay full and need pumping.
