5. In the nature of human personhood define the human person as Disembodied spirit view and human person as embodied spirit?​


Answer:

The nature of human personhood is a complex and multifaceted topic, and there are a variety of perspectives and viewpoints that can be taken. Two such perspectives are the "disembodied spirit" view and the "embodied spirit" view.

The disembodied spirit view of human personhood asserts that the human being is essentially a non-physical entity or soul, separate from the physical body. This view sees the body as a temporary vessel or container for the soul, which is believed to be eternal and transcendent.

Proponents of the disembodied spirit view often emphasize the importance of spiritual or religious practices, which are seen as a means of connecting with the divine and transcending the limitations of the physical world. In this view, the ultimate goal of human life is to achieve a state of spiritual enlightenment or union with the divine.

On the other hand, the embodied spirit view of human personhood asserts that the human being is a unified entity consisting of both a physical body and a spiritual or mental dimension. This view sees the body and the mind as intimately connected and mutually influencing each other.

Proponents of the embodied spirit view often emphasize the importance of physical health and well-being, as well as mental and emotional health. They may argue that the body and mind should be cared for holistically, with attention paid to both physical and mental health.

In conclusion, the disembodied spirit view and the embodied spirit view are two different perspectives on human personhood, each emphasizing different aspects of the human being. While proponents of the disembodied spirit view prioritize spiritual practices and transcendence, those who hold the embodied spirit view emphasize the importance of physical and mental health and well-being.
