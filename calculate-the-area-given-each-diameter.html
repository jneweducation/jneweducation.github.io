The area of a circle is calculated with this formula:

Area = π r^2

That means pi times radius squared.

If you are given the diameter (as in #1a - #1j), you can use the diameter to find the radius and then use the radius to find the area:  

Step 1: Divide the diameter by 2 and that gives you the radius.

Step 2: Multiply the 3.14 by the radius squared

So, for 1.a.:

Step 1: 84 divided by 2 = 42, so radius = 42

(Note: The radius squared is the radius times the radius, so in this case, 42 times 42 = 1764)

Step 2: area = 3.14 x 1764 = 5538.96 mm

For 1.c.:  

Step 1: 14/3 divided by 2 = 14/6 cm

Step 2: area = 3.14 x 5.44444 = 17.095 cm

Etc.

So for 2.a.:

Skip Step 1.

27. Calculate the area given each diameter35 m121 cm65 in11 mm21.98ft


Answer:

1. 962.11 m^2

2. 11499.01 cm^2

3. 3318.31 cm^2

4. 379.44 ft^2

Step-by-step explanation:

Assuming that the given diameters are circle,

The formula for the area of a circle is

[tex]A_{circle}=\pi r^2[/tex]

We also know that the diameter of a circle is twice its radius.

[tex]d=2r[/tex]

Now, solving for the areas

[tex]A=\pi (\frac{35}{2})^2=306.25\pi m^2\approx962.11m^2[/tex]

[tex]A=\pi (\frac{121}{2})^2=3660.25\pi cm^2\approx 11499.01 cm^2[/tex]

[tex]A=\pi (\frac{65}{2})^2=1056.25\pi in^2\approx 3318.31in^2[/tex]

[tex]A=\pi (\frac{21.98}{2})^2=120.7801\pi ft^2\approx 379.44ft^2[/tex]
