A man measures the angle of elevation between the ground and a building 900ft away to be 43 degrees. how tall is the building?3.) The angle of elevation of a 90 foot ramp leaning to a bridge above a highway that is 30 degrees find the height of the bridge above the highway.5.)A stick leans against a brickwall the foot of the stick is 8m from the wall the stick reaches a height of 15m on the wall. what is the angle of elevation of the stick makes with the wall?pa help po plsssss​


Answer/explanation:

1) Let x be the distance of the boat from the base of the tower. Then we have:

tan(20°) = x/75

Multiplying both sides by 75, we get:

x = 75 tan(20°) ≈ 28.8 meters

Therefore, the boat is approximately 28.8 meters away from the base of the tower.

2) Let h be the height of the building. Then we have:

tan(43°) = h/900

Multiplying both sides by 900, we get:

h = 900 tan(43°) ≈ 885.4 meters

Therefore, the building is approximately 885.4 meters tall.

3) Let h be the height of the ladder and d be the distance between the foot of the ladder and the wall. Then we have:

tan(72°) = h/d

Multiplying both sides by d, we get:

h = d tan(72°)

Since we don't know the value of d, we cannot determine the exact length of the ladder.

4) Let h be the height of the bridge above the highway. Then we have:

tan(30°) = h/90

Multiplying both sides by 90, we get:

h = 90 tan(30°) ≈ 52.2 feet

Therefore, the height of the bridge above the highway is approximately 52.2 feet.

5) Let θ be the angle of elevation of the stick. Then we have:

tan(θ) = 15/d

where d is the distance between the foot of the stick and the wall. We don't know the value of d, so we cannot determine the exact angle of elevation.
