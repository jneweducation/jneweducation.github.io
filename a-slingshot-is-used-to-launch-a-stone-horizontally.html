15. a slingshot is aimed in a small stone is released with an initial speed of 13.5 m/s. what is the velocity of this stone after 1.5 sec? •with solution •find velocity​


Explanation:

Given:

Initial velocity (u) = 13.5 m/s

Time (t) = 1.5 sec

Acceleration due to gravity (g) = 9.81 m/s² (assuming the stone is in freefall)

Using the equation of motion for constant acceleration:

v = u + at

where:

v = final velocity

u = initial velocity

a = acceleration

t = time

To solve for the final velocity, we need to find the acceleration of the stone. Since the stone is in freefall, the acceleration is equal to the acceleration due to gravity (g).

a = g = 9.81 m/s²

Substituting the given values into the equation of motion:

v = u + at

v = 13.5 m/s + (9.81 m/s²)(1.5 s)

v = 13.5 m/s + 14.715 m/s

v = 28.215 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the stone after 1.5 seconds is 28.215 m/s

1. A stone is kicked 7.0 m/s horizontally from a cliff 80 m high. How far from the base of the cliff will the stone strike the ground?

Answer And Explaination:

We can use the kinematic equations of motion to solve this problem. Since the initial velocity of the stone in the vertical direction is zero, we can use the following equation to find the time it takes for the stone to hit the ground:

h = (1/2)gt^2

where h is the initial height of the stone, g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s^2), and t is the time taken to hit the ground.

Substituting the values given in the problem, we get:

80 = (1/2)(9.8)t^2

Solving for t, we get:

t = √(16.33) ≈ 4.04 s

Now, we can use the horizontal velocity of the stone to find the horizontal distance it travels in 4.04 seconds. Since there is no acceleration in the horizontal direction, we can use the following equation:

d = vt

where d is the distance, v is the horizontal velocity, and t is the time.

Substituting the values given in the problem, we get:

d = (7.0 m/s)(4.04 s) ≈ 28.3 m

Therefore, the stone will strike the ground approximately 28.3 meters from the base of the cliff.

2. If you launch a ball, moving horizontally at a speed of 1.15 m/s from a table that is 0.77 m tall, how far out from the table will the launched ball land?

Answer And Explaination:

Assuming there is no air resistance, we can use the kinematic equations of motion to solve this problem. Let's use the following variables:

h = height of the table = 0.77 m

v0x = initial horizontal velocity of the ball = 1.15 m/s

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.81 m/s^2

t = time of flight of the ball

d = horizontal distance traveled by the ball

The vertical motion of the ball can be described by the equation:

h = 1/2 gt^2

Solving for t, we get:

t = sqrt(2h/g) = sqrt(2 x 0.77 / 9.81) = 0.395 s

The horizontal distance traveled by the ball can be described by the equation:

d = v0x t

Substituting the values we know, we get:

d = 1.15 m/s x 0.395 s = 0.455 m

Therefore, the launched ball will land 0.455 meters out from the table.

27. a slingshot is aimed in a small stone is released with an initial speed of 13.5 m/s. what is the velocity of this stone after 1.5 sec?•with solution•find velocity​


Answer:

We can use the kinematic equation to solve for the final velocity of the stone. The kinematic equation that relates the final velocity (v), initial velocity (u), acceleration (a), and time (t) is:

v = u + at

where:

v = final velocity

u = initial velocity

a = acceleration

t = time

In this problem, the initial velocity (u) is 13.5 m/s and the time (t) is 1.5 seconds. The acceleration of the stone is due to the force of gravity, which is approximately -9.81 m/s^2 (negative because it acts in the opposite direction of the initial velocity).

Substituting these values into the kinematic equation, we have:

v = u + at

v = 13.5 m/s + (-9.81 m/s^2)(1.5 s)

v = 13.5 m/s - 14.715 m/s

v = -1.215 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the stone after 1.5 seconds is -1.215 m/s. The negative sign indicates that the stone is moving in the opposite direction of its initial velocity.
