11. A 5kg block initially at rest is pulled to the right along a horizontal, frictionless surface by a constant horizontal force of 12N. Find the speed of the block after it moved 3m.


Answer:

Speed of the block is 3.795 m/s.

Explanation:

For this problem we have to use 2 different working equations.

First we will use the equation for force.

F = ma

Where

F = forcem = mass of the objecta = acceleration

We are given with force of 12 N and we don't have acceleration but we have the mass of the object which is 5 kg. From these values and equation we can solve for the acceleration.

a = F / ma = 12N / 5kga = 2.4 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

Now that we already have the acceleration, we can now solve for the velocity or speed of the block by using the rectilinear motion equation

[tex]v^2[/tex] = [tex]u^2[/tex] + (2)(a)(ΔS)

Where

v = velocity of the block (required in the problem)u = initial velocity of the block (equal to zero since starting from rest)a = accelerationΔS = change in distance or simply the distance traveled

Substituting the given values and the acceleration value computed earlier

[tex]v^2[/tex] = 0 + (2) (2.4 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]) (3 m)

[tex]v^2[/tex] = 14.4 [tex]m^2[/tex]/[tex]s^2[/tex]

v = [tex]\sqrt{14 m^2/s^2}[/tex]

v = 3.795 m/s

17. Two blocks, B1 and B2 with masses of 12.0 kg and 5.60 kg, respectively, are in contact with each other on a frictionless horizontal surface. A purely horizontal force of 30.0 N is applied to B1, which in turn pushes B2 and both blocks accelerate together uniformly. The net horizontal force acting on B2 is


FORCES AND ACCELERATION OF OBJECTS

Two blocks, B1 and B2, with masses of 12.0 kg and 5.60 kg, respectively, encounter a purely horizontal force of 30.0 N on a level surface. The net horizontal force applying on B2 is also 30.0 N since the two blocks accelerate uniformly at 1.70 m/s2.

SOLUTIONSince the two blocks are in touch and going in similar heading, they experience a similar speed increase and can be treated as a solitary framework.The system's total mass is the sum of the masses of the two blocks:

m_total = 12.0 kg + 5.60 kg = 17.6 kilogram

Because there is no friction to oppose it, the applied force of 30.0 N is the net force operating on the system. As a result, we can use Newton's second law to calculate the system's acceleration:

F_net = a * m_total30.0 N = 17.6 kg * aa = 30.0 N / 17.6 kga = 1.70 m/s^2

Because the two blocks are travelling in the same direction, the net horizontal force acting on B2 is similarly 30.0 N.

19. a 15 lb block slides down a plane inclined at 30° to the horizontal. find the acceleration of the block : (a) of the plane is frictionless, (b) if the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4​


Answer:

a. The acceleration for a frictionless surface is 4.9 m/s²

b. The acceleration with kinetic friction is 1.51 m/s²

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to apply Newton's 2nd law of motion to solve for the acceleration of the block.

For the formula, will use:

a. F = mg sin θ

b. F = mg sin θ - fk

where

F     is the resultant force, the unit is in Newtons, N

m    is the mass of the block, the unit is in kg

θ     is the inclined angle

fk     is the kinetic friction

g     is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/s²

Given information

m = 15 lb

θ = 30°

μk = 0.4

a = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve the acceleration if the plane is frictionless using the given formula

F = mg sin θ

Applying 2nd Newton's law of motion

ma = mg sin θ

Simplify the equation

a = g sin 30°

Then substitute the given data

a = (9.8) sin 30°

a = 4.9 m/s²

b. Now, let us solve the acceleration if the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4

F = mg sin θ - fk

Take note that the kinetic friction fk = μN where N is the normal force

ma = mg sin θ - μN

The normal force in an inclined plane is N = mg cos θ

ma = mg sin θ - μmg cos θ

ma = m (g sin θ - μg cos θ)

Simplify the equation

a = g sin θ - μg cos θ

Then substitute the given data

a = 9.8 sin 30° - 0.4(9.8) cos 30°

a = 1.51 m/s²

Therefore, the accelerations of the inclined plane are 4.9 m/s² and 1.51 m/s² respectively.
