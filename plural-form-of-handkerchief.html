In contrast to singular nouns, plural nouns are nouns that are used to indicate that the object is more than one. In general, it is formed from a singular noun by adding an additional s/es to the noun.

Actually, it's not just adding -s or -es to a noun. However, there are also other rules for this form of transformation based on the last letter of the noun itself. Among others are:

Adding the -es ending must follow a singular noun ending in s, x, z, sh, or ch.If the singular noun ends in “y”, just add -s. However, there are also words ending in -y that must be converted to the letter "i" and added with the ending -es to change to a plural form.For singular nouns that end in a letter or have an "f" element, changing the plural only adds the ending -s or -es like other nouns.Similar to nouns ending in -y, nouns ending in -o have less strict grammatical rules. So, there are some words that can be added with -s and -es endings. Then, how to tell the difference? There is one trick you need to remember. If the noun is related to sound, then you just need to add the ending -s.Nouns ending in -on will usually change to the plural by adding only the -s ending. However, there are some words ending in -on that end in -a. However, this is very rare.Nouns ending in -is are also deformed by removing the ending -is and replacing it with the ending -es.
