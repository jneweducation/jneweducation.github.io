11. A solution is formed by adding 35 g of ammonium nitrate to 250 g of water.What is the percent by mass of ammonium nitrate?


The percent by mass of ammonium nitrate : 12.28%

Further explanation

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight / volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm) or mole fraction. The concentration shows the amount of solute in a unit of the amount of solvent.

So the concentration of a solution can be expressed  as a percent which is the ratio of solute to solution. Can be expressed in percent of mass (m / m) or volume percent (v / v)

[tex]\rm Percent\:by\:Mass=\dfrac{mass\:of\:solute}{mass\:of\:solution}\times 100[/tex]

[tex]\rm Percent\:by\:Volume=\dfrac{volume\:of\:solute}{volume\:of\:solution}\times 100[/tex]

Solution = Solute + Solvent

The solute is the substance that is dissolved usually in smaller amounts when compared to solvents

A solution that has a lot of solutes per  solvent is described as concentrated, while a solution with a small amount of solute is called dilute.

Adding 35 g of ammonium nitrate(NH₄NO₃), to 250 g of water (H₂O)

solute = ammonium nitrate = 35 g

solvent = water = 250 g

Solution = Solute + Solvent

Solution = 35 + 250 = 285 grams

Then the mass percentage of ammonium nitrate

[tex]\rm Percent\:mass=\dfrac{mass\:of\:ammonium\:nitrate}{mass\:of\:solution}\times 100\\\\Percent\:mass=\dfrac{35\:grams}{285\:grams}\times 100\\\\Percent\:mass=12.28\%[/tex]

Answer:

The mass percent of N in Ammonium Nitrate can be calculated by using the formula:

mass percent = (mass of element / molar mass of compound) x 100

First, find the mass of N in Ammonium Nitrate by multiplying the molar mass of N (14.01 g/mol) by the number of moles of N in Ammonium Nitrate (1 mol of N in NH4NO3)

mass of N = 14.01 g/mol x 1 mol = 14.01 g

Next, find the mass percent of N by dividing the mass of N by the molar mass of Ammonium Nitrate (80.06 g/mol) and multiplying by 100

mass percent of N = (14.01 g / 80.06 g/mol) x 100 = 17.5 %

17. A solution is formed by adding 35g of ammonium nitrate to 250g pf water. What is the percent by mass of ammonium nitrate? (show your formula and solution)


Percent by MassDiscussion:

Percent by mass (also called percent by weight or weight percent) is the ratio of the mass of a solute to the mass of the solution, multiplied by 100. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} × 100}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100}[/tex]

The percent by mass is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

A solution is formed by adding 35 g of ammonium nitrate to 250 g of water. What is the percent by mass of ammonium nitrate?

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 35 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent = 250 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute} = \text{ammonium nitrate}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solvent} = \text{H}_{2}\text{O}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{percent by mass}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\text{percen by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{35 g}}{\text{35 g + 250 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = 12.28\%}[/tex]
