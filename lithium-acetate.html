5. what is the electronics configuration of lithium (lithium has 3 electrons)​


Answer:

Lithium is the third element with a total of 3 electrons. In writing the electron configuration for lithium the first two electrons will go in the 1s orbital. Since 1s can only hold two electrons the remaining electron for Li goes in the 2s orbital. Therefore the Li electron configuration will be 1s22s1.

Boron is the fifth element with a total of 5 electrons. In writing the electron configuration for Boron the first two electrons will go in the 1s orbital. Since 1s can only hold two electrons the next 2 electrons for B goes in the 2s orbital. The remaining electron will go in the 2p orbital. Therefore the B electron configuration will be 1s²2s²2p¹.

6. determine the atomic mass of lithium given the isotopic composition 92.4% lithium -7 (mass 7.016u) and 7.60% lithium -6( mass 6.015u)​


Given:

[tex]\text{isotopic mass}_{Li-6} = \text{6.015 u}[/tex]

[tex]\text{isotopic mass}_{Li-7} = \text{7.016 u}[/tex]

[tex]\text{natural abundance}_{Li-6} = 7.60\% = 0.0760[/tex]

[tex]\text{natural abundance}_{Li-7} = 92.4\% = 0.924[/tex]

Required:

atomic mass

Solution:

[tex]\text{atomic mass} = (\text{isotopic mass}_{Li-6})(\text{natural abundance}_{Li-6}) + (\text{isotopic mass}_{Li-7})(\text{natural abundance}_{Li-7})[/tex]

[tex]\text{atomic mass} = (\text{6.015 u})(0.0760) + (\text{7.016 u})(0.924)[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{atomic mass = 6.94 u}}[/tex]

16. Lithium has two known stable isotopes—lithium-6 and lithium-7. Lithium-6 has an isotopicmass of 6.015 amu and percent abundance of 7.59%; lithium-7 has a mass of 7.016 amu at 92.41%abundance. Calculate the average atomic mass of lithium.​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & A_1 = \text{6.015 amu} \\ & f_1 = 7.59\% = 0.0759 \\ & A_2 = \text{7.016 amu} \\ & f_2 = 92.41\% = 0.9241 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the atomic mass of chlorine.

[tex]\begin{aligned} A & = A_1f_1 + A_2f_2 \\ & = \text{(6.015 amu)(0.0759) + (7.016 amu)(0.9241)} \\ & = \boxed{\text{6.940 amu}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the average atomic mass of lithium is 6.940 amu.

Comparing Lithium (cation) and Lithium atom:

                                   Lithium cation                    Lithium atom

proton #                             3                                           3

electron #                           2                                          3

# of shells                         1                                            2

base on the data above:

Lithium cation have less than 1 number of electron compared to lithium atom. Lithium cation has small atomic radius compared to lithium atom since it has a lesser number of shells.

Lithium atom will become lithium cation during chemical reaction because according to octet rule, lithium atom wanted to have the same electron configuration of He (helium) which is the nearest noble gas of lithium atom. Helium atom have 2 electrons and lithium atom have 3, so lithium atom loses one electron to have the same number of electrons with helium and becomes a lithium cation.
