Otto Frederick Warmbier

Otto Frederick Warmbier a student from University of Virginia. A resident of Ohio. Came to visit North Korea as a tourist but was imprisoned due to larceny. Was accused of stealing an item with a propaganda slogan from the hotel where he was staying at that time. Was sentenced to 15 years of hard labor.

Answers to the Questions:

4. The United States government actually denies that they had released $2,000,000 as payment for Warmbier's freedom. In fact, in his tweet former president Trump said that the United States did not pay any money to North Korea as it sought the release of Otto Warmbier. a day after a report said Trump had approved a two-million-dollar bill from Pyongyang for the American student’s care. The Treasury Department received the bill from North Korea and it remained unpaid through 2017, the Post reported. It was not clear whether the administration paid the invoice later.

5. According to the doctors, Otto Frederick Warmbier had suffered severe brain damage while in North Korean detention, fell into a coma and died days after arriving back in the US. This was after he was sentenced to 15 years of hard labor for allegedly trying to steal an item with a propaganda slogan from his hotel. But, North Korea has dismissed claims that it tortured the student instead they put the blame on food poisoning and a sleeping pill.
