1. recognize how individual form societies and how individual transformed by societies?


Societies and communities were initially formed as a matter of survival. In the old times, the more you are in a community, the better you chances of surviving if attacked by another group of people or of animals. In modern society, communities are formed because of a common goal and perhaps as a way to utilize the skills of each other. This way, each one is able to contribute to the growth of the community.

The society is then transformed as the individual people mature, change goals or are forced to change. For example, a barangay that was at first focused on making abaca slippers might find themselves shifting to the production of masks using the same raw materials due to the pandemic. For a transformation to happen, majority of the people in the community must be onboard. Otherwise, change would not happen.

3. Explain how individuals form societies and how individuals are transformed by societies​


Individuals are then transformed by societies in such a way that their decisions and principles are affected and transformed in a positive way to boost their morale and well-being.

We are all aware that a society is defined as a group of people in which social interaction plays an important role in the exchange of ideas and expressions. People of various ages, communities, languages, cultures, and customs comprise society. The stratification and dominance of society's subgroups or subcategories are involved. Society is also made up of different norms, values, and ethnicities. The word society was derived from the Latin word societas. Society can also be classified according to its geographical, social, political, and historical power.

Humans are responsible for shaping society. The system of practice was used to classify the society, and the major four categories included in the system are tribals, hunter-gatherers, stratified structures, and civilizations. The expansion of society has resulted in a significant change in the structure of a village to a nation. Humans create various histories of societies at various stages of development.

The three major categories of I'd societies are preindustrial, industrial, and postindustrial. In the case of pre-industrial society, the primary job of humans and animals is food production. These people's main activity is hunting and gathering food from their surroundings. Humans used to hunt wild animals in order to satisfy their hunger. They would always travel from one location to another in search of food. As a result, the size of society can only be measured in terms of numbers.

The pastoral group is the next category for society formation; in this group, they will domestic animals to meet their hunger needs. They will not move from one location to another in search of food in this category. This society they created is significantly larger than the other group. The trade began within this group as well, as they began to make various types of tools and other hunting tools for trading purposes. Horticultural groups are included in the third category of social formation. Fruits and vegetables are grown here to meet food needs as well as for trade purposes. As previously stated, this group cultivates. Society began to develop or transform as people began to mature.
