7. This perspective shows that a ""piece of art as an expression of an individual and an evaluation criterion based on beauty or expressive qualities.""


The perspective that shows that a work of art is an individual expression and the evaluation criteria is based on the beauty or quality of the expression is a philosophical perspective.

Explanation details:

Artwork is something that can be seen from various points of view. Starting from the perspective of aesthetics, commercial, to philosophy as mentioned above. The way we judge a work of art depends on the perspective we use. For example, if we use a commercial perspective, then we will tend to see art as a treasure with a selling value and judge it based on how much the selling value of the work is.

Meanwhile, from a philosophical perspective, art is considered as a medium for individuals to express their souls. So, if we use a philosophical perspective to look at works of art, then we can see or wonder what emotions or expressions the artist put in his work and our judgment will also be based on the beauty and quality of expression that the artist puts into his work.
