Answer:

Butterflies undergo a four-stage life cycle: egg, larva, pupa, and adult. The eggs are laid on the underside of leaves, and the larvae (caterpillars) hatch and eat the leaves. The caterpillars grow and shed their skin (molting) several times as they eat. When they have grown large enough, they form a pupa (chrysalis), inside which their bodies transform into adults. The adults emerge from the pupa and live for a short time, during which they mate and lay eggs.
