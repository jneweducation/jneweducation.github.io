26. Homologous chromosomes pair up and form tetrad


Homologous chromosomes are pairs of chromosomes that carry the same genes, one from each parent. During meiosis, homologous chromosomes pair up and form a structure called a tetrad, which consists of four chromatids. This pairing and crossing over between homologous chromosomes during meiosis is important for genetic diversity, as it can result in the exchange of genetic material between the two chromosomes. This process helps to produce genetically unique gametes, which are necessary for sexual reproduction.
