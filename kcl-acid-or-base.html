2. How is the bond in F2, different from the bond in KCl?a. F, is covalent and KCl is ionicb. F, is ionic and KCl is ionicc. F, is ionic and KCl is covalent​


Answer:

Letter a

Explanation:

The bond between two fluorine molecules is covalent. They share an electron so they both have a complete octet of 8 electrons in their valence shell.

Meanwhile, KCl is ionic. Rather than sharing electrons, the potassium loses an electron to obtain a 1+ charge while the chlorine gains an electron to obtain a 1- charge. Since opposite charges are attracted, these two form an ionic bond.

19. 1. Determine the concentration of KCl in ppm when 4 mg of KCl is dissolved in 8mg of solution. ​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{mass of solute} = \text{4 mg} \\ & \text{mass of solution} = \text{8 mg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the concentration of KCl in ppm using the formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{parts per million} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} \times 10^6 \\ & = \frac{\text{4 mg}}{\text{8 mg}} \times 10^6 \\ & = \boxed{\text{500,000 ppm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the concentration of KCl in ppm is 500,000 ppm

28. How many moles are there in 10 grams of KCl? *​


Answer:

0.14 mol KCl

Explanation:

First, get the molecular weight of KCl using the Periodic table atomic masses.

K = 39 g

Cl = 35 g

39 g + 35 g = 74 g/mol (Molecular Weight)

Next, solve for mole of KCl.

Formula:Mol = Given Mass in g divided by Molecular Weight in g

10g/74g/mol = 0.14 mol KCl

Answer = 0.14 mol KCl
