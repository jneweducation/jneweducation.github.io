mass of CH₃OH = 5.5 g

mass of H₂O = 40 g

molar mass of CH₃OH = 32 g/mol

[tex]X_{\text{CH}_{3}\text{OH}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

First Step: Calculate the number of moles of CH₃OH and H₂O.

For CH₃OH

[tex]\text{moles of} \: \text{CH}_{3}\text{OH} = \dfrac{\text{mass of} \: \text{CH}_{3}\text{OH}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{CH}_{3}\text{OH}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of} \: \text{CH}_{3}\text{OH} = \dfrac{\text{5.5 g}}{\text{32 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} = \dfrac{\text{40 g}}{\text{18 g/mol}}[/tex]

Last Step: Calculate the mole fraction of CH₃OH and H₂O.

For CH₃OH

[tex]X_{\text{CH}_{3}\text{OH}} = \dfrac{\text{moles of} \: \text{CH}_{3}\text{OH}}{\text{moles of} \: \text{CH}_{3}\text{OH} + \text{moles of} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \dfrac{\text{moles of} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}{\text{moles of} \: \text{CH}_{3}\text{OH} + \text{moles of} \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = \dfrac{\text{2.2222 mol}}{\text{0.1719 mol} + \text{2.2222 mol}}[/tex]

21. Solve for the volume. 1. cube e = 4.6 cm 2. cone h = 27 cm r = 16 cm 3. sphere r = 32 mm4. square pyramids = 40 dm h = 58 dm 5. prism b = 190 mm² h = 120 mm 6. cylinder r = 24 cm h = 60 cm ​


Answer:

1. Cube:

The volume of a cube is given by V = s^3, where s is the edge length. Substituting s = 4.6 cm, we get:

V = (4.6 cm)^3 = 97.336 cm^3

2. Cone:

The volume of a cone is given by V = (1/3)πr^2h, where r is the radius and h is the height. Substituting r = 16 cm and h = 27 cm, we get:

V = (1/3)π(16 cm)^2(27 cm) ≈ 21,466.45 cm^3

3. Sphere:

The volume of a sphere is given by V = (4/3)πr^3, where r is the radius. Substituting r = 32 mm, we get:

V = (4/3)π(32 mm)^3 ≈ 137,202.15 mm^3

4. Square Pyramid:

The volume of a square pyramid is given by V = (1/3)Bh, where B is the area of the base and h is the height. Substituting s = 40 dm = 4000 mm and h = 58 dm = 5800 mm, we get:

B = (40 dm)^2 = 160000 cm^2 = 16000000 mm^2

V = (1/3)(16000000 mm^2)(5800 mm) = 309,333,333.33 mm^3

5. Prism:

The volume of a prism is given by V = Bh, where B is the area of the base and h is the height. Substituting B = 190 mm^2 and h = 120 mm, we get:

V = (190 mm^2)(120 mm) = 22,800 mm^3

6. Cylinder:

The volume of a cylinder is given by V = πr^2h, where r is the radius and h is the height. Substituting r = 24 cm and h = 60 cm, we get:

V = π(24 cm)^2(60 cm) ≈ 108,508.73 cm^3

28. An insulated steam pipe located where the ambient temperature is 32°C, has an inside diameter of 50 mm with 10 mm thick wall. The outside diameter of the corrugated asbestos insulation is 125 mm and the surface coefficient of still air, h0 = 12 W/m2 -K. So If the heat loss per unit becomes 100w/m determine the required thickness of the pipe?


Answer:

Using this formula, we can calculate the thickness:

Heat Loss (W/m) = (h0 * (T1 - T2)) / (Rtotal)

Where:

h0 = 12W/m2 -K

T1 = 32 (Ambient Temperature in Celsius)

T2 = 0 (Steam Temperature)

Rtotal = (1/h1) + L/k + (1/h2)

h1 = Thermal conductivity through the pipe wall

L = Length of the pipe in m

k = Thermal conductivity through the insulation

h2 = Heat transfer coefficient of the outside surface

The heat loss per unit can be converted to Rtotal.

Rtotal = (100W/m) / (12W/m2-K)

Rtotal = 8.33 m2-K/W

Now, we can solve for the thermal conductivity through the pipe wall (h1):

h1 = 1/Rtotal – L/k – (1/h2)

h1 = 1 / 8.33 - (0.5m / 0.04W/m-K) - (1 / 12W/m2-K))

h1 = 0.086W/m-K

The thickness of the pipe can now be calculated using the thermal conductivity and the inside and outside diameters of the pipe:

Thickness (δ) = (h1 * (Do - Di)) / k

Where:

Do = Outside diameter of the pipe

Di = Inside diameter of the pipe

Thickness (δ) = (0.086W/m-K * (125 - 50)) / 0.04W/m-K

Thickness (δ) = 8.5mm

Therefore, the required thickness of the pipe to achieve a heat loss of 100w/m is 8.5mm.

Answer:

To determine the required thickness of the pipe insulation, we can use the heat transfer equation:

Q = 2πLk (Ti - To) / ln(Do / Di)

where:

Q = heat loss per unit length of pipe (W/m)

L = length of pipe (m)

k = thermal conductivity of insulation (W/m-K)

Ti = inside temperature of pipe (°C)

To = outside temperature of insulation (°C)

Do = outside diameter of insulation (m)

Di = inside diameter of pipe (m)

We are given:

Ti = 100°C (since it is a steam pipe)

To = 32°C

Di = 0.05 m

Do = 0.125 m

h0 = 12 W/m2-K

Q = 100 W/m

We need to find k and L.

First, we can calculate the inside surface temperature of the insulation:

Ti' = Ti - Q / (2πLh0Di)

Ti' = 100 - 100 / (2πL * 12 * 0.05) = 93.3°C
