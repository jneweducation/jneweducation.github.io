Another measure of variation is the interquartile range, which is the difference between the upper and lower quartiles of the data. To find the interquartile range, you first need to find the median, or middle value, of the data. For Lucy, the median is (6+7)/2=6.5 miles, and for Dena, the median is (6+8)/2=7 miles. The upper quartile is the value that is greater than or equal to 75% of the data, and the lower quartile is the value that is less than or equal to 25% of the data. For Lucy, the upper quartile is 8 miles and the lower quartile is 5 miles, so the interquartile range is 8-5=3 miles. For Dena, the upper quartile is 9 miles and the lower quartile is 4 miles, so the interquartile range is 9-4=5 miles.

The range and interquartile range both show the differences in the girls' mileage, as they measure the spread of the data. However, the range is more sensitive to outliers, or values that are much larger or smaller than the rest of the data, while the interquartile range is less sensitive to outliers.

28. how High resolution scanning microscope/ scanning electron microscope works?​


Answer

Areas ranging from approximately 1 cm to 5 microns in width can be imaged in a scanning mode using conventional SEM techniques (magnification ranging from 20X to approximately 30,000X, spatial resolution of 50 to 100 nm).

The resolution limit of light microscopes is about 200nm, the maximum useful magnification a light microscope can provide is about 1,000x. The resolution limit of electron microscopes is about 0.2nm, the maximum useful magnification an electron microscope can provide is about 1,000,000x.
