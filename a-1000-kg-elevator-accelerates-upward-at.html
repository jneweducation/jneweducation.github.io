2. Assume your mass is 80 kg, and you are standing on a scale in an elevator. The elevator accelerates UPWARD with acceleration of 2.5 m/s^2, what would the reading of the scale be?


The reading of the scale would be 82.5 kg. The reason for this is that when the elevator accelerates upward, the normal force that acts on the object on the scale also increases.

The normal force is the force that acts perpendicular to the surface of an object and is equal to the weight of the object. The weight of an object is equal to the mass of the object multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity (g).

In the case of an elevator, the normal force also depends on the net force acting on the object. The net force is equal to the force of gravity minus the force of the elevator's acceleration.

So, the weight of the object is given by the formula:

Weight = mass x g + ma

Where m is the mass of the object and a is the acceleration of the elevator.

In this case, the weight of the person standing on the scale would be:

Weight = 80 kg x 9.8 m/s^2 + (80 kg x 2.5 m/s^2) = 784 N + 200 N = 984 N

So the reading of the scale would be 984 N or 984 kg which is equivalent to 82.5 kg

Keep in mind that this is an idealized scenario, and in real life, other factors such as friction and air resistance could affect the reading of the scale.

7. 2. An elevator was used to lift three persons to the 14th floor. If the elevator has lifting force of 4500N and each person weight 100 kg, how much will the elevator accelerate upward? Neglect the mass of the elevator. Assume that 1 kg is equivalent to 10N.​


Answer:

The total weight lifted by the elevator is the sum of the weight of the three persons, which is:

3 persons x 100 kg/person = 300 kg

To convert this weight to force, we use the conversion factor of 1 kg = 10 N. Therefore, the weight of the three persons is:

300 kg x 10 N/kg = 3000 N

The lifting force of the elevator is 4500 N, so the net force acting on the elevator is:

net force = lifting force - weight of persons

net force = 4500 N - 3000 N

net force = 1500 N

To calculate the acceleration of the elevator, we use Newton's second law:

net force = mass x acceleration

Since we are neglecting the mass of the elevator, the mass in this equation is the total mass of the persons:

mass = 300 kg

Substituting the values, we get:

1500 N = 300 kg x acceleration

Solving for acceleration, we get:

acceleration = 1500 N / 300 kg

acceleration = 5 m/s^2

Therefore, the elevator will accelerate upward at a rate of 5 m/s^2.

14. A man weighing 700 nb is in an elevator that is accelerating upward at 4 m/s2. the force exerted on him by the elevator floor is


Answer:

The force exerted by the elevator is 985.71 N

Explanation:

In our case, a man was inside an elevator moving upward with an acceleration. Here, we are going to sum up the vertical forces and apply the 2nd Newton's Law of Motion.

It is best to analyze this problem using a force diagram. I have provided the illustration below.

For the formula, we will use:

∑Fy = ma

Fe - W = ma

where

∑Fy   is the sum of all vertical forces

Fe     is the force of the elevator upward, unit is in Newtons (N)

W      is the weight force of the man, unit is also in Newtons (N)

m      is the mass of the man, unit is in kg

a       is the acceleration, unit is in m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

For the given information

W = 700 N

a = 4 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

Solving the problem

1. First, let us solve for the mass of the man, using weight force, we have:

W = mg

700 N = m (9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])

m = 71.43 kg

2. Let us use the formula above for summing the vertical forces, then substitute the given information, we have:

∑Fy = ma

Fe - W = ma

Take note that, upward is positive sign and downward is negative sign

Fe - mg = ma

Fe = ma + mg

Fe = m(a + g)

Fe = 71.43 kg (4 m/[tex]s^2[/tex] + 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])

Fe = 985.71 N

Therefore, the force exerted by the elevator to the man is 985.71 N.

29. A man enters an elevator holding 2 boxes- one on top of the other. The top box has a mass of 6 kg. And the bottom box has a mass of 8 kg. The man sets the two boxes on a metric scale sitting on the floor. When accelerating upward from rest, the man observes that the scale registers a value of 166 N; this is the upward force upon the bottombox. Determine the acceleration of the elevator (and boxes) and determine the forces acting between the boxes.


Answer:

a. The acceleration of the elevator (and boxes) is 2.06 m/s²

b. The forces acting between the boxes are 58.8 N and 78.4 N

Explanation:

In this problem, we will apply the concept of 2nd Newton's law of motion. To do this, we will get the vertical net force to solve for the acceleration of the elevator.

Formula to use

F - W = ma

where

F     is the force of the acceleration, the unit is in Newtons (N)

W    is the weight of the person or objects inside the elevator, the unit is

      also in Newtons (N)

m    is the mass, the unit is in kg

a     is the acceleration, the unit is in m/s²

Given information

m1 = 6 kg

m2 = 8 kg

F = 166N

a = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve for the acceleration using the given formula

F - W = ma

Take not that W = mg, therefore, we write

F - mg = ma

Then substitute the given data

166N - (6 kg + 8 kg)(9.8 m/s²) = (6 kg + 8 kg) a

Simplifying the equation

166 N = (6 kg + 8 kg)(9.8 m/s²) + (6 kg + 8 kg) a

166 N = (6 kg + 8 kg) (9.8 m/s² + a )

divide both sides by (6 kg + 8 kg)

9.8 m/s² + a = 166N / (6 kg + 8 kg)

a = 11.86 m/s² - 9.8 m/s²

a = 2.06 m/s²

b. Now, let us solve for the force acting between the boxes. Let us use the wieght foce formula to solve each force.

For box with mass of 6kg:

Fw = mg

Fw = (6 kg)(9.8 m/s²) = 58.8 N

For box with mass of 8kg:

Fw = mg

Fw = (8 kg)(9.8 m/s²) = 78.4 N

Therefore, the box on the bottom has greater force upward the the box on top with force acting downward.
