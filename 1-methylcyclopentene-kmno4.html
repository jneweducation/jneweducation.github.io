2. 1. Calculate the mass in grams of KMnO4 that should be used to prepare 2.0 L of0.25 M KMnO4 solution.


Strategy:

To calculate the mass of the solute, we need to know the number of moles of solute and the molar mass of the solute.

Solution:

The definition of molarity (M) is

[tex]M = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

First, we find the number of moles of solute in 2.0 L of 0.25 M KMnO₄ solution. Rearranging the equation above to calculate the number of moles of solute

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = M \times \text{volume of solution (L)}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{0.25 mol KMnO₄}}{\text{1 L solution}} \times \text{2.0 L solution}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \text{0.50 mol KMnO₄}[/tex]

Finally, we can calculate the mass of solute using its molar mass as the conversion factor. Therefore,

[tex]\text{mass of solute} = \text{0.50 mol KMnO₄} \times \frac{\text{158.04 g KMnO₄}}{\text{1 mol KMnO₄}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{\text{mass of solute = 79 g KMnO₄}}[/tex]

13. How many present moles in 237g of KMnO4​

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of KMnO₄.

In KMnO₄, there are 1 K atom whose atomic mass is 39.10 g/mol, 1 Mn atom whose atomic mass is 54.94 g/mol, and 4 O atoms whose atomic mass is 16.00 g/mol.

molar mass = (39.10 g/mol × 1) + (54.94 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 158.04 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of KMnO₄.

Using dimensional analysis, the number of moles of KMnO₄ can be written as

[tex]\text{moles of} \: \text{KMnO}_{4} = \text{237 g} \: \text{KMnO}_{4} × \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{KMnO}_{4}}{\text{158.04 g} \: \text{KMnO}_{4}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of} \: \text{KMnO}_{4} = \text{1.50 mol}}[/tex]

17. How many formula units are in 17.7 g of KMnO4?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of KMnO₄.

The molar masses of K, Mn, and O are 39.0983 g, 54.9380 g and 15.9994 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass} & = \text{39.0983 g + 54.9380 g + 4(15.9994 g)} \\ & = \text{158.0 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of molecules of KMnO₄ by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{number of} \: \text{KMnO}_4 \: \text{formula units} & = \text{17.7 g} \: \text{KMnO}_4 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{KMnO}_4}{\text{158.0 g} \: \text{KMnO}_4} \times \frac{6.022 \times 10^{23} \: \text{KMnO}_4 \: \text{formula units}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{KMnO}_4} \\ & = \boxed{6.75 \times 10^{22} \: \text{formula units}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 6.75 × 10²² formula units in 17.7 g of KMnO₄.
