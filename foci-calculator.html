5. Find the standard equation of a locus of point whose sum of distances to the foci of the curve is equal to 10. one of the foci is at (3,0)​


Answer:

x²/25 + y²/16 = 1

Step-by-step explanation:

This is an ellipse

sum of distances to the foci = d1 + d2 = 10

In an ellipse, the sum of distances is equal to 2a, where a is the semi-major axis

2a = 10

a = 5

Since the given is not enough, we assume that the major axis is on the x-axis and the center is at the origin. Equation of that ellipse is

x²/a² + y²/b² = 1

Since the center is at the origin, the distance from the center to foci, c, is 3.

c = 3

Using the relationship of a, b, c

a² = b² + c²

5² = b² + 3²

25 = b² + 9

25 - 9 = b²

b² = 16

b = 4

substituting the value of a and b

x²/5² + y²/4² = 1

x²/25 + y²/16 = 1

28. vertices (+-6,0), foci(+-4,0)


The given conic section has its vertices at (±6, 0) and its foci at (±4, 0). The conic section is an ellipse since a > c.

The vertices are at a distance from the center, which means the center is at the origin (0, 0) and a = 6. The foci are at c distance, which means c = 4. The vertices and foci have the same y-coordinate, which means the ellipse is horizontal.

Lastly, all we need to do is solve for b. We can do this using the formula a² = b² + c².

a² = b² + c²

b² = a² - c²

b² = 36 - 16

b² = 20

The standard equation of the ellipse is [tex] \frac{x^{2}}{36} + \frac{y^{2}}{20} = 1 [/tex].
