What is the mass in grams of 4.23 mol of indigo?


Answer:

1. To calculate the mass of urea in each sack of fertilizer, we need to use the Avogadro's number and the molar mass of urea. The molar mass of urea is 60.06 g/mol.

Number of moles of urea in 1.69x10^26 molecules:

n = N/NA = 1.69x10^26/6.022x10^23 = 28.1 mol

Mass of urea in the sack of fertilizer:

mass = n x molar mass = 28.1 mol x 60.06 g/mol = 1687.586 g or 1.69 kg

Therefore, the mass of urea in each sack of fertilizer is approximately 1.69 kg.

2. To calculate the number of formula units of SnF2 in a 100 g tube of toothpaste, we first need to find the number of moles of SnF2, and then use Avogadro's number to calculate the number of formula units.

Molar mass of SnF2 is 145.81 g/mol.

Number of moles of SnF2 in 5.97 g of SnF2:

n = m/M = 5.97 g/145.81 g/mol = 0.0409 mol

Number of formula units of SnF2 in the toothpaste tube:

N = n x NA = 0.0409 mol x 6.022x10^23 = 2.46x10^22

Therefore, there are approximately 2.46x10^22 formula units of SnF2 in the toothpaste tube.

3. To calculate the mass of 4.23 mol of indigo, we need to use the molar mass of indigo, which is 262.28 g/mol.

Mass of 4.23 mol of indigo:

mass = n x molar mass = 4.23 mol x 262.28 g/mol = 1109.12 g or 1.11 kg

Therefore, the mass of 4.23 mol of indigo is approximately 1.11 kg or 1109.12 g.

Tin (II) fluoride (SnF2) is often added to toothpaste as an ingredient to prevent tooth decay. If a 100 g tube of toothpaste contains 5.97 g SnF2, how many formula units of SnF2 are present in the tube of the toothpaste? Indigo (C16H10N2O2) is an organic blue dye. What is the mass in grams of 4.23 mol of indigo? 0.016 g


Answer:

First problem:

To calculate the mass of urea in the sack, we need to use the Avogadro constant to convert the number of molecules to mass. The Avogadro constant is approximately 6.022 x 10^23 molecules per mole.

Number of moles of urea = number of molecules / Avogadro's number

n = (1.69 x 10^26) / 6.022 x 10^23 = 28.08 moles

The molar mass of urea is 60.06 g/mol.

Mass of urea in the sack = number of moles x molar mass

m = 28.08 moles x 60.06 g/mol = 1686 g

Therefore, the mass of urea in the sack is 1686 grams.

Second problem:

To calculate the number of formula units of SnF2 in the toothpaste, we need to use the molar mass of SnF2 and the given mass of SnF2 in the toothpaste.

The molar mass of SnF2 is 156.70 g/mol.

Number of moles of SnF2 = mass of SnF2 / molar mass

n = 5.97 g / 156.70 g/mol = 0.0381 moles

One mole of SnF2 contains Avogadro's number of formula units, which is 6.022 x 10^23 formula units.

Number of formula units of SnF2 in the toothpaste = number of moles x Avogadro's number

= 0.0381 moles x 6.022 x 10^23 formula units/mol

= 2.29 x 10^22 formula units

Therefore, there are 2.29 x 10^22 formula units of SnF2 in the toothpaste.

Third problem:

To calculate the mass of 4.23 mol of indigo, we need to use the molar mass of indigo.

The molar mass of indigo is 262.28 g/mol.

Mass of 4.23 moles of indigo = number of moles x molar mass

= 4.23 moles x 262.28 g/mol

= 1108 g (rounded to the nearest whole number)

Therefore, the mass of 4.23 moles of indigo is 1108 grams, or 1.108 kg, or 1108000 mg.

23. How many fluorides ions are in 1.46 moles of aluminum fluoride


There are  2.64 x 10²⁴  Fluorides ions in 1.46 moles of Aluminum fluoride

Further explanation  

A mole is the number of particles (atoms, molecules, ions) contained in a substance that is the same amount as many atoms in 12 gr C-12  

1 mole = 6.02.10²³ particles  

Can be formulated :

N = n x No  

No = 6.02.10²³ = Avogadro's number  

Given

1.46 moles of Aluminum fluoride (AlF₃)

Required

Number of ions

Solution

AlF₃ ⇒ Al³⁺ + 3F⁻

There are 3 moles F  in 1 mole of AlF3

So for 1.46 moles AlF₃ :

= 3 x 1.46

= 4.38 moles F

= 4.38 x 6.02 x 10²³

= 2.63676 x 10²⁴  Fluorides ions
