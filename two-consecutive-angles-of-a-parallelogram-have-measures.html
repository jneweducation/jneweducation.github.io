1. Two consecutive angles of parallelogram have measures (×+30)° and 2(×-30)° what is the measure of the smaller angle


Theorem:  Consecutive angles of a parallelogram are supplementary.  

Therefore, the sum of the consecutive angles of a parallelogram is 180°.

(x + 30) + 2(x - 30) = 180
x + 30 + 2x - 60 = 180
3x - 30 = 180
3x = 180 + 30
3x/3 = 210/30
x = 70

Substitute, x = 70:

First angle: x + 30 = 70 + 30 = 100°
Second angle: 2(x-30) = 2(70-30) = 80°

ANSWER:  The smaller angle measures 80°.

Two consecutive angles of a parallelogram have measures (x + 20)° and (2x - 20)°, what is the measure of the larger angle?

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

» The two consecutive angles are supplementary, then their sum is 180 degrees. Solve for x.

[tex](2x - 20) \degree + (x + 20) \degree = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]2x - 20 + x + 20 = 180[/tex]

[tex]3x = 180[/tex]

[tex] \frac{ \cancel3x}{ \cancel3} = \frac{180}{3} \\ [/tex]

[tex]x = 60[/tex]

» Thus, the value of x is 60, find the measure of the larger angle.

[tex] \qquad \implies 2x - 20[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \implies 2(60) - 20[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \implies 120 - 20[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \implies 100[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The measure of the larger angle is 100 degrees.
