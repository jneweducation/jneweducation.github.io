9. The water level in a cylindrical tank is 2.4 m high. The tank is 3.4 m high with a diameter of 1.8m.How much more water is needed to fill the tank?


The volume of a cylinder can be calculated using the formula:

V = πr^2h

where r is the radius, h is the height, and π is approximately equal to 3.14.

We are given that the tank has a diameter of 1.8 m, which means the radius is half of this value:

r = 1.8 m / 2 = 0.9 m

The total height of the tank is 3.4 m. Therefore, the empty space in the tank is:

empty space = 3.4 m - 2.4 m = 1 m

The volume of this empty space can be found by using the formula for the volume of a cylinder:

V = πr^2h

V = 3.14 x (0.9 m)^2 x 1 m

V ≈ 2.54 m^3

So the tank has 2.54 cubic meters of empty space. This is how much more water is needed to fill the tank.

30. The surface area of an open cylindrical tank is 68 square meter. if the diameter is 2/3 of its height, what is the height of the tank


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Since the diameter is 2/3 of height

d = (2/3)(h)

Since d = 2r

2r = (2/3)(h)

r = h/3 (1)

The formula for the surface area of an open cylindrical tank is

S = πr² + 2πrh

Substituting (1) and value of S to the formula to solve for the height

68 = π(h/3)² + 2π(h/3)(h)

68 = π(h²/9) + 2π(h²/3)

68 = (π/3)[(h²/3) + 2h²]

68 = (π/3)[(h²/3) + (6h²/3)]

68 = (π/3)(7h²/3)

68 = (7π/9)(h²)

h² = [68 / (7π/9)]

h² = 612/(7π)

h² = 27.8294

h = √27.8294

[tex]\boxed{h = \text{5.28 m}}[/tex]

Therefore, the height of the tank is 5.28 m.
