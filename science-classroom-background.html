5. A grade 8 students is holding her science textbook that has a mass of 1000 kg, she holds it outside her classroom window, which is 3.5 meter above the ground. After some time she releases it. how much work did the student do by holding the textbook out of the classroom window?


Answer:

34,300 J in work

Explanation:

Work = Mass * Gravity * Height and is measured in Joules.

Given:

Gravity: 9.8m/s²  (constant)

mass: 1000 kg

height: 3.5 meter

Work: ?

Solution:

W=mgh

W= (1000 kg)(9.8m/s²)(3.5 meter)

w= 34,300 J

11. How does a 21 century science classroom look like?​


Answer:

The 21st Century classroom is student centered, not teacher centered. Teachers no longer function as lecturers but as facilitators of learning. The students are learning by doing, and the teacher acts as a coach, helping students as they work on projects. ... Students no longer study each subject in isolation.


12. Appreciate the benefits and limitationd of science and its applications Your classroom activities learning experience​


Answer:

Benefits

Scientific knowledge allows us to develop new technologies, solve practical problems, and make informed decisions — both individually and collectively. Because its products are so useful, the process of science is intertwined with those applications: New scientific knowledge may lead to new applications.

Limitations

Due to the need to have completely controlled experiments to test a hypothesis, science can not prove everything. For example, ideas about God and other supernatural beings can never be confirmed or denied, as no experiment exists that could test their presence.
