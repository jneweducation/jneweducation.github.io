7. List the sample space for a three-digit number formed from the digits 1, 9, 5, and 3 with no digit to be used more than once. ​


QUESTION:

List the sample space for a three-digit number formed from the digits 1, 9, 5, and 3 with no digit to be used more than once.

ANSWER:

24 ways or combinations

SOLUTION:

We can solve this using Permutation formula,

[tex]\sf P(n,r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}\\\\where,\\n = 4\\r = 3\\\\\sf P(4,3) = \frac{4!}{(4-3)!}\\\\\sf P(4,3) = \frac{4!}{(1)!}\\\\\sf P(4,3) = 4\times3\times2\times1\\\\\sf P(4,3) = 24[/tex]

But memorizing formulas are no FUN! Let's solve this by logic since we may forget the formula!

Imagine we have 3 slots, to write our three-digit number from our 4 digits consisting of 1, 9, 5, and 3

___ , ___, ___

On the first slot, we can choose to put any of the 4 digits numbers from 1, 9, 5, and 3

 4   ,     ,    

On the 2nd slot, we can put the remaining 3 digit numbers (say we put 1 as first digit, then we are left with the 3 digits consisting of 9, 5, 3)

 4   ,  3   ,    

On the 3rd slot, we can put the remaining 2 digit numbers (say we put 1 as first digit, then 9 as second digit, then we are left with the 2 digits consisting of 5, 3)

 4   ,  3   ,  2  

Then the whole combination is simply the product of the three numbers,

4 × 3 × 2 = 24

So there are 24 ways of creating a three-digit number from the given digits of 1, 9, 5, 3

PROOF:

Let's check and verify the answer because we are not magicians!

Let's say our 1st digit is the number 1, then all the possible three-digit number combinations from the given digits of 1, 9, 5, 3 are listed below,

195

193

159

153

139

135

As seen above, we can have 6 ways of arranging the three-digit number when our first digit is 1 and no repetition of digit is allowed.

Now we do the same process to the remaining digits 9, 5, 3. And each time, it will produce 6 ways of arrangement.

Hence, 6 ways x 4 digits = 24 ways and we have proven our answer because we are mathematicians and not magicians!
