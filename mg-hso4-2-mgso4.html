18. why does h2so4 break up to h+ and hso4-?


H2SO4 (Sulfuric Acid) is a diprotic acid. This type of acid means that they ionize or separate from H+ ions two times. So for the first ionization reaction, H2SO4 separates into H+ and HSO4-, and for the second ionization reaction, HSO4- then separates into H+ and SO4 2-.
