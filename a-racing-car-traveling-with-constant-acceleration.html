3. A car racing travelled with a constant acceleration of 7 m/s2. if the initial velocity of the car is 8 m/s, find its final velocity and displacement after 10 seconds.


Explanation:

To solve this problem, we can use the following kinematic equations:

vf = vi + at

d = vit + 1/2at^2

where:

vf = final velocity

vi = initial velocity

a = acceleration

t = time

d = displacement (or distance traveled)

Given:

vi = 8 m/s

a = 7 m/s^2

t = 10 s

Using the first equation, we can solve for the final velocity:

vf = vi + at

vf = 8 m/s + (7 m/s^2)(10 s)

vf = 78 m/s

Therefore, the final velocity of the car after 10 seconds is 78 m/s.

Using the second equation, we can solve for the displacement:

d = vit + 1/2at^2

d = (8 m/s)(10 s) + 1/2(7 m/s^2)(10 s)^2

d = 430 m

Therefore, the displacement (or distance traveled) by the car after 10 seconds is 430 meters.

10. A race car accelerates uniformly from 18.5 m/s to 46.1 m/s in 2.47 seconds. Determine the acceleration of the car and the distance travelled. Help meee!


Answer:

The acceleration of the car is [tex]11.17\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex] and the distance traveled is [tex]79.781 m[/tex].

Explanation:

Average acceleration is the rate of change of a moving body with respect to time. Acceleration is a vector quantity and this is usually use for motion in straight line.

The formula for average acceleration is:

[tex]a[/tex] = Δ[tex]v[/tex]/Δ[tex]t[/tex]     equation 1

where:

[tex]a[/tex]     is the average acceleration, units is in [tex]\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex]

Δ[tex]v[/tex]   change in velocity where Δ[tex]v=[/tex] [tex]v_{f} -v_{i}[/tex], units is in m/s

      [tex]v_{f}[/tex] is final velocity and [tex]v_{i}[/tex] is initial velocity

Δ[tex]t[/tex]    change in time, unit is in seconds

To solve for the acceleration, substitute the given information to the formula, we get:

[tex]a[/tex] = Δ[tex]v[/tex]/Δ[tex]t[/tex]

[tex]a=\frac{46.1-18.5}{2.47}[/tex]

[tex]a=11.17\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex]

To solve for the distance traveled by the car, we use the formula:

[tex]x=v_{0}t+\frac{1}{2} at^{2}[/tex]

where:

[tex]x[/tex]     is the distance, unit is in meters

[tex]v_{0}[/tex]    initial velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]a[/tex]     is the acceleration, unit is in [tex]\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex]

[tex]t[/tex]      is time, unit is in seconds

Substitute the value of [tex]v_{0}[/tex], [tex]a[/tex] and [tex]t[/tex], we get:

[tex]x=18.5(2.47)+\frac{1}{2} (11.17)(2.47^{2} )[/tex]

[tex]x=79.781m[/tex]

20. 3. A race car accelerates uniformly from 18.5 m/s to 46.1 m/s in 2.47 seconds. Determine the acceleration of the car and the distance traveled.​


Answer:

To find the acceleration of the car, we can use the formula:

a = (vf - vi) / t

where

a = acceleration

vf = final velocity = 46.1 m/s

vi = initial velocity = 18.5 m/s

t = time taken = 2.47 s

Substituting the given values, we get:

a = (46.1 m/s - 18.5 m/s) / 2.47 s

a = 10.97 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the car is 10.97 m/s^2.

To find the distance traveled by the car, we can use the formula:

d = vi * t + (1/2) * a * t^2

where

d = distance traveled

vi = initial velocity = 18.5 m/s

t = time taken = 2.47 s

a = acceleration = 10.97 m/s^2

Substituting the given values, we get:

d = 18.5 m/s * 2.47 s + (1/2) * 10.97 m/s^2 * (2.47 s)^2

d = 71.99 m

Therefore, the distance traveled by the car is 71.99 m.
