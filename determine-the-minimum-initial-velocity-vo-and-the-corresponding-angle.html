25. Determine to test the law of gravity for herself, Gwen walks a skyscraper 180 m high, stopwatch in hand, and starts her freefall (zero initial velocity). Five seconds later, Spiderman arrives at the scene and dives off the roof to save the girl he loves. Spiderman leaves the roof with an initial speed vo that he produces by pushing himselt downward from the edge of the roof. He then falls with the same acceleration as any freely falling body. What must be the value of vo so that Spiderman catches Gwen just before they reach the ground?


Answer:

To determine the value of vo that Spiderman needs to catch Gwen, we need to use the equations of motion for freefall under gravity. Specifically, we will use the equation for displacement during freefall, which is:

h = (1/2)gt^2

where h is the height, g is the acceleration due to gravity (approximately 9.8 m/s^2 near the surface of the Earth), and t is the time.

For Gwen, we know that she falls for 5 seconds and travels a distance of 180 m. Therefore, we can plug in these values and solve for g:

180 m = (1/2)(9.8 m/s^2)(5 s)^2

g = 9.8 m/s^2

Now, we can use the same equation to find the time it takes for Spiderman to fall from the roof to the ground. We know that the total distance he needs to travel is 180 m, but we don't know how long it takes him to get there. We can use the equation:

180 m = (1/2)g(t_catch)^2

where t_catch is the time it takes for Spiderman to reach the ground after jumping off the roof.

For Spiderman, we also know that he needs to catch Gwen just before they reach the ground. This means that he needs to arrive at the ground at the same time as Gwen, so we can set the time for his fall equal to 5 seconds:

180 m = (1/2)g(5 s)^2

Solving for g gives us:

g = (2 x 180 m) / (5 s)^2 = 14.4 m/s^2

Now we can use this value of g to solve for the initial speed vo that Spiderman needs to catch Gwen. We can use the equation for displacement during freefall again, but this time we need to account for the fact that Spiderman has an initial speed vo:

180 m = (1/2)g(t_catch)^2 + vo(t_catch)

We also know that Spiderman falls for the same amount of time as Gwen, so we can set t_catch equal to 5 seconds:

180 m = (1/2)(14.4 m/s^2)(5 s)^2 + vo(5 s)

Solving for vo gives:

vo = (180 m - (1/2)(14.4 m/s^2)(5 s)^2) / 5 s

vo = 54 m/s

Therefore, Spiderman needs to jump off the roof with an initial speed of 54 m/s in order to catch Gwen just before they reach the ground.
