25. When a company uses a perpetual inventory system, all merchandise transactions are updated as and when they occur; so, the inventory account will show the current balance at all times.A. TrueB. False


When a company uses a perpetual inventory system, all merchandise transactions are updated as and when they occur; so, the inventory account will show the current balance at all times. [TRUE]

Understanding The perpetual method

The perpetual method is a method of recording each time adjusting the input and output transactions of inventory. Usually this perpetual method is referred to as the book method, because every incoming or outgoing inventory stock will be recorded through the books. If you use the perpetual method, it will be easier to prepare the company's income statement and balance sheet.

Because if the recording is done regularly in the books, it can make it easier for you to know the stock of goods without having to physically count the goods.

System in recording inventory of goods perpetual method

In the perpetual method, there are 3 methods of valuation related to inventory, including:

- LIFO Perpetual Method

The perpetual LIFO (Last In First Out) method is a recording system method for stock inventory, where the cost of each unit of product sold is the cost of the last purchase. If you use the perpetual LIFO method, then you need a subsidiary ledger.

- FIFO Perpetual Method

The method of assessing the inventory of goods, in this case the goods are adjusted according to the order according to the order in which the goods were purchased. You have to take several steps when compiling inventory records with this perpetual method, namely by calculating HPP.

The perpetual FIFO (First In First Out) method is generally used by businesses that sell products with an expiration date such as medicines, food, and other products.

Therefore, usually the perpetual method is also related to the system of movement of goods and data with results that are not much different from the results of physical calculations.

-Average Perpetual Recording

The perpetual average method or average cost can be used in managing inventory to calculate the average cost of each type of goods when purchases occur. Then, the unit cost is used to calculate the cost each time a purchase and sale occurs, then calculate the average cost.
