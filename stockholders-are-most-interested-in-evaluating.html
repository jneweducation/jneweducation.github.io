16. How a company configures its strategy -making processes to reduce the probability that managers will pursue their own self-interest at the expense of stockholders​


Answer:

In order to reduce the probability that managers will pursue their own self-interest at the expense of stockholders, a company should configure its strategy-making processes to ensure that managers are subject to significant oversight.

This oversight can be achieved through a system of checks and balances that includes multiple stakeholders with different interests, as well as a comprehensive review process. The review process should include both internal and external perspectives.
