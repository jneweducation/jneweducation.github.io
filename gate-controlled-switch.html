13. when you insert a switch in series with lamps will the switch be able to control the lamps infeoendently can you tell why​


Answer:

If you insert a switch in series with lamps, the switch will control all the lamps in the circuit simultaneously. When the switch is open, it interrupts the flow of electricity through the circuit, and all the lamps will turn off. When the switch is closed, electricity can flow through the circuit again, and all the lamps will turn on.

This is because when lamps are connected in series, they share the same current, which means that the same amount of electricity flows through each lamp. So, if you add a switch in series with the lamps, the switch will control the flow of electricity to all the lamps, and they will all turn on or off together.

If you want to control each lamp independently, you can connect them in parallel instead of series. In a parallel circuit, each lamp has its own separate branch, so you can control each one with a separate switch.
