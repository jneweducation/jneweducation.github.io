➛Question:

. A 500-kg block slides from rest down a 300- inclined plane. If the coefficient of sliding friction is 0.3, calculate the velocity of the block 5 secs after sliding down the plane. How far along the plane does the block travel?

Answer:

First, we need to find the acceleration of the block down the inclined plane. We can use the following formula:

a = g sin(θ) - μg cos(θ)

where g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2), θ is the angle of the inclined plane (300), and μ is the coefficient of sliding friction (0.3).

Plugging in the values, we get:

a = (9.81 m/s^2) sin(300) - (0.3)(9.81 m/s^2) cos(300)

a = -4.905 m/s^2

The negative sign indicates that the acceleration is in the opposite direction of motion (up the incline).

Next, we can use the kinematic equation:

v = u + at

where v is the final velocity, u is the initial velocity (which is zero in this case), a is the acceleration, and t is the time elapsed.

Plugging in the values, we get:

v = 0 + (-4.905 m/s^2) × 5 s

v = -24.525 m/s

The negative sign indicates that the velocity is in the opposite direction of motion (up the incline). We can take the absolute value to get the magnitude of the velocity, which is:

|v| = 24.525 m/s

Next, we can use the conservation of energy principle:

PE + KE + Wnc = constant

where PE is the potential energy, KE is the kinetic energy, and Wnc is the work done by non-conservative forces (such as friction). Since the block starts from rest at the top of the incline, its initial kinetic energy is zero. We can also assume that the potential energy is converted entirely into kinetic energy (since there is no external work done). Therefore, we can write:

mgh = 1/2 mv^2 + Wnc

where m is the mass of the block, g is the acceleration due to gravity, h is the height of the incline (which we can assume to be 1 meter), and Wnc is the work done by friction.

Plugging in the values, we get:

(500 kg)(9.81 m/s^2)(1 m) = 1/2 (500 kg)(24.525 m/s)^2 + Wnc

Solving for Wnc, we get:

Wnc = -6045.38 J

The negative sign indicates that the work done by friction is in the opposite direction of motion (up the incline).

Finally, we can use the work-energy principle:

Wnc = Fd

where F is the force of friction and d is the distance traveled by the block along the incline. We can solve for d:

d = Wnc / F

where F = μmg cos(θ)

Plugging in the values, we get:

d = (-6045.38 J) / [(0.3)(500 kg)(9.81 m/s^2) cos(300)]

d = 10.55 m

Therefore, the velocity of the block 5 seconds after sliding down the plane is 24.525 m/s, and the block travels a distance of 10.55 meters along the plane.

16. a 15 lb block slides down a plane inclined at 30° to the horizontal. find the acceleration of the block : (a) of the plane is frictionless, (b) if the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4​


Answer:

a. The acceleration for a frictionless surface is 4.9 m/s²

b. The acceleration with kinetic friction is 1.51 m/s²

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to apply Newton's 2nd law of motion to solve for the acceleration of the block.

For the formula, will use:

a. F = mg sin θ

b. F = mg sin θ - fk

where

F     is the resultant force, the unit is in Newtons, N

m    is the mass of the block, the unit is in kg

θ     is the inclined angle

fk     is the kinetic friction

g     is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/s²

Given information

m = 15 lb

θ = 30°

μk = 0.4

a = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve the acceleration if the plane is frictionless using the given formula

F = mg sin θ

Applying 2nd Newton's law of motion

ma = mg sin θ

Simplify the equation

a = g sin 30°

Then substitute the given data

a = (9.8) sin 30°

a = 4.9 m/s²

b. Now, let us solve the acceleration if the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4

F = mg sin θ - fk

Take note that the kinetic friction fk = μN where N is the normal force

ma = mg sin θ - μN

The normal force in an inclined plane is N = mg cos θ

ma = mg sin θ - μmg cos θ

ma = m (g sin θ - μg cos θ)

Simplify the equation

a = g sin θ - μg cos θ

Then substitute the given data

a = 9.8 sin 30° - 0.4(9.8) cos 30°

a = 1.51 m/s²

Therefore, the accelerations of the inclined plane are 4.9 m/s² and 1.51 m/s² respectively.
