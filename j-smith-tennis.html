8. 3. A tennis ball is traveling at 60 m/s and has a kinetic energy of 85 J. Calculate the mass of the tennis ball. • REQUIRED: • GIVEN: • FORMULA: • SOLUTION: • Final Answer:


Mass is 0.0472 kg

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

KE = 85 J

v = 60 m/s

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Mass

Step 3: Solve for Mass

Solution:

The formula for Kinetic Energy is given by:

KE = ½ mv²

where: KE - Kinetic Energy

m - mass

v - velocity

Substituting the value of KE and v in the formula,

85 J = (½)(m)(60 m/s)²

m = (85 J)(2)/(60 m/s)²

m = 0.0472 kg

24. A tennis ball is travelling at 50 m/s and has a kinetic energy of 75 J. Calculate the mass of the tennis ball.


Answer:

m = 0.06kg

Explanation:

Kinetic Energy (KE) = ½mv²

KE = 75 J

v = 50 m/s

m = ?

➞ 75 = ½ × m × (50)²

➞ 75 = ½ × m × 50 × 50

➞ m = 75 × 2/50 × 50

➞ m = 150/2500

➞ m = 0.06kg
