1. topic: angle of depression and angle of elevation find the angle of elevation of the sun if a person 189 cm tall casts a shadow 223 cm on the ground.


Calculating the angle of elevation.

Question:

Find the angle of elevation of the sun if a person 189 cm tall casts a shadow 223 cm on the ground.

The picture above would be our graphical representation of the question provided, where what we are finding is the angle of elevation made from the shadow and the height of the person.

Since the position of the angle has an opposite and an adjacent value is already given, we will going to use one of the trigonometric functions, the best function to use is tangent.

The function of tangent is

[tex]\sf{ \tan( \dfrac{o}{a} ) }[/tex]

But we are finding the measurement of the angle so we are going to use its inverse function. Substitute the values on the formula.

Given

Opposite (o) = 189 cmAdjacent (a) = 223 cmAngle of Elevation (θ) = ?

[tex]\sf{θ = \tan ^{ - 1} ( \dfrac{o}{a} ) }[/tex][tex]\sf{θ = \tan ^{ - 1} ( \dfrac{189}{223} ) }[/tex][tex]\sf{θ = \tan ^{ - 1} ( 0.8475 ) }[/tex][tex]\sf{θ = 40.28°}[/tex]

4. with the sun a girl 1.4m tall casts a 3.6m shadow find the angle of elevation from the tip of the shadow from the sun


To find the angle of elevation from the tip of the shadow from the sun, we will do the following:

List the given valuesKnow the formula to be used that corresponds to the given dataApply the given values to the formula and solve

Step-by-step Solution:

1. List the given values:

Based from the problem statement, let:

Height of the girl = 1.4 m

Length of the shadow = 3.6 m

If we will illustrate the situation, we will come up with a triangle where the values given are the values of the sides of the triangle, specifically a right triangle. Therefore:

Opposite side = 1.4 m

Adjacent side = 3.6 m

2. Since the given illustrates a right triangle, we will use the triangle laws, SOHCAHTOA which is a guide for triangle sides-angles relationship. The formula that we will use  based on the given data is:

TOA = Tangent Θ = Opposite side / Adjacent side

3. We will apply the data to the formula and solve:

Tangent Θ = Opposite side / Adjacent side

Tangent Θ = 1.4 m / 3.6 m

Tangent Θ = 0.3888888889

Θ = Tangent ⁻¹ (0.3888888889)

Θ = 21.25°

Answer:

The angle of elevation from the tip of the shadow to the sun is 21.25°.

5. if the angle of elevation of the sun is 55 degrees. What is the length of the shadow on the level ​


Answer:

To determine the length of the shadow on the level ground, we need to know some additional information such as the height of the object or the angle of elevation of the object.

Assuming that we have the height of the object, we can use trigonometry to solve for the length of the shadow. Let's call the height of the object "h" and the length of the shadow "x".

We know that the angle of elevation of the sun is 55 degrees. This means that the angle between the ground and the line from the top of the object to the sun is also 55 degrees.

Using the tangent function, we can write:

tan(55) = h/x

Solving for x, we get:

x = h / tan(55)

Using a calculator, we can find that:

x = h / 1.4281

Therefore, the length of the shadow on the level ground is equal to the height of the object divided by the tangent of the angle of elevation of the sun, which is approximately 0.7003 times the height of the object.
