There is a great distance between the particles of solid; the attraction between particles are negligible. 4. Answer:

1. A.

2. B

3.C.

4.A.

5. D.

Explanation:

1. All matter is composed of tiny indivisible particles too small to see. These particles do not share the properties of the material they make up. The spacing between particles of a matter is called inter molecular space.

2. Liquids are non-compressible and have constant volume but can change shape. A liquid’s shape is dictated by the shape of the container it is in.

3.Solid particles are closely packed and the force of attraction between them are strong. That's why they only vibrate.

4.Solids have strong intermolecular forces of attraction. The constituent particles of solids cannot move from their positions they can only vibrate from their mean positive. That is why do solids have a definite shape.

5. Gases do not have a definite shape or volume because the molecules in gases are very loosely packed, they have large intermolecular spaces and hence they move around. The force of attraction between molecules is also very less, as a result gases acquire any shape or any volume.
