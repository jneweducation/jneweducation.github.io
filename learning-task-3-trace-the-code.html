Amino acid - indicates a specific amino acid (thus, it is a triplet code) (hence, it is a triplet code).

         In mRNA, each trio of nucleotides is referred to as a codon, and each codon designates a certain amino acid (hence, it is a triplet code). Hence, the chain of amino acids that makes up a protein is assembled in order using the mRNA sequence as a template. One amino acid codes triplet three nucleotide. The total number of nucleotides is now 16, with the inclusion of adenosine residue. Hence, the mRNA sequence will code for five amino acids while mRNA is a particular kind of RNA called messenger RNA is required for the synthesis of proteins. The part of the mRNA that is actually translated into protein is the coding region, often known as the coding sequence or CDS.

Order of DNA bases: Adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G), and thymine (T) are the four bases of DNA (T). These bases come in certain pairings (A with T, and G with C). Base pair can also refer to the actual number of base pairs in a sequence of nucleotides, such as 8 base pairs.

TAGCATCAGGGTTACCAC

Order of bases in mRNA (codon): During translation, an mRNA's codons are read starting at a start codon and continuing until they reach a stop codon.

AUCGUAGCCCAAUGGUG

Order of bases in tRNA: Codons are three-nucleotide mRNA sequences that specify gene function.

UAGCAUCAGGGUUACCAC

ISOLEUCINE - The pigment inside red blood cells that transports oxygen. It could aid in blood sugar regulation.
