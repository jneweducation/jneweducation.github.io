The image of an object is the location where light rays from that object intersect upon reflecting from a mirror. By constructing at least two sets of incident and reflected rays, this image location can easily be found.



3. in an concave mirror, what is the orientation of a farther than a center of curvature?​


Answer:

If the object is at a point farther from the mirror than the center of curvature, the image is real (i.e., it is formed directly by the reflected rays), inverted, and smaller than the object. If the object is at the center of curvature, the image is the same size as the object and is real and inverted.

Answer:

Q14. In a concave mirror, if the object is placed farther than the center of curvature, the image is smaller and inverted. If the object is placed at the center of curvature, the image is the same size but still inverted. If the object is placed between the center of curvature and the focal point, the image is larger and inverted. If the object is placed at the focal point, there is no image formed. And if the object is placed between the focal point and the center of the lens (vertex), the image is enlarged, virtual and upright.

In a convex mirror, the image is always smaller, virtual, and upright regardless of the location of the object.

Q15. Dentists usually use a concave mirror to clearly see the images of our teeth because a concave mirror forms a magnified and inverted image, making it easier for dentists to examine the teeth closely and in detail.

Q16. Most department stores use convex mirrors as a security measure. These mirrors are used to cover blind spots and to increase the visibility of the store, allowing the security personnel to see a larger area and deter theft.
