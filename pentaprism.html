Answer:

Methane and pentane are specific types of chain compounds called hydrocarbons, meaning they consist of only carbon and hydrogen. However, methane and pentane differ in the number of carbon bonds they have. Methane is the simplest alkane, only containing one carbon bond, while pentane has five carbon bonds.

18. a pentation scale is the most common type of scale​


Answer

Common Scale Types

Pentatonic (five-note) scales are used more widely than any other scale formation. In fact, Western art music is one of the few traditions in which pentatonic scales do not predominate. Their frequency is especially notable in the Far East and in European folk music. The most common varieties of pentatonic scales use major seconds and minor thirds, with no half steps (anhemitonic). A representative type could be spelled C–D–E–G–A, for example. The pentatonic scale is so pervasive that melodies exhibiting tetratonic (four-note) scales often appear to be pentatonic with one pitch omitted. Hexatonic (six-note) scales appear rather rarely in folk music and nonliterate cultures. Examples that are known often seem to be fragments of the seven-note Western diatonic scale.

Heptatonic scales are especially prominent in the world’s art-music traditions. The tone systems of India, Iran, and the West are entirely heptatonic, and seven-note scales are also present in the art music of some cultures that do not use such scales exclusively (e.g., the ritsu scale in Japan and the pelog scale in Java).

With some exceptions in both the distant and the recent past, Western art music has been based largely on one heptatonic scale, known as the diatonic scale. The origins of this scale can be traced to ancient Greece, and it has been formulated to some extent according to acoustical principles. Since the octave in Western music is normally divided into 12 equal half steps, the characteristic intervals of the diatonic scale can be constructed upon any one of the 12 pitches. Such transpositions of the scale are known as keys.

19. Burke dramatism pentad and guilt redemption


Kenneth Burke initially established dramatism as a method for understanding the social uses of language. An examination of Burke's major rhetorical concepts—identification, the definition of the human being, the concept of reality, and terms for order—reveals the epistemology of his dramatism as a marriage of paradox and metaphor. However, recently Burke has shifted dramatism towards a philosophy. Three shifts establish a dramatism based upon “act,”; not the tension between “action”; and “motion,”; dramatism that employs language “literally,”; rather than exploiting its ambiguity, and dramatism that is more “reality”; oriented rather than the link that orders and relates “reality”; to our abstract values.

21. How to prepare methyl pentanoate​


Answer:

Methyl pentanoate, also known as isopentyl acetate, can be prepared through the esterification of acetic acid and isopentyl alcohol. The process typically involves the following steps:

1. Prepare a solution of acetic acid and isopentyl alcohol in a molar ratio of 1:1. The exact amounts will depend on the desired yield.

2. Add a small amount of sulfuric acid or hydrochloric acid as a catalyst to the solution. This will help to speed up the esterification reaction.

3. Heat the solution to a temperature of 60-70°C, with constant stirring. This will help to promote the reaction between the acetic acid and isopentyl alcohol.

4. Monitor the reaction using an indicator such as an acid-base indicator or a pH meter. The reaction is considered complete when the acidity of the solution reaches a neutral level.

5. Once the reaction is complete, remove the heat and cool the solution to room temperature.

6. Separate the ester product from the water and any excess acid by distillation or by using a separatory funnel.

7. The final product, methyl pentanoate, can be purified by a series of recrystallization or distillation to remove impurities.

It is important to mention that the reaction must be carried out in a well-ventilated area and with the appropriate safety measures as the chemicals used are flammable and toxic
