8. The region below is bounded above is bounded above by * = (y - 3)³ + 1 and the line y = 1 on [-2, 4]. Suppose the region is rotated around y = 1. Find the volume of the solid.​


Answer:

The region below is bounded above is bounded above by * = (y - 3)³ + 1 and the line y = 1 on [-2, 4]. Suppose the region is rotated around y = 1. Find the volume of the solid.

To find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating the region around the y-axis, we can use the formula

V = ∫2πr²h dy

where r is the distance from the y-axis to the point (x, y) on the curve, h is the distance between the curve and the axis of rotation, and the integral is taken over the interval of y-values spanned by the region.

To find r, we note that the points on the curve are of the form (x, y) where y is between 1 and * = (y - 3)³ + 1. We can then set y = 1 + √[* - 1], so r = √[* - 1]. The distance h is just the difference between the two bounding curves, which is * - 1.

Substituting these expressions into the formula, we get

V = ∫2π[* - 1] √[* - 1] dy

The limits of integration are y = 1 and y = 4. Substituting these values and performing the integration gives

V = 2π ∫4³ - 1 dy - 2π ∫1³ - 1 dy

= 2π(64 - 4 - 1) = 118π

Therefore, the volume of the solid is 118π.
