21. what are the factors affecting in community power structures?​


There are several factors that can affect community power structures, including:

Socioeconomic status: This refers to the level of education, income, and occupation of individuals in the community. People with higher socioeconomic status may have more power and influence in the community.

Political affiliation: Political affiliations can influence the distribution of power in a community. People who align themselves with a particular political party or ideology may have more power and influence in the community.

Gender and ethnicity: Gender and ethnicity can also affect power structures in a community. Certain ethnic groups or genders may be marginalized or underrepresented in positions of power.

Geography: The physical location of a community can also affect power structures. Communities located in urban areas may have different power structures than those in rural areas.

History and culture: The history and culture of a community can also affect power structures. Some communities may have longstanding power structures that are difficult to change, while others may be more open to change and new ideas.

Access to resources: Access to resources such as education, healthcare, and transportation can also affect power structures in a community. People who have greater access to resources may have more power and influence in the community.

These factors are often interconnected and can have a complex relationship with each other. Understanding these factors can help individuals and organizations work towards creating a more equitable and inclusive community.

28. How the different factors affect the organization structure​


Answer:

"Factors Affecting Organization Structure"

1. Strategy:

Strategy determines a course of action to direct various organizational activities. It makes plans to co-ordinate human and physical resources to work towards a common objective.

2. Technology :

The technology for manufacturing goods and services also affects the organization stricture.

3. People:

Organization structure defines work, groups it into departments and appoints people to run those departments.

4. Tasks :

(a) Skill variety:

It is the extent to which creativity and variety of skills and talents are required to do a task.

(b) Task identity:

Whether to produce a product in whole or in parts determines its task identity. When a product is produced as a whole, it has greater task identity.

(c) Task significance:

The importance of task affecting the well-being or lives of people working inside and outside the organization determines significance of the task.

(d) Autonomy:

Whether or not an individual plans the task on his own determines autonomy of

the task.

(e) Feedback:

It is the information that people receive about successful completion of their task.

6. Informal organization:

Informal organizations are and outgrowth of formal organizations Social and cultural values, religious beliefs and personal likes and dislikes of members which form informal groups cannot be overlooked by management.

7. Size:

A group known as Aston Group conducted research on firms of different sizes and concluded that as firms increase in size, the need for job specialization, standardization and decentralization also increases and organizations are structured accordingly.

8. Environment:

Organization structure cannot ignore the effects of environment. Organizations must adapt to the environment, respond to incremental opportunities and satisfy various external parties such as customers, suppliers, layout unions etc.

9. Managerial perceptions:

Organizations where top managers perceive their subordinated as active, dynamic and talented entrepreneurs, prefer organic form of structure, If they hold negative opinion about their subordinates, they prefer mechanistic organization structure.

30. Classify the factors that can affect the skin in a logically structured scheme give an example for each factor. ?​


ANSWER

The skin - the boundary interface of the human body with the environment - represents a factor of particular importance in terms of our protection from environmental factors (external factors), as well as the protection of the body's internal functions.

~These environmental factors from which the body is "protected" by the skin (because protection cannot be provided entirely by the skin, but several factors intervene) can be classified according to the nature of their action on the skin, as follows:

1. Mechanical factors

» Hits with hard objects (metals, wood, hard plastic, etc.)» Injury with sharp objects» Prolonged exposure to physical exertion - can create skin damage (bruises, scars, open wounds, etc.)

2. Physical factors:

» Electric shocks (on skin contact with current sources)» Strong radiation (eg UV radiation from prolonged exposure to strong sun in summer - can cause skin burns)» Temperature - particularly influences the skin and is an essential factor in the health of the skin.

3. Chemical factors:

» The daily substances with which we come into contact - acids, bases, salts, oxides, etc. - can cause chemical burns, severe irritations or other complications.» Experiments in laboratories - which can damage the skin if the protective equipment (gloves, glasses, gown) is not appropriate or missing.» Excessive consumption of alcohol/smoking - affects the skin over time and favors the appearance of wrinkles earlier in life.

4. Biological and environmental factors:

» Hygiene - one of the main causes of skin conditions (if it is poor) and a factor to which increased attention is served daily.» Weather conditions in the living environment:» A predominantly cold climate requires greater protection of the skin (thicker clothes, maintaining an optimal temperature in homes, etc.). » A predominantly tropical climate requires continuous hydration of the skin (to avoid dehydration or other complications).» An improper diet - manifests adverse skin reactions such as irritations, acne problems, etc.

~Therefore, skin protection is very important and requires increased attention to avoid conditions or other complications that may occur over time.
