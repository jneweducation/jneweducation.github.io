6. a sign of mass 20kg hangs from the end of a rod of length 2m and mass 4kg. a wire is attached to the end of the rod and to a point 1m above point P. find the tension in the wire and the force exerted by the wall on the rod at point P.


Answer:

To solve this problem, we need to draw a free body diagram of the rod and apply the equations of equilibrium.

Free Body Diagram:

```

T

|

|

|

W = 20 kg --------------O---O (rod)

P

```

Here, W is the weight of the sign, T is the tension in the wire, and P is the point where the rod is in contact with the wall.

Using the equations of equilibrium:

∑F_x = 0: Tsinθ = 0 (since there is no horizontal force)

∑F_y = 0: Tcosθ = W + F_P (where θ is the angle between the rod and the horizontal axis, and F_P is the force exerted by the wall on the rod at point P)

We also need to find the moment of the forces about point P and set it equal to zero to ensure that the rod is in rotational equilibrium.

∑M_P = 0: Tsinθ × 2m - W × 1m = 0

Solving for T and F_P:

From the first equation, we get sinθ = 0, so θ = 0° (or 180°, but we choose the former since it corresponds to the tension in the wire pulling upwards). Substituting this into the second equation, we get:

Tcos0° = 20 kg × 9.81 m/s² + F_P

T = (20 kg × 9.81 m/s² + F_P) / cos0°

From the third equation, we get:

Tsin0° × 2m - 20 kg × 9.81 m/s² × 1m = 0

T = (20 kg × 9.81 m/s² × 1m) / sin0° / 2m

Equating the two expressions for T:

(20 kg × 9.81 m/s² + F_P) / cos0° = (20 kg × 9.81 m/s² × 1m) / sin0° / 2m

F_P = (20 kg × 9.81 m/s² × 1m) / tan0° / 2m - 20 kg × 9.81 m/s²

T = (20 kg × 9.81 m/s² + F_P) / cos0°

Simplifying:

F_P ≈ 392.4 N

T ≈ 215.7 N

Therefore, the tension in the wire is approximately 215.7 N, and the force exerted by the wall on the rod at point P is approximately 392.4 N.

Change of Temperature (ΔT) = 0 °C → 100 °C

α = ΔL / LΔT

α = (60 + 0.102) / (60)(100 - 0)
