Hence, 19.1 L of O₂ is required for the complete conbustion of 7.64 L of C₂H₂.

20. The Lewis dot model of a molecule is shown. A molecule is shown with atoms arranged in the order HCCH. There are three horizontal lines connecting the two C atoms. The C atom on the left is connected to an H atom by a single horizontal line. The C atom on the right is connected to the other H atom by a single horizontal line. H - C = C - H (the equal sign is supposed to be 3 lines rather than 2) Based on the model, which of the following is true? A)Each carbon has three lone pairs of electrons on it. B)The octet of carbon atom remains incomplete in the molecule. C)The two carbon atoms share a total of six electrons with each other. D)The difference between the electronegativity of carbon and hydrogen is greater than 1.7.


Answer:

A, B, and C are true.

Explanation:

Lewis Dot Model of a Molecules

A Lewis dot model shows the bonding between atoms of a molecule and its corresponding lone pair of electrons, if there is any.

In the given problem, the structure of ethylene is given. Let us analyze each statement if it is true.

A. Each carbon has three lone pairs of electrons on it. - In the structure of ethylene, there is no lone pairs of electron in any of the carbon atom. You will see that there are no dots around carbon. Thus, there are no lone pairs.

B. The octet of carbon atom remains incomplete in the molecule. - The octet of both carbon atoms are complete. For example, in the first carbon, there is one pair on the left side. Hence, there are two electrons. On its right side, it has a triple bond wherein each bond has two electrons. Thus, a total of six. Electronegativity refers to the ability of an atom to attract shared electrons in a covalent bond. The higher the value of the electronegativity, the more strongly that element attracts the shared electrons.

The concept of electronegativity was introduced by Linus Pauling in 1932; on the Pauling scale, fluorine is assigned an electronegativity of 3.98, and the other elements are scaled relative to that value. Other electronegativity scales include the Mulliken scale, proposed by Robert S. Mulliken in 1934, in which the first ionization energy and electron affinity are averaged together, and the Allred-Rochow scale, which measures the electrostatic attraction between the nucleus of an atom and its valence electrons.

Electronegativity varies in a predictable way across the periodic table. Electronegativity increases from bottom to top in groups, and increases from left to right across periods. Thus, fluorine is the most electronegative element, while francium is one of the least electronegative. (Helium, neon, and argon are not listed in the Pauling electronegativity scale, although in the Allred-Rochow scale, helium has the highest electronegativity.)
