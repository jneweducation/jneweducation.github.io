5. what causes wind on top of hills and mountains


What causes wind on top of hills and mountains An anabatic wind from the Greek anabatos, an anabainein verbal meaning to fly upwards, is a warm one  Wind blowing up a steep hill or side of the mountain, powered by insolation from the hill heating.  They are also called  As upslope streaming.  These at cool, sunny weather  Winds generally occur during the day. The Sun will radiantly warm a hill or mountain top  and in effect heats up the air just above it.  Owing to the greater distance to the ground below it, air at a  comparable altitude over an adjacent valley or plain doesn't get so much warmed. If the lower lying land is shaded by the mountain  and therefore receives less sunlight, the effect can be enhanced.

At a comparable altitude around it, the air above the top of  the hill is now cooler than the surface, and will rise by convection.  This creates a lower pressure region which allows the wind  to flow into the air at the bottom of the slope.  Air rising from the tops of large mountains is normal to reach a  height where it cools down adiabatically below its dew point and forms cumulus clouds. Then these can cause  rain, or even thunderstorms.

10. A car parked at the top of a hill does not move down the hill due to what type of friction?


Answer:

Static friction is friction between two objects that are not moving relative to each other. This frictional force is what prevents a parked car from sliding down a hill, for example. Before an object at rest on a surface can move, it must overcome the force of static friction.

23. what kind of energy is in person on top of a hill​


Answer:

Potential Energy

Explanation:

Since there is no motion yet, there is no kinetic energy. This means that the work needed to get up the hill is the potential energy at the top. Once you have coasted down the hill and are at the bottom, all of the energy is kinetic. Since the bottom of the hill is considered height = 0 m, there is no potential.
