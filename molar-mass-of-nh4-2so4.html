4. How many grams of NH3 is needed to produce 50.0 g of (NH4)2SO4? 6.44g 8.67g 12.88g 194g


Answer:

mass NH₃ = 12.88 g

Explanation:

Writing the balanced chemical equation

2NH₃ + H₂SO₄ → (NH₄)₂SO₄

Calculating the molar mass of NH₃ and (NH₄)₂SO₄

For NH₃

MM NH₃ = (14.006703 g × 1) + (1.007941 g × 3)

MM NH₃ = 17.031 g

For (NH₄)₂SO₄

MM (NH₄)₂SO₄ = (14.006703 g × 2) + (1.007941 g × 8) + (32.064787 g × 1) + (15.999405 g × 4)

MM (NH₄)₂SO₄ = 132.14 g

Calculating the mass of NH₃ required

mass NH₃ = 50.0 g (NH₄)₂SO₄ × (1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄ / 132.14 g (NH₄)₂SO₄) × (2 mol NH₃ / 1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄) × (17.031 g NH₃ / 1 mol NH₃)

mass NH₃ = 12.88 g

14. The fertilizer ammonium sulfate, (NH4)2SO4, reacts with sodium hydroxide to produce sodium sulfate, water and ammonia. Determine the mass of sodium hydroxide needed to react with 15.0 g of ammonium sulfate.​


Solution:

Step 1: Write and balance the chemical equation.

(NH₄)₂SO₄ + 2NaOH → Na₂SO₄ + 2H₂O + 2NH₃

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of substances needed.

For (NH₄)₂SO₄

molar mass = (14.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 8) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 132.15 g/mol

For NaOH

molar mass = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 40.00 g/mol

Step 3: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄ : 2 mol NaOH

Step 4: Calculate the mass of NaOH needed by using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of NaOH = 15.0 g (NH₄)₂SO₄} × \frac{\text{1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄}}{\text{132.15 g (NH₄)₂SO₄}} × \frac{\text{2 mol NaOH}}{\text{1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄}} × \frac{\text{40.00 g NaOH}}{\text{1 mol NaOH}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of NaOH = 9.08 g}}[/tex]

17. Calculate the boiling point elevation in oC of an aqueous solution of 18.73% by mass of (NH4)3PO4. Express your answer in decimal form in 4 significant figures, not in scientific notation. Kb of H2O = 0.512 oC/m molar masses: H2O = 18.015 g/mol (NH4)3PO4= 149.087 g/mol


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the mass of solute [(NH₄)₃PO₄].

Assume that the mass of solution is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of solute} & = \frac{\text{percent by mass}}{100\%} \times \text{mass of solution} \\ & = \frac{18.73\%}{100\%} \times \text{100 g} \\ & = \text{18.73 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solvent (water).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of solvent} & = \text{mass of solution} - \text{mass of solute} \\ & = \text{100 g} - \text{18.73 g} \\ & = \text{81.27 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{18.73 g}}{\text{149.087 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.12563 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the molality of the solution.

The mass of solvent must be expressed in kilograms. 81.27 g = 0.08127 kg

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.12563 mol}}{\text{0.08127 kg}} \\ & = \text{1.5458 mol/kg} \\ & = 1.5458 \: m \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Determine the van't Hoff factor.

Since (NH₄)₃PO₄ is a strong electrolyte, it will dissociate completely into three NH₄⁺ and one PO₄³⁻ ions. Then, the van't Hoff factor is

i = 4

Step 6: Calculate the boiling-point elevation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \Delta T_{\text{b}} & = iK_{\text{b}}m \\ & = (4)(0.512^{\circ}\text{C/}m)(1.5458 \: m) \\ & = \boxed{3.166^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the boiling point elevation of an aqueous solution of 18.73% by mass (NH₄)₃PO₄ is 3.166°C.

23. Calculate the molar mass of ammonium hydrogen phosphate, (NH4)2 HPO4.​


✒️MOLAR MASS

[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to solve for the molar mass of ammonium hydrogen phosphate, (NH₄)₂HPO₄. To calculate molar mass, we need to add all the atomic masses in the compound. For each element, we multiply the atomic mass of the element by the number of atoms of that element in a compound. We find atomic masses in the periodic table.

Since there are two N atoms, nine H atoms, one P atom, and four O atoms, then the molar mass of ammonium hydrogen phosphate is

molar mass of (NH₄)₂HPO₄ = 2(14.01 g/mol) + 9(1.008 g/mol) + 30.97 g/mol + 4(16.00 g/mol)

[tex]\boxed{\text{molar mass of} \: (\text{NH}_{4})_{2}\text{HPO}_{4} = \text{132.06 g/mol}}[/tex]
