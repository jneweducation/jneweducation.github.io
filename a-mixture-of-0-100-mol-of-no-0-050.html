22. A reaction that i econd-order in one reactant ha a rate contant of 2. 2 x 10–2 L/(mol · ). If the initial concentration of the reactant i 0. 360 mol/L, how long will it take for the concentration to become 0. 180 mol/L?.


Answer:

To solve this problem, we need to use the rate equation for a second-order reaction:

rate = k[A]^2

where k is the rate constant, [A] is the concentration of the reactant, and rate is the rate of the reaction.

We are given that the rate constant for this reaction is 2.2 x 10^-2 L/(mol * s), and the initial concentration of the reactant is 0.360 mol/L. We are trying to find the time it takes for the concentration to become 0.180 mol/L.

We can set up the following equation to solve for the time:

rate = k[A]^2

(2.2 x 10^-2 L/(mol * s)) = (2.2 x 10^-2 L/(mol * s)) * (0.360 mol/L)^2

(2.2 x 10^-2 L/(mol * s)) = (2.2 x 10^-2 L/(mol * s)) * (0.180 mol/L)^2

We can then solve for the time by dividing both sides of the equation by the rate constant and the concentration of the reactant:

time = (2.2 x 10^-2 L/(mol * s)) / (2.2 x 10^-2 L/(mol * s)) * (0.180 mol/L)^2 / (0.360 mol/L)^2

Simplifying the equation, we get:

time = (0.180 mol/L)^2 / (0.360 mol/L)^2

Performing the calculations, we find that the time it takes for the concentration of the reactant to become 0.180 mol/L is 2.25 s.

Therefore, the solution to the problem is that it will take 2.25 s for the concentration to become 0.180 mol/L.

27. From the values of ∆H and ∆S, what would be the value of ∆G at 25.0°C? Reaction B: ∆H = -15.80 kJ/mol ∆S = - 125.0 J/K . mol ∆G = ? At what specific temperature would the ∆G be equal to zero (0); ∆G = 0? T = ? * 1 kJ/mol = 1000 J/mol * 1.0 °C = 298.0 K


1. ΔG = 21,468.75 J/mol

2. T = 126.4 K

Further explanation  

Gibbs free energy is the maximum possible work given by chemical reactions at constant pressure and temperature. Gibbs free energy can be used to determine the spontaneity of a reaction  

If the Gibbs free energy value is <0 (negative) then the chemical reaction occurs spontaneously. If the change in free energy is zero, then the chemical reaction is at equilibrium, if it is> 0, the process is not spontaneous  

Free energy of reaction (G) is the sum of its enthalpy (H) plus the product of the temperature and the entropy (S) of the system  

Can be formulated: (at any temperature)  

ΔG = ΔH-T.ΔS  

Given

∆H = -15.80 kJ/mol  = -1.580x 10⁴ J/mol

∆S = - 125.0 J/K . mol

Required

1. ∆G at 25.0°C

2. T for ∆G=0

Solution

T = 25 °C = 298.15 K

Input the value :

ΔG = -1.580x 10⁴ J/mol - (298.15 K x -125 J/K.mol)

ΔG = -1.580x 10⁴ J/mol - (-37,268.75 J/mol)

ΔG = 21,468.75 J/mol

∆G = 0

ΔH = T.ΔS  

-1.580x 10⁴ J/mol = T. - 125.0 J/K . mol

T = 126.4 K
