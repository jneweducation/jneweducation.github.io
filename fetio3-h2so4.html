7. A 33 - ml solution of sulfuric acid (H2SO4) is completely neutralized by 28 ml of 1.5 M NaOH solution. What is the concentration of H2SO4 solution ?​


The concentration of H2SO4 solution : 0.63 M

Further explanation  

Titration is a procedure for determining the concentration of a solution (analyte) by reacting with another solution whose known concentration (usually a standard solution) is called the titrant. Determination of the endpoint /equivalence point of the reaction can use indicators according to the appropriate pH range  

Titrations can be acid-base titration, depositional titration, and redox titration. An acid-base titration is the principle of neutralization of acids and bases  

Acid-base titration formula  

Ma Va. na = Mb. Vb. nb  

Ma, Mb = acid base concentration  

Va, Vb = acid base volume  

na, nb = acid base valence  

Given

Va = acid = 33 ml

Vb = base = 28 ml

Mb = 1.5 M

Required

Ma = the concentration of H2SO4 solution

Solution

na = 2 (valence 2 because it releases 2 H⁺)

nb = 1 (valence 1 because it releases 2 OH⁻)

Input the value :

Ma x 33 ml x 2 = 1.5 M x 28 ml x 1

Ma = 0.63 M

19. 1. Calculate the mole fraction of sulphuric acid (H2SO4) in 8% (w/w0 aqueous H2SO4 solution. (molar masses: H2SO4 = 98 g/mol; H2O = 18 g/mol)​


The mole fraction of Sulphuric acid H₂SO₄ : = 0.016

Further explanation  

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm), or mole fraction.  

Percent mass of the component shows the total mass of the component in the overall mass of a compound/mixture  

[tex]\tt \%mass=\dfrac{mass~solute}{mass~solution}\times 100\%[/tex]

Solution = Solute + Solvent  

The mole fraction shows the mole ratio of a substance to the mole of solution /mixture  

Can be formulated :

[tex]\tt xa=\dfrac{na}{na+nb}[/tex]  

xa + xb = 1

Given

8% (w/w) aqueous  H₂SO₄ solution

Required

the mole fraction

Solution

For 100 g solution :

H₂SO₄ = 8% x 100 g = 8 g

H₂O = 92% x 100 g = 92 g

mol H₂SO₄ :

= mass : molar mass

= 8 g : 98 g/mol

= 0.082

mol H₂O :

= 92 g : 18 g/mol

= 5.1

total mol :

= 0.082 + 5.1

= 5.182

mol fraction of H₂SO₄ :

= 0.082 : 5.182

= 0.016
