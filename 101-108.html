11. 2. In August 2004, Taipei 101 captured the record as the world's tallest building, according to the Council on Tall Buildings and Urban Habitat. Measured at a point 108 m from its base, the angle of elevation of the top of the spire is 78°. From a distance of about 95 m, the angle of elevation of the top of the roof is also 78°. a). How tall is Taipei 101 from the street level to the top of the spire? b). How tall is the spire itself?​


Answer:

y = 95 * tan 78°

= 95 * 7.74

= 734.3 m

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve this problem, we can use trigonometry and the fact that the angles of elevation to the top of the spire and the top of the roof are both 78°.

First, let's consider the angle of elevation to the top of the spire. We can draw a right triangle with one angle equal to 78° and one of the sides equal to 108 m, the distance from the base of the building to the point where the angle of elevation is measured. We can label the unknown side of this triangle as "x", which represents the height of the building from the street level to the top of the spire. The other side of the triangle, the distance from the base of the building to the point where the angle of elevation is measured, is 108 m.

[asy]

unitsize(1 cm);

pair A, B, C;

A = (0,0);

B = (108,0);

C = (0,20);

draw(A--B--C--cycle);

draw(rightanglemark(B,A,C,5));

label("$78^\circ$", (0.5,0.5));

label("$108$ m", (54,-0.5));

label("$x$", (0,10), N);

[/asy]

We can use the tangent function to relate the sides of the triangle to the angle of 78°. The tangent of an angle is equal to the opposite side divided by the adjacent side, so we can set up the following equation:

tan 78° = x / 108

We can solve for x by rearranging the terms and using the fact that the tangent of 78° is approximately 7.74:

x = 108 * tan 78°

= 108 * 7.74

= 839.52 m

So, the height of the building from the street level to the top of the spire is approximately 839.52 m.

Now, let's consider the angle of elevation to the top of the roof. We can draw a similar right triangle, with one angle equal to 78° and one side equal to 95 m, the distance from the base of the building to the point where the angle of elevation is measured. The unknown side of this triangle represents the height of the spire itself.

[asy]

unitsize(1 cm);

pair A, B, C;

A = (0,0);

B = (95,0);

C = (0,20);

draw(A--B--C--cycle);

draw(rightanglemark(B,A,C,5));

label("$78^\circ$", (0.5,0.5));

label("$95$ m", (47.5,-0.5));

label("$y$", (0,10), N);

[/asy]

We can set up a similar equation using the tangent function:

tan 78° = y / 95

Solving for y, we get:

y = 95 * tan 78°

= 95 * 7.74

= 734.3 m

So, the height of the spire itself is approximately 734.3 m.
