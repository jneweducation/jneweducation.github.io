17. Find two (2) Similarities and two (2) differences between Multinational Corporation Corporations (MNCs) and Transnational Corporations (TNCs)


Multinational Corporations (MNCs) and Transnational Corporations (TNCs) are similar in that:

1. They both operate in multiple countries and have a global presence.

2. They both have significant economic power and influence in the countries where they operate.

However, there are also differences between MNCs and TNCs, including:

1. MNCs are typically defined as companies that have operations in multiple countries, but each operation is largely independent and operates as a separate entity. In contrast, TNCs are characterized by a high degree of integration across their global operations, with decisions made centrally and implemented locally.

2. MNCs often adapt their products and marketing strategies to suit local markets, while TNCs tend to promote a standardized global image and brand identity.

Overall, the key difference between MNCs and TNCs is the level of integration and centralization of their global operations. MNCs tend to have more decentralized operations, while TNCs operate in a more integrated and centralized manner.
