Further explanation  

Determination of the enthalpy of formation of a reaction can be through a calorimetric experiment, based on the principle of Hess's Law, enthalpy of formation table, or from bond energy data  

The principle of Hess's Law is to calculate the enthalpy of a chemical reaction based on its initial and final states

A chemical reaction to produce a product can have a different step-by-step reaction, but the enthalpy change that occurs will remain the same

This reaction stage can be described in the form of an energy level diagram

ΔH₁ = ΔH₂ + ΔH₃

Given

The two chemical equations

Required

the enthalpy change

2Al (s) + 3/2O₂ (g) ⇒ Al₂O₃ (s) ; ΔH°rxn = - 1669.8 kJ/mol

the second reaction is reversed (ΔH°rxn=+) :

Fe₂O₃(s)⇒2Fe(s) + 3/2O₂(g) ; ΔH°rxn = + 822.2 kJ/mol

2 Al (s) + Fe₂O₃(s) ⇒ 2 Fe (s) +  Al₂O₃ (s) ; ΔH°rxn = -847.6 kJ/mol

14. How many grams of Al (MM = 26.98 g/mol) can be created decomposing 9.8g of Al2O3 (MM = 101.96 g/mol)? Al2O3 → Al + AlO2


✏️STOICHIOMETRY

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

2 Al₂O₃ → 4 Al + 3 O₂

Final Step: Calculate the mass of Al created by decomposing 9.8 g of Al₂O₃ using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of Al₂O₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 4 moles of Al.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of Al} & = 9.8 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3}}{101.96 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3}} \times \frac{4 \: \cancel{\text{mol Al}}}{2 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3}} \times \frac{\text{26.98 g Al}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol Al}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{5.2 g Al}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 5.2 g of Al can be created by decomposing 9.8 g of Al₂O₃.

21. Suppose 20.50 g of Al reacts with excess O2, how many grams of Al2O3 is produced?


For this problem, we first need to determine the reaction involved. That is,

Al + O2 -> Al2O3

We then need to balance the equation. Giving, 

4Al + 3O2 -> 2Al2O3

Given the mass of Al with O2 as an excess reactant. We then know that Al is the limiting reactant and the reaction will cease to proceed one all the Al has been consumed. We now compute for the moles of Al using its molecular weight.

20.50 g Al x (1 mol Al/26.982 g) = 0.759766 moles Al

Determining the mole-to-mole ratio of Al to Al2O3 based on the balanced equation and knowing the moles of Al available to react, we can now compute for the moles Al2O3 produced which we can further convert to mass using its molecular weight.

0.759766 moles Al x (2 mole Al2O3/4 moles Al) = 0.37988 moles Al2O3
0.37988 moles Al2O3 x ( 101.96 g/ 1 mole Al2O3) = 38.73 g Al2O3

Therefore, reacting 20.50 g of Al with excess O2 will produce 38.73 g Al2O3

30. Suppose synthesis of Al2O3 is done in the lab using 20.50 g of Al and excess of O2. Upon purification, 35.48 grams of Al2O3 is produced. What is the percent yield of Al2O3?


The percent yield of Al₂O₃ : 91.6%

Further explanation

Percent yield is the compare of the amount of product obtained from a reaction with the amount you calculated

General formula:

 [tex]\rm percent~yield=\dfrac{actual~yield}{theoretical~yield}\times 100\%[/tex]

The actual yield is the amount of product actually produced by the reaction. Theoretical yield is the amount of product that you calculate from the reaction equation according to the product and reactant coefficients or the maximum amount of product that can be produced from the reactant.

Synthesis of Al₂O₃ is using 20.50 g of Al and excess of O₂.

35.48 grams of Al₂O₃ is produced.

Reaction

Al + O₂ ⇒ Al₂O₃

We determine the mole of the compound in the reaction

[tex]\rm mole~Al=\dfrac{mass}{atomic~mass}\\\\mole~Al=\dfrac{20.5}{27}=0.7592\\\\mole~Al_2O_3=\dfrac{2}{4}\times mole~Al=\dfrac{2}{4}\times 0.7592=0.3796 \\\\Then~mass~Al_2O_3=0.3796\times 102(molar~mass)=38.7192~gr\\\\percent~yield=\dfrac{35.48}{38.7192}=0.916=\boxed{\bold{91.6\%}}[/tex]
