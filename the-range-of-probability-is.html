4. Suppose that the heights of male basketball players are normally distributed with an average of 70 inches and a variance of 4. What is the probability that a randomly selected male basketball player has a height that is in the range 69 inches to 72 inches?


PROBLEM:

Suppose that the heights of male basketball players are normally distributed with an average of 70 inches and a variance of 4. What is the probability that a randomly selected male basketball player has a height that is in the range 69 inches to 72 inches?

SOLUTION:

Step 1: Identify the parts of the word problem. The word problem will identify:

The mean (average or μ) — 70 inches

Standard deviation (σ) — 2

Standard deviation is the square root of variance. [tex]\rm \sqrt{4} = 2 [/tex]

X: the numbers associated with “between” — 68 inches and 73 inches

The height is in the range 69 inches to 72 inches thus they are between 68 and 73 inches.

Step 2: Figure out the z-scores and find the area in the z-table. Plug the X values into the z value formula and solve. The μ (the mean), is 70 inches.

[tex]\small\underline{\boxed{z=\frac{X-μ}{σ}}}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{68-70}{2}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{-2}{2}[/tex]

[tex]z=-1=0.34134[/tex]

[tex]\small\underline{\boxed{z=\frac{X-μ}{σ}}}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{73-70}{2}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{3}{2}[/tex]

[tex]z=1.5=0.43319[/tex]

See z-table and the graph (illustration) on the attached pictures.

Step 3: Add the computed z-scores to get the probability or area between -1 and 1.5. You can also convert them in percent.

[tex]\boxed{0.34134+0.43319=0.77453=77.45\%}[/tex]

ANSWER:The probability that a randomly selected male basketball player has a height that is in the range 69 inches to 72 inches is 0.77453.

15. if x is the random variable with an elements then what is the range of each probabilities of p(x)​


Answer:

The range of probabilities of p(x) depends on the possible values of the random variable x and the probability distribution function of x.

If x is a discrete random variable, then the range of probabilities of p(x) will consist of probabilities assigned to each possible value of x. In this case, the sum of all probabilities assigned to each possible value of x will be equal to 1.

For example, let's say x is the random variable representing the number of heads obtained when tossing a fair coin twice. The possible values of x are 0, 1, or 2. The probability distribution of x is given by:

p(0) = 1/4

p(1) = 1/2

p(2) = 1/4

The range of probabilities of p(x) is {1/4, 1/2}, which are the probabilities assigned to each possible value of x.

If x is a continuous random variable, then the range of probabilities of p(x) will consist of probabilities assigned to each interval of values of x. In this case, the integral of p(x) over the entire range of possible values of x will be equal to 1.

For example, let's say x is the random variable representing the height of students in a class, and it follows a normal distribution with mean 170 cm and standard deviation 10 cm. The probability distribution of x is given by:

p(x) = (1 / (10 * sqrt(2*pi))) * exp(-(x-170)^2 / (2 * 10^2))

The range of probabilities of p(x) is the interval [0,1], and the integral of p(x) over the entire range of possible values of x (i.e., from negative infinity to positive infinity) is equal to 1.

21. The average age of a company's president is 56 years old. Assume the variable is normally distributed. If the standard deviation is four years, find the probability that the age of the randomly selected president in the following range: between 52 and 58.​


Answer:

0.383 or 38.3%

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the probability that the age of the randomly selected president is between 52 and 58, we need to standardize the range of values using the standard deviation and mean:

z1 = (52 - 56) / 4 = -1

z2 = (58 - 56) / 4 = 0.5

We can now look up the probabilities associated with these z-values in a standard normal distribution table or use a calculator with a normal distribution function. The probability of a z-value between -1 and 0.5 is approximately 0.383, or 38.3%.

Therefore, the probability that the age of the randomly selected president is between 52 and 58 is 0.383 or 38.3%
