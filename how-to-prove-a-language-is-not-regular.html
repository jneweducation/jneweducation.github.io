26. How would you differentiate literal langauge from figurative language​


Answer:

Literal language uses words exactly according to their conventionally accepted meanings or denotation. Figurative (or non-literal) language uses words in a way that deviates from their conventionally accepted definitions in order to convey a more complicated meaning or heightened effect

30. Examine how prose and poetry differ from one another: language use

Prose refers to a form of literature having ordinary language and sentence structure. Prose are straight forward and informative. On the other hand, poetry are expressive or decorated which is aesthetic by nature because it has sound, cadence, rhyme, metre that adds to its meaning.
