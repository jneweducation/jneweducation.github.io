29. science: List down the changes that occur in a developing baby every month until it is born, 1st month to 9th months,Ty very much, asap, nonsense answer report.​


Answer

Sure, here are the changes that occur in a developing baby each month until it is born:

1st month:

- The fertilized egg implants in the uterus and begins to develop into an embryo

- The placenta begins to form

- The neural tube, which will eventually become the brain and spinal cord, begins to form

2nd month:

- The embryo begins to look more like a human, with the formation of head, body, arms, and legs

- The heart begins to beat and pump blood

- Facial features, such as eyes and ears, begin to form

- The nervous system continues to develop

3rd month:

- The embryo is now called a fetus

- The fetus can move its limbs and can swallow and urinate

- Fingers and toes are fully formed

- The sex of the fetus can be determined

- Internal organs continue to develop

4th month:

- The fetus grows to be about 6 inches long

- Lanugo, a fine hair that covers the body, begins to grow

- The fetus can hear and may respond to sound

- The digestive system begins to function

- The skin becomes less transparent

5th month:

- The fetus grows to be about 10 inches long

- The mother may feel the fetus moving for the first time

- The bones begin to harden

- The fetus develops a sense of taste and may be able to taste amniotic fluid

6th month:

- The fetus grows to be about 12 inches long

- The eyes begin to open

- The lungs begin to produce surfactant, a substance that helps the lungs inflate

- The fetus may develop a regular sleep-wake cycle

7th month:

- The fetus grows to be about 14 inches long

- Fat begins to accumulate under the skin

- The nervous system continues to develop

- The fetus may respond to touch and sound more strongly

8th month:

- The fetus grows to be about 18 inches long

- The brain continues to develop rapidly

- The fetus may turn head-down in preparation for birth

- The fetus may begin to move lower in the uterus

9th month:

- The fetus is fully developed and ready for birth

- The lungs are fully mature and can function independently

- The fetus may weigh between 6 and 9 pounds and be about 20 inches long

- The mother may experience contractions and go into labor, leading to the birth of the baby.
