16. EM WAVES ACTIVITY 1. A photon has a frequency of 3.4 x 107 Hz. Calculate its energy. 2. A photon of light has a wavelength of 4X10-8m. Calculate its energy. 3. Calculate the wavelength and energy of light that has a frequency of 1.5 x 1015 Hz.​


Answer:

1. To calculate the energy of a photon, you can use the equation E=hf, where E is the energy of the photon, h is the Planck constant (6.63 x 10^-34 J*s) and f is the frequency of the photon. Plugging in the given values, we get:

E = (6.63 x 10^-34 J*s) * (3.4 x 10^7 Hz)

E = 2.27 x 10^-26 J

2. To calculate the energy of a photon using its wavelength, you can use the equation E=hc/λ, where E is the energy of the photon, h is the Planck constant (6.63 x 10^-34 J*s), c is the speed of light (2.998 x 10^8 m/s) and λ is the wavelength of the photon. Plugging in the given values, we get:

E = (6.63 x 10^-34 J*s) * (2.998 x 10^8 m/s) / (4 x 10^-8 m)

E = 3.99 x 10^-19 J

3. To calculate the wavelength of light with a given frequency, you can use the equation λ=c/f, where λ is the wavelength, c is the speed of light (2.998 x 10^8 m/s) and f is the frequency of the light. Plugging in the given values, we get:

λ = (2.998 x 10^8 m/s) / (1.5 x 10^15 Hz)

λ = 2 x 10^-7 m

To calculate the energy of the light with this wavelength, you can use the equation E=hc/λ, where E is the energy of the photon, h is the Planck constant (6.63 x 10^-34 J*s), c is the speed of light (2.998 x 10^8 m/s) and λ is the wavelength of the photon. Plugging in the given values, we get:

E = (6.63 x 10^-34 J*s) * (2.998 x 10^8 m/s) / (2 x 10^-7 m)

E = 4.99 x 10^-19 J
