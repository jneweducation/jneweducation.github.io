11. A box with 10 kilograms and a force of 20N acting to the left of the box, and a force of 5N acting to the right of the box, ignoring friction on the floor, what is the acceleration of the box?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F_{net} = ma}[/tex]

where:

[tex]F_{net} =[/tex] net force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the net force

[tex]F_{net} = F_{left} - F_{right}[/tex]

[tex]F_{net} = \text{20 N} - \text{5 N}[/tex]

[tex]F_{net} = \text{15 N}[/tex]

Solving for the acceleration

[tex]F_{net} = ma[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{F_{net}}{m} = \dfrac{ma}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{F_{net}}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{15 N}}{\text{10 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{1.5 m/}\text{s}^{2} \: \text{to the left}}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of the box is 1.5 m/s² to the left.
