26. What is your opinion of the present fringe benefits for employees? Do you think the companies are becoming too liberal in the grant of these benefits?


Fringe benefit, any nonwage payment or benefit (e.g., pension plans, profit-sharing programs, vacation pay, and company-paid life, health, and unemployment insurance programs) granted to employees by employers. It may be required by law, granted unilaterally by employers, or obtained through collective bargaining. Employers’ payments for fringe benefits are included in employee-compensation costs and therefore are not usually liable to corporate income tax. If the cost of fringe benefits were paid directly as wages, workers would pay personal income tax on this amount and therefore have less to spend on such benefits as they might elect to furnish for themselves. Thus, with the same amount of money, employers can obtain more benefits for employees and can also take advantage of lower group rates for insurance.
