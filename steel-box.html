2. A steel box has a mass of 108 g with a volume of 6 cubic meters. What is the density of the box


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The density of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{d = \frac{m}{V}}[/tex]

where:

d = density

m = mass

V = volume

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for d

Note: 108 g = 0.108 kg

[tex]d = \dfrac{m}{V}[/tex]

[tex]d = \dfrac{\text{0.108 kg}}{\text{6 m}^{3}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{d = \text{0.018 kg/}\text{m}^{3}}[/tex]

Therefore, the density of the steel box is 0.018 kg/m³.
