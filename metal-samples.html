11. A 100.0 g Sample of metal is warmed 20.0°C when 60.0 cal is added. what is the specific heat of this metal?HELP​


Answer:

0.03 cal/ g °celcius

Explanation:

Calculating specific heat

The given are mass of the metal of 100 grams, temperature change of 20°celcius and heat produced of 60 cal. We need to know the specific heat of the metal.

Step 1: We will use the formula that relates heat (q), specific heat (Cp), mass (m), and change in temperature (ΔT) as follows:

q = mCpΔT

Step 2: Since the specific heat is the unknown variable. We will rearrange the formula equating to the specific heat. Thus,

Cp = [tex]\frac{q}{ m (ΔT)}[/tex]

Step 3: Substitute the given values to our derived formula.

Cp = [tex]\frac{60 cal}{100 grams x 20 deg celcius}[/tex]

Cp = 0.03 cal/ g °C

Therefore,  the specific heat of the metal is 0.03 cal/ g °C.

18. How many moles of Cu are in a 500 g sample of the pure metal?


Given:

[tex]\text{mass of Cu = 500 g}[/tex]

Required:

moles of Cu

Solution:

Step 1: Determine the molar mass of Cu.

According to the periodic table of elements, the atomic mass of Cu is 63.55 amu. Therefore, the molar mass (mass of 1 mole) of Cu is 63.55 g.

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Cu by using dimensional analysis (factor-label method).

[tex]\text{moles of Cu = 500 g Cu} × \frac{\text{1 mol Cu}}{\text{63.55 g Cu}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of Cu = 7.87 mol}}[/tex]

Another Solution:

Step 1: Determine the molar mass of Cu.

According to the periodic table of elements, the atomic mass of Cu is 63.55 amu. Therefore, the molar mass (mass of 1 mole) of Cu is 63.55 g.

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Cu by using the formula for the number of moles of any substance.

[tex]\text{moles of Cu} = \frac{\text{mass of Cu}}{\text{molar mass of Cu}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of Cu} = \frac{\text{500 g}}{\text{63.55 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of Cu = 7.87 mol}}[/tex]

29. a 50g sample of an unknown metal warms from 18 °c to 58°c after absorbing 800 joules. What is the specific heat of the metal?​


SPECIFIC HEAT

Given

Heat Energy (Q) = 800 JChange of temperature (ΔT) = 18 °C → 58 °CMass (m) = 50 kgSpecific Heat Capacity (c) = ?

Use the derived formula of heat to calculate the specific heat capacity.

c = Q / mΔT

c = 800 / (50)(58 - 18)

c = 800 / (50)(40)

c = 800 / 2000

c = 0.4 J / g °C
