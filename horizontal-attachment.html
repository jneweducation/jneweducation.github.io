7. A man pulls a box to a distance of 5 m along a horizontal floor through a cord attached to the box which makes an angle of 30° with the horizontal. How much work is done, if the force exerted on the cord is 60N?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at an angle θ and at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd \: \cos \theta}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

θ = angle of the force with the horizontal

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = Fd cos θ

W = (60 N)(5 m)(cos 30°)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{259.81 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 259.81 J.

9. 1. A 0.15 kg ball attached to the end of a horizontal cord is revolved in a circle of radius 0.50 m and the ball angular speed is 8 m/s. What is the centripetal force?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The centripetal force exerted on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \frac{mv^{2}}{r}}[/tex]

where:

Fc = centripetal force

m = mass

v = velocity

r = radius

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for Fc

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{mv^{2}}{r}[/tex]

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{(\text{0.15 kg})(\text{8 m/s})^{2}}{\text{0.50 m}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \text{19.2 N}}[/tex]

Therefore, the centripetal force is 19.2 N.

14. A horizontal spring with kinetic force of 400 N/m and a mass of 1.40 kg attached to it is undergoing simple harmonic motion. Calculate the (a) period, (b) frequency, and (c) angular frequency of this oscillator.​


Answer:

The period of a simple harmonic oscillator is given by T = 2π√(m/k), where m is the mass and k is the spring constant.

Substituting the given values, the period of this oscillator is calculated to be T = 2π√(1.40/400) = 0.2080 seconds.

The frequency of the oscillator is then simply calculated by f = 1/T = 1/0.2080 = 4.8148 Hz.

Finally, the angular frequency is calculated by w = 2πf = 2π * 4.8148 = 30.05 rad/s.

27. A 25 kg child sits in a swing that is attached to ropes 1.6 m long. Find the potential energy of the child relative to his lowest position when the ropes (a) are horizontal, (b) and are vertical.


Answer:

The potential energy of the child when the ropes

a) are horizontal is 235.2 J

b) are vertical is 0 J

c) make 30° with the horizontal is 117.6 J

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the potential energy of the child. To do this, I have given an illustration below for some analysis.

For the formula, we use:

PE = mgh

where

PE     is the potential energy, unit is in Joules, J

m      is the given mass, unit is in kg

g       is the gravity, 9.8 m/s²

h       is the height lifting the child by the swing, unit is in meter, m

For the given information

m = 15 kg

a) h = 1.6 m

c) θ = 30°

Solving the problem

a) Let us solve the potential energy of the child relative to lowest position of the ropes.

Here, when the ropes are horizontal, it means that the angle created is 180° below the horizontal or the child's height is at 1.6 meters or maximum height. Therefore, the potential energy is:

PE = mgh

PE = (15 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(1.6 m)

PE = 235.2 J

b) Now, let us solve for the potential energy when the ropes are vertical. This means that the height of the child relative to lowest position of the ropes is zero. Therefore, the potential energy is:

PE = mgh

PE = 0 J

c) Finally, when the ropes make 30° with the horizontal, the child was lifted at a certain height. So, let us solve for that height.

From the given illustration below, let us solve first for y, we have:

Sin 30° = y / 1.6

y = 1.6 Sin 30°

y = 0.8 m

Now, let us solve for h (child's height)

h = length of the rope - y

h = 1.6 m - 0.8 m

h = 0.8 m

Now, we are ready to solve for the potential energy, we have:

PE = mgh

PE = (15 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(0.8 m)

PE = 117.6 J
