20. The transistor amplifier is said to be saturated if a change in base current does not change the collector current. a. true b. false


A. True

As the base current increases the collector increases proprtionally till the operating point reaches the boundary between the active region and the saturation region. At this point the collector current will be equal to collector saturation current.

If one increases the base current further, the collector current clamps at this point since the transistor is saturated and the most of the power supply voltage will be dropped on the collector load resistance.

This means while the transitor amplifier to be saturated in base, the collector current will remain unchanged because that the transistor enters the saturation region.

22. Explain the transistor in how it work and uses


Answer:

Transistor is an electronic device made of semiconductors.

Explanation:

A simple transistor has 3 pins, collector, emitter, and base.

With emiter to the ground, the base voltage can control the collector voltage.

It can also be said the base current controls the collector-emitter current.

The basic uses of the transisor are:

1. As switch,  the transistor can be turned  on or off.  This used to form logic gates. Logic gates can perform a simple computations like addition, etc.  And with several gates you can do more even more complex computations we can call a process. Now a machine processing several of these computations is called a processor that powers your basic computer. Do you know that the brain inside you cellphone is based on millions of transistors crammed into a space not smaller than the nail in your pinky?  The Snapdragon 835,  a cellphone processor or cpu ,  has 3 billion  on an area 72.3 mm². And that was in 2017

2. As an amplifier, A transisor amplifying both voltage and current is called a power amplifier.  A small input voltage and current can be output a larger proportional power. Any signal can be sent in to the transistor and come out with a higher power on the other side.  Uses include sound systems where your voice in the mic is amplified and sent out louder to the speakers, and wireless communications like wifi, bluetooth, and radio stations where the the antenna signal is amplified so that even your radio at home can receive the signal so you can hear your favorite artist sing.
