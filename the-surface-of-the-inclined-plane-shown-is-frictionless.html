The work done by an object of mass m on a frictionless inclined plane with an angle θ and at a length of an inclined plane d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = mgd \: \sin \theta}[/tex]

where:

W = work

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

d = length of the inclined plane

θ = angle of repose of the inclined plane

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the work

W = mgd sin θ

W = (1 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(10 m)(sin 30°)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{49 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 49 J.

4. A 15 lb block slides down a plane inclined at 300 to the horizontal. Find the acceleration of the block: (a) if the plane is frictionless (b) if the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4.


Answer:

a. The acceleration for a frictionless surface is 4.9 m/s²

b. The acceleration with kinetic friction is 1.51 m/s²

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to apply Newton's 2nd law of motion to solve for the acceleration of the block.

For the formula, will use:

a. F = mg sin θ

b. F = mg sin θ - fk

where

F     is the resultant force, the unit is in Newtons, N

m    is the mass of the block, the unit is in kg

θ     is the inclined angle

fk     is the kinetic friction

Given information

m = 15 lb

θ = 30°

μk = 0.4

a = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve the acceleration if the plane is frictionless using the given formula

F = mg sin θ

Applying 2nd Newton's law of motion

ma = mg sin θ

Simplify the equation

a = g sin 30°

Then substitute the given data

a = (9.8) sin 30°

a = 4.9 m/s²

b. Now, let us solve the acceleration if the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4

F = mg sin θ - fk

Take note that the kinetic friction fk = μN where N is the normal force

ma = mg sin θ - μN

The normal force in an inclined plane is N = mg cos θ

ma = mg sin θ - μmg cos θ

ma = m (g sin θ - μg cos θ)

Simplify the equation

a = g sin θ - μg cos θ

Then substitute the given data

a = 9.8 sin 30° - 0.4(9.8) cos 30°

a = 1.51 m/s²

Therefore, the accelerations of the inclined plane are 4.9 m/s² and 1.51 m/s² respectively.

INCLINED PLANE

An inclined plane is a rigid sloping surface over which heavy loads can be raised or lowered to a certain height or depth. 23. how does the surface of an inclined plane affect doing work?


Answer:

The sloping surface of the inclined plane supports part of the weight of the object as it moves up the slope. As a result, it takes less force to move the object uphill. The trade-off is that the object must be moved over a greater distance than if it were moved straight up to the higher elevation.
