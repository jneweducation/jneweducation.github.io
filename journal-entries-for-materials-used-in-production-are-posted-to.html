4. It is a good practice to post all the debits of all the journal entries of the month before posting all the credits of the same period.


No, it is not a good practice because entries are recorded chronologically, hence, posting should also follow similar procedure.

Also, posting an entry, both debits and credits maintain the equality of account balances or debits and credits. This will help you detect posting errors. If you have not yet finished your posting, your debits is more than your credits, hence, the equality of debits and credits is not maintained.

In a computerized system, most system will not accept a debit posting without credit posting.

Answer:

The second step in the cycle is the creation of journal entries for each transaction. Point of sale technology can help to combine steps one and two, but companies must also track their expenses. The choice between accrual and cash accounting will dictate when transactions are officially recorded.

The second step in the cycle is the creation of journal entries for each transaction

Posting

Once a transaction is recorded as a journal entry, it should post to an account in the general ledger.

Step 4: Unadjusted Trial Balance

At the end of the accounting period, a trial balance is calculated as the fourth step in the accounting cycle.

Step 5: Worksheet

Analyzing a worksheet and identifying adjusting entries make up the fifth step in the cycle.

Step 6: Adjusting Journal Entries

In the sixth step, a bookkeeper makes adjustments.

Step 7: Financial Statements

After the company makes all adjusting entries, it then generates its financial statements in the seventh step.

Step 8: Closing the Books

Finally, a company ends the accounting cycle in the eighth step by closing its books at the end of the day on the specified closing date.

28. what is the difference between simple journal entry and compound journal entry?​


Answer:

Compound journal entry is an accounting entry which affects three or more account heads. A simple journal entry has just two rows i.e. one debit and one credit, whereas a compound journal entry has three or more rows.
