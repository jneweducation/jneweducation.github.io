16. The amount of illumination I is inversely proportional to the square of the distance d from a light source. If 70 foot-candles of illumination is measured 2 feet away from a lamp, what level of illumination might we expect foot away from the lamp?​


Step-by-step explanation:

The given information can be expressed as the equation: I ∝ 1/d^2, where I is the illumination and d is the distance from the light source.

We are given that I = 70 foot-candles when d = 2 feet, so we can set up an equation to solve for the value of I when d = 1 foot:

I ∝ 1/d^2

I = 70 foot-candles

d = 2 feet

Substituting these values into the equation, we get:

I = (70 foot-candles) * (1/2^2)

= 70 * 1/4

= 17.5 foot-candles

Therefore, the level of illumination we might expect 1 foot away from the lamp is 17.5 foot-candles.
