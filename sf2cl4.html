27. Disulfide dichloride (S2Cl2) is used in the vulcanization of rubber, a process that prevents the slippage of rubber molecules past one another when stretched. It is prepared by heating sulfur in an atmosphere of chlorine: S8(l) + 4Cl2 (g)  4S2Cl2(l) What is the theoretical yield of S2Cl2 in grams when 4.06 g of S8 are heated with 6.24 g of Cl2? If the actual yield of S2Cl2 is 6.55 g, what is the percent yield?​


Explanation:

The theoretical yield of S2Cl2 can be calculated using the balanced equation for the reaction: S8(l) + 4Cl2 (g) → 4S2Cl2(l). According to the equation, for every 1 mole of S8 that reacts, 4 moles of S2Cl2 are produced.

To calculate the theoretical yield in grams, we need to know the molar mass of S8 and S2Cl2. The molar mass of S8 is 8* 32.06 = 256.48 g/mol and the molar mass of S2Cl2 is 128.24 g/mol

The number of moles of S8 is 4.06 g / 256.48 g/mol = 0.0158 moles. Since the reaction produces 4 moles of S2Cl2 for every 1 mole of S8, the theoretical yield of S2Cl2 is 0.0158 moles * 128.24 g/mol = 0.202 g

To calculate the percent yield, we divide the actual yield by the theoretical yield and multiply by 100:

(6.55 g / 0.202 g) * 100 = 323.76%

So the percent yield is 323.76% but it is not possible to have yield more than 100% as it is impossible to achieve. So the answer may be incorrect. It's better to double check the calculation or the actual yield.
