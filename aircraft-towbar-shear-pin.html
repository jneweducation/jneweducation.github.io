22. a. Two strips of metal are pinned together as shown, with a rod of 10mm diameter. The Ultimate shear stress for the rod is 60MPa. Determine the maximum force required to break the pin


Answer:

4.71kN

Explanation:

The ultimate shear stress in the pin is deduced from the equation below:

[tex]\tau = \frac{F}{A}[/tex]

Where F is the maximum force applied and A is the area of the pin

The are of the pin is calculated from

[tex]\pi r^{2}[/tex] where r is the radius of the pin which is (10/2)mm = 5mm in our case

[tex]A = \pi (5\times10^{-3}) = 7.85\times10^{-5}m^{2}[/tex]

Therefore [tex]F = A\times\tau[/tex]

[tex]F = 60\times10^{6}\times7.85\times10^{-5} = 4710N = 4.71kN[/tex]

27. Determine the average shear stress develop in pins A and B of the smooth two‐tine grapple that supports the log having a mass of 3Mg. Each pin has diameter of 25mm and is subjected to double shear. someone help me solve this


Answer:

[tex]\mathsf{\tau=138.13 \ MPa}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

W = mg

[tex]\mathsf{W=(3\times10^3 \ kg)(9.81 \ m/s^2)}[/tex]

W = 29430 N = 29.43 kN

[tex]\\[/tex]

From Free-Body Diagram 1:

[tex]\mathsf{{\uparrow}+ \ \Sigma{F} = 0;}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{2(Fsin(30^\circ))-29.43=0}[/tex]

F = 29.43 kN

[tex]\\[/tex]

From Free-Body Diagram 2:

[tex]\mathsf{{\Sigma}M_E=0; (clockwise: \ positive)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{29.43cos(30^\circ)(1.2)-29.43sin(30^\circ)(0.4cos(30^\circ))-P_Bcos(20^\circ)(0.2)=0}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{P_B=135.61 \ kN}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{P=P_A=P_B=P_C=P_D=135.61 \ kN}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

From Free-Body Diagram 3:

[tex]\mathsf{2V = P}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{P}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{135.61}{2}}[/tex]

V = 67.805 kN

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{{\tau}=\dfrac{67.805\times10^3}{\dfrac{\pi}{4}(25)^2}}[/tex]           (Note: N/mm² = MPa)

[tex]\mathsf{\tau=138.13 \ MPa}[/tex]


28. tape measure is?Meter or yard stick is?Ruler is?dressmaking shears is?tracing wheel is?ball point is?pin cushion is?needle threader is?thimble is?​


Answer:

Tape measure is- a flexible ruler used to measure size or distance.

Meter or yard stick is- A yard stick is 36 inches, which is the customary system of measurement. A meter stick is 100 centimeters, which is the metric system of measurement. A yard stick is a little shorter than a meter stick.

Ruler is- a tool or device used to measure length and draw straight lines.

Dressmaking shears is-heavy duty scissors that are designed specifically with the needs of seamstresses in mind.

Tracing wheel is- A tracing wheel, also known as a pattern wheel, pounce wheel, and dart wheel, is an instrument with multiple teeth on a wheel attached to a handle. The teeth can be either serrated or smooth.

Ball point is- a type of pen with a tiny metal ball at its very tip.

Pin cushion is- A pincushion is a small, stuffed cushion, typically 3–5 cm across, which is used in sewing to store pins or needles with their heads protruding to take hold of them easily,

Needle threader is- a device for helping to put thread through the eye of a needle.

Thimble is- a metal or plastic cap with a closed end, worn to protect the finger and push the needle in sewing.
