Explanation:

character vs character:

this is when the character's problem/conflict is due to another character (ex.: the main character got in a fight with another character and that's the main problem/conflict in the text)

character vs self:

this is when the character's problem/conflict revolves around their state of mind.(ex.: internal conflicts, psychological, the conflict happens inside their head)

character vs nature:

this is when the conflict was not caused by another character or by themselves but rather, by nature or environment (ex.: natural disasters)

character vs society:

this is when society or the people around the character are the ones causing the conflict. (ex: prejudice of society toward the character, actions or ideas that the character has but society can't accept).

16. explain the covenantal character of the two nature of christ​


Answer:

The covenantal character of the two natures of Christ refers to the belief that Jesus Christ, as both fully human and fully divine, entered into a covenant with God the Father on behalf of humanity. This covenantal relationship is often referred to as the covenant of grace, which involves God's promise to provide salvation to humanity through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ. The two natures of Christ, divine and human, are seen as essential components of this covenant, with Christ as the mediator between God and humanity. Through his death and resurrection, Christ fulfilled the covenant and provided salvation for all who believe in him. This covenantal understanding of Christ's nature is a central tenet of Christian theology, particularly within the Reformed and Presbyterian traditions

19. the leading character fight the forces of nature.​


Answer:

A force of nature characters are as unstoppable as a hurricane or a flood – all you can do is get out of the way while they change the world around them. They are who they are, unaffected by the characters around them, and they do what has to be done.

Unsurprisingly, these characters are very common in action films.

One canonical example of this is James Bond. Although some tweaks have (thankfully) been made to the formula in recent years, the classic James Bond is a definition of a force of nature: he beats the bad guy, orders a vodka martini, and saves the day. He invariably ends the film in the arms of one lovely young llady, and half the time he starts the next film in the arms of another one. Bond just is. The films work in almost any order because Bond is fundamentally unchanging.

23. i want you to search and read what is character vs nature and character vs supernatural​


Answer:

What is character vs nature?

To put simply, it means that your personality is what you make of yourself in life; while your natural abilities are more or less set into stone at birth. The most obvious example being height - many people would love to be a little taller but will never achieve anything because their body won't allow them to grow any taller. But if someone else were to get bitten by a radioactive spider they could have super powers like a spider then use those skills to do amazing things with their lives. Your character is how much control you can exert over yourself, and it's limited only by your own imagination and goals in life. On the other hand, nature has its hands on you from day one until death and nothing can change some of these fundamental traits, such as eye color and hair length. A person can still try to influence his/her behavior though. This is why a good parent doesn't just teach their kids what to do or not do, but rather helps guide them towards making the right choices for themselves.

What is character vs supernatural​?

In contrast to my previous answer, there is something called 'supernatural ability'. These are skills or abilities gained after birth, often granted by magic or gods. The most common example of this in fantasy would be those who gain magical abilities like mages and witches but even other things like strength and speed can be considered part of this category as well. Even if these gifts aren't given to us directly, like being able to run fast, then there are still ways to acquire them; by training ourselves in different activities and practicing them every day.
