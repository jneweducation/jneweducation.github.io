Types of Thai Silk

Thai silk has many unique characteristics, including a tightly-woven fabric, fine grain and lustrous colours that do not fade after washing. There are also many different traditional styles or designs that mark true Thai silk, including:

Mudmee – Known for its complex geometric patterns and often using two colours, mudmee is made using a weaving method called Silk threads are dyed prior to weaving so that the vertical warp provides the fabric’s colour while the horizontal weft provides the pattern.

Plain Weave – Plain weave silk uses uniform threads to produce an even, consistent and smooth finish. Often two colours are woven together to create a two-toned shine.

Dupion – For dupion, threads of varying thickness with knots or lumps are used for a more organic, textured finish.

Brocade – Brocade patterns are crafted using extra silk thread during the weaving process to produce an embossed or embroidered texture. This is the most complicated type of Thai silk to produce.

Answer:

The notation "EA || AC" typically means that line EA is parallel to line AC, rather than perpendicular. Similarly, "FD || DB" means that line FD is parallel to line DB. If two lines are perpendicular, the notation would typically be something like "EA ⊥ AC" or "FD ⊥ DB".

If EA ⊥ AC and FD ⊥ DB, then the angle formed between the two lines would be a right angle, which is defined as an angle of 90 degrees. This follows from the definition of perpendicular lines, which states that two lines are perpendicular if they intersect at a right angle.

20. difference between youth civic engagement and leadership civic engagement​


Answer:

Youth civic engagement refers to the participation and involvement of young people in civic activities and decision-making processes that affect their communities. This can include volunteering, participating in community projects, or becoming involved in political campaigns.

Leadership civic engagement, on the other hand, refers to the development and demonstration of leadership skills in the context of civic engagement. This can include taking on leadership roles in community organizations, running for office, or leading grassroots movements. The focus is more on developing leadership skills and taking initiative in creating positive change in the community.

Explanation:

In summary, youth civic engagement focuses on the participation and involvement of young people in civic activities, while leadership civic engagement focuses on the development and demonstration of leadership skills in the context of civic engagement.
