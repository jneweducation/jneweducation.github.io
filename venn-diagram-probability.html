Problem: In a class of 30 students, 20 students play basketball and 15 students play volleyball. If 10 students play both sports, what is the probability that a randomly selected student plays either basketball or volleyball?

Solution:

To solve this problem, we can use a Venn diagram to visualize the number of students who play basketball, volleyball, or both sports.

Based on the information given, we can create the following Venn diagram:

```

Basketball (20)

/ \

/ \

Volleyball (15) Both (10)

\ /

\ /

Neither (5)

```

To find the probability that a randomly selected student plays either basketball or volleyball, we need to add the number of students who play basketball and the number of students who play volleyball (excluding the students who play both sports) and then divide by the total number of students in the class.

Number of students who play basketball only = 20 - 10 = 10

Number of students who play volleyball only = 15 - 10 = 5

Number of students who play either basketball or volleyball = 10 + 5 + 10 = 25

Therefore, the probability that a randomly selected student plays either basketball or volleyball is:

P(Basketball or Volleyball) = 25/30 = 5/6

Thus, the probability of a mutually inclusive event can be calculated using the formula:

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)

where P(A) is the probability of event A, P(B) is the probability of event B, and P(A and B) is the probability of both events happening together.
