17. starting from rest, an airplane reach a velocity of 71.0m/s for take off. if the runway is 1.0 km long, what must be the constant acceleration of an airplane?


We can use this formula to solve the problem:

[tex]v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2ad[/tex]

where [tex]v_f[/tex] is the final velocity,

[tex]v_i[/tex] is the initial velocity,

[tex]a[/tex] is the acceleration, and

[tex]d[/tex] is the distance the airplain covered,

First thing we need to do is express the equation in terms of [tex]a[/tex], since we're interested in finding the acceleration.

Remember that the airplane started from rest, so the initial velocity, [tex]v_i[/tex], will be 0.

So the equation will be reduced to

[tex]v_f^2 = 2ad[/tex]

Then, we solve for [tex]a[/tex]. Divide both sides by  [tex]2d[/tex]

[tex]\frac{v_f^2}{2d} = \frac{2ad}{2d}[/tex]

[tex]2d/2d = 1[/tex] so, we're gonna be left with:

[tex]a = \frac{v_f^2}{2d}[/tex]

Now, we plug in the values.

But hey, our distance is 1.0 km. We convert those to meters first so the units stay consistent.

1.0 km = 1000 m, so we use that instead.

Now, we plug in the values we have:

[tex]a = \frac{v_f^2}{2d}[/tex]

[tex]a = \frac{(71.0 m/s)^2}{2(1000 m)}[/tex]

[tex]a = \frac{(71.0 m/s)^2}{2(1000 m)}[/tex]

[tex]a = 2.5205 m/s^2[/tex]

So, the airplane was accelerating at a [tex]2.5205 m/s^2[/tex] rate.

d = vit + 0.5a*t^2

where:

d = distance traveled

vi = initial velocity (0 m/s, since the airplane starts from rest)

a = acceleration (6.50 m/s²)

t = time (48.3 s)

Substituting the given values, we get:

d = 0*(48.3) + 0.5*(6.50)*(48.3)^2

d = 7491.405 m

Therefore, the airplane traveled a distance of 7491.405 meters before takeoff.

To determine the distance traveled by the airplane before takeoff, we can use the kinematic equation:

d = vi*t + (1/2)*a*t^2

where d is the distance traveled, vi is the initial velocity, a is the acceleration, and t is the time elapsed.

In this case, the initial velocity vi is 0 m/s, since the airplane starts from rest. The acceleration a is 6.50 m/s², and the time elapsed is 48.3 s.

Substituting these values into the equation, we get:

d = 0*(48.3) + (1/2)*(6.50)*(48.3)^2

d = 7442.5 meters

Therefore, the distance traveled by the airplane before takeoff is approximately 7442.5 meters.
