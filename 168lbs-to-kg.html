11. The masses of six children are 34.5 kg, 42.6 kg, 39.8 kg, 40.1 kg 53.4 kg and 33. 8 kg. Find the mean mass


Solving for the Mean or Average

Mean Mass = [tex]\frac{sum of masses}{total number of masses}[/tex]

    Sum of masses = 34.5 kg + 42.6 kg + 39.8 kg + 40.1 kg + 53.4 kg + 33.8 kg

                               = 244.2 kg

    Total number of masses = 6

Mean Mass = [tex]\frac{sum of masses}{total number of masses}[/tex]

Mean Mass = [tex]\frac{244.2 kg}{6}[/tex]

Mean Mass = 40.7 kg

Step-by-step explanation:

The average is also known as the mean. It tells us the typical value in a given set or describes the most common value in the group of values. To solve for the average or mean, below are the steps:

1. Count the number of values

2. Add up all the numbers or values.

3. Divide the sum by how many numbers or values there are.

#BRAINLYFAST

MEAN OF UNGROUPED DATA

Problem:

The masses of six children are 34.5 kg, 42.6 kg, 39.8 kg, 40.1 kg 53.4 kg and 33. 8 kg. Find the mean mass.

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

The word problem above involves calculation of mean. Mean is one of the measures of central tendency along with median and mode. By definition, the mean refers to the average of the raw data. So in order to solve for the mean mass of the given data above, we just need to simply obtain the summation of all the raw data and divide it to the total number of the raw data. Here are the steps:

Add: 34.5 + 42.6 + 39.8 + 40.1 + 53.4 + 33.8 = 244.2Divide: 244.2 ÷ 6 (because there are a total of 6 masses of children) = 40.7

Therefore, the mean mass is 40.7 kilograms.
