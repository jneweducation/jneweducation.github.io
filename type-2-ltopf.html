18. 3types of plots​ give me. 3 types


I. What is Plot?

In a narrative or creative writing, a plot is the sequence of events that make up a story, whether it’s told, written, filmed, or sung. The plot is the story, and more specifically, how the story develops, unfolds, and moves in time. Plots are typically made up of five main elements:

1. Exposition: At the beginning of the story, characters, setting, and the main conflict are typically introduced.

2. Rising Action: The main character is in crisis and events leading up to facing the conflict begin to unfold. The story becomes complicated.

3. Climax: At the peak of the story, a major event occurs in which the main character faces a major enemy, fear, challenge, or other source of confl

ict. The most action, drama, change, and excitement occurs here.

4. Falling Action: The story begins to slow down and work towards its end, tying up loose ends.

5. Resolution/Denoument: Also known as the denouement, the resolution is like a concluding paragraph that resolves any remaining issues and ends the story.

Plots, also known as storylines, include the most significant events of the story and how the characters and their problems change over time.

What is a Conditional Sentence?

Conditional sentences are statements that are used to state hypothetical situations and their consequences.

What Are the Types of Conditional Sentences?

There are four types of conditional sentences: Type-0, type-1, type-2, and type-3

1. Type-0 (Zero conditional sentences)

This type expresses general truth or situations.

Structure: If + Simple present, Simple present

Example: If you eat too much candy, you get cavities

2. Type-1 (First conditional sentences)

This type expresses something that would like to happen (but not guaranteed)

Structure: If + simple present, Subject + will + V1

Example: If you go to school later, your father will drive you

3. Type-2 (Second conditional sentences)

This type expresses something unrealistic or will not likely happen in the future.

Structure: If + simple past, Subject + would + V1

Example: If I were you, I would punch him in the face

4. Type-3 (Third conditional sentences)

This type expresses present circumstances would be different if something different had happened in the past.

Structure: If + Past Perfect tense, Subject + would+have+V3

Example: If I had proposed to you, you would not have married him.
