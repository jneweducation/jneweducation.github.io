1. a steel rod having a length of 1m has a cross sectional area of 100sq.mm. it is subjected to an axial tensile force of 27KN .modulus of elasticity is 200,000MPa. what is the deformation of the steel bar?​


Answer:

The deformation δ[tex]=0.00135m[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: To get the deformation of the steel bar, the formula for stress and strain is used.

  δ[tex]=\frac{FL}{AE}[/tex]

  it is derived from the formula σ[tex]=\frac{F}{A}[/tex], ε=δ/L and E= σ/ε

  where:

  δ = strain (or the deformation in [tex]m[/tex])

  F = force (in [tex]N[/tex])

  L = length (of steel rod in [tex]m[/tex])

  A = area (of steel rod [tex]m^{2}[/tex])

  E = modulus of elasticity

  Given:

  [tex]L=1m[/tex]

  [tex]A=100mm^{2}[/tex]

  [tex]E=200000MPa[/tex]

  [tex]F=27kN[/tex]

  Required:

  δ = ?

Step 2: Convert the value of area to [tex]m^{2}[/tex]

  [tex]A=100mm^{2} (\frac{1m}{1000mm} )^{2}=0.0001m^{2}[/tex]

Step 3: Solve for the value of δ

  δ[tex]=\frac{FL}{AE}[/tex]

  δ[tex]=\frac{(27kN)(1m)}{(0.0001m^{2})(200,000MPa)}}[/tex]

  δ[tex]=\frac{(27kN)(1m)}{(0.0001m^{2})(200,000M\frac{N}{m^{2} } )}}[/tex]

  δ[tex]=0.00135m[/tex]

Note: After applying 27kN force to a 1m steel rod having 100[tex]0.0001m^{2}[/tex] section area, it is elongated by 0.00135m.

11. what is axial skeleton?​


Answer:

The adult human body has 206 bones. These bones are divided into two main parts: Your appendicular skeleton and your axial skeleton. Your axial skeleton is made up of the bones along your vertical axis. Axial comes from the word “axis,” which means line. The bones line up along the central core of your body.

Your axial skeleton is made up of the 80 bones within the central core of your body. This includes bones in your skull (cranial and facial bones), ears, neck, back (vertebrae, sacrum and tailbone) and ribcage (sternum and ribs).
