In balancing the chemical equation, we need to follow the law of conservation of mass. The mass of the reactant is equal to the mass of the product. Which means, the number of atoms in the reactant is equal to the number of atoms in the product.

1.)   Fe + H2SO4 ➡️ FeSO4 + H2O ( this is a single displacement, Fe will replace H and the product will be FeSO4 + H2 not FeSO4 + H2O)

               Fe + H2SO4 ➡️ FeSO4 + H2 (this equation is already balanced)

                     Fe  = 1                 Fe = 1

                     SO4 = 1                 SO4 = 1

16. Calculate the molar mass of KAl(SO4)2·


Answer:

The molar mass of KAl(SO₄)₂ is 285.202 g/mol

Explanation:

The name of the compound KAl(SO₄)₂ is potassium aluminum sulfate.

The molar mass of a chemical compound can be computed by getting the sum of the standard atomic masses (in g/mol).

To solve for the molar mass of the this compound, we need first the individual molar masses of the elements present is the compound.

Here is the list of the involved elements with their molar mass:

Potassim (K) - 39.0983 g/molAluminum (Al) - 26.981539 g/molSulfur (S) - 32.065 g/molOxygen (O) - 15.999 g/mol

These information can be found in the periodic table.

Looking at the chemical formula of potassium aluminum sulfate, we can notice the subscripts in oxygen atom (O), and in the sulfate (SO₄). These subscripts indicates how many times the atom is to be multiplied.

Solving for the molar mass:

Potassim (K) - multiply by one since there is no subscript beside it

→ 39.0983 g/mol * 1 = 39.0983 g/mol

Aluminum (Al) - multiply by one since there is no subscript beside it

→ 26.981539 g/mol * 1 = 26.981539 g/mol

Sulfur (S) - multiply by 2 since there is subscript 2 outside the bracket

→ 32.065 g/mol * 2 = 64.13 g/mol

Oxygen (O) - multiply by 8 since it has a subscript 4 on its side and has another subscript 2 outside the bracket

→ 15.999 g/mol * 8 = 127.992 g/mol

Now we add all the underlined numbers:

39.0983 + 26.981539 + 64.13 + 127.992 = 258.202 g/mol

Therefore, the molar mass of KAl(SO₄)₂ is 285.202 g/mol.

21. If PC=3, AC=5x+1, PD = 4, and PB = 4x+2, find PA and PB.​​


Answer:

PA=5 and PB=3.2.

Step-by-step explanation:

We can solve for PA and PB using the information that we have.

First, to find PA, we can use the line equation AC=5x+1 and PC=3. Since AC and PC are on the same line, they have the same slope and y-intercept.

Therefore, PC=3, AC=5x+1, PD = 4, and PB = 4x+2, find PA and PB. Give a detailed

solution

We can solve for PA and PB using the information that we have.

First, to find PA, we can use the line equation AC=5x+1 and PC=3. Since AC and PC are on the same line, they have the same slope and y-intercept.

Therefore, we can set AC=PC and solve for x.

5x+1=3

5x=2

x=2/5

We can now plug in x=2/5 into the equation AC=5x+1 to find AC when x=2/5.

AC=5(2/5)+1

AC=4

Now that we know AC=4, we can use this information to find PA.

We can use the equation PA=2AC-PC to solve for PA.

PA=2(4)-3

PA=5

We can now solve for PB using the equation PB=4x+2 and the known value of x=2/5.

PB=4(2/5)+2

PB=3.2

Therefore, PA=5 and PB=3.2.

28. How many moles of Fe2(SO4)3 are present in 300 grams of Fe2(SO4)3 ?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Fe₂(SO₄)₃.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass} & = \text{2(55.85 g) + 3(32.07 g) + 12(16.00 g)} \\ & = \text{399.9 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Fe₂(SO₄)₃ by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3 & = \text{300 g} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3}{\text{399.9 g} \: \text{Fe}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.750 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there is 0.750 mol of Fe₂(SO₄)₃ in 300 g of Fe₂(SO₄)₃.
