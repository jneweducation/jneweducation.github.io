24. find the greatest common factor of 20m^3n^2, 30m^3 and 44mn​


Answer and step-by-step explanation:

The Greatest Common Factor (GCF) of a set of polynomials is the largest monomial that is a factor of all those polynomials.

To find it easier, we just have to find the GCF of the coefficients first and then the variables.

One of the ways in solving this faster is we just have to choose the common variable with the lowest exponent as part of the GCF.

20m^3n^2, 30m^3 and 44mn

20 = 1 × 20, 2 × 10, 4 × 5

30 = 1 × 30, 2 × 15, 3 × 10, 6 × 5,

44 = 1 × 44, 2 × 22, 4 × 11

Therefore, the GCF of the given polynomials is 2m.
