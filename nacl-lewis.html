19. convert 0.0035% NaCl by mass into parts per million of NaCl​


Answer:

35 ppm

Explanation:

The formula in solving parts per million is

ppm = solute / solution × 10⁶

Problem:

Convert 0.0035% NaCl by mass into parts per million of NaCl​.

Given data:

solute = 0.0035

solution = 100

Computation:

Note: 10⁶ = 1,000,000

ppm = solute / solution × 10⁶

         = 0.0035 / 100 × 10⁶

        = 0.000035 × 1,000,000

       = 35 ppm

27. What is the mass of nacl in a 1 l bottle of 2.00 m nacl?


73 g

Explanation:

The molecular weight of NaCl is 58.44 g/mol

So, one mole of NaCl weighs 58.44 g.

A 2.5 M solution is 2.5 moles per liter (Molarity is just the number of moles per liter).

Therefore, 0.5 L would contain 1.25 mol. Hence, you would need 1.25 × 58.44 g = 73 g.

30. G calculate the density of nacl if the edge length of nacl unit cell is 564 pm


Answer:

The volume of a unit cell, Vcell, is given by:

Vcell = ZM/dN

where Z is the formula units per unit cell

M is the molar mass per formula unit

d is the density in g cm^-3

N is the Avogadro constant

NaCl has a fcc lattice and for fcc, Z = 4

For NaCl, M = 58.44 g mol^-1

As the unit cell is a cube, the edge length 564 pm = 3√Vcell

5.64 x 10^-8 cm = 3√(ZM/dN)

(5.64 x 10^-8 cm)^3 = (ZM/dN)

dN = ZM/(5.64 x 10^-8 cm)^3

d = [ZM/(5.64 x 10^-8 cm)^3]/N

d = [(4 x 58.44 g mol^-1)/(5.64 x 10^-8 cm)^3)]/6.022 x 10^23 mol^-1

d = 2.16 g cm^-3
