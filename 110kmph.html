11. A freight train left the main station heading east at average speed of 100kph. An hour later an express train left the same station heading west at an average of 170kph. At what time will they be 600kms apart from each other?Representation:Equation:Answer:


Answer:

Representation: The equation to solve this problem is d = rt, where d is the distance between the two trains, r is the relative speed of the two trains, and t is the time. The relative speed of the two trains is 270 kph (170 kph - 100 kph).

Equation: Thus, the equation can be written as d = 270t. Substituting 600 for d, we get t = 600/270 = 2.22 hours.

Answer: Therefore, the two trains will be 600kms apart from each other.

Explanation: The equation that represents the situation is d=vt, where d is the distance between the two trains, v is the relative velocity (difference between the speeds of the two trains), and t is the time. Since the express train is heading west, the relative velocity is -270kph. The equation becomes 600 = (-270)t, which simplifies to t = 2.222 hours. This means that it will take 2.222 hours for the two trains to be 600 kilometers apart from each other.
