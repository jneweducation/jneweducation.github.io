5. If AD is an angle bisector of LAB and mZDAB= 49°, what is m/LAB? (Sample) answer: 10 degrees)​


If AD is an angle bisector of ∠LAB and m∠DAB = 49°, what is m∠LAB?

The angle bisector divides the angle into two equal parts. That means, in this problem, m∠DAB is equal to m∠LAD.

m∠DAB = m∠LADm∠DAB = 49°m∠LAD = 49°

m∠LAB is the sum of m∠DAB and m∠LAD. Let's put it like this:

m∠LAB = m∠DAB + m∠LAD

Substitute the value/measure of ∠DAB and ∠LAD to the equation above. Solve to find the answer to the original problem.

m∠LAB = m∠DAB + m∠LADm∠LAB = 49° + 49°m∠LAB = 98°

Thus, the answer to the question: “what is m∠LAB?”, is 98°.

11. POST-LAB QUESTIONS Experiment 1 :TRANSPORT ACROSS THE MEMBRANE 1. Explain the effect of temperature on the rate of diffusion of solute particles.​


Answer:

Temperature has a significant effect on the rate of diffusion of solute particles. As temperature increases, the kinetic energy of the solute particles also increases, leading to more collisions with the solvent particles. This results in a higher rate of diffusion as the solute particles move faster and spread out more quickly in the solvent.

Conversely, when the temperature decreases, the kinetic energy of the solute particles decreases, leading to fewer collisions with the solvent particles. This results in a slower rate of diffusion as the solute particles move slower and take more time to spread out in the solvent.

Therefore, it can be concluded that temperature has a direct impact on the rate of diffusion of solute particles, with higher temperatures leading to faster diffusion rates and lower temperatures leading to slower diffusion rates.

27. POST-LAB QUESTIONS Experiment 1 TRANSPORT ACROSS THE MEMBRANE 1.Explain the effect of temperature on the rate of diffusion of solute particles. Give short explanation?​


Answer:

The effect of temperature on the rate of diffusion of solute particles can be explained by the kinetic theory of matter. As temperature increases, the kinetic energy of the solute particles also increases, causing them to move more rapidly and collide more frequently with the solvent molecules. This results in an increase in the rate of diffusion of solute particles. On the other hand, at lower temperatures, the kinetic energy of the solute particles is lower, resulting in slower diffusion rates. Therefore, increasing the temperature of the system generally leads to a faster rate of diffusion of solute particles. However, it is important to note that excessively high temperatures can denature proteins and cause other cellular damage, which can lead to a decrease in the rate of diffusion.
