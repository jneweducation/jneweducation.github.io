MOLAR MASS

Calculate the molar mass of water.

The chemical formula of water is H₂O

Molar Mass of Oxygen = 16 g/mol

16 • 1 = 16 g

Molar Mass of Hydrogen = 1.01 g/mol

1.01 • 2 = 2.0 g

Molar Mass of H₂O = molar mass O + molar mass H₂

Molar Mass of H₂O = 16 g + 2.0 g

Molar Mass of H₂O = 18 g/mol

Magnesium: 24.305 grams per mole

Chlorine: 35.45 grams per mole

Then write the chemical formula of magnesium chloride down. I assume you know how to determine chemical formulas of simple compounds.

MgCl2

30. a compound of carbon and hydrogen contains 92.3% and has molar mass of 78.1 g/mol.What is its molar formula​


Solution:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\text{mass C = 92.3 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass H = 7.7 g}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]n \: \text{C = 92.3 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.01 g}} = \text{7.685 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{H = 7.7 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{7.639 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_{x}\text{H}_{y}[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles.

[tex]x = \frac{\text{7.685 mol}}{\text{7.639 mol}} = 1[/tex]

[tex]y = \frac{\text{7.639 mol}}{\text{7.639 mol}} = 1[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{CH}[/tex]

Step 6: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = \text{(CH)}_{n}[/tex]

Step 7: Calculate the empirical mass.

empirical mass = (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 1)

empirical mass = 13.018 g/mol

Step 8: Divide the molar mass by the empirical mass.

[tex]n = \frac{\text{molar mass}}{\text{empirical mass}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{78.1 g/mol}}{\text{13.018 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n = 6[/tex]

Step 9: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = \text{(CH)}_{6}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molecular formula} = \text{C}_{6}\text{H}_{6}}[/tex]
