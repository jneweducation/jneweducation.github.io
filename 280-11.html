8. 7. What is the sum of the first 12 terms of the sequence 3, 7, 11, ...? a. 300 c. 280 b. 290 d. 270​


Answer:

A. 300

Step-by-step explanation:

Sum of Arithmetic series

The sum of an arithmetic series can be calculated by the formula:

[tex]S_{n}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{n}{2}[/tex]{ 2a + (n-1)d}

where [tex]S_{n}[/tex]   is the sum of the arithmetic series, n is the number of terms, a is the first term, and d is a common difference.

In this problem, we are given the first three terms of 12 terms which are 3, 7, and 11. We need to calculate the sum of the 12 terms of the sequence.

Notice that from the given terms, our d is 4. Thus, substitute all the known constants to the formula.

[tex]S_{n}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{n}{2}[/tex]{ 2a + (n-1)d}

[tex]S_{12}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{12}{2}[/tex]{ (2 x 3) + (12-1)(4)}

[tex]S_{12}[/tex] = 6 { 6+ (11)(4)}

[tex]S_{12}[/tex]  = 6 ( 50)

[tex]S_{12}[/tex]  = 300

Therefore, the sum of the first 12 terms of the sequence is 300.
