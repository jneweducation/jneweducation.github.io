27. what is the slope of line M if the slope of line L is 7 and lines M and L are perpendicular?


✦ Slope ✦

_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _

✦ If the problem provides us with the information that two lines are perpendicular, it's giving us a clue. First, we know that these lines intersect at a 90° angle. The clue is hidden in the other fact - the slopes are opposite reciprocals of each other.

This means that we take a line's slope, change its sign, and flip it over.

 Here's what two perpendicular lines look like:

[tex]\setlength{\unitlength}{1cm}\begin{picture}(6,6)\put(2,3){\vector(0,1){3}}\put(2,3){\vector(1,0){3}}\end{picture}[/tex]

Or

[tex]\setlength{\unitlength}{1cm}\begin{picture}(6,6)\put(3,3){\vector(0,1){4}}\put(2,3){\vector(3,){3}}\end{picture}[/tex]

See, these lines intersect at a right angle.

Now, let's calculate the slope.

Change the sign

7 => -7

Flip it over

-7 => -1/7

_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _

We come to the conclusion that the slope of this line is -1/7.

[tex]\stackrel{\diamond}{\boxed{\boxed{\begin{gathered} \sf{\underline{Additional ~ Formulas}}\\\hookrightarrow~\bigstar\quad \bold{Slope-intercept:}~~\rm{y=mx+b}\\\hookrightarrow\bigstar\quad \bold{Point-slope~form:~~}\rm{y-y_1=m(x-x_1)}\\\hookrightarrow\bigstar\quad\bold{Slope:} ~~\rm{\frac{Rise}{Run}=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}}\\\end{gathered}}}}[/tex]

I hope it helps! Have a pleasant day.

Answer:

-1/7
