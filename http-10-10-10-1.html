7. Luis walks 10 m to the East, 5 meters to the North, 10 m to the East, 1m to the North, 1 m to the West and 3 m to the North, from school to hishouse.AdisplacementFigure 5. Source: https://slideplayer.com/slide/8666999/1. What is the total distance covered by Luis?2. How about his displacement?​


Answer:

1. The total distance covered by Luis is 10 m + 10 m + 5 m + 1 m + 1 m + 3 m = 30m

2. His displacement is the total change in position and is not affected by the route taken. Luis started at school and ended up at his house, so his displacement is the straight-line distance between the two points. By measuring the change in East and North direction, the displacement can be calculated using the Pythagorean theorem. The displacement can be calculated as follows:

Displacement = [tex]\sqrt{(10m+10m)^{2} + (5m+1m+3m)^{2} } = \sqrt{(20m)^{2} + (9m)^{2} } = \sqrt{400+81} = \sqrt{481m}[/tex]

Therefore, Luis' displacement is approximately 22m.
