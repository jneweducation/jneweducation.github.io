9. Management science techniques focus primarily on observation, model construction, and implementation to find an appropriate solution to a problem


Answer:

Management science is a discipline that uses various techniques, including mathematical modeling, statistical analysis, and optimization, to help organizations make better decisions and solve complex problems. These techniques are used to analyze data, develop models, and identify solutions that can improve efficiency, reduce costs, or increase profits.

The goal of management science is to provide managers with the tools and insights they need to make informed decisions that align with the goals and objectives of the organization. Some common areas where management science techniques are used include supply chain management, operations management, and finance.

One of the key features of management science is its reliance on empirical data and objective analysis. By using systematic and quantitative methods, management scientists aim to uncover patterns and relationships that can inform decision-making. This approach can help organizations make more informed and evidence-based decisions, rather than relying on intuition or subjective judgment.

Overall, management science techniques play a crucial role in helping organizations solve complex problems and make better decisions, enabling them to operate more effectively and achieve their goals.

According to information provided in question, if patient will die without the aid of a dialysis machine, then demand for dialysis machine by patient will be perfectly inelastic because that machine is required for survival of patient until they receive a kidney transplant. Therefore, patient will be willing to pay any price for machine.

Explanation: Diagrammatically, shape of perfectly inelastic demand curve is represented by vertical line, and price is determined by supply curve because only supply curve can be shifted upward or downward.
