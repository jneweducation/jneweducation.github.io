General Journal

-this is part of recording accounting transactions that refers as book of original entry. Once a financial transaction occurred, the transaction should be recorded in specialty journal or in the general journal.

Some examples of transactions recorded in the general journal are:

Asset salesDepreciationInterest income and interest expenseStock sales

General Journal normally provides a chronological record of all non-specialized entries.

Special Journal

-specific types of routine transactions are recorded in these journals which are:

Sales journalCash receipts journalPurchases journalCash disbursements journal

All transactions that will not be recorded under these special journals will be recorded under general journal.

12. A simple journal entry requires one debit to an account and one credit to an account


Answer:

A simple journal entry requires only one debit to an account and one credit to an account. A compound journal entry requires several debits to one account and several credits to one account. Transactions are recorded in alphabetic order in a journal. A journal is also known as a book of original entry.

14. In recording an accounting transaction in a double-entry system


Answer:

The double-entry system of accounting or bookkeeping means that for every business transaction, amounts must be recorded in a minimum of two accounts. The double-entry system also requires that for all transactions, the amounts entered as debits must be equal to the amounts entered as credits.


15. what is adjusting entries in accounting​


Answer:

In accounting/accountancy, adjusting entries are journal entries usually made at the end of an accounting period to allocate income and expenditure to the period in which they actually occurred
