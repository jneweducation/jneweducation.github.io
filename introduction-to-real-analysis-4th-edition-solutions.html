Waste management is a critical issue facing our planet. Every day, we generate huge amounts of waste, and it is our responsibility to ensure that this waste is managed in a way that is safe, sustainable, and environmentally friendly.

One of the biggest problems with waste management is that we are generating more waste than we can effectively manage. Landfills are reaching capacity, and the burning of waste generates harmful emissions that contribute to climate change. In addition, much of the waste that we generate is not being properly recycled, which means that valuable resources are being lost.

There are several solutions to these problems. One solution is to reduce the amount of waste that we generate by adopting sustainable consumption practices. This means choosing products that are made from recycled materials, using reusable containers, and avoiding single-use plastics. Another solution is to improve our recycling efforts by investing in better technology and infrastructure, and by educating the public about the importance of recycling.

In conclusion, waste management is a complex issue that requires a multi-faceted approach. By adopting sustainable consumption practices and improving our recycling efforts, we can work towards a more sustainable future and reduce the negative impact that waste has on our planet.

4. Direction: Explain the importance of the following concepts in speech writing process.1. Audience Analysis2. Selecting a Topic3. Preparing n Outline4. Preparing the introduction5. Creating the body of the Speech6. Conclusion 7. Editing and Revising8. Rehearsing​


Answer:

1. Audience Analysis- you should know your audience so that the topic you'll going to share have been related to them.

2. Selecting a Topic- choose your personal interest to share your ideas freely.

3. Preparing n Outline- make an outline and get an ideas from it.

4. Preparing the introduction- purpose of your topic.

5. Creating the body of the Speech- discusses the main ideas and key concept of the speech.

6. Conclusion-to summarize the main points of your speech.

7. Editing and Revising-you take a second look at the words and sentences you used to express your ideas and fix any problems in grammar, punctuation, and sentence structure in order to improve the content.

8. Rehearsing- the last important part because it allows you to practice different parts before you actually deliver the total speech to an audience.

1.Audience analysis involves identifying the audience and adapting a speech to their interests, level of understanding, attitudes, and beliefs. Taking an audience-centered approach is important because a speaker's effectiveness will be improved if the presentation is created and delivered in an appropriate manner

2.Some common synonyms of purpose are aim, design, end, goal, intention, intent, objective, and object. While all these words mean "what one intends to accomplish or attain," purpose suggests a more settled determination.

3.the subject of a discourse or of a section of a discourse.

4.In narrowing down your topic, pay attention to the specific aspects of the topic that are addressed within the primary text(s) for your essay and within class discussion. This will give you an indication of the specific ways that you can write about your source material.

5.is the process of gathering and measuring information on variables of interest, in an established systematic fashion that enables one to answer stated research questions, test hypotheses, and evaluate outcomes.

6.An outline is a tool used to organize written ideas about a topic or thesis into a logical order. Outlines arrange major topics, subtopics, and supporting details. Writers use outlines when writing their papers in order to know which topic to cover in what order

7.The body of a speech is the center part of the speech that discusses the main ideas and key concepts of the speech. The body is everything but the introduction and the conclusion. The body of a speech is made up of main points.

8.The introduction is a sales pitch and a useful outline in one, so it should maintain a good balance of hype and content.

9.conclusion is to restate the main argument. It reminds the reader of the strengths of your main argument(s) and reiterates the most important evidence supporting those argument(s).

10.During revising, you add, cut, move, or change information in order to improve content. During editing, you take a second look at the words and sentences you used to express your ideas and fix any problems in grammar, punctuation, and sentence structure.

11.A writing pattern is a specific way of organizing ideas to convey a certain type of argument. Writing patterns are also sometimes referred to as patterns of organization, rhetorical modes, or rhetorical styles. The writing pattern an author uses will vary based on the desired purpose.

12.Rehearsal is important because you can apply your knowledge of public speaking to test out what works for you and learn from the experience in a safe environment.
