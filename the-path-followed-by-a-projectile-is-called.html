28. why do projectiles follow a curved path instead of straight path?​


Answer:

Projectiles follow a curved path instead of a straight path because of the influence of gravity. When a projectile is launched, it has both a horizontal and a vertical component of velocity. The horizontal component of velocity is constant, while the vertical component is influenced by gravity, causing it to accelerate downward. As a result, the path of the projectile is curved, following a parabolic trajectory.

The degree of curvature of the path of the projectile depends on the initial velocity of the object, the angle at which it was launched, and the acceleration due to gravity. The greater the initial velocity and the shallower the launch angle, the flatter the curve of the path of the projectile. Conversely, the lower the initial velocity and the steeper the launch angle, the more curved the path of the projectile will be.
