16. It is a bend in the rock that has formed in response to compressive forces?


The answer is FOLDS.

These are a result of ductile deformation of rocks in response to external forces. A fold can be defined as a bend in rock that is the response to compressional forces. Folds are most visible in rocks that contain layering.

17. It happens when a force compresses the crust , causing the crust to bend from its sides.


Answer:

When the Earth's crust is pushed together via compression forces, it can experience geological processes called folding and faulting. Folding occurs when the Earth's crust bends away from a flat surface. A bend upward results in an anticline and a bend downward results in a syncline.

When a beam is simply supported at each end, all the downward forces are balanced by equal and opposite upward forces and the beam is said to be held in Equilibrium (i.e. the total load exerted by the beam's own weight plus any additional applied load are completely balanced by the sum of the two reactions at the two supports).

Place a length of timber, say 1 metre long, between two sets of kitchen scales as shown.Adjust the scales until they both read zero - this will take away the weight of the timber itself.Place a 2kg mass directly above each of the scales in turn and note the readings - it should be 2kg each timeNow place the 2kg mass exactly in the centre of the beam and note the readings - they should each be 1kg because the load has been equally shared between the two supports.

28. it is defined as the bend of rock due tocompressional forces


Answer:

When rocks deform in a ductile manner, instead of fracturing to form faults or joints, they may bend or fold, and the resulting structures are called folds. FOLDS result from compressional stresses or shear stresses acting over considerable time.
