19. What is the difference between chemical reaction from nuclear reaction?​


Answer:

Nuclear reactions involve a change in an atom's nucleus, usually producing a different element. Chemical reactions, on the other hand, involve only a rearrangement of electrons and do not involve changes in the nuclei.

Answer:

A chemical reaction involves the rearrangement of atoms and molecules to form new substances. In a chemical reaction, the atoms and molecules that make up the reactants (the starting materials) are rearranged to form new molecules or compounds, known as products. Chemical reactions involve changes in the electron configuration of atoms, but the nuclei of the atoms remain unchanged.

On the other hand, a nuclear reaction involves changes in the nuclei of atoms. In a nuclear reaction, the nucleus of an atom can be split apart (nuclear fission) or combined with another nucleus (nuclear fusion), resulting in the formation of new atoms and the release of a large amount of energy. Nuclear reactions involve changes in the number of protons and/or neutrons in the nucleus of an atom.
