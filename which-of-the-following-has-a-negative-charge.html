A neutron has a negative charge and a mass smaller than the mass of a proton. C. A neutron has a neutral charge and a mass larger than the mass of an electron. D. A proton has a positive charge and a mass smaller than the mass of an electron.


The following comparisons correctly describes subatomic particles :

C. A neutron has a neutral charge and a mass larger than the mass of an electron.

Further explanation

An atom is the smallest material consisting of a nucleus, which contains protons, neutrons, and electrons that surround it  

Protons are positively charged (+1), electrons are negatively charged (-1). and neutrons are uncharged (neutral)  

While the mass of 1 proton is equal to the mass of 1 neutron, equal to 1 amu and the electron has a mass smaller than both.  

The hydrogen atom is the lightest atom whose mass is 1.67 x 10⁻²⁷kg (measured using a mass spectrometer) and simplified to 1 amu (atomic mass unit).  

1 amu = 1,66.10⁻²⁴ grams  

The mass of each basic particle :

electron mass = 9.11.10⁻²⁸ grams  proton mass = 1.6726.10⁻²⁴ grams  neutron mass = 1,675.10⁻²⁴ grams

So electrons have the smallest mass compared to protons and neutrons

Then the correct answer should be :

A. An electron has a negative charge and a mass smaller than the mass of a proton.

B. A neutron has a neutral charge and have the same mass as a proton.

D. A proton has a positive charge and a mass larger than the mass of an electron
