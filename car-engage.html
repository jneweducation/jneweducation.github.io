10. III. Engagement A. Instruction: Formulate the null and alternative hypothesis in the statement below in terms of parameter (uor p). 1. The mean number of cars a person owns in her lifetime is not more than ten. 2. About half of Americans prefer to live away from cities, given the choice. 3. Europeans have a mean paid vacation each year of six weeks. 4. The chance of developing breast cancer is under 11% for women. 5. Private universities' mean tuition cost is more than $20,000 per year.‚Äã


Answer:

1. Null hypothesis: The mean number of cars a person owns in her lifetime is ten or more (u >= 10)

; Alternative hypothesis: The mean number of cars a person owns in her lifetime is less than ten (u < 10).

2. Null hypothesis: Less than half of Americans prefer to live away from cities, given the choice (p <= 0.5);

Alternative hypothesis: About half or more than half of Americans prefer to live away from cities, given the choice (p > 0.5).

3. Null hypothesis: Europeans have a mean paid vacation each year of six weeks or less (u <= 6);

Alternative hypothesis: Europeans have a mean paid vacation each year of more than six weeks (u > 6).

4. Null hypothesis: The chance of developing breast cancer is 11% or higher for women (p >= 0.11);

Alternative hypothesis: The chance of developing breast cancer is less than 11% for women (p < 0.11).

5. Null hypothesis: Private universities' mean tuition cost is $20,000 per year or less (u <= $20,000);

Alternative hypothesis: Private universities' mean tuition cost is more than $20,000 per year (u > $20,000).
