Statements 1. 2. AX || IS and AS || IX 3. ZXAI ZSIA 5. ZXIA = ZSAI 6. AAXI = AISA 1. Given 2. 5. X 6. A 3. 4. Reflexive Property Reasons S


Answer:

ince the statement to be proved is AAXI = AISA, the proof should start with constructing the two triangles AAXI and AISA and showing that they are congruent. Here's one possible proof:

Given: Parallelogram AXIS with diagonal AI

To prove: AAXI = AISA

Proof:

Given

AX || IS and AS || IX // Opposite sides of a parallelogram are parallel

ZXAI = ZSIA // Diagonal AI bisects angle XAS

Reflexive property

Triangles ZXI and ZSA are congruent by angle-angle-side (AAS) postulate

(Angle ZXI = Angle ZSA, Angle XIZ = Angle AIS, and side ZI = side SA since they are opposite sides of a parallelogram)

Therefore, AAXI = AISA by CPCTC (corresponding parts of congruent triangles are congruent)

Hence, the diagonal AI of parallelogram AXIS divides it into two congruent triangles, AAXI and AISA.

30. 3moles of agci is given.What is the atomic mass of agci?


Good Day

Problem:

3moles of agci is given.What is the atomic mass of AgCl?

Note: its not agci but AgCl (Silver (I) Chloride)

Given atomic mass unit AgCl:

Ag = 107.87 amu

Cl = 35.45 amu

Total mass = 107.87 amu + 35.45 amu = 143.32 amu AgCl

If AgCl has an atomic mass of 143.32 amu, it means in every 1 mole of  AgCl,  has 143.32 grams.   Conversion factor: 143.32 g/1 mole

Solution:

Multiple 3 moles to the conversion factors

3 moles AgCl  × 143.32 g/1 mole = 429.96 grams

Answer:

3 moles of AgCl has a mass of 429.96 grams
