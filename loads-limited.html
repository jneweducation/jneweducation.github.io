7. The lap joint shown in the figure is fastened by four ¾-in.-diameter rivets. Calculate the maximum safe load P that can be applied if the shearing stress in the rivets is limited to 14 ksi and the bearing stress in the plates is limited to 18 ksi. Assume the applied load is uniformly distributed among the four rivets.


Answer:

The minimum value of the load between the shearing and bearing stress is the maximum safe load.

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to solve for the applied force or load using the concept of shearing and bearing stress.

Formula to use:

a. Shearing stress

   τ = P/A

where

τ     is the shearing stress, the unit used is in ksi (kilopound per square inch)

P    is the load or applied force, the unit is in kips (1000 pounds-force)

A    is the cross-sectional area of the member, the unit used in²

b. Bearing stress

   σb = Pb/Ab

where

σb     is the bearing stress, the unit used is in ksi (kilopound per square

         inch)

Pb     is the compressive load,  the unit is in kips (1000 pounds-force)

Ab    is the cross-sectional area, the unit used in²

Given information

d = 3/4 in

τ = 14 ksi

σb = 18 ksi

Pmax = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve for the P load based on the shearing stress of the 4 rivets. Let us use the formula for shearing stress.

τ = P/A

Cross-multiply

P = τ A

P = 14 ksi [4(π/4)(3/4)²]

Take note that the area here is the area of the rivets which is a circle. So, taking the area of a circle is A = πd²/4 and we multiply this with 4 because there are 4 rivets to consider.

So, the load for the shearing stress of rivets is:

P = 24.74 kips

b. Now, let us solve the P load for the bearing stress of the plates. Let us use the 2nd given formula.

σb = Pb/Ab

Cross-multiply

Pb = σb · Ab

Then substitute the given data

Pb = 18 ksi [4(3/4)(x)]

Here, let x be the thickness of the plate to completely solve for the load on the plates.

After substituting the value of x, compare the load solved on each stress. The minimum value of the load is the maximum safe load.

28. A cast iron column supports an axial compressive load of 250KN. Determine the outside diameter of the column if its inside diameter is 185mm and the limiting compressive stress is 50MPa​


Answer:

[tex]D_{o} = 201.48mm[/tex]

Explanation:

We will use  the formula of applied stress in getting the measurement of outside diameter

Given:

[tex]Force = 250KN\\[/tex] or [tex]250000N[/tex]

[tex]Stress = 50MPa[/tex] or [tex]50N/mm^{2}[/tex]

[tex]Outside diameter = 185mm[/tex]

The given are converted to equivalent value with common unit of measurement used for easier computation

Formula:

[tex]stress = \frac{F}{A}[/tex] where F is Force and A is cross sectional area

[tex]A_{column} = \frac{\pi}{4} (D_{o} ^2-D_{i} ^2)[/tex] where [tex]D_{o}[/tex] is outside diameter, [tex]D_{i}[/tex] is inside diameter, and [tex]\pi[/tex] is 3.14

Therefore, to get outside diameter:

[tex]D_{o} = \sqrt{D_{i}^2+\frac{F}{s(\frac{\pi }{4})}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]D_{o} = \sqrt{185mm^2+\frac{250000N}{50N/mm^2(\frac{3.14}{4})}}[/tex]

[tex]D_{o} =\sqrt{40594mm^2}[/tex]

[tex]D_{o} = 201.48mm[/tex]
