Answer:

A train travels 90 miles in 15 minutes. How many miles does it travel in 2 hours?

720 miles.

This is assuming the train continues to move at the same speed.

You can think about the problem in the following way:

The 2 hour journey is split up into x number of 15 minute segments.

2 hours = 120 minutes

120/15 = 8

Therefore, x = 8.

If the journey can be split into 8 15 minute segments, and each 15 minute segment carries it 90 miles, the total distance traveled is given by:

8 * 90 miles = 720 miles

Atlernatively, you can calculate the average speed of the train.

Speed = Distance/Time

So: Speed = 90 miles/15 minutes = 6 miles/minute.

Reusing the above formula, substituting in speed = 6 miles/minute and time = 120 minutes,

6 = Distance/120

Distance = 6*120 miles = 720 miles

15. grace rides her horse 36 miles 2 hours 15 minutes.What is the average in miles per hour


The formula to get the average speed is s = [tex] \frac{d}{t} [/tex] where s is the average speed, d is the distance traveled, and t is the time it takes to travel.

To start, let's identify the given.
The distance traveled is 36 miles.
The time it takes to travel the distance is 2 hours and 15 minutes or 2.25 hours.
d = 36 miles
t = 2.25 hours
let's solve for s.

 s = [tex] \frac{d}{t} [/tex] 
 s = [tex] \frac{36 mi}{2.25 h} [/tex] 
 s = 16 mi/h

The average speed of Grace's horse is 16 miles per hour.

18. An airliner travels 15 miles in 2 minutes. What is its speed in miles per hour? ​


Question:

An airliner travels 15 miles in 2 minutes. What is its speed in miles per hour?

Given:

[tex] \textsf{Distance or d} = 15\ \textsf{miles} [/tex]

[tex] \textsf{Time or t} = 2\ \textsf{minutes} [/tex]

Solution:

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{Speed} = \frac{\textsf{Distance}}{\textsf{Time}} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = \frac{\textsf{d}}{\textsf{t}} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = \frac{15\textsf{ miles}}{2\textsf{ minutes}} [/tex]

Convert miles/minutes to miles/hour:

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = \frac{15\textsf{ miles}}{2\textsf{ minutes}} \cdot \frac{1\textsf{ minute}}{\frac{1}{60}\textsf{ hour}} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = \frac{15\textsf{ miles} \cdot 1\textsf{ minute}}{2\textsf{ minutes} \cdot \frac{1}{60}\textsf{ hour}} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = \frac{15\textsf{ miles}}{\frac{2}{60}\textsf{ hour}} [/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = 15\textsf{ miles} \cdot \frac{60}{2}\frac{1}{\textsf{ hour}}[/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = 15\textsf{ miles} \cdot 30\frac{1}{\textsf{ hour}}[/tex]

[tex] \displaystyle \textsf{V} = 450\frac{ \textsf{miles}}{\textsf{ hour}}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex] \displaystyle 450\frac{ \textsf{miles}}{\textsf{ hour}}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]\large\boxed{450\: miles\: per\:hour}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Here, we need to know how to find the speed of an airliner.

Speed (airliner) = d/t

d = distance

t = time

Solve:Given information:

Distance = 15 miles

Time = 2 minutes = 2/60 = 1/30 hours

Given expression:

S = d/t

Substitute values into the expression.

S = (15)/(1/30)

Simplify by division

S = 450
