Therefore, if we react 180g of Sulfur trioxide with excess water, and if the reaction only yields 75%, we are expected to collect 165.39 grams of sulfuric acid.

(the original answer is 165.385961, you can adjust this depending on the amount of decimal places are necessary)

SULFURIC ACID

Considering a yield of 75%, 180g of sulfur trioxide generates 165.37g of sulfuric acid.

Reaction Stoichiometry

In the equation we have a reaction involving 1 mol of SO3 reacting with 1 mol of H2O, as a product we have 1 mol of sulfuric acid.

First we have to find the molar mass of sulfur trioxide and sulfuric acid, for this we will use the value of the masses that are in the periodic table, which are:

S 32g16gH 1g

[tex]SO3 = 32+3(16) = 80g[/tex]

[tex]H2SO4 = 2+32+4(16) = 98g[/tex]

Now let's use rule of 3 to find the value generated with 180g.

80 -------- 98

180 -------- x

[tex]80x = 180*98 \\ 80x = 17,640 \\ x = 17,640/80 \\ x = 220.5[/tex]

With 180g of SO3, 220.5g of acid are formed, as it only has a 75% yield, let's multiply by 0.75, which is the same thing as 75%.

[tex]220.5*0.75 = 165.37g[/tex]

21. What is the volume of 7 moles of SO3? A. 0.313 L of SO3 B. 3.2 L of SO3 C. 156.8 L of SO3 D. 560 L of SO3


Good Day....

Problem:

What is the volume of 7 moles of SO3?

Answer:

C. 156.8 L of SO3

Given:

7 moles of SO3 = ___________Volume

Note:

Every 1 mole of gas will occupy a volume of 22.4 L at STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure.

Conversion factor:      

22.4 L / 1 mol

Solution:

To solve for the Volume of SO3, multiply the given 7 moles of SO3 by the conversion factor 22.4 L /1 mol.

Volume of SO3 = 7 mol × 22.4 L / 1 mol     (cancel the unit mol)

                          = 156.8 L / 1

                          = 156.8 L

7 moles of SO3 will occupy a volume of 156.8 L at STP
