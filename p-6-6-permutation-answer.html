Permutation means aranggement of objects in which order matters. To solve the permutations problem we need to used the formula which is:

P(n, r) = n!/(n - r)!

Solving the permutations problem using the formula.

P(n, r) = n!/(n - r)!P(6, 4) = 6!/(6 - 4)!P(6, 4) = 6!/(6 - 4)!P(6, 4) = 6!/2!P(6, 4) = 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2!/2!P(6, 4) = 6 × 5 × 4 × 3P(6, 4) = 360

Answer:

720

Step-by-step explanation:

The formula for permutation is:

[tex]_nP_r=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}[/tex]

Where n is the total number of objects, and r is the number of objects taken from n. The letter n is always greater or equal than r. In this particular problem, there are a total of 6 items, and out of them, 5 are selected. That is why the denominator is n subtracted to r. So, we now know that n = 6 and r = 5.

Now, we will try to substitute the givens to the formula.

[tex]_6P_5=\frac{6!}{(6-5)!}[/tex]

Since we know 6! is just a shorthand notation for 6 x 5 x 4 x 3 x 2 x 1, we will proceed to solve the problem with this in mind.

[tex]_nP_r=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!} \\_6P_5=\frac{6!}{(6-5)!}\\_6P_5=\frac{6!}{(1)!}\\_6P_5=\frac{720}{1!}\\_6P_5=\frac{720}{1}\\_6P_5=720[/tex]

So, the answer for the permutation ₆P₅ is 720.

8. Please answerPermutations 1. P(n,3) = 60 2.P(7,r) = 840


Permutation of n taken r has the formula,
[tex]P(n,r)= \frac{n!}{(n-r)!} [/tex]

1. P(n,3)=60

[tex] \frac{n!}{(n-3)!} =60[/tex]
[tex] \frac{n(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)!}{(n-3)!} =60[/tex]            -Factor n! up to (n-3)!
Since (n-3)! divided by itself is equal to 1, Then,
[tex]n(n-1)(n-2)=60[/tex]
The left side of the equation has 3 consecutive descending factors, do the same with 60, 
[tex]n(n-1)(n-2)=(5)(4)(3)[/tex]
Both sides have the same order, therefore, n = 5

2. P(7,r)=840

[tex] \frac{7!}{(7-r)!} =840[/tex]
[tex] \frac{5040}{(7-r)!} =840[/tex]
[tex] \frac{5040}{840} =(7-r)![/tex]
[tex]6=(7-r)![/tex]
Note that there is only one factorial that would result to 6, that is, 3!.
So, 
7-r=3
r=4
