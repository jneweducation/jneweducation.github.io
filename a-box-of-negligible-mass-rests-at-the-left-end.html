2. to maintain equilibrium condition at what point (from the right end ) should a uniform 1 meter beam with negligible mass be supported so that it can balance a 50gram weight on the left end an 80gram weight on the rigth end and a 60 gram weight 45cm from the 50 gram weight


See diagram in the attached file. Figure 1 shows a diagram of the system.  

The quantities in the diagram are defined as follows:  

[tex]m_{beam}=0[/tex] (This is because the problem states that the beam has negligible mass. Negligible means almost nothing. So, it is the same as assuming that the mass of the beam is zero. Several physics problems in equilibrium deals with a rigid beam with negligible mass or a rigid bar of negligible mass or a rigid bar of negligible weight. So, having an object in the system with negligible mass is a common assumption in physics problems.)[tex]m_{1}=0.08kg[/tex] (This is equivalent to 80 g. Recall that 1 kg = 1000 g.) [tex]m_{2}=0.06kg[/tex] (This is equivalent to 60 g. Recall that 1 kg = 1000 g.) [tex]m_{3}=0.05kg[/tex] (This is equivalent to 50 g. Recall that 1 kg = 1000 g.)

It is also given that the total length of the beam is 1.0 m. Positions of the three masses are also identified as the following:


[tex]x_{1}=0m[/tex] (This is the position of [tex]m_{1}[/tex]. [tex]m_{1}[/tex], according to the problem is positioned at the right end of the beam. Since the problem asks about the position of the support relative to the right end, the right end is the reference point set at zero.) [tex]x_{2}=0.55m[/tex] (This is the position of [tex]m_{2}[/tex] from the reference point. [tex]m_{2}[/tex], according to the problem is positioned 45 cm (equivalent to 0.45 m) from [tex]m_{3}[/tex], which is at the left end. This means that from the right end, the position of [tex]m_{2}[/tex] is at 0.55 m position.) [tex]x_{3}=1.0m[/tex] (This is the position of [tex]m_{3}[/tex] from the reference point. Since [tex]m_{3}[/tex] is at the left end of the beam, its position from the right end is the same as the total length of the beam that is 1.0 m.)

Note that the position unit cm given in the problem is converted to m and the mass unit g is converted to kg. When units of the same quantities are the same, this will facilitate less complicated computation. So, for the solution of this problem, kilogram (kg) will be used as the unit of mass while meter (m) will be for the unit of length or position.


Tables of unit conversions for different quantities can be found at https://brainly.ph/question/1498814.

Assume that mass is concentrated at the center of an object. So objects in the diagram are illustrated in such a way that their centers of mass correspond to the measurements of the objects’ distances from the chosen reference point along the x-axis.


To achieve balance or equilibrium, support should be positioned at the center of gravity. A brief discussion about equilibrium is provided in https://brainly.ph/question/1576716.  

Hence, to answer the problem, the position of the center of gravity needs to be determined. With the assumption that the acceleration due to gravity is constant in the whole system, the position of the center of gravity is similar to that of the center of mass. Therefore,


[tex]x_{cg}= x_{cm}[/tex]     [Eq. 1]


where  

[tex]x_{cg}[/tex] is the position of the center of gravity along x while


[tex]x_{cm}[/tex] is the position of the center of mass along x. The position of center of mass can be determined using the following expression:


[tex]x_{cm}= \frac{summation of mx}{summation of m}[/tex]     [Eq. 2]


Substituting Eq. 2 to Eq. 1 will result to:

[tex]x_{cg}= \frac{summation of mx}{summation of m}[/tex]


[tex]x_{cg}= \frac{m_{1}x_{1}+m_{2}x_{2}+m_{3}x_{3}}{m_{1}+m_{2}+m_{3}}[/tex]     [Eq. 3]


Next, substitute the values for all masses and positions.


[tex]x_{cg}= \frac{0.08kg(0m)+0.06kg(0.55m)+0.05kg(1.0m)}{0.08kg+0.06kg+0.05kg}[/tex]

Performing the operations in the equation results to:  

[tex]x_{cg}=0.44m[/tex] or 44 cm


Therefore, to maintain equilibrium, the support should be placed at 0.44 m or 44 cm from the right end of the beam.


Though in solving this problem the position of the center of gravity is the same as the position of the center of mass, these are two different concepts. Definition and additional information about center of gravity are provided in https://brainly.ph/question/402466.

24. how can you possibly prove that gases have negligible mass


Gas Has Mass:

Gas are made up of gas particle of matter in which each element have specific atomic mass which contribute to its mass depending on the amount present.

Example:

Empty LPG gas container versus full LPG gas container. Empty container is lighter without LPG gas and heavier when it is filled with gas.

Gas Has Temperature:

Gas particle have kinetic energy and temperature directly affects the gas kinetic energy.

Example:

You feel colder inside the air conditioned room and warmer outside.

Gas Has volume:

Gas is made up of matter and all matter have protons, electrons, and neutrons which occupies space.

Example:

When you inflate the balloon with air, the volume increases and when deflated the volume decreases.

Gas Has Pressure:

Example:

When set the plunger of the syringe at 10 cc without needle and covers the tip of it without allowing the gas to escape. The moment to push to plunger you will find it difficult due to increasing pressure.

29. A dockworker applies a constant horizontal force of 80.0 N to a block of ice on a smooth horizontal floor. The frictional force is negligible. The block starts from rest and moves 11.0 m in 5.00 s. (a) What is the mass of the block of ice? (b) If the worker stops pushing at the end of 5.00 s, how far does the block move in the next 5.00 s?


Answer:

a. 90.90kg

b. 22m

Step-by-step explanation:

For letter a:

Using kinematic equation, the expression for find acceleration is

[tex]s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^{2} \\\\a = \frac{2s}{t^{2} }[/tex]

Insert values in the above equation  

[tex]a = \frac{2 (11m)}{(5.00s)^{2} }\\\\= 0.88 m/s^{2}[/tex]

The expression for find mass of the block ice is

[tex]F = ma\\\\m = \frac{F}{a}[/tex]

Substitute values in the above equation

[tex]m = \frac{80.0N}{0.88m/s^{2} }[/tex]

[tex]= 90.90kg[/tex]

For letter b:

Using kinematic equation, the expression for finding final velocity is

[tex]v = u + at[/tex]

Substitute values in the above equation  

[tex]v = 0 + (0.88m/s^{2}) (5.00s)\\\\= 4.4 m/s[/tex]

The expression for finding distance is

[tex]d = vt\\[/tex]

Insert values in the above equation  

[tex]d = (4.4m/s) (5.00s)[/tex]

[tex]= 22m[/tex]
