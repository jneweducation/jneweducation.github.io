1. A ferris wheel rotating initially at an angular speed of 0.16 rad/s accelerates to an angular speed of 0.7 rad/s. What is its angular acceleration if it moved to an angular position if 2.5 rad? ​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \theta = \text{2.5 rad} \\ & \omega_0 = \text{0.16 rad/s} \\ & \omega = \text{0.7 rad/s} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the angular acceleration.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \alpha & = \frac{\omega^2 - \omega_0^2}{2\theta} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.7 rad/s})^2 - (\text{0.16 rad/s})^2}{2(\text{2.5 rad})} \\ & = \text{0.09288 rad/s}^2 \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{0.093 rad/s}^2} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the angular acceleration of a ferris wheel is 0.093 rad/s².

3. If an object of radius 3 m that experiences a constant angular acceleration starting from rest, rotates 10 rads in 2 s, what is its angular acceleration


Angular Acceleration

The temporal rate at which angular velocity changes is referred to as angular acceleration. There are types of angular acceleration, referred to as spin angular acceleration and orbital angular acceleration, just as there are two types of angular velocity, namely spin angular velocity and orbital angular velocity.

Problem:

If an object of radius 3 m that experiences a constant angular acceleration starting from rest, rotates 10 rads. in 2 s, what is its angular acceleration

Solution:

θ = θo + wot + ½ αt²

        10 rad = 0 rad + (0 rad/s)(2 s) + ½α(2 s)²

           10 = ½α(2 s)²

         10 = 2α

          5 rad/s² = α

Therefore, its angular acceleration is 5 rad/s²

Additional information:

As opposed to orbital angular acceleration, which is the angular acceleration of a point particle around a fixed origin, spin angular acceleration describes the angular acceleration of a rigid body about its center of rotational.

The change in angular velocity that a spinning object experiences per unit of time is expressed mathematically as angular acceleration, also known as circular acceleration. It is a vector field that has two main orientations or perceptions as well as an element of quantity.

23. Calculate the angular momentum of a 2 kg cylinder pulley with radius 0.1 m that rotates at a constant angular speed of 2 rad/s.​


Answer:

0.02

Explanation:Formula

By using the formula,

[tex]L = Iω[/tex]

Where L is the angular momentum, I is the inertia, and ω is the angular speed

And the inertia:

[tex]I = \frac{1}{2} m {r}^{2} [/tex]

Where m is the mass and r is the radius.

Solution

Substituting these values for inertia:

[tex]I = \frac{1}{2} m {r}^{2} = \frac{1}{2} \times 2kg \times (0.1m) {}^{2} = 0.01 \: kg \: • \: m {}^{2} [/tex]

Therefore, the inertia is 0.01 kg • m²

Since ω = 2 rad/s and I = 0.01 kg • m²

[tex]L = Iω = 0.01 \times 2 = 0.02[/tex]

Therefore, the angular momentum of the cylinder pully is 0.02

25. A rotating disk experiencing uniform angular acceleration starts from rest to an angular speed of 3.14 rad/s in 10.0 s. what is the magnitude of the angular acceleration of the disk


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

The angular velocity of a rotating object at rest is zero.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & t = \text{10.0 s} \\ & \omega_0 = 0 \\ & \omega = \text{3.14 rad/s} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the angular acceleration.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \alpha & = \frac{\omega - \omega_0}{t} \\ & = \frac{\text{3.14 rad/s} - 0}{\text{10.0 s}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.314 rad/s}^2} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the angular acceleration of a rotating disk is 0.314 rad/s².
