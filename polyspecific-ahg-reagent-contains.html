14. what reagent is added into food to identify the presence of starch?what will happen if the food contains starch?​


Answer:

The reagent used to test for presence of starch is Iodine. Starch turns an intense "blue-black" colour upon addition of aqueous solutions of the triiodide anion, due to the formation of an intermolecular charge-transfer complex. In the absence of starch, the brown colour of the aqueous solution remains. This interaction between starch and triiodide is also the basis for iodometry.
