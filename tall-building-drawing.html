22. solve the prodlems related to Pythagorean theorem a flagpole is mounted on top a building if the flagpole is 25 feel tall and the building is 50 feel tall how long is the flagpole to the of the building?​


Step-by-step explanation:

1. Write down the given information:

Height of flagpole (hypotenuse) = 25 feet

Height of building (one leg) = 50 feet

2. Use the Pythagorean theorem to find the length of the flagpole to the base of the building:

x^2 = (25)^2 + (50)^2

where x is the length of the flagpole to the base of the building that we want to find.

Simplifying and solving for x:

x^2 = 625 + 2500

x^2 = 3125

x = sqrt(3125)

x ≈ 55.9

Therefore, the length of the flagpole to the base of the building is approximately 55.9 feet.

Answer:

The answer to this problem is 145.04 ft.

Step-by-step explanation:

This problem is best illustrated by the photo attached below.

Let x = height of the building measured by the theodolite with respect to its line of sight

Since the angle of elevation refers to the angle above the line of sight, the total height must be the sum of x and the height of the theodolite which is 5 ft.

[tex]tan(35) = \frac{x}{200ft} \\x = 200tan(35)\\x = 140.04 ft\\\\total height = x + 5ft = 140.04ft +5ft = 145.04ft[/tex]
