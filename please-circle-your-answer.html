8. Which circle has a greater area: a circle with a radius of 6.5 cm or a circle with a diameter of 13 cm? Show solutions to prove your answer. (ENGLISH PLEASE) (NONSENSE=REPORT)


✒️CIRCLES

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

Which circle has a greater area: a circle with a radius of 6.5 cm or a circle with a diameter of 13 cm? Show solutions to prove your answer.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \large \rm{No \: differences} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \large \rm{Both \: circles' \: areas \: are \: equal.} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» The area of the circle can be calculated by multiplying pi to the square of the radius.

[tex] Area = \pi r^2 [/tex]

» If the diameter is given, them convert the radius which is half the diameter.

[tex] Area = \pi \bigg(\frac{\,d\,}{2}\bigg)^2 \\[/tex]

» Compare the areas:

If the radius is 6.5cm:[tex] Area = \pi (6cm)^2 [/tex]

[tex] Area = \pi (42.25cm^2) [/tex]If the diameter is 13cm:[tex] Area = \pi \bigg(\frac{\,13cm\,}{2}\bigg)^2 \\[/tex]

[tex] Area = \pi (6cm)^2 [/tex]

[tex] Area = \pi (42.25cm^2) [/tex]

» If we find their areas in terms of pi, there is no such difference.

Another thing is that the diameter of the other circle is 13cm, which gives the radius as 6.5cm that is as same as the first circle.

Therefore, the two circles are congruent, and as they have the same radius and size, they also have the same area and circumference.

[tex] \color{magenta} \huge\mathfrak{Question:} [/tex]

1. The diameter of the circle is twice the length of the radius.

[tex] \color{violet} \huge\mathfrak{Answer:} [/tex]

True

[tex] \color{pink} \huge\mathfrak{Explanation:} [/tex]

The diameter of a circle is twice its radius (not the other way around), so a circle with radius 20 has diameter \displaystyle 2 \times 20 = 40.

⊱─━━━━━━⊱༻●༺⊰━━━━━━─⊰

[tex] \color{blue} \huge\mathfrak{Question:} [/tex]

2. The longest chord in a circle is the diameter.

[tex] \color{magenta} \huge\mathfrak{Answer:} [/tex]

True

[tex] \color{brown} \huge\mathfrak{Explanation:} [/tex]

A chord that passes through the center of a circle is called a diameter and is the longest chord of that specific circle.

⊱─━━━━━━⊱༻●༺⊰━━━━━━─⊰

[tex] \color{lightblue} \huge\mathfrak{Question:} [/tex]

3. if a radius measures 12 cm, then the measure of the diameter is 6 cm

[tex] \color{lime} \huge\mathfrak{Answer:} [/tex]

False

[tex] \color{black} \huge\mathfrak{Explanation:} [/tex]

The statement is false because the diameter connects two points on a circle and it goes straight across the circle while the radius is from the center to any point of the circle. Therefore, the measure of the diameter should be longer than the radius and 6 cm is shorter than 12

⊱─━━━━━━⊱༻●༺⊰━━━━━━─⊰

[tex] \color{magenta} \huge\mathfrak{Question:} [/tex]

4. the length of every chord in a circle is always greater than the length of a radius

[tex] \color{green} \huge\mathfrak{Answer:} [/tex]

False

[tex] \color{red} \huge\mathfrak{Explanation:} [/tex]

A chord may connect two points a with a shorter distance along the circumference of the circle. Therefore, it can be shorter or less than the length of the radius.

⊱─━━━━━━⊱༻●༺⊰━━━━━━─⊰

[tex] \color{pink} \huge\mathfrak{Question:} [/tex]

5. A circle is labeled by its center point

[tex] \color{orange} \huge\mathfrak{Answer:} [/tex]

True

[tex] \color{magenta} \huge\mathfrak{Explanation:} [/tex]

A circle is labeled by its center point: circle O means the circle with center point O. ... A radius is a line segment from the center of the circle to any point on the circle. The radius of a circle is one-half the length of the diameter. ⊱─━━━━━━⊱༻●༺⊰━━━━━━─⊰
