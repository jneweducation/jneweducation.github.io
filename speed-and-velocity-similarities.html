Explanation:

SPEED:

• Speed is a scalar quantity that refers to "how fast an object is moving." Speed can be thought of as the rate at which an object covers distance. ... An object with no movement at all has a zero speed.

VELOCITY:

• The velocity of an object is the rate of change of its position with respect to a frame of reference, and is a function of time. ... Velocity is a physical vector quantity; both magnitude and direction are needed to define it.

SIMILARITIES:

• Speed and velocity both represent a way to measure the change in position of an object relative to time. In fact, for a straight line motion, the speed and velocity of an object are the same (since distance and displacement will be the same). Speed and velocity are measured in the same units: meters per second or m/s.

CONSTANT SPEED:

• When the speed of an object remains the same - it does not increase or decrease - we say it is moving at a constant speed.

CONSTANT VELOCITY:

• Constant velocity means that the object in motion is moving in a straight line at a constant speed. ... The velocity can be positive or negative, and is indicated by the sign of our slope. This tells us in which direction the object moves.

SIMILARITIES:

• To summarize, an object moving in uniform circular motion is moving around the perimeter of the circle with a constant speed. While the speed of the object is constant, its velocity is changing. Velocity, being a vector, has a constant magnitude but a changing direction.

25. what are the similarities and differences of distance,displacement,speed,and velocity?​


Answer:

Distance, displacement, speed, and velocity are all important concepts used to describe motion.

Distance is a scalar quantity that describes the total path covered by an object during a journey. Displacement, on the other hand, is a vector quantity that describes the change in position of an object from its initial to its final position.

Speed is a scalar quantity that describes how fast an object is moving, while velocity is a vector quantity that describes an object's speed and direction of motion. In other words, velocity includes the direction of the object's motion, while speed does not.

While distance and speed are both scalar quantities, displacement and velocity are both vector quantities. The SI unit for speed is meters per second (m/s), while the SI unit for velocity is also meters per second but with a direction specified (e.g. 10 m/s eastward).

Average speed is calculated by dividing the total distance traveled by the time taken, while average velocity is calculated by dividing the displacement by the time taken. In summary, distance and displacement differ in that displacement takes direction into account, while speed and velocity differ in that velocity takes direction into account, while speed does not.
