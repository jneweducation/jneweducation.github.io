1. Do PLANES have ANTI-SKID??? How to PREVENT SKIDDING?  ​


Answer:

Commercial airplanes typically have anti-skid systems installed to help prevent skidding during takeoff and landing. Anti-skid systems work by detecting when a wheel is skidding and then applying the brakes to that wheel to regain traction. This helps to prevent the airplane from losing control on a slippery runway surface.

To prevent skidding, pilots also use a technique called "thrust reversing" which is used during landing to slow the plane down. Thrust reversing involves directing the engines' thrust forward, instead of backwards, to slow the plane down. Additionally, pilots also use spoilers, which are panels that rise above the wing surface to disrupt the smooth airflow over the wing and reduce lift, helping to slow the plane down.

Another way to prevent skidding is by using anti-skid brakes which automatically sense when a wheel is about to skid and it applies the brake to that wheel to restore traction. Also, during takeoff, pilots use a technique called "rotation" to lift the nose of the plane off the ground, which helps to prevent skidding.

In addition, the use of de-icing and anti-icing fluids on the runway and the aircraft before takeoff can help to prevent skidding by removing snow, ice, and frost from the runway and aircraft surfaces.

In summary, anti-skid systems, thrust reversing, spoilers, anti-skid brakes, rotation, and de-icing/anti-icing fluids are techniques used by pilots and airport personnel to prevent skidding during takeoff and landing.

Answer:

The skidding distance of the car is 57.12 meters.

Explanation:

This topic is about motion along a straight line where the motion is a continuous change of position.

For the formula, we use:

[tex]v=\frac{d}{t}[/tex]

where

[tex]v[/tex]     is the velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]d[/tex]     is the distance, unit is in meters (m)

[tex]t[/tex]      is the time, unit is in seconds (s)

For the given information:

[tex]v=22.4 m/s[/tex]

[tex]t=2.55sec[/tex]

Solving for the distance, use the formula above:

[tex]v=\frac{d}{t}[/tex]

Substitute the given information, we get:

[tex]22.4m/s=\frac{d}{2.55s}[/tex]

Using cross- multiply, we get:

[tex]d=(22.4m/s)(2.55s)[/tex]

[tex]d=57.12m[/tex]

Therefore, the skidding distance of the car is 57.12 meters.

19. a car traveling at 22.4m/s skids to a stop in 2.55 s. determine the skidding distance of the car (assume uniform acceleration.​


Answer:

We can use the kinematic equation:

$$d = v_i t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$

where $d$ is the skidding distance, $v_i$ is the initial velocity, $t$ is the time it takes to stop, and $a$ is the acceleration.

In this case, $v_i = 22.4\text{ m/s}$, $t = 2.55\text{ s}$, and we want to find $d$.

Since the car is skidding to a stop, we know that the final velocity $v_f$ is 0. We can use the equation:

$$v_f = v_i + at$$

to solve for $a$:

$$0 = 22.4\text{ m/s} + a(2.55\text{ s})$$

$$a = -8.78\text{ m/s}^2$$

Now we can use the first equation to find $d$:

$$d = (22.4\text{ m/s})(2.55\text{ s}) + \frac{1}{2}(-8.78\text{ m/s}^2)(2.55\text{ s})^2$$

$$d = 28.6\text{ m}$$

Therefore, the skidding distance of the car is 28.6 meters.
