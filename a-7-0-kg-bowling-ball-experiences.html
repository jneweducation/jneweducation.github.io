2. A 7.0-kg bowling ball experiences a net force of 6.0 n. what will be its acceleration?


Answer:

The acceleration of the bowling ball is 0.86 m/s²

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept of the 2nd Newton's law of motion to solve for the acceleration of the bowling ball. to do this, we will use the formula:

F = ma

where

F     is the force, the unit is in Newtons (N)

m    is the mass, the unit is in kg

a     is the acceleration, the unit is in m/s²

Given information

m = 7 kg

F = 6 N

a = ?

Solving the problem

Let us now solve the acceleration using the given formula

F = ma

Then substitute the given data

6 N = (7 kg) a

Cross-multiply

a = 6N / 7kg

a = 0.86 m/s²

Therefore, the acceleration of the bowling ball is 0.86 m/s².

Answer:

4.8 m/s

Explanation:

This problem is an example of conservation of momentum.The formula for conservation of momentum is at follows:

Sum of Initial Momenta=Sum of Final Momenta

M1V1+M2V1=M1V2+M2V2;

Where m is mass and v is velocity.

Let us first list what we know:

Before Collision:

M1=8 kg

V1=0 m/s(At rest)

M2=5 kg

V1=?

After Collision:

M1=8 kg

V2=3 m/s

M2=5 kg

V2=0 m/s(Stops)

Let us plug them in our conservation of momentum equation:

M1V1+M2V1=M1V2+M2V2

(8)(0)+(5)V1=(8)(3)+(5)(0)

5V1=24

V1=4.8 m/s

Therefore,the velocity of the lighter ball before collision is 4.8 m/s.

Problem:

A 7.0 Kg bowling ball is moving on a bowling lane with a velocity of 5.0 m/s. What is the kinetic energy of the ball at that instant?

Note:

Kinetic energy is energy in motion. The formula of kinetic energy is  ( K.E. = 1/2 mv²  ).

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 7 kg

v ( velocity ) = 5 m/s

K.E. ( kinetic energy ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve the problem using the formula ( K.E. = 1/2 mv²  )

K.E. = 1/2 mv²

      = 1/2 × 7 kg × ( 5 m/s )²

      = 1/2 × 7 kg ×  25 m²/s²

      = 1/2 × 175 kg·m²/s²

      = 87.5 kg·m²/s²

      = 87.5 J

Answer:

the kinetic energy of the ball at that instant is equal to 87.5 J  or                     87.5 kg·m²/s²

30. 9. If a 8.57 kg bowling ball is rolled down the bowling lane with a force of 88 N, what is the acceleration of the ball? someone please help me ​


To find the acceleration of the bowling ball, we need to use Newton's second law, which states that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. The formula for this law is:

a = F/m

Where:

a = acceleration (in m/s^2)

F = force (in N)

m = mass (in kg)

Substituting the given values, we get:

a = 88 N / 8.57 kg = 10.25 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the bowling ball is 10.25 m/s^2.
