20. A cylindrical pipe with water flowing downward at 0.02 m3/s having top diameter of 0.08 m, bottom diameter of 0.04 m, and height of 1.5 m. find the pressure between the pipe


Answer:

The pressure between the pipe is AP = 15.27 Psi

Explanation:

Q = 0.02 m³/s

d₁ = 0.08 m

d₂ = 0.04 m

h₁ = 1.5 m

h₂ = 0

A₁ = [tex]\frac{πd₁²}{4}[/tex]

   = [tex]\frac{π}{4}[/tex] (0.08)² = 0.005026 m²

P₂ = [tex]\frac{πd₂²}{4}[/tex]

    = [tex]\frac{π}{4}[/tex] (0.04)² = 0.001256 m²

Flow Rate. Q = A₁V₁ = A₂V₂

                  V₁ = [tex]\frac{Q}{A₁}[/tex] , V₂ = [tex]\frac{Q}{A₂}[/tex]

                  V₁ = [tex]\frac{0.02}{0.005026}[/tex] , V₂ = [tex]\frac{0.02}{0.001256}[/tex]

                  V₁ = 4 m/s  , V₂ = 16 m/s

between (1) and (2)

P₁ + [tex]\frac{1000 - (4)²}{2}[/tex] + 1.000 ˣ 9.81 ˣ 1.5 = P₂ + [tex]\frac{1000 ˣ (16)²}{2}[/tex] + 1.000 ˣ 9.81 ˣ (0)

P₁ - P₂ = 105285 Pa  ( 1 Pa = 0.000145038 Psi

25. A cylindrical silo has a circumference of 18.5 m. Find its diameter


Answer:

Diameter is 5.88 m

Step-by-step explanation:

Find the diameter of a cylinder.

Given

C = 18.5 m

With the given circumference, we can use the equation of C = 2πr to derive a radius equation, where diameter is equivalent to twice of a circle's radius.

Use the equation,

radius = C/2π

where, π = 3.14

r = C/2π

= 18.5/2(3.14)

= 18.5/(6.28)

r = 2.94 m

Now, after we obtain the radius

Use the equation,

diameter = 2r

d = 2r

= 2(2.94)

d = 5.88 m

27. two cylindrical boilers are similar,and the diameter of the one is twice the diameter of other.what is the ratio of their lateral surface and the areas of their cross section


Since the first cylinder is twice the diameter of the other, (note that diameter is twice the radius) then diameters and radius are as follows:
First Cylinder = 2d= 4r
Second Cylinder = d = 2r

In terms of lateral surface area which has the formula [tex]A=2 \pi rh[/tex], substitute the radii above for r.
First Cylinder [tex]A=2 \pi (4r)h=8 \pi rh[/tex]
Second Cylinder [tex]A=2 \pi (2r)h=4 \pi rh[/tex]
The ratio of the lateral surface area is 2:1. The lateral surface area of the first cylinder is twice the lateral area of the second cylinder.

For the cross section.
a. If you cut the cylinders horizontally, a circle will be formed. The area of the Circle is [tex]A= \pi r^{2} [/tex], so
First Cylinder [tex]A= \pi r^{2} = \pi (4r)^{2} = 16 \pi r[/tex]
Second Cylinder = [tex]A= \pi r^{2} = \pi (2r)^{2} =4 \pi r[/tex]
If you divide the area of the circle for the first cylinder by the second, we have 4. Thus the ratio is 4:1. The vertical cross section of the first cylinder is 4 times the area of the second.
b. If you cut the cylinders vertically, a rectangle will be formed. The area of the rectangle is [tex]A=lw[/tex], length is represented by the circumference and width is represented by height.
First Cylinder [tex]A=CH=(2 \pi (4r))(H)=8 \pi rH[/tex]
Second Cylinder [tex]A=CH=(2 \pi (2r))(H)=4 \pi rH[/tex]
The ratio for vertical cross section area is 2:1.
