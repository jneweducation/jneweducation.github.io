5. A baseball is thrown in the air and follows a parabolic trajectory. Point A is the maximum height while point B is the point just before the ball hits the ground. If air resistance is neglected, which of the following displays the correct relationship between the speeds and the acceleration at those two points?a. VA = VB and aA < aBb. VA > VB and aA = aBc. VA < VB and aA = aBd. VA < VB and aA > aB​


Answer:

D. VA < VB and aA > aB.

Explanation:

The correct relationship between the speeds and the acceleration at points A and B is option (d), VA < VB and aA > aB. At point A, the velocity of the ball is zero and its acceleration is the acceleration due to gravity pointing downward. At point B, the velocity of the ball is nonzero and its acceleration is also the acceleration due to gravity pointing downward. Since the ball is accelerating faster at point B than it is at point A, its velocity must be greater at point B than it is at point A.
