6. How long must a runway be for a plane to reach a takeoff velocity of 75m/s if it can accelerate at 20m/s²? ​


We can use the kinematic equation:

v² = u² + 2as

where:

v = final velocity = 75 m/s

u = initial velocity = 0 m/s (plane is at rest)

a = acceleration = 20 m/s²

s = distance or runway length (what we are solving for)

Substituting the given values, we get:

75² = 0² + 2(20)(s)

Simplifying:

5625 = 40s

Dividing both sides by 40:

s = 140.625 meters

Therefore, the runway must be at least 140.625 meters long for the plane to reach a takeoff velocity of 75 m/s if it can accelerate at 20 m/s².

Explanation:

1. The Philippine tarsier is capable of jumping to a height of 2.0 m in hunting for food. Determine the take-off speed of the tarsier.

We have the following data:

Vf (final velocity) = 0 m/s (at maximum height)

Vi (initial velocity) = unknown

g (acceleration of gravity) ≈ -9.8 m / s²

h (height) = 2.0 m

Solution:

To solve this problem, we can use the formula Vf² = Vi²  + 2ad, to solve for Vi,  we can use the formula Vi = √Vf² - 2ad

Vi = √Vf² - 2ad

    = √0 - (2× -9.8 m / s² × 2.0 m)

    = √0 - (-39.2 m²/s²)

    = √39.2 m²/s²

    = 6.26 m/s

It's strictly done to stop the wheels from vibrating as they wind down, which can scare the pax and sometimes the pilot at first.
