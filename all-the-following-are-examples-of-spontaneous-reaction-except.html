15. From the following ∆H and ∆S values, predict whether the reaction would be spontaneous. If not, at what temperature might the reaction become spontaneous? ∆H = -126 kJ ∆S = 84 J/K​


Answer:

To determine whether a reaction is spontaneous or not, we can use the Gibbs free energy equation:

∆G = ∆H - T∆S

where ∆G is the change in Gibbs free energy, ∆H is the change in enthalpy, T is the temperature in Kelvin, and ∆S is the change in entropy.

If ∆G is negative, the reaction is spontaneous. If ∆G is positive, the reaction is non-spontaneous. If ∆G is zero, the reaction is at equilibrium.

Given the values:

∆H = -126 kJ

∆S = 84 J/K

We need to convert ∆H from kJ to J by multiplying it by 1000:

∆H = -126,000 J

Substituting the values in the equation:

∆G = ∆H - T∆S

We get:

∆G = -126,000 J - T(84 J/K)

To determine the temperature at which the reaction becomes spontaneous, we need to find the temperature at which ∆G is zero:

0 = -126,000 J - T(84 J/K)

T = 1,500 K

Therefore, at a temperature of 1,500 K (1,226.85°C), the reaction would become spontaneous.
