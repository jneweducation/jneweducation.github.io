• Angle Bisector is a ray from the vertex of an angle to a point in its interior which divides the angle into two congruent parts.

A ray which extends from the vertex of an angle through the interior such that it divides the angle into two congruent (same shape and measure) angles is called an angle bisector.

• Two angles are called complementary if their sum is 90 degrees .

Two angles are called complementary when their measures add to 90 degrees. Two angles are called supplementary when their measures add up to 180 degrees.

• Two angles are called supplementary if their sum is 180 degrees.

Two angles are called supplementary when their measures add up to 180 degrees.

• Adjacent Angles are two angles with a common vertex, a common side, and no common interior points.

Adjacent Angles are two angles that share a common vertex, a common side, and no common interior points. (They share a vertex and side, but do not overlap.)

• A linear pair are two adjacent angles whose non common sides are opposite rays. Angles forming a linear pair are supplementary .

A Linear Pair is two adjacent angles whose non-common sides form opposite rays.Two angles forming a linear pair are always supplementary.

• Vertical Angles are two nonadjacent angles formed by two intersecting lines .

Two nonadjacent angles formed by two intersecting lines are vertical angles.
