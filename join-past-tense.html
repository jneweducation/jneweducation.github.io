16. Directions: Write sentences with PAST PERFECT, PRESENT PERFECT, and FUTURE PERFECT TENSES OF THE VERB for each verb below. join write come share make​


1. join

Past perfect: Before I joined this school, I had already completed my elementary education.

Present perfect: I have joined this school for two years, and I have already made many friends.

Future perfect: By the end of this school year, I will have joined the school's basketball team for three seasons.

2. write

Past perfect: Before I wrote the essay, I had reviewed my notes and conducted thorough research.

Present perfect: I have written many essays for this school, and I have received good grades.

Future perfect: By the end of the semester, I will have written three research papers for my classes.

3. come

Past perfect: Before I came to this school, I had attended a different high school.

Present perfect: I have come to this school for four years, and I have seen many changes.

Future perfect: By the time I graduate, I will have come to this school for a total of five years.

4. share

Past perfect: Before I shared my ideas with my classmates, I had already discussed them with my parents.

Present perfect: I have shared many class projects with my classmates, and we have all learned from each other.

Future perfect: By the end of the school year, I will have shared my knowledge and skills with my peers and made a positive impact on the school community.

5. make

Past perfect: Before I made the science project, I had gathered all the necessary materials and equipment.

Present perfect: I have made many projects for this school, and I have received recognition for my creativity.

Future perfect: By the end of the year, I will have made a lasting impression on this school, and I will be remembered for my contributions.

23. B. LET'S DO MORE Directions: Rewrite the sentences from present progressive tense to past progressive tense.1. I am answering the test.2. We are staying with my brother. 3. They are joining the contest. 4. The president is speaking in front. 5. The teachers are reviewing the modules.​


Answer:

1. I am answering the test.

Answer: I WAS answering the test.

2. We are staying with my brother.

Answer: We WERE staying with my brother.

3. They are joining the contest.

Answer: They WERE joining the contest.

4. The president is speaking in front.

Answer: The president WAS speaking in front.

5. The teachers are reviewing the modules.​

Answer: The teachers WERE reviewing the modules.​

Explanation:

The PAST PROGRESSIVE TENSE indicates continuing action, something that was happening, going on, at some point in the past. This tense is formed with the helping "to be" verb, in the past tense, plus the present participle of the verb (with an -ing ending): I was riding my bike all day yesterday.

The present progressive tense (sometimes called the present continuous) is a tense which describes an action which began in the past and is still going on now. ... Any sentence that uses 'is' or 'are' and then a verb with the '–ing' suffix is present progressive tense.
