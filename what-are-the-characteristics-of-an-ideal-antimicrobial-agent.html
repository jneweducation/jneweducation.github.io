The answer in comparing and contrasting of these words are as follows:

Chemotherapeutic agents (synthetic antibiotics) are synthetic antimicrobial medicines (antibiotics) used to treat bacterial and viral infections. Sulfonamides, isoniazid, ethambutol, AZT, nalidixic acid, and chloramphenicol are other examples. Note that a chemotherapeutic drug, as defined by microbiologists, must be used for antimicrobial purposes and excludes synthetic medicines used to treat diseases that are not caused by bacteria. As a result, pharmacology classifies the chemotherapeutic medication used by the microbiologist as a "synthetic antibiotic." Antibiotics are antimicrobial substances produced by bacteria that kill or inhibit bacteria. This is how a microbiologist defines the term. A broader definition of an antibiotic covers any natural substance (derived from any type of cell) that kills or inhibits the growth of other types of cells.
