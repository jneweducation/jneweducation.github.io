6. if the specific weight of liquid is 52lb/ft3 what is it density?​


Answer:

the density of a liquid with a specific weight of 52 lb/ft^3 is approximately 1.62 slugs/ft^3.

Step-by-step explanation:

The specific weight of a liquid is defined as the weight per unit volume, and the density of a liquid is defined as its mass per unit volume.

Since the weight of an object is equal to its mass multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity, we can convert the specific weight of a liquid to its density by multiplying it by the acceleration due to gravity. The acceleration due to gravity is approximately 9.8 m/s^2, so we can use the following formula to convert the specific weight to density:

density = specific weight / (acceleration due to gravity)

For a liquid with a specific weight of 52 lb/ft^3, the density can be calculated as follows:

density = 52 lb/ft^3 / (32.2 ft/s^2) = 1.62 slugs/ft^3

So the density of a liquid with a specific weight of 52 lb/ft^3 is approximately 1.62 slugs/ft^3.
