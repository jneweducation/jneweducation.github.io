25. 1. What is a Tsunami2. Describe what is a Tsunami3. Describe the waves generated by a Tsunami​


Answer:

Tsunami - A series of strong tidal waves caused by displacement underwater, usually an earthquake below or near the ocean floor. The force creates waves that radiates throughout the ocean, forming big waves.

The waves of tsunamis are called seismic sea waves. They don't appear large out in the ocean, but as it travels toward the land, its height increases. The speed of a tsunami depends on the depth of the ocean. In the deep ocean, tsunamis are barely noticeable but can move as fast as a jet plane, over 500 mph. As the waves enter shallow water near land, they slow to approximately 20 or 30 mph. That is still faster than a person can run.
