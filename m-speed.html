4. a plane has a take off speed of 98.4 m/s and requires 14.85 m to reach the speed: Determine the acceleration of the plane and the time required to reach this speed? ​


Answer:

To determine the acceleration of the plane, we can use the following formula:

a = (v_f - v_i) / t

Where a is the acceleration, v_f is the final velocity (takeoff speed), v_i is the initial velocity (0 m/s), and t is the time required to reach the final velocity.

We know that the takeoff speed is 98.4 m/s, and the initial velocity is 0 m/s. We also know that the plane requires 14.85 m to reach the takeoff speed.

To find the time required to reach the takeoff speed, we can use the following formula:

d = (v_f + v_i) / 2 * t

Where d is the distance traveled, v_f is the final velocity, v_i is the initial velocity, and t is the time required to travel the distance.

We know that the distance traveled is 14.85 m, the final velocity is 98.4 m/s, and the initial velocity is 0 m/s.

Plugging in the values, we get:

a = (98.4 m/s - 0 m/s) / t

t = 2 * d / (v_f + v_i) = 2 * 14.85 m / (98.4 m/s + 0 m/s) = 0.302 s

a = (98.4 m/s - 0 m/s) / 0.302 s = 325.82 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the plane is 325.82 m/s^2, and the time required to reach the takeoff speed is 0.302 seconds.

19. A. Calculate the speed and velocity of three cars traveling to the east in different distances and displacements in the same time of 2 hours. 1. 200 m SPEED: ________________________________________________________________ VELOCITY: ____________________________________________________________ 2. 1000 m SPEED: ________________________________________________________________ VELOCITY: ____________________________________________________________ 3. 800 m SPEED: ________________________________________________________________ VELOCITY: ____________________________________________________________​


Answer:

To calculate speed, we use the formula:

Speed = Distance / Time

To calculate velocity, we use the formula:

Velocity = Displacement / Time

Note that distance and displacement are not the same thing. Distance is the total length of the path traveled, while displacement is the straight-line distance between the starting and ending points.

Car traveling 200 m:

Distance = 200 m

Time = 2 hours

Speed = Distance / Time = 200 m / 2 hours = 100 m/hour

Velocity = Displacement / Time = 200 m / 2 hours = 100 m/hour (since the car traveled in a straight line, the distance and displacement are the same)

Therefore, the speed and velocity of the car traveling 200 m to the east in 2 hours are both 100 m/hour.

Car traveling 1000 m:

Distance = 1000 m

Time = 2 hours

Speed = Distance / Time = 1000 m / 2 hours = 500 m/hour

Displacement = 1000 m to the east (since the car traveled in a straight line)

Velocity = Displacement / Time = 1000 m / 2 hours = 500 m/hour

Therefore, the speed and velocity of the car traveling 1000 m to the east in 2 hours are both 500 m/hour.

Car traveling 800 m:

Distance = 800 m

Time = 2 hours

Speed = Distance / Time = 800 m / 2 hours = 400 m/hour

Displacement = 800 m to the east (since the car traveled in a straight line)

Velocity = Displacement / Time = 800 m / 2 hours = 400 m/hour

Therefore, the speed and velocity of the car traveling 800 m to the east in 2 hours are both 400 m/hour.
