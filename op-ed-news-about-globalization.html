Globalization

The term "globalization" is used to describe how technology and international trade have boosted connectedness and interdependence. The ensuing societal and economic changes are also covered by the definition of globalization. It may be pictured as the threads of a huge spider web that has expanded in size and reach over a long period of time.

Further explanation

Technology, cross-border trade in goods and services, as well as movements of money, labor, and information have all contributed to the growing interconnectivity of the world's economies, cultures, and people. This phenomenon is referred to as "globalization." Nations have formed economic alliances over many years to support these initiatives.

Concepts, information, know-how, goods, and services traverse national and international borders as a result of globalization. In the business sector, the term is used to describe interconnected economies that promote free commerce, the free flow of money across countries, and easy access to foreign resources, particularly labor markets, in order to increase profits and benefit society as a whole.

Countries focus on the products and services that provide them a competitive advantage in the global market. This frequently refers to what they are able to produce and provide more affordably than other countries while utilizing less resources. In theory, global production should be more effective, prices should be lower, economic progress should be universal, and every nation should prosper if it concentrates on what it does best.

Policies that promote free trade, open borders, and international cooperation are the main drivers of economic globalization. They provide businesses access to more expansive, growing global labor markets, less costly raw material and component markets, and cheaper raw material and component markets.

Globalization has an impact on national governments, international corporations, and other institutions like colleges and universities. Globalization has an influence on a government's ability to implement certain economic policies as well as a university's capability to diversity and grow its student body.

The phenomenon of globalization is not new, as it has been affecting people and societies for more than two centuries. The old globalization, represented by the colonialist expansion in Europe and sea trade among nations, had important economic and social consequences, both positive and negative, on the new territories, as well as on the European society itself. More recently, however, in the decades at the turn of the 20th and 21st centuries, we are faced with a new globalization, deep and fast, that covers now practically every aspect of human life.

For most authors, recent globalization is an economic, social and cultural process established in the last two or three decades of the 20th Century and the first decade of the 21st Century, whose main features include, on a scale never achieved before:

· growth in international trade of goods and services;

· transnationalization of mega companies;

· free movement of capital without any regulation;

. unprecedented concentration of income;

· privatization of the economy and minimization of the role of governments and nation-states;

· fall of protectionist trade barriers and regulation of international trade, according to the rules of the World Trade Organization (WTO);

· ease of transit of people and goods among several countries of the world;

· expansion of the possibilities of communication due to the emergence of the so-called information society and ease of contact among people due to the emergence of various instruments and tools, among which the Internet.

Several authors, commissions and organizations have been critical regarding that process. The brief 20th Century, a term coined by the historian Eric Hobsbawm to describe the recently completed century, brought, according to the admirable author, an extraordinary “revolution in transportation and communications, which virtually annulled time and distance”. 5. Neodadaism and pop art,conceptual art,op art,the new realism


Answer:

Neo Dadaism— Neo-Dada was a movement with audio, visual and literary manifestations that had similarities in method or intent with earlier Dada artwork. It sought to close the gap between art and daily life, and was a combination of playfulness, iconoclasm, and appropriation.

Pop art— art based on modern popular culture and the mass media, especially as a critical or ironic comment on traditional fine art values.

Conceptual art— art in which the idea or concept presented by the artist is considered more important than its appearance or execution.

Op art— a form of abstract art that gives the illusion of movement by the precise use of pattern and color, or in which conflicting patterns emerge and overlap. Bridget Riley and Victor Vasarely are its most famous exponents.

New realism— a form of realism that was developed at the beginning of the 20th century in opposition to idealism, that emphasizes the distinction between the object and the act of sensation, and that holds the objective world to exist independently of the knowing mind and to be directly knowable.

28. What is the correct APA format in a multi- volume work? Select one: a. Wiener, P. (Ed.). (1973). New York, NY: Scribner's. Dictionary of the history of ideas. b. Dictionary of the history of ideas. Wiener, P. (Ed.). (1973). New York, NY: Scribner's. c. Dictionary of the history of ideas (Vols. 1-4). New York, NY: Scribner's. Wiener, P. (Ed.). (1973). d. Wiener, P. (Ed.). (1973). Dictionary of the history of ideas (Vols. 1-4). New York, NY: Scribner's.


Answer:

D. Wiener, P. (Ed.). (1973). Dictionary of the history of ideas (Vols. 1-4). New York, NY: Scribner's.

Explanation:

A multi-volume work is any set of writing that explores many topics but belongs to one cohesive title. The topics are divided into volumes and are categorized using themes, but sometimes they are merely divided alphabetically. Examples of multi-volume works are anthologies and encyclopedia.

To cite a multi-volume work using the APA format, we have to specify six details:

1. Name(s) of the author(s)

2. Year of publication

3. Title of the multi-volume work

4. Number of volumes

5. City or place of publication

6. Publisher

The details are written in that order, with the year and volume number(s) enclosed in parentheses. The correct answer is

D. Wiener, P. (Ed.). (1973). Dictionary of the history of ideas (Vols. 1-4). New York, NY: Scribner's.
