Unsaturated, Saturated, and Supersaturated Solutions

1. saturated

6. unsaturated

Explanation:

Solutions are made up of solute that is dissolved in a solvent. The amount of solute in a solution can determine the concentration of the solution. A solution can be an unsaturated, saturated, or supersaturated solution. Unsaturated solutions are solutions wherein the dissolved solute is less than the saturation point of the solution. Since it does not meet the saturation point, it can still dissolve more solute.  However, if the dissolved solute in a solution reaches the saturation point, the solution is said to be a saturated solution. If we add more solute to this type of solution, the solute will not be dissolved already. The supersaturated solution contains solute more than what the solvent can dissolve. It contains more solute than the saturated solution. In this type of solution, there will be undissolved solute since the excess solutes will crystallize and form a precipitate.

To determine whether the solution is unsaturated, saturated, or supersaturated solution, we determine the saturation point. This is the maximum amount of solute the solvent can dissolve. Less than this, it is unsaturated; more than this, it is supersaturated; and equal to the saturation point is saturated solution.
