Problem: The quadrilateral ABCD is inscribed in a circle. If m∠BAD = 68 and m∠ABC = 76, find m∠BCD?

Solution: According to the inscribed quadrilateral theorem, an interior angle and it's opposite angle are supplementary.

[tex]m \angle BAD +m \angle BCD = 180 \degree[/tex][tex]m \angle ABC +m \angle CAD = 180 \degree[/tex]Find m∠BCD:

[tex]m \angle BAD +m \angle BCD = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]68 \degree +m \angle BCD = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]m \angle BCD = 180 \degree - 68 \degree[/tex]

[tex]m \angle BCD = 112 \degree[/tex]

- Therefore, the measure of angle BCD is:

[tex] \large \rm m \angle BCD = \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 112 \degree \: }}[/tex]===============================

27. In a triangle bcd, bc = 25 m. and cd = 10 m. the perimeter of the triangle may be.


The perimeter of the triangle may be around 50 > DB > 70.

Step-by-step explanation:

It is known that there is a triangle BCD with the following side lengths:

BC = 25m

CD = 10m

DB = ?

BCD perimeter = BC + CD + DB

So to find the length of the perimeter of the triangle, we need the length of side DB.

Because we only know information on the lengths of sides BC and CD, the length of side DB will depend on how many angles are formed by BC and CD or angle C. So there are 3 possibilities that will happen, namely:

Angle = 90 : DB = 24 (Pythagorean Theory)

Angle > 90 : 24 < DB < 35

Angle < 90 : 15 > DB > 24

So the possible side length of DB based on angle C is 15 < DB < 35.

So that the length of the perimeter of the triangle BCD is:

= 25 + 10 + DB

= 35 + DB

= 35 + (15 > DB > 35)

= 50 > DB > 70

28. ABCD is an isosceles trapezoid if ADC is 2x-10 and ABC is 3x+40 what is BCD


Since ABCD is an isosceles trapezoid, we know that its non-parallel sides (AD and BC) are congruent. Therefore, we can set the two expressions equal to each other and solve for x:

ADC = 2-10x
ABC = 3x+40

2-10x = 3x+40

Adding 10x to both sides gives:

2 = 13x + 40

Subtracting 40 from both sides gives:

-38 = 13x

Dividing by 13 gives:

x = -2.92 (rounded to two decimal places)

Now that we know x, we can substitute it into either expression to find the measures of ADC and ABC:

ADC = 2-10x
ADC = 2 - 10(-2.92)
ADC = 31.2

ABC = 3x+40
ABC = 3(-2.92) + 40
ABC = 30.24

Since BCD is an isosceles triangle, we know that the measure of angle BCD is equal to half the measure of angle ABC. Therefore:

BCD = 0.5(ABC)
BCD = 0.5(30.24)
BCD = 15.12

So, the measure of angle BCD is 15.12 degrees.
