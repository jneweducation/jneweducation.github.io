3. What do you call <NEA ?​


Answer:

The National Education Association of the United States Committee on Secondary School Studies known as the NEA Committee of Ten was a working group of educators that convened in 1892. They were charged with taking stock of current practices in American high schools and making recommendations for future practice. They collected data via surveys and interviews with educators across the United States, met in a series of multi-day committee meetings, and developed consensus and dissenting reports.

14. What do you think are the problems farmers face today and will face in the nea future


Answer:

There are many challenges that farmers face today and are likely to face in the future. Some of the key challenges include:

Climate change: Farmers are on the front lines of climate change and are often among the hardest hit by extreme weather events such as droughts, floods, and heatwaves. These events can cause crop failures and reduce yields, leading to financial losses for farmers.Soil health: Soil is a critical resource for farmers, but it is increasingly under threat from overuse, erosion, and pollution. Poor soil health can lead to reduced crop yields and make it more difficult for farmers to grow healthy, nutritious food.Pests and diseases: Pests and diseases can cause significant damage to crops, reducing yields and potentially leading to the loss of entire crops. Farmers must constantly monitor their crops for signs of pests and diseases and take steps to control them.Market fluctuations: The prices of agricultural products can be highly volatile, and farmers must navigate these fluctuations in order to make a profit. This can be particularly challenging for small-scale farmers who may not have the resources to withstand prolonged periods of low prices.Access to capital: Many farmers, particularly small-scale farmers in developing countries, face challenges in accessing the capital they need to invest in their operations. This can make it difficult for them to adopt new technologies and techniques that could improve their productivity and profitability.Labor shortages: Farmers often struggle to find enough workers to help with planting, harvesting, and other key tasks. This can be especially challenging in regions where there are limited labor pools or where there are seasonal fluctuations in demand for farm labor.

Overall, the challenges facing farmers today are complex and multifaceted, and addressing them will require a range of solutions
