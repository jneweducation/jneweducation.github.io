8. an ncr committee consists of 5 representatives from manila, 6 from quezon city and 4 from makati. in how many ways can a subcommittee be chosen consisting of two members from different cities?​


Answer:

There are 900 ways.

Step-by-step explanation:

This is a combination problem, where it asks for a number of ways to make a set wherein the order of the elements do not matter. To solve this problem, we need to know the formula for combination which is:

[tex]C (n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!r!}[/tex]

Wherein we look for the number of combinations of [tex]n[/tex] things taken [tex]r[/tex] at a time.

We use this formula to find the number of combinations for each group of city representatives:

1) Combinations of subcommittee from MANILA:

[tex]C(5,2) = \frac{5!}{(5-2)!2!} = 10[/tex]

2) Combinations of subcommittee from QUEZON CITY:

[tex]C (6,2) = \frac{6!}{(6-2)!2!} = 15[/tex]

3) Combinations of subcommittee from MAKATI:

[tex]C (4, 2) = \frac{4!}{(4-2)!2!} = 6[/tex]

Then, following the Fundamental Counting Principle, we multiply all the combinations together:

4) [tex]10[/tex]×[tex]15[/tex]×[tex]6[/tex] = 900 ways

17. An ncr committee consists of 5 representatives from manila, 6 from quezon city and 4 from makati. in how many ways can a subcommittee be chosen consisting of two members from different cities?​


Answer:

74

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us take all the possible city combinations.

Case 1: Manila and Makati

There are 5 representatives from Manila and 4 from Makati, making a total of 5 x 4 = 20 combinations.

Case 2: Manila and Quezon

There are 5 representatives from Manila and 6 from Quezon, making a total of 5 x 6 = 30 combinations.

Case 3: Quezon and Makati

There are 6 representatives from Quezon and 4 from Makati, making a total of 6 x 4 = 24 combinations.

The total number of combinations is the sum of all the numbers of possible combinations, 20+30+24 = 74 total combinations.

21. 19. There are some children in the botanical garden. 1/3 of them are from Makati City. 0.5 of them are from Quezon City and the rest are from Manila City. If there are 30 children from Manila City How many children are there altogether?​


Let's use the information given to determine the number of children from Makati City and Quezon City.

Let's assume that the total number of children is represented by the variable "x".

From the given information, we know that:

1/3 of the children are from Makati City, which means there are (1/3)x children from Makati.

0.5 of the children are from Quezon City, which means there are (0.5)x children from Quezon.

The rest of the children are from Manila City, which means there are 30 children from Manila.

To find the value of "x", we can set up an equation:

(1/3)x + (0.5)x + 30 = x

Simplifying the equation, we get:

(0.83)x = 30

Dividing both sides by 0.83, we get:

x = 36.14 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, there are approximately 36 children altogether in the botanical garden.

28. There are 4 routes from La Union to Quezon City and 6 routes from Quezon City to Makati City. How many different routes can be taken if Aaron needs to travel from La Union to Makati City? Illustrate it through a tree diagram.


Answer:

There are 24 different routes that can be taken if he needs to travel from La Union to Makati City.

Please see the attached file for the illustration of the tree diagram.

Step-by-step explanation:

This problem is an application of the Fundamental Principles of Counting which is part of the Statistics and Probability Curriculum. This is one of the basics of combinatorics. It is important that we know how to determine the total possible outcomes of a particular event since these are useful in making conclusions or generalizations as you study higher-level statistics.

Some Combinatorics TopicsPermutationCombination

The fundamental principle of counting defines as if an even has [tex]n[/tex] possible outcomes or ways and another event as [tex]m[/tex] possible outcomes or ways, then there [tex]n \times m[/tex] possible outcomes or ways if these two events happen together. So basically, the total possible outcomes or ways, considering all of the events, is just the product of the individual outcomes.

So if there are 4 routes from La Union to Quezon City and 6 routes from Quezon City to Makati City, then there are 24 different routes that can be taken if he needs to travel from La Union to Makati City.

This is simply multiplying 4 by 6, as a result, we have 24.

Please check the attachment for the tree diagram.

Looking at the tree diagram. The routes from La Union to Quezon City are in different colors, namely, red, green, violet, and yellow. Let's label the route from Quezon City to Makati City with numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.

Listing all the possible routes, we will have the following.

Red1, Red2, Red3, Red4, Red5, Red6

Green1, Green2, Green3, Green4, Green5, Green6

Violet1, Violet2, Violet3, Violet4, Violet5, Violet6

Yellow1, Yellow2, Yellow3, Yellow4, Yellow5, Yellow6

Thus, there are 24 possible routes that Aaron can take.
