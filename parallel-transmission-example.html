2. how do you understand parallel transmissions​


Answer:

In a parallel transmission, multiple bits of data are transmitted at the same time over multiple parallel channels or wires. This is in contrast to a serial transmission, in which data is transmitted one bit at a time over a single channel or wire.

Parallel transmission can be faster than serial transmission because multiple bits can be transmitted simultaneously, rather than waiting for each bit to be transmitted in sequence. However, parallel transmission requires more wires or channels and is more complex to implement than serial transmission.

There are many different ways to implement parallel transmission, including parallel buses in computers, parallel ports for connecting devices to computers, and parallel transmission in networking. The specific details of how parallel transmission works can vary depending on the particular application and the requirements of the system.


3. How do you understand Parallel transmissions?​


Explanation:

In data transmission, parallel communication is a method of conveying multiple binary digits simultaneously. It contrasts with serial communication, which conveys only a single bit at a time.
