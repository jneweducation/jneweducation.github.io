9. We invested $10,000 into two bank accounts. One account earns 14% per year, the other accountcains 8%per year. How much did we invest into each account if after the fust year, the combinedinterest from the twoaccounts is $1238?​


Answer:

$7300 ; $2700

Step-by-step explanation:

Assume that the money in Bank A earns 14% per year, and that of Bank B earns 8% per year.

Now, let x be the amount of money invested in Bank A. Using the first sentence of the question and the previous declaration, we will know that 10000 - x is the amount of money invested in Bank B.

We will now start crafting the equation needed for this problem.

We will multiply 14% to the amount in Bank A and 8% for Bank B to compute for their profits.

Now, we have the expression 14%(x) + 8%(10000-x).

The next step is to equate it into the total profit. Since in this case the total is $1238, we now have the equation 14%(x) + 8%(10000-x) = 1238.

To make the equation simpler, we can multiply 100 into both sides to remove the percent symbol.

Now, we have 14x + 8(10000-x) = 123800.

Solving this,

14x + 80000 - 8x = 123800

14x - 8x = 123800 - 80000

6x = 43800

x = 43800 / 6 = 7300

We now solved how much money was invested into Bank A. Now to find the money invested on Bank B, remember that we used 10000 - x a while ago as a substitution for this. Now, we just have to substitute.

10000 - x = 10000 - 7300 = 2700

Therefore, $7300 was invested in the first account, and $2700 was invested in the second account.

Answer:

FV = PV (1 + r)^n

where:

FV is the future value

PV is the present value (i.e. the initial investment amount)

r is the interest rate

n is the number of years the money is invested

Plugging in the values from the problem, we get:

FV = $50,000 (1 + 0.04)^2 = $50,000 (1.04)^2 = $50,000 * 1.0816 = $54,080

Therefore, the future value of your investment after 2 years will be $54,080

18. Laura invested P12,000 in two bank accounts. One of the accounts pays 6% annual interest, and the other account pays 5% annual interest. If the combined interest earned in both accounts after a year was P700, how much money was invested in each account? RESCA FORMAT REPRESENTATION EQUATION SOLUTION CHECKING NEED NOW


REPRESENTATION:

Let x be the amount invested in the account paying 6% annual interest.

Then, the amount invested in the account paying 5% annual interest is 12000 - x.

The total interest earned from both accounts is P700.

EQUATION:

The total interest earned can be expressed as the sum of the interest earned from each account, so we have:

0.06x + 0.05(12000 - x) = 700

SOLUTION:

Expanding the equation and simplifying, we get:

0.06x + 600 - 0.05x = 700

0.01x = 100

x = 10000

Therefore, Laura invested P10,000 in the account paying 6% annual interest and P2,000 in the account paying 5% annual interest.

CHECKING:

To check if our solution is correct, we can calculate the total interest earned from each account and verify that it adds up to P700.

Interest earned from 6% account = 0.06 * 10000 = 600

Interest earned from 5% account = 0.05 * 2000 = 100

Total interest earned = 600 + 100 = 700

Therefore, our solution is correct.
