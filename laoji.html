5. "A leader is best when people barely know he exist, when his work is done, his aim fulfilled, they will say: we did it ourselves. "-Laozi what does this mean?


This quote by Lao Tzu may mean that a leader, may be considered a good one at what he does, when he leads people passively. He may not tell and boast what he have done, but you cannot underestimate the effort he has exerted. On top of this, although he may work "behind the scenes", he ensures that his goals and aims are fulfilled, and never forgets the fact that he will not be a good leader, without the people who followed him and did their best to finish a task.

23. Why is there debate on Laozi being a historical or a mythical figure?​


Answer:

Many historians consider Laozi to be a mythical figure rather than a historical one. His existence is widely contested, as even the literal translation of his name (Laozi, meaning Old Master) indicates a deity rather than a man.
