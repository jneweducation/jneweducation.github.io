13. The ability of the body to change direction quickly


Agility is a person's ability to quickly change the direction of movement without losing balance. The higher the agility capacity of an athlete, the better his physical handling ability. Because this agility is the basic ability of an athlete.

Agility Benefits

Agility has many benefits. Specifically for:

-In order to improve their performance, athletes or sportsmen must practice agility regularly.

-It facilitates the mastery of difficult techniques in all related sports. Because with good agility, an athlete also has a proportional body.

-Facilitate orientation towards the opponent.

-To facilitate orientation with respect to the environment.

-Agility is also useful for coordinating certain movements or stimuli.

Factors affecting athletic agility

1. Balancing

To be agile, a person needs the power of balance to maintain a proper body position. This balance must be achieved under both static and dynamic conditions.

2. Flexibility

Flexibility is a person's ability to maximize the flexibility of their limbs when moving. This optimizes the widest possible shared space. This flexibility is influenced by the shape of the joints and the elasticity of the muscles and ligaments of the athlete himself.

3. Speed

Speed is the ability of an athlete to move quickly and steadily. To move quickly, an athlete must maximize the dynamics of his limbs as efficiently and effectively as possible in the shortest possible time.

 4. Coordination

The last factor that affects agility is physical coordination. These are complex biological motor skills, including the connection of nerve impulses and the ability of the limbs to respond to external stimuli.
