5. 4. Explain the difference between replication, randomization, and local control.​


Replication, randomization, and local control are all important concepts in research design and are used to help ensure the validity and reliability of research findings. Here's a brief explanation of each concept:

Replication: Replication involves repeating a research study or experiment to test its validity and reliability. In other words, if the results of a study can be replicated by other researchers using the same methods and procedures, it provides stronger evidence that the findings are valid and reliable. Replication is important because it helps to rule out the possibility that the original results were due to chance or other factors.

Randomization: Randomization involves randomly assigning participants to different groups or conditions in a study. This helps to reduce the impact of bias and other extraneous variables that could affect the results of the study. Randomization ensures that each participant has an equal chance of being assigned to any of the groups or conditions, which helps to control for individual differences that could impact the results.

Local Control: Local control involves manipulating or controlling for specific variables within a study to test the effects of other variables of interest. For example, in an experiment examining the effects of a new medication, local control might involve controlling for factors like age, gender, or pre-existing medical conditions that could impact the results. Local control is important because it helps to isolate the effects of the variables of interest and rule out alternative explanations for the results.

In summary, replication, randomization, and local control are all important concepts in research design that help to ensure the validity and reliability of research findings. Replication involves repeating a study to test its validity and reliability, randomization involves randomly assigning participants to different groups or conditions to reduce the impact of bias, and local control involves manipulating or controlling for specific variables within a study to isolate the effects of the variables of interest.

20. does the central government exercise direct control and supervsion of all local government units (LGUs)​


answer:

7 "The President under the Constitution has the right to "exercise general supervision over all local governments as may be provided by law." This constitutional provision carefully excludes the power of control over all local governments from the scope of the President's authority. General supervision is not the equivalent of control and denotes a less inclusive authority. The President has to exercise this general supervisory power over local governments not as he pleased but as Congress provides. It is, therefore, obvious that local governments are subject to the control of Congress which has the authority to prescribe the procedure by which the President may perform his constitutional power of general supervision.

"The Constitution in this respect give the President a more limited power over local governments than what the Jones Law gave to the Governor General. The Governor General had supervision and control over them." (Sinco, Philippine Political Law, 10th ed., p. 294.
