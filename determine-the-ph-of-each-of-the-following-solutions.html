2. Determine the ph of a 0.300m solution of aniline hydrochloride.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Determine the concentration of the conjugate acid of aniline at equilibrium by using ICE table.

[tex]\begin{array}{lccccccc} & \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_3\text{Cl}(aq) & \rightarrow & \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_3^+(aq) & + & \text{Cl}^-(aq) \\ \text{Initial} \: (M): & 0.300 & & 0 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (M): & -0.300 & & +0.300 & & +0.300 \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (M): & 0 & & 0.300 & & 0.300 \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 2: Summarize the concentrations for the dissociation of the conjugate acid of aniline by using ICE table.

[tex]\begin{array}{lccccccc} & \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_3^+(aq) & \rightleftharpoons & \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_2(aq) & + & \text{H}^+(aq) \\ \text{Initial} \: (M): & 0.300 & & 0 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (M): & -x & & +x & & +x \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (M): & 0.300 - x & & x & & x \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 3: Write the Ka expression.

[tex]K_{\text{a}} = \dfrac{[\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_2][\text{H}^+]}{[\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_3^+]}[/tex]

Step 4: Substitute the given values to the Ka expression.

The Ka of C₆H₅NH₃⁺ is 2.6 × 10⁻⁵.

[tex]\begin{aligned} 2.6 \times 10^{-5} & = \frac{(x)(x)}{0.300 - x} \\ 2.6 \times 10^{-5} & = \frac{x^2}{0.300 - x} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Apply approximation method to solve for the hydrogen ion concentration at equilibrium.

Note: 0.300 - x ≈ 0.300

[tex]\begin{aligned} 2.6 \times 10^{-5} & = \frac{x^2}{0.300} \\ x^2 & = (2.6 \times 10^{-5})(0.300) \\ x^2 & = 7.8 \times 10^{-6} \\ x & = \sqrt{7.8 \times 10^{-6}} \\ x & = 2.7928 \times 10^{-3} \: M\end{aligned}[/tex]

Testing the approximation

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{value} & = \frac{[\text{H}^+]}{[\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_3^+]_{\text{initial}}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{2.7928 \times 10^{-3} \: M}{0.300 \: M} \times 100\% \\ & = 0.93\% \end{aligned}[/tex]

Since the value is less than 5%, the approximation is valid. Therefore,

[tex][\text{H}^+] = x = 2.7928 \times 10^{-3} \: M[/tex]

Step 6: Calculate the pH of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = -\text{log} \: [\text{H}^+] \\ & = -\text{log} \: (2.7928 \times 10^{-3}) \\ & = \boxed{2.554} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pH of 0.300-M aniline hydrochloride is 2.554.

Answer:

Question:

1. A solution of a weak acid, H2X, with a concentration of 0.1 M has a pH = 4.7. Determine the acid Ka! [4x10-9]

2. A solution has a pH = 1. How many grams of solid NaOH (Mr = 40) must be added to one liter of this solution to bring the pH to 3? [3.96 grams]

Answer:

1. From pH = 4.7 = 5 – 0.3 = 5 – log 2, we get [H+] = 2 x 10-5.

Since H2X is a weak acid, [H+] is calculated by the formula:

[H+] = [tex] \sqrt{K_a.M}[/tex]

We substitute the values of [H+] = 2 x 10-5 and M = 0.1 M based on the problem information, we get:

2 x 10-5 = [tex]\sqrt{K_a. 0.1}[/tex], the square of the left and right sides

4 x 10-10 = Ka x 10-1

So Ka = 4 x 10-9

2. Initial pH = 1, meaning [H+] = 10-1 while final pH is 3, meaning [H+] changes to 10-3.

Since the volume of the solution is 1 liter, the moles of H+ change from 10-1 moles to 10-3 moles.

This means that there has been a reaction between H+ and OH- as much as (10-1 – 10-3) mol = 99 . 10-3 moles.

NaOH is a strong base with a valence of 1, so moles of OH– = moles of NaOH = 99.10-3 .

So, mass of NaOH = n . Mr = 99. 100-3 . 40 = 3.96 grams

15. In a typical analysis, 15 mL of an aqueous solution containing unknown amount of acetylcholine had a pH of 7.65. When incubated with acetylcholinesterase, the pH of the solution decreased to 6.87. Assuming that there was no buffer in the assay mixture, determine the number of moles of acetylcholine in the 15 mL sample.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the hydrogen ion concentration of acetylcholine before incubation.

Note that the pH of acetylcholine before incubation is 7.65

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{H}^+]_{\text{before}} & = 10^{-\text{pH}} \\ & = 10^{-7.65} \\ & = 2.24 \times 10^{-8} \: M \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the hydrogen ion concentration of acetylcholine after incubation.

Note that the pH of acetylcholine after incubation is 6.87

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{H}^+]_{\text{after}} & = 10^{-\text{pH}} \\ & = 10^{-6.87} \\ & = 1.35 \times 10^{-7} \: M \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the difference in hydrogen ion concentration before and after incubation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{H}^+] & = [\text{H}^+]_{\text{after}} - [\text{H}^+]_{\text{before}} \\ & = 1.35 \times 10^{-7} \: M - 2.24 \times 10^{-8} \\ & = 1.13 \times 10^{-7} \: M \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the number of moles of acetylcholine.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of acetylcholine} & = 15 \: \cancel{\text{mL}} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{L}}}{1000 \: \cancel{\text{mL}}} \times \frac{1.13 \times 10^{-7} \: \text{mol}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{L}}} \\ & = \boxed{1.7 \times 10^{-9} \: \text{mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the number of moles of acetylcholine in the 15-mL sample is 1.7 × 10⁻⁹ mol.

30. Acid XYZ (MW = 180 g/mol) is prepared by dissolving 3.60g into a 1.00 L solution. The pH of this solution was determined to be 2.60. What is the kA?


The Ka = 3.15.10⁻⁴

Further explanation  

pH is the degree of acidity of a solution that depends on the concentration of H ions. The greater the value the more acidic the solution and the smaller the pH.  

pH = - log [H⁺]  

So that the two quantities between pH and [H⁺] are inversely proportional because they are associated with negative values.  

According to Arrhenius, Acid is a compound which in water releases H⁺ ions

Electrolytes (acids or bases) with a small degree of ionization are classified as weak electrolytes

H⁺ concentration and Molarity can be related to the formula

[tex]\rm [H^+]=\sqrt{Ka.M}[/tex]

Ka = acid ionization constant

M = molarity

Acid XYZ (MW = 180 g/mol) is prepared by dissolving 3.60g into a 1.00 L solution and the pH  :  2.60

Molarity of Acid

[tex]\rm mol=\dfrac{3.6~g}{180}=0.02[/tex]

[tex]\rm Molarity=\dfrac{mol}{volume(L)}=\dfrac{0.02}{1}=0.02~mol/L[/tex]

Concentration H :[H⁺]

pH = 2.6, then

[tex]\rm [H^+]=10^{-2.6}[/tex]

Ka

[tex]\rm 10^{-2.6}=\sqrt{Ka.0.02}\\\\10^{-5.2}=Ka.0.02\\\\Ka=\dfrac{10^{-5.2}}{0.02}\\\\Ka=5.10^{-4.2}=\boxed{\bold{3.15.10^{-4}}}[/tex]
