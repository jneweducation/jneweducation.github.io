Philippine Theatrical Forms

Zarzuela

ELEMENTS SHORT DESCRIPTION COSTUMES PROPS SETTINGS

A popular Spanish opera form that features a unique combination of spoken dialogue, music, and dance.

The performers wear elaborate and colorful costumes that reflect the period in which the story is set.

Props are typically minimal and used only to enhance the story and setting.

The settings vary depending on the production, but often include traditional Spanish architecture and landscapes.

Shadow Dance

A form of dance theatre that features performers using only their bodies and a screen to create intricate shadow images.

Costumes are non-existent in this form as performers are only seen through their shadows.

Props are also minimal, with performers using only their bodies to create the shadow images.

Settings vary depending on the story being told, but often feature minimalistic background elements to enhance the shadow images.

Puppetry

A form of theatre that involves using puppets to tell a story.

Costumes for the puppets are typically brightly colored and reflective of the characters they are portraying.

Props are used to enhance the story being told and often include simple set pieces or objects that the puppets interact with.

Settings are created using elaborate puppet stages or sets that allow the actors to move the puppets freely and bring the story to life.

Drama

A form of theatre that features a more serious tone and explores heavier topics such as death, love, and morality.

Costumes are typically realistic and reflective of the time period or setting of the story being told.

Props are used to enhance the story being told and often include realistic set pieces or objects that the actors interact with.

Settings are designed to enhance the emotional impact of the story and often include elaborate set pieces and lighting.

Comedia

A humorous form of theatre that features exaggerated characters, physical comedy, and witty dialogue.

Costumes are often brightly colored and reflect the exaggerated nature of the characters.

Props are used to enhance the comedic elements of the story and often include exaggerated set pieces or objects that the actors interact with.

Settings are often minimalistic and used only to enhance the comedic elements of the story.

Senakulo

A form of theatre that depicts the story of the passion and death of Jesus Christ.

Costumes are intended to be historically accurate and reflective of the time period being depicted.

Props are used to enhance the story and often include set pieces such as the cross, thorn crowns, and other religious objects.

Settings are often minimalistic and designed to enhance the emotional impact of the story.

Teacher Samute Aspe

A form of Indigenous theatre that features traditional dances, music, and storytelling.

Costumes are reflective of the traditional attire of the community the story is being told in.

Props are often minimalistic and used only to enhance the storytelling.

Settings are designed to reflect the traditional landscape and surroundings of the community where the story is taking place.

20. 2. Which of the following representation of a wet that well-defined description of the elements and it is enclosed in curly braces? 1. Set C, Roster Form B. Set Builder Form D Statement Form​


WAYS OF EXPRESSING SETS

Answer: The correct answer is B. Set Builder Form.

Step-by-step explanation:

There are different ways to represent a set. Here are the two most common ways to represent a set:

ROSTER NOTATION - This method is also known as the LISTING METHOD. This representation of the set encloses the list of possible elements with common properties inside the curly braces, where each element is separated by a comma from one another. Example, the set is composed of all positive even integers less than 10, so we will express this set as: P = {2, 4, 6, 8} We did not include 10 even though it is an even number because it says that it should be less than 10.SET BUILDER NOTATION - This method is also known as the RULE METHOD. This representation also uses curly braces to enclose the description of the elements about a certain property. We will take the same example in No. 1. Instead of constructing a set like above, we will express it as: P = {x l x is a positive even integer, x < 10} This can be read as: Set P is equal to the element x, such that x is a positive even integer less than 10.
