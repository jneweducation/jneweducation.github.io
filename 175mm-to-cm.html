17. Find the Volume 8 12 cm 6m 8mm 15mm 5m​


To find the volume of a rectangular prism, we multiply the length, width, and height. However, we need to make sure that all the measurements are in the same units before we perform the calculation. In this case, we have measurements in centimeters, millimeters, and meters, so we need to convert everything to the same unit. Let's convert everything to millimeters, since that is the smallest unit given:

8 cm = 80 mm (since 1 cm = 10 mm)

6 m = 6000 mm (since 1 m = 1000 mm)

8 mm = 8 mm

15 mm = 15 mm

5 m = 5000 mm (since 1 m = 1000 mm)

Now we can multiply to find the volume:

Volume = length x width x height

Volume = 80 mm x 6000 mm x 8 mm x 15 mm x 5000 mm

Volume = 3.84 x 10^16 cubic millimeters

Therefore, the volume of the rectangular prism is 3.84 x 10^16 cubic millimeters.

26. A cylinder with a movable piston contains 250 cm air at 15°C. If the pressure is kept constant,at what temperature would you expect the volume to be 175cm?


Good Day

Problem:

A cylinder with a movable piston contains 250 cm³ air at 15°C. If the pressure is kept constant,at what temperature would you expect the volume to be 175 cm³?

Note: in solving any gas law problems, kelvin temperature will be used.

kelvin = degree celsius + 273.15

Kelvin = 15°C + 273.15  = 288.15 K

Given Data:

V₁ (initial volume) = 250 cm³                      

V₂  (final volume) = 175 cm³

T₁ (initial temperature)= 15°C  or 288.15 K

T₂  (final temperature) = ? unknown

Solution:

Charles' Law

states that volume is directly proportional to kelvin temperature of gases if amount of gas and pressure are constant. If temperature decreases, volume decreases.

We can solve the problem using the formula T₂  = V₂T₁  / V₁

T₂  = V₂T₁  / V₁

     = 175 cm³ × 288.15 K  / 250 cm³     (cancel unit cm³ )

     = 50426.25 K / 250

     = 201.705 K

Answer:

The temperature will be 201.705 K
