Answer:

distance = 2469.6km.

Step-by-step explanation:

speed of sound: 1234.8km/h

time: 1100H to 1300H = 2hours

distance= speed x time

distance = (1234.8km/h)(2hours)

distance = 2469.6km.

5. it is a modern logo designs were created on the early 1900s, evolving alongside mass printing 1900s​


Answer:

The history of logos goes back to ancient family crests, hieroglyphs and symbolism. Early versions of logos developed in the Middle Ages (around 1300 AD), as shops and pubs used signage to represent what they did. The first modern logo designs were created in the early 1900s, evolving alongside mass printing.

Read on for a quick guided tour through the history of logo design, that will highlight the historical connections, and help anyone hoping to design a logo to create something more powerful and effective.

Ancient foundations of symbolism in graphic arts

—

ancient pottery

the white nefer-sign, black neb-sign and the wedjat-eye can be read as “all good and healthy things”. Via Guity Novin.

Between 70,000 BC and 7000 BC, primitive peoples from all over the world laid the foundations of the graphic arts by painting animals in caves. Around 8000 BC, people in Assyria, Egypt, Carthage, Persia, Media and Sumer created pottery that communicated aesthetic, ethical, cultural, socio-political and religious information.

Even in these distant, primitive stretches of history, people and cultures were representing themselves and their ideas with symbols and illustrations. Nowhere was that more apparent than in Ancient Egypt, starting around the fourth millennium BC. Not only did the Egyptians develop hieroglyphics, a formal writing system, where images represented words or sounds, but they were also prolific artists. Their paintings and sculpture included specific symbolic images and colors that held specific meanings.

egyptian grid design

Ancient Egyptians used a grid system in their design, much like many logo designers do today. Via the Houston Museum of Natural Science.

Between 2125 and 1991 BC, grids appeared in Egyptian designs. This development is essential to logo design, because it ensures that artists effectively maintain proportions and ratios—and guarantees a uniform reproduction of the same design.

Not to say that the Egyptians had a monopoly on using images symbolically. During the same timeframe, the roots of calligraphy in the form of characters developed in China. Here each word or idea had its own symbol, and this foundation influenced later languages, even those that were less visual (like English).

26. Modern dance was developed in the early 1900’s the time when dancers came out against the stiffness and restraints of classical ballet.


Modern dance was developed in the early 1900s, the time when dancers came out against the stiffness and restraints of classical ballet. [TRUE]

It created its own techniques, costumes, shoes, and gave more attention to self-expression and individual creativity rather than technical expertise.

Modern dance has its own characteristics that make this type of dance growing. Generally, the influence of the development of modern dance is based on the creativity and innovation of the art activists.

Types of Modern Dance

Here are the types of modern dance:

1. Ballet dance

Ballet dance developed in the 18th century AD and comes from the Italian language, namely ballo which means dance. When viewed in more detail, the movement in ballet is similar to fencing. The difference is that this movement combines artistic elements, such as music, costumes, and stage decorations.

2. Tango dance

According to the book Art of Culture by Yoyok RM, et al, the tango dance is a social dance originating from Latin America and has been popular all over the world.

The rhythm is based on a 2/4 or 4/4 count, while the tempo is moderate. This dance movement is very attractive because it is done with steps that give the impression and feeling of floating.

3. Flamenco dance

Flamenco dance is a type of Spanish dance. Its distinctive feature is the rhythmic pattern of various strokes of the dancer's feet, namely the pounding of the toe, heel, or the entire sole. This dance is usually accompanied by guitar, applause, pounding feet, and shouts from the audience.

4. Salsa dance

Salsa is a dance in pairs related to salsa music. The word salsa comes from Spanish which means sauce which means taste or style.

5. Break dance

Break dance is a dance that comes from a group of black American workers. The form of the dance is in the form of broken movements and is acrobatic.

This dance was also born at the same time as the development of rap music which was famous in the black working American community.

6. Contemporary dance

This dance is accompanied by a mix of traditional musical instruments with the addition of unusual modern musical instruments, for example songs from simple ones to using computer music programs, such as midi, Nuendo, and even orchestras.

7. Waltz dance

Waltz is a type of dance in pairs that are danced in 3/4 bars with different tempos. In Germany, for example, this dance is performed at a slow tempo. Meanwhile in Vienna, this dance is performed at a faster and more vibrant tempo.
