27. how many moles of H2O are produced by the conplete reaction of 10.0 mole of H2 and 5.0 mole of 02?​


Solution:

Step 1: Write the balanced equation.

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Step 2: Determine the mole ratios needed.

Using H₂

mole ratio = 2 mol H₂ : 2 mol H₂O

Using O₂

mole ratio = 1 mol O₂ : 2 mol H₂O

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of H₂O produced by each reactant.

Using H₂

[tex]\text{moles of H₂O = 10.0 mol H₂} × \frac{\text{2 mol H₂O}}{\text{2 mol H₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of H₂O = 10.0 mol}[/tex]

Using O₂

[tex]\text{moles of H₂O = 5.0 mol O₂} × \frac{\text{2 mol H₂O}}{\text{1 mol O₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of H₂O = 10.0 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Determine the limiting reactant.

Since the two reactants produce equal number of moles of H₂O

[tex]\text{There is no limiting reactant. The reactants are both consumed.}[/tex]

Step 5: Determine the number of moles of H₂O produced.

Since there is no limiting reactant

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of H₂O = 10.0 mol}}[/tex]
