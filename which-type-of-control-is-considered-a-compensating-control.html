They are now implementing more inclusive ways to support their employees' food needs and give them the choice.

10. identify the factors that managers consider in setting a compensation policy and explain their importance​


Answer:

1. Years of experience and education level

It probably goes without saying, but the more experience and education a candidate has, the higher their expected compensation. So, if you’re hoping to attract job seekers with master’s degrees or more than 5 years’ experience, you need be ready and willing to compensate accordingly.

2. Industry

Workers with similar, or even the same job title can expect vastly different wages depending on what industry they’re in. There are many reasons for this discrepancy – in some cases their job function may be critical to a particular industry, or it may simply be a matter of one industry being considerably larger than the others.

3. Location

Cost of living, a major factor to consider when determining compensation, is largely dependent on location and, more specifically, the cost of housing. This is at least partially why salaries in large urban areas are generally higher than salaries for similar positions in more rural locations. However, with the surge in remote work, many employers have shifted to role-based compensation, rather than location-based. Do some research to see what the trend is in your field.

4. In-demand skill sets

When it comes to determining compensation, key skills may be an even more reliable metric to compare against than job title. After all, different companies may have very different definitions of the same job title. On top of that, many skill sets can apply to a wide variety of roles – all of which are effectively competing for the same talent. That’s why it’s important for employers to consider the value of key skills when determining compensation.

5. Supply and demand

It’s crucial to be aware of the availability of relevant talent in the geographic region where you’re recruiting. If you’re recruiting in an area where the demand for a certain skill sets and experience outweighs the supply, you should expect to pay more in order to attract talent.

24. Guys pahelp naman!Organization mamagement toExplore the different types of wages and compensation that are commonly used in the Philippines, including hourly wages, salary, bonuses, and benefits. Consider the pros and cons of each type and how they may impact workers and businesses.​


Answer:

Hourly Wages: This is the most common type of wage in the Philippines, and it is generally used for employees who are paid for the hours they work. Pros: Hourly wages are a good way for businesses to accurately keep track of time spent on a job, and they are often easier to manage than salary or other types of compensation. Additionally, hourly wages can be adjusted to reflect the cost of living and other factors. Cons: Hourly wages can be difficult to manage in terms of budgeting, and they may not always be enough to cover the cost of living.

Salary: This type of wage is usually used for salaried employees, and it is usually paid out on a monthly basis. Pros: Salaried employees are usually more motivated to work hard, as they are guaranteed a certain amount of income regardless of hours worked. Additionally, salary-based wages are easier to budget for, as they are more consistent. Cons: Salaried employees may not always be motivated to work hard, as they are not always rewarded for their effort. Additionally, salary-based wages may not always be enough to cover the cost of living.

Bonuses: Bonuses are additional payments that are made to employees as a reward for their good performance. Pros: Bonuses are a great way to reward employees for their hard work, and they can be a great incentive for employees to stay with a company. Additionally, bonuses can be used to attract and retain valuable employees. Cons: Bonuses can be costly for businesses, and they may not always be the best way to motivate employees.

Benefits: Benefits are additional payments or services that are provided to employees to help them with their daily living expenses. Pros: Benefits can be a great way to attract and retain employees, as they provide additional financial security and stability. Additionally, benefits can help to increase employee morale, as they are a sign of appreciation from the employer. Cons: Benefits are often costly for businesses, and they may not always be appreciated by employees. Additionally, some benefits can be difficult to manage and enforce.
