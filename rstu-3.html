9. Given RT=10.210.2 and SU=30.630.6, find the area of rhombus RSTU. Round your answer to the nearest tenth if necessary.


Detailed aswer

To find the area of a rhombus, we need to multiply the lengths of its diagonals and divide the result by 2.

In this case, we are not given the diagonals directly, but we can use the fact that the diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular bisectors of each other. That means they intersect at a right angle and divide each other into two equal parts.

Let's label the point where the diagonals intersect as V. Then, RV = TV = 1/2 * RT = 5.1 and SV = UV = 1/2 * SU = 15.3.

Now we can use the formula for the area of a rhombus:

Area = (diagonal 1 x diagonal 2) / 2

Area = (RV x SV) / 2

Area = (5.1 x 15.3) / 2

Area = 39.03

Rounding to the nearest tenth, the area of rhombus RSTU is 39.0.

Simpler answer

RV = TV = 5.1, SV = UV = 15.3, Area = (RV x SV) / 2 = 39.0.

The area of rhombus RSTU is 39.0.
