22. A baseball batter hits an incoming 45-m/s fastball. The ball leaves the bat at 50 m/s after a ball-on-bat contact time of 0.050 s. What is the force exerted on the 0.10-kg baseball?


Answer:

The force exerted on the baseball is 10 N

Explanation:

In this problem, we will solve the force exerted on the baseball using the concept of Newton's first law of motion and uniform acceleration.

Formula to use

1. Vf = Vi + at

2. F = ma

where

Vi, Vf     is the initial and final velocity respectively, the unit is in m/s

a            is the acceleration, the unit is in m/s²

t             is the time between the initial and final velocity, the unit is in

             seconds

F            is the force, the unit is in Newton (N)

m           is the mass, the unit is in kg

Given information

Vi = 45 m/s

Vf = 50 m/s

t = 0.050 s

m = 0.10 kg

F = ?

Solving the problem

First, let us solve the acceleration of the baseball using the first given formula

Vf = Vi + at

Then, substitute the given data

50 m/s = 45 m/s + a(0.05 s)

Simplify the equation

a = (50 m/s - 45 m/s) / 0.05 s

a = 100 m/s²

Now, let us solve the force exerted by the baseball bat on the ball using the second given formula

F = ma

Then, substitute the given data

F = (0.10 kg)(100 m/s²)

F = 10 N

Therefore, the force exerted on the baseball is 10 N.
