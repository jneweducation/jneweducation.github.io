ACTIVITY 1.

PROPERTIES OF SOLID

• It has definite shape, particles cannot flow and are tightly packed.

PROPERTIES OF LIQUID

• Its particles slide past each other.

PROPERTIES OF GAS

• It is the most compressible, no definite shape and has a large expansion with temperature increase.

PROPERTIES OF SOLID and LIQUID

• They have a small expansion with temperature increase and have definite volume.

PROPERTIES OF LIQUID and GAS

• Their particles can flow and they move quickly.

PROPERTIES OF SOLID, LIQUID and GAS

• They are all states of matter and are all made up of atoms.

-

GUIDE QUESTIONS:

1. What properties are common to solid, liquid and gas?

• They are all states of matter and are all made up of atoms.

2. What properties present only in solid, liquid and gas?

• Solid has definite shape, particles cannot flow and are tightly packed. Liquid has particles that slide past each other. While gas is the most compressible, no definite shape and has a large expansion with temperature increase.

3. How will you compare the properties of solid, liquid and gas?

• A solid has definite volume as well as shape. A liquid, on the other hand, has a definite volume but no definite shape while gas has neither a definite volume nor shape.
