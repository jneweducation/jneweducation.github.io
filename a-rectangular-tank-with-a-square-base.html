1. A rectangular water tank is 3 meters high. It has a square base whose side is 0.8 meters. What is the volume of the tank?​


The volume of a rectangular water tank can be calculated as:

volume = base area x height

    In this case, the base of the water tank is a square with side 0.8 meters, so its base area is:

base area = side x side = 0.8 m x 0.8 m = 0.64 square meters

The height of the water tank is 3 meters.

Therefore, the volume of the tank is:

volume = base area x height = 0.64 sq m x 3 m = 1.92 cubic meters

So the volume of the rectangular water tank is 1.92 cubic meters.

2. A rectangular water tank is 3 meters high.It has a square base whose side is 0.8 water What is the volume of the tank?​


Answer:

the volume of the rectangular water tank is 1.92 cubic meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

The volume of a rectangular tank is given by the formula:

V = lwh

where

l = length of the tank

w = width of the tank

h = height of the tank

In this case, we know that the tank has a square base, which means that its length and width are equal. Let's use x to represent the length (and width) of the base.

So, we have:

l = x

w = x

h = 3

Also, we know that the side of the square base is 0.8 meters, so:

x = 0.8 meters

Substituting these values into the formula for volume, we get:

V = lwh = x * x * 3 = 3x^2

Plugging in x = 0.8 meters, we get:

V = 3 * (0.8)^2 = 1.92 cubic meters


3. A rectangular tank with a square base contains 3042 cm³ of water. The height of thewater level is 18 cm. Find the length of one side of the square base.​


Answer:

13 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to determine the length of a square base, we must manipulate the volume's formula.

Volume = lwh

V= s²h

V/h = s²

√(V/h) = s

Substitute the given into the formula above,

√(3042/18) = s

√169 = s

13 = s

Therefore, the length of one side of a square base is 13 cm.
