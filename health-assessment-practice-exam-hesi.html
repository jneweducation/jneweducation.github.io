ANSWER:

Science educators often characterize the degree to which tests measure different facets of college students’ learning, such as knowing, applying, and problem solving. A casual survey of scholarship of teaching and learning research studies reveals that many educators also measure how students’ attitudes influence their learning. Students’ science attitudes refer to their positive or negative feelings and predispositions to learn science. Science educators use attitude measures, in conjunction with learning measures, to inform the conclusions they draw about the efficacy of their instructional interventions. The measurement of students’ attitudes poses similar but distinct challenges as compared with measurement of learning, such as determining validity and reliability of instruments and selecting appropriate methods for conducting statistical analyses. In this review, we will describe techniques commonly used to quantify students’ attitudes toward science. We will also discuss best practices for the analysis and interpretation of attitude data.

14. active voice and passive voice. 1.the exam is made by the practice teacher 2. the cake is baked by the baker​


Active and Passive Voice

The exam is made by the practice teacher. - Passive voiceThe cake is baked by the baker. - Passive voice

Active voice refers of what a person or thing does. The subject performs the action verb on the object.

Subject + Verb + Object

He made a cake.Anna is painting the wall.She posted a poster in a wall.

Passive voice refers of what is being done to someone or something. The object is acting upon the subject. It is actually often used the word "by".

Object + Verb + Subject

A cake made by him.The wall is painted by Anna.The poster in a wall was posted by her.

16. the lived experience of practical exam of ict students?​


Answer:

Practical exams for ICT students typically involve hands-on tasks that require the application of technical skills and knowledge. This may involve performing tasks on a computer or working with other types of hardware.

Students may be required to troubleshoot errors and solve problems related to programming, networking, or database management.

Additionally, the practical exam may be timed, with students needing to complete the tasks within a set amount of time.

The experience of a practical exam can be challenging and stressful, as students need to perform under pressure and demonstrate their proficiency in the subject matter.

However, it can also be an opportunity for students to showcase their skills and knowledge, and demonstrate their readiness for real-world applications of ICT.
