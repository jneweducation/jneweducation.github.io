1. Compare and contrast road and rail transport?​


Answer:

Rail transportation is more affordable than road transportation for long distances due to the capacity these freight trains can handle. Moreover, hauling cargo by rail transportation is less expensive due to rail's lower fuel consumption. On the other hand, road transportation is cost-effective for short distances.

Answer:
Contrast

Road and rail transport have several key contrasts:

1.Flexibility: Road transport is considered more flexible as it allows for door-to-door delivery and can reach remote or inaccessible areas while rail transport is more efficient for moving large quantities of goods over long distances, but it is less flexible as it depends on tracks and stations.

2.Cost: Road transport is relatively inexpensive compared to rail transport, which requires a specialized infrastructure and is generally more expensive.

3.Distance: Road transport is widely used for short and medium-distance travel while rail transport is most commonly used for long-distance travel.

4.Environmental impact: Road transport generates more pollution and emissions while rail transport is considered more environmentally friendly.

Comparison
Road and rail transport have several key comparisons:

1.Purpose: Both road and rail transport are used to move goods and people from one place to another.

2.Infrastructure: Both types of transport require infrastructure, but road transport doesn't require specialized infrastructure as rail transport does.

3.Efficiency: Both road and rail transport can be efficient for moving goods and people, but rail transport is generally more efficient for moving large quantities of goods over long distances, while road transport is more efficient for short and medium distance travel.

4.Capacity: Both road and rail transport have the capacity to move large quantities of goods and people, but rail transport can handle larger quantities than road transport.

6. advantages and disadvantages of Japanese rail tunnel and disadvantages of Japanese rail tunnel


Answer:

Advantages of Japanese rail tunnels include:

Faster travel times: Tunnels allow trains to bypass mountainous terrain and other obstacles, reducing travel time between destinations.Increased capacity: Tunnels can accommodate more trains than surface tracks, which can help alleviate congestion on the rail network.Improved safety: Tunnels provide protection from inclement weather and natural disasters, such as landslides and floods.Reduced environmental impact: Tunnels can minimize the impact of rail infrastructure on the surrounding landscape and ecosystems.

Disadvantages of Japanese rail tunnels include:

High construction costs: Building tunnels is a complex and costly process, which can make it difficult to secure funding for new projects.Difficulties with maintenance: Tunnels can be difficult to access for maintenance and repair work, which can increase costs and downtime.Limited access: Tunnels can only be accessed by train, so they may not be a viable option in areas where other forms of transportation are needed.Impact on surrounding area: Tunneling can cause disruptions to local communities, such as noise and vibration during construction, and may also lead to land subsidence.

9. what likely happen to the river, the road, and rail road​


Answer:

Rivers, road, and railroad tracks are affected by fault movements of the earth. Fault movement is the fracture zone that occurs between blocks of rocks and causes relative movement with each other. There are three major fault types – normal, reverse, and transcurrent faults. In a normal fault, rivers flow toward a hanging wall like waterfalls. In reverse fault, the river settles to form a lake or pond. In a transcurrent or strike-slip fault river flow will change its course. Railroad along faults will lead to derailment. Roads can creek or pave due to fault movements. Depending upon the fault zones, railroad tracks and roads must be laid to prevent damage.

19. Rail road Crossing Pedestrian lane​


A pedestrian crossing (or crosswalk in American English) is a place designated for pedestrians to cross a road, street or avenue.
Marked pedestrian crossings are often found at intersections, but may also be at other points on busy roads that would otherwise be too unsafe to cross without assistance due to vehicle numbers, speed or road widths. They are also commonly installed where large numbers of pedestrians are attempting to cross (such as in shopping areas) or where vulnerable road users (such as school children) regularly cross. Rules govern usage of the pedestrian crossings to ensure safety; for example, in some areas, the pedestrian must be more than halfway across the crosswalk before the driver proceeds.
