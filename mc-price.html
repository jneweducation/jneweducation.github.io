5. Suppose a firm’s inverse demand curve is given by P 120 .5Q and its cost equation is C 420 60Q Q2. a. Find the firm’s optimal quantity, price, and profit (1) by using the profit and marginal profit equations and (2) by setting MR equal to MC. Also provide a graph of MR and MC.


Answer:

To find the firm's optimal quantity, price, and profit, we can use the profit and marginal profit equations or we can set MR (marginal revenue) equal to MC (marginal cost).

Using the profit and marginal profit equations:

The profit equation is given by profit = total revenue - total cost. The total revenue is given by the product of the quantity sold and the price, or TR = P * Q. The total cost is given by the cost equation, or TC = 420 + 60Q + Q^2.

The marginal profit is the change in profit per unit of output, or MP = d(profit)/dQ.

Substituting the expressions for TR and TC into the profit equation, we have:

profit = (P * Q) - (420 + 60Q + Q^2)

Taking the derivative of both sides with respect to Q, we get:

MP = P - (60 + 2Q)

To find the optimal quantity, we set MP = 0 and solve for Q:

0 = P - (60 + 2Q)

2Q = P - 60

Q = (P - 60)/2

Substituting this expression for Q back into the inverse demand curve equation, we get:

P = 120 - 0.5Q

(P - 60)/2 = Q

120 - 0.5Q = 2Q

2.5Q = 120 - 60

2.5Q = 60

Q = 24

Substituting this value for Q back into the inverse demand curve equation, we get:

P = 120 - 0.5Q

P = 120 - 0.5 * 24

P = $72

Substituting this value for P and Q back into the profit equation, we get:

profit = (P * Q) - (420 + 60Q + Q^2)

profit = ($72 * 24) - (420 + 60 * 24 + 24^2)

profit = $1728 - (420 + 1440 + 576)

profit = $1728 - 1936

profit = $-208

So the optimal quantity is 24 units, the optimal price is $72, and the profit is $-208.

Setting MR equal to MC:

The marginal revenue (MR) is the change in total revenue per unit of output, or MR = d(TR)/dQ.

Since the total revenue is given by TR = P * Q, the marginal revenue is given by:

MR = P + Q * dP/dQ

Substituting the expressions for P and dP/dQ into this equation, we get:

MR = P + Q * (-0.5)

MR = 120 - 0.5Q

The marginal cost (MC) is the change in total cost per unit of output, or MC = d(TC)/dQ.

Substituting the expression for TC into this equation, we get:

MC = 60 + 2Q

To find the optimal quantity, we set MR = MC and solve for Q:

MR = MC

120 - 0.5Q = 60 + 2Q

2.5Q = 120 - 60

2.5Q = 60

Q = 24
