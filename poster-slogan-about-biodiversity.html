24. the effects of deforestation on biodiversity make a slogan and peom​


The effects of deforestation on biodiversity

Deforestation occurs when a forest or cluster of trees is removed from the land and transformed to non-forest usage. Forest areas can be converted to farmland, ranches, or urban usage as part of deforestation.

Tropical rainforests, which are extremely vital to the globe because they offer habitat for millions of species of organisms, manage the world's climate, retain about half of the world's rainwater, and contain plants utilized in contemporary medications, see the most concentrated destruction. Rain forests also absorb a significant quantity of CO2 (CO2).

Forests encompass around 31% of the Earth's land surface. When animal species that live in trees lose their home and are unable to relocate, deforestation can result in biodiversity loss.
