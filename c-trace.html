12. 5 Used in transferring marks with dressmaker's carbon paper.b. Hip curvea. Hem markerc. Tracing wheeld. Tracing paper​


Answer:

I think C.

Explanation:

A track or tracing wheel (example in the picture), also known as a pattern wheel, pounce wheel, and dart wheel, is an instrument with many teeth on a wheel attached to a handle. Teeth can be serrated or smooth. It is used to transfer marks from sewing patterns onto fabric with or without the use of tracing paper, and can be used to make slotted perforations. Such marks may include pleats, darts, buttonholes, notches or placement lines for appliques or pockets.

30. Trace the path of oxygen.Trace the path of Carbon dioxide.​


Answer:

Path of Oxygen

During gas exchange oxygen moves from the lungs to the bloodstream. At the same time carbon dioxide passes from the blood to the lungs. This happens in the lungs between the alveoli and a network of tiny blood vessels called capillaries, which are located in the walls of the alveoli.

Path of Carbon

In 1946, the long-lived radioisotope of carbon, carbon-14, became available and we set out to pursue the work on the path of carbon itself from carbon dioxide to the reduced carbon compounds, using labeled carbon as the means of tracing that path.
