Let x be the abundance of the isotope with mass 172amu, and y be the abundance of the isotope with mass 174amu.

We know that the relative atomic mass (RAM) of the element is 172.25amu, and we can use the following formula to solve for x and y:

RAM = (mass of isotope 1 x abundance of isotope 1) + (mass of isotope 2 x abundance of isotope 2)

Substituting the given values, we get:

172.25 = (172x) + (174y)

We also know that the sum of the abundances must equal 1, since these are the only two isotopes of the element. So we have:

x + y = 1

Now we have two equations and two unknowns, which we can solve using substitution or elimination. Let's use substitution:

y = 1 - x

Substituting into the first equation, we get:

172.25 = (172x) + (174(1 - x))

Simplifying and solving for x, we get:

x = 0.4

So the abundance of the isotope with mass 172amu is 0.4, and the abundance of the isotope with mass 174amu is:

y = 1 - x = 0.6

Therefore, the abundances of the two isotopes are 0.4 and 0.6, respectively.
