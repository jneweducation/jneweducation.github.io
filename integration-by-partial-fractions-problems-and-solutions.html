How to decompose the fraction into partial fraction?

The method is called "Partial Fraction Decomposition", and goes like this:

Step 1: Factor the bottom

Step 2: Write one partial fraction for each of those factors.Step 3: Multiply through by the bottom so we no longer have fractions.Step 4: And we have our answer.

5. answer the problems below. use guesa. in a gas mixture composed of n2, ne, and he, the partial pressure of n2 is o.50 atm, that of ne is 1.1 atm, and that of he is 0.80 atm. calculate the mole fraction of each gas?​


Answer:

X_N₂ = 0.21

X_Ne = 0.46

X_He = 0.33

Explanation:

Calculating the total pressure

P_T = n_N₂ + n_Ne + n_He

P_T = 0.50 atm + 1.1 atm + 0.80 atm

P_T = 2.4 atm

Calculating the mole fraction of each gas

For N₂

X_N₂ = n_N2 / n_T

X_N₂ = 0.50 atm / 2.4 atm

X_N₂ = 0.21

For Ne

X_Ne = n_Ne / n_T

X_Ne = 1.1 atm / 2.4 atm

X_Ne = 0.46

For He

X_He = n_He / n_T

X_He = 0.80 atm / 2.4 atm

X_He = 0.33

25. In the problem 62 x 45, what are the partial products


Partial products are the first 'answers' you get when you multiply double or more digits to each other. So, in this particular equation, 62 x 45, the first step in multiplying the 2 numbers is multiplying 62 by 5, and then multiply 62 again by 4, which will give you the partial products of 310 and 248, respectively. To put it more simply, 62x 5 = 310 and then 62 x 4 = 248.

A mixture of 2.00 moles of hydrogen gas (H2), 3.00 moles of ammonia (NH3), 4.00 moles of carbon dioxide(CO2) and 5.00 moles of nitrogen gas (N2) exerts a total pressure of 800 torr. What is the partial pressureof each gas?2. The partial pressure of fluorine gas (F2) is 300 torr in a mixture of gases where the total pressure is 1.00 atm.What is the mole fraction of F2?​


Answer:

1. PH2 = 114 to rr

PNH3 = 171 to rr

PCO2 = 229 to rr

PN2 = 286 to rr

2. XF2 = 0.395

Explanation:

Problem No. 1

Quantities:

nH2 → number of moles of H2

nNH3 → number of moles of NH3

nCO2 → number of moles of CO2

nN2 → number of moles of N2

nT → total number of moles

XH2 → mole fraction of H2

XNH3 → mole fraction of NH3

XCO2 → mole fraction of CO2

XN2 → mole fraction of N2

PT → total pressure

PH2 → partial pressure of H2

PNH3 → partial pressure of NH3

PCO2 → partial pressure of CO2

PN2 → partial pressure of N2

Calculating the total number of moles.

nT = nH2 + nNH3 + nCO2 + nN2

nT = 2.00 mol + 3.00 mol + 4.00 mol + 5.00 mol

nT = 14.00 mol

Calculating the mole fraction of each gas.

For H2

XH2 = nH2 / nT

XH2 = 2.00 mol / 14.00 mol

XH2 = 0.143

For NH3

XNH3 = nNH3 / nT

XNH3 = 3.00 mol / 14.00 mol

XNH3 = 0.214

For CO2

XCO2 = nCO2 / nT

XCO2 = 4.00 mol / 14.00 mol

XCO2 = 0.286

For N2

XN2 = nN2 / nT

XN2 = 5.00 mol / 14.00 mol

XN2 = 0.357

Calculating the partial pressures of each gas.

For H2

PH2 = (XH2)(PT)

PH2 = (0.143)(800 to rr)

PH2 = 114 to rr

For NH3

PNH3= (XNH3)(PT)

PNH3 = (0.214)(800 to rr)

PNH3 = 171 to rr

For CO2

PCO2 = (XCO2)(PT)

PCO2 = (0.286)(800 to rr)

PCO2 = 229 to rr

For N2

PN2 = (XN2)(PT)

PN2 = (0.357)(800 to rr)

PN2 = 286 to rr

Problem No. 2

Quantities

PF2 → partial pressure of F2

PT → total pressure

XF2 → mole fraction of F2

Calculating for the mole fraction of F2.

PF2 = (XF2)(PT)

XF2 = PF2 / PT

Note: 1.00 atm = 760 to rr

XF2 = 300 to rr / 760 to rr

XF2 = 0.395
