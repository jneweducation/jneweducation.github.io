5. The fertilizer ammonium sulfate, (NH4)2SO4, reacts with sodium hydroxide to produce sodium sulfate, water and ammonia. Determine the mass of sodium hydroxide needed to react with 15.0 g of ammonium sulfate.​


Solution:

Step 1: Write and balance the chemical equation.

(NH₄)₂SO₄ + 2NaOH → Na₂SO₄ + 2H₂O + 2NH₃

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of substances needed.

For (NH₄)₂SO₄

molar mass = (14.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 8) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 132.15 g/mol

For NaOH

molar mass = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 40.00 g/mol

Step 3: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄ : 2 mol NaOH

Step 4: Calculate the mass of NaOH needed by using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of NaOH = 15.0 g (NH₄)₂SO₄} × \frac{\text{1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄}}{\text{132.15 g (NH₄)₂SO₄}} × \frac{\text{2 mol NaOH}}{\text{1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄}} × \frac{\text{40.00 g NaOH}}{\text{1 mol NaOH}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of NaOH = 9.08 g}}[/tex]

16. if 6.0 moles of iron(III)oxide react with water,how many moles of iron (III) hydroxide will be produced?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

Fe₂O₃ + 3H₂O → 2Fe(OH)₃

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Fe(OH)₃ produced by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Fe₂O₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 2 moles of Fe(OH)₃.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Fe(OH)}_3 & = \text{6.0 mol} \: \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \times \frac{\text{2 mol} \: \text{Fe(OH)}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{12 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 12 mol of Fe(OH)₃ will be produced.

Many salts have poor solubility in water, so their dissociation in aqueous solution is expressed as an equilibrium between the solid undissolved form and the solvated ions.
