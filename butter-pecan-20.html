3. A random survey of 250 students was conducted at a middle school to determine which flavor of ice cream students prefer. The results of the survey are recorded in the table below. Student Ice Cream Preferences Vanilla,75 Chocolate,25 Butter Pecan,10 Strawberry,9 Chocolate Chip Mint,8 Neapolitan,11 Other,112 Based on these results, what is the probability that a student selected at random does NOT prefer Butter Pecan ice cream? 10% 96% 4% 90%


Answer:

24/25 or 96%

Step-by-step explanation:

Probability is defined as the likeliness of an event to occur,

Probability = # of favored outcomes / # of total outcomes

Here, our favored outcomes is everything EXCEPT those who prefer Butter Pecan ice cream. The total outcomes, on the other hand, is the total number of everything. Since the total is already given as 250, it would be easier to represent it as follows:

Instead of,

P (Not prefer Butter Pecan) = (75 + 25 + 9 + 8 + 11 + 112) / 250

We can show,

P (Not prefer Butter Pecan) = (250 - 10) / 250

= 240 / 250

= 24 / 25

= 96%

8. A box containing 74.25 ounces of pecans was donated to a local store. The store places the pecans into 8.125- ounce containers and sells each container for $6.95. Determine which expression shows how much the store will earn if they sell all the containers of pecans. Find the store’s earnings.


Answer:

There are different ways to approach this problem, but one possible method is:

- Divide the total weight of pecans by the weight of each container to find the number of containers:

74.25 oz ÷ 8.125 oz/container ≈ 9.13 containers

- Round up to the nearest whole number of containers, since the store cannot sell a partial container:

9.13 ≈ 10 containers

- Multiply the number of containers by the price per container to find the store's earnings:

10 containers × $6.95/container = $69.50

Therefore, the expression that shows how much the store will earn if they sell all the containers of pecans is:

earnings = (number of containers) × (price per container)

Plugging in the values, we get:

earnings = 10 × $6.95 = $69.50

Note that the answer may vary if the rounding method or the unit of currency is different. Also, this calculation assumes that all the containers are sold at the same price and that there are no additional costs or discounts involved.
