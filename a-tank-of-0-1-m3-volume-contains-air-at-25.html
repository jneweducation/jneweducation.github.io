1. A cylindrical tank has a tight-fitting piston that allows the volume of the tank to be changed. The tank originally contains 0.110 m3 of air at a pressure of 0.355 atm. The piston is slowly pulled out until the volume of the gas is increased to 0.390 m3 . If the temperature remains constant, what is the final value of the pressure?​


Answer:

0.10013 atm

Explanation:

Applying Boyle’s Law,

P’V’ = PV………………. Equation 1

Where P’ = Initial pressure of air, V’ = Initial volume of air, P = Final pressure of air, V = Final volume of air.

make P the subject of the equation

P = P’V’/V………………… Equation 2

Given: P’ = 0.355 atm, V’  0.110 m³, V = 0.390 m³

Substitute into equation 2

P = 0.355(0.11)/0.39

P = 0.10013 atm.

4. A rigid tank of 1 m3 contains air at 600 kPa, 400 K. By mistake, someone lets 0.5 kg flow out. If the final temperature is 375 K a. Determine is the final pressure. b. Determine the mass of the gas remained in the tank.


Formula:

Ideal gas law is given by

[tex]PV=mRT[/tex]

where P is pressure, V is volume, m is mass, R is ideal gas constant, T is temperature.

Solution:

At the initial state of the tank, we have

[tex]P_{1}V=m_{1}RT_{1}[/tex]

For air, the ideal gas constant is

[tex]R=287\text{ J/kg-K}[/tex]

The initial mass is

[tex]m_1=\dfrac{P_{1}V}{RT_{1}} \\\\m_1=\dfrac{(600\times10^3\text{ Pa})(1\text{ m}^3)}{(287\text{ J/kg-K})(400\text{ K})} \\\\m_1=5.23\text{ kg}[/tex]

After the process, the mass of the gas remained in the tank is

[tex]m_{2}=5.23\text{ kg}-0.5\text{ kg}=4.73\text{ kg}[/tex]

At the final state of the tank, we have

[tex]P_{2}V=m_{2}RT_{2}[/tex]

The final pressure is

[tex]P_{2}=\dfrac{m_2RT_2}{V}\\\\P_{2}=\dfrac{(4.73\text{ kg})(287\text{ J/kg-K})(375\text{ K})}{1\text{ m}^3}\\\\P_{2}=509\times10^3\text{ Pa}=509\text{ kPa}[/tex]

Answers:

[tex]a.)\:\boxed{m_2=4.73\text{ kg}}\\b.)\:\boxed{P_2=509\text{ kPa}}[/tex]

10. A tank (not rigid) contains 2.3 L of helium gas at 25°c. What will be the volume of the tank after healing it and its content to 40°c temperature at constant pressure?


Good Day..

Problem:

A tank (not rigid) contains 2.3 L of helium gas at 25°c. What will be the volume of the tank after healing it and its content to 40°c temperature at constant pressure?

Note:

Convert degree celsius to kelvin when solving gas laws problem.  

kelvin = degree celsius + 273.15  

Kelvin = 25°c + 273.15 = 298.15 K  

Kelvin = 40°c + 273.15 = 313.15 K

Given:

V1 (initital volume) = 2.3 L

T1 (initial temperature) = 25°c or 298.15 K  

V2 (final volume) = unknown ?

T2 (final temperature) = 40°c  or 313.15 K

Solution:

We can solve this problem using Charles' law which states that, volume is directly proportional to kelvin temperature when the amount and pressure of the gas are constant. Formula is V1/T1 = V2/T2 and to solve for V2, we can use V2 = V1T2 / T1.

V2 = V1T2 / T1

    = 2.3 L  × 313.15 K  / 298.15 K      (cancel unit kelvin)

    = 720.245 L / 298.15

    = 2.416 L

Answer:

The volume is 2.416 L

11. There are 500 m3 of air in a shop at 20°C. What is the difference in volume if the temperature is 0°C?​


The new volume(V₂) : 465.87 m³

The difference : 34.13 m³

Further explanation  

Charles's Law states that  

When the gas pressure is kept constant, the gas volume is proportional to the temperature  

[tex]\tt \dfrac{V_1}{T_1}=\dfrac{V_2}{T_2}[/tex]  

Given  

V₁ = 500 m³

T₁= 20 C  + 273 = 293 K

T₂ = 0 C + 273 = 273 K

Required

The new volume(V₂)

Solution

Input the value :

V₂=(V₁.T₂)/T₁

V₂=(500.273)/293

V₂=465.87 m³

The difference in volume : 500 - 465.87=34.13 m³

15. A 200 m3storage tank is being constructed to hold LNG, liquified natural gas, which may be assumed to be essentially pure methane. If the tank is to contain 95 % liquid and 5 % vapor by volume at 100 kPa, what mass in kg of LNG will the tank hold? What is the quality in the tank?​


Answer:

Methane CH₄ has:

[tex]v_{f}=0.002366\:\textrm{m}^3\textrm{/kg}[/tex] (interpolated)

[tex]v_{g}=0.55665\:\textrm{m}^3\textrm{/kg}[/tex] (first entry 100 kPa)

Solving the masses of the liquid and vapor of the methane,

[tex]m_{\textrm{liq}}=\dfrac{V_{\textrm{liq}}}{v_{f}}=\dfrac{0.95(200\:\textrm{m}^3)}{0.002366\:\textrm{m}^3/\:\textrm{kg}} =80,304.31\:\textrm{kg}[/tex]

and

[tex]m_{\textrm{vap}}=\dfrac{V_{\textrm{vap}}}{v_{f}}=\dfrac{0.05(200\:\textrm{m}^3)}{0.55665\:\textrm{m}^3/\:\textrm{kg}} =17.96\:\textrm{kg}[/tex]

The mass of LNG in the tank is

[tex]m_{\textrm{total}} =80,304.31\:\textrm{kg}+17.96\:\textrm{kg}=80,322.27\:\textrm{kg}[/tex]

and the quality in the tank is

[tex]\dfrac{m_{\textrm{vap}}}{m_{\textrm{total}}} =\dfrac{{17.96\:\textrm{kg}}}{80,322.27\:\textrm{kg}}}=2.24\times10^{-4}[/tex]
