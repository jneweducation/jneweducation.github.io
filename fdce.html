17. 13. Quadrilateral CDEF is a rhombus If m/FCE = 3x - 5 and m. DCE = 2x, find m/FCD.​


Answer:

Since CDEF is a rhombus, opposite angles are congruent. Therefore, we have:

m/FCE = m/CEF

We can use this relationship to express m/CEF in terms of x:

m/FCE = 3x - 5

3x - 5 = m/CEF

Similarly, we have:

m/DCE = m/DEC

Again, we can use this relationship to express m/DEC in terms of x:

m/DCE = 2x

2x = m/DEC

Since CDEF is a rhombus, angles FCD and ECD are congruent. Therefore:

m/FCD + m/DEC = 180 degrees

We can substitute the expressions for m/DEC and m/CEF in terms of x:

m/FCD + 2x + 3x - 5 = 180

m/FCD + 5x - 5 = 180

m/FCD = 185 - 5x

Therefore, the measure of angle FCD is given by:

m/FCD = 185 - 5x

We can solve for x by substituting the given expressions for m/FCE and m/DCE in terms of x:

3x - 5 = m/CEF

2x = m/DEC

Since CDEF is a rhombus, angles FCE and DCE are congruent. Therefore:

m/FCE + m/DCE = 180 degrees

Substituting the given expressions for m/FCE and m/DCE in terms of x:

3x - 5 + 2x = 180

5x - 5 = 180

5x = 185

x = 37

Substituting x = 37 into the expression for m/FCD, we get:

m/FCD = 185 - 5x = 185 - 5(37) = 10

Therefore, the measure of angle FCD is 10 degrees.
