16. A person was standing 50meters west of a pole he wolkpast the pole towards the east and ended up 50meters from the pole find the volocity if the total displacement happen in 100second what is given and solution formula​


Answer:

To calculate velocity, we need to know both the displacement and the time taken.

In this case, the person started 50 meters west of the pole, walked past the pole towards the east, and ended up 50 meters from the pole. Therefore, the total displacement is the distance from the starting point to the ending point, which is:

Displacement = 50 m (east) + 50 m (west) = 0 m

This is because the person walked both east and west, and ended up at the same distance from the pole as they started.

We are also given that this displacement happened in 100 seconds. Therefore, the velocity can be calculated using the formula:

Velocity = Displacement / Time

Substituting the values, we get:

Velocity = 0 m / 100 s = 0 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the person is 0 m/s, which means they did not move relative to their starting point over the 100-second period.

28. the perimeter of a rectangular garden is 50meters .The length of the rectangle is 5less than twice the width .Find the length and with of rectangle​


Question:

The perimeter of a rectangular garden is 50 meters.The length of the rectangle is 5 less than twice the width. Find the length and with of rectangle.

Given:

[tex]\tt \big \green{Perimeter = 50 \: meters}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \blue{Length = 2w - 5}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \purple{Width = w}[/tex]

Formula:

[tex]\tt \large \green{Perimeter = 2 \: Length + 2 \: Width}[/tex]

Solution:Find the width.

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow \green{Perimeter = 2 \: Length + 2 \: Width}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow 50 = 2(2w - 5) + 2(w)[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow 50 = 4w - 10 + 2w[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow 50 = 4w + 2w - 10[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow 50 = 6w - 10[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow 50 + 10 = 6w[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow 60 = 6w[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow 6w = 60[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow \frac{\cancel{6}w}{\cancel{6}} = \frac{60}{6}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \rightarrow \huge \purple{width = 10 \: meters}[/tex]

Find the length.

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow Length = 2w - 5[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow Length = 2(10) - 5[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow Length = 20 - 5[/tex]

[tex]\tt \rightarrow \huge \blue{Length = 15 \: meters}[/tex]

Substitute the results to the formula.

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow \green{Perimeter = 2 \: Length + 2 \: Width}[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow Perimeter = 2(10) + 2(15)[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow Perimeter = 20 + 30[/tex]

[tex]\tt \big \rightarrow \green{Perimeter = 50 \: meters}[/tex]

Final Answer:

[tex]\tt \big The \: \: length \: \: of \: \: the \: \: rectangle \: \: is \: \: \large \blue{15 \: \: meters} \\ \tt \big while \: \: the \: \: width \: \: is \: \: \large \purple{10 \: \: meters}.[/tex]
