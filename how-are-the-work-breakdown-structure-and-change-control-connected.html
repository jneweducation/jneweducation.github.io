8. Identify three ways you can document the Work Breakdown Structure.


Answer:

1. Using a hierarchical chart or diagram: This is a visual representation of the Work Breakdown Structure, with the most general tasks at the top and more specific tasks branching out below.

2. Using a spreadsheet or table: This is a functional way to document a Work Breakdown Structure and can include columns for task descriptions, assigned team members, start and end dates, and other relevant information.

3. Using project management software: Many project management tools have built-in templates for documenting a Work Breakdown Structure, which can make it easier to create and update as the project progresses. These tools can also link tasks to resources and budgets, making it easier to manage the project as a whole.

22. What is decomposition as a method for segmenting the work breakdown structure (WBS)?


Answer:

Decomposition is a method for dividing the work breakdown structure (WBS) into smaller and more manageable components or work packages. In decomposition, the project manager and project team break down the larger deliverables or work packages into smaller, more specific tasks or sub-deliverables. This enables the team to more efficiently manage the project and allows for more accurate estimating, planning, and tracking of project progress. Decomposition is an iterative process and can be repeated at different levels of detail until the work breakdown structure has reached its appropriate level of granularity.

24. why do you think there is a preventing notion that globalization contribute to the breakdown of some democratic structure​


Answer:

Because it is relatively easy for non-democratic entities to manipulate an extremely complicated system with simple fiats to their benefit. The simplicity of the processes of control, legitimacy of authority, and centralization of power in a non-democratic, strictly-hierarchical structure allow agents in the highest tiers of said structure to act totally pragmatically without concerns about a variety of interests.

Take for example a for-profit corporation. A corporation is only democratic in terms of shares. Once board members and positions are decided, there is little smaller shareholders with differences on the prevailing opinions can do to divert resources from their corporate, state-like entity’s designated course. Now, consider the actual function or purpose of such an incorporated entity. It exists only to produce profit from capital invested so that more capital may then be invested for the production of more profit, all through the media of services or goods. There is only one interest of real concern to any agents in this structure, in this system, and that is profit. So if the most meager of shareholders has concerns about a company’s direction, those concerns are easily assuaged by successful short-, mid-, and long-term returns.

If such a corporate entity operates globally, it will have to navigate several different schemes of law, governance, and social/cultural normalcy. While this is an innately complicated task, for the corporation and its leading agents, the solution is intuitively simple: Do what works where it works. This is why you see many companies catering to the desires of some highly-questionable regimes like those in Saudi Arabia, China, etc.

30. 1. What is decomposition as a method for segmenting the work breakdown structure (WBS)? 2. How do you determine and estimate cost and duration in decomposition? 3. What determines the level of decomposition? Give atleast 2.


1. Decomposition is a method of breaking down a larger project into smaller and more manageable components, known as work packages, within the work breakdown structure (WBS). This method helps to identify each task required to complete the project, making it easier to manage, assign responsibilities, and track progress.

2. To determine and estimate cost and duration in decomposition, one must consider the resources required to complete each work package, such as time, labor, materials, and equipment. Cost and duration can be estimated by analyzing historical data and determining the effort required for each task. The team can also use tools like PERT (Program Evaluation and Review Technique) to estimate the expected duration for each work package.

3. The level of decomposition is determined by the complexity and size of the project, the level of detail required for effective management and control, and the project team's ability to manage and execute the work. Other factors that can influence the level of decomposition include the project's criticality, stakeholder expectations, and the availability of resources.
