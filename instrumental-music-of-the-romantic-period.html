7. what is the most influencial during Romantic period if it is Vocal Music or Instrumental Music?, Why?​


Answer:

During the Romantic period, both vocal music and instrumental music were influential, but it's difficult to say which was more influential overall, as both forms of music had their own unique characteristics and contributed to the period in different ways.

Vocal music was particularly important during the Romantic period because it allowed composers to express emotions and feelings in a way that was accessible to a wide audience. Many of the most famous and influential composers of the time, such as Franz Schubert, Robert Schumann, and Johannes Brahms, were known for their vocal works, including lieder (German art songs), choral works, and operas. The Romantic period saw a renewed interest in national and folk music, and vocal music was often used to express the identity and culture of a particular country or region.

On the other hand, instrumental music was also influential during the Romantic period, particularly in the development of new forms and genres. Composers such as Ludwig van Beethoven, Franz Liszt, and Richard Wagner pushed the boundaries of traditional instrumental forms like the symphony and sonata, and experimented with new forms like the tone poem and the symphonic poem. Instrumental music was also used to convey emotions and feelings, often through programmatic music that told a story or depicted a scene.

Ultimately, it's difficult to say which form of music was more influential during the Romantic period, as both vocal and instrumental music played important roles in shaping the music of the time.
