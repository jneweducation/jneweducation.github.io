18. the owner of a coffee shop recorded the number of costumer who came into his each hour of the day the results was 14,10,12,9,17,5,8,9,14,10 and 11, find the lower quartile and upper quartile of the data​


Answer:

To find the lower and upper quartile of the data set, the first step is to arrange the observations in ascending order:

5, 8, 9, 9, 10, 10, 11, 12, 14, 14, 17

The median of the entire data set is the middle value, which is 10. The lower quartile (Q1) is the median of the lower half of the data set, which includes all values less than or equal to 10. To find Q1, we can find the median of the first half of the data set:

5, 8, 9, 9, 10

The median of this data set is 9, so Q1 is 9.

The upper quartile (Q3) is the median of the upper half of the data set, which includes all values greater than or equal to 10. To find Q3, we can find the median of the second half of the data set:

11, 12, 14, 14, 17

The median of this data set is 14, so Q3 is 14.

Therefore, the lower quartile is 9 and the upper quartile is 14 for the given data set.

23. The cost of the cup of coffee, a sandwich, and fries is p49. the sandwich costs twice as much as the coffee. the fries costs p17 more than the coffee. what is the cost of the coffee?


Answer:

The cost of the coffee is ₱8.

Explanation:

Let:

x + 17 = cost of the fries

2x = cost of the sandwich

x = cost of the coffee

x + 17 + 2x + x = 49

4x + 17 = 49

4x = 49 - 17

4x = 32

x = ₱8 <= cost of the coffee
