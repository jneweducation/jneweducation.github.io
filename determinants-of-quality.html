5. 1.why absence of air, water, land pollutants determine the quality of life?2.why healthy organisms determine the quality of life?3.why abundant supply of natural resources determine the quality of life?​


Answer:

1. Pollution is the introduction of harmful contaminants into the air, water, or soil. These contaminants can have dire effects on entire ecosystems, making life more difficult for humans, plants, and animals. Children and the elderly are particularly susceptible to the health effects of these toxins.

2. The environment directly affects health status and plays a major role in the quality of life, years of healthy life lived, and health disparities. Poor air quality is linked to premature death, cancer, and long-term damage to respiratory and cardiovascular systems.

3. Natural resources play a huge role in the quality of life. Life would not exist without water; people, animals, and land all need it to live and grow. Water can also be harnessed to be used as a source of energy.
