3. Evaluate: ln(25/16) − 2 ln(5/7) + ln(32/49)


Answer:

ln 2

Step-by-step explanation:

Use the following logarithm rules

[tex]\boxed{\begin{aligned} & \small \text{Quotient Rule:} \log\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)=\log(a)-\log(b)\\ & \small \text{Power Rule:} \quad \ \log(c^d)=d\log(c)\end{aligned}}[/tex]

By using the quotient rule, we obtain

[tex]\begin{aligned} \ln\left(\frac{25}{16}\right)&= \ln 25-\ln 16\\ \ln\left(\frac{32}{49}\right)&=\ln 32-\ln 49 \end{aligned}[/tex]

and by using the power rule and quotient rule,

[tex]\begin{aligned} 2\ln\left(\frac{5}{7}\right)=\ln\left(\frac{5^2}{7^2}\right)=\ln \left(\frac{25}{49}\right)=\ln 25-\ln 49.\end{aligned}[/tex]

Thus, the expression is equivalent to

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \ln 25 - \ln 16 - (\ln 25 - \ln 49)+\ln 32 - \ln 49\\ \end{aligned}[/tex]

Many terms cancel out and the resulting expression is

[tex]\begin{aligned} -\ln 16 + \ln 32\end{aligned}[/tex]

which can be written as

[tex]\begin{aligned}-\ln\left(\frac{32}{2}\right)+\ln 32 =-(\ln 32 - \ln 2)+\ln 32=\ln 2\end{aligned}[/tex]
