9. how to apply electromagnetic waves in medicine​


Answer:

There are several ways in which electromagnetic waves can be used in medicine. Low-energy electromagnetic waves, such as magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), can be used to create images of internal structures of the body. Pulsed electromagnetic field (PEMF) therapy is used to stimulate tissue healing and reduce inflammation. High-frequency diathermy uses microwaves to generate heat, which is then used to help reduce swelling and kill cancerous cells. Electromagnetic radiation can also be used to treat conditions such as incontinence and depression, since some frequencies can interact with brain activity. In addition, therapeutic ultrasound is used to treat musculoskeletal issues such as tendinitis and bursitis.

Answer:

Electromagnetic waves have a variety of applications in medicine,

including:

X-rays: X-rays use electromagnetic waves to produce images of bones and tissues. X-rays can help diagnose bone fractures, tumors, and other conditions.

Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI): MRI uses strong magnetic fields and electromagnetic waves to produce images of internal organs and tissues. MRI can help diagnose conditions such as tumors, strokes, and multiple sclerosis.

Ultrasound: Ultrasound uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of internal organs and tissues. Ultrasound is commonly used to monitor fetal development during pregnancy.

Radiation therapy: Radiation therapy uses high-energy electromagnetic waves to destroy cancer cells. Radiation therapy can help shrink tumors and kill cancer cells.

Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT): ECT uses electromagnetic waves to induce seizures in the brain. ECT is used to treat severe depression and other psychiatric conditions.

Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS): TMS uses electromagnetic waves to stimulate nerve cells in the brain. TMS is used to treat depression, migraines, and other conditions.

Electromagnetic therapy: Electromagnetic therapy uses electromagnetic fields to stimulate healing in tissues and bones. Electromagnetic therapy can help heal fractures, reduce inflammation, and relieve pain.
