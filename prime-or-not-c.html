The intersection of two sets A and B ( denoted by A∩B ) is the set of all elements that is common to both A and B.

Explanation:

In mathematical form, For two sets A and B, A∩B = { x: x∈A and x∈B } Similarly for three sets A, B and C, A∩B∩C = { x: x∈A and x∈B and x∈C }
