18. The Luteal Phase of the Ovarian Cycle corresponds with which phase of the Uterine Cycle?


Answer:

The Luteal Phase of the Ovarian Cycle corresponds with the Secretory Phase of the Uterine Cycle. During the luteal phase, the ovary produces progesterone which causes the endometrial lining of the uterus to thicken and become more vascularized, preparing it for possible implantation of a fertilized egg. If implantation does not occur, the corpus luteum degenerates and progesterone levels decrease, leading to the shedding of the endometrial lining during menstruation which marks the beginning of a new menstrual cycle. The Secretory Phase of the uterine cycle occurs during the second half of the menstrual cycle and corresponds to the luteal phase of the ovarian cycle.
