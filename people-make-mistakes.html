17. What are some common mistakes people make when it comes to taking care of their skin?


Answer:

Over-washing: Many people believe that washing their face multiple times a day is good for their skin, but over-washing can actually strip the skin of its natural oils, leading to dryness, irritation, and even breakouts.

Not wearing sunscreen: Sun damage is one of the leading causes of premature aging and skin cancer. It's essential to wear sunscreen every day, even on cloudy days, to protect the skin from harmful UV rays.

Using harsh products: Some skincare products, especially those with strong fragrances or active ingredients, can be harsh on the skin and cause irritation. It's important to choose products that are gentle and specifically formulated for your skin type.

Skipping moisturizer: Even if you have oily skin, it's important to moisturize regularly. Moisturizer helps to keep the skin hydrated and protected from external aggressors.

Picking at blemishes: It's tempting to pick at pimples or blackheads, but this can actually make the problem worse by spreading bacteria and causing scarring. It's best to leave blemishes alone and let them heal on their own.

The aim of the argument

Based on the available options, the argument aims to express a point of view and support it with evidence.

Let’s explain the argument

An argument is a statement used to prove or disprove the viewpoint of another person. Arguments are used to support a different idea or opinion, to be discussed, and to find a solution. In stating an argument, theory, and facts that explain the reasons and evidence for believing an opinion are required. The facts and evidence must be described logically.

To state an argument, three components are required: the premise, inference, and conclusion.

1. A premise is a statement that explains facts and is supported by evidence for the claim or inference to be believed.

2. claims or inferences, i.e. simple conclusions or something resolved after the argument.  In simple arguments, there are usually only premises and conclusions, with no inference.

3. Conclusions, also known as final inference, are reasoning at the end of an argument.

We can conclude that an argument is a statement that expresses one's point of view on a topic of discussion. This statement must be supported by evidence or logical reasons to convince everyone about the truth that we are expressing.

Answer:

When something goes wrong, are you more likely to own up to the mistakes you made, or play the blame game?

Many people are quick to point fingers and play the blame game. In fact, recent research has shown that we expect this behavior to happen. We expect to experience others engaging in blame-shifting, placing the blame on others for their own mistakes.

My hands aren’t clean. I’ve blamed people for my own mistakes more than once, that’s for sure. Why? It’s easy.

Why We Blame Others

Simply put, it’s much easier to place the blame on someone else than to take full responsibility for your actions. It’s also easier to blame someone for our actions rather than take a deeper look at why we made the mistake that we did and face possible consequences — whether it was something you did at work or something that happened during a tiff between you and your partner. Blame shifting takes less effort, and it’s easier on us emotionally — at least in the moment.
