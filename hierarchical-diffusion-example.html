30. PASAGOT NAMAN PLEASE Differentiate technology substitution from technology diffusion and give example for each.


Answer:

Technology substitution and technology diffusion are two distinct concepts in the field of technology management. The main difference between the two is as follows:

- Technology substitution: This refers to the process of replacing an existing technology with a newer or better technology. It involves the complete replacement of the old technology and the adoption of a new one. A common reason for technology substitution is the obsolescence of an old technology or the availability of a more efficient or cost-effective alternative.

Examples of technology substitution include replacing incandescent light bulbs with LED bulbs or replacing fax machines with email.

- Technology diffusion: This refers to the process of spreading a new technology or innovation throughout a society or industry. It involves the adoption of a new technology by a large number of people or organizations. Technology diffusion can occur through various channels such as social networks, marketing campaigns, or government policies. A common example of technology diffusion is the widespread adoption of smartphones in the last decade.
