About Issei Sagawa

Issei Sagawa Issei Sagawa was born prematurely on April 26, 1949 into a middle-class family. As an adult, Sagawa's interest and ambition in literature eventually led him to Paris to get his Ph.D. Unfortunately it wasn't the PH.D title he was carrying, but the title as a human cannibal.

It all started when he invited his friend, a Dutch student named Renée Hartevelt, to come to his place on June 11, 1981. Sagawa (then 32 years old) reasoned that he would help Hartevelt to translate his German text. It turns out that Sagawa didn't help Hartevelt, but shot him dead. It didn't stop there, Sagawa also ate Hartevelt until a few days later. Later it was discovered that Sagawa had indeed targeted to eat Hartevelt with the reason to "absorb his energy" because Hartevelt was described as a beautiful and healthy girl, while Sagawa considered himself "weak, ugly and small" (only 1.52 m tall).

Sagawa also tried to cover his tracks by dumping Hartevelt's remains in the lake, and that's when the local police found out what he was doing. When his house was searched, there were still body remains found in his refrigerator. Instead of being punished, Sagawa was declared insane so he could not attend court in France. Finally Sagawa was sent back to Japan to account for his actions in a Japanese court.
