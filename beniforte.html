4. 1. Ano ang health benifit? 2. Ano ang social benifit?


Answer:

1.Health benefit or health benefits may refer to: Health benefits (insurance), a payment received through a health insurance. Health benefit (medicine), the phenomenon that a food, substance or activity is improving health. Health claim, a usually unproven claim as to medical health benefits of food, etc.

2.Social benefits are current transfers received by households intended to provide for the needs that arise from certain events or circumstances, for example, sickness, unemployment, retirement, housing, education or family circumstances

Answer:

1. Summary. Healthful eating has many health benefits, such as reducing the risk of heart disease, stroke, obesity, and type 2 diabetes. It can also boost a person's mood and provide them with more energy. A doctor or dietitian can provide tips on eating a more healthful diet.

2.Social benefit is the total benefit to society from producing or consuming a good/service. Social benefit includes all the private benefits plus any external benefits of production/consumption. If a good has significant external benefits, then the social benefit will be greater than the private benefit.

16. Benifits of decantation


My husband and I spent Saturday morning decanting my vintage splash bottles into 30ml and 10ml spray bottles. Obviously it's much easier to toss a little sprayer into your bag, rather than a massively expensive splash bottle. I'm renowned for my clumsiness and have 'priors' (for you English readers 'previous') for dropping and spilling expensive perfumes, so I've always been paranoid about dabbing.

Now I have Farouche, My Sin, Amour Amour, Vintage Arpege EDT, Chanel No 5 Cologne, Sortilege, Vintage Maja, L'Aimant and Crepe de Chine, all of which I shall be using with gay abandon, now that they are so accessible. So, today, I am basking in Sortilege, and what a lovely thing it is.But that isn't the real reason for this post.

It occurred to me that decanting has other benefits. Firstly, many of the sprayers have such a wide spray pattern and dispense so much at a time, that your perfume is going as much into the air as onto you. Good for those who like to spray and walk through, not so good for those with limited resources, or limited amounts to use. The narrower spray pattern on the little decants like my 10mls means I can direct virtually all of it onto my skin. This is certainly the way to make your perfume go further, and therefore to save money in the long run.

Secondly, I have a number of intensely strong perfumes that I didn't wear, because injudicious spraying could clear a city block. I finally remembered to include some roll-ons with my latest decant order. Having decanted the vintage Chloe, vintage Youth Dew, Nahema and vintage Knowing, I was finally able to control the amount on my skin. 

Yesterday I learned to appreciate the subtlety of Nahema for the first time since owning it. It was so much quieter and more personal a wearing experience than ever before. 

I have disposed of so many of my powerhouse frags because of being concerned about the amount that was dispensed by sprayer. So, not only can I control the amount I use, but also use a miniscule amount compared to the original bottle's dispenser. I would imagine my little 50ml Nahema will last for years this way.

I intend to decant my two favourite Amouages into roll-ons too. These are seriously costly perfumes and because they are so sumptuous in construction, should lend themselves very well to using this way.
