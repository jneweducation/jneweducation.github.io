6. A skateboard is rolling at a velocity of 3.0m/s with a momentum of 6.0 kg-m/s what is it's mass? Given: Asked: Solution:


Answer:

We can use the formula for momentum, which is given by:

Momentum (p) = mass (m) × velocity (v)

Rearranging the formula to solve for mass, we have:

mass (m) = momentum (p) / velocity (v)

Substituting the given values into the formula, we have:

momentum (p) = 6.0 kg-m/s velocity (v) = 3.0 m/s

Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

mass (m) = 6.0 kg-m/s / 3.0 m/s

Simplifying, we get:

mass (m) = 2.0 kg

So, the mass of the skateboard is 2.0 kg.

20. a skateboarder starts from rest and accelerate at a rate of 3m/s for 10s.what is the final velocity of the skateboarder?​


Using the equation:

final velocity = initial velocity + acceleration x time

We know that the skateboarder started from rest (initial velocity = 0), the acceleration is 3 m/s², and the time is 10 seconds. Therefore:

final velocity = 0 + 3 m/s² x 10 s

final velocity = 30 m/s

Therefore, the final velocity of the skateboarder is 30 m/s.

To find the final velocity of the skateboarder, we can use the formula for uniformly accelerated motion:

Final velocity (v) = Initial velocity (u) + (Acceleration (a) × Time (t))

Given:

Initial velocity (u) = 0 m/s (since the skateboarder starts from rest)

Acceleration (a) = 3 m/s²

Time (t) = 10 s

Plugging these values into the formula:

Final velocity (v) = 0 m/s + (3 m/s² × 10 s)

= 0 m/s + 30 m/s

= 30 m/s

Therefore, the final velocity of the skateboarder after 10 seconds of acceleration is 30 m/s.
