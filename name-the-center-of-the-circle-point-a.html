1. The center of the circle is the point equidistant from the points on the edge. On the figure that is represented by point M.

2. The radius of the circle is any of the line segments from its center to its perimeter. On the figure the radii of the circle must contain the center such as line segment AM, TM, IM, and HM.

3. The diameter of the circle is the length of the line through the center that touches two points on the edge of the circle. On the figure that is represented by line IH since that is only the line that touches the center and the other two points on the edge of the circle.

5. An inscribed angle is is the angle formed in the interior of a circle when two secant lines intersect on the circle. On the figure it is represented by ∠HIE.

6. The chord of the circle is is a straight line segment whose endpoints both lie on a circular arc. On the figure the chords are represented by IE and TC.

Name the segment as AF and AG. 4. 5.​


Answer:

Here are the steps to construct a circle with a radius of 6 cm:

1. Using a compass, draw a circle with a radius of 6 cm. Label the center of the circle as point A.

2. Using a ruler, draw a line segment BC whose endpoints are on the circle and passing through the center A. Make sure that BC is longer than the diameter of the circle.

3. Draw another line segment DE whose endpoints are on the circle and passing through the center A. Make sure that DE is longer than the diameter of the circle.

4. To construct segment AF, place the compass at point A and draw an arc that intersects the circle at some point F. Then, using the same compass width, place the compass at point F and draw another arc that intersects the circle at some point G. Connect points A and G to form segment AG.

5. To construct segment AG, place the compass at point A and draw an arc that intersects the circle at some point F. Then, using the same compass width, place the compass at point F and draw another arc that intersects the circle at some point H. Connect points A and H to form segment AF.

Your construction should now have a circle with radius 6 cm, line segments BC and DE passing through the center A, and segments AF and AG connecting point A to two points on the circle.
