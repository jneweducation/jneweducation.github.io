4. Generate the solubility product expression for Al(NO3)2.


The solubility product expression for Al(NO₃)₂ :

Ksp = [Al³⁺][NO₃⁻]³

Ksp = 9s⁴

Further explanation

Solubility product constants (Ksp) is the product of ions from ionic compounds in a saturated state (equilibrium)

For the reaction:

MₐAₓ (s) ⇒ aMˣ⁺ (aq) + xAᵃ⁻ (aq)

s                  as               xs

Then the Ksp value can be expressed in terms of molarity and solubility

Ksp = [Mˣ⁺]ᵃ [Aᵃ⁻]ˣ

Ksp = (as)ᵃ (xs)ˣ

So for Al(NO₃)₃ :

Al(NO₃)₃ ⇒ Al³⁺ + 3NO₃⁻

s                 s          3s

in terms of molarity ⇒ Ksp = [Al³⁺][NO₃⁻]³

in terms of solubility ⇒ Ksp = s.(3s)³ = 9s⁴

20. 1. What volume 0.300M NaOH is required to react completely with 2.50g al(no3)33NaOH(aq) + Al(NO3)3 = 3NaNO3(aq) + Al(OH)3(aq)2. Using the same equation in no.1, What mass of Al(OH)3 os produced from 25ml 1.0M Al(NO3)3 pa turo din sa solution pano kunin hehe​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{1. \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of NaOH by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 3 moles of NaOH is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al(NO₃)₃. Note that the molar mass of Al(NO₃)₃ is 213.0 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NaOH} & = 2.50 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}}{213.0 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \times \frac{\text{3 mol NaOH}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \\ & = \text{0.0352 mol NaOH} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the volume of NaOH solution.

Note that 1 molar (M) is equal to 1 mole per liter (mol/L).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of NaOH solution} & = \frac{\text{moles of NaOH}}{\text{molarity of NaOH solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0352 mol}}{0.300 \: M} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0352 mol}}{\text{0.300 mol/L}} \\ & = \text{0.117 L} \\ & = 0.117 \: \cancel{\text{L}} \times \frac{\text{1000 mL}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{L}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{117 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of NaOH needed is 117 mL.

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{2. \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of Al(NO₃)₃.

Note that 25 mL is equal to 0.025 L.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 & = \text{molarity of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 \: \text{solution} \times \text{volume of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 \: \text{solution} \\ & = 1.0 \: M \times \text{0.025 L} \\ & = \text{1.0 mol/L} \times \text{0.025 L} \\ & = \text{0.025 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the mass of Al(OH)₃ produced using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Al(NO₃)₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al(OH)₃. Note that the molar mass of Al(OH)₃ is 78.00 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 & = 0.025 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \times \frac{\text{78.00 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{2.0 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of Al(OH)₃ produced is 2.0 g.

1. Caffeine, C8H10N4O2

To calculate the percentage composition of caffeine, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Caffeine = (8 x 12.01 g/mol) + (10 x 1.01 g/mol) + (4 x 14.01 g/mol) + (2 x 16.00 g/mol) = 194.19 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in caffeine:

Percentage of Carbon = (8 x 12.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 49.48%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (10 x 1.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 5.19%

Percentage of Nitrogen = (4 x 14.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 28.85%

Percentage of Oxygen = (2 x 16.00 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 16.48%

2. Ammonium nitrate, NH4NO3

To calculate the percentage composition of ammonium nitrate, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Ammonium Nitrate = (1 x 14.01 g/mol) + (4 x 1.01 g/mol) + (3 x 16.00 g/mol) = 80.04 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in ammonium nitrate:

Percentage of Nitrogen = (1 x 14.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 17.50%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (4 x 1.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 5.03%

Percentage of Oxygen = (3 x 16.00 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 37.51%

Percentage of Ammonium ion (NH4+) = (4 x 1.01 g/mol + 1 x 14.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 40.96%

3. Benzene, C6H6

To calculate the percentage composition of benzene, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Benzene = (6 x 12.01 g/mol) + (6 x 1.01 g/mol) = 78.11 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in benzene:

Percentage of Carbon = (6 x 12.01 g/mol) / 78.11 g/mol x 100% = 92.26%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (6 x 1.01 g/mol) / 78.11 g/mol x 100% = 7.74%

4. Iron(II) oxide, FeO

To calculate the percentage composition of iron(II) oxide, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Iron(II) oxide = 55.85 g/mol + 16.00 g/mol = 71.85 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in iron(II) oxide:

Percentage of Iron = 55.85 g/mol / 71.85 g/mol x 100% = 77.67%

Percentage of Oxygen = 16.00 g/mol / 71.85 g/mol x 100% = 22.33%
