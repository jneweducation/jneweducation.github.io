22. An observer is 120 meters away from the base of a television tower, the towers' height is 50 meters. find the angle of elevation from the observer to the top of the tower​


Answer:

We can use trigonometry to solve this problem. Let's call the angle of elevation we want to find "θ".

In a right triangle with the tower as the vertical side and the distance from the observer to the tower as the horizontal side, the tangent of θ is equal to the opposite side (height of the tower) divided by the adjacent side (distance from the observer to the tower):

tan(θ) = 50/120

We can simplify this by dividing both sides by 10:

tan(θ) = 5/12

Now we need to find the angle whose tangent is 5/12. We can use inverse tangent (or arctan) to do this:

θ = arctan(5/12)

Using a calculator, we find that θ is approximately 22.62 degrees. Therefore, the angle of elevation from the observer to the top of the tower is 22.62 degrees.
