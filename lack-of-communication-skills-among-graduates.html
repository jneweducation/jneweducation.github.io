In psychology, attachment theory holds that each person's "style" of doing relationships is shaped by the type of care they received from their earliest caregivers. If one or both people have an insecure attachment style in their relationship—that is, they tend to form insecure attachments with others instead of steady and secure ones—then communication will be driven by anxiety rather than authenticity.

Insecure attachment is when a person responds to their own needs for connection by either desperately avoiding them (avoidant attachment) or desperately pursuing them (anxious attachment). 11. lack of marketing skills​


Answer:Product:

• The lack of proper marketing relative to the product is related both to product quality and the ability of the product or service to provide value to consumers based on their interests, needs and preferences. Products and services that do not provide value or whose quality does not satisfy consumers once they have made a purchase decision will have a negative impact on marketing effectiveness, regardless of how well the product is promoted. Effective marketers will take steps to not only understand their market’s needs but will research customers to determine how well their products meet those needs and continually take steps to improve product quality.

Price:

• Products and services that are not priced optimally represent a common lack of proper marketing. Ineffective pricing can occur at both ends of the spectrum, from pricing a product or service too low to pricing it too high. Pricing is as much an art as a science, and marketers must consider both attributes of their own products, as well as how other competitive products are priced, the image they wish their brand/product to have in the marketplace and consumer perception. There are no right answers that will apply to all organizations. The right price point will be determined individually. For instance, clearly Lexus has a different pricing strategy than Saturn, yet both have achieved success in their respective markets.

Place:

• Place is an element of the marketing mix that can often create problems for marketers. Place, which may also be thought of as access, refers to consumers’ ability to actually access or purchase a product. Products that are not readily available on a retailer’s shelf, for instance, regardless of their high quality or well-chosen price points will obviously not achieve sales success. On the other hand, some marketers have had success in generating demand for products that are scarce, which can be a positive marketing approach. Consider the holiday season where it seems that each year there is some toy that is in high demand, yet limited access, driving demand and word-of-mouth in positive ways.

Promotion:

• Lack of proper marketing can certainly be reflected in an organization’s promotional efforts, which include advertising, sales promotion, public relations and events. Marketers today have the ability to choose from a broad range of communication channels to get information about their products and services to their target audiences, but they must choose wisely both to ensure that resources are used effectively and that results are positive. Promotional efforts should be selected based on the specific audience being targeted and a thorough understanding of that audience and the communication tools most likely to reach them at a point in time when they will be attentive to and interested in the message.

Nonverbal communication is important as well, and it can include anything from gestures and facial expressions to a sense of time, personal distance, and even seating arrangements.

Cultural differences account for how people move to complete tasks. Reasons include different resource availability, different notions of time, and different ideas of how relationship-building and task-oriented work should fit together. Ex. Different approaches to knowing. Different cultures have different ideas when it comes to gaining knowledge. Europeans consider information gained through counting and measuring more valuable than other means. Some African cultures rely on knowledge gained through symbolic imagery, while some Asian cultures emphasize the validity of knowledge gained through transcendence.

25. Skills and Competencies of ABM Students/Graduates​


ABM stands for Accountancy, Business, and Management, a strand in the senior high school curriculum in the Philippines that prepares students for careers in finance, accounting, marketing, and other business-related fields. Here are some of the key skills and competencies that ABM students/graduates should possess:

1. Financial literacy: ABM students should be knowledgeable about basic accounting principles, financial management, budgeting, and financial analysis.

2. Business acumen: ABM students should have a solid understanding of business operations, including marketing, sales, human resources, and operations management.

3. Analytical thinking: ABM students should be able to analyze and interpret data, identify patterns, and make informed decisions based on evidence.

4. Communication skills: ABM students should be able to communicate effectively both orally and in writing, and have the ability to convey complex information to diverse audiences.

5. Leadership skills: ABM students should possess the ability to work collaboratively, manage teams, and lead others effectively.

6. Critical thinking: ABM students should be able to evaluate information, identify problems, and propose effective solutions.

7. Ethical awareness: ABM students should be aware of ethical issues in business and finance, and be able to make ethical decisions in complex situations.

8. Technological proficiency: ABM students should be comfortable using technology and software commonly used in the business world, such as accounting software, data analysis tools, and presentation software.

9. Adaptability: ABM students should possess the ability to adapt to changing circumstances and be willing to learn and apply new skills as needed.

10. Entrepreneurial mindset: ABM students should have an entrepreneurial mindset and possess the creativity and innovation needed to start and run successful businesses.
