12. Give the three Types of Speech Contexts *** Give the Three Types of Speech Acts***​


Answer:

Three types of speech contexts

1. Intrapersonal

2 Interpersonal

3 Public

Three types of speech acts

locutionary

illocutionary

perlocutionary

Answer:

Three Types of Speech Contexts:

Intrapersonal

- this communication happens within oneself. This means that the speaker acts as the source and the receiver of the message. When you do self-reflection, you analyze your thoughts and feelings. By knowing what you feel, you can assess yourself on how to react to it.

Interpersonal

- this communication involves two or more people. The conversation can be formal or informal, intimate or casual depending on the relationship people involved have.

Mass communication

- this refers to communicating that takes place through television, social media, newspaper, magazines, and the like as channels. This type of communication reaches millions of people.

Three Types of Speech Acts:

Locutionary

- this act occurs when a natural unit of speech, such as a sound, a word, or even a sentence, is uttered. What is necessary for an utterance is to be Locutionary Act is that it makes sense and, more significantly, that it has the same meaning for both the Speaker and the Listener. In reality, when the Speaker adjusts the utterance for the Listener, it creates shared meaning. The utterance follows the norms of language, regardless of whether it is a sound, a word, or a phrase.

Illocutionary

- it is not merely stating anything in an illocutionary speech act, but the act of expressing something to express a viewpoint, verify or refute a claim;

making a forecast, making a pledge, or making a request; issuing a decision or an order; or advising or approving.

- this speech act uses the illocutionary force of a statement, a confirmation, a denial, a prediction, a promise, a request, etc.

Perlocutionary

- this occurs when a certain impact is desired from the Speaker, the Listener, or both. The response, which may or may not be physical or vocal, is provoked by:

inspiring or demeaning; persuading/convincing; or deterring/scaring.

- the goal of the Perlocutionary Speech Act is to influence people's feelings, thoughts, or behaviors.

25. Give three advantages and three disadvantages sound?​


Answer:

Advantages of Sound:

1. Communication: Sound is an essential means of communication, allowing individuals to convey messages and ideas effectively. It is used in various fields such as entertainment, education, news, and emergency services.

2. Entertainment: Sound provides a rich and immersive experience for entertainment purposes such as music, movies, and video games. It enhances the overall experience and makes it more engaging and enjoyable.

3. Alarm and Warning Systems: Sound is used in alarm and warning systems to alert people in case of danger or emergency. For example, smoke detectors, car horns, and sirens rely on sound to convey their message.

Disadvantages of Sound:

1. Noise Pollution: Excessive and unwanted sound can lead to noise pollution, causing physical and mental health problems such as hearing loss, stress, and sleep disturbance.

2. Miscommunication: Sound can be subject to misinterpretation or miscommunication, leading to misunderstandings, confusion, and conflict.

3. Distraction: Sound can be distracting, making it difficult to concentrate or focus on a particular task. It can be especially problematic in work environments, schools, and public spaces where quiet is necessary.

26. Give at least Three (3) Weaknesses of a Research Give at least Three (3) Strengths of a Research ​


Answer:

Three weaknesses of a research study are:

1.) Sampling bias
— This occurs when the sample used in the study is not representative of the population being studied, leading to inaccurate conclusions.

2.) Lack of generalizability
— Sometimes, the findings of a research study may only be applicable to a specific group or situation and may not be generalized to other groups or situations.

3.) Methodological limitations
— Research studies may be limited by their methodologies, such as flaws in the data collection process, measurement errors, or issues with the research design.

Three strengths of a research study are:

1.) Provides evidence-based information
— Research studies provide objective, verifiable data that can be used to support or refute claims and inform decision-making.

2.) Rigorous testing of hypotheses
— Research studies are designed to test hypotheses using rigorous and systematic methods, providing a more reliable way of determining cause-and-effect relationships.

3.) Contributes to the body of knowledge
— Research studies help to expand the body of knowledge in a particular field, providing a foundation for future research and innovation.
