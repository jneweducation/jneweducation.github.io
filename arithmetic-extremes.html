19. how do we findthe arithmetic mean of two arithmetic extreme​


Answer:

We can find the arithmetic mean of two arithmetic extremes by adding both of the arithmetic means and dividing the answer by 2.

Step-by-step explanation:

For example:

The two arithmetic extremes are 8 and 16. Find the arithmetic mean. Add both of the arithmetic mean, [tex]8+16=24[/tex], then divide it by 2, [tex]\frac{24}{2}=12[/tex]. The arithmetic mean is 12.

Another example:

What is the arithmetic mean if the arithmetic extremes are 50 and 100. Add 50 and 100, it equals to 150. Divide it by 2, it equals to 75. The arithmetic mean is 75.

20. How do we find the arithmetic mean of two arithmetic extremes?​


To find arithmetic mean of two arithmetic extremes is to sum the two extremes, then divide it by two.

Further explanation:

An arithmetic sequence is a mathematical progression in which each successive term increases by a fixed amount. This fixed amount is called the common difference. In an arithmetic sequence, the common difference is constant throughout the sequence. The general form of an arithmetic sequence is:

a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d,...

where a is the first term in the sequence and d is the common difference.

The arithmetic mean is the sum of the data values divided by the number of data values. To calculate the arithmetic mean, first add together all of the data values. Then, divide this sum by the number of data values.

The arithmetic mean of a pair of numbers is also called the average. Therefore, we can get the mean by getting the average of the two arithmetic extremes.
