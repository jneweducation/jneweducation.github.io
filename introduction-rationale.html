Title: The Effects of Online Learning on the Academic Performance of Senior High School Students in the Philippines

Introduction:

Rationale:
The ongoing COVID-19 pandemic has forced educational institutions to shift to online learning as a means of continuing education despite the restrictions on face-to-face classes. While online learning has been implemented in some schools prior to the pandemic, its widespread use has brought about questions on its effectiveness, particularly in terms of academic performance. This study aims to investigate the effects of online learning on the academic performance of senior high school students in the Philippines.

Seriousness of the Problem:
The senior high school level is a crucial stage in the education of Filipino students as it prepares them for higher education or the workforce. The shift to online learning has disrupted the traditional classroom setup and has challenged both students and teachers to adapt to new learning methods. Understanding the effects of online learning on academic performance is important in ensuring that students receive quality education despite the limitations brought about by the pandemic.

Setting:
The study will be conducted in selected senior high schools in the Philippines that have implemented online learning as a means of continuing education during the pandemic. The study will cover students from different regions in the country to ensure a diverse sample.

General Objective:
The general objective of this study is to determine the effects of online learning on the academic performance of senior high school students in the Philippines.

General Purpose or Overall Purpose:
The overall purpose of this study is to contribute to the understanding of the effectiveness of online learning in the Philippine education system and to provide insights for educators and policymakers in improving the delivery of education during times of crisis.

Literature Foundation:
Previous studies have shown mixed results on the effectiveness of online learning on academic performance. Some studies have shown that online learning can be as effective as traditional classroom learning, while others have shown that it may have a negative impact on academic performance. This study aims to contribute to the literature by focusing on the Philippine context and by investigating the effects of online learning on senior high school students, a crucial stage in the education of Filipino students.

Title: The Impact of Social Media on Mental Health among University Students in the Philippines

Introduction:
Rationale: Social media has become an integral part of modern society, with millions of people around the world using various platforms to communicate, share information, and connect with others. While social media has many benefits, recent research has also highlighted its potential negative effects, particularly on mental health. University students are particularly vulnerable to the impact of social media on mental health due to their high levels of stress, academic pressure, and social isolation.

Seriousness of the Problem: The mental health of university students is a growing concern in the Philippines, with rates of anxiety, depression, and suicide on the rise. Social media use has been linked to increased feelings of loneliness, anxiety, and depression, as well as decreased self-esteem and academic performance. It is therefore important to examine the relationship between social media use and mental health among university students in the Philippines.

General Objective: The general objective of this study is to explore the impact of social media use on the mental health of university students in the Philippines.

Literature Foundation: Previous research has identified a number of potential mechanisms through which social media use may impact mental health, including social comparison, fear of missing out (FOMO), and cyberbullying. However, there is limited research on the specific impact of social media on mental health among university students in the Philippines. This study will build on existing literature by examining the unique experiences of university students in the Philippines, and by exploring the potential role of cultural and contextual factors in shaping the relationship between social media use and mental health.

Deforestation refers to the large-scale removal of trees from forests, primarily for the purpose of commercial use of forest resources such as timber, pulp, and fuelwood. This study aims to explore the causes, impacts, and potential solutions to the problem of deforestation.

Rationale of the Study:

Deforestation is a serious issue that has various environmental, economic, and social consequences. The clearing of forests not only contributes to the loss of biodiversity but also to the release of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, leading to global climate change. Additionally, deforestation often negatively impacts local communities who depend on forests for their livelihoods. Understanding the causes and impacts of deforestation is crucial to develop effective strategies to address this problem.

Theoretical Background:

There are various theories that attempt to explain the causes and effects of deforestation. The political economy perspective argues that deforestation is a result of uneven power relations between actors, with powerful actors benefiting from resource extraction. The ecological perspective emphasizes the importance of forests in maintaining ecological balance and biodiversity. The social justice perspective highlights the effects of deforestation on marginalized communities.

Abstract:

Statement of the Problem:

Deforestation is a growing concern worldwide, with an estimated 18.7 million acres of forest lost annually. This study seeks to understand the underlying causes and consequences of deforestation and explore potential solutions to mitigate its negative impacts.

Significance of the Study:

The study of deforestation is important due to its far-reaching impacts on the environment, economy, and social welfare. By examining the root causes and effects of deforestation, this study is expected to inform policy-makers and stakeholders on effective strategies to address this critical issue.

Research Methodology:

Research Design:

This study will employ a mixed-methods research design, using both qualitative and quantitative data. The research will collect data through literature review, interviews, and surveys. Data analysis will include both content analysis and statistical analysis.

Definition of Terms:

Rationale:

Mindfulness meditation has gained increasing attention in recent years as a way to improve mental health and wellbeing. Studies have shown that practicing mindfulness can reduce stress, anxiety, and depression, and enhance cognitive functions such as attention and memory. However, there is limited research on the effects of mindfulness meditation on academic performance, particularly among college students who face unique stressors and pressures.

Seriousness of the Problem:

College students are at a high risk of experiencing stress and mental health issues, which can negatively impact their academic performance and overall wellbeing. According to a 2018 report by the American College Health Association, 63% of college students reported feeling overwhelming anxiety in the past year. Additionally, academic stressors such as exams and deadlines can exacerbate these issues, leading to poor academic outcomes.

Setting:

The study will take place at a large public university in the United States, with a diverse student population. Participants will be recruited from various majors and academic levels.

General Objective:

The objective of this study is to investigate the effects of mindfulness meditation on the academic performance of college students.

General Purpose or Overall Purpose:

The overall purpose of this study is to contribute to the existing literature on mindfulness meditation and its potential benefits for college students. By understanding the effects of mindfulness on academic performance, we can inform interventions and support services that promote student success and wellbeing.

Literature foundation:

Previous research has demonstrated the positive effects of mindfulness on mental health and cognitive function. A meta-analysis by Khoury et al. (2015) found that mindfulness-based interventions were effective in reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression. Additionally, studies have shown that mindfulness can enhance attention and working memory (Zeidan et al., 2010), which are important factors for academic success. However, there is a gap in the literature on the effects of mindfulness on academic performance specifically, particularly among college students. This study aims to address this gap and build upon existing research.
