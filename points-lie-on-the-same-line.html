18. collinear points lie on the same line​


In Euclidean geometry, Collinear points are points that all lie in the same line, whether they are close together, far apart, or form a ray, line segment, or line. A little Latin helps: col + linear = collinear. col means "together." Our word college comes from the same prefix. Linear means line.

Collinear Points Examples

We will leave you with a side view of a little street brazier for making skewered meat kebabs. Notice the legs cross and have a bottom brace, which creates two triangles to keep the brazier stable. Can you find at least 1010 sets of collinear points?

We are sure you saw sets like points A and B, C and D, and points A − F − E − I − DA - F - E - I - D, but did you also pick up on ones like CH, HE, EG,CH, HE, EG, and GBGB?

The points C − H − EC - H - E and E − I − DE - I - D, which form two sides of a triangle (the bottom triangle) are also collinear.

Look at points H − E − GH - E - G and E − G − BE - G - B. Each of these three points are collinear as well. Keep looking; more sets of collinear points are waiting to be found!

Objects are coplanar if they lie in the same plane. We typically think of these objects as points or lines, or 2D shapes. Points, lines, or shapes are non-coplanar if they do not lie in the same plane. ... Points A, B, C, and D lie in plane M so are coplanar but not collinear since they do not lie on the same line.
