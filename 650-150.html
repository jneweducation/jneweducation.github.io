Answer: A) 70,650 km2

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the area of coverage for the cell site, we need to calculate the area of a circle with a radius of 150 km. Using the formula A = πr^2, where r is the radius and π is approximately 3.14, we get an answer of approximately 70,650 km^2. Therefore, option A is correct.

Answer:

A. 70,650 km^2.

Step-by-step explanation:

Assuming the transmission tower is at the center of a circular area of coverage, the area can be calculated using the formula for the area of a circle:

Area = πr^2

where r is the radius of the circle. If the signals are transmitted 150 km in all directions from the transmission tower, then the radius of the circle is also 150 km.

Area = π(150 km)^2

Area = 22,500π km^2

Area ≈ 70,685.84 km^2

Therefore, the area of coverage is approximately 70,685.84 km^2. The closest option provided is A. 70,650 km^2.
