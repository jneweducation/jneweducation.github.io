Answer:

The COUNT function is generally used to count a range of cells containing numbers or dates excluding blanks. COUNTA, on the other hand will count everything... numbers, dates, text or a range containing a mixture of these items, but does not count blank cells. COUNTA stands for count all.

29. A bill was passed by a vote of 7:5. What part of the vote counts were in favor of the bill?


Answer:

7/12 or 58.33%

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, since the question is vague, I will assume that the asked is the fractional part of the vote counts in favor of the bill. Saying this, we can express this in either a fraction or a percent.

First, we need to figure out which side of the ratio voted in favor of the bill. Since the bill passed, then we can imply that the higher number is the vote in favor, in this case 7.

Now, we can treat 7 as the numerator. We can get the denominator by adding the count of all voters, in this case 7 + 5 = 12.

Now, we have the fraction is 7/12. We can find the percent by dividing it. Saying this we get 0.5833... Turning this into a rate, we get 58.33%.

Therefore, 7/12 or 58.33% of the voters voted in favor of the bill.
