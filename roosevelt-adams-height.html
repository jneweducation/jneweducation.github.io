29. Adam sighted a dove from the top of a tree with an angle of 60 above his line of sight. At the same time, he spotted a dog from the foot of the tree with an angle of 30 below his line of sight. If Adam’s height is 5 ft and he is 7 ft away from the dog, what is the height h of the tree? Express your answer in nearest tenths.


Answer:

h = 10.2 ft

Step-by-step explanation:

Let h be the height of the tree.

Since the angles are complementary, the angles in the triangle formed by Adam, the dove and the tree are 60, 30, and 90 respectively.

We can use the Law of Sines to solve for h:

sin(60°) / h = sin(30°) / 7

h = 7sin(60°) / sin(30°)

h ≈ 10.2 ft

The height of the tree is 10.2 ft, rounded to the nearest tenth.
