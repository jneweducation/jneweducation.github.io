11. what is the difference between tacit and explicit knowledge??​


Answer:

Tacit knowledge is the knowing of things without knowing how you know; explicit knowledge is the knowing of things that you can explain. For example, most people can speak grammatically without being able to explain the rules of grammar. This is Tacit knowledge.

12. What is a journal? When does a company record a transaction in the journal?


Answer:

TWO MAJOR TYPES OF BOOKS OF ACCOUNTS ARE JOURNAL AND LEDGER Journalization  The process of recording transactions in journal Journal  A book of original entry; a permanent chronological record of the debits and credits of individual transactions. 2 types of Journal  

1. General Journal  

2. Special Journal

Transaction  The term used in the unit of organization in a journal. Sources of Journal Entries   Taken from business papers which are known as source documents. Source Documents:

1.Beginning balance sheet: used for making the opening

2. Official receipt: A documents that gives evidence to a transaction involving receipt of cash

3. Service Invoice: Gives evidence involving the rendering services,

4. Credit Memo: A document involving allowances granted on services rendered to a client by giving information on the name of client, the amount to be credited and the reason for crediting it.

5. Cash Voucher- A document that gives evidence of the payment of cash by giving information as to the name and address of the payee, date of payment, amount and explanation of payment.

6. Check- A document that may be prepared together with the cash voucher; an order in writing, signed by the depositor, ordering the bank to pay cash from the depositor’s account.

7. Purchase Invoice- A business form furnished to the buyer by the seller on which are listed the goods sold, the method of shipment and the cost of the items.

8. Sales Invoice- A business form furnished by the seller to a buyer.

The journal makes several significant contributions to the recording process  It discloses in one place the complete effects of a transaction  It provides chronological record of transaction  It helps to prevent or locate errors because the debit and credit amounts for each entry can be easily compared. Parts of the Journal Entry:

• Date-  Used to record the date when the transaction occurred.

• Particulars/Description- used to record the accounts affected by the transaction together with a brief explanation of a transaction.

• F or P/R: -stands for Folio or post reference and serves.

22. give tow example of tacit knowledge​


Answer:

What is tacit knowledge?

Tacit knowledge or implicit knowledge—as opposed to formal, codified or explicit knowledge—is knowledge that is difficult to express or extract, and thus more difficult to transfer to others by means of writing it down or verbalizing it.

A few, more pertinent examples of tacit knowledge:

Being able to identify the exact moment a prospect is ready to hear your sales pitch.Knowing just the right words to use within your copy to attract and engage your audience.Knowing which specific piece of content to deliver to a customer based on their expressed needs.
