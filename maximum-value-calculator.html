3. A 25-CYCLE TRANSFORMER HAS 2, 250 PRIMARY TURNS AND 150 SECONDARY TURNS. IF THE MAXIMUM VALUE OF THE MUTUAL FLUX IS 2.64 x 106 Maxwells, CALCULATE: (A) THE PRIMARY AND SECONDARY INDUCED VOLTAGES (B) THE RATIO OF TRANSFORMATION​


Solution:

(A) The primary and secondary induced voltages can be calculated as follows:

Primary induced voltage (Ep) = Np(dΦ/dt)

Secondary induced voltage (Es) = Ns(dΦ/dt)

where Np and Ns are the number of turns in the primary and secondary coils, respectively, and dΦ/dt is the rate of change of the magnetic flux.

Given that the maximum value of the mutual flux is 2.64 x 106 Maxwells and the frequency is 25 cycles per second, we can calculate the rate of change of the flux as follows:

dΦ/dt = (2 x max. value of flux x frequency)/(cycles per revolution)

= (2 x 2.64 x 106 x 25)/(1)

= 132 x 106 Maxwells per second

Substituting the values into the equations for the primary and secondary induced voltages, we get:

Ep = Np(dΦ/dt) = 2,250 x 132 x 106 = 297,000,000 volts

Es = Ns(dΦ/dt) = 150 x 132 x 106 = 19,800,000 volts

Therefore, the primary induced voltage is 297,000,000 volts and the secondary induced voltage is 19,800,000 volts.

(B) The ratio of transformation is simply the ratio of the number of turns in the primary coil to the number of turns in the secondary coil:

Ratio of transformation = Np/Ns = 2,250/150 = 15:1

Therefore, the ratio of transformation is 15:1.
