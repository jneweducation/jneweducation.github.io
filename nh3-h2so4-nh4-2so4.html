29. How many grams of NH3 is needed to produce 50.0 g of (NH4)2SO4? 6.44g 8.67g 12.88g 194g


Answer:

mass NH₃ = 12.88 g

Explanation:

Writing the balanced chemical equation

2NH₃ + H₂SO₄ → (NH₄)₂SO₄

Calculating the molar mass of NH₃ and (NH₄)₂SO₄

For NH₃

MM NH₃ = (14.006703 g × 1) + (1.007941 g × 3)

MM NH₃ = 17.031 g

For (NH₄)₂SO₄

MM (NH₄)₂SO₄ = (14.006703 g × 2) + (1.007941 g × 8) + (32.064787 g × 1) + (15.999405 g × 4)

MM (NH₄)₂SO₄ = 132.14 g

Calculating the mass of NH₃ required

mass NH₃ = 50.0 g (NH₄)₂SO₄ × (1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄ / 132.14 g (NH₄)₂SO₄) × (2 mol NH₃ / 1 mol (NH₄)₂SO₄) × (17.031 g NH₃ / 1 mol NH₃)

mass NH₃ = 12.88 g
