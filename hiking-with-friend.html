Before doing an exercise to a location you have never visited before, you should conduct research on that location, schedule your hike, bring your valuable first aid supplies, particularly a phone for emergencies, and be prepared to enjoy new experiences.

What is cardiovascular exercise?

Cardiovascular exercise, also known as aerobic or endurance exercise, is some form of operation that uses aerobic metabolism. That is, during the activity, oxygen plays a significant role in the cellular reactions that provide the energy required to support the activity. Your heart rate increases, and you breathe more heavily in order to reduce the amount of oxygen in your blood to make you use oxygen more effectively. As a result, you are more energized and do not tire easily. Cardiovascular exercise is any physical movement that boosts heart rate and respiration while still increasing oxygen and blood supply across the body by using large muscle groups repetitively and rhythmically. This type of operation gradually stresses the most important internal organs and strengthens the structure and efficiency of your heart, lungs, and circulatory system. Cardiovascular exercise benefits many facets of fitness, including cardiac health, emotional health, mood, sleep, weight control, and metabolism. Brisk walking, biking, cycling, diving, rowing, and cross-country skiing are also examples of aerobic activities. Cardio equipment in the gym include treadmills, elliptical trainers, stationary cycles, stepping machines, rowing machines, and ski trainers. Lifting weights, using weight machines, resistance training, and core drills are examples of non-cardio exercises. They are designed to test strength rather than aerobic stamina.

Warming Up and Stretching

Warming up before the most intense part of the exercise increases blood supply to your muscles and helps you loosen up. This is critical; you should not begin your workout at full force. Traditionally, during the warm-up, you can stretch the main muscles that will be used in the exercise. There are several schools of thought about the use and efficacy of stretching, with some researchers recommending a dynamic warm-up but not static stretches before aerobic exercise.

Cooling Down

After finishing your exercise in your goal heart rate zone, you can cool down with five to ten minutes of lower intensity exercise (again, 50 to 60 percent of your normal heart rate). Traditionally, you will finish the workout by gently stretching the muscles that you used during the workout. This is no longer widely recommended, but you are free to do so if you so desire.

Navigation Tools: Make sure to bring a map, compass or GPS for your hiking trip. You’ll want to know where you are and where you’re going at all times, but you will also want to know where all the campsites, emergency exits, water sources and rest areas are located. The American Hiking Society recommends bringing along a map or compass — even if you have a GPS as a backup. When you know what to expect, you can plan other details better and enjoy the hike without worrying about getting lost.

Adequate Water: Water is perhaps the most important item on your hiking gear list. Without enough water, your body cannot perform at its best. Make sure to hydrate before the hiking trip and sip water throughout the day. Whether it is cold or hot outside, an adequate water supply should always be a priority.
