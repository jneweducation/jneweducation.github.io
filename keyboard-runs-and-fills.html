4. 5. What are the two types of Keyboard? A. Numeric keyboard and Wireless Keyboard B. Wireless Keyboard and ergonomic keyboard C. Ergonomic Keyboard and Typewriter Keyboard D. Typewriter keyboard and Numeric Keyboard​


The answer is B. Wireless Keyboard and ergonomic keyboard.

A wireless keyboard is a type of keyboard that connects to a computer or other device using a wireless connection, such as Bluetooth or Wi-Fi, instead of a physical cable. This allows for greater flexibility in positioning the keyboard and eliminates the need for a cable, making it ideal for use with laptops, tablets, and other mobile devices.

An ergonomic keyboard, on the other hand, is a type of keyboard designed to reduce strain and fatigue on the hands, wrists, and arms while typing. This can be achieved through various design features such as split or contoured key layout, adjustable key spacing, and integrated palm supports. These features are intended to promote a more natural and comfortable typing posture, reducing the risk of injury and increasing overall typing comfort.

Frequently used shortcuts

F5-Start a presentation from the beginning.

Shift+F5-Start a presentation from the current slide.

Alt+F5-Start the presentation in Presenter View.

PowerPoint 2010 and 2007: Not available

N/Enter/Page Down/Right arrow key/Down arrow key/Spacebar-Perform the next animation or advance to the next slide.

P/Page Up/Left arrow key/Up arrow key/Backspace-perform the previous animation or return to the previous slide.

Ctrl+H-Hide the pointer and navigation buttons.

B/Period (.)-Display a blank black slide, or return to the presentation from a blank black slide.

W/Comma(,)-Display a blank white slide, or return to the presentation from a blank white slide.

S-Stop or restart an automatic presentation.

Esc-End the presentation.
