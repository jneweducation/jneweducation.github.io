Beef marcon dish

1. Cool the morcon in the sauce.

2. Chill overnight to make slicing easier and neater. Lift the morcon off the sauce.

3. Reheat the sauce in a small sauce pan until simmering.

Pork hamonado dish

1. In a large wok, add 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Add garlic and saute until golden brown for 1-2 minutes.

3. Don’t forget to turn over the pork rolls from one side to another every 10-15minutes.When sauce becomes thick, separate the pork rolls  and transfer on a serving platter. Gently cut the string around the rolls and slice into serving pieces.

6. B. What is Garnishing? What is the purpose of garnishingin terms of preparing the food?Ans:Ans:​


Answer:

A garnish is an item or substance used as a decoration or embellishment accompanying a prepared food dish or drink.

In many cases, it may give added or contrasting flavor. Some garnishes are selected mainly to augment the visual impact of the plate, while others are selected specifically for the flavor they may impart.

12. canape garnish ingredients​


Answer:

All crustacian canape,garnished with deep-fried capers?

Explanation:

INGREDIENTS

2 cups small cooked shrimp, lobster, or lump crabmeat, coarsely chopped (use any 1 of the crustaceans or a mix of all 3)

Enough mayonnaise to bind (about 1/3 to 1/2 cup)

Enough paprika so it doesn't look anemic

At least 1/2 cup chopped red onion

At least 1/2 cup parmesan

Salt and pepper to taste

Small scallop shells for serving

The Capers:

1/4 cup large salt-packed capers

1 1/2 cups vegetable oil

PREPARATION

Turn on broiler.

Mix all ingredients and—if this is 1959—put into scallop shells.

Run under broiler until brown and shiny, and serve with tiny cocktail forks. If you are less retro, put on any sturdy cracker or black bread.

Run under broiler. Invariably devoured.

The capers:

Rinse capers and soak in fresh water. After 1/2 hour, change water and soak for another 1/2 hour. Remove capers and pat very dry.

Heat oil and fry capers in 2 batches, until quite crisp and lightly browned, about 30 seconds each.

Drain on paper towels and serve immediately.

Garnish the above or just serve in a bowl.

Answer:

What is a garnish?

It’s a decorative element that should simultaneously elevate the taste of a dish or drink.

Too often appearance is given more importance than taste. This is a mistake. A garnish should elevate appearance and taste. In other words, the garnish should be in line with the flavor of the dish.

Some garnishes that go well with fish and will add a pop of color are:

Fresh lemon or lime wedges.

Sprigs of fresh herbs.

Grilled fruits.

Salsa.

Grape tomatoes.

Hot peppers.

Olives.

Red and yellow bell peppers.
