14. How many molecules are there in 0.75 mole of sodium chloride? Select one: a. 4.5165 x 1023 b. 4.1656 x 1023 c. 4.6515 x 1023 d. 4.5615 x 1023


Hello!

How many molecules are there in 0.75 mole of sodium chloride?

Select one:

a. 4.5165 x 10²³  b. 4.1656 x 10²³  c. 4.6515 x 10²³  d. 4.5615 x 10²³

Solving:

By the law of avogrado, we know that for each mole of substance, we have 6.022 * 10²³ molecules, then:

1 mole -------------------------- 6.022*10²³ molecules

0.75 mole --------------------- y molecules

[tex]\dfrac{1}{0.75} = \dfrac{6.022*10^{23}}{y}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]1*y = 0.75*6.022*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{y = 4.5165*10^{23}\:molecules}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

a. 4.5165 x 10²³

Solution:

[tex]\text{number of H atoms = 2 mol NH₃} × \frac{\text{3 mol H}}{\text{1 mol NH₃}} × \frac{\text{6.022 × 10²³ H atoms}}{\text{1 mol H}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{number of H atoms = 3.61 × 10²⁴ atoms}}[/tex]
