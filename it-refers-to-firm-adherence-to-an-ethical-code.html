19. It refers to the ethical values and the system of moral practice directly resulting from an adherence to the beliefs *A. CodeB. CultC. CreedD. Christian​


The ethical values and the system of moral practice resulting from beliefs are called a A. Code.

Codes are a set of rules and guidelines that followers of a certain belief system must follow. These set of rules usually manifest in what is considered permissible, prohibited, bad, and good. For example in the religion of Christianity polygamy is prohibited, drinking alcohol is allowed, and premarital sex is considered bad while the heterosexual marital union is good.

In other religions like Judaism, some branches allow for divorce, premarital sex is also prohibited, compassion for others is good and destruction of the environment is bad.

People who grew up in the religion they are in and even if they consider themselves not part of that religion anymore tend to internalize the code of that religion subconsciously. The more you gain knowledge of the different codes of different religions the more you will realize the social and arbitrary nature of it. When a person internalizes a religion’s code there’s a tendency to assume that such morality and practices are universal when it is not.

While Code is the rules and guidelines, Creed on the other hand is the accepted truth of a certain belief system. For example, the Roman Catholic creed includes Jesus as the Son of God that has come to Earth to cleanse us of our sin. For Muslims, it is Muhammad who is the Last Prophet of God.

Cult, in its contemporary meaning, is defined as “a group or movement held together by a shared commitment to a charismatic leader or ideology. It has a belief system that has the answers to all of life’s questions and offers a special solution to be gained only by following the leader’s rules.”

This definition comes from Holly Meyer’s article What makes a cult a cult? Contemporary cults are usually characterized by their excessive worship and belief that the public considers out of the ordinary or straight-up delusional. Cults also tend to have an authoritative power structure and restrictive codes.
