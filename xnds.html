1. A. Define each musical term. 1. End Repeat Sign2.Da Capo3.Dal Segno4.Al Fine5.Al Coda6.1st snd 2nd endings â€‹


Answer:

1. End Repeat Sign - tells the performer to go back one time to the start repeat sign, or, if there is no start repeat, the beginning of the movement.

2.Da Capo - is an Italian musical term that means "from the beginning". It is often abbreviated as D.C. The term is a directive to repeat the previous part of music, often used to save space, and thus is an easier way of saying to repeat the music from the beginning.

3.Dal Segno - which is Italian for â€œfrom the sign," refers to a symbol or navigation marker that instructs a musician to repeat a passage starting from the sign. In word form, it is most often abbreviated D.S. in musical notation or just called a segno in English. It does not make sense to refer to a segno as a "segno sign," since that redundantly translates to mean "sign sign."

4.Al Fine - is an indication to play to the end of the music, or until you reach the word fine. Al fine is usually seen in the commands D.C. al fine and D.S. al fine.

5.Al Coda - means â€œfrom the sign to the coda mark.â€ D.S. al coda is an indication to start back at the segno, play until you encounter a coda, then skip to the next coda to continue.

6.1st snd 2nd endings - are used when the bulk of a repeated part is the same; when the only difference between the way it is played the first and second times through is the way the two end.

9. Find the amount of the following payments at the end of 5th year, 3000p at the end of 1st year, 4500p at the end of 2nd year and 6000p at the end of 4th year if money worth 12% per annum.


Answer:

To find the amount of the payments at the end of the 5th year, you will need to compound the interest on the payments for the first four years. Here's how you can do that:

Calculate the interest on the payment for the first year: 3000 * 12% = 360

Add the interest to the payment to find the total amount at the end of the first year: 3000 + 360 = 3360

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the first year for the second year: 3360 * 12% = 403.2

Add the interest to the total amount at the end of the first year to find the total amount at the end of the second year: 3360 + 403.2 = 3763.2

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the second year for the third year: 3763.2 * 12% = 4515.84

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the third year for the fourth year: 4515.84 * 12% = 5419.008

Add the interest to the total amount at the end of the third year to find the total amount at the end of the fourth year: 4515.84 + 5419.008 = 9934.848

Add the payment for the fourth year to the total amount at the end of the fourth year: 9934.848 + 6000 = 15934.848

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the fourth year for the fifth year: 15934.848 * 12% = 1915.181

Add the interest to the total amount at the end of the fourth year to find the total amount at the end of the fifth year: 15934.848 + 1915.181 = 17849.028

So, the total amount at the end of the fifth year would be 17849.028. This is the amount of the payments at the end of the fifth year.
