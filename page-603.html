2. Presidental decree no 603 summary


Presidential Decree No. 603, also known as the "Child and Youth Welfare Code," is a law in the Philippines that was signed on December 10, 1974, by then-President Ferdinand Marcos. It aims to promote and protect the rights and welfare of Filipino children and youth.

The following are some of the key provisions of the law:

1. The State recognizes the rights of the child to life, survival, development, education, and participation in decision-making processes that affect their lives.

2. Parents are primarily responsible for the upbringing and development of their children. They are expected to provide for their children's basic needs, such as food, shelter, education, and health care.

3. The State is responsible for providing programs and services that promote the welfare of children and youth. These programs include child and youth welfare services, educational programs, and other related services.

4. The law prohibits the employment of children below 15 years old in hazardous work, and restricts the employment of children below 18 years old in certain types of work.

5. The law provides for the establishment of a juvenile justice and welfare system that focuses on the rehabilitation and reintegration of children in conflict with the law.

6. The law also contains provisions on adoption, child abuse, and neglect, and other matters related to the welfare of children and youth.

Overall, Presidential Decree No. 603 is a comprehensive law that seeks to ensure the protection and welfare of Filipino children and youth.
