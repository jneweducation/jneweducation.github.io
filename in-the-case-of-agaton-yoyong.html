Tropical cyclones are storms with great strength. The average radius of a tropical cyclone is 150 to 200 km. Tropical cyclones form over large oceans which generally have warm sea surface temperatures, more than 26.5 °C. Strong winds rotating near its center have wind speeds of more than 63 km/h.

Technically, a tropical cyclone is defined as a synoptic-scale, non-frontal low-pressure system that grows over warm waters with an area of ​​convective cloudiness and a maximum wind speed of at least 34 knots in more than half of the area encircling its center, and lasts for at least six hours.

Sometimes at the center of a tropical cyclone an area with relatively low wind speeds and no clouds is formed which is called the eye of the cyclone.
