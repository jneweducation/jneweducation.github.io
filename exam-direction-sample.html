23. I. Find the percentage of the students who passed the exam in a 50-items. The mean is 35 and a standard deviation of 15. a. From 35 to 45 b. Between 37 to 40 c. Less than 32 d. Greater than 20 II. Given population is {12, 14, 16}. a. List down the possible samples in a sample size of 2. b. Solve for each sample mean. c. Create a Sampling Distribution. d. Solve for mean, variance and standard deviation.


Answer:

I. To find the percentage of students who passed the exam, we need to first determine the passing score. Assuming a passing score of 50%, or 25 items correct out of 50, we can standardize the passing score using the given mean and standard deviation:

z = (x - μ) / σ

z = (25 - 35) / 15

z = -0.67

a. To find the percentage of students who scored between 35 and 45, we need to find the area under the standard normal curve between z = -0.67 and z = 0, since this is the range of scores one standard deviation below the mean to the mean:

P(35 ≤ x ≤ 45) = P(-0.67 ≤ z ≤ 0) = 0.2514

Therefore, approximately 25.14% of students scored between 35 and 45.

b. To find the percentage of students who scored between 37 and 40, we need to standardize these values and find the area under the standard normal curve between the resulting z-scores:

z1 = (37 - 35) / 15 = 0.1333

z2 = (40 - 35) / 15 = 0.3333

P(37 ≤ x ≤ 40) = P(0.1333 ≤ z ≤ 0.3333) = 0.1003

Therefore, approximately 10.03% of students scored between 37 and 40.

c. To find the percentage of students who scored less than 32, we need to standardize this value and find the area under the standard normal curve to the left of the resulting z-score:

z = (32 - 35) / 15 = -0.2

P(x < 32) = P(z < -0.2) = 0.4207

Therefore, approximately 42.07% of students scored less than 32.

d. To find the percentage of students who scored greater than 20, we need to standardize this value and find the area under the standard normal curve to the right of the resulting z-score:

z = (20 - 35) / 15 = -1

P(x > 20) = P(z > -1) = 0.8413

Therefore, approximately 84.13% of students scored greater than 20.

II.

a. The possible samples of size 2 that can be drawn from the population {12, 14, 16} are: {12, 14}, {12, 16}, {14, 12}, {14, 16}, {16, 12}, {16, 14}.

b. The mean of each sample is as follows:

{12, 14}: (12 + 14) / 2 = 13

{12, 16}: (12 + 16) / 2 = 14

{14, 12}: (14 + 12) / 2 = 13

{14, 16}: (14 + 16) / 2 = 15

{16, 12}: (16 + 12) / 2 = 14

{16, 14}: (16 + 14) / 2 = 15

c. The sampling distribution is a distribution of the sample means. In this case, the sample means are {13, 14, 13, 15, 14, 15}.

d. The mean of the sampling distribution is the same as the mean of the population:

μ = (12 + 14 + 16) / 3
