The theory of evolution was first proposed by Charles Darwin after he made a trip to the Galapagos Islands in the Pacific. He noticed that different species of animals have small morphological changes, even if they live in the same area. Another scientists who can be considered as one of the early proponents of the theory is Jean Baptiste Lamarck, who proposed that animals that could survive and adapt to the environment will share their genes to the next generation of species.
