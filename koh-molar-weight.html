10. 1. Calculate the molar concentration of a solution that contains 18g KOH in 220mL solution.​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute (KOH).

Note that the molar mass of KOH is 56.11 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{18 g}}{\text{56.11 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.3208 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the molarity of solution.

Note that 1 molar (M) = 1 mole per liter (mol/L).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molarity} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.3208 mol}}{\text{0.220 L}} \\ & = \text{1.46 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{1.46 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molar concentration of the solution is 1.46 M.

16. What volume of 0.250 M KOH solution contains 6.31 grams of KOH


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute (KOH)

The molar mass of KOH is 56.11 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{6.31 g}}{\text{56.11 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.1125 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solution.

1 molar (M) = 1 mole per liter (mol/L)

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of solution} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{molarity}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.1125 mol}}{0.250 \: M} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.1125 mol}}{\text{0.250 mol/L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.450 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of 0.250 M KOH solution is 0.450 L.

25. A sample has a specific volume of 6.5(m³) (kg-¹ ) and a molar volume of 990(m³) (kg mol-¹). Determine the molecular weight of the material. ​


MOLECULAR WEIGHT OF A MATERIAL

If a sample has a specific volume of 6.5 m³/kg and a molar volume of 990 m³-kg/mol, the molecular weight of the material is 0.00657 moles.

This problem can be solved using unit analysis. When we say unit analysis, we only have to look at the units and then decide on the arithmetic approach that we can use.

In our case here, we have to get moles as the unit so we only have to divide the specific volume by the molar volume to get the molecular weight.

Solving:

molecular weight = [tex]\frac{6.5 m^3/kg}{990 m^3-kg/mol}[/tex] = 0.00657 moles.
