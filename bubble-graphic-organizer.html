13. I. Graphic Organizer Directions: On the separate sheet of paper, copy the diagram. List the creation of marketing mix and give insights in the bubble map provided. Marketing Mix​


Answer:

I don't have access to physical paper to copy the diagram. However, I can explain the creation of marketing mix and provide insights in the bubble map.

The marketing mix is a set of tools and tactics that a company can use to promote its products or services to its target market. It is also known as the 4Ps of marketing, which include product, price, promotion, and place.

1. Product: This refers to the goods or services that a company offers to its customers. Companies need to ensure that their products meet the needs and wants of their target market.

2. Price: This refers to the amount that a customer is willing to pay for a product or service. Companies need to set prices that are competitive and provide value to their customers.

3. Promotion: This refers to the methods that a company uses to promote its products or services. This includes advertising, sales promotions, public relations, and personal selling.

4. Place: This refers to the channels that a company uses to distribute its products or services to its customers. Companies need to ensure that their products are available to their target market through the right channels.

The bubble map can provide insights into how these elements of the marketing mix are interconnected and how they can influence each other. For example, the product and price elements can influence each other, as a company may need to adjust the price of a product to make it more appealing to its target market. Similarly, the promotion and place elements can influence each other, as a company may need to use different channels to reach different segments of its target market. Overall, the marketing mix is an important tool for companies to use to create a cohesive and effective marketing strategy that meets the needs and wants of their target market.

17. what do you think is the relevance of studying entrepreneurship subject in senior higth school? Make a bubble map graphic organizer. ​


Answer:

The relevance of studying entrepreneurship as a subject in senior high school is to prepare students for careers in the modern workforce. Entrepreneurship is the process of starting and running a successful business, and the skills and knowledge gained through an entrepreneurship course can be applied to a wide range of career paths. Additionally, entrepreneurship classes can give students practical experience in business management, communication, finance, and other essential skills that will be useful in any job. By learning about entrepreneurship in high school, students can get a head start on their career and acquire valuable skills and knowledge that will serve them well throughout their professional lives.

Explanation:

To create a bubble map graphic organizer about the relevance of studying entrepreneurship as a subject in senior high school, I would start with a central topic at the center of the page, such as "entrepreneurship."

From there, I would extend branches with key subtopics related to entrepreneurship, such as "business model development," "marketing and sales," "financial management," "product development," "human resources management," and "innovation and technology."

At the end of each branch, I might add smaller subtopics, such as "understanding the target market," "developing a marketing strategy," "conducting a feasibility study," "writing a business plan," "developing a pricing strategy," "understanding financial statements," "hiring and managing employees," "integrating technology into business operations," and "identifying and utilizing innovation opportunities."

The bubble map would serve as a visual aid for students to organize and prioritize information about the subject, and would also serve as a template for creating presentations or other written assignments. This organizer would help students understand the key concepts and elements of entrepreneurship, and would provide a framework for learning and applying these concepts in the real world.
