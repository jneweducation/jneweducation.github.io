Set p into 150, you get 150/20 = 15/2 = 7.5 minutes.

If printer B can print x pages per minute, It will take p/x minutes to print p pages because

x (pages) : 1 (minute) =1:1/x for any x

then 1:1/x = p:p/x for any x and any p


Rate here is measured as amount of page) over time, so the rate (of speed) of printer A is 40/2 = 20 pages per minute,
and the rate of printer B is x pages per minute.

so, as the time decreases, the rate increases because it is the denominator.
as the number of pages increases, the rate increases too because it is the numerator.

23. When you printed your 30-- page research paper, youobserved that the printer A in the Internet shop finished printing in 2minutes. How long will it take printer A to print 150 pages? How longwill it take printer A to print p pages? If printer B can print x pages perminute, how long will it take to print p pages?​


Answer:

a.) 10 minutes

b.) p = 15t pages

c.) t = p/x minutes

Step-by-step explanation:

Printer A prints 30pages in 2 minutes; we can define printer A speed rate as 30pages/2minutes= 15pages in 1 minute

[tex]A_{speed} = \frac{30}{2} = 15\frac{pages}{minute} \\[/tex]

to find how long will it take to print 150pages:

[tex]A_{speed} = \frac{pages}{minute} =\frac{150pages}{t \ minute} = 15 \frac{pages}{minute} \\\frac{150 pages}{15 pages} =\frac{t \ minute}{1 minute} \\\\t = 10 minutes[/tex]

Solving for p pages:

[tex]A_{speed} = \frac{p \ pages}{t \ minute} =15\frac{pages}{minute} \\\\p = 15t[/tex]

Solving for Printer B:

[tex]B_{speed} = \frac{p \ pages}{t \ minutes} = x\frac{pages}{minute} \\\\t = \frac{p}{x} \ minutes[/tex]
