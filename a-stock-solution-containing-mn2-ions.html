8. A 8-liter sample of water contains 500 mg iron ions. Express the concentration of the solution in pbb.


Answer:

The concentration of the solution can be expressed in parts per billion (ppb) by using the formula:

ppb = (mass of solute) / (volume of solution) * (10^9)

Where the mass of solute is 500 mg and the volume of solution is 8 liters. Converting liters to milliliters:

8 liters = 8000 milliliters

So, the concentration of the solution in ppb is:

ppb = (500 mg) / (8000 milliliters) * (10^9)

ppb = 500 / 8000 * 10^9

ppb = 62,500 ppb

Therefore, the concentration of the solution is 62,500 ppb.

14. does an acidic solution contain hydronium ions


Answer:

As mentioned, a neutral aqueous solution will have equal amounts of hydronium ions (H3O+) and hydroxide ions (OH-). At a temperature of 25°C, this amount is 1.0 x 10-7 moles H3O+ per liter os solution (abberviated as 1.0 x 10-7 M). An acidic solution will have a hydronium ion (H3O+) concentration that is greater than 1.0 x 10-7 M. And a basic solution will have a hydronium ion (H3O+) concentration that is less than 1.0 x 10-7 M. You should know that a number like  1.0 x 10-3 M is greater than 1.0 x 10-7 M; and a a number like  1.0 x 10-10 M is less than 1.0 x 10-7 M.

18. Calculate the normality of phosphoric acid, H3PO4, solution containing 2.50 g of phosphoric acid in 135 mL of solution in reactions that replace all three hydrogen ions.


Solution:

Note: H₃PO₄ is the solute and water is the solvent.

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 3) + (30.97 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 97.99 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the number of replaceable ions of H₃PO₄ (solute).

Since the given solute is an acid, the number of replaceable ions is equal to the number of [tex]H^{+}[/tex] ions. H₃PO₄ has 3 replaceable [tex]H^{+}[/tex] ions.

Therefore,

no. of replaceable ions = 3 eq/mol

Step 3: Calculate the equivalent weight.

[tex]\text{equivalent weight} = \frac{\text{molar mass}}{\text{no. of replaceable ions}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalent weight} = \frac{\text{97.99 g/mol}}{\text{3 eq/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalent weight = 32.663 g/eq}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the no. of equivalents of solute.

[tex]\text{equivalents of solute} = \frac{\text{mass}_{solute}}{\text{equivalent weight}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalents of solute} = \frac{\text{2.50 g}}{\text{32.663 g/eq}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalents of solute = 0.0765 eq}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the normality of the solution.

[tex]\text{normality} = \frac{\text{equivalents of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{normality} = \frac{\text{0.0765 eq}}{\text{0.135 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{normality = 0.567 N}}[/tex]

29. 11. A 1-liter solution contains 10 to the power of –8 moles of hydrogen ions. Determine whether the solution is acidic, neutral, or basic.


pH SCALE

Problem:

A 1-liter solution contains 10 to the power of –8 moles of hydrogen ions. Determine whether the solution is acidic, neutral, or basic.

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

To determine whether the solution is acidic, basic or neutral, we need to find out its pH scale. pH stands for power of Hydrogen. To solve for the value, we need to get first the ratio of hydrogen ions for every 1 liter of solution, so we have 10 raised to –8 hydrogen ions per liter. With this, we can substitute this to the formula:

pH = –Log(H+)

pH = –Log(1x10^–8)

pH = 8

So the pH of the solution is 8. When the pH of the solution is less than 7, the solution is acidic. When the pH of the solution is greater than 7, the solution is basic. When the pH of the solution is equal to 7, the solution is neutral. Most solutions have pH that range from 0 to 14.

Since 8 is greater than 7, therefore the solution can be classified as a base.
