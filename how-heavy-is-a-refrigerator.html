Answer:

Given :

F = 50N

a = 5m/s^2

m = ?

Formula :

F = ma

Solution with units :

F =ma (isolate "m")

m = F/a

m = (50 N) / (5m/s^2)

m = 10 kg.

11. How does the refrigerator functions?​


Answer:

Refrigerators work by causing the refrigerant circulating inside them to change from a liquid into a gas. This process, called evaporation, cools the surrounding area and produces the desired effect. When you release the contents into the lower pressure open space, it turns from a liquid to a gas.

How a refrigerator system works

Refrigerators work by causing the refrigerant circulating inside them to change from a liquid into a gas. This process, called evaporation, cools the surrounding area and produces the desired effect. You can test this process for yourself by taking some alcohol and putting a drop or two on your skin. As it evaporates, you should feel a chilling sensation - the same basic principle gives us safe food storage. To start the evaporation process and change the refrigerant from liquid to gas, the pressure on the refrigerant needs to be reduced through an outlet called the capillary tube. The effect is similar to what happens when you use an aerosol product such as hair spray. The contents of an aerosol can is the pressure/liquid side, the outlet is the capillary tube, and the open space is the evaporator. When you release the contents into the lower pressure open space, it turns from a liquid to a gas.To keep a refrigerator running, you need to be able to get the gaseous refrigerant back to its liquid state, so the gas needs to be compressed to a higher pressure and temperature again. This is where the compressor comes in. As mentioned earlier, the compressor provides a similar effect to that of a bike pump. You can sense the heat increase in the pump while you pump and compress the air.Once the compressor has done its work, the gas should be under high pressure and hot. It needs to be cooled in the condenser, which is mounted on the back of the refrigerator, so its contents can be cooled by the ambient air. When the gas cools off inside the condenser (still under high pressure), it changes back into a liquid.Then, the liquid refrigerant circulates back to the evaporator where the process starts all over again.

12. how refrigerator works?


Answer:

A refrigerator is a mechanical machine that that makes or keep things cold. And it can keep a food for a long time because it helps it avoid from spoiling.

How does a refrigerator works?

Every fridge has a compressor that can be found usually at the bottom. It compresses the what we called the refrigerant vapor, and raises the inside pressure then pushes it up until it will reach the refrigerators' coil that is located outside and the back of the fridge.

When the heat that was pumped out from inside that is now in the coils meets the cold air from the outside (the surroundings), it evaporates and becomes a liquid. (the liquid or small amount of water that is being kept in its water reservoir.)

With the high pressure a refrigerator uses, there are a lot of liquid under- as the refrigerant cools down and run to the coils now making it cold because it cools down every heat it can absorb from the inside.  

This will be now a cycle since the cooled down refrigerant becomes an air through evaporation, use it for cooling and then the pressure happens again. Definition of Terms

Refrigerant- a cooling substance of a refrigerator.

Water Reservoir /Tank - The plastic tank or tube located at the lower back that is to keep the refrigerator liquids made from the compressed refrigerant.
