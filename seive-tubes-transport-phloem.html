Xylem2. Phloem3. Diffusion4. Cell transport5. Circulation6. Arteries7. Veins8. Valves9. Systemic Circulation10. Pulmonary Circulation​


Answer:

1.Xylem-the vascular tissue in plants that conducts water and dissolved nutrients upward from the root and also helps to form the woody element in the stem.

2.Phloem-the vascular tissue in plants that conducts sugars and other metabolic products downward from the leaves.

3.Diffusion-is the net movement of anything from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration.

4.Cell Transport-refers to the movement of substances across the cell membrane.

5.Circulation-movement to and fro or around something,especially that of fluid in a closed system.

6.Arteries-the arteries are the blood vessels that deliver oxygen-rich blood from the heart to the tissues of the body.

7.Veins-veins are vessels of the circulatory system that support circulation by conveying blood to the heart.

8.Valves-a device for controlling the passage of fliud or air through a pipe,duct,etc,especially an automatic device allowing movement in one direction only.

9.Systemic Circulation-provides the functional blood supply to all body tissue.

10.Pulmonary Circulation-is the portion of the circulatory system which carries deoxygenated blood away from the right ventricle,to the lungs,and returns oxygenated blood to the left atrium and ventricle of the heart.
