The Indent commands will adjust the indent by 1/2-inch increments.

11. An open rectangular gutter is to be made by turning up the sides of a peice of metal 18 inches wide. If the cross-section area of the resulting gutter is 36 square inches, find the gutter's depth.


The gutter's depth is 2 inches.

The solution for the problem here:

The sides of a piece of metal: are 18 inches wide.

The cross-section area of the resulting gutter is 36 square inches.

The gutter shape is rectangular.

So, to find the depth:

area =  (A) = Length(L) × Breadth(B)

⇒ 36 = 18 x depth

⇒ depth = 36 ÷ 18

⇒ depth = 2 inches

Hence, the depth of the rectangular is 2 inches.

RECTANGULAR

A rectangle is a closed, flat, four-sided shape with each angle equal to 90 degrees. The opposing sides of the rectangle are parallel and equal in length.

A rectangle is a closed, two-dimensional geometry with four sides, four corners, and four right angles (90°). Contrary sides of a rectangle are parallel and equal. Examples of rectangles in the real world include books, doors, tabletops, blackboards, etc.
