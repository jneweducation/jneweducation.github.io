Definition of Even and Odd Numbers

Even and odd numbers are types of integers studied in basic mathematics, which have very significant differences so that we can easily distinguish between even and odd numbers.

An even number is a number that is divisible by 2. Examples of even numbers include 2,4,6,8, and so on. While odd numbers are numbers that are not divisible by two or the number that remains when divided by two. Examples of odd numbers include 1,3,5,7, 11, 13, and so on.

The two types of numbers in mathematics have special characteristics that make it easier for us to identify the types of numbers.

We can distinguish between these two numbers by knowing the characteristics possessed by a number.

The characteristic of an odd number is that it has a unit digit of 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9. The characteristic of an even number is that it has a unit digit of 2, 4, 6, 8, or 0.
