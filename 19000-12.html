7. 1. Paul makes a base monthly salary of $1500. As a vendor, he must sell $19000 worth of items per month. He also makes a 6% commission on all sales beyond the monthly quota. If Paul sold $26600 worth of items this month, what is his total salary for the month including base salary and commission to the nearest dollar? Calculate a) Amount past quota sold b) Amount made from commission c) Total (gross) monthly salary


Sales commission calculation.

a) Amount past quota sold:

The monthly quota that Paul must sell is $19000. If he sold $26600 worth of items this month, then the amount he sold beyond the monthly quota is:

$26600 - $19000 = $7600

b) Amount made from commission:

Paul's commission rate for sales beyond the monthly quota is 6%. Therefore, the amount he made from commission is:

0.06 * $7600 = $456

c) Total (gross) monthly salary:

To calculate Paul's total (gross) monthly salary, we need to add his base salary to the amount he made from commission. Therefore, his total salary for the month is:

$1500 + $456 = $1956

Therefore, Paul's total (gross) monthly salary including base salary and commission is $1956 to the nearest dollar.

Answer:

a.) Amount past quota sold

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the amount past quota sold, we need to subtract the monthly quota from the total sales for the month:

$26600 - $19000 = $7600

So Paul sold $7600 worth of items past the monthly quota.

b) Amount made from commission:

Paul earns a 6% commission on all sales beyond the monthly quota. So, he earns a commission on the $7600 worth of items he sold past the monthly quota.

Commission = 6% of $7600 = 0.06 x $7600 = $456

Therefore, Paul made $456 from commission.

c) Total (gross) monthly salary:

To find Paul's total monthly salary, we need to add his base salary to the commission he made:

Total salary = base salary + commission

Total salary = $1500 + $456

Total salary = $1956

Therefore, Paul's total salary for the month, including base salary and commission, is $1956 to the nearest dollar.
