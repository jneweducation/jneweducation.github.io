Constant speed refers to a type of motion where an object moves at a constant rate and does not change its velocity. In other words, the object's speed remains the same and is not accelerating or decelerating. This means that the distance traveled and the time taken to travel that distance are directly proportional to each other, and that the object travels the same distance in equal time intervals.

Constant speed is an example of uniform motion, which is motion that occurs at a steady pace and does not change velocity. This is in contrast to non-uniform motion, which occurs when an object changes its velocity, such as when it speeds up or slows down.

Constant speed can be measured in different units such as meters per second (m/s), kilometers per hour (km/h) or miles per hour (mph). An example of an object that is moving at a constant speed is a car traveling down a highway. The car's speedometer shows that it is traveling at a specific speed, and as long as the car is not accelerating or decelerating, it is moving at a constant speed.

In physics, Constant speed is a key concept in kinematics, which is the study of motion. It is also related to other concepts such as distance, displacement, velocity, and acceleration, and is used to calculate these quantities.
