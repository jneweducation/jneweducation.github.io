3. The lap joint shown in the figure is fastened by four ¾-in.-diameter rivets. Calculate the maximum safe load P that can be applied if the shearing stress in the rivets is limited to 14 ksi and the bearing stress in the plates is limited to 18 ksi. Assume the applied load is uniformly distributed among the four rivets.


Answer:

The minimum value of the load between the shearing and bearing stress is the maximum safe load.

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to solve for the applied force or load using the concept of shearing and bearing stress.

Formula to use:

a. Shearing stress

   τ = P/A

where

τ     is the shearing stress, the unit used is in ksi (kilopound per square inch)

P    is the load or applied force, the unit is in kips (1000 pounds-force)

A    is the cross-sectional area of the member, the unit used in²

b. Bearing stress

   σb = Pb/Ab

where

σb     is the bearing stress, the unit used is in ksi (kilopound per square

         inch)

Pb     is the compressive load,  the unit is in kips (1000 pounds-force)

Ab    is the cross-sectional area, the unit used in²

Given information

d = 3/4 in

τ = 14 ksi

σb = 18 ksi

Pmax = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve for the P load based on the shearing stress of the 4 rivets. Let us use the formula for shearing stress.

τ = P/A

Cross-multiply

P = τ A

P = 14 ksi [4(π/4)(3/4)²]

Take note that the area here is the area of the rivets which is a circle. So, taking the area of a circle is A = πd²/4 and we multiply this with 4 because there are 4 rivets to consider.

So, the load for the shearing stress of rivets is:

P = 24.74 kips

b. Now, let us solve the P load for the bearing stress of the plates. Let us use the 2nd given formula.

σb = Pb/Ab

Cross-multiply

Pb = σb · Ab

Then substitute the given data

Pb = 18 ksi [4(3/4)(x)]

Here, let x be the thickness of the plate to completely solve for the load on the plates.

After substituting the value of x, compare the load solved on each stress. The minimum value of the load is the maximum safe load.

19. A steel bar is 10 mm diameter and 2 m long. It is stretched with a force of 20 kN and extends by 0.2 mm. Calculate the stress and strain.


Answer:

a. The stress is 2.55 x 10⁷ Pa

b. The strain is 1 x 10 ⁻⁴

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the stress and strain of a steel bar. To do this we will needing the following formula:

a. σ = F/A

where

σ     is the stress, unit is in N/m² or Pascal, Pa

F     is the force, unit is in Newton, N

A     is the cross-sectional area, unit is in m²

b. ε = ΔL/L₀

where

ε     is the strain, unitless

ΔL   is the change in length

L₀    is the original length

For the given information

d = 10 mm = 0.01 m

L₀ = 2 m

F = 20 kN = 20 x 10³ N

ΔL = 0.2 mm = 0.0002 m

Solving the problem

a. First, let us solve for the cross-sectional area of the steel bar.

A = πd²/4

A = π (0.01 m)²/4 = 7.85 x 10⁻⁵ m²

Now, let us solve for stress.

σ = F/A

σ = 20 x 10³ N/ 7.85 x 10⁻⁵ m²

σ = 2.55 x 10⁷ Pa

b. Now, let solve for the strain of the steel bar using the 2nd given formula.

ε = ΔL/L₀

ε = 0.0002 m / 2 m

ε = 0.0001 or

ε = 1 x 10 ⁻⁴

Therefore, the stress and the strain of the steel bar are 2.55 x 10⁷ Pa and   1 x 10 ⁻⁴ respectively.

30. A helical spring is made by wrapping steel wire 20 mm in diameter around forming a cylinder 150 mm in diameter. compute the number of turns required to permit an elongation of 100 mm without exceeding a shearing stress of 140 mpa. use g = 83 gpa.


Note: All units were converted to the needed units.

Given:

diameter of steel wire = 20 mm --> 0.787 in

diameter of cylinder = 150 mm

elongation = 100 mm --> 3.937 in

shearing stress = 140 mpa --> 20.32 ksi

g = 83 gpa

Formula to be use for Hellical Springs

Tmax= 16PR/πd³{1+(d/4r)}

20.31ksi= 16P(3.937in)/π(.787)³ {1+(.787)/4(3.937)}

P= .47 lb

Next we will use elongation formula to find the n turns.

Elongation= 64Pr³n/Gd⁴

3.937= 64(.47)(3.937)³(n)/(12.04×10^6)(.787)⁴

N= 9906 turns

therefore it needs 9906 turns to permit an elongation of 100 mm without exceeding a 140 MPa shear stress.
