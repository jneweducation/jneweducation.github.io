A disk whose mass (M) is 10 kg with a radius (R) of 1.3 m is spinning at an angular speed (ω) of 17 rad/sec.

We need to find the Moment of inertia (I) of the solid disk and also we need to calculate the rotational kinetic energy (K.E) of the disk.

[tex]\dag\:\underline{\tt Moment \: of \: Inertia :}[/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow\:\: I = \sf \dfrac{MR^2}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow\:\: I = \sf \dfrac{10 \times {(1.3)}^{2} }{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow\:\: I = \sf \dfrac{10 \times 1.69}{2}[/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow\:\: I = \sf \dfrac{16.9}{2}[/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow\:\: I = \sf \dfrac{16.9}{2}[/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow\:\: \underline{ \boxed{ I = \sf 8.45 \: Kg.m^2}} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\dag\:\underline{\tt Rotational \: Kinetic\: Energy :} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\dashrightarrow\:\:\sf K.E = \dfrac{1}{2} \times \rm I \times \sf \omega^2 \\ [/tex]

[tex]\dashrightarrow\:\:\sf K.E = \dfrac{1}{2} \times 8.45 \times {(17)}^{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\dashrightarrow\:\:\sf K.E = \dfrac{1}{2} \times 8.45 \times 289 \\ [/tex]

13. Two small weights of mass 5.0 kg and 7.0 kg, are mounted 4.0 m apart on a light rod (whose mass can be ignored), as shown in the accompanying figure. Calculate the moment of inertia of the system (a) when rotated about an axis halfway between the weights, and (b) when rotated about an axis 0.50 m to the left of the 5.0 kg mass.


a. I = 48 kg.m²

b. I = 87 kg.m²

Further explanation

The moment of inertia is a measure of the inertia of an object to rotate on its axis.  

Factors affecting moment of inertia:  

The mass of objects  form  rotary axis  moment arm  

Can be formulated  :

[tex]\tt I=m\times R^2[/tex]

m is the mass of particle/object (kg)  

R is the distance between the particle or mass of the object against the rotary axis (m)

I = ∑mₙRₙ²

The moment of inertia of the object is the sum of all the moments of inertia of the object's particles :

Given

mass 5.0 kg and 7.0 kg

4.0 m apart

Required

the moment of inertia

Solution

a. R₁=R₂=2 m( halfway between the weights)

I = (m₁+m₂)R²

I = (5+7)2²

I = 48 kg.m²

b. 0.50 m to the left of the 5.0 kg mass.

R₁(5 kg) = 0.5 m

R₂(7 kg) = 4 - 0.5 = 3.5 m

I = 5 x 0.5² + 7 x 3.5²

I = 1.25 + 85.75

I = 87 kg.m²

17. 1. A 3 m uniform rod with a mass of 1.5 kg acts as a physical pendulum. Calculate on what is the inertia of the rod about its axis of rotation and the period of the pendulum.​


Answer:
4.5 kg m^2
3.54 s.

Explanation:
I = (1/3) * m * L^2
I = (1/3) * 1.5 kg * (3 m)^2 = 4.5 kg m^2

T = 2 * pi * sqrt(I/m * g)

T = 2 * pi * sqrt(4.5 kg m^2 / 1.5 kg * 9.8 m/s^2)

= 2 * pi * sqrt(3)

= 3.54 s
