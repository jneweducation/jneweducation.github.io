1. How many gallons of paint are required to paint the walls of a building 33 ft. long, 31 ft. wide, and 47 ft. high, if 1 gallon of paint will cover 500 ft² of the wall?


Answer:

12.032 gallons of paint are required to paint the walls of the building.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Write the given

Length of the room= 33 ft Width of the room= 31 ftHeight of the room= 47 ft

Step 2: Find the surface area of the wall

The building is considered as rectangular prism, which has six faces. The area of the six faces are as follows: two 31 ft by 33 ft "rooftop" and base, and two 33 ft by 47 ft and two 31 ft by 47 ft walls. [tex]surface area of wall= (2)(33 ft)(47 ft) +(2)(31 ft)(47 ft)[/tex][tex]surface area of wall=6016 ft^{2}[/tex]

Step 3: Find the number of gallons necessary to paint the walls.

Use the relationship: 1 gallon of paint covers 500 square ft of wall[tex]Gallons of paint=surface area of wall*(\frac{gallon of paint}{surface area of wall})[/tex][tex]Gallons of paint=(6016ft^{2} )*(\frac{1 gallon}{500ft^{2} })[/tex][tex]Gallons of paint=12.032 gallons[/tex]

Therefore, 12.032 gallons of paint will be needed to paint the walls of the building.

Next, we need to determine the width of the wall. From the diagram, we can count that there are 11 blocks in each column and 2 columns. Therefore, the total width is:

Width = 11 cubes/column x 2 columns = 22 cubes

Since each cube has a side length of 1/8 ft, the width of the wall is:

Width = 22 cubes x 1/8 ft/cube = 2.75 ft

Finally, we need to determine the height of the wall. From the diagram, we can count that there are 5 layers of cubes. Therefore, the height of the wall is:

Height = 5 layers x 1/8 ft/cube x 9 cubes/layer = 5.625 ft

Explanation:

1. Canvas

Canvas is commonly used as a painting surface and offers many advantages: it’s absorbent, has a wonderful fabric texture, is lightweight and portable.

2. Paper and Cardboard

Paper and cardboard are great support choices if you are a beginner or just want to experiment. Both are economical and easy to find.

3. Wood and Composite Panels

Wood is a great support for paintings, especially for thick applications of paint and other techniques that require a rigid, sturdy support.  

4. Patterned Fabric

I love browsing in fabric stores to get ideas for colors, patterns and textures. Sometimes I buy small pieces of fabric just to hang around my studio for inspiration.

6. Metal

The two issues of concern for preparing a metal support are adhesion and rust control. This technique works best for ferrous metals like steel and will provide a long-lasting rustproof support for indoor or outdoor use, suitable for coating with acrylic paint.

7. Glass

One reason to paint on glass is to take advantage of its clarity. The main concern with painting on glass is adhesion. Etching or sandblasting the surface will add tooth. Both methods will make the glass slightly cloudy, so etch only in the areas where paint will be applied.

8. Objects

Painting on 3-D sculptures or objects encourages you to think differently because the object needs to be considered from all angles. You can make your own ceramic sculpture, have a metal shop cut shapes according to your template, or just use an interesting shell, branch or rock.
