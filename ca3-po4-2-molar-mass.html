Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Ca₃(PO₄)₂.

molar mass = (40.08 g/mol × 3) + (30.97 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 8)

molar mass = 310.18 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass percent of P in Ca₃(PO₄)₂.

[tex]\text{mass percent of P} = \frac{\text{30.97 g/mol × 2}}{\text{310.18 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass percent of P = 19.97\%}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of P in Ca₃(PO₄)₂.

[tex]\text{mass of P} = \frac{\text{mass percent of P}}{100} \: × \:\text{mass of Ca₃(PO₄)₂}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of P} = \frac{19.97}{100} \: × \:\text{8.95 g}[/tex]

7. Calculate the boiling point of the solution that contains 0.12 moles of Calcium phosphate (electrolyte) (Ca3(PO4)2 dissolved in 575 g of water.​


Given:

moles of solute = 0.12 mol

mass of solvent = 575 g = 0.575 kg

solute: Ca₃(PO₄)₂

solvent: H₂O

Required:

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molality.

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{0.12 mol}}{\text{0.575 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]m = 0.5342\: m[/tex]

Step 2: Determine the van't Hoff factor.

Since Ca₃(PO₄)₂ is an electrolyte, we will assume that Ca₃(PO₄)₂ is dissociated completely into three calcium ions and two phosphate ions. Therefore, there are five ions in a formula unit of Ca₃(PO₄)₂. This means that,

[tex]i = 5[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the boiling point elevation.

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = iK_{\text{b}}m[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = (5)(0.208696 \: m)(0.512^{\circ}\text{C/}m)[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = 0.534^{\circ}\text{C}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the boiling point of solution.

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = \text{BP}_{solvent} + \Delta T_{\text{b}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = 100^{\circ}\text{C} + 0.534^{\circ}\text{C}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{BP}_{solution} = 100.534^{\circ}\text{C}}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = 100.534^{\circ}\text{C}[/tex]

22. The solubility of Ca3(PO4)2 is 0.03 g/liter. Calculate its solubility product constant, ksp.


Solubility Product ConstantDiscussion:

The solubility product constant of a compound is the product of the molar concentrations of the constituent ions, each raised to the power of its stoichiometric coefficient in the equilibrium equation.

Consider a saturated solution of [tex]\text{A}_{x}\text{B}_{y}[/tex] that is in contact with a solid [tex]\text{A}_{x}\text{B}_{y}[/tex]. The solubility equilibrium can be represented as

[tex]\text{A}_{x}\text{B}_{y}(s) \rightleftharpoons x\text{A}^{y+}(aq) + y\text{B}^{x-}(aq)[/tex]

The solubility product constant [tex]K_{\text{sp}}[/tex] for this reaction is

[tex]\boxed{K_{\text{sp}} = [\text{A}^{y+}]^{x}[\text{B}^{x-}]^{y}}[/tex]

There are two ways to express a substance's solubility: molar solubility, which is the number of moles of solute in 1 L of a saturated solution (mol/L), and solubility, which is the number of grams of solute in 1 L of a saturated solution (g/L).

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

The solubility of Ca3(PO4)2 is 0.03 g/liter. Calculate its solubility product constant, ksp.

Given:

[tex]\text{solubility of} \: \text{Ca}_{3}(\text{PO}_{4})_{2} = \text{0.03 g/L}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]K_{\text{ksp}}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Ca₃(PO₄)₂.

molar mass = (40.08 g/mol × 3) + (30.97 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 8)

molar mass = 310.18 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the molar solubility of Ca₃(PO₄)₂.

[tex]s = 0.03 \: \frac{\text{g}}{\text{L}} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{310.18 g}}[/tex]

[tex]s = 9.6718 × 10^{-5} \: \text{mol/L}[/tex]

Step 3: Construct ICE table.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\left[\begin{array}{cccccccc} & \text{Ca}_3(\text{PO}_4)_2 & \rightleftharpoons & 3\text{Ca}^{2+} &+& 2\text{PO}_4^{3-}\\\text{initial} & & & 0 && 0\\\text{change} & -s & & +3s && +2s\\\text{equilibrium} & & & 3s && 2s\\\end{array}\right]\end{gathered}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the equilibrium concentration of constituent ions.

[tex][\text{Ca}^{2+}] = 3s = 3(9.6718 × 10^{-5} \: \text{mol/L}) = 2.9015 × 10^{-4} \: M[/tex]

[tex][\text{PO}_4^{3-}] = 2s = 2(9.6718 × 10^{-5} \: \text{mol/L}) = 1.9344 × 10^{-4} \: M[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the solubility product constant.

[tex]K_{\text{sp}} = [\text{Ca}^{2+}]^3[\text{PO}_4^{3-}]^2[/tex]

[tex]K_{\text{sp}} = (2.9015 × 10^{-4})^3(1.9344 × 10^{-4})^2[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{K_{\text{sp}} = 9.1 × 10^{-19}}[/tex]
