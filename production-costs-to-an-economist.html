3. Economists use gross national product (GNP) to measure


Economists use gross national product (GNP) to measure

• To measure the total economic activity of the nation

• The nations development

• The economist will have an idea of the economic standing of the country.

What is Gross National Product?

• This is the total market value of final goods and services produced by the residents of the country for a given period.

• The GNP is expressed in terms of market value of goods and services, that is, in terms of the prices which consumers pay to acquire or avail of goods and services.

• The only final products and services are included

• The Gross National Product formula calculates the income earned by the citizens, companies domestic and the companies abroad.  

• It is the accumulated values of all finished goods and services produced by the country annually.    

What are the factors that are excluded from Gross National Product?

1. Intermediate goods- those which are produced during the same period but used in the production of other goods.

Example:

For instance, if we were to include in our Gross National Product (GNP) the value of crocodile skin, the hide, the leather and finally the shoes made out of leather, we in effect will be counting the value of crocodile skin four times when it should be counted only once.

• Final products are made available either for direct consumption or for increase in the capital stock whereas intermediate goods are utilized in the production of final commodities.

GNP is measured by adding the expenditures for final products valued at current market prices incurred by four sectors namely:

1. Household sectors

• This includes individuals in their capacity as consumers and non profit institutions serving the household such as churches and charitable institutions.

2. Business and enterprise sectors

• This is composed of sole proprietorship, corporations , partnerships, non profit institutions serving business, independent practitioners and government enterprises engage in business.

3. Government sector  

• The national, city and municipal levels of government

4. Rest of the world sector

• Residents of foreign countries

27. Suppose you are an economist and you are hired by the government to help analyze the cost of an environmental cleanup. The cost in pesos of the cleanup is given by 30,000 ()= 1− , where represents the percentage of the area to be cleaned up (0 ≤ < 1). The government wants to know the cost of cleaning up 50% of an affected area and the cost of cleaning up 75% of the area.


Answer:

The cost of cleaning up 50% of the affected area is 30,000 pesos and 90,000 pesos for 75% of the affected area.

Step-by-step explanation:

Problem:

Suppose you are an economist and you are hired by the government to help analyze the cost of an environmental cleanup. The cost in pesos of the cleanup is given by C(p) = 30,000p/1−p where p represents the percentage of the area to be cleaned up (0 ≤ p < 1).

The government wants to know the cost of cleaning up 50% of an affected area and the cost of cleaning up 75% of the area.

Given:

Cost of clean-up: C(p) = 30,000p/1-p

Where:

0 ≤ p < 1

Cost of cleaning up 50% of affected area:

C(p) = 30,000p/1−p

p = 0.50

C(0.50) = [30,000(0.50)]/(1-0.50)

C(0.50) = [15,000]/(0.50)

C(0.50) = 30,000

The cost of cleaning up 50% of the affected area is 30,000 pesos.

Cost of cleaning up 75% of the affect area:

C(p) = 30,000p/1−p

p = 0.75

C(0.75) = [30,000(0.75)]/(1-0.75)

C(0.75) = [22,500]/(0.25)

C(0.75) = 90,000

The cost of cleaning up 75% of the affected area is 90,000 pesos.
