Answer:

1.The surface a solid object stands on, or the bottom line of a shape such as a triangle or rectangle.

2.The major types of solid shapes are: cubes, cuboids, prisms, pyramids, platonic solids, torus, cone, cylinder, and sphere.

3.The lateral area of any figure is the area of the non-base faces only. Lateral area formulas help in calculating the lateral surface area of different figures including cuboid, cube, cylinder, cone, and sphere. Every 3-dimensional object has a lateral area that can be touched.

4.In geometry, a base is a side of a polygon or a face of a polyhedron, particularly one oriented perpendicular to the direction in which height is measured, or on what is considered to be the "bottom" of the figure.

5.The total surface area of a solid is the sum of the areas of all of the faces or surfaces that enclose the solid. The faces include the tops and bottoms (bases) and the remaining surfaces. The lateral surface area of a solid is the surface area of the solid without the bases.
