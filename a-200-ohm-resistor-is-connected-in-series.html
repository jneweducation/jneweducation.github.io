Answer:

The resistance of the parallel component is

[tex]\dfrac{30R}{R+30}[/tex]

If [tex]R'[/tex] is the equivalent resistance of the circuit,

[tex]R'=\dfrac{1}{\dfrac{1}{30}+\dfrac{1}{R}  } +6=\dfrac{36(R+5)}{R+30}[/tex]

The current through the [tex]6\:\Omega[/tex] resistor is

[tex]I=\dfrac{120}{R'} =\dfrac{10R+300}{3R+15}[/tex]

The voltage drop across [tex]R[/tex] is

[tex]\frac{\left(\dfrac{30R}{R+30} \right)}{\left(\dfrac{36(R+5)}{R+30} \right)} (120)=\dfrac{100R}{R+5}[/tex]

So, if the power dissipation is the same,

[tex]P=I^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R} \\\\\left(\dfrac{10R+300}{3R+15} \right)^2(6)=\dfrac{\left(\dfrac{100R}{R+5} \right)^2}{R}\\\\R=11.5\:\Omega[/tex]

11. What is the total resistance of a circuit with three 100 resistors in parallel and two 200 resistors in series? Select one: a. 150 Ohms b. 100 Ohms c. 75 Ohms d. 50 Ohms​


b.100

To find the total resistance, we need to first calculate the equivalent resistance of the resistors in parallel and series.

Resistors in parallel:
1/Req = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3
1/Req = 1/100 + 1/100 + 1/100
1/Req = 3/100
Req = 100/3

Resistors in series:
Rtotal = R1 + R2
Rtotal = 200 + 200
Rtotal = 400

Now we can find the total resistance:
Rtotal = Req + Rtotal
Rtotal = 100/3 + 400
Rtotal = 533.33 Ohms

Therefore, the total resistance of the circuit is approximately 533.33 Ohms.

20. two 30 ohm resistor are connected in series. when a resistor R is connected across one of them the total curcuit resistance is 40 ohms. Calculate the ohmic value of R​


Refer to attached image,

[tex]\large\overbrace{\underbrace{\text{$>>>$ see attached picture $<<<$}}}[/tex]

As seen in Figure 1, Initially,

Total Resistance = R1 + R2

Total Resistance = 30 + 30

Total Resistance = 60 Ω

Now refer to Figure 2,

When a resistor R is connected across one of them say to R1,

then the New Total Resistance became 40 Ohms,

So,

New Total Resistance = (R1 // R) + R2

where

New Total Resistance = 40 Ω

R1 // R = total resistance when R1 is connected in parallel to R

solve for R1 // R,

New Total Resistance = (R1 // R) + R2

40 = (R1 // R) + 30

R1 // R = 40 - 30

R1 // R = 10 Ω

Now we solve for R, using parallel resistance formula

[tex]\sf \frac{1}{R_1//R} =\frac{1}{R_1} +\frac{1}{R} \\\\\sf \frac{1}{10} =\frac{1}{30} +\frac{1}{R} \\\\\text{\sf solving for R,}\\\\R = 15 \;ohms[/tex]

[tex]\therefore \large\boxed{\text{\sf The value of R = 15 ohms}}[/tex]
