1. Enumerate and explain the rules of rendering a hand salute. ​


Answer:

Bring your hand up to the correct position in one, smart motion without any preparatory movement. When dropping the salute, bring your hand directly down to its natural position at your side, without slapping your leg or moving your hand out to the side. Any flourish in the salute is improper.

11. architectural image and animation rendering available products/services in the market related to technical drafting business​


Answer:

There are several products and services available in the market related to architectural image and animation rendering for technical drafting businesses:

3D modeling software: AutoCAD, SketchUp, 3D Studio Max, Blender, and Rhino are some of the most popular 3D modeling software in the market. These software allow users to create 3D models of buildings, interiors, and exteriors.

Rendering software: V-Ray, Lumion, Corona Renderer, and Enscape are some of the popular rendering software in the market. These software are used to produce high-quality images and animations of architectural designs.

Virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) tools: VR and AR technologies allow users to experience the architectural designs in a virtual environment. Tools such as Unity, Unreal Engine, and A-Frame are popular for creating VR and AR experiences.

3D Printing Services: 3D printing services provide a way to physically produce models of architectural designs. Companies such as Shapeways, Sculpteo, and i.Materialize offer 3D printing services for architectural models.

Online Marketplace: Online marketplaces such as Upwork, Fiverr, and Freelancer offer access to a pool of freelance professionals who specialize in architectural rendering and animation.

These products and services can be customized and combined to meet the specific needs and requirements of technical drafting businesses.

22. why the arbitral ruling was rendered in favor of the philippinesGive atleast 3 reasons. Guys pahelp plss​


Answer:

Metro Manila (CNN Philippines) — After seven months of deliberation, an international panel of judges unanimously ruled in favor of the Philippines in a 3-year-old case against China's claims to virtually all of the South China Sea.

The Arbitral Tribunal at the Permanent Court of Arbitration in The Hague released a 479 page “Award,” or ruling, that shot down China's claim that it has historically exercised exclusive control over the waters within its "nine-dash line" boundary. The five-member panel also found that China had caused "severe harm" to the marine environment because of its land reclamations.

The Tribunal's “final and binding” decision was based on documents from the Philippines and China, records of the United Nations, and jurisprudence on international maritime cases.

The judgment revolves around United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea, or UNCLOS — an international treaty that sets the limits of countries' territorial waters and guidelines for the use of marine resources. The Philippine and China are signatories to the treaty.

Among other things, UNCLOS provides countries with a 200 nautical mile-Exclusive Economic Zone measured from their coasts. Countries have the sole right to fish, mine, drill or use other resources within their respective zones.
