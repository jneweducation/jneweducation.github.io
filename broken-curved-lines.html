Basically, if you have a line, and that line forms the hypoteneuse of a right triangle, then doubling one dimension of that triangle will double the other dimensions, including the hypoteneuse, so long as the angle is the same. Halfing does the same thing—half one side and you halve all three sides, again, so long as the angle is the same.
