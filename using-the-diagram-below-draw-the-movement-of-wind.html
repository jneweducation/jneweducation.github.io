LAND AND SEA BREEZE

Breeze is the movement of air from the land to the sea and vice versa.

The land breeze is known as the air that is moving towards the surface of the sea.

On the other side, the sea breeze is defined as the air that moves towards the landmass from the sea.

Land breeze and sea breezes happen most of the time in a location that is close to wide bodies of water.

The main difference between the 2 is the types of water to preserve and heat up longer. These kinds of variations in the temperature of land and water causes modifications to the densities of the air above them.

The result of low pressures has an effect of alternating movement of the air which can be manifested as breezes.

In any kind of breeze, they serve as an instrumental in humidity and temperature levels, and precipitation rates.

Breeze or gentle blow of air has a direct result in setting the humidity levels, (it can be high or low), which is the high precipitation, and temperature level moderation in areas near the coast.

Sea breezes and land breezes are considered important elements that are always affects the coastal regions' prevailing winds

Since I can't see the diagram, I will answer the guide questions.

1. A sea breeze describes a wind that blows from the ocean inland towards land. This breeze occurs most often in the spring and summer months because of the greater temperature differences between the ocean and nearby land, particularly in the afternoon when the land is at maximum heating from the sun.

2. A land breeze is a local wind system characterized by a flow from land to water late at night. Land breezes alternate with sea breezes along coastlines adjacent to large bodies of water.

3. The greater the temperature differences between land and sea, the stronger the land breezes and sea breezes. After sunset, the air mass above the coastal land quickly loses heat while the air mass above the water generally remains much closer to it's daytime temperature.

Where does the wind come from?

The sun's energy heats the planet's surface, which causes air to rise, most intensely around the equator. This rising air generates a low-pressure area near the surface into which cooler air is pushed, and it is this airflow that we call "wind."

Where does the wind come from?

The majority of the time, winds blow in one direction. Trees develop asymmetrical if the prevailing winds are strong. Wind direction is always determined by the direction from which the wind is blowing, rather than the direction to which it is blowing. For example, a southerly wind blows from the south to the north.

At what time (day/month) does it occur?

Daily extreme wind speeds of 10 m are most likely in the early afternoon, while those of 200 m are most likely between midnight and sunrise to the north

Sea Breeze
