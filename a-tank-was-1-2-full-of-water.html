6. a rectangular tank 40 cm by 50cm by 20cm is 1/2 full of water.How much Water is in the tank?


Answer:

To solve this problem, we need to first find the volume of the tank and then find half of that volume to determine the amount of water in the tank.

The volume of the tank is:

V = l x w x h

V = 40 cm x 50 cm x 20 cm

V = 40,000 cm³

Half of the volume is:

V/2 = 40,000 cm³ / 2

V/2 = 20,000 cm³

Therefore, there is 20,000 cm³ of water in the tank.

28. A tank was 1/2 full of water. After 420 gallons had been taken out. It was 1/8 full. Find the capacity of the tank


Answer:. Afet

The capacity of the tank is 1,120 gallons.

Step-by-step explanation:

First, is to translate the English sentence of the problem to Mathematical sentence.

It state that a tank was 1/2 full of water. After 420 gallons had been taken out, it was 1/8 full. This statement is equivalent to a mathematical sentence of:

[tex]\frac{1}{2}x[/tex] - 420 = [tex]\frac{1}{8} x[/tex]

Let x represent the full water capacity of the tank

Therefore, using the equation above to solve for x, we get:

[tex]\frac{1}{2} x[/tex] - 420 = [tex]\frac{1}{8} x[/tex]

Using transposition, we get:

[tex]\frac{1}{2} x[/tex] - [tex]\frac{1}{8} x[/tex] = 420

[tex]\frac{4x-x}{8}[/tex] = 420

[tex]\frac{3x}{8}[/tex] = 420

Using cross - multiply, we get:

x = [tex]\frac{420(8)}{3}[/tex]

x = 1120 gallons

Therefore, the full water capacity of the tank is 1,120 gallons.

30. A rectangular tank is 45cm by 15cm by 20cm its full of water. How much water is needed to make the tank 2/3 full?please dont troll i need it in a rush and please give full explanation or step by step procedure ​


Answer:

The volume of the tank is:

V = l x w x h = 45cm x 15cm x 20cm = 13500 cm^3

To fill the tank 2/3 full, we need to fill it with 2/3 of its volume, which is:

2/3 x 13500 cm^3 = 9000 cm^3

So, we need to add 9000 - V cm^3 of water to the tank to make it 2/3 full. Since the tank is already full, we need to remove V - 9000 cm^3 of water from the tank.

The volume of water to be removed from the tank is:

V - 9000 cm^3 = 13500 cm^3 - 9000 cm^3 = 4500 cm^3

Therefore, we need to remove 4500 cm^3 of water from the tank to make it 2/3 full.

Answer:

9000 cubic cm of water

Step-by-step explanation:

The volume of the rectangular tank is given by:

V = l x w x h

Where l is the length, w is the width, and h is the height.

Substituting the given values, we have:

V = 45 cm x 15 cm x 20 cm = 13500 cubic cm

To make the tank 2/3 full, we need to find 2/3 of the tank's volume:

2/3 x 13500 = 9000 cubic cm

So we need 9000 cubic cm of water to make the tank 2/3 full.
