21. A brief summary of Much Ado About Nothing


Much Ado About Nothing is a play written by Shakespeare. The play is focused on how Elizabeathen women were to behave, and how the attitude was presented to those who refused to behave that way.

The main two actors in this play were Hero and Beatrice, both being opposite people yet loving and trusting each other very much.

The whole play focuses on the love between Hero and Claudio, which was very obvious, and the love between Beatrice and Benedick, which started off as very heated arguments and insults being thrown back and forth at each other.

A trick was played by the "bad guy" of the play - Don John - who ruined Hero's reputation in front of Claudio, making her look as though she was cheating on him, but all that got sorted out in the end, thanks to Leonato (hero's father), Beatrice and Benedick, who all believed Hero to be innocent.
