1. A 1000kg airplane is flying perfectly level flight. The drag is 25000N and the thrust is 40000N. Find its acceleration.


Acceleration

[tex]\red{\overline{\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad}}[/tex]

[tex]\huge\bold\fcolorbox{red}{re}{Problem}[/tex]

A 1000kg airplane is flying perfectly level flight. The drag is 25000N and the thrust is 40000N. Find its acceleration.

[tex]\huge\color{red}{\underline{\overline\color{blue}{\sf{ Solution}}}}[/tex]

Let's have a Short Discussion:

Accceleration is the qoutient of force and mass. We divide force by mass to get acceleration. The two variables needed for acceleration are given as f (25,000 N) and m (1,000 kg).

But the problem above, the given are three, there are thrust (40,000 N), f (25,000 N), and m (1,000 kg). To solve this situation, we will add thrust and force to get the final force of the airplane, and then divide the final force by mass to get the acceleration of the airplane.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \bold{40,000 N + 25,000 N} \\ = \bold\red{65,000 N} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Now that we already get the final force, let us solve the problem by using the formula below:

[tex]\large\bold{\red{A} = \frac{\green{force}}\blue{mass} }[/tex]

Solving Acceleration

[tex]\large\bold{A = \frac{force}{mass} }[/tex]

[tex]\large\bold{\red{A} = \frac{\pink{65 \: 000 \: N}}{\orange{1 \: 000 \: kg}} }[/tex]

[tex]\bold{A = \boxed{\red{\bold{65 \: m/s {}^{2} }}}} \: \green\longleftrightarrow \: \bold\blue{answer}[/tex]

[tex]\huge\star{\underline{\mathsf{\red{A}\pink{N}\green{S}\blue{W}\purple{E}\orange{R}}}}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of the airplane is 65 m/s².

27. A Pitot tube on an airplane flying at standard sea level reads 1.07 x 105 N/m2. What is the velocity of the airplane? (Round the final answer to two decimal places.) The velocity of the airplane is m/s.


PITOT TUBE AND VELOCITY

If a pitot tube on an airplane flying at a standard sea level reads 1.07 x [tex]10^5[/tex] N/[tex]m^2[/tex]. The velocity of the airplane is 96.47 m/s.

How did we get 96.47 m/s?

The formula to get the velocity of an object through a pitot tube experiment is through using the formula: V = [tex]\sqrt{\frac{2(P_o-P}{\rho} }[/tex]

Where:

V is the velocity of the moving object

[tex]P_o[/tex] is the variable pressure

P is the static pressure

[tex]\rho[/tex] is the density

Solving:

V = [tex]\sqrt{\frac{2(1.07x10^5 -1.013x10^5)}{1.225} }[/tex] = 96.46824580648075 m/s

Rounding off the final answer to two decimal places, the velocity of the airplane is 96.47 m/s.

28. A 1000kg airplain is flying perfectly level flight. the drag is 25000n and the thrust is 40000n. find it's acceleration.


The acceleration is 15 m/s².

Solution:

Given: Drag = 25000 N, thrust = 40000 N, and mass(m) = 1000 kg

Total Net Force on the aircraft is: thrust - drag (40000N-25000 N = 15000 N).

The formula for force is:

Force(F) = mass(m) X acceleration(a)

F=mxa

15000=1000 x a

a = 15 m/s²

About acceleration

Straight changing motion is closely related to acceleration. According to the World of Physics, acceleration is the change in velocity per unit time. Acceleration is a vector quantity that has both magnitude and direction.

Acceleration is divided into slowed acceleration and accelerated acceleration. Acceleration is slowed down meaning the direction of acceleration is opposite to the direction of velocity. While acceleration is accelerated, it means that the direction of acceleration is in the same direction as the speed.

Acceleration is negative if the speed of an object decreases over a certain time interval (slows down). Acceleration is positive when the speed of an object increases in a certain time interval (accelerates).

We can illustrate the acceleration and deceleration of events in a moving car. A car that is moving at a certain speed and direction will experience a deceleration in the opposite direction of the speed. When the car is braked because it is about to stop, and the car will be accelerated in the direction of its acceleration in the same direction as the speed when the car is gassed because it is about to overtake another vehicle in front of it.
