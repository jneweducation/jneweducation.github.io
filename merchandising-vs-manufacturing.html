14. if you are going into business would you consider engaging in a service business ?merchandising ?manufacturing? why or why?​


Answer:

Manufacturing and merchandising are both components necessary to provide consumers with the goods they demand, but each one occupies a different space and role throughout the process. While manufacturing begins the process of designing and creating goods, merchandising completes the task by taking products and getting them into the hands of consumers. As each type of company has very different roles, it is natural that they also operate with different needs, goals and concerns.

Explanation:

Manufacturing companies take raw materials and transform them into something else desired by the end consumer. The U.S. government in its Industry Classification System further defines manufacturing as the "mechanical, physical, or chemical transformation of materials, substances, or components into new products." There are a wide range of companies and industries that engage in this type of activity. Some of the many manufacturing processes include:

Transforming wood into furniture

Refining raw metals from ore or pig into usable forms such nails, insulated wires and cables

Forming glass from sand or pottery from clay

Preparing fiber to make thread, yarn, woven fabrics, lace or felt

Not all manufactures use only raw materials to produce their product. Some companies rely on the manufacturing processes of others. For example a company in the apparel manufacturing industry uses purchased materials to sew and put together their fashions. In order to complete their own manufacturing processes they use goods such as woven fabrics, thread, zippers and buttons made by others.

Merchandising is a bit more complex in that it is an activity but it also is a strategy of how to get people to buy products. It is not only what a company does to make money (sell merchandise), but how they go about doing it (marketing those goods). For example, a grocery store's primary goal is to sell their merchandise, lots of different types of food products and household essentials, to their customers. But the in-house marketing and sales techniques that they use to increase sales of particular items is the strategy component of merchandising. Some methods the store might use include:

End-cap displays featuring sale items

Specialty services at a deli, bakery or meat counter

Soup, salad and pick-up lunch bar

Taste-testing promos

Reward cards to earn free items
