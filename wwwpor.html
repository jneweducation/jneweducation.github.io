Water vapor is the gaseous state of water. Water vapor can be formed by the evaporation or boiling of liquid water or by the sublimation of ice. Answer:

2. Epididymis- a highly convoluted duct behind the testis, along which sperm passes to the vas deferens.

3. Mucus- is a normal, slippery and stringy fluid substance produced by many lining tissues in the body.

4. Prostate Gland- prostate is a walnut-sized gland located between the bladder and the penis

5. Urethra- the duct by which urine is conveyed out of the body from the bladder, and which in male vertebrates also conveys semen

6. Puberty- is the time in life when a boy or girl becomes sexually mature.

7. Endometrium- the mucous membrane lining the uterus, which thickens during the menstrual cycle in preparation for possible implantation of an embryo.

8. Uterus- is a hollow muscular organ located in the female pelvis between the bladder and rectum.

9. Fertilization- the action or process of fertilizing an egg, female animal, or plant, involving the fusion of male and female gametes to form a zygote.

10. Gametes- a mature haploid male or female germ cell which is able to unite with another of the opposite sex in sexual reproduction to form a zygote.

11. Sperm cell- is the male reproductive cell, or gamete, in anisogamous forms of sexual reproduction.

12. Egg cell- The egg cell, or ovum (plural ova), is the female reproductive cell, or gamete, in most anisogamous organisms (organisms that reproduce sexually with a larger, "female" gamete and a smaller, "male" one).

13. Zygote- is the union of the sperm cell and the egg cell

14. Internal fertilization- is the union of an egg and sperm cell during sexual reproduction inside the female body.

15. External fertilization- is a mode of reproduction in which a male organism's sperm fertilizes a female organism's egg outside of the female's body.
