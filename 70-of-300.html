5. a container holds 300 ml of c02 at 3mm hg! what will be the volume if the pressure is increased to 70 mm hg.​


12.857 mL

Explanation:

To solve this problem, we can use Boyle's Law, which states that the pressure and volume of a gas are inversely proportional when the temperature is constant. Mathematically, this can be expressed as:

P1 x V1 = P2 x V2

where P1 and V1 are the initial pressure and volume, and P2 and V2 are the final pressure and volume, respectively.

Using the given values:

P1 = 3 mm Hg

V1 = 300 mL

P2 = 70 mm Hg

V2 = ?

We can rearrange Boyle's Law to solve for V2:

V2 = (P1 x V1) / P2

Substituting the values:

V2 = (3 mm Hg x 300 mL) / 70 mm Hg

V2 = 12.857 mL

Therefore, the volume of the container will be 12.857 mL if the pressure is increased to 70 mm Hg, assuming the temperature remains constant.
