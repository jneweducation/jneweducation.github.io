24. how do hackers hack your password?


How do hackers hack your password?

Hackers are people who are gaining unauthorized access to your computer or system. Their activity is called hacking. This is considered to be an illegal activity. These people could apply various activities to gain access to your system or computer without you knowing it. Some of their activities are the following:

Phising - this is a technique that hackers applied to their victims by means of showing you a link to a webpage or site that would make you input your account details (such as username, passwords, account number) to a banking website or any website. They could make it appear like it is a legit website. Then, once the victim has input his or her details in the form, the hacker could get this and used their obtained information to log in to the website. Phishing is considered an effective way of the hacker to get your details. To prevent this, do not click on links which you are not sure of and always ask first your bank or service provider to verify if they are really in need of your details.  

Brute Force Attack - this technique applies when the hacker would want to guess your password or credentials of your online account. Normally, they would have a software that could perform brute force guessing on letters of your password. The software can compare each letter of your password until they would guess the right key for it. Once that they have obtained all the correct letters, they can use it to log in your account. To prevent this attack, always use a strong password. Stronger passwords are harder and longer to guess using brute force attack.

26. As an Individual how can you secure your Account from the Hackers?​


Answer:

• Don’t access personal or financial data with public Wi-Fi.

This may seem like a no-brainer, but you’d be surprised how many people check their bank accounts or make purchases with a credit card while using public Wi-Fi. It’s best to do those things on a secure connection.

• Turn off anything you don’t need.

Hackers can use certain features on your phone to get at your information, location or connection. So, instead of keeping your GPS, wireless connection and geo-tracking on all the time, just turn them on when you need them.

• Choose your apps wisely.

Only download apps from trustworthy sources that have established a good reputation. Make sure you update your software and apps regularly and get rid of old apps you don’t use.

• Use a password, lock code or encryption.

Make sure your passwords are at least eight characters long, with a mix of upper and lower case, and include numbers or other characters, and never use the auto-complete feature for passwords. You can use the storage encryption feature on your phone to protect your private data, and set your screen to timeout after five minutes or less.

• Be skeptical about links and attachments.

If you’re not sure about the source, don’t use the link or open the attachment.

•Trace or erase.

Make sure your data is secure if your mobile device is stolen or lost. You can set up your device to lock itself after a pre-set number of failed log-in attempts.
