[tex]\color{magenta}{\boxed{{\colorbox{black}{1.Electric \: flat \: iron}}}}[/tex]

A flat iron, an electric toaster and a stove all covert electrical energy into thermal energy.

[tex]\color{magenta}{\boxed{{\colorbox{black}{2.Flashlight}}}}[/tex]

The correct answers are “chemical energy into electrical energy” and then “the electrical energy into light energy”. Explanation: In the battery-powered flashlight, the battery supplies the chemical energy which makes the electrons to flow in the circuit and constitutes the current.

[tex]\color{magenta}{\boxed{{\colorbox{black}{3.Battery-operated\:radio }}}}[/tex]

A battery-operated radio converts the chemical energy to electrical energy which in turn is converted to sound and heat energy.

[tex]\color{magenta}{\boxed{{\colorbox{black}{4.Electric \:fan }}}}[/tex]

(a) An electric fan converts electrical energy into mechanical energy.

[tex]\color{magenta}{\boxed{{\colorbox{black}{5.Cellphone}}}}[/tex]

A cell phone transforms electrical energy to electromagnetic energy that travels to other phones. Your body transforms the chemical energy in your food to mechanical energy you need to move your muscles. Chemical energy in food is also transformed to the thermal energy your body uses to maintain its temperature.
