5. explaination of pyrolysis?​


Answer:

Pyrolysis is a thermochemical process in which organic materials are broken down into simpler chemical compounds by heating them in the absence of oxygen. The term "pyrolysis" comes from the Greek words "pyro" (fire) and "lysis" (to separate), and it is sometimes referred to as "dry distillation" or "thermal cracking."

During pyrolysis, the organic material is heated to high temperatures (typically between 350 and 750 degrees Celsius) in a reactor vessel. As the material is heated, it begins to break down into smaller molecules, such as gases, liquids, and solids. The specific products of pyrolysis depend on the type of material being processed and the conditions of the process.

Pyrolysis is used for a variety of applications, including the production of biofuels, the conversion of waste materials into useful products, and the production of chemicals and materials. One example of its application is in the recycling of plastic waste, where plastic is heated and broken down into smaller molecules that can be used to make new products.

Pyrolysis can also be used as a destructive method to analyze the chemical composition of a material, such as in the case of pyrolysis-gas chromatography/mass spectrometry (Py-GC/MS) which is a powerful analytical technique for characterizing the composition of complex organic mixtures.

In summary, pyrolysis is a process of breaking down organic materials into simpler compounds by heating them in the absence of oxygen, it can have different application in different fields like recycling, energy production and chemical analysis.

16. give the meaning of pyrolysis​


What is the meaning of Pyrolysis?

The process of pyrolysis (or degassing) is the pyrolysis of materials at high temperatures in an inert atmosphere. This is due to a change in chemical composition. The word is derived from the Greek derivatives pyro "fire", "heat", "fever" and lysis "separation".

Pyrolysis is most often used for processing organic materials. This is one of the processes of burning wood. As a rule, during pyrolysis of organic substances, volatile products are formed and coal remains. It is a  solid residue rich in carbon. Extreme pyrolysis in which the residue is mostly carbon  is called carbonization. Pyrolysis is considered the first step in the  gasification or combustion process.

This process is widely used  in the chemical industry. For example, it is used to produce ethylene, various forms of carbon and other chemicals from petroleum, coal and even wood, and to produce coke from coal. It is also used to convert natural gas (primarily methane) into environmentally friendly hydrogen gas and environmentally friendly solid coal, most recently on an industrial scale. Preferred applications of pyrolysis are conversion of biomass into syngas and biochar, waste plastics  into reusable oil, or waste into safe single use products.
