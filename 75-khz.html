12. 5. What is the period of a wave with a frequency of 50 KHZ ?​


Answer:

The period of a wave can be calculated using the formula:

[tex]T = \frac{1}{f}[/tex]

where [tex]T[/tex] is the period in seconds, and [tex]f[/tex] is the frequency in Hertz (Hz).

Since the frequency given in the problem is 50 KHz, we need to convert it to Hz by multiplying it by 1000:

[tex]f = 50 KHz(1000) = 50,000 Hz[/tex]

[tex]T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{50,000 \mathrm{Hz}} = \boxed{0.00002 \mathrm{s}}[/tex]

Therefore, the period of a wave with a frequency of 50 KHz is 0.00002 seconds.
