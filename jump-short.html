3. Create a short paragraph to explain about jump starting vehicle​


Answer:

A jump start, also called a boost, is a procedure of starting a motor vehicle (most commonly cars or trucks) that has a discharged or depleted battery. A temporary connection is made to the battery of another vehicle, or to some other external power source. The external supply of electricity recharges the disabled vehicle's battery and provides some of the power needed to crank the engine. Once the vehicle has been started, its normal charging system will recharge, so the auxiliary source can be removed. If the vehicle charging system is functional, leaving the engine running will restore the charge of the battery, although it is usually recommended to drive the vehicle for a few minutes after starting to speed up the recharging process.

30. Watch how high it _____. jump jumped jumping jumps


The correct verb form to complete the sentence "Watch how high it _____." is "jumps"

This is because the sentence is in the present tense, and the subject "it" is in the third person singular form, so the verb should be in its present simple form "jumps".

It's an imperative sentence, a command or an order, and the structure of imperative sentence is subject + base form of verb.

In English, the present simple tense is used to describe an action that is currently happening or is a regular occurrence. It is formed by using the base form of the verb, which is the infinitive form without "to" (e.g. "jump," "walk," "run").

For example, "I jump rope every day." or "She jumps high" The subject "I" and "She" are in the first and third person singular form respectively and the verb "jump" and "jumps" are in the present simple form.

On the other hand, "Watch how high it jumps" is an imperative sentence, which is a type of sentence that gives a command or an order. The subject of the sentence is usually omitted as it is implied that the person being spoken to is the subject of the sentence. In this case, the subject is "you" and the verb "jumps" is in the base form because it is an imperative sentence.

So in this case, "Watch how high it jumps" is the correct form because it is in the present simple form and it's an imperative sentence.
