18. When is primary survey of the victim done? a. When the victim is conscious b. During the survey of the scene c. When the victim is unconscious d. After the victim has regained consciousness


The primary survey of the victim is done during the survey of the scene. It is important to get a gauge of the extent of the damage to the victim and even the surroundings, so that proper help can be given to the victim so that proper prioritization can be done. In doing the primary survey, the following technique is usually done:

DRABC of Primary Survey

Danger - ensure the surrounding is safe. If the victim can be moved, move to a safer place. If not (due to potential danger if moved), then secure the area as much as you can, by getting help from others.Response - check if the victim is responsive or not. It does not necessarily mean that they can speak but if they are able to follow a command, such as "Open your eyes", "Nod once" or "Move a finger" they are considered responsive. Airway - check if the airway is open and clear. If they are not breathing, be prepared to administer CPR.Breathing - Check if they are breathing. If not, then perform CPR.Circulation  - check if there are signs of bleeding. Apply direct pressure to any wound if you see one.

When trying to help someone in an accident, make sure that you know what you are doing and confident in doing it. What we are trying to avoid is further harm to the casualties.
