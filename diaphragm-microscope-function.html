12. what can you infer as to the function of the diaphragm?(microscope)


The diaphragm controls the amount of light that reaches the specimen. It is an adjustable part of the microscope that monitors the strength of light and the size of the light beam that enters the specimen. It is located above and beneath the condenser stage.

Most compound microscopes have two types of diaphragm:

Disc Diaphragm- of the two forms, this is the simplest and least expensive. This lies between the source of light and the  slide or specimen. It contains a rotating (usually fixed) disk with five to ten diameter opening which  restricts the amount of light that passes through the specimen.Iris Diaphragm- of the two types, this is more efficient but more costly. This has a continuously variable diameter (such as an eye iris or a camera shutter) that has a feature to restrict the opening size through which light passes from the light source the resolution, contrast, and sharpness are optimized. It is typically operated using a lever.

The diaphragm is essential in controlling the light that reaches the specimen, if not properly adjusted, there might be a failure in seeing the specimen samples under a microscope.
