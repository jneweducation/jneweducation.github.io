30. 2. In how many ways can a committee of six be formed from a group of nine members?​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

In this case, we have a total of 12 people (9 women and 3 men) and we want to choose 6 of them. So, the total number of ways to form a committee without any restrictions is: C(12, 6) = 12! / (6! * (12-6)!) = 924 ways.

Answer:

84

Step-by-step explanation:

The number of ways to form a committee of 6 from a group of 9 members can be calculated using the formula for combinations:

nCr = n! / r!(n-r)!

where n is the total number of members and r is the number of members to be selected for the committee.

In this case, we have:

n = 9 (total number of members)

r = 6 (number of members to be selected)

Using the formula, we get:

9C6 = 9! / 6!(9-6)! = 84

Therefore, there are 84 ways to form a committee of 6 from a group of 9 members.
