4. A circle has its center at (3,-2)is tangent to the line 3x+4y-26=0. What is the radius of the circle? what is the equation of the diameter that is perpendicular to the line? what is the equation of the normal line?


Answer:

The radius of the circle is 5.

The equation of the diameter that is perpendicular to the tangent line and at the same time, the equation of the normal line is [tex]y=\frac{4}{3}x-6[/tex].

Step-by-step explanation:

The circle is the locus of all points equidistant from the fixed point called the center. The standard form of the circle equation is given by [tex](x-h)^2 +(y-k)^2=r^2[/tex] where [tex](h, k)[/tex] is the center of the circle and [tex]r[/tex] is the radius of the circle.

The tangent line is the line that intersects a curve at one point.

The normal line is defined as the line that is perpendicular to the tangent line at the point of tangency.

The standard form of the equation of a line is given by [tex]Ax+By=C[/tex].

Solving the Radius of the Circle

The radius is the distance from the center to the tangent line, which is given by the definition, [tex]r=\frac{|A(x_1)+B(y_1)+C|}{\sqrt{A^2+B^2}}[/tex], where A, B and C are the constants in the equation of the tangent line, and [tex]x_1[/tex] and [tex]y_1[/tex] are the values from the center of the circle. Substitute the values [tex]A=3[/tex], [tex]B=4[/tex], [tex]C=26[/tex], [tex]x_1=3[/tex] and [tex]y_1=-2[/tex] in the radius formula and simplify.

Note that:

                                    [tex]\begin{aligned}3x+4y-26&=0\\3x+4y&=26\end{aligned}[/tex]

                                     [tex]\begin{aligned}r&=\frac{|A(x_1)+B(y_1)+C|}{\sqrt{A^2+B^2}}\\r&=\frac{|3(3)+4(-2)+26|}{\sqrt{3^2+4^2}}\\r&=\frac{|9-8+26|}{\sqrt{9+16}}\\r&=\frac{|27|}{\sqrt{25}}\\r&=\frac{27}{5}\\r&=5.4\\r&\approx{5}\end{aligned}[/tex]

So we have the equation of the circle, [tex](x-3)^2 +(y+2)^2=5^2[/tex].

Solving the Equation of the Diameter of the Circle

The normal line and the equation of the diameter are the same in this context since both lines, the normal and equation of the diameter are perpendicular to the tangent line, [tex]3x+4y-26=0[/tex].

Remember that [tex]3x+4y-26=0[/tex] can be written as

                                     [tex]\begin{aligned}3x+4y-26&=0\\4y&=-3x+26\\y&=\frac{-3x+26}{4}\\y&=-\frac{3x}{4}+\frac{13}{2}\end{aligned}[/tex]

We know that the slope of the tangent line is [tex]m=-\frac{3}{4}[/tex] since it is in the form [tex]y=mx+b[/tex] (Slope-intercept form of the line).

The two are lines are said to be perpendicular if and only if their slopes are negative reciprocal of each other.

So, the slope of the equation of the diameter must be [tex]m_d=-(-\frac{4}{3})=\frac{4}{3}[/tex].

We have the equation of the diamter perpendicular to the tangent line,  

                                  [tex]y=\frac{4}{3}x+b[/tex].

To fill the value of [tex]b[/tex], substitute the value of [tex]x=3[/tex] and [tex]y=-2[/tex] to the equation.

                                [tex]\begin{aligned}y&=\frac{4}{3}x+b\\-2&=\frac{4}{3}(3)+b\\-2&=4+b\\-2-4&=b\\-6&=b\end{aligned}[/tex]

So, the equation of the diameter that is perpendicular to the tangent line is

[tex]y=\frac{4}{3}x-6[/tex]. The same equation also serves as the equation of the normal line. Refer to the definition above.

15. Find the equation of the tangent and normal lines to the functiony = x2 - 6x + 4 at the point (2,-4).​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

To determine the slope of the tangent line, get the first derivative of the curve and evaluate it at the given x-coordinate of the point.

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=x^2-6x+4} \longleftarrow \textsf{differentiate wrt x}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{d}{dx}(y=x^2-6x+4)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dx}=2x-6} \longleftarrow \textsf{evaluate at x = 2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dx}=2(2)-6}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dx}=-2}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The slope of the tangent line is m₁ = -2, the tangent line is also passing through point (2, -4).

Using point-slope form of a line:

[tex]\mathsf{y-y_1=m(x-x_1)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m=m_1=-2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(x_1,y_1)=(2,-4)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y-(-4)=(-2)(x-2)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y+4=-2(x-2)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y+4=-2x+4}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=-2x} \Longleftarrow \textsf{equation of tangent line}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The slope of the normal line is the negative reciprocal of the slope of the tangent line. m₂ = -(1/m₁). The normal line is also passing through point (2, -4).

[tex]\mathsf{m_2=-\dfrac{1}{m_1}=-\left(\dfrac{1}{-2}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m_2=\dfrac{1}{2}}[/tex]

Using point-slope form of a line:

[tex]\mathsf{y-y_1=m(x-x_1)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m=m_2=\dfrac{1}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(x_1,y_1)=(2,-4)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y-(-4)=\dfrac{1}{2}(x-2)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y+4=\dfrac{1}{2}x-1}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\dfrac{1}{2}x-5} \Longleftarrow \textsf{equation of the normal line}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The equation of the tangent line is: [tex]\boxed{\textsf{y = -2x}}[/tex]

The equation of the normal line is: [tex]\boxed{\mathsf{y=\dfrac{1}{2}x-5}}[/tex]


16. Find the equation of the tangent and normal lines to the curve2x3 + 2y3 - 9xy = 0 the point (1, 2).​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{2x^3+2y^3-9xy=0}[/tex]

To determine the slope of the tangent line, get the first derivative of the curve and evaluate it at the given x and y coordinates of the point.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Using implicit differentiation:

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{d}{dx}(2x^3+2y^3-9xy=0)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{6x^2+6y^2\dfrac{dy}{dx}-9\left(x\dfrac{dy}{dx}+y\right)=0}[/tex]

Isolate dy/dx

[tex]\mathsf{6x^2+6y^2\dfrac{dy}{dx}-9x\dfrac{dy}{dx}-9y=0}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{9x\dfrac{dy}{dx}-6y^2\dfrac{dy}{dx}=6x^2-9y}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(9x-6y^2)\dfrac{dy}{dx}=6x^2-9y}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{6x^2-9y}{9x-6y^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{2x^2-3y}{3x-2y^2}} \longleftarrow \textsf{evaluate at x = 1, y = 2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{2(1)-3(2)}{3(1)-2(2)^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{4}{5}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The slope of the tangent is m₁ = 4/5. The tangent line is also passing through point (1, 2).

Using point-slope form of a line:

[tex]\mathsf{y-y_1=m(x-x_1)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m=m_1=\dfrac{4}{5}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(x_1,y_1)=(1,2)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y-2=\dfrac{4}{5}(x-1)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y-4=\dfrac{4}{5}x-\dfrac{4}{5}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\dfrac{4}{5}x-\dfrac{4}{5}+2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\dfrac{4}{5}x+\dfrac{6}{5}} \Longleftarrow \textsf{equation of the tangent line}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The slope of the normal is line is equal to the negative reciprocal of the slope of the tangent line. m₂ = -(1/m₁) = -(5/4). The normal line is also passing through point (1, 2).

Using point-slope form of a line:

[tex]\mathsf{y-y_1=m(x-x_1)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{m=m_2=-\dfrac{5}{4}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(x_1,y_1)=(1,2)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y-2=-\dfrac{5}{4}(x-1)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y-2=-\dfrac{5}{4}x+\dfrac{5}{4}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=-\dfrac{5}{4}x+\dfrac{5}{4}+2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=-\dfrac{5}{4}x+\dfrac{13}{4}} \Longleftarrow \textsf{equation of the normal line}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The equation of the tangent line is:

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{y=\dfrac{4}{5}x+\dfrac{6}{5}}}[/tex]

The equation of the normal line is:

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{y=-\dfrac{5}{4}x+\dfrac{13}{4}}}[/tex]

Ax + By + C = 0


Where A, B, and C are real numbers

[tex]m = \frac{y2 - y1}{x2 - x1}[/tex] and


m = tan (Θ)

(x1,y1) as the first point


(x2,y2) as the second point


Θ as the angle of inclination



Since in the problem, we are given the angle by which the tangent line is inclined, we are to use the second formula.



Substituting the given values to the equation, we have


m = tan (Θ)


m = tan (45°)


By using the calculator, we have the value of m as


m = 1.61977 or simply  

m = 1.62
