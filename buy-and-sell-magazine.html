To conjugate a verb in English, we need to select the correct form based on three factors:

Tense: The tense determines when the verb takes place. For example, simple past tense (like "bought") refers to an action that took place and ended in the past, while present perfect tense (like "has bought") refers to an action that started at some point in the past but continues to the present.Person: The person determines who or what is performing the action. In this case, "Grandpa" is the third person singular.Aspect: The aspect determines whether the action is completed, ongoing, or habitual. In this case, "bought" is a simple past tense form of the verb "to buy," which denotes a completed action.

By selecting the appropriate tense, person, and aspect, we can correctly conjugate the verb "buy" to form the sentence "Grandpa bought that magazine last week from the bookstore." In this case, the correct conjugated form is "Grandpa bought that magazine last week from the bookstore," which denotes a completed action performed by the third person singular, "Grandpa," in the past tense.
