19. the temperature of a sample of gas in a steel tank at 30000 pa is increased from -100.0°C to 1000°C.What is the final pressure inside the tank?


Good Day...

Problem:

the temperature of a sample of gas in a steel tank at 30000 pa is increased from -100.0°C to 1000°C.What is the final pressure inside the tank?

Note:

Kelvin temperature should be used in solving gas laws.

Kelvin = degree celsius + 273.15

Kelvin = -100.0°C  + 273.15 = 173.15 K

Kelvin = 1000°C + 273.15  =  1273.15 K

Given:

P1 (initial pressure) = 30000 pa (pascal)

T1 (initial temperature) = 173.15 K  or -100.0°C

P2 (final pressure) = unknown ?

T2 (final temperature) = 1000°C or 1273.15 K

Solution:

This problem can be solve using Gay-Lussac's Law with the formula

P1/T1 = P2/T2 amd to solve for P2, we can use P2 = P1T2/T1

P2 = P1T2/T1

      = 30000 pa × 1273.15 K / 173.15 K   (cancel the unit Kelvin)

      = 38,194,500 pa / 173.15

      = 220586.197 pa

Answer:

Pressure is 220586.197 pa (pascal)

21. The temperature of a sample of LPG gas in a steel tank at 27.0 kPa is increased from – 97.0˚C to 22.0˚C. What is the final pressure inside the tank?​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{27.0 kPa}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = -\text{97.0°C + 273.15 = 176.15 K}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{22.0°C + 273.15 = 295.15 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = P_{1} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{27.0 kPa} × \frac{\text{295.15 K}}{\text{176.15 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{45.2 kPa}}[/tex]

25. A sample of gas at 3.00 x 103 mm Hg inside a steel tank is cooled from 500.0 °C to 0.00 °C. What is the final pressure of the gas in the steel tank?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273.15 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = 3.00 \times 10^3 \: \text{mmHg} \\ & T_1 = 500.0^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{773.15 K} \\ & T_2 = 0.00^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{273.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure by using Gay-Lussac's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{P_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_2}{T_2} \\ P_2T_1 & = P_1T_2 \\ \frac{P_2T_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ P_2 & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ & = \frac{(3.00 \times 10^3 \: \text{mmHg})(\text{273.15 K})}{\text{773.15 K}} \\ & = \boxed{1.06 \times 10^3 \: \text{mmHg}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the final pressure of the gas in steel tank is 1.06 × 10³ mmHg.

28. how to weld carbon steel pipe in 1G position?​


Answer:

Welding carbon steel pipe in the 1G position can be done using several welding processes, including Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW), Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW), Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW), and Flux-Cored Arc Welding (FCAW).

Here are the general steps for welding carbon steel pipe in the 1G position using SMAW:

Prepare the pipe - Cut the pipe to the desired length and bevel the ends to create a groove for the weld. Make sure that the bevel angle and root gap are according to the welding procedure specification (WPS).

Set up the equipment - Set up the welding machine, select the appropriate welding rod, and adjust the current and polarity settings.

Position the pipe - Position the pipe in the 1G position, which means it is lying horizontally, and the weld is in a vertical direction.

Tack weld - Tack weld the two ends of the pipe to hold them in place. Ensure that the tack welds are aligned with the beveled edges.

Weld the root pass - Start welding the root pass, making sure to maintain a consistent speed and angle. Use the stringer technique to deposit the weld metal into the root gap. The root pass should penetrate the full thickness of the pipe, and the reinforcement should be flush with the surface of the pipe.

Fill and cap passes - After completing the root pass, continue with the fill and cap passes, making sure to overlap the previous pass slightly. Use the weave technique to deposit the weld metal, and ensure that the final weld bead is smooth and free of porosity, cracks, or other defects.

Clean and inspect - Clean the weld area to remove any slag or spatter, and inspect the weld for any defects. If any defects are found, repair them before proceeding.

By following these steps and adhering to the welding procedure specification (WPS), you can successfully weld carbon steel pipe in the 1G position using SMAW.
