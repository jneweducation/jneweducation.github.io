10. j(n)=n-4k(n)=2nfind (j•k)(n)​


SOLVING FUNCTION

Given the function j(n) = n - 4 and k(n) = 2n, find the value of (j•k)(n).

[tex]\bullet[/tex] Substitute the given values of n to the given equation to get the answer.

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \ (j \times k)(n) \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: (n - 4)(2n) \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed { = 2n^{2} - 8n} [/tex]

The answer is 2n² - 8n.

24. If J = 46, and j = 24 find the ∠A and ∠R and sides a and r. Nonsense = Report ​


✏️TRIGONOMETRY===============================

Problem: If J = 46, and j = 24 find the ∠A and ∠R and sides a and r.

Find ∠A: ΔJAR is a right triangle and in every right triangles the opposite side of 90° or the right angles is always be the hypotenuse, side a is the hypotenuse the opposite angle of it is angle A.

- Therefore the angle A is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 90 \degree \: }}[/tex]

[tex] \large\sf[/tex]

Find ∠R: In every triangle, the sum of its interior angles is 180°.

[tex] \angle J + \angle A + \angle R = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]46 \degree + 90 \degree + \angle R = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]136\degree + \angle R = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]\angle R = 180 \degree - 136 \degree[/tex]

[tex]\angle R = 44 \degree[/tex]

- Therefore, the angle R is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 44 \degree \: }}[/tex]

[tex] \large\sf[/tex]

Find side a: Refers to the given angle J, the side j which is the opposite of the given angle, find side a which is the hypotenuse. In the relation of the opposite side and the hypotenuse. Use the sine ratio.

[tex] \begin{gathered} \sin \theta = \frac{opposite}{hypotenuse} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} \sin46 \degree = \frac{24}{a} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} a = \frac{24}{ \sin46 \degree} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]a = 33.36[/tex]

- Therefore, the side a is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 33.36 \: }}[/tex]

[tex] \large\sf[/tex]

Find side r: Refers to the given angle J, the side j which is the opposite of the given angle, find side r which is the adjacent to the given angle. In the relation of the opposite side and the adjacent. Use the tangent ratio.

[tex] \begin{gathered} \tan \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} \tan 46 \degree = \frac{24}{r} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} r = \frac{24}{ \tan46 \degree} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]r = 23.18[/tex]

- Therefore, the side r is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 23.18\: }}[/tex]===============================
