Graphs and Other Charts

A GRAPH is a diagram that shows the relation between variable quantities, typically of two variables, each measured along a pair of axes at right angles.

A TABLE, also called a matrix, is an orderly arrangement of quantitative data in columns and rows.

A PICTOGRAM uses pictures to represent a particular number of items.

A LINEGRAPH shows you how numbers have changed over time. They are used when you have data that are connected, and to show trends, i.e. average night time temperature in each month of the year.

A PIECHART shows you how a whole is divided into different parts. You might, for example, want to show how a budget had been spent on different items in a particular year.

A BAR GRAPH is a graphical representation of data, quantities or numbers using bars or strips. It is used to compare and contrast numbers, frequencies or other measures of distinct categories of data.

A FLOW CHART shows the sequence of movements or actions of people or things involved in a complex system or activity.

A MAP is a collection of data showing the spatial arrangement or distribution of something over an area.

CAPTION GRAPHICS provide a title, short explanation, or a description accompanying an illustration or a photograph.

GRAPHS AND CHARTS can show trends and cycles and make the information easier to visualize.
