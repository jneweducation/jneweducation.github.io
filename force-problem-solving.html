4. Describe how the frictional force affects the movement of: Math teacher uses the whiteboard and marker to solve mathematical problems.


Answer:

Frictional force affects the movement of a math teacher using the whiteboard and marker in the following ways:

Writing on the whiteboard: Frictional force between the marker and the whiteboard surface affects the movement of the teacher's hand as they write. A higher level of friction between the marker and the whiteboard will make it more difficult for the teacher to write smoothly and make precise markings.

Erasing mistakes: Frictional force also affects the movement of the teacher's hand when erasing mistakes on the whiteboard. If there is too much friction between the eraser and the whiteboard, it will make it harder for the teacher to erase effectively.

Holding the marker: Frictional force between the teacher's hand and the marker affects the ability of the teacher to hold and control the marker. If there is too much friction, the teacher may have difficulty holding the marker securely, which can affect their ability to write or draw on the whiteboard.

Movement of marker: Frictional force between the marker tip and the whiteboard surface can affect the ink flow of the marker, and may make it harder for the teacher to write legibly.

Overall, frictional force plays a crucial role in the movement of the math teacher using the whiteboard and marker. It affects the teacher's ability to write, erase and control the marker, which in turn affects their ability to effectively communicate and teach mathematical problems

Explanation:

A force may cause a moving object to either speed up or slow down. This force produces a change in the object's velocity, which causes its acceleration.

____________________________

Newton perceived this effect of force on the motion of an object. Newton's second law of motion, or law of acceleration, states that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force Facting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. In symbols,

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: [/tex][tex]a \ = \frac{f}{m} [/tex]

Where:

F=Net force

A=Accelaration

m=mass of the object

_____________________________

This equation says that with a constant mass, if the force is doubled, then the acceleration will be doubled (2F will cause 2a); if it is tripled, then the acceleration will be tripled (3F will cause 34); and so on.

From experience and observation, you know that if you have several objects of different masses and you apply the same force on each, the objects will not move at the same rate.

Why? The reason can be found in the second part of Newton's second law; that is, acceleration is inversely proportional to mass.

______________________________

This means that if the force is constant, an increase in mass will produce a corresponding decrease in acceleration. If F is constant, m will have an acceleration of a; 2m will have an acceleration of [tex] \frac{a}{2} [/tex] and [tex] \frac{m}{4} [/tex] will have an acceleration of 4a.

What is the acceleration of a ball with a mass of 0.40 kg is hit with a force of 16N?

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 0.4 kg

F ( Force ) = 16 N or 16 kg·m/s²

a ( acceleration ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula of Force ( F = m × a ), and to solve for acceleration we can have the formula ( a = F / m )

a = F / m

 = 16 kg·m/s² / 0.4 kg

 = 40 m/s²

Answer:

30. B. PROBLEM SOLVING. Solve the following problem. Show your solutions 1. A cabinet was moved using a force of 197N. It has a mass of 24 kg. Calculate the acceleration​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F = ma}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

Solving for a
