4. learning task 1: Match the definition in column A with the correct terms in column B. Column A: 1.) the capacity to undergo a change of physical form without breaking. 2.) the resistance of a material to deformation of an indenter of specific size and shape under a known load. 3.) Occurs between metals and nonmetals. 4.) the measure of the ease at which an electric charge or heat can pass through a material.5.) A property referring to the ability for a given substance,the solute, to dissolve in a solvent. Column B:A. Accuracy B. Conductivity C. Ductility D. SolubilityE. Boiling PointF. Ionic Bond G. Hardness​


Answer:

1. Ductility

2. Hardness

3. Ionic Bond

4. Conductivity

5. Solubility

Explanation:

1. Ductility is a mechanical property of a material to be amenable to drawing or be plastically deformed without breaking or fracture.

2. Hardness is the materials resistance to deformation. It is the ability of a material to resist penetration of its surface. Usually the harder a material is, the lower its ductility.

3. Ionic bond is formed when a metal and a non-metal exchange electrons. This is formed between a cation, which is usually from a metal, and an anion, which is usually from a non-metal.

4. Conductivity in metals is the measurement of a material’s ability to transmit heat or electricity.

5. Solubility is a property that refers to the ability of a solute to dissolve in a solvent. It is measured by the maximum amount of solute dissolved in a solvent at equilibrium resulting to a saturated solution.
