In most computer languages, equals sign (=) denotes an operation of assignment, which means making a value of the left part equal to the value of the right part, where parts are separated by the equals sign.

Equals sign repeated twice (==) is an operation that compares left part with right part, answering the question of whether these parts are equal or not.

There are languages that use different combination of symbols for the above operations. The reason of using two equal signs for comparison and one for assignment is that programmers use assignment significantly more often than comparison.

18. A baseball is thrown in the air and follows a parabolic trajectory. Point A is the maximum height while point B is the point just before the ball hits the ground. If air resistance is neglected, which of the following displays the correct relationship between the speeds and the acceleration at those two points?a. VA = VB and aA < aBb. VA > VB and aA = aBc. VA < VB and aA = aBd. VA < VB and aA > aB​


Answer:

D. VA < VB and aA > aB.

Explanation:

The correct relationship between the speeds and the acceleration at points A and B is option (d), VA < VB and aA > aB. At point A, the velocity of the ball is zero and its acceleration is the acceleration due to gravity pointing downward. At point B, the velocity of the ball is nonzero and its acceleration is also the acceleration due to gravity pointing downward. Since the ball is accelerating faster at point B than it is at point A, its velocity must be greater at point B than it is at point A.
