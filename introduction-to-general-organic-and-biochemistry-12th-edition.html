19. why is our knowledge and understanding of the characteristics of living matter/organisms important in the study of fisheries and biochemistry​


Answer:

Our knowledge and understanding of the characteristics of living matter and organisms are important in the study of fisheries and biochemistry for several reasons:

Understanding the characteristics of living organisms is crucial in fisheries to identify and manage fish populations effectively. This knowledge helps to determine the species, size, age, and health of fish populations, which is essential for developing sustainable management practices and ensuring the long-term viability of fisheries.

Biochemistry is the study of the chemical processes that occur within living organisms, including the structure and function of biomolecules such as proteins, nucleic acids, and carbohydrates. This knowledge is crucial in the study of fisheries because it helps to understand the metabolic processes that occur in fish, including the breakdown and synthesis of biomolecules, which is essential for developing effective feed formulations and improving fish growth and health.

Understanding the characteristics of living matter and organisms is also important in the study of fisheries and biochemistry because it helps to identify and address environmental threats to fish populations, such as pollution, climate change, and habitat degradation. This knowledge is essential for developing effective management strategies to protect and conserve fish populations.

In summary, our knowledge and understanding of the characteristics of living matter and organisms are essential for the study of fisheries and biochemistry because they provide insights into the biology, physiology, and ecology of fish populations, which is essential for developing effective management strategies and improving the health and productivity of fisheries.
