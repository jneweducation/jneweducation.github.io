Answer:

The Formula for Calculating the EMF

There are two main equations used to calculate EMF. The fundamental definition is the number of joules of energy each coulomb of charge picks up as it passes through the cell.

ε=EQ

ε electromotive force

E the energy in the circuit

Q Charge of the circuit.

If we know the resulting energy and the amount of charge passing through the cell. It is the simplest way to calculate the EMF.

Instead, we may use the definition more like the Ohm’s law i.e V = IR. So the formula is,

ε=I(R+r)

I Current

ε The electromotive force of cell.

R Resistance in the circuit.

r Internal resistance of a cell.

V Voltage

Now, expanding this:

ε=IR+Ir

ε=V+Ir

This shows that we can calculate the EMF if we know the voltage across the terminals, the current flowing and the internal resistance of the cell.

V = 3.2 V

I = 0.6 A

r = 0.5 ohm

Using the formula: ε=V+Ir

ε=3.2+0.6×0.5

= 3.2 V + 0.3 V

= 3.5 V

So the EMF of the circuit is 3.5 V.

The emf

22. An RL series circuit is connected to an AC generator with a maximum emf of 20V. If the maximum potential difference across the resistor is 16V, then the maximum potential difference across the inductor is:


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_{\text{S}} = \text{20 V} \\ & V_{\text{R}} = \text{16 V} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the voltage across the inductor.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{L}} & = \sqrt{V_{\text{S}}^2 - V_{\text{R}}^2} \\ & = \sqrt{(\text{20 V})^2 - (\text{16 V})^2} \\ & = \boxed{\text{12 V}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the voltage across the inductor is 12 V.
