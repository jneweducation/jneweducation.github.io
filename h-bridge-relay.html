2. The main cable of a suspension bridge forms a parabola, described by the equation y = a(x − h)2 + k. y =height in feet of the cable above the roadway x =horizontal distance in feet from the left bridge support a =a constant (h, k) =vertex of the parbola What is the vertex of the parbola?


Since we are given points, we can just substitute them into the equation:

y= a(x-h)^2 + k

If x = 0, we get 27 = ah^2 + k

If x  = 210, we get 27 = a(210-h)^2 + k


Equating these two, since they are both equal to 27, we get:

ah^2 + k = a(210-h)^2 + k.

Then we can subtract k from both sides, then divide both sides by a. We then get:

h^2 = (210-h)^2 = 210^2 - 420h + h^2

Then subtracting h^2 from both sides, and moving 420h to the other side, we get:

420h  =210^2

Now remember that 420 = 2*210, so we can cancel one 210 from each side

2h = 210

h = 105.


From the table, we can see that if x = 105, then y = 7.

So the answer is V = (105,7).


Another way to look at this is to just remember that if you "fold" the parabola in half, that's where the vertex is. This means that since we already found two points with the same y-value (0,27) and (210,27), the x-value of the vertex is the midpoint of 0 and 210, or 105. We get the same if we get the midpoint of the x-values of (52.5,12) and (157.6,12).

4. Charles walks over a railway-bridge. At the moment that he is just ten meters away from the middle of the bridge, he hears a train coming from behind. At that moment, the train, which travels at a speed of 90 km/h, is exactly as far away from the bridge as the bridge measures in length. Without hesitation, Charles rushes straight towards the train to get off the bridge. In this way, he misses the train by just four meters! If Charles would, however, have rushed exactly as fast in the other direction, the train would have hit him eight meters before the end of the bridge.


Answer:

Let the length of the bridge be x meters.

Running towards the train, Charles covers 0.5x-10 meters in the time that the train travels x-4 meters. Running away from the train, Charles covers 0.5x+2 meters in the time that the train travels 2x-8 meters.

Because their speeds are constant, the following holds:

(0.5x-10) / (x-4) = (0.5x+2) / (2x-8)

which can be rewritten to

0.5x2 - 24x + 88 = 0

Using the abc formula we find that x=44, so the railway-bridge has a length of 44 meters.

14. A train loaded with fuel, 120m long, travels from Laguna de Bay to Manila Bay, with a constant speed of 50 km/h. This train takes 15s to completely cross the bridge over the Pasig River. The bridge length is:​


The bridge is 88.3m long.

Hi! To resolve this issue, we must take into account that for the train to completely cross the bridge, it needs to completely cross the bridge, that is, exceed its 120m plus the distance from the bridge.

As it has a speed of 50 km/h (or 13.9 m/s) and takes 15 s, we have:

Vm = space/time

13.9 = space/15s

Space = 208.3m

We will have that the size of the bridge is given by the entire route traveled (208.3m) minus the size of the truck (120m), which gives us a value of 88.3 meters.
