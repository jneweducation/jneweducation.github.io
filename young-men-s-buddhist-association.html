Answer1. In a nutshell, explain how a Buddhist view that man is an embodiment of a spiritual?

In Buddhism, the soul of a man with good conduct "level-up" when he dies and is reborn again. If the person evil, the soul "level-down" in rebirth. For example, a poor person with good conduct becomes rich in reincarnation. Likewise, an evil person becomes an animal when he is reincarnated. In Buddhism, this is called Karma.
