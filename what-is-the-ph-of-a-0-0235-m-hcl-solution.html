2. What is the ph of a 0.0075 m solution of hcl?


SOLUTION:

The hydrogen or hydronium concentration of a strong monoprotic acid at equilibrium is equal to the initial concentration of the acid.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = -\text{log} \: [\text{H}^+] \\ & = -\text{log} \: (0.0075) \\ & = \boxed{2.12} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pH of a 0.0075 M solution of HCl is 2.12.

16. Calculate the pH during the titration of 30.00 mL of 0.1000 M KOH with 0.1000 M HCl solution after 25.00 mL of the titrant was added.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of HCl in 25.00 mL.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{25.00 mL} \times \frac{\text{0.1000 mol HCl}}{\text{1 L HCl}} \times \frac{\text{1 L}}{\text{1000 mL}} = 2.500 \times 10^{-3} \: \text{mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of KOH originally present in 30.00 mL.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{30.00 mL} \times \frac{\text{0.1000 mol KOH}}{\text{1 L KOH}} \times \frac{\text{1 L}}{\text{1000 mL}} = 3.000 \times 10^{-3} \: \text{mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the amount of KOH left after partial neutralization.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{amount of KOH left} & = 3.000 \times 10^{-3} \: \text{mol} - 2.500 \times 10^{-3} \: \text{mol} \\ & = 5.000 \times 10^{-4} \: \text{mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the OH⁻ concentration in 55.00 mL (30.00 mL + 25.00 mL) of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{OH}^-] & = \frac{5.000 \times 10^{-4} \: \text{mol KOH}}{\text{55.00 mL}} \times \frac{\text{1000 mL}}{\text{1 L}} \\ & = 9.091 \times 10^{-3} \: \text{mol KOH/L} \\ & = 9.091 \times 10^{-3} \: M \: \text{KOH} \\ & = 9.091 \times 10^{-3} \: M \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the pOH of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pOH} & = -\text{log} \: [\text{OH}^-] \\ & = -\text{log} \: (9.091 \times 10^{-3}) \\ & = 2.041 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the pH of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = 14 - \text{pOH} \\ & = 14 - 2.041 \\ & = \boxed{11.959} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pH of the solution is 11.959

18. What is the [OH-] of 0.050 M HCl solution?​

[OH-] = Kw / [H+]

26. 15 ml of a 0.105 m solution of naoh is titrated with 0.116 m of hcl solution. 5.0 ml of acid is added to the solution.what is the ph of the solution


Answer:

1. The initial pH, before the addition of strong base.

In dealing with strong acids and bases, assume that they dissociate completely in water.

[H+] = [HCl]

In this example, we have a 0.1 M solution of HCl, so the [H+] = 0.1 M

- log (0.1) = 1 = pH

2. The pH after addition of 10 mL of base.

We need to determine the moles of H+ remaining and the volume of the solution to calculate [H+].

moles = [concentration] x volume

moles OH- = (0.1 mol/L) x (0.010 L) = 0.0010 mol OH-

starting moles H+ = (0.1 mol/L) x (0.025 L) = 0.0025 mol H+

moles H+ remaining after addition of 10 mL base = 0.0025 – 0.0010 = 0.0015 mol H+

volume after addition of 10.0 mL base = 25.0 mL acid + 10.0 mL base = 35 mL = 0.035 L

[H+] = 0.0015 mol / 0.035 L = 4.28 x 10-2 M H+

pH = -log (4.28 x 10-2) = 1.37

3. The pH after addition of 20 mL of base.

The same steps are followed, as outlined above.

moles OH- = 0.0020

moles H+ remaining = 0.0025 – 0.0020 = 0.0005

volume = 25.0 mL + 20.0 mL = 45.0 mL

[H+] = 0.0005 / 0.045 L = .0111 M

pH = 1.95

4. pH at the equivalence point

moles OH- = 0.025

moles H+ remaining = 0.0025 – 0.0025 = 0

[H+] = [OH-] pH = 7.00

There is a large change in the pH at the equivalence point. The pH jumps from about 2 to 7.

5. pH after addition of 35.0 mL of base

After the equivalence point, there will be an excess of base, which will determine the pH.

moles OH- added = 0.0035 moles

moles H+ reacting with OH‑ = 0.0025

moles OH- remaining – 0.0035 – 0.0025 = 0.0010

volume = 25.0 mL + 35.0 mL = 60.0 mL

[OH-] = 0.0010 mol / 0.060 L = 0.01667 M

pOH = - log (1.67 x 10-2) = 1.78 pH = 12.22

Again, there is a large jump in the pH above the equivalence point (pH = 7.00 to 12.22)

29. What is the concentration of H+ in a 2.5 M HCl solution?


Answer:

The concentration of H+ ions in a 2.5 M HCl solution can be calculated using the molarity of the solution and the acid dissociation constant for HCl. The acid dissociation constant for HCl is known to be equal to 1 x 10^-7.

The concentration of H+ ions in a 2.5 M HCl solution can be calculated as follows:

HCl -> H+ + Cl-

The initial concentration of HCl is 2.5 M, and the acid dissociation constant for HCl is 1 x 10^-7. This means that the concentration of H+ ions in the solution is equal to the initial concentration of HCl multiplied by the acid dissociation constant, or 2.5 M * 1 x 10^-7 = 2.5 x 10^-7 M.

Thus, the concentration of H+ ions in a 2.5 M HCl solution is 2.5 x 10^-7 M.
