1. How is the subject Readings in Philippine History different from that of the subject Philippine History?​


Answer:

This course critically analyzes Philippine history from multiple perspectives through the lens of selected primary sources.  Priority will be given to primary sources that describe the important turning points in Philippine history from prehistoric times up to the contemporary period and articulate various perspectives. 

 

The approach, though historical, will deal with interdisciplinary subjects so as to broaden and deepen the student’s understanding of Philippine political, economic, social and cultural history and equip the learner with the competencies necessary to analyze and evaluate different types of information: print, visual and audio-visual, and quantitative.   The end goal is to develop the historical and critical consciousness of the students so that they will become versatile, broad-minded, morally upright and responsible citizens.

13. expectations in the subject: READINGS IN THE PHILIPPINE HISTORY​


Readings in Philippine History

My expectations are to develop:

An empathy and respect for the people being discussed and also for the diversity of the human experience and the human record. An awareness that “the past is a foreign country” but also remains present in our contemporary world. This awareness of the “otherness” and the presence of the past, which exist in some tension, serve as an antidote to presentism: Not everything can be understood solely in terms of what is manifest today. An awareness of the strengths and weaknesses of one’s own perspective and the perspectives of primary and secondary sources. Together these perspectives inform my understanding. A clear presentation of that understanding. By the end of this subject, I will be able to use effective, clear writing, including proper mechanics and citations, and can speak lucidly about that writing.

Why do I have these expectations?

We all live in the present and we plan for the future – but how do we understand where we’re going and what progress looks like? To know exactly where you’re going, you first need to understand where you have come from. For that, you need an appreciation of history.

21. what makes "reading in Philippine history" different from other Philippine history subject​


Answer:

"Reading in Philippine History" is a specialized course that focuses on the critical analysis and interpretation of primary sources in Philippine history. Unlike other courses in Philippine history that may rely heavily on secondary sources such as textbooks, journal articles, and monographs, "Reading in Philippine History" puts primary sources at the forefront of its curriculum.

Primary sources are firsthand accounts of historical events, including diaries, letters, official documents, photographs, and artifacts. By analyzing these sources, students can gain a deeper understanding of the historical context, the people involved, and the various perspectives and motivations that shaped Philippine history.

"Reading in Philippine History" also places a strong emphasis on developing critical thinking and research skills. Students learn how to evaluate the credibility and reliability of primary sources, and how to use them to construct evidence-based arguments and narratives.

Overall, "Reading in Philippine History" provides students with a unique and valuable perspective on Philippine history, allowing them to engage with the past in a more nuanced and critical way.

"Reading in Philippine history" is a specific course that focuses on the critical analysis of primary and secondary sources related to Philippine history. The course aims to develop students' skills in interpreting and evaluating various sources, such as letters, diaries, government documents, historical narratives, and oral traditions.

In this course, students are taught how to analyze and interpret historical texts critically. They learn how to identify bias, evaluate arguments, and assess the reliability of sources. Moreover, this course provides an opportunity for students to explore different perspectives and interpretations of Philippine history.

Compared to other Philippine history subjects, "Reading in Philippine history" is more focused on the development of analytical and critical thinking skills, rather than just the memorization of historical events. It emphasizes the importance of reading and interpreting primary sources, which is an essential skill for any historian or social scientist.
