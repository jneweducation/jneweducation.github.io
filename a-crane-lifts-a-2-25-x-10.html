5. A crane lifts a 85kg.mass a height of 7m.Calculate the gravitional potential energy gained by the mass.


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

A crane lifts a 85kg.mass a height of 7m.Calculate the gravitional potential energy gained by the mass.

Given Data:

m (mass) = 85 kg

d (distance) = 7 m

g (gravitational acceleration) = 9.8 m/s²

P.E. = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve the problem using P.E. = mass × gravity × height  (P.E.= mgh)

P.E.= mgh

     =  85 kg × 9.8 m/s² × 7 m

     = 5,831 kg.m²/s²

    =  5,831 J

Answer:

The gravitational potential energy is equal to 5,831 J or 5,831 kg.m²/s²

In gravitational potential energy, the more height and mass,  the more the gravitational potential energy. Gravitational potential energy is directly proportional to mass and height since gravity is constant 9.8 m/s².

8. 1. In a construction site, a crane is used to lift a 399-kg metal beam to a height of 10 meters in 0.75 minute. Calculate the power of the crane.​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power of an object doing a work W in a given time t is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

W = work

t = time

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the work

W = mgh

W = (399 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(10 m)

W = 39,102 J

Solving for the power

Note: 0.75 min = 45 s

[tex]P = \dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{39,102 J}}{\text{45 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{868.93 W}}[/tex]

Therefore, the power of the crane is 868.93 W.

10. A crane lifted a 95kg. mass with a PE of 280 J. Calculate the height


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The height or position of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{h = \frac{PE}{mg}}[/tex]

where:

h = height

PE = potential energy

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the height

[tex]h = \dfrac{PE}{mg}[/tex]

[tex]h = \dfrac{\text{280 J}}{(\text{95 kg})(\text{9.8 m/}\text{s}^{2})}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{h = \text{0.30 m}}[/tex]

Therefore, the height is 0.30 m.
