Answer:

yes, because In order to keep as much material out of the landfill as possible, it’s important for each of us to do our part. One of the ways to put that plan into action is through the 3 Rs of waste management — Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.

Reduce means to cut back on the amount of trash we generate.

Reuse means to find new ways to use things that otherwise would have been thrown out.

Recycle means to turn something old and useless (like plastic milk jugs) into something new and useful (like picnic benches, playground equipment and recycling bins).

Activity and Assessment (10 PTS EACH) In a study determining the effectiveness of special reading and writing program to non-readers of Grade 7 students of Lanzones National High School, researchers decide to conduct 1-hour remedial reading and 30 minutes writing activity every day to those identified non-readers and students who have difficulty in writing. Furthermore, the extent to which the program will be administered to the learners is determined, Write down at least 3 interventions might be used in this research


Answer:

Pre-assessment: Before the program begins, researchers might conduct a pre-assessment of the non-reader students' reading and writing abilities in order to establish a baseline for measuring progress. The assessment may include tests on reading comprehension, vocabulary, and writing skills.

Direct Instruction: The program will involve direct instruction in reading and writing skills, which will be delivered through a combination of individual and group activities. The reading instruction may include decoding, phonics, fluency, and comprehension strategies. The writing instruction may include grammar, punctuation, sentence structure, and composition.

Scaffolding: Researchers might use scaffolding techniques to support the students during the program. This might include providing students with a structured approach to reading and writing, using visual aids, providing explicit instruction, and building on students' existing knowledge. Additionally, the use of graphic organizers, sentence frames, and other tools can be used to help students organize and express their ideas in writing.

Progress Monitoring: Researchers will closely monitor the progress of the students during the program, using a variety of assessment tools, such as reading and writing fluency assessments, comprehension tests, and writing samples. This will allow them to track the effectiveness of the program and make adjustments as needed.

Feedback and Support: Researchers will provide feedback and support to the students throughout the program. This might include providing students with constructive feedback on their reading and writing, highlighting areas for improvement, and giving them strategies for addressing difficulties. Additionally, researchers may provide emotional support for the students who are struggling, and help them to build their confidence and self-esteem.
