16. A mine extracts 2 metric tons of coal in an hour. The mine uses ton of the extracted coal every hour to generate electricity for the mine and sells the rest. If t is the number of hours spent mining, which expression represents the amount of ore sold? How much ore can the mine sell after extracting ore for 12 hours?


Answer:

12 Tons of the ores

Step-by-step explanation:

If the mine only uses 1 ton every hour to generate electricity and acquire 2 tons of coal every hour, then they could sell 12 tons of the ore collected from the mining. Correct?

Answer:

a) f(t) = t

b) 12 tons

Step-by-step explanation:

With the given, we need to determine the amount created by hour. In order to get that, we get the difference between the amount produced and the amount used to retain it.

# of coal to be sold = (Extracted - Used per hour) * Number of hours

f(t) = (2 - 1) * t

f(t) = 1t

f(t) = t

We can then substitute t = 12 to the function in order to determine how much would be sold,

f(12) = 12

Therefore, the expression for the amount of ore sold is f(t) = t or simply t while the amount of ore sold after 12 hours is 12 tons.
