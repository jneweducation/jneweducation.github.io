3. The following are examples of application for logistic regression


Answer:••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••Examples of Application for Logistic Regression:

A credit card company wants to know whether transaction amount and credit score impact the probability of a given transaction being fraudulent. To understand the relationship between these two predictor variables and the probability of a transaction being fraudulent, the company can perform logistic regression.

The response variable in the model will be “fraudulent” and it has two potential outcomes:

The transaction is fraudulent.

The transaction is not fraudulent.

The results of the model will tell the company exactly how changes in transaction amount and credit score affect the probability of a given transaction being fraudulent. The company can also use the fitted logistic regression model to predict the probability that a given transaction is fraudulent, based on the transaction amount and the credit score of the individual who made the transaction.

1.) The variables in a study of a cause-and-effect relationship are called the independent and dependent variables.

•The independent variable is a cause.It's value is independent of other variables in your study.

•The dependent variable is the effect. It's value depends on changes in the independent variable .

2.) A regression line is a straight line that describes how a response variable y changes as an explanatory variable x changes.We often use a regression line to predict the value of y for a given value of x.

12. 14. Which two are examples of descriptive statistics?a. Mean and Standard Deviationc. Variance and Regression Analysisb. Median and Correlationd. Hypothesis TestingandHistograms​


Mean and standard deviation are examples of descriptive statistics.

Let's discuss about descriptive statistics.

What are descriptive statistics?

Descriptive statistics are used to summarize and organize a data set (a collection of data in the form of responses received after conducting a survey, or the results of observations made from a sample.

Types of descriptive statistics

There are three main types of descriptive statistics :

1. Distribution: The frequency of occurrence of each value. A dataset must have a distribution of values or scores. You can summarize the number of times a variable's value appears in a table or graph, either numerically or as a percentage.

2. Central tendency : This is related to the average of the obtained values. This measure of central tendency is useful for estimating the center or average of a data set. The mean, median, and mode are available methods.

3. Variability/Dispersion: This relates to how widely the values obtained are spread out. A measure of the variability of information about the degree of variability in the value of variables obtained after research or observation. Range, standard deviation, and variance represent different aspects of the value distribution.

These are advantages of descriptive statistics :

1. Descriptive statistics are very useful when presenting data.

Give an overview and explain how the information in the data sets works. Descriptive statistics should be able to provide an idea of what information can be obtained from your data. It's more interesting to use graphs and tables than just numbers.

2. What are the properties of the data.

Descriptive statistics also provide properties about the data used. This is important because the state data used affects any data analysis you perform. Understanding the characteristics can later lead to a more in-depth analysis and appropriate treatment.
