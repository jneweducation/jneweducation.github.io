5. A 2000 lb elevator is lifted upward with an acceleration of 4 ft/s2. What is the tension in the supporting cable?


Answer:

The tension in the supporting cable is 2248.45 lbs

Explanation:

Tension is a pulling force to move an object. Its unit is in Newtons (N) or pounds (lbs).

(Note: a free body diagram was illustrated below)

Applying Newton's Law of Motion to the problem, we use the formula

a. ∑Fy = ma

b. W = mg

where

Fy      is the sum of all vertical forces, unit is in Newtons (N) or

         pounds (lbs)

         Remember: Upward direction = positive

                             Downward direction = negative

m       is the mass of the object, unit is in kg

a        is the acceleration of the object, unit is in ft/[tex]s^2[/tex]

W       is the weight force, unit is in Newtons or pounds

g        is the acceleration due to gravity 32.2 ft/ [tex]s^2[/tex]

Given information

W = 2000 lbs

a = 4 ft/[tex]s^2[/tex]

T = ?

Solving the problem

1. Use the weight force formula to derive the formula for the mass

W = mg

m =  [tex]\frac{W}{g}[/tex]

2. Use Newton's law formula to solve for the tension

∑Fy = ma

Substitute the formula for m

∑Fy = [tex]\frac{W}{g} (a)[/tex]

T - W =  [tex]\frac{W}{g} (a)[/tex]

T = W +  [tex]\frac{W}{g} (a)[/tex]

T = 2000 +  [tex]\frac{2000}{32.2} (4)[/tex]

T = 2248.45 lbs

11. A large wrecking ball is held in place by two light cables. If the mass m of the wrecking ball is 4090 kg. What is a) the tension tb in the cable that makes an angle of 40 degree with the vertical? b) the tension ta in the horizontal cable?


Answer:

The tension force Tb is 62356.52 N and the tension force Ta is 4767.87 N.

Explanation:

First is to provide a FBD or Free Body Diagram for the problem, shown below  this page.

From the given body diagram, let's collect all the vertical forces acting on the wrecking ball.

Since Tb creates an angle of 40°, it has two forces acting on it, vertical and horizontal forces

∑Fy = 0:      this represents all forces along y-axis or vertical force.

Tb Sin40° + Ta Sin0° - Fw (wrecking ball) = 0

Fw represents the weight force of the wrecking ball with a formula of Fw=mg and since Sin0 = 0, we get:

Tb Sin40° + 0 - mg = 0      

Tb Sin40° + 0 - 4090(9.8) = 0

Tb = [tex]\frac{4090(9.8)}{Sin 40}[/tex]

Tb = 62356.52 N

∑Fx = 0:     this represent all forces acting along the horizontal or x-axis

- Tb Cos40 + Ta Cos0 = 0     the negative sign(-) represent the horizontal        

                                                force in angle 40° is pulling to the left.

- (62356.52)(Cos40°) + Ta = 0     since Cos0 = 1

After simplifying and using transposition, we get:

Ta = 4767.87 N

Therefore, the tension Ta is 4767.87 N and Tb is 62356.52 N.

Newton’s Laws

Let's refer to the angles of the traffic signals in relation to the vertically downward direction as alpha and beta, and the tension in the cable segments that the traffic signals are connected to as T1 and T2, respectively. The requirements for static equilibrium can be used to establish the equilibrium configuration of the system. This implies that the sum of the forces acting in the horizontal and vertical directions must be zero.

According to the horizontal axis, we have the following:

[tex]T1\cos(\alpha) + T2\cos(\beta) = 0[/tex]

According to the vertical axis, we have the following:

[tex]T1\sin(\alpha) - T2\sin(\beta) - T = 0[/tex]

where T represents the cable's tension.

We only have two equations and three unknowns (alpha, beta, and T), thus we need a third equation to solve for the unknowns. We can obtain this third equation by considering the prerequisite for static equilibrium in the horizontal direction at the top traffic light.

The top traffic light has the following equation:

[tex]T1 - T2*cos(\beta-\alpha) = 0.[/tex]

Now that we have three equations with three unknowns, we can find the values of T, alpha [tex](\alpha )[/tex], and beta [tex](\beta)[/tex]. We can utilize the following steps to find the unknowns:

In the first and second equations, replace the expression for T1 in terms of T2 from the third equation.The resulting set of equations for alpha [tex](\alpha)[/tex] and beta [tex](\beta)[/tex] must be solved.To find T, rewrite any of the initial equations using the values for alpha [tex](\alpha)[/tex] and beta [tex](\beta)[/tex].

An empty elevator weighing 100 kgs, stops at the third -level of a building and got 3 passengers weighing 200 kgs. Considering a single cable supporting the elevator cart, what is the *(a) tension of the cable if it is going up at an acceleration of 1m/s². * (b) tension of the cable if it is going down with the same acceleration of 1 m/s².

Note:

Total mass = 100 kg + 200 kg = 300 kg

(a) tension of the cable if it is going up at an acceleration of 1m/s²

Given Data:

m (mass) = 300 kg

g (acceleration due to gravity) = 9.8 m/s²

a(acceleration) = 1 m/s²

T (Tension) =  ? unknown

Solution:

T = mg + ma

  = (300 kg × 9.8 m/s²) + (300 kg ×  1 m/s²)

  = 2940 N + 300 N

  = 3,240 N

(b) tension of the cable if it is going down with the same acceleration of 1m/s²

Given Data:

m (mass) = 300 kg

g (acceleration due to gravity) = 9.8 m/s²

a(acceleration) = -1 m/s²

T (Tension) =  ? unknown

Solution:

T = mg + ma

  = (300 kg × 9.8 m/s²) + (300 kg ×  -1 m/s²)

  = 2940 N - 300 N

  =  2,640 N

Answer:

(a) 3,240 N going up

(b) 2,640 N going down
