14. Which is equivalent to 1 mole? a. 37.0 g of aluminum pan b. 0.18 g of diamond (pure C) c. 3.50 g of silicon chip d. 24.0 g of magnesium ribbon


Answer:

(d) 24.0 g of magnesium ribbon.

Explanation:

One mole of any substance is defined as the amount of that substance that contains the same number of entities (such as atoms, molecules, or ions) as there are in 12 grams of carbon-12. This number is known as Avogadro's number and is approximately equal to 6.02 x 10^23.

To determine which of the given options is equivalent to 1 mole, we need to calculate the molar mass (the mass of 1 mole of that substance) for each option.

a. The molar mass of aluminum is approximately 26.98 g/mol. Therefore, 37.0 g of aluminum is approximately 1.37 moles of aluminum.

b. The molar mass of diamond (pure C) is approximately 12.01 g/mol. Therefore, 0.18 g of diamond is approximately 0.015 moles of diamond.

c. The molar mass of silicon is approximately 28.09 g/mol. Therefore, 3.50 g of silicon is approximately 0.125 moles of silicon.

d. The molar mass of magnesium is approximately 24.31 g/mol. Therefore, 24.0 g of magnesium is approximately 0.987 moles of magnesium.

Therefore, the option that is equivalent to 1 mole is (d) 24.0 g of magnesium ribbon.

28. silicon dioxide made up of element silicon and oxygen contains 46.7% by mass of silicon with what mass of oxygen will 10 g of silicon combine ​


Answer:

The first step is to calculate the mass of silicon dioxide that contains 10 g of silicon. Since silicon makes up 46.7% of silicon dioxide by mass, we can use the following equation to find the mass of silicon dioxide:

mass of silicon dioxide = mass of silicon / fraction of silicon in silicon dioxide

mass of silicon dioxide = 10 g / 0.467

mass of silicon dioxide = 21.41 g

So, 10 g of silicon will combine with 21.41 g of silicon dioxide.

Now, we can use the molecular formula of silicon dioxide (SiO2) to calculate the mass of oxygen that is present in 21.41 g of silicon dioxide.

The molecular weight of SiO2 is 60.08 g/mol (28.09 g/mol for silicon and 31.99 g/mol for oxygen), so we can use the following equation to calculate the mass of oxygen:

mass of oxygen = (mass of silicon dioxide x fraction of oxygen in SiO2) / molecular weight of SiO2

mass of oxygen = (21.41 g x 0.533) / 60.08 g/mol

mass of oxygen = 0.190 g

Therefore, 10 g of silicon will combine with 0.190 g of oxygen to form 21.41 g of silicon dioxide.
