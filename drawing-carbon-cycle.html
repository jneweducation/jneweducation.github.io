3. Cask 3: In your notebook, draw the air cycle below. Then, in 4-5 sen-Oxygenkplain how air cycle occurs.PholosynthesisRespirationCarbonDioxideF THE VERB​


See Article History

Oxygen cycle, circulation of oxygen in various forms through nature. Free in the air and dissolved in water, oxygen is second only to nitrogen in abundance among uncombined elements in the atmosphere. Plants and animals use oxygen to respire and return it to the air and water as carbon dioxide (CO2). CO2 is then taken up by algae and terrestrial green plants and converted into carbohydrates during the process of photosynthesis, oxygen being a by-product. The waters of the world are the main oxygen generators of the biosphere; their algae are estimated to replace about 90 percent of all oxygen used. Oxygen is involved to some degree in all the other biogeochemical cycles. For example, over time, detritus from living organisms transfers oxygen-containing compounds such as calcium carbonates into the lithosphere.

Air Cycle

The air cycle starts from pollution such as factory smoke, car smoke, and organism respiration residue that contains [tex]CO_{2}[/tex]. The pollutants will be absorbed by plants through plant chlorophyll. Then plants process [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] into [tex]O_{2}[/tex]  through photosynthesis, which [tex]O_{2}[/tex] will be inhaled by humans and animals, and humans release [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] which will be absorbed again by plants.

Explanation:

The air cycle is also called the carbon cycle or biogeochemical cycle in which carbon is exchanged between the biosphere, geosphere, hydrosphere, and Earth's atmosphere. In this cycle, there are four main carbon reservoirs connected by exchange pathways. These reservoirs are the atmosphere, terrestrial biosphere, oceans, and sediments. The annual movement of carbon, the exchange of carbon between reservoirs, occurs due to a variety of chemical, physical, geological, and biological processes.

Carbon can be found in the atmosphere as carbon dioxide ([tex]CO_{2}[/tex]), in the tissues of all living things, and is mostly found in sedimentary rocks and fossil fuels found in the bowels of the earth. Green plants and animals and other organisms play an active role in the continuity of the carbon cycle. With the help of light energy, [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] is one of the main components of photosynthesis to take place. With the help of light energy, [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] and [tex]H_{2} O[/tex] by green plants will be converted into organic compounds in the form of glucose ([tex]C_{6}H_{12}O_{6}[/tex]) and Oxygen ([tex]O_{2}[/tex]) through a simplified reaction as follows:

6 [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] + 6 [tex]H_{2} O[/tex] ↔ [tex]C_{6}H_{12}O_{6}[/tex] + 6 [tex]O_{2}[/tex]

The oxygen produced in photosynthesis will be used by animals and other organisms for respiration. The respiration process will produce [tex]CO_{2}[/tex], [tex]H_{2} O[/tex] and energy through a simplified reaction equation as follows:

[tex]C_{6}H_{12}O_{6}[/tex] + 6     [tex]O_{2}[/tex] ↔ 6 [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] + 6 [tex]H_{2} O[/tex] + Energy

The [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] produced in respiration will be released back into the environment, then it will be used for photosynthesis of green plants and so on. From these two activities, it appears that photosynthesis and respiration work together for the continuity of the carbon and oxygen cycle. Some carbon is temporarily in plant or animal tissue, but the carbon will return to the cycle after the plant or animal dies and then decomposed by decomposers.

If the remains of organic matter from the decay of animals and plants are buried in the soil layer for more than 600 million years, the carbon contained will be released from the main carbon cycle, but by heat and pressure in the earth's crust, these substances will be converted into fossil fuels. such as coal, oil, and natural gas. If these fossil fuels are used as fuel in various industries, the carbon contained will be released back into the environment and will be reused by green plants for photosynthesis. Through the process of photosynthesis, green plants play a role in the carbon cycle.
