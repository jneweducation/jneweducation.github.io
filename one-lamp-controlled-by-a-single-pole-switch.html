18. 2. Two bulbs connected in series controlled by a single pole switch using direct current as source​


Answer:

When two bulbs are connected in series using direct current as a source and controlled by a single pole switch, the following happens:

The switch controls the flow of current to both bulbs at the same time.Because the bulbs are connected in series, the current flowing through both bulbs is the same.The voltage across each bulb is divided equally between them, which means that each bulb receives half the voltage of the total voltage supplied by the DC source.The brightness of each bulb depends on its resistance. If one bulb has a higher resistance than the other, it will glow less brightly than the other bulb.If one bulb burns out, the circuit will be broken, and both bulbs will stop glowing.

20. 10 steps how to install 2 bolbs in single pole switch and 1 bolb in single pole switch​


For 2 bulbs in a single pole switch:

Turn off the power supply to the switch by switching off the circuit breaker or removing the fuse from the electrical panel.Remove the cover plate from the switch to access the wiring.Identify the hot wire (usually black) and the neutral wire (usually white) in the electrical box.Connect the hot wire to the common terminal on the switch.Connect one of the bulb wires to one of the switch terminals and the other bulb wire to the other switch terminal.Connect the neutral wire to the remaining terminal on the bulb sockets.Carefully tuck the wires back into the box and attach the switch to the box with screws.Replace the cover plate.Turn the power supply back on and test the switch and bulbs.If everything works properly, secure the switch in place and close the electrical box.

For 1 bulb in a single pole switch:

Turn off the power supply to the switch by switching off the circuit breaker or removing the fuse from the electrical panel.Remove the cover plate from the switch to access the wiring.Identify the hot wire (usually black) and the neutral wire (usually white) in the electrical box.Connect the hot wire to the common terminal on the switch.Connect the bulb wire to one of the switch terminals.Connect the neutral wire to the remaining terminal on the switch.Carefully tuck the wires back into the box and attach the switch to the box with screws.Replace the cover plate.Turn the power supply back on and test the switch and bulb.If everything works properly, secure the switch in place and close the electrical box.
