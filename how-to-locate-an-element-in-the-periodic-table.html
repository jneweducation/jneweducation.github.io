Answer:

1. In 1869, Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev created the framework that became the modern periodic table, leaving gaps for elements that were yet to be discovered. While arranging the elements according to their atomic weight, if he found that they did not fit into the group he would rearrange them.

2. The chemical elements are arranged from left to right and top to bottom in order of increasing atomic number, or the number of protons in an atom's nucleus, which generally coincides with increasing atomic mass.

3.In chemistry, a group (also known as a family) is a column of elements in the periodic table of the chemical elements. There are 18 numbered groups in the periodic table; the f-block columns (between groups 3 and 4) are not numbered.

4. Groups and periods are two ways of categorizing elements in the periodic table. Periods are horizontal rows (across) the periodic table, while groups are vertical columns (down) the table. Atomic number increases as you move down a group or across a period.

5. All the members of a family of elements have the same number of valence electrons and similar chemical properties.

The periodic table has gone through many changes since Dmitri Mendeleev drew up its original design in 1869, yet both the first table and the modern periodic table are important for the same reason: The periodic table organizes elements according to similar properties so you can tell the characteristics of an element just by looking at its location on the table.

Scientists use the periodic table to quickly refer to information about an element, like atomic mass and chemical symbol. The periodic table's arrangement also allows scientists to discern trends in element properties, including electronegativity, ionization energy, and atomic radius.

Another useful feature of the periodic table is that most tables provide all the information you need to balance chemical reactions at a glance. The table tells each element's atomic number and usually its atomic weight. The typical charge of an element is indicated by its group.

15. 1) Where is the atomic number located in the periodic table of elements? 2) What sub-atomic particles are associated with the mass number? 3) How is the number of neutron determined given the mass number? 4)What is an isotope?nonsense answer=report​


Answer:

1. The periodic table displays all of the known elements and is arranged in order of increasing atomic number. In this table, an element's atomic number is indicated above the elemental symbol. Hydrogen, at the upper left of the table, has an atomic number of 1. Every hydrogen atom has one proton in its nucleus.

2.The number of neutrons is called the neutron number and can be found by subtracting the proton number from the atomic mass number. The neutrons in an element determine the isotope of an atom, and often its stability. The number of neutrons is not necessarily equal to the number of protons.

28. Guide Questions: 1. What is the trend of atomic number across a period? 2. What are the Alkali metals? 3. How are elements arranged in the periodic table? 4. What are the properties of Noble Gasses? 5. What are metalloids? 6. What are the Representative Elements and Transition Elements in the periodic table? 7. Where are nonmetals located on the periodic table? 8. What are the properties of nonmetals? 9. What are the properties of metals?​


1.) What is the trend of atomic number across a period?

The periodic table shows an increase in atomic number across a period.

2.) What are the Alkali metals?

Alkali metals are elements in Periodic Table Group 1 that include Lithium (Li), Sodium (Na), Pótassium (K), Rubidium (Rb), Cesium (Cs), and Francium (Fr). They are highly reactive and have only one valence electron.

3.) How are elements arranged in the periodic table?

The periodic table arranges elements in increasing atomic number order, which reflects the amount of protons in anatom's nucleus.

4.) What are the properties of Noble Gasses?

The noble gases are a group of elements in Periodic Table Group 18 that include helium (He), neon (Ne), argon (Ar), krypton (Kr), xenon (Xe), and radon (Rn). They have no odor, no color, and are normally unreactive.

5.) What are metalloids?

Metalloids are elements that have both metal and nonmetal characteristics. They are found in the periodic table along the diagonal line that separates metals from nonmetals.

6.) What are the Representative Elements and Transition Elements in the periodic table?

Representative elements are those found in Periodic Table Groups 1, 2, and 13-18, which comprise alkali metals, alkaline earth metals, and p-block elements. The elements in Groups 3-12, which include the d-block elements, are transition elements.

7.) Where are nonmetals located on the periodic table?

Nonmetals are found in Groups 14-18 of the periodic table, to the right of the table.

8.) What are the properties of nonmetals?

Nonmetals have low melting and boiling temperatures, are poor heat and electricity conductors, and tend to generate anions in chemical processes.

9.) What are the properties of metals?

Metals have high melting and boiling temperatures, are good conductors of heat and electricity, and tend to generate cations in chemical processes.
