WHAT IS CARBON FIBERS? AND ITS DEFINITION?

Carbon fibers or carbon fibres are fibers about 5 to 10 micrometers in diameter and composed mostly of carbon atoms. Carbon fibers have several advantages including high stiffness, high tensile strength, low weight to strength ratio, high chemical resistance, high temperature tolerance and low thermal expansion.

7. what is the chemical formula of carbon fiber​


carbon fiber (alternately called graphite fiber) is a polymer (made from another polymer, called polyacrylonitrile) which is a form of graphite material consisting of extremely thin fibers about 0.005-0.010 mm in diameter and composed mostly of carbon atoms. the carbon atoms are bonded together in microscopic crystals (hexagonal aromatic rings) that are more or less aligned parallel to the long axis of the fiber. The crystal alignment makes the fiber incredibly strong for its size. Several thousand carbon fibers are twisted together to form a yarn, which may be used by itself or woven into a fabric. the density of carbon fiber is also considerably lower than the density of steel making it ideal for applications requiring low weight. the properties of carbon fiber such as high tensile strength, low weight, and low thermal expansion make it very popular in aerospace, military, and motorsports along with other competition sports.

these fibers are not used by themselves, instead, they're used to reinforce materials like epoxy resins and other thermosetting materials. We call these reinforced materials composites because they have more than one component.

Carbon is a versatile element that can be used in a wide range of applications. Some other things that can be developed with carbon include:

Batteries: Carbon-based materials are used in the production of batteries, such as lithium-ion batteries, due to their high surface area and conductivity.

Solar cells: Carbon-based materials can be used in the production of solar cells due to their ability to conduct electricity and absorb light.

Lubricants: Carbon-based lubricants can be used to reduce friction and wear in machinery.

Ink: Carbon black is a common pigment used in ink, especially for printing.

Medical implants: Carbon-based materials, such as carbon nanotubes, can be used to develop medical implants due to their biocompatibility and strength.

Water filtration: Activated carbon is an effective material for filtering water due to its ability to adsorb impurities.

Heat sinks: Carbon-based materials can be used as heat sinks in electronic devices to dissipate heat.

Rocket fuel: Oxygen is used as an oxidizer in rocket fuel to help combustion and increase the burn rate.
