3. Determine whether the events are independ or dependent. Then, find the probability. ). A toy box contains 12 toys, 8 stuffed ani 3 board games. Maria randomly chooses for the child she is babysitting What is the probability that she chove animals as the first two choices. p.².​


Answer:

The events are dependent because Maria is choosing without replacement.

The probability of choosing a stuffed animal on the first choice is 8/12.

After choosing a stuffed animal, there are only 7 stuffed animals left out of a total of 11 toys, so the probability of choosing another stuffed animal on the second choice is 7/11.

Therefore, the probability of choosing stuffed animals as the first two choices is:

(8/12) * (7/11) = 56/132 ≈ 0.4242 ≈ 42.42%

total number of choices = 23
probability of picking a stuffed animal = 8/23
probability of choosing a second stuffed animal = 7/22
Note: picking 2 objects simultaneously and picking 2 objects one after another is the same
probability of simultaneously picking 2 stuffed animals = 8/23 x 7/22 = 56/506
