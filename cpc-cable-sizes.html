12. Activity 1 1. What is the first thing to do when skinning cables? 2. In splicing cable, how long is the standard size of the cable to be skinned? Activity 2 What are the techniques in skinning wires using: A. Knife B.Pliers C. Wire Stripper​


Answer:

Activity 1:

1. The first thing to do when skinning cables is to ensure that the power is off and the cable is not connected to any electrical source.

2.The standard size of the cable to be skinned in splicing is around 1 inch.

Activity 2:

A. Techniques in skinning wires using a knife:

Use a sharp and sturdy knife.

Score the insulation gently around the circumference of the cable.

Hold the cable firmly and cut through the insulation with the knife.

Peel off the insulation carefully using the knife.

B. Techniques in skinning wires using pliers:

Hold the wire with the pliers and locate the insulation to be removed.

Apply gentle pressure with the pliers to break the insulation.

Carefully pull the insulation off with the pliers.

C. Techniques in skinning wires using a wire stripper:

Select the appropriate wire gauge size on the stripper.

Place the wire inside the wire stripper.

Squeeze the stripper tightly and pull it away from the wire to remove the insulation

20. Does the sizes of wires and cables used matter in energy transmission?


Answer:

Yes the sizes of wires matter in energy transmission.

Please refer to the explanation part below for the explanation to the answer.

Explanation:

Electricity powers almost everything inside your house; your television, computer, and kitchen appliances. Before the electricity can power all of those things, it first came from a power source called power plant.

The electrical energy coming from power plants/ stations passes through an extensive network of conducting cables and wires which forms the distribution systems and electricity transmission.

The size of wires and cables used in energy transmission is vital.

Electricity travels more easily using a conductor or elements having high conductivity. Good conductors are copper, silver or gold.  

If less conductive the material is used, a larger the wire/ cable diameter is needed in order to carry the same current load.  

It is the job of engineers to select the right wire diameter for electrical transmission. The selection of the appropriate size of wires and cables can reduce energy loss during transmission, and prevents accidents and hazards.

Aside from the computing the appropriate sizes, here are some other factors that are being considered in selecting materials for transmission wires:

Quality of material - material with good quality refers to the purity of the material. Since wires are made out of elements, it is hard to get 100% pure of this kind of material. Suppliers usually treat the elements chemically in order to wash out impurities that cling to the mined elements. This method will help the quality of the material increase.Resistivity - resist the excitation of electrons in the wire's conductor material. Electrons' excitation usually results in heat generation, and eventually causing loss of efficiency.

27. Activity 1 1. What is the first thing to do when skinning cables? 2. In splicing cable, how long is the standard size of the cable to be skinned? Activity 2 What are the techniques in skinning wires using: A. Knife B.Pliers C. Wire Stripper​


Answer:

Activity 1:

1. The first thing to do when skinning cables is to ensure that the power source is turned off and the cables are safe to handle.

2. There is no standard size for cable to be skinned during splicing, as it will depend on the specific application and type of cable being used.

Activity 2:

A. Knife:

- Use a sharp, high-quality knife specifically designed for wire stripping

- Score the insulation along the length of the wire without cutting the metal conductor

- Gently pull the insulation away from the wire

B. Pliers:

- Use a pair of wire strippers with the appropriate size notch for the wire being stripped

- Place the wire into the notch and squeeze the stripper handles together

- Gently pull the insulation away from the wire

C. Wire Stripper:

- Use a wire stripper with the appropriate size and gauge range for the wire being stripped

- Place the wire into the stripper and adjust the cutting depth if needed

- Squeeze the stripper handles together to cut through the insulation and remove it from the wire
