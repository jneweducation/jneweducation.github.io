Answer:

1.  The two drums -snare drum and bass drum

2. Both drums are made of wood and calfskin, and are also played with two sticks.

3. They differ in many ways, the snare drum has a cylindrical shape and is made of wood/brass covered at each end with tightly stretched calfskin, the side on which the player beats is caled "batter head" while the lower side is called "snare head" , bass drum on the other hand, is made of a wooden shell covered with calfskin which can be tightened by metal rods. The sticks used in snare drum are rather thin with small wooden heads unlike the sticks used in bass drum, which is heavier and have larger heads made of felt or lamb's wool.

4. The transition/signal word used in indicating the similarities is 'both'.

5. The transition/signal words used in indicating the differences are 'however' 'while' and 'on the other hand'.
