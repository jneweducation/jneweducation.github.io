[tex]( - 25 \: c \times \frac{9}{5} ) + 32 \\ \\ = ( - 5 \times 9) + 32 \\ \\ = - 45 + 32 \\ \\ = - 13 \: f[/tex]

Answer:

15 °C = 59 °F

When you are asking to convert 15 C to F, you are asking to convert 15 degrees Celsius to degrees Fahrenheit.

Here we will show you how to convert 15 C to F so you know how hot or cold 15 degrees Celsius is in Fahrenheit.

The C to F formula is (C × 9/5) + 32 = F. When we enter 15 for C in the formula, we get (15 × 9/5) + 32 = F.

To solve (15 × 9/5) + 32 = F, we first multiply 9 by 15, then we divide the product by 5, and then finally we add 32 to the quotient to get the answer. Here is the math to illustrate:

15 x 9 = 135

135 / 5 = 27

27 + 32 = 59

Therefore, the answer to 15 C to F is 59 which can be written as follows:

15 °C = 59 °F

Step-by-step Solution:

Given:

f(x)=x/25 where f(x)=y so y=x/25

Solution:

Interchange y and x

y=x/25 >> x=y/25

Solve for y (To solve for y, multiply both sides by 25)

x=y/25

25(x=y/25)25

25x=y >> y=25x >> f(x)=25x

Explanation:

The inverse of the function is the opposite of the function. If the operation in the funtion is division then the inverse is multiplication and vice versa. If the operation is addition then the inverse is subtraction and vice versa. This does not apply to all functions though. Just functions with this format.
