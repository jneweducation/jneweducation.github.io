Some tips to remember for the number system:

- Natural numbers or counting numbers, are numbers that start from 1 up to infinite, which doesn't include 0.

Examples: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...

- Whole numbers are numbers that include 0 and natural numbers, in short, it starts from 0 up to infinite.

Examples: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...

- Integers are numbers that include whole numbers and negative numbers, simply from negative infinite to positive infinite.

Examples: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

- Rational Numbers is a number system which includes fractions, integers, and decimals, which can be terminating, repeating, or it can be both, including divisible square roots. These numbers can be written in simple fractions.

Examples: 2.555..., 0.25, -6, 3, [tex]\frac{-1}{2}[/tex], [tex]\frac{-4}{-5}[/tex], [tex]\frac{5}{-6}[/tex], [tex]\sqrt{4}[/tex], and many more.

- Irrational Numbers are numbers that includes indivisible square roots, non-terminating and repeating decimals. These numbers cannot be written in simple fractions.

Examples: π or pi (3.14159265358...), e or Euler's Number (2.718281828459...), [tex]\sqrt{6}[/tex], 8.649371284..., and many more.

- Real Numbers are numbers that includes all numbers, which are Irrational, Rational, Integers, Whole Numbers, and Natural Numbers.
