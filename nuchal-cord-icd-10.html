3. The nurse assesses a patient for signs of meningeal irritation and observes for nuchal rigidity. what indicates the presence of this sign of meningeal irritation


Answer:

Nuchal rigidity is a clinical manifestation of meningitis. During assessment, the patient will resist passive flexion of the neck by the health care provider. Tonic spasms of the legs, curling in a fetal position, and arching of the neck and back are not related to meningeal irritation.

1. Mary are going to make a macramé plant hanger with 8 pcs of 3mm cord with the length of 4 yards and 10 inches and an added cord of 2 ft. in length for wrapping knot, how much macramé cord does she need Express your answer in yard.

GIVEN: 8pcs of 3mm cord = 4yards and 10 inches.

Added cords wrapping knot = 2ft.

CONVERSIONS:

a. 1 yard = 36 inches

b. 1ft. = 12 inches

SOLUTION 1:

4 yards= 36 inches x 4

= 144 inches + 10 inches

= 154 inches (total cord for 3 mm)

SOLUTION 2:

total and 3mm x 8 pcs cords

= 154 inches x 8 pcs cords

=1,232 inches.

SOLUTION 3:

Total cords + 2ft added cords for wrapping knot (convert 2ft in inches =24 inches)

= 1, 232 + 24 inches

= 1,256 inches

ANSWER: 1,256 INCHES TO YARDS

= 34.89 YARD

6. 3-ply rope

- is cord made three entwined strands which is sturdier than single twisted string and therefore easier to knot as a beginner

- the yarn or thread composing the strands of a rope , a yarn of fibers loosely twisted up right- handedly
