22. Cameron works at an electronics store as a salesperson. Cameron earns a 4% commission on the total dollar amount of all phone sales he makes, and earn a 6% commission on all computer sales. Cameron made a total of $3600 in sales and earned $178 in commission. Write a system of equations that could be used to determine the dollar amount of phone sales Cameron made and the dollar amount of computer sales he made. Define the variables that you use to write the system.​


VARIABLES

[tex]\sf{===========================================}[/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{ A} {\large \mathrm { NSWER: }}}} \\[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{Let $\boxed{x}=\boxed{\textsf{The total dollar amount of all phone sales}}$}\:.[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{Let $\boxed{y}=\boxed{\textsf{The total dollar amount of all computer sales}}$}\:.[/tex]

[tex] \textsf{System of Equations} : [/tex]

[tex]\qquad\boxed{ \bold{ \: \: x + y = 3600 \: \: } }[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\boxed{ \bold{ \: \: 0.04x + 0.06y = 178 \: \: }} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\sf{===========================================}[/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{S} {\large \mathrm {OLUTION : }}}} \\[/tex]

[tex] \textsf{Given Information:}[/tex]

4% commission on the total dollar amount of all phone sales.6% commission on all computer sales.Total sales = $3600Total commission = $178

[tex] \textsf{Define the variables:}[/tex]

Let x = The total dollar amount of all phone sales.Let y = The total dollar amount of all computer sales.

[tex] \textsf{Convert the percentages into decimal form:}[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies \sf 4\%=\dfrac{4}{100}=0.04[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies \sf 6\%=\dfrac{6}{100}=0.06 \\ [/tex]

Therefore, the system of equations that could be used to determine the dollar amount of phone sales and computer sales is:

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{cases} \sf{x + y = 3600}\\ \sf{0.04x + 0.06y = 178}\end{cases}\end{gathered} [/tex]

[tex] \textsf{Solving the system of equations}[/tex]

[tex] \textsf{Rewrite the first equation to isolate y:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf\implies x=3600-y\\[/tex]

Substitute the expression for x into the second equation and solve for y:

[tex] \sf\implies 0.04(3600-y)+0.06y=178[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies 144-0.04y+0.06y=178[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies 144+0.02y=178[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies 0.02y=34[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies y=1700\\[/tex]

Therefore, Cameron made $1,700 of computer sales.

Substitute the found value of y into the first equation and solve for x:

[tex] \sf\implies x+1700=3600[/tex]

[tex] \sf\implies x=1900 \\ [/tex]

Therefore, Cameron made $1,900 of phone sales.

29. The average net sales per year of the products in 60 branches of D. G. Company is 85 million with a standard deviation of 15 million. Illustrate the area in normal curve and calculate the percentage. Determine how many branches have a sale of​.


To illustrate the area in the normal curve and calculate the percentage, we can use the z-score formula:

z = (x - μ) / σ

where x is the net sales of a branch, μ is the mean net sales of 85 million, and σ is the standard deviation of 15 million.

To determine how many branches have a sale of a certain amount, we need to find the corresponding z-score and then use a standard normal distribution table to find the percentage of branches below that z-score.

For example, let's say we want to find the percentage of branches that have a net sale of at least 100 million. Using the z-score formula:

z = (100 - 85) / 15 = 1

The z-score of 1 corresponds to a percentage of 84.13% from the standard normal distribution table.

Therefore, approximately 84.13% of the branches have a net sale of at least 100 million.

Similarly, if we want to find the percentage of branches that have a net sale between 70 million and 80 million, we can find the corresponding z-scores for each value:

z1 = (70 - 85) / 15 = -1

z2 = (80 - 85) / 15 = -0.33

Using the standard normal distribution table, we can find that the percentage of branches with a net sale below 70 million is approximately 15.87%, and the percentage of branches with a net sale below 80 million is approximately 37.47%. Therefore, the percentage of branches with a net sale between 70 million and 80 million is approximately 37.47% - 15.87% = 21.6%.

To determine the exact number of branches that fall within a certain net sale range or above a certain net sale threshold, we would need to know the total number of branches in the company and the net sales of each individual branch.
