The assets and owner's equity are reported at fair value but business obligations are reported at historical cost. 3. The company maintains only one employee to function as accountant, internal auditor, collector and treasurer. 4. The financial reports are always delayed. 5. Due to recession, estimated future earnings are reported in the current financial reports of Prospere General Merchandise. 6. The accounting methods and procedures used are regularly changed semi annually. 7. The accountant records transactions as desired by the owner without proper supporting documents. 8. The financial reports do not include minutes of the meeting, business con tracts, and future plans because they not financial in nature. 9. The company refuse to be audited by an external auditor because it has already an internal auditor who religiously verifies the financial reports. 10. The company acquired a refillable pentel amounting to P200 and depreciated it because its estimated life is good for five years.​


Answer:

slayed slay slay slay slay slay slay slay

The situation described appears to be a case of poor accounting practices at Prospere General Merchandise. Here is my analysis and justification of each point:

The fact that the financial reports are based on SFAS promulgated by the ASC is a positive sign, as it indicates that the company is following generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP).Reporting assets and owner's equity at fair value but business obligations at historical cost is not necessarily a problem, as this is a common practice in accounting. However, it may be worth examining the company's specific reasoning for this choice.The fact that the company has only one employee serving as accountant, internal auditor, collector, and treasurer is a red flag. It is highly unlikely that this individual has the necessary skills and expertise to perform all of these roles effectively, which could lead to errors, fraud, or other issues.The consistent delay of financial reports suggests that the company lacks a proper accounting system or process. This could indicate a lack of organization, resources, or commitment to financial transparency.Reporting estimated future earnings in the current financial reports is not acceptable under GAAP, as financial statements are meant to reflect past performance and current conditions, not future projections.Changing accounting methods and procedures regularly can create confusion and inconsistency in financial reporting, which can make it difficult for stakeholders to understand the company's performance and financial position.Recording transactions without proper supporting documents is a serious breach of accounting ethics and can lead to inaccurate financial reporting.While minutes of meetings, business contracts, and future plans may not be financial in nature, they can provide important context and insight into the company's operations and performance.Refusing to be audited by an external auditor is a red flag, as it suggests a lack of transparency and accountability. While having an internal auditor is important, an external audit provides an objective and independent review of the company's financial reporting.Depreciating a refillable pen over five years is not a material error, but it is an indication that the company may not have a clear understanding of accounting principles and the concept of materiality.
