What is latitude and longitude?

The lines that are horizontal and show how far a location is from the equator is called latitudes. Longitudes are vertical lines that show the east and west directions with relation to the meridian at Greenwich, England. For geographers, cartographers, and others, latitude and longitude can be used to identify specific points or locations on the globe.

For measuring and conveying locations directly on the Earth like latitude and longitude, the geographic coordinate system uses spherical or ellipsoidal coordinates. The majority of other spatial reference systems are built on this one since it is the most basic, traditional, and often used one.

Latitude lines go east and west parallel to the equator from the zero degree latitude point (0 degrees latitude). Latitude lines are measured in degrees north or south of the equator to 90 degrees at the North or South poles.

Longitude is calculated using imaginary lines that circle the planet vertically (up and down) and converge at the North and South Poles. Meridians are the name for these lines.
