1. Approximate the absolute pressure at the bottom of a freshwater lake at a point whose depth is 27.5 m.


Answer:

[tex]P_{abs} = 371.1kPa[/tex]

Explanation:

[tex]P_{abs} = P_{g} + P_{atm}[/tex]

[tex]P_{abs} = pgh + P_{atm}[/tex]

[tex]P_{abs} = (1000)(9.81)(27.5) + 101.325[/tex]

[tex]P_{abs} = 371.1kPa[/tex]

The absolute pressure at the bottom of lake at 27.5m is 371.1kPa.


2. what are the total force and the absolute pressure on the bottom of a swimming pool 22.0 m by 8.5 m whose inform depth is 2.0 m? What will be the pressure against the side of the pool near the bottom?


Find the pressure:
(Note: Absolute Pressure is the total pressure of the liquid + the pressure exerted by the atmosphere.)

Pressure absolute = Pressure atm + density x acceleration due to gravity x depth
(note atm means atmosphere)

P = 1.103x10^5Pa (101325 Pa)  + 1000 kg/m3 x 9.8 m/s2 x 2.0 m 
= 101325 Pa + 19600 N/m2
= 120925 Pa or 1.21 x 10^5 Pa

Find Total Force:
(Note: Total force is the total weight of the water)

Mass = Density of Water x Volume 
m = 1000 kg/m3 x (22.0 m x 8.5 m x 2.0 m)
m = 1000 kg/m3 x 374 m3
m = 374 000 kg or 3.74 x 10^5 m3

Weight = Mass x Acceleration due to Gravity
W= 374 000 kg x 9.8 m/s
W = 3 665 200 Newtons or 3.67 x 10^6 N

(b) Suppose an object of mass and density less than the density of water is placed into the tank and floats. No water overflows. What is the resulting increase in pressure at the bottom of the tank?


Answer:

[tex]{p}^{1 } = \frac{mg}{a} [/tex]

Explanation:

Area of bottom of tank = a

Water filled upto depth = h

Mass of object = m

Let density of object = ρo

Density of water = ρ.

Since the

[tex]p0 < p.[/tex]

so the object float in water as shown in the image.

Now, initial pressure (p) at bottom of tank:

[tex]p = p0 + pgh[/tex]

When mass is placed in water let'd be the height g of an object it gets submerged so mass is displaced water:

[tex]pw \: pg \: = mg\\ \\ v = \frac{m}{pw} [/tex]

or

[tex]a \times d = \frac{m}{pw} \\ \\ d = \frac{m}{apw} [/tex]

[V= volume of an object submerged]

now, increase pressure at bottom is:

[tex] {p}^{1} = pwg \times d \\ \\ = pw \times g \times \frac{m}{apw} [/tex]

[tex] {p}^{1 } = \frac{mg}{a} [/tex]

6. he g A vessel has a pressure of 276 kPa. The atmospheric pressure is 11 m of water equivalent. Find the absolute pressure in meter of water.


Explanation:

Formulas:

Pa = Pg + Patm

where:

Pa = absolute pressure

Pg = gage pressure

Patm = atmospheric pressure

P = yh

where:

P = pressure

y = unit weight (9.81 kN/m³ for water)

h = height

Given:

Pg = 276 kPa

Patm = 11 m of water

Solution:

Convert Pg to m of water

P = yh

276 = (9.81)h

h = 28.13 m of water

Pa = Pg + Patm

Pa = 28.13 + 11

Pa = 39.13 m of water

11. A boiler installed where the atmospheric pressure is 752 mm hg has a pressure reading of 12 kg/cm^2. what is the absolute pressure in kpa?


The absolute pressure : 1277.056 KPa

Further explanation

Boiler is a device used to produce steam which is then used for various needs for example for electricity generation

Inside the boiler experiences pressure due to the heating (from burner) of the water inside

Absolute pressure is the total pressure resulting from the sum of the pressures in the boiler with atmospheric (air) pressure. Pressure shown on  the boiler (pressure gauge) is the pressure inside the boiler

Can be formulated:

P gauge = P absolute  - P atmosphere

P Absolute  = P gauge +  P  atmosphere

P atmospheric standard is valued at 1 atm = 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa.

A boiler installed where the atmospheric pressure is 752 mm Hg has a pressure reading of 12 kg/cm².

So :

P atm = 752 mmHg = 752 x 0,133322 KPa =100.258 KPa

Pressure reading = P gauge =  12 kg/cm² = 12 x 98,0665 KPa = 1176.798 KPa

Then

P absolute = P gauge + P atm

[tex]\rm P~abs=1176.798 +100.258 \\\\P~abs=\boxed{\bold{1277.056~KPa}}[/tex]

Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD) is a calculation used to calculate the average absolute or absolute error. MAD is used when an analyst wants to measure forecasting error in the same unit of measure as the original data.

MAD measures forecast accuracy by averaging the forecast errors (the absolute value of each error). MAD is useful when measuring forecast error in the same units as the original series. MAD is the first measure of overall forecasting error for a model.

The MAD of a data set is the average distance between each data value and the mean. MAD is a way to describe the variation in a data set. MAD helps us understand how the values are spread out in a data set.

Step 1 Find the mean of the dataStep 2 Find the distance between each data value and the mean.Step 3 Find the sum of the distances in Step 2.Step 4 Divide the sum in Step 3 by the total number of data values.
