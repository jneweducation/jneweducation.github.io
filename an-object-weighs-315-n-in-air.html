Archimedes states that the buoyant force acting on an object which is partially or completely immersed into a fluid is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object

14. An unbalanced force of 50 n acts on an object weighing 100 n. what acceleration is produced


I think we should first clarify the weight of the object. Is it 100kg or 100 N?

If, as stated it is 100N, then we first convert this to kg. Newtons is a unit of force, so to get the mass, we have to remove the effect of the acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2).

Then 100 N = 100 kgm/s^2.

Dividing this by 9.81 m/s^2, we get approximately 10.19 kg.

Now, we use Newton's Laws of Motion, the equation is F = ma, that is force = mass x acceleration, so we can get the acceleration, a by taking F/m.

a = F/m

a = 50N / 10.19kg

a = 4.91 m/s^2


Now if the given is 100kg, we can directly use F=ma or a = F/m:

a = 50N / 100kg

a = 0.5 m/s^2

2. If the object weighs 975 N on the moon, its weight on earth will be approximately 5850 N.

Explanation:

The weight of an object is the mass of the object multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity (g). On earth, g is equal to 9.8m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex]. However, the moon's acceleration due to gravity is weaker as compared to earth's pull of gravity. Specifically, the moon's pull of gravity is about 1/6 as that of the earth. Thus, to get the weight of the object on earth given the weight of an object on the moon, we multiply it by 6.

3. If the person weighs 980 N and 163 on Earth and Moon, respectively, the acceleration due to gravity or the gravitational strength on the moon is 1.63 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex].

Explanation:

Weight is a kind of force. It is obtained by multiplying the mass of the object (m) and acceleration due to gravity (g). Given that the person weighs 980 N on earth and since acceleration due to gravity on earth is constant, which is 9.8 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex], we can determine its mass. To get the mass, we will divide the weight by the acceleration due to gravity (m=w/g). This is derived from the formula of weight or force. Substituting the formula, the mass of the person is 100kg.

We can now use this mass to get the acceleration due to the gravity of the moon. It is important to note that mass is constant. It does not change. To solve for the acceleration due to gravity on the moon, we divide the weight of the person on the moon by its mass (g=w/m). Substituting this formula,  the acceleration due to gravity on moon is 1.63m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex].

4. The net force will be 334N and the object will not exhibit a state of equilibrium. Instead, it will move in the direction of the force of 535N.

Explanation:

The net force is the vector sum of all the forces acting on the object. Vector sum since the direction is considered here. Opposing forces mean that the forces applied are in opposite directions. Thus, one will have a negative sign and the other will have a positive sign. Combining the two forces, we will be subtracting the magnitude of two forces because they have different signs (as a rule in algebra). The direction will be on the force with a bigger magnitude. The object will only exhibit a state of equilibrium if the forces applied are equal and in opposite directions. In this case, the forces will cancel each other.
