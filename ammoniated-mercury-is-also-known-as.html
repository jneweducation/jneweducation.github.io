17. If 13.0 liters of oxygen at STP are heated to 439 °C, what will be the new volume of a gas if the pressure is also increased to 3000.0 mm of mercury?


Answer:

To find the new volume of the gas, you can use the ideal gas law, which states that the product of the pressure, volume, and temperature of an ideal gas is a constant. The equation for the ideal gas law is:

PV = nRT

where P is the pressure of the gas, V is the volume of the gas, n is the number of moles of the gas, R is the ideal gas constant, and T is the temperature of the gas.

Since you know the pressure, volume, and temperature of the gas, you can rearrange the equation to solve for the new volume:

V = (nRT) / P

Plugging in the values from the problem, you get:

V = (n * 8.31 * 439) / 3000

= (n * 3.69) / 1.2

To solve for the volume, you need to know the number of moles of the gas. To find the number of moles, you can use the ideal gas law again, this time solving for n:

n = PV / RT

= (13.0 * 3000) / (8.31 * 439)

= 0.754 moles

Plugging this value back into the equation for the new volume, you get:

V = (0.754 * 3.69) / 1.2

= 2.29 liters

Therefore, the new volume of the gas will be 2.29 liters.

28. if 10.0 liters of oxygen at STP are heated to 298 K, what will be the new volume of gas if the pressure is also increased to 1520.0 mm of mercury?​


Answer:

V2 = 5.46 L

Explanation:

Solve for the final volume using Combined Gas Law.

P2V2 / T2 = P1V1 / T1

V2 = V1 • (P1 / P2) • (T2 / T1)

V2 = (10.0 L) • (760.00 mmHg / 1520.0 mmHg) • (298 K / 273 K)

V2 = 5.46 L

29. 16.0 liters of oxygen at STP are heated to 700 °C.what will be the new volume of gas if the pressure is also increased to 1820.0 mm of mercury​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

The temperature and pressure of an ideal gas at STP are 273 K and 760.00 mmHg, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{760.00 mmHg} \\ V_1 & = \text{16.0 L} \\ T_1 & = \text{273 K} \\ P_2 & = \text{1820.0 mmHg} \\ T_2 & = 700^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{973 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume using combined gas law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_2 & = \frac{V_1P_1T_2}{P_2T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{16.0 L})(\text{760.00 mmHg})(\text{973 K})}{(\text{1820.0 mmHg})(\text{273 K})} \\ & = \boxed{\text{23.8 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the new volume of the gas is 23.8 L.
