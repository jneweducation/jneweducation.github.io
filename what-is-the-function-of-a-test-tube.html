3. What is a test tube how does it work?how does it function?​


Answer:

What is test tube?

a thin glass tube closed at one end, used to hold small amounts of material for laboratory testing or experiments.

How does a test tube work?

Test tubes intended for general chemical work are usually made of glass, for its relative resistance to heat. Tubes made from expansion-resistant glasses, mostly borosilicate glass or fused quartz, can withstand high temperatures up to several hundred degrees Celsius.

How does it function?

It to hold small amounts of substances, usually liquid, which are then manipulated in some way, such as being placed over the flame of a Bunsen burner.

24. a microbe in a test tube weighs 0.001 g what is the total weight of microbes in 456 test tubes


Answer:

The total weight of microbes is 0.456 g.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem

The problem above is asking for the total weight of microbes in 456 test tubes. To find the answer, we have to multiply the weight of each microbe by the number of test tubes.

Given:

number of test tubes - 456

weight of each microbe - 0.001 g

Equation:

0.001 × 456 = N

Solution:

In multiplying the whole number by 0.001, we just have to count the number of decimal places, then place the decimal point in the same number of places from the right of the whole number.

Since 0.001 has three decimal places, just move right three times, then put the decimal point.

456 = 0.456

Final Answer:

0.456 g
