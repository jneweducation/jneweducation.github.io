An example of calculating story problem in math.

Story problem is a type of question in math where the students are tasked to calculate the correct answer by referencing the information contained in the story. More often than not, this type of question is intentionally designed to make the students think about how to use the information available and to reach the right conclusion.

One example of a story problem is the question contained in the original question of this thread. First, we must identify the information that would be useful in calculating the correct answer. Take a look at the step-by-step explanation below:

First, we all know that the fee for 3 hours if Php 2100.Second, we should calculate the price of the service for an hour. DIRECT VARIATION

Problem:

» The service fee f of a physical therapist varies directly as the number of hours n of service rendered. A physical therapist charges PHP 2,100 for 3 hours service to patients in home care. How much would he be paid for 6 hours of service?

Answer:He will paid PHP 4,200 for 6 hours.

_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _

Step-by-step explanation:

- Based on the problem, the equation is in the form of [tex]\sf \: f = kn.[/tex]

where,

f is the service feen is the number of hours, and;k is the constant of variation

- And the givens are:

[tex]\: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: \: f_{1} = 2100 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: f_{2} = \tiny \: unknown \\ \: \sf n_{1} = 3 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: n_{2} = 6[/tex]

- So, to find the unknown value, we need to substitute the given values (first set of f and n) to the equation of variation.

[tex] \: \: \: \sf \: f = kn \\ \sf \: 2100 = k \: (3) \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: k = \frac{2100}{3} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: k = \underline{ \boxed{ \orange{ \sf700}}}[/tex]

Hence, the equation of variation is [tex]\underline {\boxed{ \sf \: f = 700n}}[/tex]

Then, solve for f when n = 6.

[tex]\sf \: f = 700n \\ \: \: \: \: \sf \: f = 700 \: (6) \\ \: \: \sf \: f = \underline{ \boxed{ \orange{ \sf 4200}}}[/tex]

Thus, the value of f is 4,200.

- Therefore, he will paid 4,200 for 6 hours.
